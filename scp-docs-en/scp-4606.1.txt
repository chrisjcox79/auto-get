I want to like this, I do. The conceit of an ancient deity classified as a modern-day anomalous entity is interesting to me. But I have numerous gripes with the execution that rob me of how much I would enjoy this. I think its reliance on other articles to tell its story is too prominent: I came here to read about and understand this anomaly, not to gain a better understanding of the established properties of other articles by proxy. There are also aspects of the story here that strain my suspension of disbelief, and I don't think there is quite enough here to separate itself from other mythological demigods-turned-SCPs out there.

feedback/rant

–block

I'll start off by explaining what I do like. I like the portrayal of the O5's employment of the reality bender. It makes sense in the contest of the article and the Foundation in general: reality benders are far too important to piss off, so having a placated reality bender on your staff is an exceptional boon.

After mulling this over, though, I actually think it's the execution of cliches here that doesn't work:

The article doesn't make me feel that this entity is a spooky murder monster. It tells me that it is, and compares it to other articles in an attempt to display that, without going into the properties that make it so. When an SCP relies so heavily on other articles as a means of comparison, it does neither its own article nor any crosslinked articles any justice. It's essentially saying . And that doesn't appeal to me.

While it makes sense that the O5s would want to employ a powerful being, how they came to trust it doesn't. This actually ties in with my next gripe as well:

The portal it can create. Why is the portal necessary to tell this story? If the entity can portal out of the site at any time, then why does it bother staying contained? I can't think of any reason why it would continue to be contained, other than because it's a Foundation employee that happens to know O5-3.

These aren't issues unique to this article. I've seen issues plaguing cliche justification throughout the contest. The point of the contest is to recontextualize old cliches in a manner that gives them new life. They're cliches for a reason: they're overused concepts that do little to make a story original. You can use a cliche as a means of telling a better story, but you have to do extra story work to make your use of the concept unique and interesting in its own way. And while I don't think you were able to execute your concept that well here, I would love to see a version of this written after the contest that doesn't rely on cliches.

https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/floppyphoenix

Response

-hide

to answer your first question, I don't think it's relying too much on crosslinks to other articles to tell it's story. I made this purely in mind as a Thaumiel that can contain Sapient Keters, and to do that without trying to kill them. Using fear as a way of keeping them in place was the perfect route. You are correct in your assumptions that this is nothing more than an employee for the Foundation. He literally said he wanted a job that would allow him to use his powers as a god, and being in the Foundation would do so. He's only classified as an SCP because the Council wants to and considering how many names and identities he's tsken over the centuries, I don't think he would mind being called anything else.
True. They don't really trust it, as it's only on the approval of O5-3 and their contract, but they really can't pass up an opportunity for an answer that takes care of their Keters that break out of containment once a week. Which also brings me to my next answer:
Firstly, the portals exist as a gateway of sorts into its dimension. This is answered in the addenda which clearly states that it is only allowed access to the site it is kept in. Gods cannot break their own laws and rules once they're set in stone. I'm sure that the Foundation is aware of this fact and uses it the way one would have a contract with a company.

All in all, I do plan on revisiting this after the contest is over. I also plan on making revisions to this since the only reason why I posted it was because I was almost out of time to post.

Icrashed.

blehh...

What I'll specifically point to is the issue Floppy points out is that there's not enough here to convince me the O5s would actually put this guy into service based off one O5's account and the scroll contract Deimos devised. Though I absolutely love Nine's reaction and outbursts.

The characterizations are better than him actually containing outside SCPs in the table I'd say. If and when you rewrite it, given how much space you're really allocated for a single SCP article, I'd focus on multiple iterations of different O5s meeting him through multiple wars.

This does a couple of things:

You can reasonably go as far back as WW1 or hell even as early as the Napoleonic War if you want to expand on Deimos via journals or even first-hand accounts (if we're going off the premise that O5s are immortal, eldritch beings).
Gives variety and subtext to the entity. You see him grow throughout these eras. Perform amazing feats sure, but always show some character growth interspersed.
Much like 4450 which was namedropped, you can expand on different journal entry formats, character interactions, and POVs.

Because the character itself is strong. I think that part above all is worth salvaging upon rewrite. Now, I think you tried to do it through the table format, but in reality, I don't think that was the strongest performance of who he was. And I think its a case of readers instinctively drawn to the fact your using really big names which draws the eye away from the main entity's pathos itself.

If you want to keep the table list, I'd recommend applying that with very simple, Series I creatures with more compact descriptions. Ones where you can focus on the entity's main avenue of inflicting terror after two parties are already in conflict or 

Elsewise, you could probably get away with having the O5's decision to even allow it near big-bad-keters and world enders like 2669 as a  option, refocusing purely on the logs and Deimos' growth throughout eras.

Or even a single conflict. This entire thing could've been scoped to WW2 honestly with multiple O5s.

These are all just suggestions I had to problems I saw in the final piece. I wish I could've contacted you earlier but my hands were really tied this week.

As it stands it's a novote atm though will change to upvote if the piece receives revisions. As it stands, I loved the logs man.
