03 Aug 2017 12:10

Problematic on several fronts.

1. SCP-3390 is NOT to be used by researchers to quickly learn the French language.
附加记录：    

Strikes off to me as lol-Foundation. Such a thing won't be considered in a professional environment. It's like a pharmacist taking experimental Viagra, with little idea on what will actually happen to said person.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Overall, the actual anomalies are very boring. It compels people to operate it, and act as an effective caricature of French patriotism. And that's all there is to the SCP, really. There is also a bulk of information that are not interesting, particularly most of the Relevant History. Focus on things directly pertinent to the SCP, not just the general type of tanks.

As for the experiment logs, the current formatting is problematic. It does not distinguish the experiment's purpose and the various components needed to conduct the experiment, which makes reading confusing. Also, most of them are effectively reiterations of whatever you have already told us in the article. The experiments do little in offering new information that would change our understanding of the SCP.

And the supposed new information given (SCP-3390's self-regeneration capabilities) is not a suitable direction. Adding a new anomalous effect at the end without context does not change our understanding of the SCP. That would be better executed if you end on a note that makes readers question the assumptions they made at the beginning of the article.
