Fold

03 Feb 2014 00:07

Fair questions. I don't really have concrete answers, but I have come up with some lines of thought on the matter.

Firstly, we don't know whether walls interrupt the ten metre radius. Keeping it in a safe may not protect people walking along nearby corridors. And the Foundation are probably still conducting some experiments on it, even if not with materials, so they'd probably prefer to just keep it in one room that's secure rather than moving an object with a ten metre radius of effect around the facility every time they want to poke at it. Secondly, as to the question of line of sight  the implication is that sight is the operative motivator but again, nothing I wrote explicitly demands it. Naturally, there's a potential for test logs with both of these!

Really, what I think it comes down to is that the containment is perhaps larger than strictly necessary, but it's hardly excessive and convoluted. A big room, a few metres of wire, a few servo motors and other little electronic oddments. That's pretty much it. I doubt that put any visible dent at all in the Foundation's budget.
