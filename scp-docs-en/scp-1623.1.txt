What to do with B and W in public? What about the captured ones? What to do, when there is a disproportional number of either? What to do with the area during the event? And then: How? etc., etc.
附加记录：    

+Response

-whatever

In a REAL scientific document you would be right. If the Foundation were real, each SCP would be dozens of pages long. Hell, each Containment Procedure would be at LEAST dozens of pages long, detailing each and every possible permutation, every reaction for any possible action. I know this, because I do technical writing as my job, and I work for a huge government agency.

But the Foundation isn't real, and thus, you don't _actually_ need to prepare for every contingency. You only need to prepare for what you think your readers may mentally call you out on. In my experience here, you basically just need to show that you were thorough and your Foundation is putting enough thought into containing everything.

Specifically:

Your first two sentences BOTH talk about containing the battlefields. You probably only need the first sentence, as the second presents a ton of useless information (is the size of the battlefields important?) and has an SCP acronym which might be a turn-off for some. Next sentence is fine. Then, you don't really need the number of MTF stations built, that's really just implied when you mention MTF at all. Next sentence is fine. But then, do you really need to go into how the Foundation gets its temperature data? Do you need to mention that they have their own temperature satellites? And then you go into a contingency plan about what happens if the satellite is down. Unless this skip has something to do with that satellite and interfering with it, I think you can just get rid of that whole piece and I guarantee you no one will question you. The Foundation has so many assets, you may assume they can get reliable temperature data without anyone questioning you.

The rest is okay, but you might get rid of the tracking from birth portion. It opens too many questions. How do they know for sure who's an instance? Etc?

Just my two cents.

This is a solid attempt, but in the end I can't get behind this one. Part of it is that there's a lot of things on the periphery that I feel are distracting. Like the future-predicting satellite that's necessary for containment, or the Horizon Initiative being involved. There's too many moving parts, in my opinion, for the narrative that you're going for. On top of that, you've got an interpretation of the Foundation that seems to involve it being many centuries old, which is interesting to me, but when it's thrown in with all the other stuff on the side you've got going, it feels like yet another distraction.

Conceptually, I find the idea of duelling sets of astral projections fighting and affecting that year's agricultural outlook interesting, but I feel that it loses something in the translation. Like, as a folk legend, I find it really intriguing, and I think it would be really cool to hang out at a tavern somewhere and listen to some old Italian farmer's tales of spiritual battles with crazy famine-imps from his youth. As a scientific report of anomalous phenomena, it's just not working for me.

There's a few wording errors related to the finer points of the English language still in the article; I'll take a look in a minute and fix up a few things, but you may want to read this over once more and check for small errors.

One last thing:
