Can SCP-825 be salvaged?

27 Aug 2009 15:09

So far SCP-825 has 100% negative rating. Disappointing as I am, I have to concede that there are a lot of problems with it. The basic idea isn't terribly original, the logs are probably too wordy and I think I got distracted by a bunch of different ideas so any ideas of any worth got lost.

I'd like to turn it around if I can, and would welcome any suggestions as to what steps I should take. Ideas I've had:

Cut my losses, chalk it up as a learning experience and leave it be as a testimony to my inability to be brief
Decommission it, free up the 825 number for something more worthwhile. I don't know that it would even make a good decommission story, since there's nothing stopping us from just throwing it in a furnace.
Split out the experiment log to its own page. Easy to do, probably won't change much.
Get rid of the experiment log, condense any worthwhile points into the main body (The guy and his sisters, the dog lover, 682 and Clef are the ones I'd like to keep, what do others think?)
Trim down the log (maybe combine with splitting it to its own page). There are probably entries that need to be a lot shorter, or maybe cut completely.
Redesign the function. The whole "Worst Fear" thing allows a lot of variety, but maybe that's the problem. Maybe I should just redesign it to be that it makes you experience attacking and torturing the ones you love most, which I personally thought was the most horrific scenario.

Thanks for your help.

09 Sep 2009 10:32

Hi people *waves* I am new here also, like Johnny, so forgive me if I overstep my boundaries. I just wanna help!

Okay, so I read your article, and I have to admit it wasn't very good… But I do think it has Bucketfuls of potential!!! I know that most of the experianced SCP writers here understand the proper formula for making an article of writing work and have encountered ideas have been horrifically overused.

Well, I have no fooking clue about writing. My problem with the SCP is that it lacks details that I feel would make it more realistic and relatable.

Here are some things that I feel might help make you SCP better.

* What part of the middle ages (yeah, I know I am useing middle ages and Medieval interchangibly, sorry) is it from?- All lot has happened in the middle ages, since it pratically took up a whole millenium. Style of clothes, language, art, architecture, traditions, RELIGION (that be a big influence) etc., have evolved a lot during that time. So pretty much the period of time in the middle ages really influences the style (and effectiveness of protection) of the helmet.

*What part of Europe is it from? (I assume it is European, or from the British islands(I don’t know if The UK is considered part of Europe or not)) Because that also has alot to do with the style of the helmet.

*What is the story behind it? I have a hard time with the fact that it causes hallucinations just because. How did it get that way? I know that SCP articles are not to be written like a story, but like data in a file, so finding a clever way to incorporate it’s origins of why it became the way it is now would give the article more depth and meaning.

The best way I can think of incorporating the story into the article without the article turning into a story is:

*if it belonged to or allegidly belonged to a famous and noble knight (it couldl be based on a REAL famous knight, either fictional or non fictional, (Like King Arthur/nonfictional, or Amadis of Gaul/fictional) because then the reader can associate more with the story cos of familiarity), but doesn’t have too.

Identification that points to the knight could be a symbol or unique feature (like a swastika, or decorative metal wings),that he used, an inscription ( Like: a qoute he was famous for uttering during battle(I shall crush you in the name of the Lord!) or the name of said person (The helmet of Amadis, Amadis’ helmet, Amadis waz here)).

*You could also have a excerpt of the legendary Knights story to indicate the scp’s owner was so important that fairytales/epics were written about their great deeds. In the middle ages people were VERY superstitious due to lack of knowledge, so it would be acceptable to have fantastical elements (plus, in the SCP world dragons and magic wouldn’t be considered that far-fetched).

Here are some possible scenarios for how the SCP got its power:

*The knight spurns the advances of a sorceress, and, well you know what they say, “Hell hath no fury like a woman scorned,”

*A king fears that the knight will bring an end to his empire (kind of like the Jesus/Herod scenario, where the king fears the “messiah” will end his empire, which resultes the killing of infants), so He has a wizard curse the helmet.

*The knight is spurned(or cheated on, like the King Arthur/Gwenivere scenario) by a Woman he has fallen in love with, And goes in to such a deep depression that his negative energy creates a poltergeist like scenario, trapping itself in the helmet.

*Same as above, but instead of a polterguiest, his negative energy attracts an entity or demon that latches on to the helmet.

*Oh, I don’t know, the man who made the helmet had a chemical inbalance, and that day he was particulary manic depressive and unknowingly fused his negative energy into the the helmet

*the man went dipshit crazy during the end of his life for whatever reason, and now haunts the helmet.

*The steriotypical fairytale scenario, a sorceress/fairy/angel/random other paranormal entity disguises itself as a beggar and asks that the knight can spare his food/money or help, and the knight selfishly refuses, cursing the helmet to make him crazy (because fairytales have to be weird like that. Instead of cursing the actual person, they can curse an object instead… so head+cursed helmet= crazy!)

Different regions of the country could have different versions of the story, so to make it more mysterious to the reader there could be an alternate version of the story. It would make the first story seem less of a fact, and instead a possibility as to why the SCP is cursed.

I really hope that made sense… Cos I really REALLY suck at writing.
