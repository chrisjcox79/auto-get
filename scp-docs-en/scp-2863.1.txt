Last Halloween I posted my first article to the site after years of being a mostly silent lurker. In the year since, I've posted 11 more, met and befriended a ton of great people, become a better author, a better critic, and have had the pleasure on serving on three separate teams as Junior Staff. Thanks for the part y'all have played in my life for the last year, here's to even more.

Happy (almost) Halloween.

Thanksreading

Article obviously heavily inspired by MrWrong's similar articles.

Thank you to the dozens of people who helped me with this one, I'd credit you all but I know I'd miss someone.

The three examples at the end are the result of, in order, the massacre at nanking, the atomic bombings, and the tohoku earthquake.

The IJAMEA was created by user Mazyrian in SCP-2937 and has been largely developed by MrWrong (And coincidentally is now one article away from qualifying for GOI status).

30 Oct 2016 12:12

Well. Sort of. While stories about similar giant skeletal creatures stretch back well over a thousand years, technically the first literary reference to Gashadokuro as such was by Saitou in '68, itself based on a Utagawa's 1844 illustration, itself depicting events from the 900s.

However, at this point the Gashadokuro have become so ingrained in both Western and Japanese culture as ancient folkloric spirits that I don't really feel like it makes a difference. It is clearly a work of fiction, as is everything here, so I see no issue with taking a few artistic liberties.
