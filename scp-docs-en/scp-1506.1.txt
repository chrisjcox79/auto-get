I'll do a small run-through of nitpicks, will edit post.

+text

-text

It's irritating to read a paragraph of strikes out text; either use a collapsible or state that it's the old procedures.

Once confirmed as an occurrence SCP-1506-1's position is to be monitored and checked against computer
附加记录：    

Missing a comma here.

No methods have yet been devised to predict when or where instances of 1506 will occur. Application to upgrade to keter denied: procedures 1506-A, 1506-B and 1506-C remain effective.
附加记录：    

I see no point in mentioning this.

Procedure 1506-D: Following incident 1506 Sigma, any instance of SCP-1506-1 larger than 200 cubic metres in commercial flight paths are cleared for immediate [REDACTED]
附加记录：    

How do you kill an SCP by [REDACTED]?

SCP-1506 is the designation for the global phenomenon which spontaneously generates instances of SCP-1506-1.
附加记录：    

This sounds silly to me. I would rather have you explain what this phenomenon is first.

Little headway has been made as to the mode of transmission but the most favoured theory is communication of learned behaviour.
附加记录：    

There are two types of people: those who like commas, and those who don't. Unfortunately, you're the latter. :(

Investigation is ongoing into how the spiders communicate. Research on mallos gregalis and other social spiders has been promising but so far no headway has been made.
附加记录：    

If you told me what the phenomenon was first, then I wouldn't have to read ahead and look back at this to understand it

By application of multiple varieties of spider silk
附加记录：    

I'm not sure if there are various types of slide silk. Might want to do some research if you haven't already.

SCP-1506-1 exhibits remarkable behaviour as individuals of mutually predatory arachnid species exhibit anomalous cooperation.
附加记录：    

This is a jumble of long words.

From what I've seen so far, this is a hostile group of cloud spiders, which I actually like. However, how is this not Keter? It's Hostile /and/ uncontained.
Also, the wording of all these sentences sound awkward, try reading them out loud
Commas can be useful.

Final Verdict

Concept has potential, but the awkward writing, unrealistic and boring dialogue, and dissapointing execution makes it a downvote. I would get this looked at by more people in the draft forums. Also, You could try and make a hook for this, if you want a higher chance in evoking emotion.

Sky spiders! This successfully gave me the heeby jeebies, so that gets a +1 from me.

The energy drink cover story is clever, and kept me cemented firmly in +1 territory.

My view would be to remove the part about it prefering human prey, unless there was a compelling reason for them to prefer humans. I don't think it needs to be specifically targetting us to be scary, and I would have thought big, dumb, slow cattle in the countryside would be a much more profitable target for them than nimble, smart, tiny humans.

Regarding the containment procedures, I would imagine the Foundation would attempt to capture an instance of SCP-1506-1 and contain it (in a warehouse like structure). Then, if they can confirm they are able to keep the captured instance alive, they would be able to seek and destroy any dangerous instances in the wild. They only need to keep one contained, after all.

Big nets between helicopters for containment. Some kind of air-dropped incendaries/napalm/madeupness for destroying wild instances?

PS: I would suggest taking out the expungement in the aeroplane encounter (and changing the redaction on the flight numbers to black boxes). The Foundation are not squeamish, so I don't think you should hide the gorey stuff. I wanna know the gorey details! Tell us about the plane!

(This critique gets a little stream-of-consciousness. Sorry for the lack of organization.)

I like the idea a lot, but the execution needs work. The entire dialogue part really doesn't work as such; if you read those lines out loud to yourself, they literally sound like a badly-written movie conversation. You have an uncapitalized genus (what cameras are capturing this?

Some of the sentences can be taken out entirely, as their general length makes them sound kind of awkward;  (it would be more realistic if the spiders had some kind of low failure rate), and I don't see why it would necessarily be mammals per se. Do birds just slide off?

And why the redactions in the final log, the plane-crashing-into-spiders thing? The story in the log is perfect without redactions; it can literally just be  or anything else, but the redaction as is is just bothersome.

Anyway, I really think this could be a great SCP with fixes, but I have to neutral vote for now.

EDIT AGAIN: Just noticed that you use a blackboxed SCP in the containment procedures. That can go too. Just generically crosstesting SCPs with one another is a terrible idea, and blackboxing in the containment procedures is heavily frowned upon. It's a fairly easy edit, but it's probably the most glaring issue at present.
