I'm glad it's provoking questions for you, and this was really about the emotion first and the story second, but there is a story I was trying to tell:

+spoilers

-OK

The dolls are scientific probes, taking little bits of tissue out of the kids and sending it somewhere (the future). The probes were actually placed by the foundation itself, who sent them back in time to monitor/assassinate these babies. (That's why some of them become inert; those babies just turned out not to be whatever the future foundation is looking for.)

So that's what the researchers discover when they do their special project, and that's why the O5s update the procedures and start letting the machines have the babies instead of keeping them separated. The unanswered question is why they were sent back in the first place, but it must be really important, or we'd never do something so terrible

I'm happy to hear I created the atmosphere that I did with the story, and love the idea that maybe it's deep enough for people to have their own interpretations (rather than just too poorly written to understand). Thanks for reading!

07 Sep 2017 20:08

I'd still say that this has some touching up needed, and I'm going to recommend a rewrite.

+block

–block

The SCProcedures could use a bit of work. I'm personally against strike throughs as used here, because it is still clunky to have so much back and forth, and it really takes the impact out of what could be a decent hook.

It could be restructured separately, and simply relabeled . I think giving each element (the perceived danger imposed to the -2s vs the mandate to allow -1s to operate) some space to breath would give this line:

SCP-3661-1 activation events are not to be interrupted under any circumstance.
附加记录：    

A lot more weight than it currently has, doubly so if it could be more strongly implied that the -2s are infants in the 'former' procedures. A good way to do that would be to change 'humanoid containment cells' to something like 'nursery'.

Couple nitpicks:

contaminated by
附加记录：    

I feel as though this should be 'containing'? There's no mention of this spreading from one stuffed toy to the next, which this seems to imply.

SCP-3661-1A, SCP-3661-1B, and so on.
附加记录：    

Once there is a certain number of them, we're going to have to append extra numbers after the letters anywaysI'd maybe designate the toys 'SCP-3661-A1', A2, A65, etc for ease of reference.

by making small incisions on the face, neck, or limbs and [REDACTED]2.
附加记录：    

Based off how this was previously written (being a tech-redaction), is the information relating to their operation/function? As it reads right now, it looks like you're redacting the areas where it makes incisions and I'm not sure that's the intent.

If so, it could be rewritten as 

 it could still stand to be slightly reworded to make it clearer (at this point) that the redaction is covering their action, not their entry point.

Also, having read the hidden content, I can't say the level of redaction is necessary; aside from perhaps the final one revealing it to be Foundation tech.

That's pretty much it for my nitpicks. Beyond that though, I think the reveal is poorly handled. I can't sustain my disbelief enough. The justification for allowing this is incredibly flimsy, there's simply not enough context to show how they arrived at this conclusion. I don't know what GOI is being referenced (Serpents Hand, I assume, due to the inclusion of the Librarians), or how a temporal paradox factors into the overall picture.

Just because it's Foundation tech doesn't instantly mean there's temporal fuckery afoot - this technology could have been stolen/recreated by spies, for starters. It could have been technology developed concurrently to the SCPF's equipment from another group, or any number of alternate interpretations. The temporal aspect is glossed over instead of cohesively incorporated into the overall narrative, making the entire thing come across as an exercise in shock over substance.

While the recent edits have improved this slightly, it's not at the point where I'd consider reversing my -1.

05 Jun 2018 17:22

Though not my kind of read, the concept itself isn't bad. But I'm with Shaggydredlocks on this.

First, only the last redaction is warranted. The rest isn't helping with the sense of a paranoid Foundation. Instead, they are creating the sense of a Foundation that wants to seed paranoia among its own ranks, because it creates too many questions and, at the end, it is simply technical details easy to guess from what is already given.

Also, the conclusion is forced and the new SCProcedures seem to be for shock value. The Foundation have no reason whatsoever to conclude time travel, there is still an infinite array of other possibilities, ranging from spies to other dimensions. IF the time travel theory had any evidence behind it, it would still be unlikely for they to fear a paradox. Several anomalies deal with time in some fashion, and each is so unique that you can't reach standard conclusions about consequences that way. What, after all, would a paradox cause?

Even if the Time Travel was confirmed, it isn't very like them to simply let an anomaly have it its way. They would, instead, keep the first procedures and set more thorough surveillance over the -Bs. You could, instead, set the first SCProcedures to include freeing the children after they are confirmed non-anomalous, and then the second one is to keep them contained indefinitely. Or you can simply drop the change in containment altogether.
