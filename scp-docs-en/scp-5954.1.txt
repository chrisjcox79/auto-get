I want to like this. The concept of a royal plant monster coming out of the sea with explosive farts and then getting upset about houseplants is hilarious, but I feel like this lacks in execution.

As it is currently written, the explosive farts feel like a tacked on attempt to make it fit to the theme. It isn't really used for anything except a few jokes, which I didn't find exceptionally funny, and the article wouldn't be worsened by the skip not having 'a condition'.

The biggest issue I have here though that the text could use a few more revisions. There are several sections that read kind of clumsily, the dialogue doesn't feel very realistic to me and there are several formatting errors throughout the piece, with names lacking bolding and spaces missing between lines.

If this saw a few more revisions and the text was cleaned up, I could see myself upvoting this, but as it currently stands I lean towards a downvote.

Mainly I think you should go over the dialogue again. The characters feels somewhat one dimensional and to a certain extent even cartoonish in a way that makes reading their interaction boring. I understand the cartoonish behavior from the anomaly itself, but when it's the case with all of the characters, it gets kind of old. Also, this is more of a personal nitpick, but I find the repeating 'over' in the end of each line jarring to read. I don't see why the Foundation couldn't have a continuous live audio feed.

I feel like one of the reasons I feel this way is that there is very little straight description of what's happening. The readers have to form a mental image out of dialogue alone, the result of which is that the characters aren't animated enough.

Aup

–block

Dam:  Hannah Dam checking in. Just finished inspecting the surf, there’s a trail of seaweed dragged out of the water and onto the sand. But it stops right by a crater in the sand, like someone buried a grenade after pulling the pin, over.
附加记录：    

She's explaining something that she has done something after it's already done, skipping the action entirely. She's just kinda standing there and explaining because there's no indication of anything else and the reader has to be like . Also seems silly to me that she would check in only after she's done doing her thing, even though Command just asked everyone to speak up. It leaves like an odd feeling gap where Command speaks, then Dam does her thing, speaks and only then everyone else speaks up.

Jefferson:  Do they? I’ve never heard a metaphor that worked as intended. Similes, on the other hand-
附加记录：    

Seems odd to me that he would just go on a random tangent about metaphors and similes in the middle of a mission when they have nothing to do with anything.

Ekblad:  Enough. There’s an arm sticking out of the beach here.

Bjugstad:  Anybody attached?

Ekblad:  Negative. Only identifying marks… paint on the remaining nails, with ring tan on a finger. Let’s move out. Nothing more we can do here. We can inspect the scene after the skip is inert. Command, you reading us still, over?
附加记录：    

Again, there's no action described here so its kind of like they all just stand there and talk.

Ekblad:  I’ve got visual contact. Directly ahead of us.
附加记录：    

Ahead of them where? On the street? On a sidestreet? I don't know. In a sense, the picture that is being painted here is incomplete, which breaks immersion.

As for formatting goes, you forgot to fix this part:

Ekblad:  Command, this is Commander Calvin Ekblad, we’ve arrived on the beach, over.

Command: Confirmed. Check in, all agents, over.
附加记录：

The self-title it proclaims reminds me a lot of some of the tales involving Carnegias/Poky; that would be a rather fun interaction with this thing.

I enjoy this, but the logs do feel kinda off; the last few lines, for example:

+block

–block

SCP-5954:  Even the most basic of fundamentals in carriership, someone didn’t know how to water a peace lily, poor thing was dry to the bone!

Elliot:  … So, you’re saying these plants were causing you psychic pain?

SCP-5954:  How they cried! Day and night! It was an unending tragedy as they faded with the dreadful lamentations unto the coil of death.

Elliot:  That… we can work with that.
附加记录：    

I don't feel like these two are actually having the same conversation. The SCP already mentioned feeling the pain of nearby plants in the previous log, presumably a short time ago, but Elliot acts like this is the first she's hearing of it. Plus, the SCP didn't actually mention pain here when Elliot realizes it. And then SCP-5954 says something that isn't really a clear answer to her question, followed by Elliot saying something that also doesn't look like a response to what it just said about plant cries.
