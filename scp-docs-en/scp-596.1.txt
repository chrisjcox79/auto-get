SCP-596

12 Apr 2009 00:22

I've had a bit of a hiatus since my first 3 SCP creations, but I found some spare time and had an idea rattling round my head.

I figured that this would be a good deterrent against tomb robbers. Try and steal it and it'll subject you to an eternity of agony. If someone try's to take it off you, you die and they end up trapped in agony. No matter how many times you kill the person, they just grow back.

I guess the only way out is to destroy the statue itself, but I think the Foundation would find an endless supply of blood and organs too good to pass up. Obviously they could practically get that from the class D's anyway, but this way they can make copies of rare blood and organs.

And I know that a Foundation ethics committee might sound like an oxymoron, but since the site is about horror, what job could be more horrible than trying to answer the ethical questions that Foundation activities raise.

Since you're using the threat diamond:

This should really be a Level 4 Physical Threat - you don't come away from touching it severely maimed, you come away from touching it dead.

Also, has anyone tried dosing the person holding the statue with lots of painkillers to see if they're screaming due to physical pain instead of some kind of mental effect?

Re: Threat Diamond

12 Apr 2009 04:05

I've made the change you suggested. I'm new to the threat diamond and I wasn't sure if it warranted a level 4. It's not dangerous to be around or near or even to touch so long as you don't let it touch your bare skin, and it's certainly not actively trying to kill anyone. But reading over the descriptions again, I think you're right, that it does kill you dead (eventually), so it should be a class 4.

I hadn't thought of experiments with painkillers as opposed to sedatives. To be honest the exact nature of what SCP-596 inflicts upon the subjects that makes then scream for the rest of their lives is perhaps the only mysterious element. I guess I see the foundation as being more interested in what they can get out of it.
