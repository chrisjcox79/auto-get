Fold

13 Sep 2013 13:48

I don't like it.

You successfully got rid of the creep factor and brought the tone up to standards, but I don't like the story. The video recording of the domestic dispute is way too cliche, and the young girl's reaction to it is also (even Star Trek TNG has an episode where a guilt-ridden little boy develops a delusion of being an Android to cope with his belief that he killed his family and a whole space station. It's been done).

What's more, the implication that her delusion about the event (I am a doll. I am perfect and pretty so Mommy will love me and Daddy will love her and everything is fine) has taken on physical reality on account of her being around a bunch of broken porcelain at the time of trauma is weak. Trauma being connected to the supernatural is like the grandaddy of all horror cliches.

Your clinical tone is good, and your containment procedures are appropriate, but the way you've re-imagined the concept and the story doesn't do anything good for me.

-1
