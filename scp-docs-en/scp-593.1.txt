Re:

21 Apr 2009 16:38

One, that it is so consistently targeted. There are plenty of real-world viruses that cause brain damage, yes. Measles itself can do so; there's an entire syndrome that attacks the brain of individuals who were infected before age two, years after the fact, and essentially turns their brain into Swiss cheese before killing them. But this virus very specifically targets one area of the brain and one only, which points directly to it being genetically engineered by whoever hitched it up to the stock exchange's trading floor.

Which leads to reason two: the amount of sheer havoc a virus that destroyed its victims' concept of number could do if released in the appropriate places. If ninety per cent of every single person on the trading floor of the New York Stock Exchange- or the Nikkei, or, hell, any air traffic control center-lost the ability to comprehend numbers, the amount of damage to the world economy would be, literally, incalculable. The Foundation gets involved because the last thing they want is the possibility of whoever created this damn thing popping up again and killing entire economies with a few well-timed cases of what looks like measles.

Can you imagine what would happen if this thing got out without a proper vaccine? With such a high infection rate, it might lead to the end of modern society! Imagine a world where nobody could count! So many things would fall apart that we'd be blasted back to the Stone Age. No ability to judge proportions, no ability to handle sets of objects…

The article notes that ninety percent of non-immune people will contract the disease within thirty days; I'm not entirely sure where they would get their immunity from, given that the article also declares the standard measles vaccine ineffective. Does innate measles resistance also protect against SCP-593? If so, this gets a bit less scary.
