I think your image might be broken; I'm not able to see it.

I won't worry about that, though. This is a curious little thing.

SCP-3693 can only be seen by observers when their eyes are closed.
附加记录：    

This is the line that hooked me. It's such a simple effect, but it's unique. I haven't seen anything in recent memory that had the same kind of effect. And the image it presents, of a floating statue staring you in the face amidst almost total darkness, is cool and slightly unsettling.

I'm unsure why this is Euclid, though. It only reacts when people observe it with their eyes closed, and is otherwise inert. At least, that's what I assumed to be the case. I'm confused why this thing requires constant supervision, and why it isn't Safe. I've read over it a few times, but I can't really begin to come up with an answer.

The discovery log, while mostly benign, did give this thing a little bit more mystery.

After securing SCP-3693 and moving it off-site, Foundation agents returned to the warehouse to continue their examination of the site. The basement could no longer be located.
附加记录：    

Curious. I'm not sure what the implications are here, but it does seem like there's a little more to this simple statue than what's in the document.

Gonna novote this one. A little brief and bare for my liking, but I did enjoy the concept and what substance was there. Not enough to upvote, though.
