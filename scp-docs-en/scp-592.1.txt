30 Apr 2011 18:06

Well, I can think of two possible reasons that they might use software to analyze this thing:

1. Class-D personnel are significantly more useful than a computer, and probably quite a bit harder to find. They can be feed things, be fed to things, clean the containment areas of things, worship things, dance for things, you get the picture. As such, I imagine that the Foundation would like any chance to have a computer do a job instead of using up a Class-D on it.

2. A researcher who designed the software for something else (or just because) pestered the administration to let them use their pet project, if only to justify the funding they receive. I can imagine the conversation going something like this:

Doctor: Hey, you know that text and image analysis software I made?

Admin: (sigh) Yes, Doctor. What about it?

Doctor: Well, I heard about that book that does things to you when you read it, and I was just wondering-

Admin: Fine, you can use it, if it'll get you to shut up about it.

Doctor: [Squeals in childlike excitement]
