19 Jan 2018 22:58

They aren't - but they have to be very insistent about it, because the alternative is that they've been needlessly torturing genuine copies of valued employees.
There are like three million test logs that couldn't make it in because they were bloating the article. I tried to reference them in a few places but didn't get much farther than that. The Samothrace thing alludes to the fact that cognitohazards don't 'stick' from one replica to the next, which means there isn't a 'host consciousness' responsible for the replicas - at least not in the traditional sense of a consciousness. The comment about electrical testing alludes to the fact that they've built up a fairly comprehensive model of how currents/data pass through this thing, and that's what led them to the suspicion that old replicas aren't deleted when a new one is created. Ultimately, though, none of that made it into the article, which means they're relegated to my headcanons rather than actual canon.
