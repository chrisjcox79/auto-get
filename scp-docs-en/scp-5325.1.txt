25 Mar 2020 05:58

I'm less than impressed. I find that some of the tone is a bit off in places to favor a more prose-like writing style, specifically in the last three logs (an easy fix if styled as if they are video logs instead of brief snippets of summaries). Additionally, I take some issues with the grammar here, particularly in places where there are multiple clauses and either commas are used too frequently or are completely missing. It's a bit of a tough read, as I found myself often revisiting sentences, and that some bits of information never come back into play (specifically the  lines)

However, I mostly don't really care for how the concept is executed. It doesn't really go anywhere with the premise, and if it does, I don't understand where it is has gone. It truly feels like it's repeating information that we understand (in the case of the testing log) and that it is conveying new information that doesn't add anything to the story told (in the case of the final logs).

Given that the above comments have expressed a satisfactory read, I am left to conclude that I have to be missing something here, because I found this to be incredibly lacking.

03 May 2020 14:53

I have to agree on RockTeethMothEyes with this one, something about this article rubs me the wrong way and I can't quite point out as to why.

Maybe it's the concept of people suddenly being taken to places most that are most meaningful to them; maybe it's the idea that this skip is almost similar to 348 (in the way that it reminds subjects of people they love, except this takes them to places that's related to people they love) but doesn't have the impact the latter has nor do I feel sympathy to those that returned home.

I do have to wonder as to why an MTF recruit seemed to recall the memories of others inside the train but there's no report or interview from Researcher Adell, what they've seen, or what they've done inside.

I also question the purpose of the Victorian-era Spanish lady and would've asked:  that we see and said film reel is recovered at the final expedition. While both stories make each other relevant, I question the purpose in the grand scheme of things and how it develops the story further.

While the tone fits with the wiki's standards (as far as I know) and the idea of a train delivering people back to their doorsteps is interesting, not much is really expanded on this idea.

No vote for me.
