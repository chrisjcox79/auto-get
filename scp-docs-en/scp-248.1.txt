Biology?

26 Jan 2010 15:21

I don't see why its effect on human skin is to become super-adhesive.

These things make  work at 110% of its optimum efficiency, yes?

And it's not limited to a single characteristic of that system - note the computer being boosted to 110% of multiple non-dependent traits.

If it's ineffective on biological systems, then… why is it bonding with the skin?

Or is it that the sticker bonds permanently with anything to which it's applied? If so, why is this only discovered during testing on a live subject?

And if not, what happens to a system from which the sticker is removed?

If we apply the sticker to , boost the system, then remove the sticker-adorned piece and put it in a different system… what happens to the second system?

What if the sticker-adorned piece is used for a different purpose in the second system?

What happens if we put 2 stickers on a single surface?

What happens if we cut one of these in half? Will a half-sticker boost a system by 5%? Will it damp a system to 55%?
