There are a couple of issues with tone and phrasing, but on the whole I enjoyed this and have no issues upvoting.

Rule 4 is currently the primary focus of testing as it has the most baring on the results of the ritual generation after the program settings themselves. For example, it has found if the user's intention is to kill someone they dislike, the resulting ritual will often result in something to cause the target harm. Many D-Class's results were rituals that allowed them to teleport or transport themselves outside the facility.
附加记录：    

This paragraph in particular stood out as an issue. First, I believe you are looking for . One influences a situation, the other exposes itself to the situation.

Second, the resulting ritual will often result in redundancy. Removing the word resulting will fix that.

Finally, I don't believe teleport is a clinical/scientific term, although I am willing to admit that I could be wrong about that.

Edit: Having just criticised redundancy, I then proceed to use redundant phrasing.
