Site Crit - Swamp Critter

16 Mar 2020 21:24

Hi, this is Cyvstvi13 and I'll be performing a criticism on this piece as part of the Swamp Critters Squad.

+Crit

-Crit

Overall

I actually loved the premise of this SCP. The combination of modern news media and amnesiac effects was done in a dynamic and satisfying way.

The topics of amnesia were treated with the professional demeanor I've come to expect from reading Jak's articles and it remained respectful throughout.

This was a suspiciously terrifying SCP that I didn't think would frighten me at first, but then I began overthinking the entire article and it dawned on me that my reactions would be quite similar to Ryan's. 'Curiosity killed the cat' and all that.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
SPaG

I noticed a few grammatical and spelling errors, as well as some tonal issues that I've noted below, some of these are suggestions to the author that they don't actually need to follow:

A computer tomography []
附加记录：    

A computed tomography []

Once the subject goes through basic recovered-memory therapies []
附加记录：    

I'd suggest 'Once the subject undergoes basic recovered-memory therapies [] so as to not break the stiff medicalese tone.

In the event that a human subject perishes due to exposure of SCP-5343 []
附加记录：    

In the event that a human subject perishes due to exposure to SCP-5343 []

These may range from anywhere between the subject's birth and the subject's death.
附加记录：    

The events covered may range from anywhere between the subject’s birth and the subject’s death. - The phrasing before took me a few reads to get around my head.

[]
附加记录：    

[]

[] before Foundation personnel entered the area.
附加记录：    

[] Prior to investigation by Foundation personnel.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Consistency/Flow

I felt the entire article flowed well and it succinctly moved from one relevant area to another, and no area felt like it overstayed its welcome.

My only suggestion here would be to include the dates inside of the diary entries of Ryan to give a sense of cohesion and indicate a diary format.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Closing Remarks

I don't have anything overly critical to say about this article. It was an expose into memory induced true media and was a startlingly wonderful piece. +1'd and would recommend to a friend.

SoullessSingularity, for any questions or concerns relating to this review.

21 Mar 2020 19:14

Up until the addendum the article was while well written, pretty much in line with most normal SCPs. The strongest part of the article and what caused me to give it a +1 was the narrative that was crafted in the addendum logs. It was an entertaining tragedy to read on how this random D-Class reacted to the anomalous properties of the app along with their personal thoughts on their closest friends and relatives, added strong character and reliability to the otherwise usually faceless orange testers.

My main criticism for this article is that the transition from reading about close friends and family to forgetting them feels too rushed. The D-Class kind of makes an instant jump in memory loss and as such the ending isn't as powerful as it could be if it were drawn out with more panic and fear about losing memories. Increasing the climax would add a heavy emotional punch similar to how other memory loss SCPs have done in the past. Overall though still good.
