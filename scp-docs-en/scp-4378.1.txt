Fold

14 Dec 2018 20:07

This is a -1 for me. No hesitation.

The idea here seems like it could work but the execution is a bit of a mess.

I don't feel anything really coherent about the way this is presented. I come into this immediately knowing what to expect out of it and it goes exactly as I imagined it would. There's no natural raising of stakes.

Those stakes are almost as high as they can be from the outset (with the secretary of state thing) and there's no subversion of expectations. That ruins any sort of coherent narrative arc when the piece is clearly trying to present a story.

I also don't get a strong sense of the thematic tone you're aiming for. Is this serious? Is it silly? It tries to be both and as a result only hits on either tone weakly. The starkly contrasting themes actually just makes the lack of good clinical tone throughout more apparent. I can see bits that are more silly that just don't really use good clinical language alongside bits that are serious and actually mostly hit the mark.

On top of that, there's insertion of interview logs with the pasta that do not seem to help develop anything in your story and aren't really good enough to work in the piece on their own.

Not to mention that there's blackboxing in the Containment procedures and while I have a personal opinion about overuse of blackbox that I am willing to put to the side most of the time that is something I cannot abide.

Good luck with your article!

Fold

14 Dec 2018 20:19

This is an amusing idea, but I ended up downvoting. The core of a noodle that must be handled very carefully to prevent chaos is good, but there is a lot of additional fluff that sends the tone all over the place (as Cimmerian already said) and also felt derivative of other articles - the infinite noodle dimension and the bad-English interview logs in particular. The whole White House setting felt bizarre (not in a good way) as there is no thematic connection between the White House and noodles, it comes across as a hamfisted way to make this as troublesome as possible without justification, and is really the sort of thing that would lead to a Broken Masquerade canon, which has a particular feel this isn't going for. The weird grammar issues are also present (mostly strange capitalisation choices).

On a lighter note, the noodle designations are questionable (couscous? really?), as are the non-noodle designations (I wouldn't really consider farfalle to be a noodle, but it has about the same level of noodle-related properties as spätzle)
