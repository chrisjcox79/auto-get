Re: Homeless Heterotrophs In-depth review

21 May 2018 06:35

Ibehalf.

–block

Homeless heterotroph review. SCP-3534 

Verdict: Downvote. 

Reasoning 

While the article is well written, it’s simply not an article yet. SCP-3534 is written in a way that, as i understand from reading it over 3 times, dodges around having to explain to the reader what SCP-3534-3 even is.
The audacious and gung-ho containment procedures seem to scream to me that i should expect an article entailing an audacious and gung-ho skip. But i don’t get one because i don’t even get a glimpse of what the danger is.  Extra-dimensional Fighters and blowing-up sites in retaliation to a single event is cool and all that, but i’m given no context as a reader to believe that it's a necessary precaution for the containment of a skip that has no description.
In my opinion, and i am adamant that this opinion is correct, SCP-3534-1 itself is simply a quick alternative to addressing the actual “Subject” of the skip itself. SCP-3534 seems to me to be false-front for the real “meat” of the article. If this where rewritten and and SCP-3534-1 and SCP-3534-3 where switched, then it would be much more relevant to the containment procedures and the article itself.
Despite the fact that this “Sekrit boat grafite code” is the harbinger of end-times. The article tells the reader 20% of the DIU submarine-of-doom’s activation code with the image . Aesthetic appeal is far less important than pragmatic article structure.

Possibly the most concerning part of this articles composition to me is how Site-self-absorbed the article is in general. This is not a user-friendly article in the slightest. a reader who doesn’t know an of the sites many side-canons has absolutely no boramiter of what is going on in this article.

“SCP-3534-2, Article I, Section 1 of the Hy-Brasil International Security Agreement of 1992 (Footnote) A binding treaty enacted by King Delbáeth II of Hy-Brasil between the Foundation, the Global Occult Coalition and several governments concerning the reappearance of entities similar to UAE-Brasil-78.

I appreciate how cleverly you blended this into the article. But this is asking way to much of the reader. We aren't all readerbots, and not all readerbots read the tome of “Site tales”

Written in 47 minutes by Homeless Heterotroph, Friday may 18th 2018

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pNFF1ZTl1oY

The Foundation isn't necessarily monitoring documents that contain this simple word. For example, if a memetic phrase existed and the Foundation was looking for it, they wouldn't look for every little thing that has one of the words of the said phrase in it. However, now that I look back on it, I could have made it much clearer. I'll get onto editing that.

The thing is, SCP-3534's anomalous properties only manifest when inscribed in a submarine, not a piece of paper. So the Foundation is actually just keeping a lookout for 4/5 of the characters or all of the characters next to one another in a document; even then, nothing bad can happen until those characters make it into a submarine's walls. Again, I'll make this more clear in the containment procedures.
