Not bad. Enough detail to sell the phenomenon as a genuinely documented scientific phenomenon, without going overboard and boring the reader with extraneous material. It hits a sweet spot in terms of tone, dry enough to help sell the more unsettling bits of the log without treading into  territory. Good work.

Two issues though, which while not affecting my overall vote I would really like to see addressed:

How can they extract DNA from the molars of the skeletons if they're all headless?
There is no reason whatsoever for that Daevite reference to be in there. I understand that crosslinking is being pushed now in some quarters, and that's fine and all, but I would prefer that stuff like that serve some sort of purpose. This article is strong enough to stand on its own; I see no reason to gratuitously throw cross-references in there.
