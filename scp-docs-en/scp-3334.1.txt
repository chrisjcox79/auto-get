Neural net diagram image was generated from a python script in the open-source machine learning library Caffe from the Berkeley Vision and Learning Center using the schematic of GoogLeNet (replicated by BVLC with help from Szegedy from a paper by Google and some universities), and then modified.

The adversarial images example with the schoolbus is actually credited in-article, but it’s from the Szegedy et al. paper “Intriguing Properties of Neural Networks” on arXiv under a Creative Commons license: https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.6199

Thanks to Leveritas on the forums, and 8ync, Jishwa, Uasciovuza, BenjaminStokes, DrMagnus, Rimple, Herlequine, Roget, and SleepyIsHollow (and FoldingPapers, sorry!) from chat for critiquing and giving feedback.

There's a new one after 6th? I totally skip the issue #'s (or was it volume? The ones in parens) and definitely don't do double spaces. Dirty little secret: as long as they're mostly correct and have the DoI #, unless the journal has their own guidelines, people mostly don't care. I'm not saying you should do them wrong, people deserve credit. But it happens.

Also noting that Kate McTiriss popularized the SCP journal citation deal, because that's what caused me to join the wiki. (Not to take away from the current author, these are very neat.)

To give some actual critique, I'm mostly with LordMetalton and CWW. The back and forth is really interesting on this (the poison image and variance versus bias discussion was neat, although I need to wrap my head around that in SDT terms) but I really don't understand how this is an anomaly. Bardeen got away with that exact issue because it gave more room to a very touching personal narrative, and even Bardeen was paratech. This doesn't have that. No vote.
