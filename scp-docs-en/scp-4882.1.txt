The last paragraph of the description is really awkwardly phrased. Too many unnecessary words and prhases in what is meant to be a concise description of the anomaly. Also, given that the BG is later referred to as simply SCP-001, it'd probably be better to refer to it as such in the description too. EDIT: Alternatively, since this directly ties into the 001 Proposal, a  event.

The addenda do an excelent job of showing just how serious the threat is, the best example being the number of incidents jumping from 39 to over a hundred in a little over a year, but it seems beyond improbable that the CotBG is aware of a simple way of rebuilding the Broken God yet there is no incident of a Maxwellist cell attempting it themselves, only indications of them introducing the tech into the industry. The Foundation would likely have to several overt attempts at bringing back the BG this way every year. I understand that would go against the spirit of this piece of only using real-life events, but the lack of any reference to such attempts is simply inconceivable. Even if said massive jumps in incidents over the last few years is meant to be Maxwellist attempts, it should be made explicit rather than simply inferred.

A couple of issues with the lore used:

The Cogwork Orthodoxy considers the use of electronic devices heresy, so it seems weird that their own scriptures would have them be beneficial to the return of the Broken God.
The term  situation). The Maxwellist refer to it as WAN and the Broken Church has no particular name.

I liked this article, but there's too many problems in it for me to ignore.

Thanks for the feedback!

Responses:

The last paragraph of the description is really awkwardly phrased

[Note on ]
附加记录：    

I disagree that the wording is awkward. The stuff about thaumaturgical models etc is needed to justify the connection to SCP-001, and the structure / word choice is there to support the climax at the end of the description.

That said, that paragraph is the last bit I wrote and the part I feel least confident about. I might rewrite it later. EDIT: This has been done  thanks Weryllium for the help :)

The Cogwork Orthodoxy considers the use of electronic devices heresy, so it seems weird that their own scriptures would have them be beneficial to the return of the Broken God.
附加记录：    

They don't care for electronics, but the four factors contributing to SCP-4882 apply just as well to mechanical clocks. The difference is that accurate electronic clocks are cheap and ubiquitous, which makes SCP-4882 much more likely to manifest through them.

附加记录：    

Note that the containment breaches we see involve rooms chock full of equipment, that have been running for months without interruption. Perhaps the Maxwellists are so fragmented and under-resourced that none of their attempts are worth talking about.
