To Put a Cat on Your Head

27 Apr 2020 00:24

Heya, Eze here with a breather article about cats and haikus. I based this on the idiom 'Neko wo kaburu', which means to feign a personality, disguising your true one. It's also a part of Japanese culture, particularly with honmae and tatemae.

Special thanks to The Great Hippo, JakdragonX, and Noodles for looking over this, revising and strengthening it, and telling me what parts I should look for.

Image credits:  by Kawabata Gyokusho. Public Domain Image

Hope you enjoy this 390-word skip!

27 Apr 2020 11:53

Cute idea and as long enough as it needs to be.

There are two things which I don't think worked well and stopped me from upvoting, though. I don't like the use of the cognitive resistance value here - it doesn't fit with what I think the CRV should be, which is basically worldbuilding fluff. At most, it only exists to be a sciency-sounding scale for  as applied to a document about the supernatural. A specific value (not less than, greater than, or a range) implies that it has some kind of inherent meaning, which gels badly with something which is much older than the Foundation and modern civilisation. I don't think an additional nod to the number 56 (the reference of which I didn't get, though that's not necessarily a fault of the SCP) is worth introducing this confusion.

I'm also wary of the phrase  comes up, and I think that could have used another line or two of fleshing out.
