06 Oct 2017 03:13

I think the clearest bit of telling being a problem is the description of the red room contents. You give a bunch of phenomena that aren't easy to visualize or coherent, then just tell us what they are using more terminology and phenomena that are difficult to visualize.

Unless the title is something really obvious (I rarely see titles), the only evidence I can see for this being a time traveler trap (which is a cool idea) is the hand-drawn diagram and the mention of the matter being from the future. I'm very curious about the origin and reason it's abandoned, but the only place I got any inkling was when you just told us what the O5s think.

Overall I guess the ratio of mundane detail/investigation to story building was weighted heavily toward the former. I could've lived not knowing, but you have way more than what would've been sufficient detail to hook me and confined the reveals to little designated exposition blobs instead of being integrated into the story, so it just feels like too little too late.

No vote.
