I think the biggest weakness of this is the actual writing style, than anything we usually deal with on the wiki. The description section's sentences are extremely choppy, like each one was written at a different time (other than the obvious nature of our linear existence, ya furious pedants.)

It ends up being hard to read, and while I enjoy the concept, from a writing perspective, this needs work.

Although lone SCP-3095-1 instances have been regularly encountered, SCP-3095-1 instances tend to form civilizations. These civilizations are often grouped by species. For example, an SCP-3095-1 civilization may consist of robins, with very few other specimens present. The populations of these civilizations vary wildly. It is known that a village consisting of 14 pelicans exists in a single village Southern Africa, while the largest known civilization had a population estimated in the millions across multiple large cities.
附加记录：    

This is a good example. Declarative statement. Declarative statement. Descriptive statement (good, adds to the flow.) Declarative statement. Declarative statement. Each of these declarations had nothing to do with each other and really detracted from the overall reading ease of the piece.

Downvote for writing quality, unfortunately.

Also, I'm not really sure why you wrote this as a raw website page, rather than using wikidot syntax. I didn't notice any particular need for the scripting, other than the delay in loading in, which I don't feel added much. The Double Collapsibles also weren't particularly necessary, especially for the effort this must have taken.
