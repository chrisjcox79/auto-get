Re:

05 Aug 2009 21:59

An attached medical report is a good idea. An autopsy report, with the coroner getting progressively more and more freaked out by the distortions, would be better, IMO.

I suggested the tracheotomy kit because it's creepy. If you're reading this SCP for the first time, and you see that the Special Containment Procedures have the following sentence, sitting by itself:

"An emergency tracheotomy kit must be kept in the medical unit adjacent to SCP-933's enclosure at all times."

You start to wonder why. And then when you read about what 933 does in the Description section, the implications hit home, and you get that sinking feeling in your guts. At least, I do.

As for the other stuff, it's true that the Foundation's primary objective is S-C-P-ing, but they aren't just throwing personnel away, either. A faceless staffer is useful for things that work around eyesight or communication, as the interview with Agent whoever suggests. And an emergency tracheotomy isn't a complicated operation. You just make a small incision in the trachea and jam in a tube, then seal it shut. The condition of the jaw and nose don't actually matter for the procedure to work.
