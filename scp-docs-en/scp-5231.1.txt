Fold

21 Feb 2020 15:45

Love it. +1 for sure.

It's a very solid concept - I love the idea of using the Amish community to obscure a technology-based anomaly, and the interview log has some great, tight dialogue. Easy and enjoyable.

My favorite part is the ending. The idea that we got these people to willingly help bury the object on their property with minimal fuss, then anesthetize them to forget it ever happened is the perfect balance of . Finding that balance is hard, so big ups Nico.

Here's my only gripe:  Using the Amish community to contain this object doesn't feel fully justified as-is. These communities aren't totally isolated - technology-using individuals could still pass within that  unsecured? Clever as heck, sure, but still not totally Foundation spec.

I feel like the actual anomaly itself needs a bit more oomph, or a lot less oomph. Either it's so benign that burying it is the most cost vs. threat effective option, or it's so dangerous that moving it poses far, far too much risk. As it is right now, some kinda' prototype Broken God recruitment beacon? Not quite enough.
