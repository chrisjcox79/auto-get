This SCP has a really strong 'Cold War' vibe to it that I can't put my finger on; also, the idea of an anomaly that centers on a murder mystery that must not be solved is very cool.

I like this as it is, although I was also hoping for something in the fragmented records that indicated multiple narratives that are all simultaneously true (IE, O5-9 was killed by several people, and several methods, at several different times), with each O5 member being made aware of a different murder, and responsible for ensuring the evidence for this particular murder remains contained/does not interact with any evidence from the other variants (else we cause the emergence of destructive causal contradictions).

That being said, this took a much more low-key approach to the idea  one which I think also works really well.

+1

Here's my headcanon:

+Show

-Hide

 ties up a lot of things except for all of the wonkiness that happened that day (The sight of a daylight comment, the undecipherable language bit, etc.) and I just think it's too clean and too mundane to say all of the evidence was fabricated by a rival O5, or even that an O5 used anomalous means to create the wonkiness as red herrings.

My headcanon is that TRAILHEAD did work, but not in the intended way. The specific language is that  I think both are simultaneously true statements.

Maybe it was a mistake, and O5-9 didn't realize the second effect. Maybe he did, and he was attempting quantum immortality by ensuring two utterly incompatible  future deaths. Either way, he was so sure that his little plan worked, he let his guard down. He had fewer security guards, fewer countermeasures, and no other anomalous protections. A would be-assassin actually made it into his dining room. But just as the assassin is about to pull the trigger, the universe goes wonky. Time speeds up. Local broadcast stations start playing messages from dozens of years in the future, in a yet-to-be-created language. Halley's Comet, not scheduled to arrive for another 75 years, appears in the sky. Individuals vanish and others skip ahead weeks or months into the future.

O5-9 is aged a lifetime in a few moments just as his assassin pulls the trigger. O5-9 dies of natural coronary failure, just as the bullets strike him. Both deaths happen simultaneously.
