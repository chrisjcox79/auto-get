Fold

05 Jan 2012 23:18

When you get right down to it, the concept behind this isn't terribly original: it's a statue that demands a terrible sacrifice to an ancient god, and makes Bad Things happen if it doesn't get it, to the point of having caused several historical catastrophes. We've got a lot of things that are like it in one way or another.

But it's not the concept that made me go ever more bug-eyed as I read this entry. The execution here is exquisite. The gradual, piecewise revelation of this thing's horror kept me reading and kept me trembling on the edge of my seat. It is awful in all the ways an SCP should be awful. This, right here, is a textbook example of why the execution matters.

+1, sir. I would upvote this repeatedly if I could.
