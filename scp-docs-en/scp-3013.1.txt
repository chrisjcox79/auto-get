Fold

15 May 2017 15:47

The idea was that the new doctor, with his fame stemming from successful terminations, was much less experimentally-minded than Dr. F, and would seek to find the most direct way to permanently contain it- hence a picture about death with a very pure visual style, that would lack complications and simply subdue 3013 easily. I envisioned the new doctor as being brusque, arrogant and a little thoughtless, essentially.

I agree, though, that isn't very clear in the text- it was so set in my head that I neglected to elaborate on it in the text. I'll try to think of a way to demonstrate that better.

17 May 2017 15:56

I understand the intended mechanism, but I'd suggest that grief, or even emotional disturbance, doesn't inevitably lead to violence. I think that a more realistic exploration of reactions to grief, even misplaced grief, would make for a much better story.

Although you'll need to work a little on smoothing down the rough edges of the explanation for the anomaly. Presumably the frame didn't create an instance of Elijah that was sad, so why does it have that effect on other pictures? The whispered explanation from the mysterious stranger seems too forced and convenient, blatantly tying together the frame's original intended effect with its current anomaly in a way that is clearly for the service of plot mechanics, rather than having a sensible in-universe explanation.

Fold

14 Dec 2020 02:34

Elijah appeared (as a -1 instance). Since the frame was made for him, this instance was stable.

She had her husband back and lived with him for decades.

This particular instance existed all the way from the 30s and beyond her passing, so this  made of grief was the haunt of the mansion.

It ceased existing when the painting was stolen, prompting the -2 event that destroyed the mansion and resulted in the frame's apprehension.

No anomalous effect made her forget anything, just the sheer shock, followed by happiness.
