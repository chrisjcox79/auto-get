After some internal deliberation, I decided that I couldn't let the Infinite Speed Computer die without a fight. So, I have massively rewritten it from scratch, and attributed its creation to Prometheus Labs.

Long live the oldies!

Edit: I pulled up the archives in an attempt to give proper credit to the original author of SCP-155, only to find that it was posted on July 28, 2008 by Anonymous back when the wiki was an open wiki. The world may never know.

It was me, actually. I was active on the old website and wrote several, including Infinite Speed Computer, Ash Bacteria, and SCP-974 (Deadly Image) . This is the only one to live, so thanks for the rewrite.

The latter was a very rudimentary cognitohazard - cognitohazards weren't as cliche back then, but still I'm not surprised it was deleted. To my excuse, I was 14 at the time. There's a twist to the story though. When I was thinking of countermeasures against cognitohazards, I invented the idea of Class A and Class C amnesiacs, which has since become a key part of the canon, though now they're more usually called 'amnestics'. It's very curious how some crappy article I wrote when I was 14 contributed a term which is now famous, and it makes me quite proud.

Now, after nearly a decade, I'm back to the wiki to write some more things.
