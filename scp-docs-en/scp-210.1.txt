You know. I like this one. I like it quite a bit.

Now, a few spelling/grammatical errors which I'll fix after posting, but it's a nice, simple SCP. I think, at least.

A couple of other bits I would like to bring up, but not fix:

1 The second paragraph should be switched with the first one. It would read better, if you know what it is, and THEN how it was found.

2 Are you going to write Document 210-01? If so, you should have before posting.

3 Can the fluid be removed? I'm thinking not. It just disappears if removed from the building?

4 How much of one's body must be covered before you're lost? Half? Submersion? Any bit? If anything short of submersion, are you drawn into the house? (I'm thinking total submersion/entrance to the building is what's happening)

I dunno. The article as a whole just feels really bare to me, but it's hard to put my finger on why. It felt like it was building up to something, with all these neat little details, but then very few of them are expanded upon any.

*rereads it*

On the other hand, though, the article itself is very straightforward and to-the-point. Adding to it would prolly clutter it. A side-page or two might be interesting, I suppose. Maybe a small-ish experiment log testing the various properties of SCP-210-01 (e.g., can you, say, dip a cup or a bucket in there and lift out some of it? That is, even though it doesn't spill out the windows/doors, can it be "lifted" out? If so, would any of it "lifted" out from the side then spill out in accordance to normal gravity? Does it retain its other properties? If it can be removed, what would happen if you tried to empty it one bucket at a time? What happens if you put a fish or other aquatic animal in there?), or a change log excerpt from the rover.

I had to reword this line in the description: "The substance possesses a refractory index nearly identical to water which has been dubbed SCP-210-01." Written like that, it looks like you've designated water as SCP-210-01. Now it's much less ambiguous but keeps the same general flow (I think, anyway).

Also, the sentence "SCP-210-02 will not take damage" really bugs me. It sounds like it came from a DD rulebook instead of an SCP article. I suggest changing that to "Instances of SCP-210-02 cannot be damaged" or somesuch.

All that said, I rather like this one, especially the picture, and I don't think it needs anything more. Graceful, almost delicate, but still plenty dangerous to the unaware.
