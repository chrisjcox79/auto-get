14 Oct 2019 16:28

Consider me well and spooked. +1

The link to 4758 is interesting; while this one isn't quite fully parse-able without having read it, it still works; it's extremely unnerving and jarring and only increases in horror after you have the context.

Some very minor things:

+block

–block

It all happened so fast, I felt something primal awaken inside of me.
附加记录：    

I saw the look of desperation in his eyes, he was praying to every god he knew for me to put him out of his misery.
附加记录：    

Take this with a grain of salt because it's malleable in creative work like this, especially in personal letter-writing, but: in the above examples, you have some comma splices where a separate sentence or semicolon would be correct. It makes the pacing a little weird, at least to someone over-attuned to grammar like I am.

I took him and wrapped him in what was left of his mother's dress. I took him to the ritual site, I had to prepare him before the ritual can begin.
附加记录：    

This is technically grammatically correct, but I'm not sure the tense it's in now is intentional; either way the verb switching in the last sentence is jarring (plus the comma splice). Consider: 

Non-grammatical:  I have mixed feelings about the frequency of INFOHAZARD REDACTED, not so much because I don't buy the explanation you gave to aismallard above but because I'm not quite sure their volume here corresponds with the context I can read. Like, in entry 7, the six line-broken repetitions between  are weird to me because I'm not sure it's reasonable that there would be seven paragraphs' worth of information there. The gap maybe isn't big enough to justify the blankness, if that makes sense.

But, on the other hand, the density of them right there does very much nail an alarm-bell  into the reader's brain. If you're confident and comfortable that they're a reasonable-enough representation of how much would be there absent the infohazard, I wouldn't change it. (and, also: ew, seven paragraphs of that scene)

multiple autopsies have shown that SCP-4756 only has two fully functioning organs, the heart and the brain, other organs are either non-functioning, heavily damaged, or missing.
附加记录：    

Grammar issue here due to two full phrases being separated by a comma. I understand why this is desired to be done, since the sentence is also connected to a semicolon. A period is still okay even if it 'feels' awkward. I would rather these related full phrases be sentences in their own paragraph if the author really wants to cement their cohesion as an idea.

–block
