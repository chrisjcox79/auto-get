Lots to love here. First sentence of the SCProcedures are a decent hook, and the anomaly itself is well constructed  there's just enough detail to provide the setup, and a good mental image of the area/subject, but it moves at a brisk pace.

Similarly, I enjoyed the brevity of the interviews  each gives us only enough to make implicit the thing's methods and goals, and it's cryptic without coming off as if it is trying to be.

The only real downside to this for me is how clueless the Foundation comes off as at the end. I feel that there's far too many blatant clues pointing towards escape for them to simply neglect and ponder labelling it Neutralized. To this, I would suggest either allowing them to make the insanely obvious connections, or otherwise remove the face-smackingly loud tell of bloodied footprints in lieu of something less Goosebumbs-level spoopy as a hint that it got out.

One nitpick:

SCP-4116 has an antimemetic effect affecting the recollection of dreams, with all victims only being able to recall the final dream in the sequence.

Not being able to remember dreams, specifically night terrors, isn't anomalous. When we're asleep we're always dreaming, we can only remember dreams during REM sleep. That aside, this is great. Hard +1!
