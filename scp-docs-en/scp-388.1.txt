Squaring a velocity is meaningless

13 May 2009 01:30

There's a bit of a problem, though. You can't "square" something's velocity, it's nonsensical. For example, if it's moving at 2 meters per second, you might expect it to "square" to 4 meters per second. But if it's moving at 2 meters per second it's also moving at 7.2 kilometers per hour. So it would "square" to 51.84 kilometers per hour, which is 14.4 meters per second. The velocity changes completely depending on which units you arbitrarily use. If you pick units where the velocity is less than 1 squaring it actually reduces the velocity.

There are a few SCPs out there where this sort of thing is actually addressed, such as SCP-233, but that sort of weirdness should be explicitly mentioned.

Re: Squaring a velocity is meaningless

02 Oct 2009 19:41

It's not "for the lulz," it's to make the curve of the data match a squaring of initial velocity by picking appropriate units. As BeeDee said before, squaring a velocity is meaningless without units. I find it unlikely that a random SCP would be so kind as to use units for its initial to final velocity that were identical to human units, but if they match a curve that's identical to a squaring of velocity if not for the unit problem, I imagine mathematically inclined scientists at the SCP Foundation (read: most of them, if not all) would find a way to make the curve match.

That's all.

If it's too much trouble, by all means, ignore it.
