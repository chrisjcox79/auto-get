I feel like this moves very quickly. Not that it's much of a detriment, but I think there could have been a small stop towards the end to give the reader some time to breathe in what had just happened and then following in with the ending note. Short, punchy notes leading up to the incident is fine, because the incident is a high point of the article. It's shocking and brutal and messy and all the stuff we can't help but keep watching.

It's hard to say whether it would have been a benefit to have a little bit more buffer before getting to the gruesome bits, a little bit more time to understand the fear expressed by 5265, but this hits the right boxes for a good short article in my book.

I’m a no vote for now because there’s a couple things I’m not getting.

Minor thing: The wording of describing the arm in what it’s clothed in seems weird; as read, it says that this arm is wearing a uniform. Clarification that this is the sleeve portion of the uniform seems necessary to me.

Major thing: The sleeve has a pocket that it pulled a bolt action rifle out of? I’m guessing this has a “hammerspace” element to it, but between “rifle coming out of pocket,” “sleeve having a pocket in the first place,” and “tiny sleeve pocket having rifle,” there were too many leaps that broke my believability.
