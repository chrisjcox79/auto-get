The problem is the sheer timescale involved - the average dog life span is around 10 years, give or take a few, but mostly give. It would likely leave the people with very inconsistent memories and a good deal of cognitive dissonance - I mean, think of petting your dog and like, not being able to escape the pervasive thought he used to be bigger and somewhat paler in colour.

That said, this just about works as a misguided effort on part of the site director and actually makes it creepier. Not the least, my parents apparently did this to me once with a hampster when I was about 5 or so - quietly replaced the one that had died after he's escaped. I noticed a number of differences, mainly that his slightly lame leg had healed, but with a bit of nudging from them I ascribed it to the grasshoppers I've been feeding him that summer having such a positive effect.
