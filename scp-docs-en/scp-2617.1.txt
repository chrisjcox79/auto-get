Thanks, Attribution, Backstory and Other Matters

07 Apr 2015 02:07

To start off, let me express thank you to all who had the pleasure (or displeasure) of reviewing this article in its various phases as a draft.

As for the pictures involved:

First picture (CC0) taken from Wikipedia (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_masts_and_towers)
Second picture taken from 4kdordy under Creative Commons; changes made: Black and White. (https://www.flickr.com/photos/8593364@N06/2724376850/)

This article involves the following other article:

SCP-516 (Intelligent Tank) by ModernMajorGeneral

The Foundation precursor  is credited to Agent MacLeod (mentioned in SCP-2237, which he plans to rewrite), although I took my personal liberties while writing this precursor.

+block

–block

So what actually happened?

The entity can only be summoned by an  him, they incapacitate him and finished up on the machine.

P.S. They can't bring a real Romanov. That would cause quite a scandal (and purging).

So why was the place abandoned?

I would say, it was ultimately outdated. It could only be used against foreign armies within the USSR, and is useless for internal policing. Furthermore, I would imagine political implications over using an entity that vows allegiance to the Tsar.

But what happened to the Seers? Can the Tsar use the skip on his own?

The Tsar is necessary (or rather, his ), but the Seers know the mechanics behind it. From my headcanon, the Seers declined since the Napoleonic War with many members in exile or arrested by Russian authorities (they would be regarded lowly by the nobility). By WWI, the Seers were virtually no more save a few individuals who knew bits of the lore.
