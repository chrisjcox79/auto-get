I already expressed my thoughts on this, but for clarity:

I dislike this largely due to the gimmick that you're using to convey the story of these two Foundations communicating with each other. The references are aggravating to read, and it makes it hard to keep reading. The tone and character of the other Foundation seems very snarky (in a bad way) and is hard to want to care for. There's also the question of this playing out in a document, when why not use a test page for that? It makes the whole thing seem unprofessional.

The anomaly itself, while interesting, doesn't feel like it plays out this well with all of the above. There's story potential here, but the article is frustrating to work with and read to get any sense of involvement in it at all.

The problem is getting worse, potentially allowing multiple other universes/timelines access to all stored data, and the response is essentially . In the SCPverse, they beat the odds that the first place they were linked to didn't immediately start trying to murder (or worse) everyone. Odds should be that whoever else is getting a look in at their computer systems has some malevolent intent.

What I meant by that was that, with this being the SCPverse, if something bad could happen, it probably will. So they got lucky once, in that the first universe they ended up being connected to is relatively benign. Any further connections, which the addendum tells us is already starting to happen, should probably end poorly.

The Foundation is one of the the most powerful, if not THE most powerful, organizations on the planet. They have access to capabilities that no one else could dream of. If the Foundation wanted to move between realities, I'm almost certain they could. If the timelines are very similar, the same must be true for the other Foundation. An ally can easily become an enemy, and they have access to all your computer systems. Maybe I am overreacting, but surely that is something that would be taken into account here. The Foundation have back up plans for their back up plans.

I enjoy the idea of a gimmick, but you don't do much with it here besides merely use it.

This is one of our many articles recently that expects to survive merely by showing up and being well-written. It doesn't do much that's interesting; it's nothing I haven't seen before plenty of times. It has a little bit of fun, but not really very much.

But, indeed, it's in the positives right now, and may well survive, and the comment above cites  not great for quality standards, since being well-written with good tone should be the minimum standard for existing on the site, but what can ya do.

A nitpick unrelated to quality: It's kind of weird to note the existence of the alternate universe being treated as such a big deal.

Another nitpick unrelated to quality: I don't like the use of Rosen, aka the character whose concept is a meta-joke that he's utter shit at his job (and indeed that seems to be how he's used here), but I think that's mainly personal.
