SCP-4547: Exterminans

12 Sep 2019 14:07

Notes About this SCP: 

Let's get this out of the way: the taronyu look like githzerai. There are no CC-compliant images of a githzerai (and even if there were, they wouldn't be realistic to include), but I imagine that the taronyu look like githzerai from DD. They're obviously non-human, but not so alien as to be completely foreign to humanity.
All of the taronyu words (in Vi'na) are actually the Na'vi conlang from Avatar, so you can look all of them up for definitions, if you want. Additionally, if you want to write something with the taronyu, use Na'vi for their words.
This SCP has been a long-time coming. It was originally planned to be a Straight on Till Morning SCP, and then many months after initially being abandoned brought back for Ad Astra Per Aspera. It's not terribly different from the original version  I didn't have much back then.
Fetch.aic is the final product of Project: LUMINAL, from MaliceAF's excellent fetch, as there is both a living and an undead copy of the AI. It's the onboard AIC for the ship, and handles any tasks for them. (It's primarily for superluminal travel, but can do other stuff as needed.)
The Martians mentioned in the Census report are an element of AAPA that will be established in a yet to be written SCP. They were brought back during a temporal based terraforming of Mars, that reverted it to a previous state.
I helped remake the AAPA theme just so this could use it.

+re:Apollyon

-Apollyon

So, this is an Apollyon. I have a feeling that people might get a little touchy about that, and want to address some clarifications as to why this is Apollyon:

For starters, from an in-universe perspective, this SCP fits just about every definition of Apollyon I've ever seen. It's worth noting that the SCP, in-universe, was written by taronyu in part. (How else would they know so much about what happened, in such good detail?) Any anthropocentric definition of Apollyon is therefore discarded.

Apollyon means uncontainable:  There is no way to contain any of the sub-anomalies on this planet, other than just leaving them alone and running away. You can't put all the zombies back into a box, you can't fix the weather, you can't stop 3125. Furthermore, you can't conceal the anomaly, since the taronyu are public knowledge.
Apollyon means world-ender:  This did end the world - several times over, in fact. It just didn't happen to Earth. In any case, a lot of the anomalies here could potentially end the world again, and do so rather easily too.
Apollyon means top-secret:  I've seen at least one user voice the opinion that Apollyon represents stuff the Foundation keeps secret from itself, which is what they're doing here. They can't reveal their involvement in rescuing the taronyu, so this is secret.
Apollyon is the name of the Scarlet King:  This is a bizarre definition I once saw on 2317 saying the class was specific to the Devourer, as it was it's name. Well, look at SCP-7777-B4-3, a Scarlet Heir. Boom.

Anyway, the out-of-universe reason for the Object Class is to sell the plot twist that comes in the second collapsible. Everything up to the point when the ITF discovers the living taronyu is meant to convey the idea that the taronyu are dead, and this is an utterly ruined world. I feel that Apollyon is crucial to selling that thought, much more than any other class would.

And finally, I just personally can't see it as anything else. Apollyon just fits it too well.
