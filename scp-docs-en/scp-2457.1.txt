Thanks, Attribution and Other Matters

13 Apr 2016 01:13

This has been sitting in my sandbox for a while. Originally, I wanted it to be for Mother's Day but the theme deviated from the Mother's Day message as I wrote and edit it with feedback.

So yes, I thank the following individuals among others: Hercules Rockefeller, minmin, WrongJohnSilver, TL333s, ignorant_genius among others. The title for this work (母を見たい)1 is credited to a RL associate of mine, who kindly aided me in the Japanese.

Image credits are as follows:

Image 1 by Bobjgalindo of Wikimedia (https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid=5654847) under GFDL
Image 2 by Nisreen M Khalifa et al of Wikimedia (https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Fetus_in_fetu_radiograph.jpg) under CC 2.0

+block

–block

SCP-2457-A and the mass of tissues are whatever's left of PoI-2457-01's unborn twin a la fetus in fetu. Either the unborn twin manifested its anomalous potential or the anomalous capacity of PoI-2457-01 that held SCP-2457-A back is losing effectiveness.

But from PoI-2457-01's POV, he rationalised the whole ordeal as a religious experience - using terms and concepts known to him as a Shinto priest.

Reading this article reminded me of another anomaly, in that it appeared to be an inverse of the other: while the babies in 2457 are split apart by seven, the humans in 2478 are multiplied by seven.

As such, editing:

SCP-2457-A will remain unburnt during the combustion of the host, but it will inevitably expire following the host's expiration due to interruption of gestation. In addition, following the host's expiration, SCP-2457-A's carcass is split into seven portions: head, left and right arms, left and right legs, upper and lower torso (cut off at waistline). Cause is unknown, although wounds on SCP-2457 suggest the application of a bladed object on the carcass.
