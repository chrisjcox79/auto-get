Ok, so I thought of three comparisons to try to figure out what is going on here, as far as what an observer would see happening in the various floors. Having read the modified article, comparison one is obsolete, but I'll include it nonetheless to illustrate my thought process.

Comparison One: Groundhog Day - A single day/time period endlessly loops with one or more people able to influence events through their actions during said time period, only to see them reset at the end of the loop period. But most people are unaware of the loop, and so act out the exact same actions repeatedly unless interfered with.

Similar examples: Edge of Tomorrow, Run Lola Run, a billion others (obsolete as of revision)

Comparison 2: Pleasantville -  passes in normal cycles (i.e. each day is different from the next, weekends follow work weeks, etc.) at the same speed for all observers but nothing ever really changes. No one ages, relocates, or changes occupation, and social, economic, political, and technological standards never change.

Similar examples: Dante's Inferno, What Dreams May Come, Silent Hill (movie)

Comparison 3: The Dark Tower -  party). Time may stretch for one person and/or compress for another, so that the second appears to age rapidly to the first, and the first appears to stop aging to the second.

Similar examples: Limbo in Inception, others I can't think of right now

Anyhow, all that is to say: Which of the two remaining comparisons fits better (if either)?
