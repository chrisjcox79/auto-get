So what follows in this collapsible was originally intended to be Addendum 1, however based on feedback I received during the writing process we determined that most people wouldn't find the following information interesting. Thus I decided to exclude it from the Article proper. However, when I originally posted the article, I had a footnote mentioning the addendum attached to a comment here on the discussion page. Based on feedback, this was fairly immersion breaking and I've ultimately decided to exclude the addendum from the article proper. Instead, I will leave the collapsible here for anyone interested on what my projected structural differences between the prion and the ATP-synthase would be. Thanks for reading.

Levelalterations.

–block

SCP-2431 is a misfolded variant of ATP synthase, as mentioned previously, however; the protein also possesses several functional peptide chains not found on correctly folded ATP synthases. In addition to these chains, misfolding of normal ATP synthase enhances and/or alters several normal functions of the protein, in addition to several anomalous functions. Following are a list of the components, and their modified functions.

FO1 Complex:

A and B subunits are refolded, hydrophobic amino acids are shifted inwards, but leave the H+ channel in tact. 3 Peptide chains are attached to both A and B subunits via disulfide bonds. 2 of the peptide chains contain 4 enzymatic active sites similar in structure and function to Chaperone proteins found in the Rough ER. The other peptide chain has 8 active sites of unknown function. Active sites use ATP as a cofactor.
Flagella-like peptide chains become attached to C subunits via disulfide bonds, rotation of C subunits enhanced by new folding of A and B subunits allows H+ ions to pass through the channel more quickly. When submersed in liquids, rotation of C causes directed propulsion.

F12 subunit:

Normal Alpha and Beta subunits refolded, 3 additional verisions of each become associated with the structure. Refolding creates a molecular drill at the tip around the hydrogen ion channel. Additional active sites for ATP production enhance activity of FO components.
Gamma stalk subunit responsible for rotation of Alpha and Beta subunits refolded and modified. Modification of C subunits in FO allows faster ATP production, refolding of Gamma subunit allows faster rotation, and an additional component which allows it to change the confirmation of two active sites at once.

So here is my third SCP folks. A lot of thanks to Aiden, Niles, Weryllium, Shaggydredlocks, Joreth, and BlackFluid for their feedback and idea storming. This one started off as a Joke and evolved into a very sad narrative. I hope you guys like it.

1. The portion of ATP Synthase normally embedded in the mitochondrial membrane
2. Portion of ATP Synthase which is responsbile for the binding of ADP and inorganic phosphate, and through the activity of the hydrogen ion channel, induces the production of ATP.

This doesn't do justice to what is an interesting core idea (protein folding = origami, what would a protein do if it could wish?).

The pseudo-science doesn't make sense. If only one misfolded protein is produced every 2 weeks, how would this ever get to 5g in mass, let alone 78g? Based on my rough calculations (happy to be proven wrong) it would take far longer than the total lifespan of the universe.

The choice to put Addendum 1 (which is not consistently capitalised) in the discussion page is odd. It breaks immersion (especially as the Discussion page post in question starts with an out-of-universe author note) and doesn't appear particularly necessary. Do you need Addendum 1 at all?

You spend a footnote justifying how the proteins can see presented items, without the Foundation seeming to be particularly interested in how these proteins are significantly smarter than domestic animals. The jump to recognising and responding to emotional indicators was particularly big. Also, how does the protein know which items are  - ie why is it not always folding to imitate the shape of petri dishes, or substrate molecules?

The body horror of the last three paragraphs of the Description seems completely out of place tonally, never pays off and requires a host of new  for the proteins, like drilling out of cells. Why don't the proteins recognise the emotional reactions of people they're mutilating?

Perhaps it's just me, but I didn't understand the significance of Mr Adachi at all. The medical status updates seem particularly odd - did the proteins use their wish to ask for Strep. pneumoniae as playmates?

I think this would be significantly improved by going back to the drawing board and concentrating on things that better tell the story of what a sentient protein might do, if it existed.

hidecriticism

The primary thing that you both address is the connection between the SCP and Mr. Adachi. Mr Adachi is physically linked to the prions spatially in a similar way to what was going on with 2378, except as you've pointed out 2431 is sentient/weakly sapient, and when shown Mr. Adachi in his current deteriorating state in addition to a portion of his children's book referring to Japanese Mythology, the prions begin to make cranes in an attempt to appeal to the god of longevity so that they might save their inadvertent creator. I attempted to drop significant narrative hints throughout the piece, but obviously some of those have been missed and the hints that were picked up on weren't enough to connect the whole story. I will need to go back to my sandbox and make the connections more solid.

Addendum 1 is primarily there as an explanation for those actually interested in the molecular and biochemical aspects of the prion. This issue is easily fixed, and I can clarify in article and arrange the comment so that it is not as immersion breaking.

The Pseudo Science not making sense is a result of poor wording on my part, Each new protein also spontaneously doubles every 2 weeks, I will fix that in the actual article and my sandbox.

Both you and Petrograd pointed out the emotional indicators, and I can easily rectify this in the experiment section, and also redress your concern about the Foundation recognizing it as being smarter than most domestic animals involving testing for further emotional recognition and quantification.

In regards to your last point Psul, the purpose of the last addenda is primarily to show the failure of the prions attempts to cure Mr. Adachi of Creutzfeld-Jakobs disease and his slow spiral downwards as his physical and mental health continue to degrade. Like most Neurodegenerative disorders and Prion diseases, CJD results in weakened lung function over time. This leaves persons susceptible to pneumonia and other respiratory infections. I primarly left the end result vague to let the reader draw an interpretation of what is going on, however in light of your feed back I may need to make the link more direct.

To summarise, Psul and Petrograd I will make some major edits in my sandbox and transfer them back over here. Hopefully these edits will make the narrative more clear, and alleviate some of the issues you are experiencing while reading the article.
