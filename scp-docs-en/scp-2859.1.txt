I have just a few questions.

1.) How does one view the habits and mating of an 80,000 KM long entity? At the range required to be able to even see all of it, it would be far enough away you would using telescopes. That would be twice as long as this planet is AROUND.

2.) How does a creature that is 80,000 KM in length  closes with it inside?

I don't mean these questions to be rude or anything. In fact, if they could be even addressed to some extent regarding the size of the creature, this could be a really great skip. 27 Feb 2017 22:35

This is very reminiscent of that one scip that's some kind of leviathan that transports people submerged in water to a vast ocean where it devours them. It's effectively the same base concept, except set in space. It seems way too overpowered here, though. Considering the fact that  is pretty vague terminology, this thing could manifest anywhere, making it impossible to contain and probably very quickly leading to a broken masquerade as a result. As a consequence, it also breaks my suspension of disbelief.

Speaking of the random manifestations, it seems as if the Foundation repeatedly encounters this anomaly in the most conspicuous of locations, raising questions that, remaining unanswered throughout the article, suggest that they were chosen purely in service to the plot. What are the chances that the Foundation first encounters the scip within one of its own facilities? What's the likelihood of a Foundation space mission finding the anomaly at just the right moment in just the right part of space? Unless I'm missing something, it just seems way too contrived.

Another key problem here is that the addenda just seem like a mishmash of different story elements included without any particular rhyme or reason. I mean, why did the one -1 instance morph after eating another SCP? What's the significance of this? How does this relate (if at all) to the apparent cult that has sprung up around the scip? Narrative-wise, there's no cohesion between these individual components, and they all fall flat as a result.
