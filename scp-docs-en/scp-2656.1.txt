Perhaps more clarification is in order in the document, but I'll just explain outright for now and consider adding a line or two of clarification later.

The switch to German is from where the audio got cut off. Since German and English don't have the same structure, cutting off half a sentence like that makes it difficult to translate what it said directly. Words can mean different things depending on what comes before and after them.

I chose to do it this way with a direct transcript followed by a literal translation to show the exact wording of the fragments followed by what those fragments could possibly translate to. Showing only the fragments would be inconvenient since it would have to be translated every time anyone wanted to know what it said, and showing only the literal translations would make it harder for readers to try and ponder what could have been said (mostly in-universe researchers I suppose, but real users too if anyone can be bothered), since they wouldn't have what was actually  said and only a possibly incorrect translation.

Maybe my logic needs a foreword or footnote to make that more clear.

For what it's worth, I do think that if the Foundation as a fictional entity is meant to be evocative of actual science and engineering, it's probably useful for all to realize that the choice of units used on the page would come from the mix of organizational culture and codified standards, with a fair amount of human arbitrariness. What the researcher thinks is the most useful unit does matter. And that verisimilitude gap means it's probably okay to use whatever unit is most convenient, with caution against getting too far away from SI. After all the Foundation is partially bureaucratic horror.
