I reflexively went  towards the end.

+[Critique]Access

-[ClickText]

Before I get too far, you refer to  in the incident description. Probably unintentional.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

This is a very creative premise and I feel as if it does compulsion in a very sneaky way. Each thing trapped in the fourth object is always something that the person needs at the time, so it feels almost natural for the person to destroy whatever is in the way of them getting to it. As soon as we get to Sinclair in the Manifestation log, I started feeling the dread.

I feel like it would have been useful at this point to mention that Sinclair's residence is under surveillance, since it seems kind of awkward for there to be a log set in someone's private home. I can make that stretch make sense if I believe that Site Director's are monitored due to the importance of their position, so it didn't totally take me out of it. And the way the incident escalates kept me locked into place. The visceral detail and the raw desperation coming off of Sinclair is borderline madness, and to have it cap off the way that it did threw me across the room. I'll admit that I don't know the significance of it, but the reaction it got out of me alone was enough to push me into upvote territory.

TL;DR I +1'd because the incident log felt like a short film horror movie. I paid for the whole seat, but I really only used the edge.

SoullessSingularity, for any questions or concerns relating to this review.
