Figured it out (spoilers)

09 Aug 2017 08:16

I found out who exactly the coin was intended to commemorate. According to the press release, the coin was to begin production on December 5, 2001, and was intended to commemorate the 100 year anniversary of a famous figure's birth. Based on the number of blacked-out letters in the figure's name within the press release, and on me looking at a list of people born on December 5th (and specifically the year 1901), I figured out that it was none other than

+warning!

-spoilers.

Walt Disney.

+ALERT!

–block

WASHINGTON — On December 5, 2001, the United States Mint will celebrate the 100th birthday of Walt Disney with the release of the American Imagination Commemorative Silver Dollar (product code 01CE). For generations, Disney's work has captured the imaginations of Americans young and old, and we honor his memory with this coin's appropriately whimsical denomination of $i — the basis of imaginary numbers.

The obverse side of the American Imagination Commemorative Silver Dollar features a portrait of Walt Disney beside the iconic Mickey Mouse logo. Inscriptions include include 

The reverse side features the Sleeping Beauty castle, as seen in the Walt Disney Pictures logo. Inscriptions include 

Please note that this coin has no real monetary value.
