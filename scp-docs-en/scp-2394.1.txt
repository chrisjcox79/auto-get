Like I said in chat, this is a huge improvement over the old one. Where the old one was emotionally manipulative (and not too good at it), this one is actually emotionally moving. It stretches disbelief like a rubber band in a lot of ways (I'm sure other readers will know what I'm talking about), and it still has facets of its emotionally-manipulative past form, but this is still a step forward. I guess I just don't have it in me to care about the plight of a teleporting cyber-frog. This is more developed than the last version, so it has that going for it in a technical sense, but this runs afoul of the  fallacy that's common in articles these days. I don't find the base concept interesting, and embroidering that base concept further doesn't help that initial problem.

There's also a lot of contrivances here that don't make a whole lot of sense in-universe. I don't see what possible reason the Foundation would have to design software just to be able to monitor a frog's dreams. Also, I'm not really sure how much neurological function an animal like a frog is capable of. I am sure that it's not going to be idealizing stuff in a manner easily relateable to humans.

Lastly, you're running up against the fact that you're relying on animal cruelty to sell the article, but what you have here pales in comparison to real-world examples of such. A single barely-sentient animal that was made to do tricks and now hurts a lot doesn't really affect me compared to what one sees when they walk into a modern factory slaughterhouse. And I still feel that the GoI reference is shoehorned. Overall, can't support this one.

13 Feb 2016 09:32

I really like this idea; I like the idea of trying to control a tree frog with a valuable anomalous ability, and the way it's done (a Firewire port!) seems realistic enough (even if the precise details are a bit iffy). The Insurgency aspect works for me.

That said, it's not interesting enough for me to upvote. It feels like it would be a fun side aspect of a bigger story, but it doesn't stand alone.

There's also way too much extra detail here. This could be cut by a third and would be better. Some of the details of the anomaly itself seem fairly pointless from a story perspective: to pick the most obvious example for me, why is it sapient? To try to make the reader care more? This in and of itself introduces a bunch of weird extra details. Etc.

An aside:
