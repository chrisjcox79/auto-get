I do like the concept of anomalous celebrity hype, but I'm novoting for the reason that despite its strange nature, before the test, SCP-4613 wouldn't have been identifiable as an anomaly. What you describe is out of place, but not nearly impossible human behavior, and while they find out later that it is in fact more than it first seems, I'm not convinced that the Foundation would even try doing tests on this unless there was more to the base anomaly.

I briefly considered this, but then I thought about how it has actual effects on the memory of attendees (at least inasmuch as they leave before the event concludes) and that social media data would show these  occurring far more often for one individual than anyone else.

That's more than enough to pique Foundation interest and get them to investigate more, and once they do it's not a huge leap to notice that people are having their memories  affected.

It was a concern of mine going in, and I very much appreciate the feedback, but I don't know that it's as serious a problem as you think.
