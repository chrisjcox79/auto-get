10 Jul 2017 06:49

The level 4 chat logs are where it's revealed that once the computer they're operating on breaks down, they will quite possibly left in eternal isolation. Even from each other. That's dark as fuck. It's way darker than anything previously revealed in the SCP. I mean, crap, even SCP-3001 at least had that red light! They won't even get that.

On top of that, grrgirl explicitly states that this isn't going to redeem them. The people they killed are going to stay dead. This isn't about redemption; this is about a group of teenagers staring at all this horror, all this darkness and rather than just giving up, finding a way to make peace with it.

I'm tired of writing SCPs where everything is awful for everyone forever. It's not good for my brain.

ETA:  I mean, it's perfectly fine if this isn't your thing  but nobody's getting 'redeemed' here. All that's happening is a bunch of terrified kids are figuring out a way to be a little less scared.

Thanks! I thought for a while about whether or not they're Thaumiel, and I finally settled on no for two reasons:

It makes the ending harder to see coming (otherwise, I'd have to use some sort of format screw to hide the fact that they're Thaumiel).
I don't think the Foundation really trusts them. As WTF_STFU puts it: They're unstoppable nigh-immortal digitalized death-gods. O5 Council said yes only because if they turned them down, they might have flipped their shit  and the only way to stop them would have been to blow the computer up and hope it actually does neutralize them.

TL;DR: The Foundation is letting them play MTF because they're good at it, and it gives them something to do (which makes them more predictable, and thusly easier to contain). But they're not Thaumiel because the Foundation perceives them as way too dangerous.

I imagine the Foundation has a remote-controlled kill-switch attached to SCP-2639-C  just in case.
