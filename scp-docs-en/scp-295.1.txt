Fold

04 May 2015 03:52

Heh

I agree with everyone calling for a rewrite, but what bothered me most was the ambiguous language. I could parse the intended meanings, but it's sloppy writing and more-so when creating the illusion of life-and-death instructions.

A single specimen of SCP-295 is capable of creating an unidentifiable substance, which has several unique qualities attributed to it.
附加记录：    

Is only a single specimen able to do so?

A notable exception is living animal matter, which SCP-295-1 is incapable of affecting in all circumstances.
附加记录：    

So is it capable of affecting animal life under SOME circumstances?

But 2 other nitpicks:

both the pupa and the specimen will combust
附加记录：    

My understanding is that the pupa is the specimen

All matter destroyed in this manner leaves no ash or other byproducts behind, although an increase in mass has been observed in specimens of SCP-295 after combustion.
附加记录：    

This isn't combustion. Also, unless the cauterpillars gain the mass of the consumed material (minus a little foe generation of heat and light) this isn't even chemical. And if it were antimatter annihilation, this would have gone XK long before containment. Silly science.

Still, gotta love the pun
