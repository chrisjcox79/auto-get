That's funny, I recently saw a video on YouTube about obscure units of measurement and one of the ones mentioned is a unit for measuring radiation based on the amount of radiation you get exposed to from eating a banana. It is called the Banana Equivalent Dose. It is a useful unit because the amount of radiation in a banana is extremely tiny, just a small fraction of the radiation you normally get in one day, so it is a good thing to compare other low radiation things with.
