Thanks for pointing that out! I originally had a clear explanation for that, but it must've gotten removed by mistake while I was making revisions. I made a small change clarifying that they don't fire because Albright specifically orders them not to, even after she's been grabbed.

As for why they're on the same side of the fence, Albright wanted to be. She wanted to interact with these entities closely, and felt confident in security to keep her safe. Obviously, that's not how it goes, but the point still stands.
