15 Apr 2020 19:20

All in all, I liked this a lot. It was extremely well set up and simple enough to understand immediately. I enjoyed how -a and -b react differently to their timelines, along with the attention to how first responders reacted to the scene.

+stuff

-hide

There were a few lines that felt a little clunky or out of place.

While most class-IO events are retroactively repaired at the end of the event (usually in a matter of seconds) this event became self-sustaining and has remained active ever since.
附加记录：    

This feels a little strange, I would change it to something like:

This event is irregular from most class-IO events due to the lack of reparation of the timelines and that the timelines became self-sustaining and remain active.
附加记录：    

The first feels a little un-clinical, and more of an explanation of what a -IO event is, rather then how this one differs, IMO.

the third bullet struck Mr. Latozzi in the left lung and struck the TH3 vertebrae.
附加记录：    

The repetition of struck is a little clunky to read, I would recommend changing it to reflect the fact it went through his lung and struck the vertebra, if that's what you intended.

Great article overall! +1
