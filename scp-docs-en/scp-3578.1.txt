This article is emotionally taxing to read, but in a good way. The distress of SCP-3578 is palpable, and it invokes empathy. The Foundation in this article may be even too accomodating for such a resource-heavy object, but this could be attributed to a long-running project that the higher-ups wanted to bring to fruitition due to curiosity.

The real problem here is the length. The numerous descriptions of different SCP-3578-A instances, despite being abridged, is a heavy read. There are some other issues that I can dismiss as stylistic preferences or an invocation of 'there is no canon', and as such I won't address.

All in all, while the feeling is all there, I will novote this.
