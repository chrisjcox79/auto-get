22 Jun 2010 00:44

Sorry it took me so long to get these onto the discussion page, I didn't realise until you asked me to chuck them on that my last application was denied and by the time I got an approval message I had gone to bed. Anyway, here's a quick brief of the suggestions from yesterday.

Add interviews. Use her as the interviewer and the interviewee.
Mention as an aside that her story might just be a load of crap. Don't make it a definite yes or no, maybe something along the lines of "though this claim cannot be verified" or "though some of the more suspicious members of the foundation have their own doubts"
Some of the pertinent facts are missing, such as the fact that she roamed freely for a month or so before being captured. Judging by your answers to questions last night you've already thought these things out, just didn't add them. Without that it seems she appeared in an SCP holding cell speaking fluent english, which is stretching the bounds of belief (and yes, I'm still aware we are talking about a time travelling pachycephaloscientist).
On the topic of language, you might want to cover or gloss over the fact that she can speak english despite a probably incompatible talky bits. She's a unique species and a scientist so don't feel limited. Mybe by a stroke of coincidence of evolution her vocal system is strikingly similar to ours, or she can mimic sounds like a parrot only with understanding of what sounds she makes, or she made a translator device during her initial month or maybe we have no freaking clue how she talks. There's no shortage of possible answers.
Give her a name. While doctors are encouraged to keep distant and only refer to her as SCP-317, she is a sentient creature and would have a name even if we never use it. Slip a line in somewhere saying that she refers to herself as ___ or claim that the human voicebox can't produce it, but it seems odd that she hasn't got a name.
Finally (I guess I lied when i said this was a brief summary), and this wasn't one of last nights suggestions since I only just thought of it: the idea that she looks on humans as zoological specimens to be studied before she casually wipes them out (no matter how well she gets along with them) has the potential to actually be quite chilling. It's entirely up to you if you don't want it to head in that direction, and I'm not really sure how you could play that point up without being a bit anvilicious but it's an idea.

Anyhoo, good luck. Keep in mind they're just suggestions, it's your article and I was kinda grouchy last night. I'll be bluntly honest and say i don't like it so far… but I do see a few things that will make it interesting if expanded on. And I'm a complete newb as i said, so a pinch of salt or two with everything I say. Anyway, hope this stuff helps. I'll definitely be coming back to check this one out.
