Thoughts:

We have a lot of articles where SCP-XXX turns its subjects into SCP-XXX-2. This one takes it a step further by adding the completely useless stage where the affected equines become SCP-XXX-2 before becoming SCP-XXX-3. Since there'd be no reason to have special containment procedures for SCP-XXX-2 or -3, as both of them are the same as normal horses and die really quickly anyways, I think you can do away with them and just refer to them as subjects or animals or whatever.

Also, I'm not excited with the idea of the object itself being a feed bag. I mean, I guess it's kind of related, but it seems like a house or something else would be more interesting (granted, it's not like the horse would be talking much, but as soon as it sticks its head in the window one day while the family's eating, BOOM, talking horse.)

Also a picture, of whatever the object ends up being (probably not one of the horses being people) would be good.

I think this is the kind of thing that could benefit from a selection of an experiment log (though not necessarily a whole separate log)- since horses are such gawky animals when you think of them doing things people do, a few gruesome examples could make it much more interesting.

Overall: I don't think the idea's terrible, but the article could use some improvement.

Explanation

18 Sep 2010 13:45

What's happening here (and I really can't think of a way for the Foundation to figure this out with the information they have) is that there's a human mind 'recorded' in the nosebag. And every time a horse sticks its nose in the bag, the mind gets imprinted on the horse (completely overwriting whatever consciousness the horse originally had - LobsterHorde suggested that the typical screaming-kicking-shitting anti-predator response manifest at this instant of the horse's mind being crushed, which is rather horrifying).

And then the human mind wakes up. And it's trapped in a horse body. And it fucking PANICS.

If the mind was able to learn, to retain memories from one horse to the next, it would eventually figure out to stop panicking, and to write in the dirt with its hooves. But every time is the first time.

The 'attacking its head with its hooves' thing… think of someone wiping at his eyes, trying to make himself wake up.

It's the mind of someone who had always, deep down, had this desire to be a horse.

What would it be like to have your deepest fantasy come true… and have it be more horrible than you could have ever imagined?
