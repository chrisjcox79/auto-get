I don't really understand why this anomaly would ever be relinquished for the purposes identified in the logs. It seems like the scip would make for a uniquely terrible password due to its anomalous properties, and none of the other proposed uses make sense to me either. (That may be my fault as a reader.)

Fold

28 Dec 2020 03:14

Its anomalous properties meaning that only you can know it, and nobody else can guess it, makes it uniquely applicable as a password to my thinking. As for the uses not making sense, I dunno; I deliberately avoided directly explaining each scenario, leaving it up to the reader to figure them out. Won't work for everybody. These days my reviewers tend to demand that I spell everything out verbatim not so, six months ago.

While I feel there's enough justification in the password's use, it's obviously up to you to decide what violates your own headcanon. For my part, I want to tell as many different stories as possible; if a particular story requires some out-of-character moves on the part of the Foundation to make it function, I work on the justification until it works for me. I don't write anything that I think directly contradicts the way the setting works, because that defeats the purpose of using the setting, but everyone's SCPF is different and it's cool that this doesn't mesh with yours. I wanted to write something like this, so I bent the rules until it fit; I'd do it again.

Thanks for the comment, the thoughts and the positives, much appreciated!

Thanks! After starting with a Euclid, which happened because the idea I came up with ended up being that, I intentionally wrote Safe, Keter, and Thaumiel next as I proceeded through all the recognized classes. I then went off the deep end with all the esoterics I could bundle together in my normal-length arms with relatively small hands, which is turning out to be nearly all of them. I think the classes work really well as creative prompts. If you were to come up with a Safe, what would it be? What would be so dangerous as to necessitate a Keter class? This isn't to say you should start with the class and then write the SCP — in most cases that's an abominable idea — but as an experiment, I've found it pretty inspirational. My highest-rated SCP exists because I forced myself to think: what's a Tiamat?

I had fun with the log format; Character Development. Turns out the answer to the question I asked myself at the start of writing this article —
