wantheadcanon?

ceaseheadcanon

This ad was developed as a recruitment tool by a group of Fifthists who successfully migrated to Alpha Centauri in the mid-20th century. They can monitor viewers telepathically when it's being played, but it has to be uploaded to the internet by co-conspirators on earth.

Previous versions unsuccessfully tried to have other GoIs as the ad's creators, but a couple reviewers pointed out why those wouldn't work. The Scientologists Fifthists are both sufficiently good at marketing AND sufficiently callous towards human life to create something with these effects.

EDIT: Since my by-golly-gee first SCP here has been hovering around zero for almost two weeks, it seems I've done some things well and other things poorly. But I'm not entirely sure which bits are which! If you happen to have a clue to spare, I would definitely appreciate the guidance.

Edit Nov 16 effect. Hopefully this is an improvement!

Thank you very much Kaiser! This skip changed a lot during the editing process, and I definitely appreciate you letting me know what works and what doesn't in the final version.

Ponderingplotting...

Pondermore

The  thing came about because I really really want to do an incident crossing over with SCP-3900, and finally solve the mystery of what happens when a person bites a 3900-infected router.

In the process, unfortunately, I made the ad a pretty cliche  The body horror used to be even worse, alas - this version is toned down considerably from early drafts. Yet you and several reviewers (including 3900's author) also felt it was over the top.

I was worried the retargeting parts and technical speak would get boring because I tend to over-explain. But I'm glad it worked, and will definitely try to clarify that a bit when I revise.

I hate to plow under the idea that got me started. But I even more hate to have an article stay crappy and unsatisfying because I couldn't make the idea work.

Again, many thanks. And I will revisit this, hopefully soon.

I think what you DO have going for you here (and take this with a grain if salt, as I'm not as familiar with Fifthism as others on this site, since I'm not particularly a fan of that GoI) is you've nailed down one of the unique aspects of Fifthist skips, which is how pervasive and difficult to contain they are.

They are designed to reach as many people as possible to spread their message and they are singularly effective at it compared to other GoIs. Remember that book they wrote?

This, I think, has that for sure. It's an advertisement you can't close, it spreads indiscriminately and there are instances in the wild of people up and disappearing because of it.
