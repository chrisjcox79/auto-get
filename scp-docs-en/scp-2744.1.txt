05 Jun 2017 05:21

I think this would probably work better if the sheet itself was given the 2744 designation, and the cat-like entity a sub-designation. From the Foundation's perspective, 2744 could reasonably be described as . The cat has no verifiable existence independent of the sheet, but the sheet is a constant. Also, you describe its anomalous properties a lot, so it could stand to have a designation.

Unless there's some specific reason, I think you could stand to have the cat completely underneath an unmarked sheet. First and foremost this lets you excise a number of sentences entirely (good for an article with a concept that's basically 'bedsheet ghost cat'); second and socemost, as a cat owner, I can attest that a cat trapped completely under a bedsheet is very funny, and a cat perpetually trapped under one would be a delightful image.

The last note doesn't work for me. The only reason given for the containment procedures being anything but  is that maybe the cat can do mischief while not contained by the cloth, which I must emphasize is not supported by any evidence, and thus is not a hypothesis the Foundation (an ostensibly scientific organization) should be acting upon. Contrast with SCP-1422, which has the Foundation acting in-character, by my estimation.

Not to mention  does nothing to strike, uh, emotions into my heart. Boy, I sure hope food containers don't start pushing themselves over table edges??? Melodramatic way to end such a cute article, if you ask me.
