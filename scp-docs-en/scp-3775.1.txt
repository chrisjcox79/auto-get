My reaction: 

09 May 2018 17:05

This doesn't really appeal to me. The basic premise is decent, but the specific wording is shoddy (read: not clinical enough) in certain spots and the overall structure relies too much on SCP-2747.

+Suggestions

-goes...

The name  seems arbitrarily contrived to me, but I might be able to accept it if everything else is made better.

My main concern: This reads too much like SCP-2747. Above all other things in the article, that must be fixed. Here's basically how it reads:

Special Containment Procedures: What to do with it.

Description: What it is and what it does.

List of Affected Narratives: Contains copious amounts of foreshadowing.

The Big Reveal: Anomalously Lost
附加记录：    

Not only is this more blatant than SCP-2747, it fails to capture my interest in quite the same way.

SCP-2747 has been referenced or implied in other articles before, but SCP-3775 barely has its own premise. You need to expand on the original ideas and make the SCP-2747 stuff less obvious. The best way to go about this is to hide the SCP-2747 narremes among everything else,

and go into more detail on the things unique to this particular anomaly. The memetic stuff seems promising to me.

Alternatively, you could decrease the drama leading up to the final [DATA CORRUPTED] and avoid SCP-2747 altogether, and instead make them related only tangentially.

In conclusion, change the structure, veer away from the SCP-2747 article to a certain degree, and beef up this article with meaningful information that further differentiates it from SCP-2747.

How exactly you go about doing this is up to you, author, but it must be done if you want the article to stand on its own.

Until this improves, I'm downvoting this, unironically bringing the vote count to 7.
