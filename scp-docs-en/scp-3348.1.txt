I'm going to novote because it certainly isn't bad enough to warrant a downvote, but I feel like there needs to be something.. more?

The clinical tone, grammar, and usage of vocabulary throughout this article is absolutely superb. I just have a few problems with the note at the end and a bit about the concept overall.

With the note, I wouldn't call the Serpent's Hand an organization, it's more of a movement; an ideology. So, yes, a select few could be the ones going after him and not staying true to the cause of the SH, but I wouldn't blane the entirety of the wanderers.

With the concept, it just kinda appeared? And all it does is make stuff not work? In the containment procedures, why not just restrict usage instead of using amnestics? Wouldn't testing just to forget what you did be a bit unnecessary?

I feel like there should be more about this book. It piqued my interest at the beginning of the description, but I just feel like this article as a whole isn't fleshed out enough.

Best of luck!

Fold

28 May 2018 17:28

Thank you for your thorough input!

Your point about the Serpent's Hand is totally right, I'll see about changing that accordingly.

On the book spontainously appearing: I intended to suggest that Motzer was able to send the book to the Foundation's dimension via the use of Ways or the like. Perhaps I need to expound upon that more?

The idea behind the concept is that it contains words from an ancient, somehow malevolent language that induces a sort of  effect whenever it is used. I can see now that perhaps in its current state, its not as strong an idea as I may have hoped.

On the amnestic usage: My thought process was that the Foundation didn't want personnel (especially D-Class) memorizing any of the words and using them for trouble. The easiest way I saw to ensure that was amnestic treatment. Its also why the words in the test log are partially censored.

I hope I've been successful in answering your questions!

Now, don’t get me wrong; if I could find a better appropriate word to use, I would. However, as it stands, “enemy” is the only word that seems to have the correct nuances I’m looking for.

Consider this: If I used “hostile”, that would mean that instead of the d-class’s feelings changing towards the officer (since he lost his “enemy” quality), the officer would simply lose his ability to be hostile (since he lost his “hostile” quality).

I hope this makes sense, and that you can see the predicament I’m in!
