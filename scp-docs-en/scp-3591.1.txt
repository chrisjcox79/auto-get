There are a lot of things not to like about this, almost all of them minor tonal issues.

Massive physical size, sufficient to crush an area of ███-███ square kilometers.
附加记录：    

Like, why is the size described in terms of what it could crush? That's just not a thing.

But there's really a ton to like. This higher-dimensional dead thing that we can't identify beyond . The Foundation's preoccupation with understanding things they never can. I particularly like that last unique reality that said it was baseline (I have to imagine that's where the last fellow came from). On that note, I'm shocked 1985 hasn't been showing up again and again this contest. Using her was a great idea.

So yeah, +1 in total.

Like, why is the size described in terms of what it could crush? That's… just not a thing.
附加记录：    

Because their other measurements are all off. The only thing that they are 100% sure of is that it can and did crush a specific area of ███-███ square kilometers.

After the contest, I'll add a note about that!

This SCP was mostly written in a single all-nighter (my IRL schedule really sucks right now), and after I've spent the last few years working on mainstream fiction in a casual voice, so any feedback on tonal issues would be very helpful.

I usually dislike all these doomsday and doomsday style artifacts, because they declare things within their specific canon that are so permanently world-changing that I feel like they belong more as tales about the object, than an object themselves.

But I personally felt this one was gorgeous. Loved it, +1. I feel the whole sense of doom without it being outright established that this will end the world. In fact, it feels like this could easily never actually be a world ending problem.

But it sure is a spooky existential one.

02 Aug 2018 15:17

This is fantastic and deserves more upvotes than I have. The physical descriptions are wonderfully evocative without compromising the essential mystery of the object, and the attempts to describe it in terms of its meaning create a sense of enormous implied meaning and scale. This one thing credibly implies a whole context in which we might as well be microorganisms colonizing one particular pebble, complete with threats so big that they haven't noticed we exist and might kill us anyway.

The connection to the contest entry feels solid, and yet ancillary enough to the article that I would feel comfortable reading about 3591 without investigating the entire entry- in short, a perfect crossover tie-in. Well done.
