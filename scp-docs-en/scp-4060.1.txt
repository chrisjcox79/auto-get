Fold

20 Nov 2018 02:58

There are just too many problems for me to not downvote it right now. But the concept is too good to not be improved, either. You had a very solid idea, just need to improve the execution.

1-Containment.

–block

1- Take out the bit about GoIs in the Containment. It is too hamfisted. From a in-universe standpoint this skip doesn't really make any difference on the matter: any sympathy for a GoI should be taken seriously regardless of any particular anomaly nearby.

2-it.

–block

2- Take a little more time on Description, well, describing it. You go very short on physical description and rush yourself into presenting the anomalous effect right on the first paragraph. At the same time it ends up lacking a little more suspense and the object becomes a little harder to imagine. Yes, I know we have a picture, but the reader should have a feeling of how it is to be near the thing.

3-side.

–block

3- Take such opportunity to distinguish between plates at each side. Not only  is hard to follow, but it actually makes no sense, since the scale on the picture is round.

4-anomaly.

–block

4- Take a look at other skips to get a hold of how to describe the anomaly. You rush things all around, and end up explaining things in a confusing order. You don't need to make it more a complex text, just reword it to be clearer.

5-something.

–block

5- Take the  makes it appear as being how the Foundation got the object, what it isn't. Then add story to it. We had no chance to know anything about the characters before, so we had no reason to comprehend or care. We have little context. It is too short a description to deliver the emotional impact it is meant to.

6-care.

–block

6- Take your time to treat the first batch of tests with more care. On most of them your goal is clear, but the test isn't actually careful enough to render that data. You know what the results will be and why, but the researchers shouldn't. If done right, you probably can get rid of the Analysis section on each test.

7-test.

–block

7- Take out the Analysis section of the last test. This is the  of the article, but in this case it doesn't need to be said aloud. At this point, most readers should be able to reach this conclusion by themselves. If you really want to explain yourself, do this through a last comment from a Site Director or something like that. As it is now, you're telling the joke and then explaining it before we have time to laugh.

8-article.

–block

8- Take out some black-boxes and redaction from the article. So many black-box in a row is awkward. You numbered the Task Force, you can number the Area. The sector could have a short description instead of number. Keep the number of breaches blackened. Give the MTF a nickname, if you need just borrow a MTF from the Background section of the wiki. If you can, identify the GoI you used here. If you can't, avoid referencing it by name four short paragraphs in a row.

All this is just me, of course. Take it with a grain of salt, but it should help with what I think was your intent here. The concept is fairly strong, and the last test was very good. But you need a better build up to reach it. And either redact her entire age, or put it, it makes no sense to redact how far she is in her twenties.
