Well, this one is right up my alley. I prefer Lynchian weirdness to straightforward sinister any day, and I was a Lit major. I have a lot of not entirely positive feelings about Faulkner, but the careful details you've written into this are pretty nifty. This is a very multi-layered SCP, tone-wise, and I personally found the mood set by the  story was a clever set-up for the coded section.

In short: I think this was a risky SCP to write and won't be universally appealing, but I am impressed by the careful crafting and it hits all my literary kinks. This is more surrealism than horror, but I think that has its place on the mainlist. We get technological/sciency SCPs pretty often, it's a reminder of the breadth of content the Foundation has to be prepared to deal with.

I'm personally shocked that any Lit Major could have any feelings about Faulkner that aren't positive! But I totally agree with you, besides that. This SCP just gives me a Lit Crit boner, and I cannot currently think of another that gave me one like this.

I love everything about this skip. Kalinin, it really feels like you have a good grasp of Faulkner's work, and that really strengthens this piece. I mean, jesus, there's miscegenation, weird ghostly females, and clear gothic themes. I know that doesn't really make or break the skip, but to someone who's familiar with Faulkner, that's just icing on the cake. The only thing to make this more Faulknerian would be if the burial rites conducted by the Native American woman weren't even correct for her tribe. (Speaking of this, if you could stomach trying to write like Faulkner, I think some sections of this would make a fucking stellar tale.)

As to the question of whether or not this is anomalous enough, I really would argue it is. I do agree with Cellar Door, however, that this is risky as hell. No one is dying in this skip. There's no monster, no radiation, no quantum mechanics, but I think it's all the stronger for it.
