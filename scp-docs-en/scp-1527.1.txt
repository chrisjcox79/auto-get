I read the GoI information, I don't think that helps this SCP. I'm not going to read a bunch of tales just to understand one SCP. (They are supposed to stand on their own, after all.) There are many members on this site: some new, some old, and I've already learned that some are more familiar with different groups and ideas than others. I read the 001 Factory by Bright before I knew anything about the Factory, and its still a great article. I've read other SCPs that list other groups, some I've upvoted, some I haven't.

Even if I had read all that knowledge, I'm not sure that would help my appreciation of the SCP, unless I'm really missing something. And I'm not sure how to explain it more than I have generic.

You've got a bell, that rings, and creates crab monsters that people have to shoot to contain. There's nothing creepy about that. Nothing funny. Nothing cool. It's got the cliche of 'needs armed men with guns to shoot monsters' to contain it. There's no cool exploration logs about going to other place, there's no motive behind the crab monsters except that they're evil.

The backstory, again, piques my interest, but not enough to save the rest of the SCP. This guy of extreme power, who went to countless dimensions near and far, some material, some immaterial and the best you could come up with was that he decides to torment his banishers by sending them crab monsters every night. Where did he go? What else did he see? These are better answers you could explore in an SCP, not have him out for petty crab-monster revenge.

Look, this seems to be appealing to many others, just not me personally. You can't please everyone. If you like your idea, and it continues to do well, then I wouldn't change it if I were you. But if you decide you want to go in a different direction, then personally, based on your backstory, I think you can do a LOT better. One possibly stupid idea: maybe its a two-way portal, and there is a way to control where it opens by how you ring the bell (number of rings, duration of each) and the Foundation tried to do it, it worked a few times, failed a few times, but enough bad things happened that they halted testing. Maybe water started gushing in through a portal at some point. Crab monsters invaded at some point. One time shoggoths. They reached an anti-matter dimension and were able to check it out but then it blew up a portion of the desert. I don't know, just brainstorming.

As you said yourself in the letter, as it stands, its mundane.
