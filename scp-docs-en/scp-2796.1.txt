Okay, I've tried to punch it up a bit, hint more as to what is happening, and create a stronger narrative both in the cult portion (trying to attract the attention of the phantom surgeons) and the Foundation angle (this SCP was quickly written off as fairly harmless and thus has fallen through the cracks despite having serious implications, playing on the increasingly massive list of ever-more worldthreatening and intensively wrangled SCPs we have on the list).

This one is a bit of a balancing act. If I hint in the document too strongly that something is up, it becomes unbelievable that the Foundation would like this slip by. If it is too subtle, it just becomes crazypaint. I hope this is a more workable balance.

The idea of something that allows you to see horrible things that go unnoticed by the rest of humanity, on its own, has been done to the point where I believe something else of interest beyond  is necessary before it's a workable article. The fact that it's unnoticeable surgery is the start of something interesting, I suppose.

I really didn't care for the  handwaved explanation.

In some ways, I think no explanation at all would be preferable. The fact that any old goober can put some household chemicals together and suddenly be aware of this bizarre phenomenon is the basis of something interesting; making it the product of the Foundationverse's apparently-thriving Spooky Doomsday Cult Community only cheapens the horror, I feel.
