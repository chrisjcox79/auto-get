Fold

21 Oct 2015 05:06

Indeed! Except the poor, poor spiny anteater.

I actually agree with Breezeblock, the bolded/capitalized/first-in-each-line letters trick is pretty meh. The action was nice and ambiguous up to that point, but the note at least partially solidified what was until that point a delicate dance.

I love the scale of the anomaly and I think it's fantastically written, but the ending might just be enough to move me from a strong upvote to a no-vote.

Edit: For more specificity:

What I liked in the article was the implication that a collection of ravens, through their natural curiosity (or perhaps grief, which they definitely also display in bizarre ways), had simply decided that it would be interesting to be a human for a while. This, balanced against the back-and-forth of dreaming and the life of the original Jessica, made for a nice push-and-pull.

Having them spell out their collective will, in plain English (even if it is , bolded text isn't subliminal) really nails them down to an at least human-like mind, and sucks a lot of the alien-ness inherent to the animal mind out of it.

I know the article is incredibly successful and that others enjoyed the gambit, but it just sapped it for me.
