There were a ton of people who looked at this and critted this over and over and over and I appreciate their help so much. Shaggydredlocks, Rimple, Roget, Weryllium, DrMagnus, AbsentmindedNihilist, Sterbai, Shio, MrWong, Ninevolt, Salvagebar and a whole crap ton of other people that I shopped this around to helped me immensely in formulating and putting this idea to paper. This was a complex concept, and I've been told several times that this was probably deserving of an 001 proposal.

In reality, this is my lead in to my actual 001 proposal. The concept of the SCP is based on the deities from Orcadian Folklore, the Mither of the Sea and Teran, where the Mither is the arthropod, and Teran in all his might and wrath is the Gulper Eel.

The image is under the public domain, and originates from https://archive.org/details/popularsciencemo23newy

Showstuff

hidestuff

Ok, so you know how I said above that the Mither is the lobster, and Teran the eel? That's sort of correct, but not really. It's more like, the lobster and the eel are pieces of the entities, they are puppets, avatars, physical representations for something much larger, and much more powerful. The Foundation doesn't know this when they set the ball in motion, but by tipping the scales in ones favor they have destabilized what was a natural equilibrium. The lobster and eel annihilating each other was just a small scale event, a pinprick with just enough force to disturb a slumbering entity.

+stuff

-

I can't tell if some of this is intentional, as it's a log but seems unclinically written at points, so I'll just address points I saw.

 - Jaw should not be capitalized.

 unless both clauses are independent. This occurs several more times throughout the log.

.

These are additional commas that may be grammatically incorrect but seem to represent prosaic pauses, so it's unclear if they're correct.

 - these are the only three lines that represent dialogue from one of the ships that are not enclosed by quotation marks. I would edit these to remain consistent.

 - its should be it's.

 - Read should not be capitalized.

 - Seems like it should end in a question mark to appropriately reflect intent.

 - Negative should not be capitalized.

None of the errors I noted are prevalent outside of the incident log, but the log does strike me as hastily edited. That aside, it's an interesting take on a lesser-heralded branch of mythology with an interesting impact on the region and it's just plain fun to see monsters fighting in the ocean. Outside the errors noted in the log I quite like it, and if the log was tightened up it'd be an easy upvote.
