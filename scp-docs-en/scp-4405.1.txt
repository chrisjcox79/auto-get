Premise is both nihilistic and fun in just the right amounts. Leaves me with a few questions unanswered6, so I wouldn't exactly mind if this was elaborated in some way or another, but I still like it as it stands, so take your upvote.

Footnotes
1. Is the TGI Friday's the only space left in the universe after it collapses?
2. What is the threshold where this starts to happen? Does this only happen after heat-death, XKs, information deletion via black holes, etc?
3. Were the employees created when the first extra-universal TGI Friday's was, or were they just people in the universe who were shoved into the restaurant when it died?
4. Is the law of conservation of mass intact in the remnants of a dead universe, or is everything gone save for what was needed for the TGI Fridays and the food/drinks it makes?
5. Without a reliable and infinite way to measure time, how do the employees handle shifts, eating, and sleeping?
SC Plastics.

I'm not a fan of this one. The exposition is all done directly in that log, and it's just the Foundation character stating what we already could have inferred from concept alone, leaving the whole thing feeling hollow. The Restaurant at the End of the Universe vibes and the Pan Galactic Gargle Blaster reference didn't do much other than make me think 'yeah, this is similar to some stuff' but there's not enough original stuff here to make it mean anything. The concept is already dependent on a real company's existence, and it is quite literally a Restaurant at the End of the Universe. Same concept, except multiples instead of one that uses time travel like in Hitchhiker's.

The logo's use doesn't seem to have an in-universe explanation, which does give it a -J feel even though the concept (including its humor) doesn't have to feel like a -J. Also, the CSS does not change the Wikidot account info text in the top right to a darker color, the logo is pixelated as hell (I'm sure my monitor resolution is to blame for that, though, but high-res monitors are becoming more and more common and it doesn't really take that much longer a load time to have a reasonably large image, especially if it takes up that much of the screen), and the completely blank header space is visually unappealing.
