My biggest problem with this article is that, while it's a security risk, there's no actual indication any of it is anomalous. It could simply be a disgruntled ex-employee, a defector, or someone with a contact on the inside, trying to expose the Foundation. It's certainly something we'd need to watch and if possible counteract, but unless there's definite proof there's some sort of anomaly involved, this shouldn't get its own designation.

Edit: I get that the episodes contain anomalies, but in that case those should get designations. The man and his robot shouldn't unless we're certain they're anomalous.
