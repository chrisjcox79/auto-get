nitpicks

do not seem fazed by its presence and react colloquially.

I think you mean to say .

Anyways, the jump-scare was neat, and I like the meta aspect of the looping page, and a lot of what's going on here, but I do feel like it's cut a bit too short. I think this could have used another iteration or two to thread everything together in a more satisfying manner, as right now the whole conflict is mostly glossed over, giving me the sense that I'm rushing through the beats that could use a bit more breathing room.

more deets:

+block

–block

So, for the first version,  I'm guessing this is referring to the entire format itself? If so, I'm wondering how it initially begins to affect its documentation (in the subsequent iteration). Seems like it 'just does'.

But alright, it does. It gets loose, screws around a bit, and messes up its file. I kinda feel like there could be a bit more at this point, because it pretty much goes from 0 to 10 in the span of a dozen words, so by the time we get to the following log, the tonal shift is a touch jarring. If not another iteration between the corrupted doc vs the Oblique doc, then I think expanding the corrupted document to sort of extend its time in that trapped state would help to flow more seamlessly into its explosion immediately afterwards.

Next up, I really feel like I'm missing something in the Oblique iteration. I'm not entirely sure what's supposed to be happening at the end, how the anomaly is being counteracted. It seems like it just cuts off because of a loss of direction, so the next scene could just get started. All ears if I'm overlooking something.

Final iteration, and the end of the description in particular is pretty solid for an endpoint. My only real complaint here is that I think that I should be feeling something for the character, but I just don't. This may be due to me not really having enough time to get emotionally invested in the character prior to this point, primarily because the conflict feels undersold at the moment.
