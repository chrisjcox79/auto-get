13 Jun 2018 02:16

I think the base idea is cute, if a tad boring. But it's poorly presented, and I don't like the story that this is trying to tell.

I don't care about D-Class personnel, nor do I really care about the ethics of using D-Class personnel to test anomalies, so when I see documents like this that try to throw a light on this ethical issue in some way, it bounces right off of me.

In this case, the entire meat of the article beyond the cute-but-boring effect, is a guy that gets mad because D-Class personnel are being killed and sent to hell, so instead of making the allegations against the doc first and taking his concerns to a Site Director, some other superior, or appeals to the Ethics Committee or some other thing that a sensible, professional human being employed by the Foundation would do, this self-righteous vigilante hero just is not the strongest case to try to make when justifying cold-blooded murder) about how there are limits to what is and isn't 'appropriate' torture/death sentences for D-Class personnel, and then he dies.

There's no story here for me. -1, unfortunately.

Fold

13 Jun 2018 06:08

Eh. I like the concept, but I'm not feeling the execution.

I feel like Calamity's hit the main issue. The main core is the conflict between Kijuro and Bates, and there just isn't any real set-up for it. We're tossed right into it without having any connection to these people.

There are two things I'd like to see expanded here. First, more examples of the effects to help sell the idea that this is sending people to actual, literal hell. The multiple voices screaming is a good start, but some more details like that could make it really pop. Maybe glimpses in the flames of previous subjects, or voices actually pleading for help rather than just screams.

Second, some more set-up of the conflict. Give us some notes and addenda to show the growing tension between the two men. Make us believe that Bates would just go to the extreme of forcing Kijuro to taste his own medicine (as opposed to, say, just shooting him). Attempts by Bates to appeal to higher authorities, attempts to reason with Kijuro. Maybe show Bates's realization that Kijuro is going outside of normal testing parameters (or the realization that those are normal parameters).

I feel like you have the potential for a good story here, but you haven't told it yet.
