08 May 2012 21:00

As ChazzK notes, permanent lightning storms are an actual phenomenon that occur in several places on Earth; the picture I used for the article is of the Catatumbo lightning, which has been going on in Venezuela at least since the 16th century. I think it can be safely assumed that the Foundation is suppressing the publication of any research regarding this particular storm, but I get enough comments about my articles being too wordy so I didn't want to go into too much detail about that either.

Since it's been brought up a few times, I am not a mechanic and I personally didn't know before writing this that being inside a car during a lightning storm is safe. Again, I think it can be assumed that the lightning, being intelligent and having a desire to target humans, manages to strike the car in just the right spot so that the electricity comes up through the dashboard instruments, or the chrome on the gearshift, or something of that nature.
