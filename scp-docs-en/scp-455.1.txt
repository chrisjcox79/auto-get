27 Jun 2012 23:57

The Keter classification is reserved for SCPs that 1.)  Are active, serious threats to human life, civilization, etc. and 2.)  require extensive protocols and/or resources to properly contain. This thing just sits around doing nothing on it's own. Horrifying? Yes. One of the scariest SCPs I've read, but it's relatively easy to contain. Hell, it could arguably be Safe, Just because something's scary doesn't mean it's Keter. Look at SCPs 993 and 1981. Both scary as balls. Both classified Safe.
