08 Oct 2017 04:20

Gonna go ahead and second a lot of what Conwell said; the worldbuilding came off strong and how Anderson's character was handled was pretty spot on. Unfortunately, I was sorely disappointed with where the story went.

Though they still label them as property in official context, and apparently use them as leverage, there's something quite human and actually somewhat decent in how MCD operates here:

They bring back a man's entire family, and allow him to return to some semblance of a normal life (up to and including the family members resuming their roles in society, not like they're being locked up and abused by cackling sadists at cocktail parties). This puts them on track to actually have the moral high ground on the SCPF for a changewhich is something I can't say I've seen examined before.

Next you introduce the notion that MCD just goes gung-ho guns-a-blazing like a group of goofy CI nuts.

Cut to the end, where it's revealed the family man in question is himself a replicate (which was made incredibly obvious from the second paragraph of the description) and it's just matter-of-factly repeated that the SCPF should give him backD rep to inject some kind of pathos into the end, because as it stands now, it's just a huge letdown.
