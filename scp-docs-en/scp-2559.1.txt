Hey there, thanks for reading! This is my first article for the site, hopefully the first of many (or, like, at least five). I realised last month that it was almost a year since I joined, so I decided to get off my ass and put together my endless scribbled drafts and notes into one proper article.

If you've finished this and are confused about the ending (or any of the rest of it), that's ok. If people seem to want it, I'll put out an explanation in a few weeks, but if you enjoyed it that's all that counts. Of course, if you didn't enjoy it, let me know. There's a solid chance I'll build upon this some other day with a linked tale or skip, but obviously the article should stand on its own two feet.

My friend Omino made this excellent MTF seal for Upsilon-4, but over the course of changes and edits I couldn't figure out a place to stick it in the article itself. Ah well. It'll probably come in handy later.

I'dthank...

...opportunity

Big thanks to TheShrike for looking at this in its crappy unfinished state, particularly Shrikey for talking to me for actual damn hours before I got around to looking at their draft.

Thanks to everyone who read it and gave me feedback in main chats - I'm sorry, I'm terrible with names and there were a number of you, but you were indeed helpful even if you just gave a thumbs up.

A wholly unironic thanks to Jekeled who looked at this, found it lacking, and talked at length about how I could make it better by, for instance, turning it into a tale or series of tales. The fact that I entirely ignored your advice does not mean it wasn't really good. I accept and anticipate your respective downvotes.

Final thanks to my Dad, who told me he had no idea what I was talking about when I explained this to him back in November 2015, but let me know that he had vitiligo and most likely so did I. I knew then that destiny had fated me to write this skip Coincidences are neat.

11 Oct 2016 04:03

I'm a little iffy on this one. On the one hand, your focus on the personal impact was often well done (I enjoyed Vihaan and the doctor, the MTF officer being iffy based on what I'll get into below), but

You've got a pretty clear-cut idea for an anomaly. I don't think your really sell it strong enough to push the horror of what it's like to face a thing that infects you due only to your traitorous thoughts, given the amount of page-length that comes after the explanation. Everyone just accepting their condition with dignity (or at least dedication) doesn't help this. You seem to be throwing out hints as to an origin (what I took to be a Fifthist reference in the palmistry book, the SCP logo), but they never seem to go anywhere. If you were making some alusion about what leads to the skin pattern, it went right over my head. (Edit: I also had no idea what was going on with the books. Am I missing something big here?)

So well thought out, a well explained anomaly, and you've checked a lot of the boxes as far as thinking about implications and human costs, but for me it's a bit too pulled back. It feels like an ongoing slow-motion tragedy written from the point of view of an impersonal and timeless Foundation, real bland dystopian disregard for human life style. That's not generally a bad thing, but when you combine it with really dire measures, statements of its unpredictability, and origin hints that suggest that the Foundation is as much in the dark as we are, that level of detachment is just really unsatisfying.

I know that the SCP is that dire and those measures are called for, it just reads as a tragedy without an out/twist. Neutral vote.
