I think demiurge = deity in this context, the ceremony essentially creates a god that can be bent (to some extent) to human will. I don't think the ceremony is capable of bestowing power on an existing being, it purely creates a new one in order to enact the changes to thaumaturgy. Without the second part to control how the demiurge acts, you are just unleashing a new god upon the world. Gods, especially in the SCP universe, have their own motivations and one that is capable of fundamentally altering how magic works would be an extremely serious threat upon the world. Finally, since there is no canon and magic is, well, magic, the rules behind thaumaturgy are a bit vague BUT the art does appear to always use this blue energy as its power source and I think, even without understanding every rule behind it, we can infer that this ritual could change how rituals occur, make established methods of  create new or undesired effects, etc. in addition to changing the distribution of thaumaturgic power across the world/universe. My best guess anyway. As a side note, +1 to this article.

In the simplest terms, a demiurge is a lower-case-god; a creator but not the creator. The Architect from the Matrix Trilogy is a good example: an entity tasked with building and regulating the matrix from within the system. Powerful, but bound within its realm, and subservient to the actual creator(s) of the matrix, the machine hive mind.

Adding on to this, the concept comes from gnostic theodicy. Some gnostics sects thought that God that allows Evil to exist cannot be the same infinitely merciful and just God that sent the Christ Logos, and in particular the God of the Old Testament is often inexplicably incompetent or just awful. So they believed that the actual Alpha and Omega God can't interact directly with Creation without destroying it so it created along with the universe entities that could run the universe from within. One or more of these entities (depending on sect) eventually either deluded themselves or lied about being actual-God and became the demiurge(s). Incidently this is the origin of quite a lot of the metaphysics and terminology in the settings, like  (what some sects called the entities left to rule the universe) and Yalboath (what some sects called the demiurge).
