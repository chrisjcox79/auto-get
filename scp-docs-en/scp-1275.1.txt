Can't get behind this, for a lot of small reasons.

There are just enough issues with tone and grammar to be noticeable as an issue overall. For instance,

 Run-on sentence, first comma should be a period or semicolon.
Mixed usage of 
 That's what gravity is.
 Bad tone.
 Poor tone, bad comma.
The text to re-collapse the two collapsibles are inconsistent: -Hide vs - hide block.
etc.

The recovery log is one paragraph of recovery log followed by two unrelated to its recovery: the second paragraph is about its inferred or deduced behavior prior to containment, while the third is about the Foundation's exploratory interactions with it after containment.
The recovery log says  Did you originally have the Incident Report and Recovery Log swapped?
?
The Incident Log is not about a single incident, but instead comprises over 19 months of behavior including at least four or five unique events worthy of being called 

In particular, the back half of this piece reads more like a story/tale than a containment document, and the escalation of hostilities feels like SCP-1322 but not as well done.

Thank you. Most of the issues here are now addressed. As for those that are not, I will at least say why.

 That's what gravity is.
附加记录：    

Weight is a measurement of an object's mass as affected by gravity. It is pulled downward by gravity, but does not register any weight upon scales/other instruments. It does not return any tactile sensation when touched. These give something of an idea of its properties.

 Poor tone, bad comma.
附加记录：    

Not sure how to rephrase this one.

The recovery log is one paragraph of recovery log followed by two unrelated to its recovery: the second paragraph is about its inferred or deduced behavior prior to containment, while the third is about the Foundation's exploratory interactions with it after containment.
附加记录：    

The recovery log deals with more than simply the recovery, yes, it is the Foundation's initial information they were able to ascertain about it. I felt it applicable. I may rework this later though, I do feel you have a point.

The Incident Log is not about a single incident, but instead comprises over 19 months of behavior including at least four or five unique events worthy of being called 
附加记录：    

I was initially going to format this as a table, but it felt wrong to me. Rather than being a group of isolated incidents, all of the incidents are connected as a prolonged attempt at escape/recovery.

The similarity to 1322 is one that I had noted and worried about, but knowing the actual story and background to it I know that it's a very different set of circumstances, so I was willing to move forward with it. Agree that 1322 is better executed though, that's one of the best on the site in my opinion.

That all said - I am impressed at your style of analysis and feedback. Keep it up with others, this was good work and very helpful.

Fold

30 Nov 2012 04:13

ClickReview

HideReview

Under no circumstances may personnel approach within 30m of SCP-1275 if it is loose, recontainment will be accomplished via specialized capture netting, delivered by air
附加记录：    

This is a really awkward sentence. It really is. Could do with some fixing, preferably something about the comma between 'if it is loose' and 'recontainment'.

SCP-1275 seems not to exist fully within the material world.
附加记录：    

This seems like quite a logical jump when you think about it, especially so much if it's considered important enough to mention in the beginning of the description.

Parts separated from the main body appear to flow extradimensionally
附加记录：    

extradimensionally? That sounds so cheesed. Hell, even 'through an unknown means' would be better than that.

objects of value
附加记录：    

This is extremely vague. Value according to whom? If I thought that my dog was valuable, would it be enveloped too? Valuable to the Foundation? So D-class would be ignored?

will vanish to parts unknown
附加记录：    

This also seems really really cheesed and iffy tone. You could better state that they have vanished without any successful recovery thus far, or something.

the object enveloped

Objects and personnel taken
附加记录：    

I see that this is inconsistent. If you're going to call them 'the objects enveloped' stick to that.

However, evidence now suggests that SCP-1275 has an end point in another part of the world that it is connected to extradimensionally, and that it shifts portions of itself between these endpoints, accounting for its apparent fluctuations in volume.
附加记录：    

Subtle as a brick to the face. I also have no idea how any evidence could suggest this. I mean, jesus hell man. If that evidence existed, it would be more fun to have it put in the article in a way that the reader could infer it than having it slammed into them like this. Also, I am becoming progressively more and more unimpressed with this article and have yet to see why it would be a keter.

The entity appears unable to remove the endpoint held by the foundation. It is theorized that either this is the primary half of the entity, or that it naturally has a divided existence.
附加记录：    

What? The Foundation has an endpoint? This wasn't mentioned in the beginning of the description at all? So confused. What do you mean remove? Like, pick it up and move it around? What is the endpoint?

deemed to be of value
附加记录：    

By who? By it? It has knowledge of the Foundation? This isn't mentioned as to how it could acquire this information? It's not even registered as through an unknown means?

presumably transporting them to its other endpoint
附加记录：    

What evidence is there for that claim? I just don't get it at this point and it sounds very thrown-together.

Evidence
附加记录：    

What evidence? It sounds so cheesed right now.

the entity sides with
附加记录：    

Uhm I don't like this tone.

is controlled by the hostile agents
附加记录：    

What hostile agents? Hostile agents were mentioned in the article? Am I reading this wrong?

To date too little data has been recovered to establish a definite link to any individual group.
附加记录：    

I don't understand, this link to a random hostile group comes out of nowhere.

though apparently none to itself
附加记录：    

The fact it can 'disgorge' high energy explosives should be an important fact mentioned in the description, methinks. I mean, that is fairly important.

Entity was moved
附加记录：    

Oh come on, not even 'the' now? I don't approve. I also have an issue with the whole 'the entity'. I would much prefer it to be more 'SCP-XXXX' in there, or something. If it's like multiple sentences in a row of SCP-XXXX, that's fine to replace one with 'the entity', but instead we have the exact opposite problem in which 'the entity' is refered to again and again.

An unknown hostile retrieval team
附加记录：    

OH! THERE THE HOSTILE AGENTS ARE! Why wasn't this explained previously? Why would you confuse the reader by specifying things that aren't even gone into until later in the article, but refuse to put in big details like BEING ABLE TO DISGORGE EXPLOSIVES AND SCPS in the description?

enemy agents armed with paranormal weaponry emerged from within SCP-1275
附加记录：    

Wait, so, are we talking about these hostile agents or the other hostile agents? Also, what idiot thinks that's a good idea? This isn't a video game, you don't just set up base in the enemy's base and expect it to work.

driven back into SCP-1275 again
附加记录：    

It was driven back previously? Also, 'driven back' seems like very odd tone.

The mesh grid has prevented SCP-1275 from disgorging objects capable of breaching its containment
附加记录：    

The fact that the SCP not only disgorges objects, but is prevented from doing so by a mesh grid seems like something that would be included in the description rather than vague allusions to ~mysterious enemy agents~ and ~a portal to another part of the world~ that isn't made clear or even explained until the last few bits. Honestly, even a (refer to Addendum-XXXX-BUTT) would've been better to the lack any explanation or warning whatsoever.

has thus far prevented transmitters from signaling its location to enemy agents
附加记录：    

Wait, how do we know this? Does it have transmitters? Not sure about the whole 'enemy agents' thing now that I think about it too.

Overall, it seems like a very confused article. Important details are specified in the beginning without context and then the context is shoved into the addendum in the kind of forced manner that resembles  is actually really cool, and the idea of some organization purposefully trying to sabotage the Foundation from within also cool. But its badly organized right now and me liking for what you were trying to do is outweighed by my dislike of how you did it.

Living the dream, or dreaming the life?
