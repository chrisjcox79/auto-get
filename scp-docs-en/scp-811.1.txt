EDIT, 2015:  Although this edit has nothing to do with the preceding post or the person who made it, I don't have any posts on this thread higher up than this one, and I'd like for this message to get as much visibility as possible.

Obviously, anyone can make any kind of SCP-811-derivative work they wish to, so long as they abide by the Creative Commons license. However, I, the author, am personally uncomfortable with certain kinds of fan work and do not wish to see them. Please do not send me (Pig_catapult) links to any content sexualising SCP-811 in any way. 

For a non-hypothetical example: Linking me to a [website redacted] group dedicated to compiling SCP-811 fanart: Good! I get warm fuzzies seeing that people like SCP-811 enough to draw her. Linking me to a [website redacted] group dedicated to compiling SCP-811 fanart, with an emphasis on SCP-811's buttocks stated in the rules: Not good! That creeps me the fuck out. Please don't do it.

Edit 2016:  A quick note to translators: ) with smaller words that she does know, and should probably not be translated literally.

Edit 2017:  I've made Aoshi's performance as Dr. Trebuchet's voice in this dramatic reading of the interview log canon. That is how Dr. Trebuchet sounds.

Edit also 2017:  To elaborate on Edit 2015: Due to issues of both communication difficulties and emotional immaturity, SCP-811 is not capable of giving informed consent to sexual activities. 

Edit 2018:  Since I can think of three questions that have been asked frequently, on- and off-site, about this article, I've decided to add an
FAQ

Q: Why is Watchel such a wuss? That's a pretty tame transformation/cannibalism description. 

A: What turned Watchel's stomach wasn't the description in and of itself, but the full sequence of events implied by that description: A little girl is kidnapped by a man with a syringe, goes through a painful transformation in which she pukes out huge quantities of blood and some of her organs, is introduced to her new hands and new Horror Hunger by killing that man in her terror, and then, after either not seeking out help from a responsible adult or seeking help and failing to receive it, that little girl goes and lives alone as a cryptid monster in a swamp until the Foundation finds her, as a developmentally-impaired adult who at some point along the way stopped considering herself a person. In my mind, when Watchel went home that night, after what was his first time interviewing a humanoid SCP, he hugged his young daughter for a long time and cried. She's about the right age and has long dark hair.

Q: Is SCP-811 based on X? 

A: Unless X is  no.

Q: How do you pronounce ? 

A: Ah-eh.

I think they call her Ae because she simply can't comprehend being called a number. Surely, they would refer to it as SCP-811 in a professional setting, but when speaking with SCP-811, it would be best to refer to her as AE.
附加记录：    

^This is exactly it. Communication with 811 is difficult enough as it is, so calling her "Aé" when speaking to her streamlines out a lot of time and confusion as she tries to get you to understand that no, her name's Aé, ah-eh, how did you get "atey-leven" out of that? She doesn't know what "leven" is, but she knows what "ate" means. Are you making fun of her because of the way she eats? The fact that it's actually the numbers "eight eleven" go right over her head, because that doesn't make sense at all to her. Numbers are for counting, not for names, so why would they be calling her a number?

She's intelligent, sure, but she lacks the frame of reference that we pretty much take for granted because she grew up alone in a swamp, and she can't speak well for the same reason. Circumstances-wise, she's essentially the equivalent of a now-adult feral child who was abandoned at a young age (somewhere in the range of 4-7 years old), but not at birth.

Of course, when talking about her and not to her, she's SCP-811 all the time.
