I thoroughly enjoyed the slow-burn twist. Predictable, but that heightened the tension. The dialogue is good, utilitarian, gets the job done. The technical writing leaves a lot to be desired.

in an isolated forest of Tacoma, Washington
附加记录：    

Why wouldn't the Foundation mention what forest?

The South-facing bedroom
附加记录：    

Unnecessary capitalization.

enter the space, rather, it manifests
附加记录：    

enter the space;  rather, it manifests

I also have no idea why you made the primary anomaly the house and not the actual time travel room, considering the rest of the house is nonanomalous. I would've gone so far as to make the primary anomaly a temporal anomaly inside the room but even making it just the room would help. As it is, you've got a completely unnecessary subdesignation in the article.

Of course, these are all technical nitpicks, and the story is respectable enough to warrant an upvote. Nice work.
