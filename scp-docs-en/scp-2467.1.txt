edit: More detailed crit after a SPaG comb-through. I like the idea: it's creepy and doesn't feel too derivative of on- or off-wiki material — which is surprising given that, to my mind, this kind of amalgam ghost ship is a centuries-old trope (SS Moloch anyone?). I think this is possibly due to the more modern elements you've included and the addition of the mind-affecting anomaly — either way, well done!

+block

–block

In terms of the writing, this feels very much like something you pushed out all at once without much proofreading, as evidenced by the number of SPaG corrections I made. (Sorry!) I'm actually quite impressed that you didn't do much checking over, because the story structure is pretty good: you've presented the information in a nice narrative order, and there aren't any sections that feel especially weaker than the others. And shaggydredlocks has taken the words right out of my mouth re: detail. But if you zoom onto the sentence level, some parts come across a bit awkward or breathless:

This is believed to be a form of mind-affecting anomaly, evidenced by any person coming into contact or close proximity with SCP-2467 for an extended period of time (3-10 days depending on the individual) comes to believe the SCP-2467 is correct in its actions, and will attempt to aid it however they are able.
附加记录：    

This is just ungrammatical, I'm afraid. I didn't want to edit this because then I'd inevitably be imposing my own style upon it, and this kind of phrasing should be totally up to the author. I'd recommend trying to express this idea more succinctly, or just splitting it up into separate sentences.

In an effort to better understand SCP-2467, particularly effects and any notable events occurring on board the vessel, four D-Class personnel trained in sailing (D-2467-01 - 04) were provided with a small (9 metre) yacht launched from the SCPS Sunderland and given orders to sail north with the SCPS Sunderland tailing them at a distance of 7 kilometres with the intention of them being boarded and abducted by SCP-2467 to observe the actives of SCP-2467 and its effects on those aboard.
附加记录：    

My internal voice ran out of air by the end of that! Sprinkle in some commas, at minimum, or break this apart into, like, five sentences. :P There are a couple of other sections with similar problems. (Additionally, that .

Foundation satellites are to constantly track the movement of SCP-2467, with plants in the meteorological bureau to divert traffic from areas likely to be visited by SCP-2467.
附加记录：    

Double  there. Repetition of structure or words crops up now and then throughout the piece, and should be rephrased when possible.

Please collapse long posts. ~Zyn

Overall verdict: good idea executed with good storytelling ability, which brings me very close to upvoting, but too much of the wording is too clumsy for me to do so right now. Go over this sentence by sentence and patch those minor issues up, and I'll happily +1.

edit 2: one more thing I forgot to mention: the non-D-class folks on the ship seem an integral part of this anomaly, and an interesting one, so you could definitely spend more time talking about them.
