I read a book (The Ghost Map, it's really good, if you're wondering) about a cholera epidemic in London in the 1850s. That book describes the impact that miasma had on public containment efforts of disease, namely keeping people from focusing on contaminated water and making them be obsessed with  So based on that, miasma was still taken seriously in public.

That said, there is an argument that the ASCI (for all of its many, many, MANY flaws) would still have been more scientifically advanced than the rest of the country, so they may have been less into the theory than I'm describing.

The way I see it (headcanonically), the Foundation would have needed vast amounts of wealth to operate in any time. That means its central membership would be upper-class white men. This is, in a realistic sense, the sort of things upper-class white 19th century men believed in, by and large.

I'm from alabama, and I've never heard anyone say that but douchey hipsters being ironic.

Okay, so.

This is damn well written, believable, and actually plausible for its period. That said, at no point does the page specify that it has been debunked outside the page title, and for that I must downvote.

You see, SCP-EX entries are SCPs that have been explained or rendered inert. While you know and I know that IRL no one believes this spew in the present age, the page never says that. Without a segment detailing how and why it was proved incorrect, this page stands alone in its canon as an SCP type article saying that black people are inferior in the most blunt and ignorant way possible, and I don't think that's okay. Poor taste aside, the way this article is formatted implies that the Foundation continues to believe this.

Add a disclaimer, in character, not out, and it will be a -EX. As it stands, it's just racism as an SCP, and my downvote reflects that.
