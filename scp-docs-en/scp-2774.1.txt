I tend on the side of adding footnotes to explain stuff when necessary. The whole point is to keep the flow of the article going. If the person has to google a term, they've distracted themselves from the immersion of the article.

Besides, this isn't a super scientific document where everyone involved is a scientist who knows what all the terms mean, an SP article is more of a summary of the object that can be given to starting researchers, security guards, etc.

EDIT: Just to be clear, I'm saying I think you should keep the footnote.

i will defend this hill to the death

03 Feb 2016 06:34

1) I find needless exposition significantly more immersion breaking than having to google a word.

2) If it's intended for a layman audience, use layman terms. From an in-character perspective it's completely pointless to have both, since you're just saying the same thing twice, with nothing gained. Going back to the medical note comparison, if I'm writing something to give to a non-medical person (like a soldier's commander), I'll just use the layman term instead of trying to teach a grunt a new SAT word. From an out-of-character perspective it's equally pointless. The reading experience is not enhanced by having both, or harmed by just having the common term. It's basic prose writing- don't use two words when one will do.
