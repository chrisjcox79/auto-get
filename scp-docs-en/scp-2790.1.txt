Let's see how this one goes. This was a tricky one to write. I tried making other changes to it past this point, but those left me unsatisfied. Squids have blood; it's just that their blood, like that of many invertebrates, contains hemocyanin (a copper-based oxygen transport molecule) instead of hemoglobin. This is because at the temperature and depth at which many of these invertebrates live, hemocyanin is more efficient than hemoglobin.

Fold

11 Oct 2015 05:39

When we're going for something that not only is hazardous enough to infect a whole site, but does infect a whole site, it better be at a level of OTT enough to do so. SCP-2690 does this via religious means (though I wasn't much of a fan otherwise), and 1561 piled it on as the article went on.

The goofy lovey-dovey tone doesn't do it favors, because, and maybe it's me, once we see something so saccharine and sweet, our indicators go off and we sense something is fucked. In addition, we get the idea very soon that oh hey, they grafted its skin onto theirs. This isn't subtle, but tries to present it as gradual and subtle. And that does no favors for me, because this would be so much better if it were going the extra steps to make this OTT.

Also, as a final note, there's no reason to not neutralize it. Say what you want about the Foundation; this is a large-scale containment breach, and it doesn't take much for this to potentially spill out into something far more dangerous. 2690 uses the religious motivators as a means to not neutralize this, and 1561 well, they want to, but can't. But there's no other reasons here. And any excuse for the Foundation to not be aware of it is dashed by the note at the end (and nothing to indicate they took care of the problem), which breaks my disbelief.

The way this is laid out hurts my suspension of disbelief. Most format screws of this type tend to have the explanation for why it is the way it is at the beginning, because that helps with the impression that this is a professional document with an actual purpose.
