I don't care for this one. I don't like the expungement at all (why do you need to redact the last numeral of the year this appeared? Or the edition of textbooks this appears in, since you've already blackboxed the title?), the fact that someone has to go looking for a specific blackboxed doctor to test this thing, or the latest iteration of .

The item itself doesn't grab me conceptually, either. So something is harvesting brain tissue from people who pass the math problem intelligence test. Why does it bother sending back scary mangled corpses with cliched  messages? Wouldn't that be completely counterproductive? It obviously wants more people to read the calculus problem, which is probably not going to happen if the textbook happens to be next to some crazy cubified corpse thing. The whole thing feels completely tacked-on for the purposes of horror.

The test logs also feel like filler, and don't really tell us anything that we didn't already get from the article other than that whatever it is that's processing these people doesn't like it when you send them the non-mathematically inclined.

23 Dec 2013 18:41

For the year, I admit that I wasn't really sure which year of the 60s to put it in, and the foundation tends to expunge dates and names. Dr. A is the person in charge of the project, but I guess that portion isn't really all that important.

I can get rid of the SCP division, since honestly that's not particularly important and it'll probably read better without it. I'll do that right now, actually.

The idea behind it sending back corpses and them having messages on them is that it implies they're meant for someone. Obviously the problems are getting into textbooks somehow, and someone's developing more of them. In the , so to speak, there are probably agents of another party who these are intended for, and they could possibly be covering up the situation. The other party may not have anything to do with the pieces, or they don't want to remove too much important material, or they feel it's contamination of the world or time line or dimension to just remove a whole body, or something.

The text logs are mostly just to prove what's stated in the description, since I got several questions of  or I could show tests that explain exactly why it works. These essentially allow us to prove some statements, with explanations for why this tells us these things.

At least part of it is that people liked it better when it was more mysterious; when you explain something like this too much, it's even less interesting than it is now.
