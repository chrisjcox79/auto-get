Can I suggest a few grammatical/structural changes that might make the early part of the article flow better?

+Open

-Close

Description:  SCP-5450 is a recipe known as shark fin soup, which manifests anomalous properties when produced from sharks within the Shanghai region of China. Selachians within this area, now designated SCP-5450-1, are biologically indistinguishable from normal sharks barring the presence of a pair of lungs. The anomalous property of SCP-5450 manifests upon consumption, causing the subject to transform into a species of selachian. Following transformation, the subject will find its way to a body of water via currently unknown means. A small number of cases have been observed to move their fins in a manner similar to walking. Testing has been preemptively denied by Site Director Jorge Clark as the effects of SCP-5450 are well documented and apparent.

Discovery:  SCP-5450 was discovered when several families went missing in Shanghai followed by several reports of walking sharks online. Witnesses to instances of SCP-5450-1 were promptly amnesticized. As of 06/07/2019, there are estimated to be 452 instances of SCP-5450-1, making them an endangered species.

Also, they wouldn't really be an endangered species, because that's a designation usually given by a governmental organization, which the Foundation isn't.
