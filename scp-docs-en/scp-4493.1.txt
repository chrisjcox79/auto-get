24 Jun 2019 18:07

I commented on the story, but I'm commenting here to say that this is still really cool, at least where it comes to 'meting out justice like you get to decide what other people get to know about who they're consuming from' and 'pulling back the curtain behind corporations with massive human rights violations' (i.e. almost all of them, probably). It'sunfortunate that the Foundation wanted to try and stop it, but it WAS anomalous, technically speaking! Mind you, so are many of Marshall, Carter and Dark's dealings, but if I remember right the Foundation raids them anyway.

Something I feel like you could emphasise a *little* bit more is the fridge horror with the potential information breach - at least ONE of the SCPs will be LGBT+ in some way (like the one who exudes that drug that makes people slaves to him, and is very apologetic about it)! What happens when the Foundation staff's twitter accounts start posting pride month stuff? What happens when the government does it?? Not that they've done, or will do, that anytime soon thanks to Trump's government, though. This could go really bad, AND none of the employees can celebrate pride, which I'm going to measure as about the same level of awful.

This is really interesting, though, and it's a shame it's so short, I feel - it could do with a bit more expounding upon, even if I'm not sure how. Presumably it has a horrific effect on the economy for example, or corporations eventually stop posting about pride altogether - thus limiting visibility!

It's really sad how even this solution has unmitigated potential negative effects, because it's the system people live under that's the problem, not the individual actions. Whether or not you include it is obvs up to you, but it feels as though the negative effects of it, or events in general, could be emphasised here. At the very least, it's a fucking bureaucratic/administrative nightmare, right?

Anyway, +1 from me :D
