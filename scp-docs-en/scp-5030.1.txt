However, this life is less noticeable and less successful, has more obstacles, never becomes a scientist, that is, is a ) and erase the memory of those who have the fewest memories (the Beta) because they received the story second hand.

+explain

–block

I preferred to leave certain ambiguous elements in the article, both because of the element of mystery associated with the contest and because of personal taste. I like articles that can be interpreted in different ways by the reader.

What the Foundation initially takes, based on the evidence it has, as a memetic infection, turns out to be something else. There is certainly a memetic element, the anomalous origin of memories, but those memories have a tangible origin. In one version of reality, Bolívar/Bolivien is a researcher at the Foundation and dies as a result. However, something decides to give him a second chance, but in exchange for living a much less remarkable life, and that is the life Bolivien has. For more details, please see the answer to ModernMajorGeneral.

Secondly; there were some minor spelling/grammar issues that broke the SCP tone for me; a look over by a kind editor would fix this.

This sentence is a long chain of relative clauses that require the reader to keep several nested comparisons in mind while reading it. As Andrés Bolívar has not been introduced yet, . Lots of things can have registrations (e.g. cars) but only people are in personnel databases.

Thanks for your reading ImperiumRomanun.

+article

-explain

In my original idea, the phenomenon was due to a reality restructuring event. In the original timeline, there was a researcher named Andres Bolivar who, due to actions beyond his duties, died during a containment gap. However, . First, it seeks to distort Bolivar's memories, so that he is forgotten, and since this is not effective, it eliminates the subjects who have the most memories (who are the ones who initiate the dissemination of history) and induces amnesia in those who heard it, in the period corresponding to when the incident occurred (similar to a computer registry cleanup)

As the Foundation does not know these antecedents, it takes the phenomenon as a conventional memetic infection, especially virulent and transmissible through the story, with certain dangerous cognitive elements, which is why it takes the measures it considers appropriate.

However, the interpretation changes when Bolivien appears, who shares the characteristics described for Bolívar, and there the questions arise: What is his relationship with the phenomenon? Is he perhaps Bolivar? Why is he there?

The differences in the name of the Junior Researcher and the Class D are due to the way memory works, it tends to be inaccurate, and at the same time to the language differences of the staff of Site-34 (they are Spanish speaking, so the name and surname sounded like )

One interpretation given by several readers that I liked a lot is that the phenomenon is due to the unfulfilled dreams and aspirations of Bolivien, who by becoming a Class D without hope of freedom or a better life, dreams of a new life in a faraway place, far from crime and violence, being someone respectable. And because as a child he wanted to be a scientist, that form took on its aspiration.
