Skeleton Coins!

15 Apr 2019 14:44

I know you said this wasn't too important, but I'd like to break down the story told in these two possible iterations of the document. There are two immediately noticeable narratives: the quarter narrative and the nickel narrative

Nickel: 

An anomalous item (nickel) becomes officially designated after Janitor Rodriguez inadvertently discovers its extra effect, Rodriguez gets promoted to researcher in the process
An unnamed subject (who we can assume is Rodriguez) tests with the nickel and creates a new anomalous item (previously non-anomalous quarter) in the process
Rogers conducts rest of testing and neutralizes the nickel in the process, making the nickel non-anomalous

Quarter: 

An anomalous item (quarter) becomes officially designated after Janitor Rogers inadvertently discovers its extra effect, Rogers gets promoted to researcher in the process
An unnamed subject (who we can assume is Rogers) tests with the quarter and creates a new anomalous item (previously non-anomalous nickel) in the process
Rodriguez conducts rest of testing and neutralizes the quarter in the process, making the quarter non-anomalous

At first, this doesn't make sense. The quarter can only be made anomalous when the nickel is designated, and vice versa. However, I propose three possible interpretations:

+interpretations

-interpretations

The documents take place in separate universes. This is the easiest explanation, but also the lamest.
The quarter mentioned in the nickel narrative isn't necessarily the quarter mentioned in the quarter narrative. This, again, is an easy explanation, but a bit lame as well, unless taken to its logical extreme where a lot more nickels and quarters are being made anomalous, designated, and neutralized.
The documents represent a loop. Let's think about this. Part of this is guessing in my part because we don't know who conducted tests 2-5, but here's my reordering of events:

Non-anomalous nickel is used in testing of designated quarter, non-anomalous nickel becomes anomalous and is added to LoAO, Researcher Rogers (who I believe is in charge of testing for quarter) is probably demoted, perhaps to a janitor role.
Rodriguez becomes researcher in charge of quarter testing, neutralizes quarter in the process and quarter becomes non-anomalous
Nickel is discovered to be designation worthy, Rodriguez is promoted in the process from janitor to researcher
Now non-anomalous quarter is used in testing of designated nickel, non-anomalous quarter becomes anomalous and is added to LoAO, Researcher Rodriguez (who I believe is in charge of testing for nickel) is probably demoted, perhaps to a janitor role
Rogers becomes researcher in charge of nickel testing, neutralizes nickel in the process and nickel becomes non-anomalous
Quarter is discovered to be designation worthy, Rogers is promoted in the process from janitor to researcher.

Wash, rinse, repeat. It's an endless loop, nickel to quarter, Rigers to Rodriguez, heads to tails.

Hippo, you mad lad. +1
