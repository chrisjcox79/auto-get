I really like this idea. I was expecting it to be a  but I expected the control to be with the users mind. I like that it comes at great cost and that the strings themselves are breakable, it all seems like a great idea. Then the author starts throwing in things seemingly at random just to add to the mystery or whatever. You use your own muscle, for that matter why 93%? And what's that other 7%?. Then you also stop eating. Victims are incited towards becoming users by some inexplicably psychic force. Tendons get turned into super effective fiber optic cables I suppose but the actual field use of this bothers me, radio communication is effectively instantaneous and it doesn't require a hard connection so I'm confused as to what downside to radio/satellite communication is being overcome here at a 57% imrovement but there's also the decreased utility due to the hardware necessity.

All in all I love the idea but I think the author ran away with the need to make it unique
