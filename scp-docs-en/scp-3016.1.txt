SCP-3016 is finished!

20 May 2017 20:41

Hello!

This marks my first attempt to do a more narrative-based SCP, although the focus is still mostly on the object itself and its properties. I anticipate that it being uncontained at the time of writing might be divisive, but I wanted this to feel like a document as it appears only a few days after a breach. The idea of SCP files as  has always kinda interested me, and the thought of a file written just after the chaos of a breach seemed like fun.

To those of you who sent me critique, I thank you - and I apologize for not implementing some of it. I just don't have the writing chops to pull off some of the suggestions.

Let me know what you think, and thank you for reading!

Edit: Oh, and if anybody knows of any CC-compliant images of a silver spinning top, against a white background, I'd love to use one. Couldn't find any that weren't an actual product to buy.

22 May 2017 14:18

I like it!

+but

-but

Some of the execution still seems bit wonky to me though in particular, you are inconsistent with the verb tenses in the addenda. It's probably best to use past tense for everything in those EXCEPT the MTF log.

Also, and this is more minor, some of the wording in Addendum 2 feels unnatural to me. Like this exchange:

Bravo:  Jesus, that's bright. Command, this skip has managed to heat the air by… a lot. Guess there was a reason it was in a vacuum.

Delta:  My thermometer reads — Wow, it's… uh… melted, actually. We should probably head out pretty soon.
附加记录：    

I dunno, this just seems too casual? I think the last sentence from each of these agents is unnecessary and kind of obvious.

Also, some of the wording in the last three paragraphs of the article is kind of weird. For example, instead of

a full investigation by the Ethics Committee took place
附加记录：    

it would probably be better to keep this in the active voice There are a few other sentences in this section that could probably be cleaned up as well, to make it clearer/easier to read.

All of these are minor things, but definitely noticeable. Still, +1 from me for a quirky little anomaly and an interesting story that I don't think has been done yet.
