Notably, SCP-4589 cultures do not divide as normal cultures would, instead, they continue to grow steadily and develop pseudopods, limbs, and rudimentary sensory organs.
附加记录：    

The comma between  ought to be a semicolon (otherwise, this is a comma splice).

This didn't work for me. The core idea is neat, but the premise alone isn't strong enough to carry the execution. Unlike, say, your salsa hammer skip, there's more I need to see here to make it  you need to show me. Either directly (via logs) or indirectly (where we somehow explore its consequences or the people involved in it).

It reminds me of the second half of Xenogears. It's an old JRPG where they ran over budget mid-way through production  they literally sit down, look at the screen, and tell you what happened in the dungeons you never got to visit).

This can work when the content is a short, fun punchline or a compelling idea that stands on its own, but when it's like the image you're trying to evoke here, we need to experience it in a way that goes beyond a brief summary.
