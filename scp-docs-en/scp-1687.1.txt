I put  in quotes because it's not really a scientific term, but what else would you call the effect?

As for the list of organisms, I was trying to go for a representative cross-section of things that have been proven to respond both positively and negatively to music, and different forms of music. And yes, I think I recall seeing some studies that show that houseplant growth rates actually do vary with statistical significance depending on whether they're grown in silent environments, environments with classical music (I think it was Bach?), and environments with rock/heavy metal.

A more technical description might be something along the lines of 

If the sound produced by affected instruments is acoustically identical to non-affected instruments, does the violin change the sound (or affect some non-acoustic property), or just the perception of that instrument's sound?

Also, is the quality-draining effect on other instruments permanent?

I'm not sure about the plant testing. Yes, plants are supposed to react differently to Bach than to heavy metal, but that's likely a result of the physical effect of the sound waves themselves, not an inherent quality of the type of music, so if this thing doesn't affect the sound waves themselves, it should have the same effect on a plant that a normal instrument would.
