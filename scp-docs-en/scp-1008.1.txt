A very very belated clarification, as looking back on some of the comments in this thread, there may be some confusion as to how this works or the religious implications behind it.

Imagine that you were a member of an ancient tribe of sun-worshippers. As punishment for a serious crime against the tribe, the shaman would curse you to never draw warmth from the sun (their god) again, effectively exiling you from the source of all life. Over time, all that lives by the sun's power shuns you, until you die cold and alone.

I'd say Aelanna's summary is spot-on, but as times have changed, I think this should be an almost fatal curse. We now know that life, as advanced as the phylum Chordata even, exists in the deep sea, a place where sunlight is completely blotted out. And warmth can be derived through natural hot springs/the earth's mantle. Could someone with this curse not survive through the means of very special containment procedures? Completely changing someone's way of life to exclude everything derived from the sun would have interesting psychological effects no doubt.

This body holding me reminds me of my own mortality.

Embrace this moment. Remember. We are eternal.
