Re: It Wont Stop Screaming

01 Aug 2018 13:13

Thank you so much, that's very kind of you to say! I'm delighted you enjoyed it.

+Block

-Close

1) Re: The title. Yeah, that's totally understandable. 'It Won't Stop Screaming' was the initial name I began with when I first came up with the idea. At that point my intention was to focus much more heavily on SCP-4000 and its psychological damage, with more in-depth physical and psychological tests and with the future mystery parts as the flavour surrounding it. In the end, that didn't quite end up working and the mystery became the main focal point of the text with only two references to SCP-4000 constant screaming remaining. However it was pretty close to the deadline by the time I finished and I couldn't think of anything else to call it, so I left the name.

I'm happy to edit it into something a little less edgy if anyone has any good suggestions.

2) Re: Redaction and Expunging. Thank you for your feedback, that's most helpful.

I do indeed know every piece of information I've concealed in this document (with the exception of dates) including what every part of SCP-4000-B originally said so that all the odd letters would be in the right places. But a problem with that is that since I do know everything I don't get to experience what it's like coming to the document without that knowledge.

Part of the frustration at the end is intentional, although my aim was to make it interesting rather than off putting. One of the major focuses of the document is the uncomfortable feeling of knowing that something is coming, but not quite being able to use that knowledge to do anything. The Foundation is teased with knowledge they can't get access to and which might end up being part of a stable time loop, and I wanted to give the reader a little bit of that feeling too, to give them a kind of cliffhanger.

While I was writing this entry my mother got some not very good medical news, so the frustration of 'an unpleasant future you can't do anything to affect and just have to wait for' was very much on my mind.

I should also probably mention that, while it might be a little self-indulgent for my first SCP, part of SCP-4000-B is also foreshadowing. I have a pretty shrewd idea of how Dr. Lecroy ends up where he does and what happens to him on the way, and I'm intending to reveal that story through a number of other SCPs and quite possibly a Tale or two. If I keep to plan - and of course if my work goes down well enough for people to want to see more - every part of B should be explained at some point.

However, that's definitely not an excuse for anything being unclear or off-putting. Any SCP should be able to stand by itself and making it interesting an appealing by itself is more important than lore building.

I can't really change the [DATA EXPUNGED] in Carter's second report because it would end up referencing something that wasn't very clear to the reader anyway, and if I elaborated further it would probably end up feeling like I'd gone off on a complete tangent since it doesn't have a clear link to SCP-4000's condition.

However, given that two people have found the redaction at the very end confusing I'm considering removing it, or at least part of it.

The reason I've picked on Site-41 is that I found it to be the most enjoyably inconsistent, canon-conflicting Foundation facility of all the sites I looked at. I was searching out if any Sites had a particular theme to them (like 17's connection to psychology, which is why I put SCP-4000 there) when I came upon some references to Site-41.

So far, I have discovered that it is in a frigid arctic landscape and has an SCP containment greenhouse. It's home to a major operations team within the Foundation and is also virtually devoid of people and partly abandoned. It been abandoned and replaced with Site-41-B, and also destroyed in a completely different way. It was constructed SCP-567 and also is SCP-1312. It is located in Japan, and also located in Leningrad during WWII.

I know there isn't just one Foundation canon, and that differences can also be put down to 'misinformation' but Site-41 attracted me as being almost intentionally chaotic. I thought it merited a reason for its many, many contradictions. And that is, currently, what I believe the final redaction covers. If I were going to remove it, I'd either take it out all together or change it to a comment about how data on Site-41 is confusing to ascertain but that official Foundation records state that the current Site-41 is indeed the only one there has ever been.

I'll leave it for the moment, but if anyone else has any feedback on the ending and thinks changing it would be a good idea, please let me know. 

Thank you.

An Update

03 Aug 2018 00:59

Update – 02.08.2018 

Thanks to everyone who's provided their thoughts and comments since the original posting.

+Explanation

-Explanation

So, due to pretty consistent feedback I’ve spent the last couple of hours tinkering with Addendum 2. I’ve come up with a version which I think improves on the original in several areas, but ultimately I’ve reached a slightly more radical conclusion.

I think it might be to the benefit of the SCP to simply remove Addendum 2 altogether. Even my revised version doesn’t really do what people seem to want of it. The last section of an SCP is often the ‘punch’ and while Addendum 2 was not intended to be this, having it as the ending seems to be diffusing the intrigue generated by the main article rather than acting as the ‘I want to learn more’ cliff-hanger I intended it to be.

Moreover, Addendum 2 does not initially appear to be particularly relevant to the majority of this SCP. While it is set up by an earlier section of the document, it doesn’t really elaborate on anything further and instead just creates more questions. Which can absolutely work – creating frustrating unanswered questions is still a major theme of this SCP – but given that Addendum 1 also does this, having it again in Addendum 2, and with far less context, is perhaps not a very effective feature.

In creating Addendum 2, I think I was working by what I considered interesting in the overall narrative I’m trying to create rather than focusing on what is important to this specific piece of writing.

So, I’m removing Addendum 2 for now. I do still have both the original and the updated version if people feel this isn’t a change for the better, or that some part of Addendum 2 is worth salvaging. Opinions on the matter are very welcome. 

In order not to completely lose my Site-41 breadcrumb dropping, I’m making a small addition to Addendum 1 in its place. A full log of changes is below.

+Changes

-Changes

Addendum 2 has been removed.
An additional reference to Site-41 has been added to SCP-4000-B
A comment on this reference, and an already existing one, has been added to Carter’s second report, essentially conveying what would have replaced the final redacted line in the previous version.
The [DATA EXPUNGED] used to conceal the identity of the individual prohibiting a line of investigation has been changed to O5-██ to give a slightly bigger hint at what is not being investigated.
The line “I hardly need to spell out the importance of the information it could potentially provide to the Foundation” has been removed for being clichéd and a bit trite.
Half a dozen sentences have received minor tweaks to make them scan a little better.

As always, feedback is welcome and appreciated.
