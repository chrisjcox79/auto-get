Fold

03 Feb 2019 21:19

All right, I need to address this: I hate the nu-SCP theme. I can appreciate wanting to make the site more accessible, but this theme is aesthetically and functionally flawed, for a few reasons:

The color that links change to when they're clicked on is already hard for me to see on my devices, and this somehow makes it worse.
It isn't immediately evident that the sidebar is meant to scroll on desktops and laptops; I legitimately thought the red bar that acts as the scroll indicator (or whatever the proper name is) was just to highlight the top few things. It's worse on smaller screens, like laptop monitors.
The fact that the dropdown lists at the top are translucent is really not a good thing, especially the canon and GOI tabs. Like, what's the point of making them see through? It just serves to make them harder to read.
It's overall too brutalist. I realize that the Foundation doesn't have the most cuddly aesthetic, but making everything hard corners is just off-putting. And the colors suck; I get it, simple color scheme is easier for colorblind people to read, points for that, but to someone who has good color vision, even adjusting the angle of a screen slightly can make the entire thing look like a massive grey mess. Plus, the deep reds clash with pretty much any other color you could put on the page, including colored photographs.

There are some things I like about it, but even those have problems; the idea of linking to translations in the sidebar is cool, but are we expected to put those in ourselves? If so, how? And the more compact tabs at the top are an amazing idea, but again, as-is, they're poorly implemented.

As it stands, this theme needs a lot of work, and I can't really support it.
