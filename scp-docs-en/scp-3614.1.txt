The ink flows down into a dark puddle

How can I write love into reality?

If I can't hear the sound of your heartbeat

What do you call love in your reality?

And in your reality, if I don't know how to love you

I'll leave you be

 by Dan Salvato
附加记录：    

Time to put that perfect SAT Writing score and that 96% in 1st semester AP English Lang to good use.

Apologies for flexing. I digress.

This is my first SCP, and I'm pumped to see how it's received on the mainlist. It's been quite a wild ride writing this thing. I've been working on this since December, and it's gone from a rather basic article to something a lot more complex. It initially started out as a Cliche-Con entry, so that's where the general idea comes from, but I didn't get it posted in time due to other reasons. Also I got a shitload of crit on this, so I really ended up taking my damn time posting this thing.

Thanks to:

u/Whoevers on Reddit for being the inspiration for the basic premise in this short story,

rain_v for helping me solidify my premise in the Ideas and Brainstorming forums,

Rounderhouse for assorted feedback, both in the forms and over PM.

You know, I kind of felt the same way when writing this. Something kept nagging at me to take a more ambitious execution, especially since the concept lends itself so easily to many ways of storytelling. Granted, this was my first SCP, so my lack of confidence in my own writing ability led me to take a more conservative approach.

On the other hand, I do feel that the straightforward nature of the reveal at the end does serve a purpose in the narrative. The note addressed to our universe is not just a straight-up explanation of the nature of 3614, but it also serves to illustrate the coldness of the Prime Foundation. To them, it seems like an act of sincerity. Yet to us, the ones being contained, it can almost be considered an act of cruelty to provide us this information and twist the knife in the wound, telling us that we are the anomaly, that everyone else is scared of us, that we are destined for eternal strife. It's a subversion on the relationship between Foundation and anomaly. Nevertheless, you are welcome to agree or disagree with my viewpoint.

In hindsight, I would have probably done a little more with this idea. Be on the lookout for more ballsy works by me.
