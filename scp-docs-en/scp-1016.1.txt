20 Nov 2011 00:27

Experiments involving autonomously-powered video surveillance of the interior show the video monitoring devices to malfunction as power is restored, in a manner consistent with manual tampering; batteries are removed, lenses covered, or power is turned off.
附加记录：    

First, a word-choice nitpick: I think it should be .

Substantial criticism: what you've done to keep what's happening inside mysterious only works for observers that are inside the house being unlocked. For example, if the Foundation set up an experiment with a house with uncovered windows in every wall, then both recording equipment and watching humans outside the house could see what happened. The SCP could disable all of the outside recording devices, but that still leaves the problem of the observing humans. Some possibilities:

Right when the door is unlocked all of the outside observers sneeze, are distracted by anything, or whatever, and don't see what happened, with all of the outside recording devices having been disabled during the sneeze.
Right when the door in unlocked all of the outside observers are teleported to inside of the house and murdered, along with anyone observing the observers, anyone observing them, and so forth.
Right when the door is unlocked blinds are dropped/drawn over the windows (if there weren't any blinds in the house before, there are now). None of the outside video recording devices have been disabled. However, reviews of the recording show that the blinds weren't pulled when the door was unlocked, but several hours before, with audio recordings of the observers saying things like  (voice analysis shows the voices to be an exact match for the voices for the observers). The recordings also show the observers spending several hours trying to gain access to the house, but somehow failing. The observers all swear up and down that nothing like that happened.
The observers are in voice-only contact with someone who isn't at or near the house, with communications happening for several hours before the house is unlocked. Right when the door is unlocked communication is briefly interrupted. When communication is restored the observers don't remember communicating with the other person for the span of several hours, but instead insist that they all fell unconscious several hours ago and are now just waking up. Inspection of the external video recorders shows footage of the observers all falling to the ground unconscious, shortly after which the recorders are all disabled. Further, immediate medical inspection of the bruises the observers have show them all being several hours old.

I'm a little tardy to the party, but I have a fifth scenario.

-Everything is normal and unremarkable until key is inserted. When key is inserted, lights dim but everything else is observable and normal. Once the unlocked door clears the frame, the room is suddenly, and without transistion, a crime scene. In the recordings, there is simply a jump, like bad editing, but of course no evidence of editing or tampering with the footage can be found.

My thinking is that the effect only occurs at that particular instant, so there is nothing untoward to observe externaly until that instant. Either the effect just transmutes the interior into evidence that seems several hours old, or the effect is retro-active in time but constrained to the unlocked area.
