Fold

Locked box test

07 Jan 2011 06:22

Whether it passes the locked box test for Safe hinges on whether you should account for a bug crawling into the box through the keyhole, and whether the plot of soil is genuinely obtainable to lock away.

However containers which seal off outside matter are almost trivial to make in modern day. Such technology was available to Louis Pasteur in 1668 and 1859 respectively, which they used to such a degree of success they were great contributions to disproving spontaneous generation.

That considered, so long as the soil can actually be extracted from the rest of the earth and locked away, I'm of the belief it should be classified Safe.

So the plot grows humans or scary animals into monsters, but a puppy grows into a nice dog instead of, for example, some kind of wolf thing? And plants just grow normally, but faster? Seems inconsistent to me.

Containment should be updated to keep the soil behind a sealed door, with the chamber constantly disinfected. Super-diseases will grow otherwise.
