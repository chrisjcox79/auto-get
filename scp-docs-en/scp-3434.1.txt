We die in the darkness, so that you may live in the light Yeah, right!
附加记录：    

This is my SCP-3000 proposal for this contest.

Firstly, allow me to express thanks to various reviewers like Gog33, Dr-Prentice, Bluemorpho, GibberingEloquence, LordStonefish, Targellon. Triple thanks to both psul and Petrograd, who took the trouble of looking at it thrice.

Image by Tormod Sandtrov of Flickr (https://www.flickr.com/photos/tormods/3841034148) under CC BY-SA 2.0; changes made include the addition of blackboxes

This article also involves the following:

Church of the Broken God hub by FortuneFavorsBold
SCP-428 (The Crowd) by DrScooter
SCP-343 () by Unknown Author
I Thought You Died Alone by A Random Day

And as for spoilers,

+block

–block

The Foundation's position re: the instances is that they are Foundation members who died violently in-line of duty. Keyword is , i.e. not the reality of it.

The instances definitely remember how they die, and that becomes their trauma even into their next lives.

Also, check the death date for -5 and birth date for -48. It's in dd/mm/yyyy formatting.

But all of them being related to other skips was disappointing for reasons I can't quite explain. It felt like a rug-pull somehow.
附加记录：    

I won't necessarily call it a rug-pull. Besides, two scenarios do not directly involve SCPs (and instead, other ways one can be KIA). Relating the deaths to other articles, I feel, expresses a message. These characters died for a greater good, to save the many by offering themselves. And in return, they suffer pain (forever).

And in the words of Monty Python's Life of Brian, while it is nice to be saved by a Messiah, being nailed onto a piece of wood and left to die is not good for the person being nailed.

Besides, it provides something to be said across the setting and this is the scale that is most deserving of this article's circumstances.

EDIT: Mind you, I can indeed conjure  scenarios (like the first and last one). However, I do not feel that the impact will be as resonating if all scenarios are things that a reader has no idea of. And frankly, the only reason why the first and last scenario is original is because I could not find a suitable death set in the early 1900s.

25 Mar 2017 15:03

Going to have to agree with TL333s; I was hyped going through the main body of the article, but my curiosity evaporated with the addenda.

I can't say I like the twist that these are reincarnated Foundation operatives. It invites a bunch of difficult to answer questions about where exactly the line between straightforward death and traumatic reincarnation lies, such as 'what makes the Foundation special enough that only their members reincarnate', 'why do so many different, seemingly unconnected anomalies all cause reincarnation', 'why do such different traumas produce the same stereotyped set of reactions'. It sort of ends up implicitly suggesting a unified theory of anomalies, which is a suggestion I never really like. It also kind of reminded me of Bell Weather, which I think gets away with the same thing because it's not constrained by the emphasis on explanations which the SCP format sets up, and because it straightforwardly implies that all Foundation personnel who die in the line of duty are cursed. This seems to be suggesting that dying for the Foundation just happens to be so awful that it traumatises the soul permanently, and given the examples (death by Brokenite torturers, death by absorption, blown up by experimental weapons, getting wished out of existence) I don't buy that only Foundation people would come back, or would be traumatised so severely by those deaths that they're a special case.

The fact that this is the last part of the article hampered the delivery. It starts of so well, with a strong idea and concept which both inspires curiosity and is deeply messed up. The question of 'what could scare an unborn child into resisting birth' is a fascinating one and works as a really strong hook into the second part of the article, and going into the addenda I expected this to be an easy upvote. Unfortunately, it felt like I was being given a bunch of vignettes which were neither strong enough to stand alone nor coherent enough to improve when taken together. Having all the deaths be due to other SCPs seemed like a little bit of a cop-out. I had expected going in that there would be new material, but the content that's given is a handful regurgitated older articles that seem to have been picked at random, with no real dramatic development within or across them save for -5 and -48 being the same person. The short interview snippets just become clipped retellings of other stories that I couldn't get much out of.

25 Mar 2017 16:35

what makes the Foundation special enough that only their members reincarnate
附加记录：    

Technically, that would be 

'why do such different traumas produce the same stereotyped set of reactions'. It sort of ends up implicitly suggesting a unified theory of anomalies, which is a suggestion I never really like.
附加记录：    

This certainly has no attempt towards the unified theory. The instances are effectively having PTSD, and the second behaviour relates to it. Perhaps the first behaviour (to not be in the light) is more unusually uniform. But I would argue that it was the darkness and loss of consciousness that relieved them of whatever suffering they went through prior to death, and that's really the one universal factor among almost all instances.

I don't buy that only Foundation people would come back, or would be traumatised so severely by those deaths that they're a special case.
附加记录：    

Upon reflection, while the article does have the Foundation assume that the instances to be former Foundation personnel, the instances never explicitly declared that they are Foundation personnel (not to mention that not all instances are shown in the article). And besides, only one of the scenarios listed here can be tied directly to the Foundation (the nuke scenario). The rest can be decontextualised such that the person dying need not be Foundation personnel.

But whether the person is former Foundation member or not does not matter, for any instance might have important information regarding anomalies.

EDIT: In fact, the 1st and last scenario makes more sense if it is the Broken Church culling a heretical Ticker, instead of a Foundation member being ceremonially executed in a cruel and interesting way.
