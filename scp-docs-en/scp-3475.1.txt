The blunted mountains weep

As he died to make men holy

Let us die to make things cheap

And say the Mea Culpa, which you gradually forgot

Year by year

Month by month

Day by day

Thought by thought

02 Jun 2018 14:50

For me, this comes across as pretty profound in and of itself. I've never seen an SCP document that explores and  the Veil.

In their increasingly complex attempts to put a wall between what's normal and what isn't, the Foundation are now in a peculiar situation where the wall itself is becoming abnormal. I've never seen anything like this, and if something similar's been done, it doesn't stick out as much as this does.

The now-anomalous concept of the Veil is getting difficult for both civilians and Foundation personnel to pass through safely, and rubbing up against the Veil, either through Foundation personnel coming into contact with normalcy or vice versa, has the potential to cause strange side effects that must themselves be contained, which makes the Veil more anomalous and more difficult to handle, which causes more side effects, which must also be contained, and so on and so on, ad infinitum.

At some point, this leaning wall has to tip over. And what that entails, I have no idea.

But I'm dying to find out. +1

This is an ambitious entry but I'll say something on similar to TL333s. First off, this article shows the use of a thaumiel class anomaly which, unlike other thaumiels such as SCP-2000, is being constantly utilized to control the entire human population. While I don't think the Foundation using secretively implanted memes to alter people's memories and perception is not a bad concept (though slightly overused) the article does leave a few holes on how they did it. One topic I'm wondering is if a GoI such as the GOC or CI knows about this.

To make my point short: This is a creative and great concept though it sort of bugs me to not have it explored in depth more. Neutral vote.
