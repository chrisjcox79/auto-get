SCP-4106

04 Feb 2019 06:59

This is my first SCP. Many thanks to RockTeethMothEyes for critiques.

My goal was to introduce an ordinary anomaly (a bird that opens locks) and show how the Foundation's decreasing funding affects the containment of Safe/Euclid anomalies. I hope you liked it!

I took the two bird photos.

01 Mar 2019 12:12

The Keter reclassification was made before the Foundation realized what happened at Site 92. Instead of death of 4106, they thought it was a containment breach (the Foundation was in a very poor state that communication between sites is barely possible) and since Dr. Tsai, the only person 4106 isn't hostile towards can't contain it, containing 4106 would be a lot more difficult.

Of course they can just duct tape 4106 in its cage, but containing unimportant anomalies was not something the Foundation had the freedom to do after Protocol HOLOTHUROIDEA. Hence the Keter: Containing it would take a lot of effort and the Foundation won't do it anyway.

It wasn't tagged as Keter because it's considered neutralized judging from the note at the end of the article.

04 Dec 2019 19:51

I think the writing is on point in terms of clinical tone, and the bird concept has the potential to be an SCP. However, I don't necessarily think the proposed canon has potential, and if it does, I don't think it should be included as the second half of a long SCP article - maybe you could write a canon/tale and backlink it to your article?

Because the way it's written now, it's not just your article that should have these heavily revised procedures, but every article. Trying to introduce a full overhaul of the state of the Foundation, relationships with major governments and GoIs, as well as all containment procedures on all articles is technically possible, but I just don't see people going along with it.

This SCP is a pet bird that opens locks. In terms of upending our understanding of the Foundation and all containment procedures, there's not much to draw people in or convince them to accept this new canon. If you really do want to push the canon, maybe shorten it down. A lot of it seems like redundant writing, and from a narrative point of view, reading very extensive containment procedures that ended up being fairly irrelevant to the nature of the SCP itself was a bit of a let down. There might be a better way to get this idea across, like in a single, shorter addendum rather than the current format.
