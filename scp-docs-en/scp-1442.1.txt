I tried something like this once, with a malignant entity growing in the stock market. Called it . No idea how mechanically it would work. This is a solid and well written version of a similar concept. I like the sampling of its lines. That said, I don't buy that the Foundation is able to translate this, and more importantly, detect that this thing exists in the first place.

I seriously doubt that the Foundation has time to investigate all stock-market shenanigans, given that so many are described by completely mundane, but incredibly hard to detect things like computer error, human embezzlement, and what have you. The SEC and FINRA can't do it with almost 7000 people working on that, and that's all they do, and they aren't phenomenally burdened by secrecy. I like this as a thing that exists in the Foundation universe, but there's no way the Foundation could uncover this on their own.

Fold

01 Aug 2013 14:02

On the first point, I absolutely agree with you. I attempted to suggest it was picked up more or less by chance via an automated mechanism to make this more plausible, but it would be incredibly hard to identify that something like this was going on just from looking at stock trades. The biggest conceptual difficulty I had with this was working out how you'd be able to tell a corporation was sentient at all; the thing with the stock trades ended up being the least bad of several bad options.

As regards translation: technically, the messages are mostly in English and are merely encrypted/enciphered; they're not also in an unknown language. While it is impossible to translate an unknown language without some equivalent of the Rosetta stone, it IS possible to decipher a code, especially a simple one, based only on the assumption that it IS a code of some kind for an intelligible message in some known language. Whether that assumption was warranted or not is another question, though! :)
