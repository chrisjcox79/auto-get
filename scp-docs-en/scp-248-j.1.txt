Aww man. Thanks at least for taking a look at my article and rating it.

I wouldn't say the punchline is non-sequitur, though. There's several ways of interpreting what's happening here, and both of my own headcanons follow logically.

Theorymuch

–block

Junior Researcher Karen knows that the Foundation is a fictional entity, and she's fully aware that the actual Foundation site doesn't allow for copyrighted images. Thus, she desperately tries to make up a working non-copyrighted alternative, in accordance of the many similar tropes that SCP writers use, like using photoshopped images.

Theoryday

–block

The crux of this headcanon is that the article for SCP-248-J is just unable to have a picture due to propabilistic reasons, or that Jude is just extremely unlucky today. Karen's flubs are merely incompetence of her part.

When Jude wises up and delegates the task to someone else, the universe starts running out of tricks to prevent the image from being shown, so the universe pulls out the big guns and goes full-on SCP-3309 on her.
