If you're interested in my other work, here is my author page

I think it was 2013 when I discovered the SCP Foundation, and I was immediately entranced. After years of lurking, this is my first attempt at writing for the site. I would love to hear your thoughts.

Big thanks to Peter Dawley for giving me their feedback in chat.

The seed of the idea was when I was thinking about Gods of nature and how I didn't find them plausible. They were often too human, too sensible and kind to be manifestations of uncontrolled nature. Then I realized that there was one god of nature I'd read about who I thought fit the bill fantastically, even though he isn't called a  That god is Yaldabaoth.

Now I'm a firm believer that there is no canon, and you can interpret 3503 in any manner you wish, or no manner. But, here's what the humble author feels about it.

+Spoilers

-collapse

The wild god Baker encountered is, in some way, Pan. It is also, in some way, a manifestation of Yaldabaoth. How exactly this works out is up to your interpretation, dear reader.

SCP-3503, more broadly, is a manifestation of Yaldabaoth's hatred of Mekhane and her gift to the intelligent peoples of the universe.

This is why the Church of the Broken God kidnapped Baker. His experience is of theological interest to the church. He's off being dissected/experimented on right about now.

I hope y'all enjoy.

Yes, PTSD is something like an overload. Or, maybe better explained, a short-circuit. It isn't natural, and it isn't more real. It isn't even heightened senses. Fear causes heightened senses.

PTSD kind of freezes a part of the brain on the exact events that caused the trauma. The patient's constant anxiety isn't based on their current circumstances and the  actually gets in the way of adaptation to new circumstances. Includes delusions and hallucinations of some degree, and even patients that fully acknowledge it isn't real have a hard time functioning under such level of anxiety, or even directing such anxiety in a new way.
