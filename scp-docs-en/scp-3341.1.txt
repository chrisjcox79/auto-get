Thanks, Attribution, and Other Matters

02 Aug 2017 02:00

Allow me to express thanks to a variety of persons including: bittermixin, LordStonefish, Sterbai, DrChandra, among several others.

Image attribution is as follows:

Image by Zivya of Wikimedia Commons (https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Shalim.JPG) under CC BY-SA 3.0

References Attribution is as follows:

Draft Proposal For Programme Arduinosis 9001 by Roget
ORIA Hub by Eskobar and his team for the GoI contest

+block

–block

SCP-3341 is originally intended to be a meatsuit for the memetic entities called djinn (of ORIA hub), wherein the two instances would have joined to provide a physical medium for the djinn to operate, like a shell. However, their creation made them sapient and the djinn who made them let 3341 to live freely.

And thus, that anomalous species spread out in reasonable numbers.

I like this - I think it's actually a really good example of how to translate mythological and folkloric material into an SCP article well. It doesn't lean exclusively on the weirdness of the myth, and makes an effort to actively integrate them into the Foundation's world rather than completely isolate and decontextualise them, which is sadly the route a lot of similar articles have taken. I particularly like the stuff about the children's dictatorship, the Cogwork Church's theological involvement, and the lack of data on SCP-3341-C. +1
