20 Aug 2018 10:06

Thanks for the feedback!

The 001 pitch was an attempt to play with the rule of threes, and kinda give the skip a bit more depth than just feeling like it should've been a -J article. I also didn't want to feel like I'd just crammed as many references in as possible, and I almost felt like I'd done too many as-is: including 173 there are six, adding more feels like I'm basically making a glorified summary of various other SCPs without doing anything unique. That's also why I ended up keeping the last conversation short - trying more pitches means either throwing in more references, or other made-up anomalies, which I felt would end up being the same punchline and come off as tacky. I'll see if I can come up with a solution on your concern though.

The problem with that version of the rule of threes is that a lot of otherwise harmless SCPs have this issue where the anomaly is otherwise completely harmless and fun, but out of the blue there's an incident where it suddenly either kills a bunch of people or shows that it knows classified information. This can be done well, don't get me wrong (look at 2317 and 1230), but when the  is just an offhand remark and the article doesn't really delve into the effects it has on the foundation/anomaly, it just kinda falls flat.

About the second issue - I think it'd be just fine, really. Maybe make up some anomalies if you feel like it's too much of a crosslinker, but it's a short-ish article so it shouldn't be an issue.
