Milestones; or the work formerly called 

31 Jul 2018 10:18

I'm deep, deep obsession work: an SCP-4000 entry mostly composed of a single dialog between two original characters and crosslinking.

WELLOPTIMISTIC.

i'mshouted

Thanks to the SCP-ES staff for putting up with my absences (particularly Metaphysician, whose contributions to the site give it a flavour that goes from sickly sweet to nightmare salty3

Thanks to the authors of SCP-1000, djkaktus; all-encompassing proposals that did not only rock, but set in stone our evolution as a community and the tone for many other great things still to come.

May we remember their wonderful concepts and exquisite executions for all time; theirs are contributions that made me grow, as they made this idea grow. This may be a grossly insufficient tribute, but I will be happy to just bring back a piece of what they inspired.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Now, also important:

I believe I obtained image GIMP.

Image comes from this stock photo site.

Images SCP-4000 were made by me via a number of image manipulation programs. They kinda are posted under CC by default, I think?
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Hope you enjoy it! As always, comments and criticisms will be appreciated. :3

Honestly, I was enjoying it up until the interview.

The language, SCP-4000-1, and the SCP-4000-2 instances have wonderful logic to them that really make that part of the article a blast read, and you are rather creative in the kinds of memories and effects the various ripe stones impart. Had you settled there with a kicker entry on the log (kind of like in Tatiana's Prison) I would have upvoted in a heartbeat.

But then we get to the interview, and the piece bogs down in such a massive storm of all caps and exposition that the pacing stops and, at least for me, the reader is left to slog through the rest.

I can appreciate the ambition of this piece, and the theme of the impact of the various X000 articles on the wiki. However, as rumetzen aptly said above, this could have probably gotten the same impact in about half the length.

No vote.
