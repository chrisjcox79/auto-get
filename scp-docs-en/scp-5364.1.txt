I feel as if the transmissions do very little in carrying this narrative. The second transmission feels like the high point in the collection because it gives glimpses of what the relationship is or was like between Martha and her late husband. The other two serve only to be fluff, as we can surmise the history of the two individuals from what the Foundation finds in the home. The only secret is the fate of Mr. Jeina, though the photograph and the second transmission should be enough to inform what happened.

Overall, I want to like this, but there seems to me something missing. The appearance of second projector is a nice touch, and while I understand why the cessation of transmissions, I think you could push in one more to really make some warm-fuzzies. Although, I could see that making the thing a little too ham-fisted and cutesy, so maybe I'm in the wrong here.

Either way, I offer a hesitant upvote. It's not really a work that I love, but I don't see much to dislike all things considered.
