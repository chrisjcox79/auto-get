hey ghostchibi did you write another goddamn animal scp

28 Jun 2017 00:02

Yeah I sure did.

I'm sure a great deal of you have heard of Hachiko, right, the dog with a statue right outside of Shibuya station, the one who waited until his death for his owner to come back from work after his owner died away from home? I'm sure a great deal of you don't know that in 2015, Tokyo University commissioned a statue of Hachiko and his owner Professor Ueno reuniting, though.

I saw it during my trip to Japan in May. And instead of being happy about the whole image of reunion, somehow my brain went  instead of waiting out of faith that his owner would return, Hachiko realizes that his owner can't come home, so out of love he hops on the train that his owner took to work every day to go meet his owner again.

SCP-3835 is not the only existing statue of Hachiko, but these other statues have failed to show any sign of anomaly as well. Survey of the area around SCP-3835's original location and the area around the statue at the university (a consistent end point of SCP-3835's travels) has revealed nothing. The anomaly isn't inherent to the physical object or location, it seems. There's only one SCP-3835, and for whatever reason it's choosing to manifest through the statue at this specific location. -Dr. Mitchell
附加记录：    

This passage tells me nothing I couldn't infer, and feels patronizing. Like a narrator in a kid's TV show saying exactly what is being shown. Makes me feel like the reader is being treated as if they're stupid.

As long as we keep replacing them, SCP-3835-1 and -2 will keep hopping into the replacements. Logically, never installing replacements would be the most effective containment. While permanently removing Professor Ueno's statue isn't much of a problem, permanently removing Hachiko's statue is impossible. Hachiko is a national icon; civilian attention on the removal would be constant and endless. Whatever the new containment procedures entail, it has to involve some sort of replacement for SCP-3835-1. We don't have the resources to amnesticize all of Japan, and then some. -Dr. Mitchell

Same with this one. These two notes are totally useless, interrupt flow, and make me feel talked down to.

It feels so intrusive that I am novoting on this premise, if they're removed I'd upvote. I understand if I'm the only person who feels this way, but notes like this annoy me to no end.

Alright, alright, a more in depth critique and have the previous containment procedures under them.

01 Jul 2017 11:27

The first passage is there to clear up the possibility of the anomaly being caused by the environment; it's a distinct possibility and I figured Foundation researchers would test for and record that kind of thing too. The Foundation would be nothing but thorough.

The second one I feel like can't be removed because it provides necessary context; the story behind Hachiko and specifically the kind of effect he had on Japan on a societal level is easily lost on someone who isn't familiar with it. I got several comments during draft review about it not being clear the reason why  Dr. Mitchell claims that it's impossible, even though there is a possibility (just one that requires a shit ton of resources), and gets insistent that it has to be replaced. It's a bit of history in their country that they don't want gone. I suppose that might not have come across as much and been too subtle.
