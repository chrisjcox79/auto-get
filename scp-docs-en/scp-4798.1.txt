+Spoilers

–block

If I express myself, will I lose myself?

if I feel myself, will I see myself?

if I become them, will I sing with them?

Is there someone here beside me or am I cursed with this feeling that every time I rise into the skies I'll be reminded of how often they cut me down and closed once again in the openness they were so proud of, but is this just another thing I say to comfort myself when I know that I've already left behind any semblance of cohesion, and where is the line you draw when you can be a genius just for doing something at the rank you were required?

I don't stay.

I won't stay.

I'll find another place where they have never seen, and I've heard of signs left undefined and little things that like to hide.

Re: No vote for now

12 Feb 2019 16:56

(ignore this as a reply to the above post, I misunderstood it)

I thought a lot of this was very promising. There is an intersection of a lot of typical SCP stuff (mutations, intelligent plants, cryptic messages) but it all works well enough together to stay fresh, and I enjoyed the poetry and the interview logs. I was going to upvote until the end, when it attained too many traits of a gory, violent, thing-that-makes-you-crazy and made me roll my eyes. I think I kind of get what you're going for here, at least as far as a poetic loss of identity/descent into madness feeling goes (?), but it could have been done much more subtly.

There are a few other issues; how are these interviews being conducted? Assuming the interviewer says something, the SCP mutates to produce a response, and a molecular analysis needs to be conducted, this would probably take more than a day (which is implied by the date) and even if you analysed everything super fast the flow would be interrupted and would not sound like the normal back and forth conversation written here. I'm not a physicist but I don't think the Earth could survive the appearance of a supergiant star's gravitational field on its surface.

I ended up novoting due to the above issues, but still a respectable article.

2) I don't know if you were clear on this, but the interviews were not conducted with instances of SCP-4798, but rather with human researchers who had been affected by it: SCP-4798-1:

3) As for the supergiant star's gravitational field, it was limited solely to the spatial range of that instance of SCP-4798-C, so that gravitational field didn't affect the rest of the planet.
