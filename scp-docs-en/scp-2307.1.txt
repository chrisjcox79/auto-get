A Commentary Was Made A Distant Moon Ago Here...

01 May 2015 19:17

This is what happens when I talk with people about Warehouse 13 and the Horizon Initiative. This is based partly off the fact that an episode W13 had a certain sword in it as a one-off joke, and not at all relevant to the main plot. Caledflwch deserves some respect, people.

This was also inspired by the fact that everyone gets King Arthur so, so, so fucking wrong. Everyone imagines Sir Pendragon as being a king in the 15th century sense, when he was around during the 5th century. The Fifth Century. Merlin's wizard hat would be a shaman crown, the Knights of the Round Table would probably be a disorganized, possibly barbarous, rabble, and things like Arthur's supposed conception of Mordred by his half-sister probably wouldn't have been as frowned upon.

Currently, the title for this is , but I'm open to suggestions as to what a better title could be.

I'd like to thank Djoric, TwistedGears, Roget, Meserach, and anyone else who might have reviewed this on chat. Enjoy!

WhileGrace...

Thisbit.

Excalibur was forged with a piece of the Broken God's pen- or to be more precise, a stylus, one that probably wrote on chalk or in wet clay. The stylus wrote the word of God, and after it broke, it became defective and started messing up stuff written by or about the people that touched it. It's one of the reasons why CotBG scripture is so contradictory; with the pen unmade, everyone had their own versions of the Word of God. Think Tower of Babel, but instead of languages being shattered and created, its' whole religious texts.
