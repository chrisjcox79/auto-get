Item #:  SCP-2895

Object Class:  Euclid

Special Containment Procedures:  All removed SCP-2895-1 instances must be contained in Standard Secure Chests in Site-17. Any areas that have been infected with SCP-2895 must be demolished if removal of SCP-2895-1 instances has no effect. Any electronic instances of SCP-2895-1 are to be deleted as a standard employee procedure.

Description:  SCP-2895 is an anomalous pathogen that effects most of the western hemisphere and parts of Europe. SCP-2895's primary anomalous effect is the ability to materialize any sort of security system (hereby designated as SCP-2895-1 instances) that are created using the pathogen as a  to materialize these structures.

SCP-2895's security systems grow more advanced and more secure overtime, as untreated buildings have developed security cameras, automated turrets, electronic keypads, barbed wire, mines, and, on rare occasions, saw traps. SCP-2895's effects are not limited to physical security however, as many agents have reported them being locked out of their personal computers, and security measures increased. Any other attempts to access an infected computer have led to numerous deaths via a security memetic generated by SCP-2895.

Discovery Log:  SCP-2895 was found on ██-██-████ in an abandoned shack in Royal Oak, Maryland after many teenage bodies were found near the perimeter with gunshot wounds and dismembered limbs. The bodies were taken in for autopsy, and the bullets were shown to be .303 caliber rounds shot at 2,000 rounds per minute, roughly the rate of fire of a standard military-grade minigun. At the time, it was unknown how the shack had gotten hold of a military grade weapon, until similar cases were reported in Raleigh, North Carolina; Hamburg, Germany; and Los Angeles, California, giving it SCP status.

+Rewrite

-whatever

Item #  SCP-2891

Object Class:  Safe

Special Containment Procedures:  All containers of SCP-2891 are to be kept on Site-43 in High Priority Containment Wing-C. SCP-2891 may only be removed with the permission of two Level 4 researchers, and only for the purposes of testing. All SCP-2891-1, -2, and -3 instances are to be destroyed following testing, with their remains kept in Wing-B.

Description:  SCP-2891 is an anomalous liquid that can cause an environment to undergo significant changes when applied to an organism or object through direct contact (SCP-2891-1). When applied to SCP-2891, it can cause the manifestation of structures and objects designed to defend SCP-2891-1. These structures, now known as SCP-2891-2, usually take the form of security cameras, turrets, and containment cells. It is currently unknown how these manifestations are formed.

Over time, SCP-2891-2 instances begin to become more and more complex. Untreated SCP-2891-2 instances following completion of stage 5 are found to be equipped with security cameras, automated turrets,1 electronic keypads, barbed wire, land mines, steel doors, and tear gas.

Additionally, humanoid entities are found to manifest at varying times in the stages of SCP-2891. These humanoid entities, designated SCP-2891-3, are all genetically identical to each other. SCP-2891-3 instances often have identical uniforms and use similar weaponry as local enforcement officers in order to appear as said officers. It should be noted that while instances of SCP-2891-3 are capable of speech in the language predominate in the affected area, it is not currently known if instances are capable of understanding said speech.

[[collapsible show=]]

Stage 1: The initial stage of SCP-2891-1. Padlocks, reinforced fencing around the affected area, and barred windows manifest. Stage usually lasts for three to five hours after initial application.

Stage 2: SCP-2891-1's clothing begins to alternate from their original designs, often including or removing materials in order to make the removal of said clothing more difficult for the particular instance of SCP-2891-1.

Stage 3: At stage 3, SCP-2891-1's clothing binds to SCP-2891-1's skin. Electronic locking systems, half-formed turrets, security cameras and barbed wire materialize around the affected area.

Stage 4: SCP-2891-1 is bound to a specific location within the affected area. This is usually accomplished with belts, metal bars, full-body strait jackets, and ropes. While this occurs, SCP-2891-1 begins to undergo a form of psychological passivity, allowing for its capture with little to no resistance. The affected area is equipped with titanium alloy fences, more developed turrets, tear gas, 180-degree security cameras, and reinforced steel doors.

Stage 5: SCP-2891-1 enters into a vegetative state, only surviving based on the sustenance it receives from SCP-2891-3 instances, which, along with concrete walls, land minds, military-grade turrets, 360-degree security cameras, built-in tasers, barbed wire increased, and saw traps, also appear.

Addendum-2891-1 

SCP-2891 was first discovered following the absorption of the many subsidiaries and divisions of GoI-2312,4 specifically for Prometheus Defense and Biotech, under a project known as . It was also discovered that a Dr. Kirk, a Foundation researcher and former Prometheus Labs employee, had worked on the project.

Interviewed:  Dr. Kirk

Interviewer:  Dr. Henderson

Date:  06/11/2003

Dr. Henderson:  Good evening, Dr. Kirk.

Dr. Kirk:  Evening.

Dr. Henderson:  Would you be willing to answer some questions regarding SCP-2891?

Dr. Kirk:  Sure.

Dr. Henderson:  Thank you. Now-

[Paper shuffling]

Dr. Henderson:  -what was your involvement with the Prometheus Labs project known as ?

Dr. Kirk:  [Sighs] What do you wanna know?

Dr. Henderson:  What was the purpose of the project?

Dr. Kirk:  It was meant to be many things, but the primary point of it was meant to be a semi-containment producer. You would slather the object or person you needed to contain and presto! You got your own containment cell in no time flat.

Dr. Henderson:  And what were you planning on doing with this object?

Dr. Kirk:  Selling it to the Foundation, of course. You see, the Foundation had come to the higher ups, asking for help. They needed something that could temporarily manifest locations that could contain an object or creature that needed to be recovered quickly and at a moment's notice.

Dr. Henderson:  When was this?

Dr. Kirk:  I'd say the early '60s? I forget. Just know that it was at around that time that I was sent into the Appalachians to a newly dug-in Site for the production of a new project, and I was one of the lead researchers.

Dr. Henderson:  This sounds like it was top secret.

Dr. Kirk:  Oh, it was. Higher ups denied any involvement in the project, of course. Needed to keep up appearances, we do have standards.

[Dr. Kirk laughs]

Dr. Kirk:  Progress was good. Now, sure, there were some kinks in the actual goo. The actual cells would produce more than was needed. A simple cell would develop into a full fucking prison, the guards were inherently violent and trigger happy, the things would get into your clothes, etc. etc. But those were just normal parts of the job, nothing unusual.

[Dr. Henderson does not respond]

Dr. Kirk:  That was until one day, one of my co-workers, Jared, comes into work, a fucking mess. You could tell that the fucker had the flu. I would have sent him home if he was in my department, but, he wasn't, and so I had to have a snotty man in my lab.

[Dr. Henderson does not respond]

Dr. Kirk:  Well, during one of the test trials, Jared, while slathering the subject down, gets it on him. It's not that hard to miss, the thing was made orange for a reason, and so he was put in isolation. I should have known that something was wrong when one of my guards were complaining about something orange coming out of Jared's nose. Thought the guy was just seeing things, I just didn't know

[Dr. Henderson does not respond]

Dr. Kirk:  Well, one night, I decide to go check up on Jared, see how things are going. Well, I get up and take my keycard, and go out to see how he's doing. Purely scientific interest, of course. I get near the guard area, and I see there dark figures near the entrance. They seem to be dangling from, like, the ceiling or something.

[Dr. Henderson does not respond]

Dr. Kirk:  I open the door, and the first thing that hits me is the putrid smell. It smelled like a dead body or something. I think 'Did something die in here' and try looking around. I then see small creatures dangling from the ceiling and hanging by a

Dr. Henderson:  See what?

Dr. Kirk:  I see that hundreds of men, all wrapped in straitjackets, gagged and bound. Hundreds of identical men, all walking around the room with guns, all in lock step. They're all silent, and turn their heads to me. I can you promise me something?

Dr. Henderson:  What is it?

Dr. Kirk:  Don't let any former Labs employees hear this.

Dr. Henderson:  I will try, but I can't make any promises.

Dr. Kirk:  I ran. I ran and ran and ran and ran until I got to the main security office in Area-22. I told them about the 'outbreak' and how we needed to stop it. Half an hour later, I see that an army has been raised of officers and maintenance workers. At least 200 to 300 men and women, armed to the teeth.

Dr. Henderson:  What was the cause of this 'outbreak'?

Dr. Kirk:  Jared's flu. Apparently, the goo must have gotten into his system and manifested itself within his flu-ness. Footage from his cell showed orange goop coming out of his nose. I looked over the cameras during the fighting. Clones clashing against men and women. They fought with a sort of savagery that I've never seen before. When they ran out of ammo, they would bash their skulls in with the butt of their guns and charge our men. They would fall on top of grenades in order to prevent the others from being harmed, and just hell.

Dr. Henderson:  How did you escape?

Dr. Kirk:  I remember yelling to my comrades below, telling them about how I was going to ask for reinforcements from the surface. How there was a local Labs militia near the mountains in case of something like this. I remember looking at their faces as I sealed the damn entrances, and how they banged against the door as the guards wiped them out.

[Dr. Kirk pauses]

Dr. Henderson:  What happened to the facility?

Dr. Kirk:  Buried. All of it, just buried underneath a hundred feet of concrete, and all evidence wiped from the record. I can still hear their screams at night, sometimes.

附加记录：    

Again, this makes grammatical sense in the context of the list. Also, sweet jesus the run-on sentence.

Nitpicks aside.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

While this has seen more revisions, it's still not where it could be. The interview, while no longer full of outdated PL tropes, is still bland straight exposition that gets in the way of the character. Having this neat, sound little backstory of the anomaly presented with a little bow on top sucks out the mystery, and detracts from the potential emotional impact of the character's story.

The second log from the MTF is simply cheap stock gore, that again, detracts from the final portion. I'll add that it also takes away from the imagery for me. You could instead reinforce the picture painted by the (stages) log, and immerse readers in the discovery of Taylor and their tragic situation

Why you'd take this from terrifying confinement- playing on autophobic and claustophobic fears- and end up at more 'blood and guts' is beyond me. You had a good thing without that.

I think more focus should be on Dr. Taylor and the correspondences. I'd open that up some and add some dates to them to help cement the forward progression. Good luck.
