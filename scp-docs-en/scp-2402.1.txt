One of the things I've really enjoyed about this contest is the chance to work with other writers and learn from their different approaches.

One of my instincts as a writer and editor is to pare back and refine ideas. Something I've been really impressed with, WJS, is your willingness to use the opposite approach - to go big, and add connections and depth to the initial idea. It has characterised your writing in the On Mount Golgotha canon generally, and this is another great example. Every draft I read had another layer added, and it has built up into something that I really like. It's fantastic to see a bit of how that works, and to think about how to use it myself in future work.

11 Mar 2016 06:07

I love this, such a clever idea. Your writing is great as always, and the last log with Hennessey was also terrific. I really liked how, for a skip that does something so extraordinary, it is essentially useless - you can make yourself grow younger, over and over, ad infinitum, but each time you lose your memories. But what is eternal life without memory? You're essentially just starting over, without anything you might have learned, or the benefit of your experiences.

I'm reminded of a passage by Marcel Proust, who postulated (paraphrasing alert!) that if the soul is real, and that we existed before we were born and will continue to exist after we die, then that still does not mean we have eternal life - because we don't remember it, all we're actually doing is living a series of successive lives, starting over from scratch, again and again, forever. Anyway, that's what your skip made me think of, sorry if that came out all pretentious-like. :P

Aside from that, some aspects of the Erich Zann collapsible were a little confusing to me (such as how Zann remembered the music when it's supposed to make you forget as it makes you grow younger, or how Mike was able to make an audio file of the music when it is supposedly impossible to duplicate, or what Mike's note at the end is supposed to mean), but I suspect that was just me missing something.

Enthusiastically upvoted.
