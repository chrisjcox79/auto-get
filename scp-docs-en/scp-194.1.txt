+1 but Suggestions

16 Apr 2020 01:30

Here they are:

+block

–block

Regarding offsets 

Overall, I like them since it helps maintain the illusion that docs are always in flux.
The following portions of text are where links to offsets can be found:

Addendum 194.E: Testing Logs
Addendum 194.E: Field Operations Log
Addendum 194.G: 194-LOC-A

Of those, 194-LOC-A is my favorite since it was introduced back with fragment:scp-194-0. It's sad the other two didn't get that kind of treatment; they're never mentioned anywhere else in the report.
I'd also include a final addendum (possibly ) which just links here.

On Accessibility 

I feel most of the readability problems can be solved by simply making the first four lines darkslategray, any new text and collapsibles in midnightblue, and everything else in black. Your end result will look something like this (assuming no other changes). This will train the eye to skip the parts the reader knows isn't important.

Mind you, this is assuming you feel the issue is worth addressing. I, myself, did not have an issue reading this at all, but I like to put forward potential solutions to problems if I can.

Misc. 

I'm not sure I understood why it needed to be in reverse alphabetical order. SCP-194-Z could've just been SCP-194-1 without any significant change logistically speaking. Does -Z have some sort of importance?

fragment:scp-194-3 is missing this text:

Due to the effects of SCP-194-Z upon the SCP-194 designation, the acting documentation of SCP-194-Z is presented in the form of various addenda to the acting SCP-194 designation. Any secondary classifications are to be assigned in reverse alphabetical order (i.e. SCP-194-Z), and all addenda are to be designated alphabetically, rather than numerically (i.e. Addendum 194.A). This is to prevent conflict with documentation applied to the SCP-194 designation.
附加记录：    

 [final offset] could use a line break (and comma removal) to be:

Researcher Laura ██████, Regarding SCP-194.

The restructuring effects of the object appear to have caused a change in baseline reality configuration. SCP-194 appears otherwise unchanged, and its anomalous effects have returned to normal. I am currently researching what triggered the effect.
附加记录：    

Otherwise, it's unclear whether Laura is being addressed or speaking.
