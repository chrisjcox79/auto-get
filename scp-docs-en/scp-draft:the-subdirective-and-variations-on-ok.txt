You mention a lot in the first document civilians accidentally affected by the termination of SCP-XXXX, but you never actually mention what those effects are as far as I can tell.

Thoughts

–block

Ok so, first of all, this is not  :p

I'll apologize if my thoughts here seem a little scatter-shot, I wasn't expecting to crit an article this long so I'm kind of piecing my thoughts on it together as I go. I'll go document by document and then try and tie it all together.

Document 1 

I initially found the vagueness of this frustrating, especially how unclear the table is - I still don't know what the causation column is meant to be. While I now understand why it's so vague, I feel like you need more than the single line at the end of the containment procedures to preface this fact. I'd consider adding a header banner with something to the effect of  to the top of the document.

Additionally, you mention resuscitating SCP-XXXX after its death but none of the injuries mentioned in the table are things you can typically resuscitate a person from. Maybe you need a different word there? Resurrect, maybe?

It's also unclear what happened to the thing that was summoned in 1987 - the first entries in the table, presumably folllowing its manifesation in the second document - start in 1999. So where was it in the interim? What happened to that version that was summoned.

Document 2 

There was an amazing  here again though - and finally piecing it together now, what you want is to describe its essence being transferred into a new form or body. You aren't bringing its dead body back to life, you're moving its very concept into a new shell. I think if you fix that in this and the first docs, the whole thing will work a lot better.

The lecture log is interesting, but I'm not really sure it adds much in its current form. The concept of Intrinsics is interesting but the stinger - that death is dying - is already known from the description. I'll leave this up to you, but I'd consider cutting out as much of this as you feel you can, and playing up the angle that SCP-XXXX manifested itself in order to turn itself in, as this is the big take away from the entire lecture, I feel. It explains the manifestation event before it, the tables from the first document and the entire 3rd document.

Document 3 

2 primary issues here. 1 - why would they transfer SCP-XXXX into the body of a largely indestructible death robot that apparently wants to escape, and why is that robot apparently the largely augmented body of the Administrator? The image is cool but I feel like I'm going to need a lot more justifaction before I buy this. The administrator thing you could explain as being one of the conditions SCP-XXXX gave - it did turn itself in to the Foundation after all. But why the giant robot?

Issue 2 is that I don't like the whole Subdirective of the Foundation thing being that it is meant to kill death, especially since when presented with the opportunity to do so, they don't because they realise what a stupid idea it is. If it were me, I'd drop that angle entirely and go with the necessity of preserving death as a concept as the angle.

Summary

–block

So, overall, despite my frustrations, I really enjoyed this. That moment during the second document when I realised what was going on was genuinely awesome and made me very impressed with everything from the first. I've obviously left a lot of notes above, which you are free to use as you choose, but I think if you can avoid the frustrations that can easily occur from an article like this, tighten up a couple of places and fix a few logical issues, this could do very well.

My one concern is that I'm fairly sure I've read an article which featured the physical embodiment of death before. I can't find it now, even through googling, so I might be misremembering but I don't think it should be a huge issue regardless. There is no canon, obviously, but with some tweaks this will be more than strong enough to endure those comparisons.

Hopefully this long, rambling mess of a feedback post is helpful to you. I'll try and check in if you want any claifications on anything I've said but feel free to drop me a PM if I don't respond in a timely manner.
