21 Jan 2020 20:27

The idea is interesting but I feel was hamstrung by a few factors. Firstly, the interview logs went on too long for the amount of characterization and information they gave, and could be trimmed. The change to a Thaumiel-class object was a bit silly to me, though I did like the deception via fake awards.

Like some of the interviews, I didn't see the final collapsible transition as necessary. Instead of making it Thaumiel (which imo is already a stretch given it is a sapient non-Foundation entity) you focus on the process that made the skip more cooperative with staff.

The anomaly itself would be more interesting to me if instead of general knowledge predictions, the information was more indirect and required a clever scrutinization to understand or make reasonable use of.

Overall, some interesting ideas here, but this is a novote for me.
