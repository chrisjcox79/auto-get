generally speaking, humanoid anomalies are not usually referred to as , and the like, depending on context.
附加记录：    

Referring to humanoids as  is fine, especially when you're going for an unsettling tone.
I think  in-universe, this file is about the procedures, and the person in the article is only relevant as an instance of an organism on which said procedures are to be used (even though they're often the only known instance).
The most realistic and humane term is probably  which is how IRL behavior management documents refer to the people they're designed for.
 is only appropriate to use in the context of a specific experiment, and generally not accurate as a label for a contained person.
In-universe documents should never refer to an anomalous person or thing as an  - SCP is an acronym for Special Containment Procedures. While it makes sense that personnel would refer to anomalous things as SCPs or skips just like we do on the site, they wouldn't do so in official documentation.
