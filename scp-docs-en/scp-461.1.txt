And as for containment worthiness, there's the security risk inherent in this thing randomly broadcasting any location, secret or not.
附加记录：    

That was my main reason for containment. I consider the event captured in 461-1 to be a secondary effect.

Perhaps ironically, the Foundation may have opened itself up to operational risk by obtaining SCP-461, since the device now appears to "know" about them and the facility it's contained in. However, the risk is relatively mild, since SCP-461 only activates for five minutes at a time and appears to draw from a large "database" of footage, making it a pretty unreliable spying tool. Still, it's enough to justify containment, never mind the protection of other national security assets unrelated to the Foundation. There's also the possibility of SCP-461 revealing information that no one is prepared to know…

At any rate, I wonder if I should clarify that there is a Foundation-supplied VCR hooked into SCP-461 and recording its footage?
