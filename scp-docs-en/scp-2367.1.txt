Ok, it's about time I addressed this.

I'm going to write a supplement and/or tale that follows up and elaborates on this thing a bit sometime very soon, but what it boils down to is this: it's a calibration error put into place by a saboteur. The saboteur could have made this thing launch everyone who uses it into the sun or the cold vacuum of space or something, but instead, they turned a single adjusting dial just a few degrees off-center, so their work would be less visible.

The Foundation hasn't fixed it because the Ahnenerbe burned all of the blueprints, and the Foundation doesn't want to break it or make it even less usable by messing with it. So instead, after the first few D-Class splatted, they decided that everyone sent through would get a parachute. Then Nazis started splatting in the Berlin streets, so they decided to just wait until it was all over before playing with their new toy.

Idunno if I should edit anything now that the contest is over, but I have a feeling people might want to see more hints at what I just said here in the actual document.

Ok, going to address your two cons:

Re: Author Post... A few days late... Look, you dont know my life, ok?

27 Jul 2015 06:38

I have no idea how he knows so much about Nazis. Is history education just that much superior in the Netherlands (Crayne lives in the Netherlands, right)? Is it just a Europe thing? Idunno. His feedback was fantastic, as was his enthusiasm.
附加记录：    

Well, there's a couple of reasons (and trust me, I am not an expert by any means).

Dutch high school is available at a couple of levels, the highest being VWO (Preparatory Scientific Education), and at that level, history education is both broad and in-depth.
The Netherlands are literally right next to Germany, and were invaded during the Second World War. This makes it a pretty prominent subject in history education.
I have always been interested in WWII, my grandfathers and -mothers having lived through it and having told me stories about both the hardships and the good things that came from it. In addition, they never thought in terms of good and evil, black and white, they thought in terms of people and unfortunate circumstances. I think it was that approach to their experiences that really fostered my interest. If they hadn't, WWII would just have been real life Star Wars to a little kid.
I have two WWII/Occult Nazi-tech scips on the site, so I've needed to do a lot of research in order to make stuff feel somewhat believable (which is a pretty odd thing to say really, considering the subject ;)), so I know a few things. And I mean a few. Again, I am not an expert by any means.
