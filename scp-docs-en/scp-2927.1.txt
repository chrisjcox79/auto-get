So I wasn't sure these frequencies would really be audible. I know that human hearing is nominally 20-20k, but people vary. Also, I am a bit older than the average reader, and I wanted to test my hearing. So I made an audio file of these two frequencies, one on stereo left and one on stereo right. I couldn't hear it through my headphones at all, so I took it to my stereo with the really good speakers (and I moved them 3 meters apart, naturally). I had to turn it up really loud, but yes I could hear them, and it was deeply unsettling . Just so you know. : )

11 Sep 2016 10:05

I like the idea, but the execution won't allow me to upvote. Generally, it's well written, but my biggest gripe comes with the last paragraph of the description. I never quite liked when SCPs make people feel uneasy for no reason (aside from real life phenomena like the uncanny valley effect or such), unless it ties with the rest of the anomaly. But the problem lies with the fact that it makes the end feel redundant. Having people feel uneasy over the possibility of the two objects merging doesn't have the same impact if people are already feeling uneasy just being near them. It just feels weird to have a contrived reason to make people scared about the object, when there's already a real reason they should be.
