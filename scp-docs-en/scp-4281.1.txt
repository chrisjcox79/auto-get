This is great.

+rant

-rant

The image is an amazing hook. You're just invited to imagine yourself entering a nondescript bathroom, and then being pulled into a conversation with an unknown figure sitting in the stall.

The dialogue really takes the typical  and just throws it out the window. He's not the meek interviewee who just answers plain questions directly, and he's not the monologuer who divulges a long story about the subject at hand. Instead, the poor interviewer is being dragged along like they're tied to the back of a truck going down the highway, trying desperately to turn the conversation to something useful as the guy just goes on and on and on.

The last interview further exemplified this, with a reference to somebody the interviewer cared about, but was let completely unsatisfied as he started rambling about a K-mart. You finish that incident with a statement by the guard, using a redaction to spark the imagination about whether he was threatened, told the bad news, or something else entirely.

And then finally the ending, when the scip realizes he may have a problem.

You can even look at this as metaphorical in a way: the scip is stuck in a bathroom, and like in life, can't really control what people he comes across. Deep down he wants to be understood and connect with other people, but his self-imposed separation prevents that. He both is physically and emotionally isolated, and he doesn't know how to pull himself out.

Well done author. +1

I'm guessing you replied to the wrong comment (pardon in advance if that's not the case!), so:

There's a lot of weird, simple solutions you can use here. One of the things to keep in mind is that your solution doesn't have to plug every  it just has to be interesting, brief, and fun enough for us to (mostly) not care. For example:

+block

–block

Describe any attempt to approach the stall from above or otherwise intrude in its space as suffering from Zeno's dichotomy paradox. You can only cover half the distance with each attempt (both people and machines perpetually stop half way, thinking they've arrived, only to  they've only traveled half the distance).
Just make him a cognitohazard that people can't describe (even when looking at a picture). If you want to elaborate on this, refer to visual recognition bots describing him as something silly ().
Have every attempt to look into his stall result in person or machine looking in the wrong stall. Make how this works vague and confusing (implying the Foundation doesn't understand it themselves).
Everyone and everything that looks inside the stall proceeds to spontaneuosly combust. Make a brief humorous aside/implication about the Foundation discovering that this includes things literally incapable of combusting.
The stall is literally empty. You can only see his feet from underneath it; you're  the same way you came in). Again, you can keep this vague and confusing.

I'm fond of the Xeno solution, if only because I think it emphasizes the futility of the skip (reflected in their inability to get useful information, and his inability to make a human connection). I'd skip the drones and just mention the problem occurs with attempts at endoscopic exploration (because endoscopic exploration of a bathroom stall is both incredibly pervy but also very logical and something the Foundation would probably do).
