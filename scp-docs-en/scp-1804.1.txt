I can see that you put a lot of work into this one, and technically speaking there are few articles that rival it. However this article suffers from some failings that really bring it down.

The footnotes: Way too many. I feel like I'm reading the idiot's version of Paradise Lost again. Aside from the one (whose existence is dubious at this moment) kid who made it impossible for someone to write about him, I have never seen so many footnotes. In this case, is starts to border on being a bad thing, and it becomes more of a chore to read them; most of the time they barely add to the text. The [REDACTED] you used is okay in small quantities, and I think you used it well, but then you started in with way too much of it towards the end.

Also, the concept. It doesn't really do anything for me. In summary, it's . It really doesn't pique my curiosity, as the effects are often mild and while there are some exciting ones, they are bogged down and weakened by the sheer volume of text which tends to err on the boring side.

You have good stuff in there somewhere, but it is buried under dull text and technical (also dull) writing. If you clean it up and put more of an edge on it, I would love to upvote it, but right now it feels like a mess.

More or less agreeing with Hyenaboy; I sort of assume that each SCP has its own experiment file, in which things like the exact poem typed up using SCP-1804, or the original designations of works associated with it, are listed. My understanding was that the point of an SCP file is to provide what information is relevant to containing the object, not to list everything the Foundation knows about the object. But you [sandrewswann] have been around considerably longer than I have, so you're probably in a better position to say whether that's actually the case.

I'm fairly neutral on the concept itself; you've supported it well, and it's an effect that seems logical, given the physical form of the SCP, but there's nothing about it that's particularly surprising after the first paragraph of the description. ( made me chuckle a little.)
