20 Dec 2011 03:19

Here's the kicker: an earthquake takes effect from anywhere for a few seconds to a minute or more, concentrated most heavily in a single epicenter and spreading out radially from there.

Nuclear detonations occur over a *very* short period of time, shorter than most earthquakes of a type that would be recorded by this machine, and it's the fact that a great number of high-intensity-short-duration instances occur within a tiny fraction of time across most of the heavily populated portions of the planet (and not a whole lot of other places) that makes for some damn worrisome data.
