16 May 2017 03:57

Generally when I write, I write so it can be easily understood by anyone who reads it. I don't like falling into Sesquipedalian Loquaciousness, as I feel that makes it harder for the reader to enjoy the piece as a creative work. I am trying, and failing, to figure how those are bad tone. They're succinct and get the point across without any subjectivity or emotion. Unless you can offer me better suggestions, I'm going to leave them

And to answer your previous question about containing -1. He disappears as soon as he provides an answer, there's not really anything the Foundation could do to catch him, but he's linked to the magic eight ball, so it's not like he's going anywhere.

Some people need to consider:

The image of a naked hairless blue guy gently waking you up at 3 am, asking if he could have some Goldfish crackers and perhaps milk.

Groggily getting up and not comprehending the situation, you oblige, and provide the blue dude with his food. Both of you are sitting at your home's kitchen table. 2654-1 is quietly munching on his crackers, and you're half-awake staring at the guy.

Thanks for the food. Oh, and uh, you know that question you asked a week ago? About you getting a promotion? Magic 8 ball?
