So I've found a lot of evidence of shit going down but it doesn't add up into a coherent picture.

+block

–block

Much ado is made of the astronauts having completed their water egress training. That's important later.

So the first blatant individual strangeness is Yates suddenly going  This seems more like mind overwriting or something than an impostor though and they haven't even found the mirrors yet? And what's with the scribbles?

There's a plasma signal image, but I have no clue what the bloody hell it's supposed to mean, it looks like it's some kind of magically-imaged side view of the place, showing that the long hall they wandered down not only wasn't there before they arrived on September 13, but that it isn't connected to the rest of the structure?

Searching the internet only suggested blood plasma structural readings so idk what else I'm supposed to be getting here.

Anyway the black shiny stuff the walls are made of is invincible. Important later.

The radio graph thing is uninformative due to lack of axis labels aside from something appearing mirrored around a particular frequency. And I'm not sure if that's even correct for how radio works but again I know nada there.

Next up is the mirror crystal breaking. I dont know if they passed through a mirror frame or not though, dialogue was ambiguous.

Next big thing is Catani. After one smash, Catani is no longer right with Yates where he should have been. After a second, something else with Catani's voice but imperfect mannerisms spooks the heck out of Yates; impostor Catani always calls him  whatever it is they have 'for defense'.

Oh, also, at some point Yates heard that song again.

Next thing we see, imposters of Yates and Catani knock on the door without warning after Milner calls for them. The imposters both say . They didn't shoot the flare like they were supposed to if audio cut out, much like impostor Catani earlier didn't notify Yates before suddenly spooking him.

The medical diagnostics of the crew at the day 36 check-in are nothing but the musical signal instead of the expected medical stuff. In spite of this one of the crew transmits . Not Albinoni's  Adagio.

Then there's the re-entry process where the crew acts nutters, or at least impostor Catani does. The brain wave readings tell us that SOMETHING happened and their brains flatlined at some point - when, why, and how, is unclear. Heart rates are constant but I dont know if those are constant comatose or constant normal active or what.

Finally the O5 Council (or more likely just O5-9 since that's who they report to) seals the recovery records and leaves a summary full of BS for reasons unclear. Security? Sabotage? Impostor on the O5 Council? I cant tell.

Earlier the article said they were supposed to meet up with another independent ship to bring them home but that seems to have not happened? Pilot capsule hatch was opened immediately. The summary blames this on not completing their water egress training which they very much completed earlier in the article. Then it blames psychological instability because of a transmission seven minutes after landing where we hear Catani and Yates going  (possibly misinterpreted). Notably, we don't know whether that seven minutes passed before or after the Foundation teams showed up and dug them out.

The spacesuits, much like the walls in the structure, are invincible. All 3 impostors. Then the bodies are to be cremated (despite two verbally wanting burial last anyone heard), presumably so that the bodies can be disappeared with no suspicion despite the fridge logic of trying to burn the invincible suits.

There's a ton of odd turns of phrase used in the summary that make no sense. , stuff like that.

The odd thing is that despite Yates shooting the real Catani with an unspecified weapon, the capsule transmission had Yates and Catani trying to get out instead of Yates and Milner. And Milner, how did he even get replaced? He was going to need to use the extra thrusters to maneuver if they didnt get there on time and they only got there right on time. Flight path deviations were likely imperfect handling of controls on the part of the impostors so how did they even have time to swap out Milner?
