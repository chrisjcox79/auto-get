Sorry, I should've been clearer.

What I mean is, I think it is reasonable to expect that setting up an SCP as centered entirely around the Dyaltrov Pass Incident, the infamous mysterious and creepy death of multiple hikers on a cold mountain, still completely unsolved 60 years later, would make at least many people assume that they are going to get a story based around the circumstances on this incident, and perhaps, an explanation. Should they not get that, it makes sense they would be disappointed, and I would argue this isn't entirely due to their own projections.

It's like watching a romance movie that sets itself up as an epic sci-fi in the first ten minutes. Even if it's really good, you aren't going to appreciate that fact because you are waiting for the aliens to show up.

Since writing the original comment, I've learned that this story is part of a series (which looks quite good and I should probably read it), which means that many or most of the people reading this are going to go in with expectations established by the series, rather than just the first few paragraphs and prior knowledge, which makes my problem mute enough to retract my downvote.

If you agree this is a problem, I think you could fix it by putting something in the description that indicates this is about more than the Dylatov Pass Incident, or put something that shows it is part of a series at like, the top, or something.

Well, while I appreciate the read, with multiple articles concerning the assassination of John F. Kennedy on the site (just for an example) I don’t know why this tragedy would be off limits for reinterpretation in the SCP universe but that tragedy wouldn’t be.

Unless you’re saying all works based on real world events are examined under the same standard?

Secondarily, I will point out that Dyatlov’s enthusiasm is based upon his personal subjective belief that he’s found something touched by the divine. As such he is blinded by his own preconceptions as to what that might entail. Folklore, and especially Slavic stories, are often filled with blind antagonists pursuing their goals despite the obvious danger an outside perspective might clearly perceive.

Moreover, I don’t know what conspicuous references are made. This article references three things established in the SCP universe (other than the GRU-P): WL, the concept of the Daeva as a culture, and SCP-3743 (which is my article). What is conspicuous about that?

(GRU-P is a government organization concerned with psionics, so I don’t see why a person being headhunted into that organization would be tonally inconsistent.)

Ultimately, I can’t possibly understand what you thought was goofy about this.
