20 Jun 2018 00:09

I take it the concept is that these tree-cordyceps-zombies are trying to absorb as much information as possible to stop all knowledge from being eaten by the dragon? I'd say Decibelles and Conwell are right in that this brushes over its content too quickly to have much effect. I feel like this is being pulled in two directions at once and doesn't really land either particularly well. More thoughts + some suggestions for improvement under the cut:

+block

-block

On the one hand you've got the body-horror tree-zombies stuff, with the straight-up Alien-esque infirmary addendum, with the exploding chest-pollen and throwing people around like ragdolls. The other is the more abstract stuff about the information-gathering and the dragon-infopocalypse. The problem is that they don't really interact very well. The infirmary scene is probably the most striking thing thing here in terms of imagery (partly because it's a recognizable trope, it's the easiest thing to grab on to and imagine), but it overpowers everything else - the stuff about the info-absorbtion seems dry and uninteresting in comparison, and it doesn't stick in the mind.

One way to fix this would be to try and write more evocative and penetrating content focusing on the info-absorption effects. Something that would give us greater insight into why they're doing this, what it means, and give some more texture and imagery to the dry description of their behaviour. That would help to rebalance this towards the overarching plot about the dragon and the 'unwriting'.

You could also try and synthesize the two directions by translating the elements from the infirmary scene into a scene that builds on the info-absorption rather than entirely ancillary to it, thereby keeping the info-absorption more central without becoming too dry. Maybe have the tree-zombies conspire to commit a big information robbery or something like that (hell, the SCP would be make a good sleeper agent to use against the Foundation if tweaked somewhat). I'd be totally down for an incident report of freaky plant zombies trying to steal a Foundation database during a sleeper raid.

This is also entirely a subjective view but, given you're kind of invoking Alien/zombie tropes here, the best way to make them distinctive is to make them as weird as possible without conceptually overloading it. The tree-zombies could definitely afford to be about twice as freaky and alien as they are.

22 Jun 2018 12:13

The core idea here isn't terrible, though without any context (having not read any of the rest of your teams doomcon entries) there just isn't really much here to work with. Your containment procedures feel overly verbose, being longer than your actual description. You mention that people who have successfully had this thing removed from them still need to be locked up. Why? Can't they be amnesticised? Is there any lingering anomalous acitvity? Following the discovery of a successful treatment, they still have this huge dedicated facility for housing these things - why aren't they all just treated?

As others have suggested the interview just doesn't seem to do anything. It only has a couple of lines of dialogue that actually contribute anything and the rest of it is just introductions. The mention of this dragon seems to come out of nowhere and doesn't seem to be connected to the rest of it at all and the process of actually getting around to mentioning it feels forced.

I do like the idea of a tree whose seeds take people over, though I feel the tree angle is brushed over pretty quickly too; where did these trees come from? Is there anything weird about them? Do their seeds just grow into normal trees if they are planted in the ground?

Very mixed feelings about this. On the one hand, I quite like the idea of it; a fungal infection that takes you over has been done before, but these ones seek nothing more than to consume knowledge, for some unknown reason possibly relating to an information apocolaypse of some kind.
