Sorry, was typing in a hurry. Obviously meant descent.

And you are correct, there is no single trait or allele which unambiguously specifies someone's race. There are, however, useful regional averages. Maybe 60% of the people from West Africa have a particular variation of a gene, and only 40% of Eastern Europeans have that allele. That single allele by itself is almost useless , but that's so misleading that I'd outright call it a lie. We can easily tell someone's race by looking at their DNA. What we can't do is tell their race by looking at individual genes, because there is no allele that all Asians have but no Caucasians have.

The Wikipedia article on Race and Genetics looks to be a decent overview of the subject.
