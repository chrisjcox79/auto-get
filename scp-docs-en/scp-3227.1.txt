I'm going to have to agree with the above. The idea is really good, but there are enough tonal issues that it makes it hard for this to really suck me in.

-Critique

-long

To start with, I'm not the most experienced, so take all of what I say with a grain of salt. Given that, here are my in-depth thoughts:

As mentioned, the repetition of 'SCP-3227's toxin' and in general the word 'toxic'. Just one example, you specify the tongue contains 'alkaloid toxins', which is redundant as you've already clarified that it's alkaloid based. A possible solution is to declare the toxins as 'SCCP-3227-1' or such, as it seems obviously slightly anomalous. Then you can describe it once, and just refer to the designation elsewhere.

The tonal issues are a bit harder for me to pin down. I'll pull a couple lines that stand out and specify my thoughts on those lines in specific

 walked in one day, as a group. If they're researchers, they're probably not just deciding one day to walk randomly around a jungle, they're planning to on a specific day walk randomly around the jungle.

 - would probably be better as 'humans are', as the impression I get is that the ban is still in place

 - I don't think you can have two 'top' priorities. Maybe a top priority and a secondary priority, or 'While containment is top priority, the foundation is willing to utilize SCP-3227 as long as it doesn't interfere with that priority.'

The information in the article could probably do with some re-organization, ditto what Sindar said.

Now, the things I liked about this: The agent interview dialogue is pretty good, especially the last couple lines I liked. The containment protocol I have no qualms with, they look good. And as noted, I really like the overall idea of this. It's a combination of weird and fascinating, and as noted the possibility of it spreading lends an element of continuous threat, no just leaving it alone.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

In conclusion I'm going to Novote, but I have hope this could be quite good with a bit of fixing up.

(Also, there's an SCP-XXXX left at the end of the article.)
