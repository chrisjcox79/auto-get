25 Jan 2012 14:34

I will remove 'morbidly comical' description, I was going on the tone of several other established SCPs such as the Game show of Death 024;

附加记录：    

The descriptions can easily be summarized and sanitized, I got the first and last ones from historical sources, which went into a similar level of detail, I agree it is graphic, perhaps more than necessary.

The poltergeist-like entity is the collector and caretaker. The ambiguity is whether it is also the artist and causes or facilitates the incidents.

The actual deaths do not change. The idea is that the mirrors have captured not just the event, but some of their personality or perhaps a scrap of their soul. This creates a certain amount of self-awareness that manifests and is forced to re-experience the event in varied ways. These tortured shards of memories are then put on display for the cruel amusement of the patrons of the gallery. I felt this would be more compelling than a simple recording that could have been made with a video camera.

There are a lot of deaths near a mirror every year. I felt that they had decided that they felt the odds of creating a new mirror for the collection were slim. Perhaps if they brought a new mirror into the containment area and did it there it might be more likely to succeed. Concern over possibly disrupting the entity's routine might be a factor, since the containment seems tenuous. It could be good to mention this in the entry. I like the philosophy that containment is the primary concern, and experimentation is secondary in importance.

I appreciate your feedback and will do some revisions.

[Edit] I am not familiar with Final Destination. Any similarities are coincidental.
