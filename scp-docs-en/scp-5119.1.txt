02 Jan 2021 21:41

I mostly liked this, but for me the conversation with the Uncle Charlie demon was immersion-breaking. I would've been able to interpret what was going on even if that scene had been wordless; we were given an abundance of context and clarification in advance. The conversation made Charlie seem like a benevolent figure, and while there's nothing inherently wrong with playing against our archetypal expectations by having a demon be benevolent, here it felt cheesy, like the demon just so happens to need to feed on the exact thing that the person would benefit most from losing. There's a logic to it all, if you start with the assumption that the demon can only feed from hosts willing to contractually sign away the food, but the contract itself is just  playing against type in that respect didn't accomplish anything. There's only so much subversion you can burden an archetype with before it stops adding intrigue or depth and starts breaking reader immersion.

I'll give it another readthrough, but it's either a novote or a downvote. I do think the final interview would hold up better if the conversation with Uncle Charlie wasn't a tell-all exposé.
