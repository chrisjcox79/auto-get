30 Jan 2015 19:48

This comes down to simple headcanon, as far as the interview bit goes.

+time!

-yayy

In interviews, I feel as if the Foundation has more freedom and leniency, both in-universe and out. Obviously, they have to treat these things as objects to contain, but there is room for humanity at some level. There are works on the site that manage to do this very well, treating the subject (provided the Foundation has no reason for hostility towards the skip, and reason to drop the clinical tone in-person) in interviews as not an object, but with some kind of humanity. So this is what I tried to do here.

Plus, figuring that she's nervous and scared and the Foundation would get better information out of her by treating her with some kind of level of humanity, that's what they opted to go for. Of course, if there's a problem with the interviewer's voice while going for that, I'm more than willing to listen to feedback on that.

-yayy

I tend to dislike telling stuff in the  narrative some skips like. What I had in mind, and what is said in the article, was that this journal had a ton of pages, most or nearly all of which were destroyed by poor management/keeping. The pages that aren't in the main body of the article are not relevant to knowing more behind the skip for researchers to investigate, and this is the one (nearly) full entry that is relevant. I tried putting it into two different quote boxes for easier reading, but it looked messy to me so I did it like this.

As for that last bit, he wouldn't know anything Jonathon hasn't told him. Maybe the Serpent's Hand are doing this with others too, but he's not aware of it. It's definitely worth exploring in other works, if I or anyone else explores it, but as far as this article is concerned, I'm leaving the implications at that.
