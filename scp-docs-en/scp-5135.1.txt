Well, here it is—my Jam 2020 Explosions entry.

I challenged myself a little extra for this: The theme was explosions, so I did everything I could to make an explosion-related SCP without using the word  one single time.

I would like to thank the critters who gave me great feedback, the friend who recorded brand-spanking-new audio for me in under 48 hours, and the community for being, y'know, awesome.

Edit : Been asked a couple times: My friend voiced the first audio clip; I provided all 3 voices for the second audio clip. I then did all the audio editing, including recreating real aviation cockpit alert sounds.

The ending appears thought-provoking but collapses after a few moments of thought, even with your attempts at lampshading. Of course people wouldn’t realize that the Iraq War was happening if there was nobody reporting it. The containment procedures don’t go nearly deep enough either — how is the media being silenced? What kind of political pressure is being employed to ensure that uninvolved countries keep silent? Most importantly, why would the Foundation want to keep this war a secret? These questions aren’t answered or even touched upon.

Have to downvote due to totally inadequate descriptions of military operations, making me unable to suspend my disbelief. There is very little hope for any other nation to launch any sort of amphibious invasion of American soil without liberal use of nuclear weapons. That the war lasted for so long implies a strangely half-hearted deployment of strategic nuclear weapons - when the deliberate launching of one would inevitably set off a counter-strike of greater magnitude. Mutually assured destruction.

The war never happened because the war as it's described in the document is too silly to have existed. Kauai can only be taken if all US air and naval presence from Hawaii and surrounding regions have been destroyed. This implies the annihilation of the US Pacific Fleet. Even if our 2020's China were mobilized for war, they still have no experience nor even doctrine for conducting naval operations of that scale. China's military today isn't even geared towards Pacific dominance. Its main concern is projecting regional power towards Taiwan and India. Its naval stance is strikingly defensive. Our PRC has the largest coastal defense force in the world, bristling with battery upon battery of anti-ship missiles. It is defensive because it doesn't have the political and financial resources to be offensive. Because it is the weaker military. A poorer 2000's China simply cannot hope to do anything about Hawaii, much less the US mainland.

The only real offensive capabilities China has against the US would be its strategic nuclear arsenal. It is tiny when compared to its American counterpart. The moment China uses one on the United States, to attack a military installation, then the Americans will use a liberal definition of the term 'military installation' and launch a significantly more devastating counter-attack. If the PRC decides to strike at a population center, then the US is sure to nuke Beijing, Shanghai, Nanking and everything else.

A total war with nukes lasting this long between two combatant nations of vastly differing capabilities is simply not possible. Your military science is unsound.
