16 Dec 2010 06:54

awesome article. very disturbing.

one point i want to make though….

"Left unchecked, the popularity of SCP-785-2 will grow unusually quickly. Within nine months, at least fourteen other additional instances of SCP-785-2 will be created. The spread of SCP-785-2 is not limited to its home city: when first discovered, ██ instances of SCP-785-2 were found in three separate cities."

at LEAST 14 instances if left unchecked….

"any customers who had previously consumed any of SCP-785-2's beef or bread during the day".

by "any" does it mean any customers who ate bread or beef during THAT day will get killed? or does it mean any customers who did so in the entire existence of that instance of 785-2 will come in to be slaughtered?

if it's the former, then as a very popular restaurant you're still probably looking at at least 30 customers per instance. so say about 400 people slaughtered every spring special…

if it's the latter, then you're probably looking at several thousand, if left unchecked

never is it possible to truly contain it because it's an idea rather than an object. preventing the slaughter requires constant attention worldwide. it's a pretty serious threat to human life in any area it starts to affect. it requires extensive, intensive, and expensive monitoring. if a breach happens it's a large and time consuming undertaking to contain the situation.

seeing how many "keter" objects have been relabeled to "safe" in the past due to the threat levels being primarily based around the difficulty in containing them rather than how much damage theyre capable of doing, i feel i must protest…

shouldnt this be keter rather than euclid?

"Keter objects fall into one of two categories: those that are of immense strategic value once controlled, or those that have been deemed impossible to destroy due to the nature of the artifact."

and

"Keter class objects are always inherently dangerous, despite the best containment procedures possible: the threat level may range from global armageddon to simply killing everything around it. A hand grenade with the pin removed may be considered Keter: a single mistake will cause casualties."

i really feel this meets all criteria and suggest it be upgraded.

16 Dec 2010 15:48

It's true that, by definition, "malevolent" means "bearing ill will", and a non-sapient idea (or restaurant) can't very well have any kind of will. I'd argue that in the Keter definition, though, "malevolent" is slightly metaphorical and could easily be replaced with "actively dangerous". Nonsapient objects that nonetheless actively seek to do harm fit the sense of the thing. Think of a heat-seeking missile. It is in no way sapient, or even sentient, but once it's fired it takes direct action to do harm.

In short, yes, this is in some sense actively malevolent and definitely Keter. Chubert, may I edit the class to reflect that?
