Fold

01 Jul 2010 04:22

Yeah, see….the human body is pretty much dependent on being made up exactly of what it's usually made up of. The removal of any element will cause the human body a great deal of distress. Especially since a great many of the basic elements of life aren't 'pure'. If all the oxygen is transformed into nitrogen, there goes all of the oxygenated blood, all of the water, etc. Every chemical in the body with an 'O' is changed to 'N'. There are no 'benign' elemental swaps.

I rarely mark down, but this deserves it. It fails on a basic scientific level, in a way that is completely ludicrous. Yes, even more ludicrous than the living statues, the evil gearbox, and the time-displaced city.
