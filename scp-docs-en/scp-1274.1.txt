1) I don't think this counts as a memetic agent, since merely seeing the dance or knowing about it has no effect. Gaining knowledge of how to do an SCP merely by observing it being done doesn't make it memetic.

2) Using DMCA take down requests wouldn't work very well, since:

2a) It only works if the server hosting the video is in the United States.

2b) A take down request can be challenged on the grounds that the issuer of the request doesn't actually own the copyrights. While in some circumstances the Foundation might be able to manufacture evidence that they do own the copyrights to the video, I don't think they'd be able to do so in all circumstances. And no one can have a copyright or trademark for an improvisational style of dance.

08 Apr 2012 11:25

Downvoted since I have a deep dislike of articles that aren't clearly anomalous in nature. This seems like the sort of thing which even the Foundation couldn't prove to be worthy of SCP qualification.

The 'discovery' paragraph also strikes me as off. Why and how would the observer have been able to notice the anomalous effect? It doesn't present itself if you watch it, so they couldn't have realised they were being affected. And honestly, who the hell is going to see that some ravers started wearing the colour orange and became a bit more suggestible and then immediately link it to the dance and call the Foundation? That's just ridiculous, as is the idea that the Foundation has contacts who hang around at parties in case something goes amiss.

The main problem I have with this article, I think, is that you really didn't go into enough detail. I imagine that you were trying to keep the article short, which is prefectly fine, but you really haven't examined the symptoms on a level where they actually jump off the page and make it obvious this is an SCP. Saying that they are more suggestible in a single line and not mentioning it again doesn't make it sound disturbing, really unusual, or frankly even all that interesting. If you examined the details a little more closely, and maybe added some more obviously unusual symptoms, it would work a lot better.

Also, this is probably just me, but the line  doesn't make the slightest bit of sense to me. Mind explaining what you meant?

Fold

09 Apr 2012 20:17

I like several things here.

-Dance as a means of communication, and hence a channel for memetics.

-A meme scp that propagates passively instead of forcing the subject to spread it 

-A memetic without an implied shadow-agenda creator. I get a feeling that this just kinda happened, and then spread more or less naturally; not that is was designed by unknowable extra-dimensional bugaboos to drive men mad in preparation for their arrival.

But there is just no reason for this to be an SCP. How would anybody tell the difference between this and, say, the macarena? There has to be some extra oomph before any agent would pay attention, much less bring it in to base for containment. Maybe the dance could be addictive, with subjects gradually doing nothing but dancing till they physically can't, eventually dieing of dehydration. I'm thinking of a meth-like effect, with maybe a slowed-down onset before obvious negative side-effects kick in.

Given that it was tossed off in an hour and a half, not bad. But it could be a lot better with a little more investment.
