Fold

04 Nov 2014 18:59

Downvote for some iffy and confusing prose. Let me give a few examples:

Suggested re-write:

Each law or set of laws that is proposed and adopted with respect to SCP-2079 is referred to as an instance of SCP-2079-A. The text of each instance of SCP-2079-A is in a 10th century dialect of Old English, regardless of the normal working language of the legislative body in question. Moreover, instances of SCP-2079-A are typically inconsistent with the the drafting styles and usages of the legislative body that enacts it. Nonetheless, members of that legislative body demonstrate thorough understanding of the meaning and legislative intent of the instance of SCP-2079-A that they debate and adopt even though those members demonstrate no additional comprehension of similar Old English writings.

And so on. A couple of general notes:

 This is confusing: if the rate is increasing, then it goes without saying that the rate varies. But does adding this information improve your piece?

.

Why do the laws have to be in Old English, anyway? It's kind of disruptive to suspension of disbelief- because in-universe, you'd expect the addendum to contain the actual text of the law in question (not a modern translation). You could get around this by having the laws be adopted in modern English, but have the addendum specify that the laws have been adopted in lots of different times and places, including ones where you wouldn't expect the laws to be adopted in Modern English (like, say, 10th century Korea, or modern Tunisia, or whatever).

I would suggest re-organizing your addendum to make it clearer, in each case, which legislative body is adopting the law in question.

For some reason, I'm reminded of SCP-1377.
