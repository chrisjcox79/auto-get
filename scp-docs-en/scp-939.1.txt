Fold

10 Apr 2011 10:25

Despite the length of the article, I didn't have any problem reading through it one go. Unlike a lot of tl;dr's one might find, it was interesting enough that its length did not impact its narrative. I initially thought that the article would be too sciene fiction-y for its own good but after reading everything, I think it has enough creep to scare plenty of people.

The article is well-written and has the clinical tone down right. It gives you a progressive step-by-step explanation of how things should be handled in a concise manner so that it wasn't boring, and doesn't leave anything important out in the description. Personally, I was hooked by the end of the second paragraph and was sold while reading the reproduction document. I really loved the extra notes and conversation snippets.

The lengthy article is also a nice change of pace. I think we've had too many articles recently that pointlessly spent too many words describing how to lock something up or were just too short in general; there's a nice hint of ADD here and there. Plenty of our high-rated SCPs are lengthy and I think people shouldn't be afraid to write longer articles as long as they're well-written.

Also to Bland's comment about needing a picture, I really don't think a picture is needed for this article specifically. Personally, I think that any picture (be it the SCP, scenery, or unrelated) would just be glaringly obvious that it doesn't fit in the article, and put bias goggles over someone reading the article for the first time, confusing them. It'd be like suggesting that Able's article needs a picture too because of its length, before or after the Clef edit. Some things are better left to the description and imagination.

Why, yes. Happily. I'll grab the first paragraph of the description for starters.

SCP-939 are endothermic, chiefly carnivorous pack-based predators adapted for life within high humidity, low light environments such as caves, displaying atrophy of various systems consistent with such a habitat. The skin of SCP-939 is a translucent red, owing not to pigmentation but a compound chemically similar to hemoglobin, and is highly permeable to moisture. SCP-939 average 215cm tall standing upright and weigh an average of 250kg with a lithe build, though weight is highly variable. Each of their four limbs end in three-fingered claws with a fourth, opposable digit. The pads of their claws are covered in setae which grant them considerable climbing abilities. Their heads are elongated and devoid of even vestigial eyes or eye sockets and contain no brain casing. Four nostrils, forming the vertexes of an inverted trapezoid, feature on the front of their snouts. The jaws of SCP-939 are lined with numerous red, faintly luminescent fang-like teeth, similar to those belonging to specimens of the genus Chauliodus, ranging from 11-57mm in length. Their jaws are encircled by heat-sensitive pit organs up to 5mm in diameter spaced at regular intervals. Regularly spaced eye spots 7mm in diameter, sensitive only to light and dark, run the length of their spined dorsal ridge. These spines may be anywhere from 12mm to 163mm long with an average diameter of 2 to 3mm and are believed to be sensitive to changes in air pressure and flow.

SCP-939 are four limbed predators with opposable digits that are adapted for life within caves, having no eyes and translucent red skins that are highly permeable to moisture. SCP-939 average 215cm tall standing upright and weigh an average of 250kg though their weight is variable. Their heads contain no brain casing, but have faintly luminescent fangs and house organs used to sense changes in heat and air flow in their surroundings, which are used for navigation and tracking prey.

God is in the details. The Devil, too.

11 Apr 2011 04:57

The whole *point* of 939 is to provide a detailed, semi-hard-science look at realistic, logic-based containment protocols and cataloging of the SCP. That's the seed this entire project was based on, and you'd not *believe* the amount of material we cut down and neglected to include in this document, all of it adding layers of detail and meaning that are simply not in this version. Everything included has a purpose, and everything we felt had a solid purpose was included.
