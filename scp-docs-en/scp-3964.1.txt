This is my first SCP so it's still very much a learning experience. I would really like to thank DoctorNovakaine from the critique forum and stormbreath from thecritters chat. Both of them offered amazing and helpful feedback which I greatly incorporated into this final(?) post.

But feel free to be as critical of this as you wish. Like I said, it's a learning experience. I want to know what I can improve upon and fix.

Interesting note: I got into this SCP stuff while working at my school’s library. And it so happens that we have a stairwell that used to go downstairs as well is up, but the downstairs part was bricked off for unexplained reasons (which is honestly ridiculous because now everyone has to physically leave the building, use an outside stairwell and round the building just to get to the downstairs room). Having been absorbed into this fandom-verse and with a slightly overactive imagination this idea was formed. Thus my first page.

I can’t be 100% sure but I did give it some thought during the initial brainstorming process. I feel like such a life changing experience like this wouldn’t be so easily forgotten, even if it isn’t explicitly remembered. I believe that SCP-3964-2-A wouldn’t have gotten over her issues completely but there will be that feeling of profound change within her that could become a positive change in her actual life.

The fact is she wasn’t planning to go through with a suicide in the first place and the only real reason she would do so was because the skip trapped her in an inescapable situation. There really nothing there for the ethics committee to intervene with.
