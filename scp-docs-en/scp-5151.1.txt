Sorrypicky

Consult the on-Site culinary team for further details on SCP-5151's  requests.
附加记录：    

This line is fine, but the footnote is in the wrong place. It should be after the next sentence, when it talks about the banquets.

Despite the appearance of having clothing and armor, every aspect of SCP-5151's body, garments, armor, longsword, and other adornments are all made up of the same composition.
附加记录：    

Technically, since aspect is singular and the list is a preposition referring to aspect, it should read 

SCP-5151 has an anomalous ability to judge a person's character and decide whether or not they are honorable.
附加记录：    

That's not an anomalous ability in itself. You should include something like  which is the actual anomalous ability. I can decide whether or not you are honorable (I'll say you are), but I'm not anomalous.

附加记录：    

You basically just said the same thing twice. Unless a perfect containment method is found for an object, or if it's Safe class, then any research will be, at least partially, to find means of containment. Just take out one of them and it's fine.

Site Criticism Team - Mod Review

04 Apr 2020 23:52

As I mentioned in IRC, this suffers from two main issues that kept me from upvoting it.

First, it's too damn long for the rather basic and predictable story that you're telling. I figured out that the blood pact was going to go sour literally as soon as the researchers made it. Your conprocs sell the reveal way too early, and then you put thousands of words between when a reader figures out your reveal and when it actually pays off.

The second, and much harder to fix, issue is that your version of the Foundation is being mind-numbingly dumb here. They cater to every desire of the SCP and give it all of the power when they have no reason to. They could have sealed the containment chamber immediately right at the start of the article and been done with it. He could have even been a Safe or Euclid anomaly if they just shoved him in a box and left him there. The incompetence of the Foundation drew me out of your plot before it had even gotten started and it helped further to telegraph your ending too early.

This is a -1 from me.

Upvoted simply because of 5151

10 Jul 2020 04:10

I dislike blackboxes and would usually downvote articles that have no justifiable reason1 to use them. I also dislike long articles because they tend to repeat quite a bit of information. You also fell victim to over-explaining the sequence of events that happened as SCP-5151 murdered MTF or researchers, an incident log instead of a whole 2 paragraphs for it is unecessary, imo. The Foundation also seems a little incompetent and stumped at how to handle 5151, but that's a plot point I can look over.

However , I really like the characterisation of 5151. I actually could sympathise with 5151 when he got sad that he killed his researcher friend due to his own blunder. It's like his morals conflict with what he actually wants to do: compromise his strictness for people that are close to him. Is that what happened to his brother as well? 5151 killed him, simply because his brother wasn't loyal but it might have been a mistake and 5151 has been depressed all these years.

You really put the human in humanoid. Love this SCP, you did a great job WhiteGuard!
