Site Criticism Team - Staff Review

21 Jan 2020 02:24

It wasn’t so much that we wanted ignorance, but that we wanted mystery.
附加记录：    

Beat me half to death with your message, why don't you? Let this be a lesson in sublety, because this article has none of it. At every turn, your narrator is there to explain exactly what happened, how, and why. This approach directly contradicts what is often the most impressive and exciting aspects of the SCP format, in that the format uniquely allows authors to disguise information about their world as little factoids of information in a clinical document.

Subverting this so obviously both doesn't work for me and is far less interesting, hence my strong downvote.

SoullessSingularity, for any questions or concerns relating to this review.

Sorta, it’s not the full story at least. As long as someone was still around who gained omniscience from the scip, any consciousnesses who wake up would become omniscient.

Furthermore, any consciousnesses that are created due to actions taken by omniscient beings would also become omniscient.

That’s why the omnisciences couldn’t set in motion the creation of a conscious; they had to wait until one would awaken on its own. Then this new consciousness could create consciousnesses that weren’t omniscient.

So, it’s not the entire truth, but as long as the scip isn’t neutralized, it holds.
