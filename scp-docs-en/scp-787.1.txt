The plane is the manifestation of the inner rage of Seymour Fitzgerald, specifically the rage against those around him. The victims of the plane are all the people who have at some point pissed this guy off to the point of wishing them dead (or at least mutilated). Seymour, as it were, has a bit of a short fuse, so a lot of people tend to make him angry. For instance, he's never met the optometrist from Atlanta, Georgia. In fact, Seymour has never even been to Atlanta, Georgia. But ███████ ██████████ was driving to his daughter's house in Birmingham, Alabama when he happened to accidentally cut off another driver when he merged into the left lane on the interstate.

Seymour doesn't like bad drivers, you know, especially ones who cut him off.

The anomalous events within the plane are various things that have happened to Seymour during his life. For example, on January 1st, 2001, Seymour was taken to the hospital after his appendix burst. The poor fellow had an extraordinarily high fever, causing him to drift in and out of delirium. In fact, at one point, he could have sworn he was on the cockpit of a plane, but a swift and brutal bout of vomiting snapped him out of it.

Back to the meat of the issue, though, Seymour is a very judgmental person. Whenever he gets mad at someone, he always some variation of the same thing: 

Yeah that kind of sucked.

Anyway, love the rewrite. Everything that made the original intriguing has been upped at least fivefold, and I for one am a fan. +1.

Long shot, but here's my best guess:

The amulet on the Indian man in the waste compartment is the root cause of the June 1987 anomaly. Somehow, it allows (or at least allowed) its carrier to manipulate space-time in four dimensions. There is loose thematic ground for this, as the Wadjet Eye in ancient Egypt represented a direct connection to the forces responsible for creating and shaping the universe. This man, working as a hitman for organized crime in California, used (rather, will use, as this must take place at some point in the future) the amulet to facilitate accurate travel and escape via the use of warping space to create wormholes.

At some point, this man was hired to kill someone on board a plane, mid-flight. Instead of stowing away on the plane, he attempted to use the amulet to  aboard the flight mid-air. However, at the same time he attempted to create a wormhole, the flight was rocked by turbulence, and the point of arrival was moved into the waste compartment. Panicking, he attempted to immediately create a second wormhole to escape the compartment, which resulted in a massive spatial anomaly which engulfed the entire airplane, sucking it into a wormhole.

Because it was incomplete, the other end of the hole closed before the transit was finished, and the plane experienced a temporal collapse, compressing all time that would have been experienced by objects within the plane into an instant. This had the immediate effect of replacing all humans on board with their corpses at the time of their deaths from various point in the  In addition, as the plane was now outside the known universe, reality contained within the plane began to decay, with numerous bizarre results detailed in the article.

After some  perhaps the corrupted reality fundamentally repels the normal version as a defense mechanism against creating a paradox.

I'm sure the physics are BS, and it doesn't explain everything, but this could be a reasonable hypothesis for explaining:

Why there was a guy in the waste compartment
Why he had a shotgun
Why he had an odd Egyptian pendant
Why everyone on board is dead in a seemingly unrelated variety of ways
Why, in general, events that take place on board are bizarre but consistently composed of recognizable elements
Why people are repelled for a time period following an event on the airplane
Why the optometrist from Georgia claims never to have been on a plane, as his plane ride takes place at some point in the future
