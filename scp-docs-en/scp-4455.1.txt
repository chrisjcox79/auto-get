08 Feb 2019 20:07

Yes, this is intended to be silly, but that doesn’t require the lack of internal consistency displayed here. The premise asks that we accept that real life, as it happens, is a “narrative” on which this anomaly can operate. Even if one goes along with the idea that this is a universe where that concept doesn’t even warrant a mention in the report (and that’s a little far from reality for my taste), the Foundation here still fails to take two obvious actions:

1) Don’t document the capture or containment of the anomaly - there is then no narrative to abridge.

2) Point out to the anomaly that a record of 29 containment attempts and then breach reports is itself a lengthy narrative, as the Foundation won’t stop trying. If the anomaly’s goal is summarisation, it might be a shorter story if it immediately submitted to capture and stayed there. The end.
