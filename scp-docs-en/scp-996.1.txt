24 Aug 2010 23:09

If it has zero mass and zero inertia, it's path would involve 0/0.

I'd expect it to be affected. If you take the limit as its mass approaches zero, it's affected. Also, General Relativity suggests that gravity is a fictitious force. It would be more accurate to say that an object in freefall isn't what's accelerating, the ground is.

It could just have a very tiny mass. If it's a membrane that somehow grew to immense area, it would have a mass comparable to an electron.

I wonder why it doesn't pass through solid objects. If it has no charge, it can't be the electromagnetic force, which is what normally keeps you from falling through the ground. My guess would be the electroweak force. I don't really know much about that though. The big question is why it has friction. I wouldn't expect that you could hold it with clamps.
