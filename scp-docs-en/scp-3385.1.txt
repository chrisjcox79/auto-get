First and foremost, not everything needs to be a mystery. The purpose of this story isn't about the mystery, it's about the death of hope.

+Spoilers

–block

Not everything gets wrapped up in neat little packages. The Foundation may never know which dimension this comes from, there are uncountable amounts of permutations through the multiverse, and this was just one of them.

In my headcanon there are uncountable mysteries in the Universe and while the Foundation spends trillions every year on research, not all of them will ever be explored.

I wanted to tell a story about how people persevered, tried, and ultimately failed. The guilt and shame of carrying the weight of all that expectation, of knowing the hope given is absolutely false, and yet keeping up appearances anyway? This was the point of story.

The war was lost long before the Far Horizon ever set out and everyone knew that. The whole Far Horizon project was a last ditch effort to try and save someone, anyone. But even that was a pipe dream, because there weren't enough people that could be saved to make the project worthwhile.

EVEN THEN, the  of people who would go was mired in corruption and desperation. Everyone who got on that boat knew that, but they got on anyway.

This isn't a mystery story. It isn't a story about good triumphing over evil, or even evil triumphing over good. It is a story about greed, corruption, desperation, hope, and the wholesale nihilistic reality that all of it was a lie from the very beginning.

It most certainly wasn't written to , and I resent that phrase being used in just about every way it is possible for me to resent it.
