"Capacitor Bank Zero"? "Warehouse Zero"? I'm going to have to say that many people frown upon that kind of naming, as it's a blatant attempt to inflate the importance of your SCP by trying to imply that it's somehow "the original" or "the secret", much like "Patient Zero".

Aside from that, the article seems to suffer from being a bit long and meanders through several side topics that don't seem to help the basic concept. Do you really need that much technobabble to explain how it infiltrates a house and electrocutes individuals? Does it really need to "hunt" certain individuals, making them paranoid in the process? (Besides, that just borders on "things what make people go crazy".) Do you really need the association with St. Elmo's Fire?

Finally, the Special Containment Procedures section is far too long. It's not a comprehensive explanation of how something is contained, it's a quick and dirty briefing in case something goes horribly wrong and we need to know RIGHT NOW how to make sure something is safely contained. Try to keep it at two paragraphs or less if at all possible.

Regarding "things what make you crazy": I think that sometimes, because we're so conditioned to watch out for this, people assume TWMYC is the situation every time an SCP causes mental problems. As far as I can see, there's two ways SCP-induced crazyness goes:

Type A: Primary Effect Insanity

-A Type A effect is the direct effect of the SCP, for example, SCP-AAA is a deck of cards that makes you depressed. That is exactly its effect, the insanity caused defines why it is an SCP. Generally, Type A insanity is a sign of bad writing.

Type B: Secondary Effect Insanity

-A Type B effect is extrapolated from exposure to the SCP, i.e. the SCP does not make you insane, the knowledge of it does. For example, SCP-BBB is a DVD that shows you how you will die. It doesn't physically make you insane, but that knowledge could be bad for your mental health. Generally, Type B insanity is a sign of, if not good writing, at least not bad writing.

Basically, I think the issue is that whenever people see insanity of any kind in an SCP, they assume that it's Type A.

Focus on the point you are trying to make or the reaction you want to get from the reader, and cut out or move anything that does not promote that reaction even if it is perfectly logical. Fold

A watery explanation

21 Jul 2011 16:56

Joules are energy. , to quote the almighty Wikipedia.

When a charge is stored in a capacitor, there's no current.

Imagine a water tower. When filled with water, it has the potential to create a flow of water. Same goes for a charged capacitor - it has a potential to create an electrical flow.

When you open the valve, water starts flowing and you have a current. The speed at which water flows through the pipe is roughly the equivalent of an ampere. The energy the water flows with is enough to propel the water at a given speed. That's Joules.

Farads, in this case, are the amount of water that can be stored in this tower.
