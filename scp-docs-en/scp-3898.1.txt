There are three kinds of SCPs, generally speaking, in which I have no idea what is going on.

First, the badly written. Nobody really knows, except possibly the writer.

Second, the well written but oblique in a way that challenges me. The Foundation knows, but I don't unless there's a hint in the comments.

Last, the well written but cryptic in a way that challenges all observers. The Foundation is trying hard, and so am I.

I put this in the last category. There's enough to give an idea, but not enough to really know. And that's what makes this a good SCP.
