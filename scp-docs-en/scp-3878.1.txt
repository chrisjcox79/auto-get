I have two minor quibbles that you're enthusiastically encouraged to pass off as stylistic choices:

The conditioning program uses a strict regiment of psychotropic drugs, electroconvulsive therapy and, at the conclusion of the program, trepannation to create an extremely strong anti-concept within the subject's mind.
附加记录：    

I seem to recall the canonical spellings are trepanning or trepanation.
If I'm not mistaken, a regiment is (in the US) a military unit consisting of 2000-4000 combatants, wheras a regimen is a regulated course of medication, diet, exercise, and so on. That one is perhaps a bit questionable, because regiment used as a verb can mean the act of organizing according to a strict pattern.
