Fold

07 Feb 2012 23:41

Because the effect isn't particularly complex. Subjects don't perceive anything that isn't actually there- no one sees a face, or hands, or really anything other than a lamp. What the effect does do is instill is the simple, unshakable premise , even if that person has to be unplugged and carried along when you want to take him anywhere. Since one does not expect a person to be a lamp under sane circumstances, the discrepany generally doesn't register.

However, a sufficiently observant subject may well notice that something is strange if put in the right situation or posed the right question, and realize that Daniel is, in fact, a lamp; but the impression of personhood is so strong that the realization becomes moot.

Since you're the one to ask, I'm suddenly reminded of your grandmother's  from that one tale.
