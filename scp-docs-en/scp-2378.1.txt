I appreciate the feed back, in regards to B and D, I thought I had included statements correlating to what you've said. Guess I didn't, I'll see if I can fix the wording there to indicate as much. As far as the Technical Jargon goes, it continues to surprise me what is and is not considered Jargon, which varies based from person to person. The text is a bit dense and long, I agree, but as far as Jargon, in comparison to the the previous drafts, it is quite trimmed.

So the linked individual being a foundation employee was, honestly, on my part completely intentional, same goes for the discovery. There's no clear indicator as to why it happens like this in-universe, I thought about making a more clear connection, however after some debate I decided it would be better to leave the reader thinking about what might be really going on here, if whatever is behind the anomaly knows about the foundation, and is guiding them in some manner.

It's also important to remember that full sequencing of the human genome didn't occur until 2003, however since foundation techniques and technology is usually at least a decade ahead of the general public they were able to employ it early. An increase in Class 1 events lead to concern that something might happen, and so they employed this novel technique to try and see if something was happening on a genetic level. What did they find? That this thing was genetically identical to one of their operatives, an operative, who, worked around the entity and was recently diagnosed with Colon cancer.

I appreciate your feedback Kalinin, and want to lay your fears to rest in regards to  and focusing more on the story the SCP has to tell, and what impacts their individual anomalous effects have. I focused more heavily on the square cube law and technical aspects in this and the previous article primarily because that's what I'm used to writing, and to establish myself on site with such high standards it became apparent that I would need to stick to my strengths and work on developing my narrative ability within clinical tone. From this point moving forward I will be attempting to balance narrative against detail, in a different manner from what you've seen so far to avoid the repetition and similarity you see between the two current pieces.

It's unfortunate that you mention the Prion article, as it's pretty clear from the writing that that particular piece was trying to play off the relative success of 2946. I critiqued that piece before it was posted, and attempted to guide the writer into a different direction from where I had tread, as well as correct his incorrect scientific assumptions. It's even more unfortunate that he only half listened to my critique, and left many of the issues I first pointed out unattended, while adding an exacerbated experiment log that was verbatim a direct copy of 2946's format.

Again, I reassure you that this won't become a formula from which to milk upvotes.

Nitpicking

Iscience

Just a forewarning some of this is just like, my opinion, man. I tend to phrase things in very deliberate ways when writing to help the flow, so feel free to disregard semantics-related bits unless you wanna go for it.

Personnel are to avoid physical interaction with SCP-2378-4 iterations at all costs.
附加记录：    

I would rearrange this to be 

Entry into SCP-2378-D is restricted to aerial corrosion-proof drones.
附加记录：    

What kind of material would be needed for this? Maybe put a footnote with recommended materials.

Personnel should avoid irritation of interior elements of SCP-2378.
附加记录：    

Another bit I'd rearrange; 

Each of these organisms is capable of sensing large concentrations of sugar, even in a human host, and will engage in aggressive predatory behavior to secure these sources.
附加记录：    

This sentence is definitely kind of wonky to me. Maybe  Although the last bit of mine is also pretty weird. Maybe footnote it? Also, what do they do with the rest of the human body bits?

viscous maroon or dark-blue/purple colored fluid compositionally similar to human plasma.
附加记录：    

Human plasma is actually a light amber colour! And if you mean deoxygenated blood, it only really ever gets to a dark maroon colour. The cells of veins appear blue because they are, but the blood within is still primarily red. Here's a visual example; the darker sample is deoxygenated.
