31 Jan 2019 12:59

My third attempt, and we all know that third time's the charm! I took the criticism I've gotten on my earlier attempts and tried making this one more a story rather than just a spooky thing. I left the appearance of the conductors vague for two reasons: so that people could make up their own looks, and because I wasn't sure where to put it in without making the article look awkward. (Or rather, even more awkward)

Anyways, it's my first time using a picture and as far as I can tell it's ok to use as long as I link the source:

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Coast_Line

EDIT:  I changed some parts of the interview (and removed the mention of the 6-hour fight). Mainly I changed it to reinforce an idea I had that was contradicted by the old interview: the conductors don't understand the concepts of decay and death. They don't know what death is, or what it's like for something to decay. They don't, and neither does the train or the railway. They just barely wrap their heads around our world being a finite ball.
