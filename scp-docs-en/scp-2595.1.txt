I understand where you were trying to go, but what is being mentioned here is that the doctor's tone is not natural. If you are a scientist/doctor/researcher trying to interview someone, it would be within your best interests to remain precise, clinical, and respectful. In other words, this doctor has no reason to have an emotional response to some anomalous being he/she is interviewing for the first time. Not only does it sound odd (the interview), but it sounds as if the instance and doctor are having some beef we don't know about.

If this instance willingly turned itself in, then it should provide answers (or at the very least provide a hint of some sort). Similarly, the doctor is expected to question the instance normally.

It seems that you are also inclined to explain your intentions behind what you wrote and why in the comments. This is good. However, whatever emotional response you are trying to elicit is poorly defined under a random addendum and stilted interview.
