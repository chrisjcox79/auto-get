Fold

22 Apr 2012 07:58

One of my pet-peeve cliches that's not on the Big List. Something that enforces the way people think the world is or should be.

SCP-1096 reifies the stereotype of the woman who fills herself with plastic because she has a rigid plastic personality, who has nothing going for her but her looks and so turns herself into a plastic doll.

The effects of SCP-1096 are broadly similar to what you should expect from undergoing plastic surgery inside SCP-1000-J. SCP-288 operates on a similar principle of trying to conform perfectly to a narrow-minded set of expectations and Gone Horribly Right.

Dunno if that clears anything up. It's more a visceral intuition than a well-reasoned theoretical framework of discourse.

ETA: Some of the other comments in this thread are illustrative, I think.

This SCP explains a lot…
