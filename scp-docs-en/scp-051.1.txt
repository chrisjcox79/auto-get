Damn if you didn't find a good picture to work with…

Agreed with both of the above:

I wouldn't do any experiments with it… but it's begging for a pre-containment incident report.

The article doesn't give any reason that the average, non-pregnant female would be particularly threatened here.

Finally:

" and the characters are equal to a known early dialect of Japanese."

Imma put on my grammar Nazi cap for a minute… equal isn't quite right for this since it's usually used for quantities, it's like saying "they are equal in appearance".

I'd suggest: similar, identical, comparable, etc.

or even "and the characters have been identified as a known early dialect of Japanese."

Overall it's compelling, and when I first saw the picture I thought "oh sh*t, here we go". But the effects aren't quite compelling enough without hearing more about that demon…
