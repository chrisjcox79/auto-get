I found myself tossing this one back and forth in my head. The read itself was excellent and you really got to show off your prose skills here, so much so that I found myself upvoting by the time I finished.

Then, I went back and reread, and that led to me getting really hung up on that final note from Gillespie. In that sense, I find myself agreeing with Rounderhouse above. The article smacked me in the face with , to a degree that I honestly have trouble ignoring. It stretched my suspension of disbelief just a tiny bit farther than I was willing to let it go, and that ultimately led me to novoting here, despite my enjoyment of the writing itself.

If I may offer a personal suggestion here, I think an exit interview with Gillespie (perhaps just before his final amnestization?) would be a better way to have this conversation with your readers. It would forego a lot of the (frankly excellent) prose that Gillespie displays in his note, but it would be far more verisimilar. Just food for thought.
