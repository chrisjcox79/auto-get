I want to love this, but I can't.

The logs, for what they are, are incredibly well written, with this sense of sweet spot that 173 pioneered. But both feel like salad dressing to an article sparse with actual content, building for a climax that never comes.

The SCP doesn't feel complete. The story presented is great for building up tension and mood, but feels meandering and pointless with the anticlimactic ending. They finally reach the end, things happen, someone is affected by the anomaly, they go out, infectee tries to go back in, doesn't go far and then is a bad idea, and despite the quality of the writing, this SCP doesn't salvage it. It's intriguing, I suppose, but far too cliche, with not enough done to set it apart or give it any character.

Ultimately, it's a well-written but incomplete story.

Fold

27 Sep 2018 23:01

This is one of those things that I think has to strike the right balance. An SCP doesn't have to be explained or inferable (though personally, I feel an intriguing and compelling explanation can be better than a mystery ending, but that's an opinion). Hanging your hat on if it provides sufficient enough information for the imagination to work with a direction. The meat and potatoes of that sort of mystery comes when there's the right amount of detail to reach speculative conclusions rather than just wild guessing.

I feel like this one just missed the mark. Not as badly as some others I've seen, to be sure - the effort was made, you can see it was trying to get to that point, but it just never made it all the way there. I don't finish the SCP feeling like I know enough facts to have an idea what's going on - not even enough for an educated maybe - and that makes the 'use your imagination' theme fall flat. It's done well enough to avoid a downvote but not well enough for an upvote. That said, I think the author's on the right path, and I hope they hone their technique because I think they can deliver.
