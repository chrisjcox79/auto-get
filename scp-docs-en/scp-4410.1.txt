HOLY MOLY I PROCRASTINATED AND JUST GOT THIS IN ON TIME. I HAD TO COLDPOST.

There will be more content to come.

Well, an Edit to this comment is a long time coming.

First off, here's my author page if you're interested in my other work: http://www.scp-wiki.net/author-page-zzuxon

At the risk of sounding like I'm tooting my own horn, I adore how 4410 turned out. Everything was riding against it. It's a pataphysics SCP, which many on the site have an automatic distaste for. It's an entry to the 4000 contest, so standards are automatically higher than your everyday skip. It is very ambitious, and therefore very easy to screw up. The basic premise is  which has also been the premise of many a new user coldpost that got soundly elbow dropped into deletion. Most of all, it was a coldpost itself.

In spite of all that, it is now my highest rated SCP by a wide margin. +67 may not seem like much, and it isn't, really. But I honestly doubted I would ever hit +50, and now I have, on the work I feel is the best and most worthy to have my highest rating.

The idea for 4410 originated in seeing a joke on /r/DankMemesfromSite19 which suggested that by Series XI, cognitohazards would crawl out of your computer screen and punch you in the face. The joke of course was about the way that SCPs and site expectations have tended to become more complex over time. I imagined site users eventually figuring out how to make actual anomalies to satisfy the communities hunger for increasingly sophisticated and immersive fiction. I originally conceptualized it as a joke 001 proposal, then realized it could be portrayed seriously as well, and thought that it would be my serious 001 proposal one day. One day being far off, because I was very intimidated by how high-concept the idea was. I thought I'd screw it up if I didn't have enough SCPs under my belt. But when I learned the 4000 contest theme was history, I knew I had to write it before I felt ready. I didn't feel like I was a good enough writer to do the concept justice, and I kind of still don't. But I do think it works, it does what I wanted it to do. I'm proud.

I want to give big thanks to Roget and /u/modulum83 (Probably a user here too, someone please tell me who he is on wikidot), whose History of the Universe and Modern Introduction to the SCP Foundation: Part 2 respectively were invaluable resources and influences for this skip. In particular, a portion of Dr. Benson's second note was based so heavily on a portion of Modern Introduction to the SCP Foundation: Part 2 that I want to attribute it as a source.

Also big thanks to FloppyPhoenix for the slick AF Pataphysics CSS.

IMO Humes and SRAs make as much scientific sense as radiation detection does. The sci-fi part is the fact that the technology is currently impossible IRL, but the mechanism makes sense. Create specialized equipment to detect something  develop a unit based upon the response and quantify your observations accordingly.

Pataphysics doesn't hold up in the same way.

Concept: 

A Multi-U look at this would have them say

The Multi-U approach acknowledges that reality depends upon frame of reference. There could be a reality construct in which a bunch of Eldritch gods are sitting around in their underwear playing chess, and the Foundation finds out that each of their pieces represents a famous Foundation action in history (or some shit; I'm spitballing for the sake of example). They would not all of a sudden define themselves as  Why? Because there could be another all-explaining situation that reveals itself. There are infinite possibilities and infinite universes and there's no reason for the Foundation to treat one as truth just because it sounds believable. Pataphysics is acknowledging our (IRL) reality construct as more real than the other ones and defining our actions on the wiki as the only real nature of existence for these characters. This is the opposite of the narrative value of having multiple reality constructs, to me.

If we take pataphysics to heart and believe what it says: Now we know what's objectively real. That's it. It's over. Hi, I'm gonna throw my hat in on this here a bit.

+ranting

-AWAY!!!

To me, everything Cyantreuse is talking about here is exactly the reason why I.H.P.'s Proposal exists. Yes, most of the  material in the SCP Foundation is based on concepts that make mechanical sense, but are technologically impossible. Humes, like you said, are a great example of this.

Pataphysics (at first glance) does not follow this rule. It isn't based on mechanical logic, and isn't something that could be technologically possible in the future.

Or is it?

This is exactly the argument that IHP's Proposal makes (and, ironically, we see a similar argument at the end of this article, too). IHP asserts that the Swann line  isn't the end of the story and never was. There is far more to investigate about this subject, and in IHP's Proposal we see a bunch of researchers only beginning to scratch the surface. In 4410 over here, Saxon suggests that some of this stuff might not be anomalous at all, but is instead a natural evolution of the writing process.

In other words, this is uncharted territory. Things like Humes, while foreign, were couched in familiar-enough scientific concepts for Foundation researchers to apply the scientific method. But no one, in our reality or theirs, has stopped to consider the possibility that storytelling might be a measurable universal constant.

What if Pataphysics is a form of science? What if writing, like space and time, is a documentable natural phenomenon, and we're just waiting for the Einstein equivalent of fiction writers to come along and paint us the whole picture? It's just my interpretation, but to me that's exactly what articles like this one and IHP are suggesting.

Basically, my point is, I don't think Pataphysics says 

As a side note, all the Pataphysics articles I've read have done (in my opinion) a good job of staying vague in the right places. Most of them reference multiple reality constructs that contain the Foundation, and imply that the meta stuff only applies to some of them (or maybe only one of them). Most Pataphysics articles give you a way in-article to disregard it as canon, or otherwise treat it as a canon for a different reality construct.

On a related note, from what I've seen, Multi-U and Pataphysics support each other more than anything else. Both of them use the multiverse concept to full effect. Actually, no sucking up here I swear, one of my favorite meta articles is one of yours, for basically this exact reason.

I've definitely gone on long enough, so final thoughts here. To me, one of the core tenants of the SCP Foundation is the mantra that  So, to me, as long as Pataphysics continues to respect the Multi-U approach and acknowledge that there's reality constructs where there is no metafiction, then there's no harm in keeping it up.

Anyway, thanks for taking the time to read. No harm intended, just sharing my thoughts.
