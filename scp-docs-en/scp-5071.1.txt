It's not bad. The writing is solid, but the story itself was just eh. I think the number of logs hurt it in my opinion, simply because there are so many of them, and they don't seem different enough narratively speaking to justify how many of them there are, or their length. Additionally, the core premise isn't super new territory, nor does it do much with the concept that feels exciting.

The reveal of the backstory at the end of the piece feels like a  sort of slapped together to post-facto us into the emotional reveal, but it's done so abruptly and all at the end in such a way that it doesn't do anything for me.

Fold

19 Jul 2020 19:05

I don't think it's really the actual number of logs in my opinion so much as it is their narrative purpose. I think all five logs more or less accomplish the same thing in the scope of the piece-expanding the feeling of weirdness/body horror, and I would argue that that makes most of them redundant.

Five logs is totally fine if they dramatically expand the story/push it forward. 6, 7, even 8 logs would also be fine if they could do the same. In this particular case though, I don't get a sense that they do much that's all that different from each other. As a result, it just feels a little bloated.
