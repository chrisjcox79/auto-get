AUTHORdrama)

You'vewarned!

This story is a little bit personal, and for that I apologize. It's based on some actual shit I've dealt with and seen (particularly the bits with the judge). I've never been admitted into a place like this, but I've been in Lindsey's position enough times in my life to get a vague notion of what's going on inside them.

I'm definitely not here to start up a conversation about psychiatry in America — and my heart goes out to anyone working in the field (it's certainly a thankless task, and I've meant enough wonderful psychiatrists to know they exist, they're saving lives, and they need as much support as we can bear to provide them with). But some of these places fucking terrify me. In part, I wrote this SCP to try and show you why.

There's no final twist, no skeleton in the closet, no 'they were ghosts all along!'. Cragstaff is just all the misery I've seen in these places poured into one horrible fucking hole. And that hole will never, ever let you leave.

I have no idea if the two of them made it out. I hope they did.

I've been thinking about this, and I think you might be spot-on. I think I'm going to edit it to remove references to shifting geography, or waking up in a different bed:

+block

–block

The only reasons I added that in was to get across that the building contains way more people than is immediately obvious but I think seeing the different faces every day gets that across just as well.

(Also, I want this place to be as non-anomalous as possible. The horror isn't that it shifts around, or that the place is spooky; that makes it seem way more interesting than it is. The horror is its banality  that this is where you spend the rest of your life. A series of faces you'll never recognize, living in a fog without hope, waiting to die. Enormous stretches of crippling, crushing boredom and tedium punctuated by tiny, acute bursts of terror, paternalism, and cruelty. The shifting geography and waking-up-in-a-new-bed don't really add to that horror, I don't think, and therefore should probably be cut. I can still have her bring up the different faces, which can still be used against her in front of the judge.)
