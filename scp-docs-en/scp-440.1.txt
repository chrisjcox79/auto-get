I really like the idea, but I'm nervous about how this will be received. Couldn't really get any feedback from chat (Not that I'm bitter or anything, just whining a little), which didn't bode well.

Full disclosure: This SCP was inspired by Kingdom of Loathing's Rock Monster Event, reversing the order (Sand makes Monsters, instead of Monsters Drop Sand) and trying to be an example of an SCP that doesn't have a hostile attitude towards humanity, but can't be let out because it's nature is inherently dangerous to the ecology.

Also, the 'additional documents' are three options I equally would like to work on, but given that I'm not sure how people would react to the SCP itself, I didn't pen any of them yet. Feel free to say which one(s) you think flesh out the concept the best.

There are other 'expanding ecology' SCPs, and other sand-based SCPs, making this one seem kind of redundant. Also, the language is repetitive in places ("an environment described as similar to that of a deep-sea environment."), some of the word choices are bizarre (You don't "inject" sand into other sand) and there are some things in there that just make me go 'now that shouldn't be that way' - in particular, the given reason for the assimilation process being unknown. There needs to be a better reason than the grains being too small. Its sand - relatively speaking, in terms of observability in a research environment, it's huge. Most of these things are fixable, but ultimately it just doesn't seem like anything that hasn't been done. Downvoted.

Fold

03 May 2010 05:08

Feedback, yay!

I used the term injected because, owing to the fact that the stuff floats, you can't just toss a few grains in and hope that it makes the seed sand into 440. So I imagined that the Foundation inserted 440 into the sand in a way similar to the way a kid makes bubbles in his drink with a straw: loading some 440 at the top end of the straw and blowing the 440 some depth into the seed sand.

90 micrometers is huge? I chose a size because it was really small, and I figured that the Foundation would try and use as little of the original sample as possible; perhaps only a few grains. Because I made it so small, though, I wrote myself into a corner concerning observing the stuff.

03 May 2010 08:09

Regardless of what you say for origins, this seems like a logical derivative of the Fossil Sea, but that's okay. It needs some editing for tone and style, though…

You don't say 0g in a scientific report, you say "microgravity environment" or "constant freefall", and, unless you're capable of manipulating vectors, you don't place forces upon something, but the forces act on the object. Conversion doesn't top off, it ceases.

Try to remove the footnotes, they are not exactly necessary the way you use them, that information can be incorporated into the body.

Holding off on voting for now, I want to see an edited version of the article, with better tone and a bit more rigorous science. This looks like it has promise, just needs some more work.

Fold

06 May 2010 02:38

No, I actually don't think the total conversion of all sand on Earth into 440 is an XK. I looked at the "K-class scenarios" thread again, and this seems more like a localized (read: limited to Earth only) CK-class Restructuring scenario. XK is when the Earth (and probably a sizeable chunk of the universe) is completely destroyed. YK is like the Reality Bomb from Doctor Who: it blows up all dimensions, leaving nothing left. ZK is basically a YK that bleeds into the real world: "a fictional disaster so powerful it even obliterates the author". So yeah, a planet covered in 440 isn't quite XK, in my opinion. The Earth would still exist, but who knows if the sand creatures would leave humans alive?
