My main problem with the article is the lack of a narrative. The concept is interesting enough, it just needs some backstory and story progression to really carry the article, as most successful articles these days have a reason why they happen, or a hint at it at least. The descriptions of the tests also seem a bit lackluster in my opinion, as they always revolve around a D-Class getting sent back home and doing some incredibly over the top thing that would definitely register on the Foundations radar.

The clinical tone is pretty on point, along with formatting and SPaG. It just needs more of a narrative to drive it forward.
