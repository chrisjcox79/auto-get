Random - Yes, I realize that in-universe it would make more sense for the description of the Barnum Event to be in the description, and it was a narrative choice to place it afterwards. My beta readers generally liked the experience of figuring out what was going on as they were reading it, and describing the Barnum Event before the test would rob them of that.

For the sake of the narrative, I'm willing to believe that the researcher who updated this had written everything after the experiment separately and just copy pasted it into the main document to minimize his workload. I'm sure he'll get called out on his sloppiness eventually.

Speaking of beta readers, they were Lazar Lyusternik
