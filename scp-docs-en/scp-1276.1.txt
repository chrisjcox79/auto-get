Fold

26 Feb 2013 18:55

Two problems:

Accelerating from 0 to 0.01c, or 1% of the speed of light, is an increase of 10,792,528 kilometers per hour. Have you ever tried to catch a flying bowling ball? Assuming you have someone holding a 5kg probe, that probe will suddenly feel like it weighs like neutronium. The best case scenario, it snaps all your fingers. Worst case scenario, you are now unarmed.

Now, remember relativity. Going from zero to 0.01c is effectively the same acceleration as going from 0.01c to zero. That means that the probe itself, even if you could hold onto it, just underwent the same kinetic force as if it had impacted a solid surface at 1% the speed of light. Your average camera won't even survive being dropped from a hot air balloon, much less while going at relativistic speed.

The death-by-acceleration only occurs if the acceleration is being imparted unevenly into the material. After all, what breaks you apart when you accelerate/decelerate is that your different bits don't all achieve the final speed at the same time.

So, assuming the human doesn't die instantly on acceleration nor tears out of their own clothes, either have them hold a camera close enough that it counts as part of them, or put it in their mouths.
