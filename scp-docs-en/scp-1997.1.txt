26 Jun 2012 18:10

Seems like an excellent idea, and well written. I had a couple of question points as I read. After describing the unfolding, I immediately wanted to know if it could be refolded. You imply that it can be during the description, as some activities are done inside but the paper was found in original dimension condition, but don't say so directly until the addendum.

Also, I'm not sure the weight would really be the issue in unfolding to that size - even a sheet at the largest size you indicate probably wouldn't require 50 people to flip, I shouldn't think. It seems the unwieldiness of flipping something that big and floppy would be the worst part.

Two totally quibbling points. Well done!

If it remains a .5cm wodge, is this necessary? Surely there would be at most a couple of visible folds each time.
附加记录：    

Some folds and some page corners, but yet it's impossible to count how many there are at any one time. I preferred that over having to specify how the number of pages would have to change during an unfolding.

I note the crossword in the image does not share the space exploration theme, though I think that's a nit-pick :) Had a quick Google to see if I could find any photos of a 'world's largest crossword' with space references and came up empty, so please feel free to disregard.
附加记录：    

Yeah. It was the best image I could find so I had to make some allowances for non-space puzzles. Puzzles at higher numbers can just be simple puzzles, but the general trend is for more complex and weird.
