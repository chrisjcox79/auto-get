16 Mar 2014 05:49

The description of the SCP's effects doesn't feel clinical enough for something implied to only have been gleaned from second-hand reports from non-catatonic test subjects. There are a few decent ideas here, but not enough holding them together, for example:

-Why present potential visitors with a key if they're just going to be forcibly abducted anyway?

-What significance does said key have after disappearing from the subject's grasp?

-Is there any sort of pattern to the after-effects? i.e. is there any relation between the subject's personality or actions while in the gallery and what happens to them before they leave? (If the three effects are tied to the whole  thing, you probably could have expanded on the concept more)

I can see a budding idea here, though; let's run this through SCP-914 and see what happens:

+block

–block

Otherworldly lich queen  of loss being proportional to time spent in the gallery and/or how much the subject supposedly enjoyed being there, or alternately she takes something as punishment for not appreciating her clearly superior craftsmanship and artistic vision, because if you cannot use your sight, emotions, or whatever to properly enjoy her obvious artistic genius, then they are wasted on you and you do not deserve to have them.

As for the third aftereffect- who knows? Perhaps if you do actually enjoy her work to a satisfactory degree, she will reward your superior tastes by offering a position by her side forever in her court as one of her UnFaced slaves, meanwhile you awaken in meatspace suddenly aware you are missing something- namely that  you- and that said spark has actively abandoned you for the Queen and you futilely rave and plead for it to return to you until you get euthanized or you die (because metaphorically speaking when Engineering gets put in the captain's chair while the captain jumps ship, who's currently maintaining the engines?)
