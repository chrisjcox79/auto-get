Co-Authors Note

01 Aug 2018 04:01

The oldest mystery in Human mythology is the end of the Epic of Etana. A wise king, desperate to provide his people an heir to stave off the chaos to come. In the real world, we know he succeeded. But we don't know how. MayD and I have provided an answer, though perhaps not one that does justice to Etana himself.

The translations presented here are heavily modified versions of real translations of the Sumerian King's list and the steles we have about the Epic of Etana. Even Sargon's boasts are about 50% true to a contemporary text, though I'm hardly the first to re-purpose that apocryphal tale (glances at story of Moses).

There's some crosslinks in here, though the text color of the Css makes them a bit hard to spot at first glance.

Lazarus is SCP-2000, it's crosslinked but its possible to miss the link due to the aforementioned text color similarity. The Foundation wants the Seed of Life to complete the Bright-Zartion Hominid Replicators used to rapidly create Human clones en masse, but in searching for it they discover more than they wanted to. Humanity is inherently anomalous, and creates anomalous phenomena just by existing. That nature was pawned off to an unknown and hostile deity, whose identity is up to you but I did leave a couple clues that could be used to form interpretations, in exchange for the Seed of Life (which if you know anything about Sumerian myths, its pretty much the universal macguffin in 'em. Even the epic of Gilgamesh ends with him trying and failing to find it) and the more metaphorical order of dynastic succession.

Many millennia in the future, the rising number of anomalies (a plot point often mentioned in tales and such on this site as a tongue in cheek reference to the ever increasing number of skip entries irl) is a sign that the mortgage is beginning to run out. But if human nature is the problem, and the Foundation can now make Humans en masse and attempt to modify them according to their whims Well, I'll just end with this quote from 2000:

Why did we have to build this thing?

When did we do it?

How long have we been doing it?

Do we even know?!

I absolutely love this. The resonating tie-ins are so far-reaching, so complete, and so comprehensive.

It requires a really in-depth knowledge of the Foundation *and* at least a cursory knowledge of real myth to fully distinguish what's going on. For my part, I definitely thought there was more going on with the whole  thing, more than Etana just literally being unable to conceive children, until I researched it and realize that was part of the real epic.

It's a bit of obscuring opaqueness to what's going on that might make some people miss the  so to speak, but you might be okay with it - it makes picking through this like picking through the symbolism of old real-world myths to suss what's actually going on.

All in all though, it's a weighty piece that beautifully fulfills the history theme, makes my brain happy, and makes my heart excited.

I hope this goes far.
