17 Feb 2018 21:53

I get that this is supposed to be a cognitohazard that makes the Foundation think these crimes are more complicated than they really are. But that isn't what you've given us. The fact that the supposed murderer doesn't actually exist (maybe? unclear) means that we don't have anything to focus on as an SCP.

Instead we're left with bumbling idiot detectives for no clear reason. That problem, combined with the self-aware tone of that last interview, makes the article read like a -j that falls flat.

Downvote for now, but I think this idea could be fantastic if you fixed those problems. It just needs to work as horror instead of failing to work as comedy.
