17 Sep 2020 22:43

There is, actually! It matches the translated text from an earlier version of the article, but it's changed since, so it doesn't correspond to the current text. With the exception of the bottom-right quadrant, the symbols are a personal stylization of Elian Script, a unique way of writing the English alphabet to appear alien and unusual. The bottom-right quadrant is a series of symbols i made up for numbers, as the translation originally had a numeric code in earlier versions of the draft.

If you go look it up, you might even be able to tell what the original text was. :)

I have my issues with the execution of this article, but I'm willing to upvote because hahahaha that's fucking awesome. I'm a sucker for this sort of intrigue-layered period tech story - I know that's a very niche genre, but there's a reason I love Halt and Catch Fire.

The description is suitably frission inducing on its own, and the idea of the electronics system fusing into someone's back makes zero sense but goddamn if it isn't cool. My biggest actual issue is the dialogue - mostly the doctor's, since the AIs is just computer shit. Like, in what world is the average reaction to someone chewing their cheek off  saying nothing at all is often infinitely more intriguing than Saturday morning cartoon villain dialogue.

But the rest of the stuff is very much in line with my tastes. Computers working with stuff humans can't quite comprehend  it almost reminded me of Age of Ultron, amusingly. Good stuff, just rather rough around the edges.
