Fold

10 Nov 2011 05:04

If it were a normal room, I'd agree, but do know how disorienting it is to be in a room with a really high ceiling? Especially when that room has patterns on it? There's an art gallery in my town with a 21-foot-high ceiling and art covering the walls (which I think acted as justification for the high ceiling when I thought the same thing you guys did). Also, what's your first instinct to do when you see an abnormally high ceiling? Stare up at it and marvel at how high it is. In a room like this, would you really want to stand there are marvel knowing its effects?

Also, ever open up a 3d level editor and build a solid cube? There's a very eerie feeling of isolation in that cube where the height is equal to the width; low ceilings feel like there's at least a roof or you're on the ground. All that negative space adds to that feeling of uneasiness.

Still, I will consider finding a way to hang a lampshade on/justify the high ceiling.
