I'm having some issues with this skip.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

+Issues

Problem 1:  I find the crossed-out information an annoyance to read. Typically, I would be fine with it, but there are at least five instances of it here. Either the crossed-out information is not important (In which case, why is it included?), or, the crossed-out information is of special importance (In which case, why is there a hurdle to read it?).

Possible Solution A:  I suggest erring on the side of simply not including any of the information that you cross out.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Problem 2:  I'm confused about the -1 instances. Is the assumption that -1 refers to elders who drilled a hole into their cranium? If that is the case, why is that not explicitly stated? I had to reread the paragraph that introduces -1 several times, but still came away from it doubtful that I'd had the right idea.

Possible Solution B:  It would aid the reader if (1)  the process of transformation were stated explicitly (instead of leading the reader to believe an entirely new entity was created  say so.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Problem 3:  There are several instances of imprecise wording, like  is referred to. For instance:

A computing company that creates various anomalous programs and products.
附加记录：    
What kind of anomalous programs do Slum's create?

SCP-5896 is functionally identical to the Windows 95 operating system with various minor cosmetic changes.
附加记录：    
What kind of cosmetic changes?

Occasionally, various computer components will grow within SCP-5896-1 instances; []
附加记录：    
What kind of computer component grows beneath the skin?

These vagueries make it difficult to picture what's being described because they forcibly deprive a reader of imagery. This is not what you want in an SCP unless there is a prescribed reason for it (like a meta infohazardous anomaly associated with the document).

Possible Solution C:  Consider what disadvantage a researcher in-universe might have if they were presented this same (low) level of information on something that's Keter. If your intent is not to elaborate, then you can simply omit the . And I understand that it later specifies one of the computer growths is a -2 instance, but the reader is still led to believe it can cause something else. Instead of promoting imagination, this is enabling confusion, when you can instead word things slightly different and achieve much better effect.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
-Close

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

+Issues

Here are the places where I think the narrative is lacking:

How did the Foundation recognize this was anomalous before they tested it? I don't see why they might have had the copies in their possession in the first place. But because there is no Discovery, this question is left unaddressed. This isn't necessarily a question I feel like I'm working toward as a reader  it instead feels like a lack of development.
Did they really terminate the employment of Dr. Reynolds, yet neither keep track of him after the fact nor amnesticize him?

These two issues are minor, but they paint a bit of a picture of what I might consider Foundation negligence. Now, you can absolutely tell that kind of story  or systemic issues. Narratives about corporate/bureaucratic shortcomings are certainly in no shortage, and they paint a more realistic setting (which I personally favour). But when Foundation negligence is introduced on its own without any real justification, it feels out of character.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
-Close

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Overall:  I can see you were aiming for a short and direct skip. For the most part, I think you succeed! But I think the instances of less-than-adequately executed 'negative space' (where the reader is asked to piece things together on their own instead of being explicitly told things) are less of a feature and more of a bug. Especially considering the gap this creates in the reader's understanding of what's going on, paired with imprecise language. I think this skip has a truly visceral body horror idea about it and would have wanted it to shine more.
