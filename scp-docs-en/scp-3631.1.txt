My entry for Cliché Con 2019. No chance that I'll actually win, but I thought it would be fun to enter anyways. I'm entering under the cliché anomaly  since I've always had a soft spot for monster SCPs - even if so few of them are done well - and they seem to have largely fallen out of favor.

+Explanation

-Expunation

Explaining the story after several requests, and also because I am starting to suspect that people are holding back crit until I do this.

Simon (aka -2, but you probably already knew that) is a reality bender. He doesn't realize it, and he can't control it to any significant degree, but he is. As a kid, he unknowingly created a monster (aka -1), which the Foundation then contains. Simon gets the concussion and forgets about the monster, causing it to go *POOF* and disappear from containment without a trace.

Fast forward, and Simon, now an adult, is shown some of his pre-concussion drawings by his mother. This brings the monster back to life. Fearful of being forgotten again, the monster captures Simon and severs his limbs so he can't escape or fight back. The monster then tries replacing his organs with those of children, thinking it will make him a kid again. But it won't. There's no going back.

There's some more details I have in my headcanon (the relationship between kid Simon and the monster specifically is something I didn't get the chance to explore here), but this is all that can be deduced with what evidence exists on the page. Everything I just explained is hinted at in some way.

Thanks to aismallard, and probably some other people I'm forgetting (really need to start writing this stuff down earlier) for crit!
