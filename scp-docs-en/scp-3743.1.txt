Modern people have been living in SCP-3743-B.
There are 13 sarcophagi and 13 winged humanoids.
Are they cupids? You tell me.
I am taking some artistic liberties with  traditionally called the Hecatoncheires. But by this point in the series, that should have been obvious.

If you are interested in my other works: Grigori Karpin's Author Page, where you can see how these interconnected skips and tales fit into place. My goal is ever to have these articles stand on their own. But the relationships and information change over time, and that is reflected in the character's behavior.

I think this has a decent stride to it, if just a little slow. The description and first moments of the exploration log are a very low ramp up into the more shocking stuff that occurs, that being the incident within the exploration log and the final message from Director Varga. Those two moments are easily the high points and make this piece worth the read.

I understand the amount of world building that's happening on the side is necessary for this bigger picture happening with the majority of these articles, but I can't help but feel like they don't add much to the immediate enjoyment of the article. This time, I think the placement works out in favor to give the reader some breathing space and let those shocking moments stand out. So, I give it an upvote.
