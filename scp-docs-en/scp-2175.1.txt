Why 2175? I looked up some Irish mythology. Someone can call me on this if I’m wrong, of course.

21 = 3 x 7 (two lucky numbers)

17 is said to be mystical.

5 is significant and found in many designs.

An amphiptere flew through town and looked through fruit stalls with a frown

He was so tired of stealing loot that he wanted to eat some fruit

The amphiptere then ate an apple, then he felt the urge to grapple

Then he ate a pair of pears, and tried to climb up some tall stairs

The amphiptere then ate a guava, then he spewed out flaming lava

Then he ate a big strawberry, and made faces that were scary

The amphiptere then ate an orange, but then he crashed into a door hinge

[]

Then he ate a peach for fun, and decided he was done.

The amphiptere flew out of town, with his frown turned upside-down.

Alright. Two references here can be made into easy in-line links that will add a little more wikiwalk depth to the article. Maybe. The first one is turning the Serpent's Hand reference into a link to their Kiryu Labs tales, and a link to those tales would not be too far out of line, I feel. As such, I am recommending the following edits:

Changing this:

Addendum 2175-1: On ██/██/████, seven SCP-2175-1 instances were recovered, each containing a unique verse variation on the lyrics of the ballad “Scarborough Fair”. These recoveries coincided with the appearance of a group of seven individuals on the bridge, one of whom was under Foundation investigation for potential connections to the Serpent’s Hand. Though Foundation personnel were able to recover the documents, none of the seven individuals could be located after their initial sighting. The circumstances of their disappearance are currently unknown.

To this:

Addendum 2175-2: Analysis of the verse variations deemed the lyrics to be non-anomalous. The Scarborough Protocol was created on ██/██/████ by Doctor R. Repeated trials of the behavioral procedure (mentally reciting the recovered verses and focusing only tangentially on the bridge) have demonstrated fairly consistent success: roughly 70% of manifested documents that do result from Foundation proxy activity consist entirely of lyrics from the ballad, with no other information present.
