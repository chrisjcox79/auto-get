I downvoted by the end of the first interview at the sight of some poorly-handled exposition. It didn't feel like a natural development of the conversation, rather, it gave the sense of trying to justify how the SCPF learned of a specific trait detailled in the Description.

Beyond that, the whole illogical and frighteningly cliche  exchange is the type of theatrics I eternally hope dies off for good for how rote it is typically in execution. It adds a needless level of melodrama to humanoid containment, and makes so little sense to think they'd dehumanize a humanoid like that on one hand, but also offer it therapy on the other.

I also feel there's probably a more sensible way to contain its effects beyond hand-wringing over Object Class and sticking it in a stronger room - hand/finger restraints seem like a logical go-to. If he can't articulate in order to grab something, he's incapable of accessing dangerous contraband.

Characterization, too, is a touch wonky: He's so good and righteous as to not attempt to kill his captors, but attempts escape using a lethal weapon?

I lost interest by the conclusion of the Incident Log: seeing another 6 or so collapsibles worth of content I was already having multiple issues with didn't inspire me to tread on.

And there's a number of  mistakes that could stand correcting.

附加记录：    

I'm going to hold you to that. I'll go through the rest later and see if I can say anything else servicable.

Response to 'Longer Response'
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

+block

–block

Yes. The line you quoted is what I was referring to. I do not think it gives the impression you think it does  'trusting of authority figures' is something that comes across already at the start of the conversation.

Rather than revealing character, it felt like the focus with that line was to exposit/reveal a facet of the anomaly.

If the exchange is made to showcase his eagerness to be transparent/helpful (or showing his trust of personnel), then a more smooth response to  should suffice. Instead of blurting out a detail about his power, he could respond by explaining his intentions in fully cooperating:

That's terrible because I'm ass a dialogue, but I hope you get my drift.

I don't think that information  cannot be included at all in conversation, but that the current implementation feels unnatural.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

I'm unsure how I'm supposed to avoid talking about it.
附加记录：    

Easy. Refer to his number in the documentation, use his name to his face. They could inform him of his designation, sure, but the forced stuffiness of calling humanoid skips by their number as a rule, I feel, adds a cliche level of melodrama.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Eh, you mean the bomb, the gun, or the knife?
附加记录：    

The gun. I completely forgot that he had a knife when I made that comment.

Thing is: the escape was clearly planned to some degree. That's evident in him manifesting improvised armor and explosives. This also comes at a time after which he's learnt of his ability to conjure up the impossible  only limited to what he could imagine.

Now, I'm going to sound like a horrible pendant here, but it's this line in particular that elicited an eyeroll:

SCP-4051 appears unable or unwilling to use lethal force against the personnel, attempting to inflict incapacitating but non-lethal injuries to legs, arms, and the torso.
附加记录：    

Because it felt like I was being led by the hand and reassured of his goodness, but in a way that made it an Informed Trait. That is, we don't see his reluctance to do harm in his actions, but rather, by being explicitly told .

To wrap back around to my comment about the extent of his ability/his planning: since he knows he can pretty much get anything from these portals, why not use something non-lethal to begin with? *Show* in his actions that he has no intent to kill, rather than *telling* with such an aside. I've no suggestions off the top of my head, besides something fantastical like, I dunno, lobbing globs of incapacitating goo? Or, something closer to reality: a gun that fires sticky foam?

And quick tangent, but I don't think they'd use tranqs against a humanoid - they could very well inadvertantly kill someone with an overdose like that. Tazers seem like a more sensible non-lethal detainment method.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

I'm a bit new around here. Is it uncommon for authors to delete under-performing but positively-rated files to make something better?
附加记录：    

You can either edit the page, or if you're going to make substantial changes, delete and repost when it's rewritten. I've done both, and I think deletion/repost is better for visibility  there's only so much one can do to bring eyes back to something they've already cast votes on.
