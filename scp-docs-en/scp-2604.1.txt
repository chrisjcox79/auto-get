22 Sep 2014 12:09

The cell is furnished with appropriate sanitary and culinary equipment for prolonged exposure without direct intervention from Foundation personnel.
附加记录：    

It's basically this (from the out of date containment procedures). They were sending D-class in there for up to days at a time, and planning on longer. Once the inadvisability of that became clear, it wasn't worth the trouble to move it from there.

Basically I asked myself what would be simpler, retrofitting a containment locker for exposure, or simply using a cell already built to contain humanoids.

As to what it is, that's something of the mystery of it, though I understand why you'd want to know what it is. Thanks for the feedback!

22 Sep 2014 16:17

Precisely. That was actually the point I was trying to make with this scip. Most fences, or barriers are actually ideas we all adhere to. For example, a traffic light showing red means  because we all say it does, so what would happen if that wasn't the case?

In the same vein, most fences are not physical barriers to movement when faced with a determined person, we don't cross them because it's a concept we share.

I love how this starts out as a simple descriptive compulsion (.

It's a simple setup/punchline format, except that the punchline derives naturally not from any part of the setup, or from the setup in combination with some separate shared reference point, but from another unstated property of the story- in this case, the larger context in which it's being told. Once you give this object to the Foundation, there's only one way events will go; all the end of the article does is remind you what that is.
