I initially read this from a direct link to the ESCP page and I unconditionally enjoyed it. However, upon reading the base SCP and the note about ESCP in the general case, I want to qualify my vote.

I particularly enjoyed the concept of the procedures changing when the stakes are high because I thought of it as part of Envelope Logistics' influence on the Foundation; they were forcing the Foundation to publicize and legitimize EL's messages in times of danger, which is scary because it shows that the Foundation can be reliant on the messages while also giving EL a reason to (causatively?) predict disaster in order to maintain its influence. It fit well with the theme of 2557 being able to actively interfere with Foundation files while remaining at least superficially compliant with the format. If ESCP was something used outside the context of , it would cheapen the impact of this article in my eyes because a lot of why this change feels significant is because it shows how much the Foundation is leaning on an outside power.

If I had started reading this from the base SCP, I don't know that I would have liked it as much. The pacing felt much better in the ESCP and you got the sense that something was wrong very quickly while still being able to read how the machine was used in states of nonemergency via the struck through text.

I loved the concept of market forces influencing an omniscient entity to inform the Foundation and the implication that disasters could provoke short sales was a great kicker to an already compelling article. However, this is the kind of screw that I feel should be specific to Envelope Logistic skips in general (or at the very least articles that are clearly being influenced by a third party). That aside, my initial +1 stands, this was very fun to read.

Thinking about this from your perspective, as someone who read the 'second' page first, it occurs to me that there's not much in the way of information on the first page that isn't on the second. If this were just ESCP-3072, it might be a worthwhile format screw a la Cross Cross City or something, and a read could figure out what the original article looked like by picking out the colored text. The main thing that would be lost by reducing this just to the ESCP format would be the redactions in the ticker tape readout, of course, but there's probably a way or two to make that happen (since it was actually kind of fun).

Point being, I initially came down hard on this, but I don't want to quash experimentation. I think it'd be worth changing things around to make this work better, if, indeed, it ends up not working.
