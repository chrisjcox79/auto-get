Fold

18 Mar 2011 02:16

But the point here, my dear doctor, is that this, like the geth, is not a single consciousness, a "group mind" - each individual retains full individuality. It's just that the more individuals there are in close proximity, the more intelligent each individual becomes. In Mass Effect 2, had Shepard decided to eradicate, say, 50% of the programs running on Legion's platform, the remaining individuals comprising the Legion platform would have been operating at about half their starting capacity.

Group minds are certainly common, as are intelligent individuals with telepathic awareness - the habitues of Callahan's Place and its successors have done this, and even increased their available time to think (almost the same thing as increasing effective IQ) by sharing available processing capacity in each others' brains - but the sort of additive intellect used by these birds is quite uncommon. It may well have been done before, but I'm willing to wager that for those here who have been exposed to the idea previously, said exposure did indeed have to do with the geth. (For instance, despite a lifeline fascination with SF, I was unaware of Hamilton's Edenists.)

I further submit that several of the entries under "non-hive minds" in that Wikipedia article do not belong there - the Fleetmind in Schlock Mercenary, for instance, isn't really a single mind, it's just that the AIs involved turn off the subroutines that slow them down to organic speeds while they communicate, and can thus act and think with greater speed than their "meat glacier" friends, while the Little People in Methuselah's Children, while they do maintain the memories in the group, no longer display the personalities of the individuals absorbed.
