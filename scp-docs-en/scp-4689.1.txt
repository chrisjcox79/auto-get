30 Sep 2019 13:05

The lion has been depicted in culture for over 30,000 years; it's one of the best-known animals on the planet. It's odd that they just got forgotten about after a century; there's an implication that every piece of film, art and literature that ever depicted or referred to lions is gone.

So, the implication could be that there was some sort of event, which not only led to the mass extinction of a good portion of animals on Earth, but also erased the majority of human culture.

Or lions as a species have some sort of anomalous quality; after all, it seems a little odd that one of the most-depicted animals on Earth, culturally, just got forgotten about after a century.

So, the Foundation is either averting an possibly anomalous-induced environmental apocalypse or protecting an anomalous species. Part of their remit is to protect anomalies, not just contain them.
