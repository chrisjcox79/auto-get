How can the Foundation be so sure that this is anomalous? No really, hear me out.

For how few  there are, why couldn't this just be random strokes or seizures?
附加记录：    

Some of the other details aside, for me, the Foundation is smart enough to know that it wasn't some random strokes or seizures because stuff like that is easily or at least can be detected in autopsies.
I could add a footnote explicitly saying that the victims had no prior illness/abnormalities that may have caused the paralysis nor any signs of seizures or strokes.

openblock

closeblock

Nowhere in this article have I read anything in particular that indicates anomalous activity.
附加记录：    

What happened to Harolds was quite anomalous. Even if he was slowly devoured by the insects, he only move after making physical contact with another human. That's weird.

And also these,

SCP-4676 only affects individuals who are alone at the time of onset, and preferentially targets those at least 55 meters from any other human being or human habitation1.
附加记录：    

Most of the victims are found at a very specific location/distance and,

SCP-4676 events have no known maximum duration, ending only upon nullification via physical contact with another human being or the death of the victim.
附加记录：    

Although not explicitly stated, the Foundation has witnessed these people only starting to move after making physical contact with another human.

You know what they say about hearing hoofbeats and leaping straight to thinking about zebras

Not really. When I hear hoofbeats, I just think . Sometimes, you can recognize who the hoofbeats belong to because horses are rhythmic and cows are usually slow and heavy.

I really wish there was more to this article. It's one of those rare concepts that has depth while being so simple, and makes itself so much room for being expanded on. Yet the article in its current state sort of glosses over the basics, each example adding very little that hasn't already been covered. I'd like to see, for example, what the Foundation is doing to try to contain it - stating that containment is impossible is a cop-out. You've set out the rules of the SCP but you haven't tried to break them, and that's where the most fun is.
