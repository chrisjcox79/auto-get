Oh, this is an interesting one

+speculation

-fun

So the DoMc note establishes that the documentation is being written by them in a very specific way, that cannot be stated outright. The following note establishes what this means, i.e. some parts of the documentation are false and that these parts are highlighted with blue background. This is further suggested by the rest of the description, which also establishes that Michaels is clearly in danger.

Moving to the interview, it seems to be implied that SCP-4428, which presumably is the reason why the documentation must be like this, is a physical entity of some kind that follows Michaels (the background of the picture is redacted, the interviewer glances above Michaels for seemingly no reason). In order to follow the rules laid out by the documentation, Michaels and the interviewer talked the interview the way it is written, with the blue background added in post by DoMc to signify which parts were false. This is also implied by a couple of things, like how they tense up when they get close to describing 4428, how Michaels urges the interviewer to skip the color question (suggesting this is pre-planned in some way), and how the interviewer gestures at Michaels (notably in a fashion that avoids being recorded).

Going through the interview, it seems that 4428 causes a very real sensation of fogginess to Michaels. It appears that 4428 also killed the rest of Michaels’ team, hinting that it can be lethal. The conversation about Michaels’ brother seems to be loosely metaphorical to 4428, which acts like a parasite, as implied at the end of that particular conversation (with “him” probably meaning “it”, i.e. 4428). After this it is also implied that 4428 has affected other people. The end bit regarding the MTF also possibly hints at how 4228 was discovered.

The final note then implies that, even with the clever documentation of DoMc, the situation is still very challenging for Michaels (as is implied in the interview by how 4428 is seemingly called a “stressor”). Based on this, and some other stuff in the article, it is obvious that 4428 poses a great danger to him, most likely because it will kill him if its effects are triggered.

TL;DR , 4428 is a physical entity that acts like a parasite and is currently attached to Dr. Michaels, after having caused the deaths of his research team. It appears to kill its host when it is described in any way (including pictures or information about its properties). The Department of Miscommunications have figured this all out and use intentionally false, but highlighted, information in the documentation to imply what is going on, without triggering 4428’s effect. And Michaels is quite stressed about the whole deal.

Regardless of how right any of that is, this is an absolutely intriguing article. Amazing work!
