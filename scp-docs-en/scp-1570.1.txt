Fold

13 Jun 2012 04:01

Procedures into fully containing SCP-1570 are still being researched, although current procedures have been largely successful at preventing public exposure.
附加记录：    

This is worded strangely. I would shift this sentence to after you actually explain how it's contained, and say something like:

Research into full containment of SCP-1570 is ongoing, though current procedures have been largely successful at controlling manifestations and preventing public knowledge.
附加记录：    

You say this:

The projections always display any clothing and items currently in physical contact with SCP-1570, including personnel and the surface upon which SCP-1570 might be standing at a range of 4.62 meters out from itself.
附加记录：    

then this:

Keeping physical contact to a minimum and magnetically levitating SCP-1570 from the floor of the chamber is critical to preventing the public from paying too much attention to SCP-1570's projections.
附加记录：    

Something about that second sentence just doesn't feel right. I can't think how to articulate why it's off beyond it explains the obvious. Specifically that they're trying to prevent the public paying too much attention to it. They're the Foundation, that's what they do.

Then this too:

Capitalizing on this behavior led to current containment procedure which both controls the phenomena and makes it less likely to threaten the normalcy of public life.
附加记录：    

Controlling something so it's less likely to pose a threat or be noticed is generally the idea of containment. I don't think reiterating that serves any purpose.

Another thing that occurs to me with no relevance to my initial comment is that if it can be dissipated by just poking it, why wouldn't we do so? Not doing so at the first opportune moment risks someone else doing it accidentally at a distinctly inopportune one.

I may have missed it, but I saw nothing which suggested it would only appear once in a given time frame, with some predictable interval between manifestations allowing for this containment strategy to actually be meaningful. Without something like that specified, it doesn't sound like we can actually prevent it showing up where we don't want it, only get it to show up where we do from time to time. Like, we arrange an event in San Fran. There's no indication it couldn't show up elsewhere the very next day. As such, why getting it to show up in these places constitutes containment is unclear.
