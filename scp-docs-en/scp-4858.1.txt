This is solid but a few things are holding me back from upvoting it at the moment. I like the core of this a lot even though I can't quite tell what's going on (me not being able to tell what's going on is not a problem, far from it), but I'm going to back up DeepViolet's concerns about the tone. They take me out of the article at a couple key moments where I feel like you need to really be roping people in, and it colors the rest of the piece for me.

Some places I'd consider re-toning things other than what Violet pointed out:

+block

–block

It is of unknown construction and design, and is believed to have been built in the 4th millenium BCE, apparently for the worship of an unknown deity named Sap'e'ha.
附加记录：    

The gist here is fine but the phrasing  or similar.

In cases where burn wounds occur, any clothing and items the SCP-4858-A manifestation has on its person will ordinarily be destroyed, although there are a small number of cases where objects have survived. The causes of this are unknown.
附加记录：    

This entire bit just feels clunky to me. I feel like there are several ways you could rephrase this to make it flow a bit better. I wish I could be a bit more specific here but I've now spent a good ten minutes trying to think of a way to make this same content flow more naturally and I can't. Still, I think there must be one.

I must clarify that ultimately these are minor complaints, I still quite like the majority of the article and I feel like it really picks up during the inscription list and recovery log. (Incidentally, I might advise noting the language that the written notes are written in, since you do that for the inscriptions.)

But yeah, I feel like these comparatively minor problems being fixed could take it from a decent article to a very good one, and I'm withholding a vote for now on that basis.

As a courtesy to our readers on mobile devices, please collapse long posts. ~Zyn

If Violet and I are both completely out in the weeds and the phrasing is an intentional stylistic choice, I'd be curious to know why, if you can divulge it without overexplaining.
