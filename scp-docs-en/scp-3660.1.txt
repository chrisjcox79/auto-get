577

18 Mar 2018 21:56

So if you've read the article, human experimentation is carried out 577 times before being halted. It's been brought up as a point of disbelief here, and it also survived the feedback sweeps during drafting so I thought I'd open it up here for discussion.

The idea was to make a mini-zoo(San Diego Zoo has 3700 individual animals and London Zoo has 16000) that was both reasonable to the Foundation's rapid consumption of D-Class personnel and supportive of the 'Scarcliffe's manic search for a reversal' narrative. However, I don't want to sacrifice disbelief and finding a sweet middle ground would be ideal if I can find one.

So what do you guys think? Should the number stay, or is there another number that you think would achieve both goals?

577 Update

19 Mar 2018 17:15

All right. Initially I was going to keep 577 and just have it ride on  However, I think it would be much better to qualify, rather than quantify, Ssscarcliffe's obsession. To that end, I've lowered the number to 177. I've also modified the interview log to give a time frame during which these 177 were carried out. I've also addressed how Snakecliffe was able to somewhat hide away these numbers.

Other supplementary changes borne from this topic include changing Noodlecliffe's first name from Allison to Stephanie for reasons those of you good with nicknames will figure out, and the integration of Jim North's excellent point about recycling animals with human minds. Ideally, this makes the entire article more realistic and also more reliant on quality of experimentation, rather than quantity. Thank you all for the feedback and I hope this reads better!

Images of animals are all either public domain or CC0 licensed. Links are: rat.
The language has been greatly expanded and tightened. Reading should be more pleasurable, flowing and less robotic.
The characters of Scarcliffe and Hanssen(formerly Wallace) have been expanded upon and given more nuance.
SCP-3660 is now kept in Site-64.
