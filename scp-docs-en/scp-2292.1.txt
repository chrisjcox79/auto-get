10 Jan 2016 10:25

I'm a little iffy on a few of the ploys here, but ultimately I still enjoyed it.

Your tone and writing style were generally solid, although the article seemed pretty long for what it was trying to say.

The supplement was a strictly paint-by-numbers pulp adventure story, and while necessary to the backstory it didn't do much for me.

Also, I'm not particularly on-board with the idea that animal treatment ethics have advanced over time to the extent that they rule out invasive methods of necessary anomaly classification. I'm all for them recommending better animal treatment in general, but denying research methods only makes sense to me as a cost-benefit analysis. Here the vivisection probably wouldn't learn anything new, and communication and trust are now more important. If SCP-xxxx walks through the door shooting radiation of it every orifice tomorrow, they might have to cut that one open.

Ultimately I upvoted because the idea of a somewhat communicative gorilla being the last survivor of an ancient civilization is pretty cool, and I'm really curious why that civilization would need his particular abilities. It would've been nice to see a bit more hinted at there, but that's fine.
