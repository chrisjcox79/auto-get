31 May 2011 15:50

Here's how it works in my head - When the program compiles it outputs the latest iteration that exists in memory. That could still be the current version if the program hasn't been running long enough to finish generating a new one.

The contents of the window that pops up on ctrl-shift-x may change each time a single version of the program is run.

When a new version is compiled, new scenes may be added to the pool and previously available scenes may be removed. Some scenes may persist and stay the same (such as Die) and others may persist with slight alterations (such as the text input field on Jacob).

I mention at the end of section 1 that Die has remained unchanged (maybe just by chance) since it's first iteration. Maybe in the future it will grow more dangerous.
