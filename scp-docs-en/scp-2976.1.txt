For me the breaks in tone were jarring enough to turn an upvote to a downvote.

+explained

-Thestructure...

and focus on objective, precise, factual descriptions.

Not clinical tone and descriptions:

Merely being around other infectees is not enough to speed up infection; effort must be made to discuss SCP-2976.
附加记录：    

The 'Hall' as described displays repeated abnormal structuring, with the dimensions, details and architecture being non-Euclidean in nature. Walls become floors, stairs lead to nowhere, and average humans who study the drawings can’t help but feel that something is fundamentally wrong with the pictures. There appears to be no underlying theme for the building, as modern, medieval, Oriental, Greek, and Arabic themes mix and mingle with no regard.
附加记录：    

All of his books were widely panned by critics. In 1932, he wrote and paid to have SCP-2976 published. The publisher was a small, vanity press who seemed to have no idea what they were printing. SCP-2976 was seen as a major failure, and caused the publisher to go under. Mister Talont vanished after production and is presumed dead.
附加记录：    

Clinical tone and descriptions:

Proximity to other infectees (hereafter designated SCP-2976-1) does not inherently accelerate infection; effort must be made between instances of SCP-2976-1 to discuss SCP-2976.
附加记录：    

Collective reports and artistic renderings depict a structure consisting of architectural elements repeating without obvious pattern, often in illogical or non-functional arrangements. No consistent architectural style is displayed.1 Sanitized computer models constructed using consistently documented features as landmarks suggest a fixed structure occupying a Non-Euclidian region of space-time.
附加记录：    

SCP-2976 was published in 1932 by a small vanity publisher at Mr. Talont's own expense. As with non-anomalous works by this author, SCP-2976 sold poorly and received little contemporary public attention beyond numerous negative critical reviews. Mr. Talont vanishes from public record following the failure of this final publication and is presumed dead.
附加记录：    

See the difference?

Footnotes
1. One well documented region, for instance, features an amalgam of Modern, Medieval, Oriental, Greek, and Arabic elements.
