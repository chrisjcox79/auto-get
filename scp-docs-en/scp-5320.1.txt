03 Mar 2020 21:11

I'm always down for an SCP which shows the people writing it being affected by the SCP, I wrote something similar myself recently. But to work, these pieces require some level of subtlety and subterfuge to lead the reader along so that revelation has some impact and meaning.

This gives up the game far too early, the reader knows what's going on the entire time and that sucks any punch or horror the fish church could have brought to the table. When the reader knows what's going on the entire time there's no tension or emotional impact. It can be amusing, but nothing more than that.

I did like the e-mail signature line, keep that if and when you make revisions to this in the future.

Whether you like it or not, history is on our side. We will bury you!
