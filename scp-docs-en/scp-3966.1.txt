04 Apr 2017 00:40

So here's some answers to your questions:

+block

–block

If they usually have to inject -A into people's spines and suddenly it infects a researcher, why isn't that change a big deal? Am I misunderstanding the non-anomalous method of transmission?
附加记录：    

That is a big deal. Basically, since the researcher started showing similar symptoms as the D-class, and they know the protein was in the D-class, they tested the researcher, too. The big concern was where did the protein come from, but the researcher was more concerned with does this mean she's going to die.

So, if I'm getting it, -A is a free-floating end of a 4D 846 web or something similar, and when it manages to hit cerebrospinal fluid, it folds into a  asleep because they are webbed, and dream of spiders sucking things.

So, why does sleeping in the chamber cure them? And are the spiders actually feeding on them, or is their getting webbed incidental?
附加记录：    

Close. -A is the free-floating end of a 4D web-like thing, and when it encounters neurons, it opens them up and sucks out the cell juice. The components are emptied in the 4th dimension. However, the brain has evolved to pick up on this, interpret it as falling, and cause a reflex response - the hypnic jerk. Physical motion unmoors these web tips and the person is saved.

Of course, the web tips have to reach the person. 848 webs are transdimensional, so they extend into the 4th dimension and snag the 3000 webs, so that they never reach the sleeper.
