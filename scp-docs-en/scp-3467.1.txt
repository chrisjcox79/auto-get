20 May 2019 14:47

After re-reading, I found out what bothers me here that make it doesn't seem too interesting.

The basic concept is an anomaly that needs, by its own nature, to be well treated instead of coerced into submission to be contained. That is pretty much a fair concept.

But the execution is through defining that a specific DCL is needed for the containment to be enacted, but the containment can be done at the Foundation site, and Foundation personnel here is basically assumed as incapable of acting with the needed kindness toward the animal.

It does strike me as both a bit of LOL Foundation, and overall a weird change of focus on containment procedures. I think first that it should be contained at WWS under supervision (given its potential to cause damage if the very search is not properly handled in case of a containment breach), instead of at a Foundation site, since WWS does have facilities specialized in keeping animals happy and healthy, while their best efforts won't change that the Foundation keep their animals on underground facilities with limited space (that is the description of Site-64).

You can then have the re-containment limited to personnel trained by the DCL, instead of only the DCL herself, with makes little sense. It can even be established that WWS personnel is eligible for this training, and that the DCL is responsible for leading and coordinating re-containment if it comes to be needed.

Overall the concept is good, but making it seem like Foundation personnel can't be good to an animal if their lives depended on it was awkward, and you lost an opportunity to place this one under WWS care (no need to turn this into a Critter Profile if it is supervised). It will be a downvote for now, but it can easily turn into an upvote.
