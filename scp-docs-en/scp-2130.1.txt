06 Jun 2015 08:30

Thanks for the comments. I have made some tweaks in the hope of winning over no-voters. Have toned down the communique, and tried to clarify that O5 isn't ignoring the issue, just not prioritising it.

Kappa radiation is, of course,  - a way of explaining a fictional effect that doesn't actually explain it. Necessary here, because the anomalous furniture needs to be detectable, but difficult to isolate within the Foundation. If it's that ubiquitous, I have the problem that a notional Foundational reader doesn't need an explanation of what it is, but it seems real world readers might - I've tried to address that in a way that doesn't break immersion too much.

As for the ending, I agree that the original intention was to imply that O5 is also compromised. However I think it's open to interpret that Site 73 is overreacting - if the reader doesn't see an issue with some of the testing logs for other SCPs, then they may agree that the current risk level is acceptable. As Mister Ious can tell you, the original ending went on to imply that O5 were the source of the furniture, deliberately increasing risky behaviour to advance the Foundation's research - I think we're all glad that got cut.

11 Jun 2015 00:33

A classic example of attempting to exit one person's frying pan and going straight into another's fire. An earlier draft had a reference to casualties , and someone asked why the Foundation wouldn't consider them, considering they are a resource. That gave rise to the footnote, but it has triggered your question.

In writing this, I was not considering a specific test of this SCP against D Class. What I was trying to get at is that there are many casualties of Foundation staff (including D Class) every day. To work out whether SCP-2130 played a role in those casualties requires a level of forensic investigation. Fair enough, if you're looking at x-hundred accidents involving the death or injury to researchers in the past year. But to investigate each of the thousands of D Class deaths, and work out whether a hidden piece of office furniture played a role - too hard.

And of course I'm trying to emphasise the whole  point.
