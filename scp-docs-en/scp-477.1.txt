This is cool enough conceptually for an upvote, though I'm left wondering about a few things.

How exactly are these creatures being observed? They might be able to move through stone as if it was water, but light certainly can't. We'd only be able to directly observe objects that are touching the surface(s) of the stone, unless some sort of sonic imaging is in use.

Also, I'd ditch the suggestion that these are "organisms that adapted to life in solid rock", it sounds a bit silly.

20 Apr 2010 16:42

This is one of those SCPs that handily makes up for a lack of creepy by generating a sense of wonder. Upvoted.

Now, down to brass tacks. Given how hard these lifeforms are to recapture or destroy if they escape containment, would this qualify for a Euclid rating? Also, it might be interesting to see the Founation intentionally allow some of them into a sample of concrete, then perform tests on the fossils and/or the concrete. How does having these things inside it affect the strength of a stone or concrete? If their presence weakens it, then these things pose a credible threat to a lot of structures if they ever become widespread. How finely-pulverized are disposed-of materials? A grain of sand is significantly larger than a microbe, which means there could be fossilized plankton swimming around all over the place.
