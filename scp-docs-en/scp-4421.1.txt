This is generally good worldbuilding and I am falling on the side of +1'ing this, but I do want to caution you against ending articles on the note of a pretty flat agreement. I'd prefer something with at least some emotional appeal to kind of stamp the end.

As it stands right now, you have this off to a short exposition transcript, and then a written document that more or less ratifies the exposition. There are facts to take away here and that's cool for your later world-building, but as a standalone piece I feel this suffers for being your LOI/GOI introductory.

25 Mar 2020 17:17

I think my main concern with the article is how much time it spends going over logistics and minutia of La Rue Macabre's agreement with the Foundation and how that agreement was started. Like, I understand that you are attempting to introduce this location, and tell the readers what the location is and why the Foundation just doesn't touch it, but the way that the story is presented feels very expository. There is a conflict but there is no struggle. There's no real overcoming of an obstacle for the arc of the story to feel satisfying.

If you want to write a story about LRM's fight for freedom, I'd want to see the struggle. Right now there are some calm conversations and a kind of bland note at the end to tidy everything up, when I think you'd want to see more pushback from both sides.

Honestly, what I think I would've like to see to this end is what would have happened if the Foundation just kept the stick for longer. I'd like to see the voodoo and swamp monsters become more than they could handle and have to admit defeat. Right now the two parties involved don't struggle enough against each other for us to see any sort of character development, and I lose investment in the story because without struggle, the conflict does not feel convincing.

I agree that it would have been fun to see the denizens of LRM slowly become too much for the Foundation but requiring that level of struggle may be overly restrictive when building a setting, especially this one. We should keep in mind that this is mostly just setting the table and that it's not unreasonable for the Foundation to view a  they try to contain a large, anomaly rich area only to realize it's not cost effective at best and a ticking time bomb at worst. Maybe the O5 council is remembering past events when Legba offers to solve their LRM problem. Maybe they think that with Beta-2 involved they can keep an eye on things while always having the option of swooping in and turning LRM into a full blown site. The subtext alone here makes me interested.

Besides, this direction is a good immediate setup for interesting entries. Just think of the Hamsterdam storyline in the Wire with anomalies involved. An entry on rougarou would be fun with the hook being how LRM handles a group of hunters coming in after dangerous prey.
