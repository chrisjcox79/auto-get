11 Apr 2017 06:17

Oh thank god. I was giving up on being able to actually read this.

I like the concept, but the execution of the mission-log was a deal-breaker for me. Meaning I didn't bother tabbing back and forth after literally every line, and mentally correlating timestamps, just to read it.

I get that the writer wants to emphasize the individual perspective of each MTF member, but what's wrong with plain old name tags? Current writeup emphasizes perspectives at the expense of continuity and reader immersion. IMHO the following format is more readable, and still makes it clear who is perceiving what:

00:00:00: Washington:  All present. Let's move in.

00:24:06: Washington:  Approaching the town.

00:37:20: Jefferson:  Do you see that? Over there, by the lamppost. Looks like a ghost.

You know, like a movie or play script.

The only problem I can see with this approach is that seeing Jefferson:  Jefferson during the check-in seems pretty redundant. The solution to this is to skip the first check-in.

Thanks again for the transcript, Dr. Coyle. Good luck in the contest, Rejekyll. IMHO the above suggested format change would really help your chances.

I got mixed feedback on the log format but ultimately decided to keep it. I did this for a few reasons:

Multiple members of the team split up. I found it easier to show those parts this way rather than synthesize the log into a normal format
This way, the order in which you read the log is not chronological. So Adams has the last entry rather than Lincoln
I wanted to emphasize how every person's experience in-A is unique

But now I've changed it to a format similar to the one you suggested, because overall I think it reads better.
