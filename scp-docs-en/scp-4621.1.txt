Goddamn that is some GUD formatting. Holy guacamole.

When I saw this in #critters, my only serious critique was the ‘informal’ dictation of the final log and I bought this up. Lines like

Cpt. DeLuca cautiously advances down the stairs, keeping his sword in front of him the entire time. SCP-4621-2 appears transfixed, his eyes locked on to Cpt. DeLuca's sword.
附加记录：    

Are a far cry from the detached, stenographer prose that alot of us expect, and I have voted because of this before in other works. However, I don't mind it here. The article is very sword-focused, and it would be a shame to miss out on a sword fight. Furthermoreclinical tone isn’t exactly broken. And that’s thanks to something that has changed since #critters, the addition of:

BEGIN AETHERIC TRANSCRIPTION…
附加记录：    

And the accompanying description of this term.

Hecking stuff. +1 for entertaining stories, entertaining formatting, and entertaining solutions to creating entertaining stories.

22 Nov 2020 05:36

This article has a solid core. There is something quite enchanting about the simplicity of 'a guy chasing you'. I like how the German is characterised by how he changes over time - there is a subtle dread to knowing that he is improving his craft. I really appreciate that the excerpts of the book are not told from his perspective - just a little drip of characterisation every now and then was enough.

Beyond the core, though, things are a little lacking. I wish it had been a little clearer in-universe on why those book excerpts were worth attaching. I found the excerpts from the journal unsatisfying. The points they introduced felt too convenient for cheap character development - not unforgivable, seeing as they are excerpts after all - but to me they didn't feel like entries of a journal. The decline was far too linear for excerpts taken more than 100 days apart. It's fine that this was used to explain why the character failed in their task, but it would have been a good opportunity for passive characterisation of the German.

I am tired of  as a cliffhanger. I don't feel like I've gained anything by knowing that there is undeveloped plot, and I don't think it's worth the confusion at the start of trying to decide whether or not I care about reading the previous conprocs/desc or if I just want to get into the flow of the article. There's also a divergence that I'd've liked to have seen developed. A lot of stuff happens during the dream described as if it happens in the real world. I'd like to know what actually happened.

Novote overall.
