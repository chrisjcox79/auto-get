This can be recovered with some editing. It has an excellent base concept. The idea that this could happen to anyone, for no explained reason, and the part about how the dying researcher could interact with 451… Just chilling.

Suggestions:

-Make him more resigned and prone to paranoia, and less suicidal. Actually, here's what to do- one time, he sees a gun lying around, and randomly tries to shoot himself, only to have the researcher get shot and the rest of the events happen. Just say it's a a sudden thing.

-He should be contained -somehow-. I'm not sure how you can do that and still have the rest of the article intact, but he does need to be contained. Hm… Maybe, after he entered, he just can't leave Site 19? You'll have to think about it.

-Second paragraph of the Description: Revise it for appropriate clinical tone, look through some other articles for how to structure the concepts if you're not sure.

-Move the part mentioning the linked document to the bottom of the page, like in other SCPs.

Why does he need to be contained? Is making SCPs containable some great sign of humility that must be adhered to? It's one thing if it reads "THIS SCP IS WAY TOO BADASS TO CONTAIN. THE FOUNDATION BOWS BEFORE HIS ALMIGHTYNESS."

19 Mar 2010 10:06

i love the concept to this article, i just dont like the overall…feel of how it's written. minor tweaking would fix that though.

as far as him being uncontainable, while he's currently confining himself to site 19, i cant see him doing that forever. i cant see anyone just settling for staying in one place for the rest of their life in a situation like that, unable to kill themselves yet alone. something does need to be done somehow to ensure he remains there, loyalty just doesnt cut it when you think the organization you're loyal to is gone forever.

one possible idea that comes to mind is if they attempt to keep him contained by giving him the illusion that there's something going on that he needs to solve, some conspiracy, some hope for being able to reverse the things he's done, or maybe some greater threat that he needs to protect against. something that gives him a goal to work toward, something he desperately needs to do that he can only do at site 19.

he would still be technically "uncontainable" insofar as he could still leave at any time, but that would make the containment objective be to monitor his activities and come up with ways to keep him occupied, preferably in ways that do not involve other SCP's.

i dont know if that would destroy the tone of helplessness in his situation though, which is one of the strong parts of the article. just some thoughts.
