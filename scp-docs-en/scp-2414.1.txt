I have been wavering about this one. I like the basic idea (although it's similar to a few other items) and the proposed containment revisions section is a neat illustration, if a bit familiar. I'm also not sure about the Serpent's Hand having a  - I thought they were a bit more amorphous than that. But I'm starting to have concerns about how this skip actually works:

Additionally, SCP-2414 functions as an infohazard that affects Foundation personnel who become aware of its existence as an object considered by the Foundation to be anomalous  personnel unable to [liberate it] will attempt to recruit assistance from those who are.
附加记录：    

I think I get this. Senior personnel who were told  that the problems start.

Doesn't that lead to a tipping point - if enough senior personnel are affected, then  no longer thinks that it's anomalous, so the infohazard won't spread any further. Of course, by that stage the mind-wipe effect could be problematic, depending on how this next bit is intended:

In the event that SCP-2414 is successfully removed from Foundation custody, all memory and knowledge of the Foundation will be erased from the individual or individuals responsible.
附加记录：    

Only the individuals responsible for it being removed from Foundation custody? That would seem to be a relatively small proportion of the number of people infected by the infohazard - it would only take a small subset (albeit including some very senior personnel) to actually cause the release. If the intention was to mindwipe all personnel with any knowledge of SCP-2414, that would be different, but that's not how you've phrased it.

It strikes me that if  is the primer for the infohazard, and release is the trigger for the mindwipe, there are two slightly better ways of containment. One is to make this an -EX, so that the Foundation does not consider it anomalous. The second is to simply take the hit on mindwipes and get it out of Foundation custody in a way that makes it unlikely to come back - space or deep ocean come to mind.
