Junior Staff - Site Criticism

05 Sep 2019 19:03

I really enjoyed this!

There’s an interesting and mysterious (in a good way) conflict between SCP-4859-B and the vultures built up here that we’re given just enough hints on to sort-of work out, with not so many that you’re flat-out stating anything and spoiling the mystery.

+rambles

-OFF

A few little things that jumped out at me while reading:

There is no known trigger for this manifestation event, as it occurs at random.
附加记录：    

This seems like the Foundation is giving up a little too easily for me. Just because the trigger isn’t known, doesn’t mean that it is actually random. Personally, I’d rephrase this to acknowledge that there could have been a trigger here, we just didn’t find out what it was.

SCP-4859 will manifest. Passersby fail to notice the object.
附加记录：    

I could be wrong about this, but I think the second sentence should be ‘Passersby will fail to notice the object here’, just to keep the tense consistent. Same on the next sentence, too.

Soon after, Hay begins laughing. Within eight seconds, all people present in the food court begin laughing except SCP-4859-B. SCP-4859-B stands up, bringing their rifle to bear.
附加记录：    

I have to agree with TheMightyMcB here; this is fantastic imagery, and you really get the sense of the kind of conflict SCP-4859-B is involved with just from these three sentences!

Identified to be ectoplasm.
附加记录：    

I love the casual mention of ectoplasm here; the idea of these things just being accepted fact in the anomalous world is always really appealing to me. Plus, just having this be a known quantity lets us forgo any long technobabble explanation and just bring in the associations we already have with ectoplasm: spooky shit.

+1
