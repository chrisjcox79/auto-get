Fold

28 Jul 2015 20:07

I have no issue with the writing itself, and I like the core idea of an entity that hunts, consumes, and replaces negative dream imagery. That could go in some pretty wild places.

Dream-based articles are difficult to write in my experience, especially in a manner that's intended to be an informative document for an organization's records. Dream imagery is amorphous and impenetrable. This makes it ideal for experimental prose and surrealistic exercises, but it also means that it's very difficult to write something relating the experiences of another person, much less a common experience between multiple people.

I won't say that something like this can't work in the SCP format, but I do think the documentation needs to delve a little more deeply (I would prefer much deeper, personally) into what this thing is and what it does to people for it to really take advantage of the starting concept. There's the beginnings of something really good here, but I don't think it's there yet.

While its a headcanon-to-headcanon thing, I don't think all D-Class are violent criminals. Hell, in some SCPs you can't have violent criminals dealing with the object.

Non-violent D-Class are just people the Foundation has acquired or demoted to the role, whether criminial or not. I can't really tell you to cancel downvote for that (as you have more reasons than just that to downvote) but frankly its not unheard of and opens up the opportunity for some D-Class to be random people abducted by the Men In Black and experimented with. Which is a twist on the classic MIB urban legend, which fits with the basic idea of the Foundation (and is exactly what I use as a feature in my articles).

Using three people allows it to cycle between the three in a predictable pattern, allowing for regular tests as it moves from an 'empty' host to a  one.

Say Host A has held the SCP for a few days, and is having clean, restful dreams each night because of it. Meanwhile, Host B hasn't held 2840 for two weeks now, and is beginning to have her regular terrors again. So they can then guess when 2840 will jump to Host B, measuring the intensity of the dreams that it seems to be attracted to. Host C has begun to have unpleasant dreams as well, but won't be quite 'tasty' enough to attract 2840 for a while, allowing them to study Host B while Host C's condition continues returning to its baseline, and Host A is allowed a brief reprieve from his nightmares after 2840 'cleansed' him.

Otherwise, yes, it is rather awkward in parts, thats a weakness of my own. I'm going to update it soon. EVERYONE has told me its missing something, I need to find what.

But that's just it: it cannot jump from its host to anyone else unless you specifically put another person there and have them sleep within 100 meters of each other. It doesn't matter where it gravitates to, it's specifically got a 100 meter jumping ability. Based on the rules you've set up regarding this SCP, the containment procedures don't make sense. Now, maybe, since this thing  nightmares, it needs to feed or it will starve to death? But that needs to be included.

Now, the concept of a beneficial creature who wants to protect people from nightmares, or just happens to have bad dreams as its natural food source, has potential as an idea. The thing I think is missing, and what will probably help once you get that down, is a sense of tone. This creature is inherently the opposite of scary - it's a thing that helps make scary things go away - so what do you want the audience to feel if not fear?

I think once you get that down, the rest will flow from there.
