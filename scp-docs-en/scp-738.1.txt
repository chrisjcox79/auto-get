Re:

27 Jun 2009 16:39

In my mind, there are documents that present these detailed measurements, as well as detailed assays of the material, etc. What the SCPs are, then, are what are probably best called "executive summaries". Information dense brevity is probably what the SCP should be written towards to be "professional". In addition, the tone is scientific, but geared somewhat toward nonscientists, particularly a security team assigned to the object or a collection of objects.

An SCP should feel, at it's most scientific, like a good paper abstract. Informative, evocative, clear, but many of the less important computations, measurements, etc. may be in the body of the paper. Even those are probably few, with most of such things left to the reader or secreted away in an appendix.
