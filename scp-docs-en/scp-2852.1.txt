EDIT: I guess if I had to guess what this is all about, it sorta reads like the messenger / angel / servant of some sort of old god / deity / thing-of-worship, one that feels threatened by Catholicism and Anglicism, likely one that has some special relationship to cicadas, whether being a  or just using them as messengers / angels, probably with paganistic themes.

2852 instances are sent to withdraw the blessings of this old god, which Catholics and Anglicans unknowingly draw upon (probably by coincidence) to give fertility, family stability, good health, and an overall harmonious existence with the world at large. It guides the family in the (literal) rituals of divesting themselves of the old god's blessings, which results in infertility, family dissolution, health troubles, and violence / anti-capitalism / terrorism (not to equate those last three, mind you).

The rituals themselves take place at religious events because the rituals need to happen at a place of religious significance, and they act like distorted Christian rituals because those Christian motifs are effectively perversions of the rituals associated with the thing of worship behind all this.

But none of this is necessarily true.

There are good strong themes running throughout the SCP, with the whole focus on bringing out fear and disgust in the reader, as well as having every event involve something to do with cicadas and children participation the whole time.

Where I start to lose interest is how every event can be boiled down to . This turned into the fact that by the third event I could probably guess what effects the people in it would face later on in life before really reading it and getting some of it right. There's not too much diversity. If anything, it would have been more of a slam to the gut if everyone surviving the third event improved afterwards, especially given that it's a Black Event.

Now admittedly, I don't know much about cicadas (due to my intensely huge fear of any bugs or insects1), but there's also little connection as to what actually goes on throughout. A baby's skin molts in the first, teeth ripped out in the second, exsanguination and cannibalism in the third.

This all adds up to a downvote. It is well-written, I can say that much, but it's not something I really enjoy much.

EDIT: Also, I think you forgot to blackbox that one dude's eyes in the picture.

1. Not expecting the cicada thing gave me a bit of a challenge to push on :P
