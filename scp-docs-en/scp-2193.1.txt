Re: this is the stupidest thing ever

21 Nov 2016 17:49

When the procedure was first written back when the site was getting started and the s were originally implemented, it was a method of including human test subjects that could be used without bringing up to much outright maliciousness in favor of justifiable moral ambiguity, while also leaving a somewhat sinister loophole. The monthly termination, in this regard. was simply the commuted sentence being fulfilled.

However, from a logistical standpoint and the way current trends in politics, ethics, and various prison systems are going, there just aren't enough to go around. Fewer countries even allow a death penalty to begin with than just a decade ago. It would simply be unwise to eliminate them for the sake of security when amnestics exist. The US currently only has around 2000 inmates awaiting such sentences, and with ever last one of them enlisted as D-Class it would only give about .8 D-class personnel per object.

Even ignoring the more sympathetic reasons most people don't like it (they feel bad that D-class are treated as disposable, which is what the D stands for in the first place). it just doesn't make sense for a budgeted government-subsidized organization on the scale of the Foundation to throw away sources simply for being violent criminals when many of them would probably be repentant and cooperative, or at the very least manageable with amnestics and behavioral reprogramming.

18 Sep 2016 17:43

Good tone, good structure. Can't fault you there, really. However, I'm going to no-vote because I'm fundamentally uncomfortable with the idea that we should retcon away the whole concept of monthly terminations. The concept is too deeply embedded into the site to just wish away under the pretence of some memetic effect. Obviously, I understand exactly why you'd want to do that, but I believe there's a way to save the idea without scrubbing it away as some regrettable aspect of early-site culture.

Edit:  rather than reply to every mention of this comment elsewhere in the thread, I'll edit this one. I'm not uncompromisingly against the idea of articles on this site contradicting each other - generally, I don't give a hoot about it, at all. But to me, this isn't a matter of my headcanon, this is a matter of actual canon. The role of D-class as test subjects is referred to in, what, about half of all articles? The idea is mentioned so often, as is so fundamental to so many parts of the site, that, to me, it sets some definite limits on the wiggle room actively encouraged by this contest. From these contest entries, I'm expecting a re-imagining or a new perspective, sure, but one that still chimes with the huge preponderance of D-class-related content on-site. And I'd rather not have to take as a prerequisite that the entire Foundation is hopelessly cankered head-to-toe with some memetic infohazard. This entry (and others like it), with the overzealous retconning and excessive  plot device, feel like someone trying way too hard to brush the concept of D-class testing and termination under the carpet because it's distasteful.

In short: the ideas are too established to mess with too much, so we just have to work around them, not chuck 'em in the garbage. I know this thinking might be a little rigid, but delegation on this site1. (I also actively believe that the immersiveness of the site improves with a certain level of canon-building, but that's a whole other debate.)
