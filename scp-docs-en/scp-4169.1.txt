The core premise of this - a collection of undiscovered prehistoric animals kept alive by some anomalous climate controlled area - is interesting, but you have a lot of issues here, which I'll detail below. Beyond that though, there's just not much done with the concept that I find interesting. Some prehistoric bugs and some hints at other unseen species down there, with very little else going on.

I'm also not sure about the sizes of everything at play here. The chasm is ~250m long and 70m deep, but most of the life forms you are listing here are meters in size themselves. That doesn't seem like a lot of space for colonies of these species to be able to exist with enough space. I could be wrong (I've not done the math), but that's my gut feeling.

Ultimately, I feel like there's a lot more that could be done here. The core concept is interesting, but for me it needs developing into something more than just some weird bugs. -1.

Issues

–block

First up, your containment procedures are a bit of a mish-mash, the information is presented in a weird order, and you have some wording and grammar issues:

You have a random new line in the first paragraph. Is that meant to be a whole new paragraph? If so, you need to add another return between the two; otherwise, you need to remove the new line.

MTF Echo-5  were assigned to Site-56 to counter any possible breach of containment by the instances.
附加记录：    

Were assigned? So they aren't any more? Beyond that, this line is a overly wordy a dramatic, and you mention ) are assigned to Site-56 to aid with containment.

Instances of SCP-4169-3 attempting to escape SCP-4169 are to be moved back into it on discovery.
附加记录：    

Not the rest of the instances then?

SCP-4169 is fed a live cow every 14 days to ensure that SCP-4169's fauna does not die out.
附加记录：    

Why would they die out without being fed externally? Clearly they have survived for millions of years before Foundation containment.

Your description has similar problems, starting with the weird ordering of information. You tell us that it's a chasm, and then start by detailing how the chasm was found and contained before telling us anything else interesting about it. Since the only explicitly anomalous thing about this place is that is somehow maintains an environment from 300 million years ago, you should definitely lead with that before anything else.

maximum depth of 70 metres at its deepest parts.
附加记录：    

Half of this sentence is unnecessary - you've already said its max depth is 70m, you don't need to then clarify that those are the deepest parts.

Researchers have theorized that the colonies are capable of hibernating for years at a time, this hibernation was most likely utilitized to survive harsh conditions.
附加记录：    

The comma in the middle of this sentence should be a semi-colon.

Incident 4169-3:
附加记录：    

This should probably be in bold.

4 were found curled up in a ball position with no signs of life able to be detected by Echo-5. The unit noticed that the last instance had trouble walking and standing up, instance was sedated and brought back in SCP-4169. The deceased instances were incinerated except for one which was kept for autopsy. Autopsy revealed the four SCP-4169-3 instances died of asphyxiation due to the lack of air.
附加记录：    

I wanted to look at this paragraph specifically as it has a lot of issues. You've got a bunch of non-clinical language in here, some awkward wording and more redundant information.

4 were found curled up in a ball position with no signs of life able to be detected by Echo-5.
附加记录：    

This feels non-clinical and a bit of a run-on sentence. You could just say .

The unit noticed that the last instance had trouble walking and standing up, instance was sedated and brought back in SCP-4169.
附加记录：    

Should be a semi-colon instead of a comma here.

The deceased instances were incinerated except for one which was kept for autopsy.
附加记录：    

This reads clumsily; you should reverse the information order to clean it up a bit. ie 

Autopsy revealed the four SCP-4169-3 instances died of asphyxiation due to the lack of air.
附加记录：    

That's what asphyxiation means - you don't need to define it again at the end.

A cow was thrown into SCP-4169,
附加记录：    

You said earlier that SCP-4169 is 70m deep. How did the cow survive being thrown in?
