So, 4427-B is a character from a few tales in this canon (originally 4427, changed to 5527 because I thought I wouldn't get the slot in time, I guess now it's gonna change back oops) and I'd been stumped on writing them because I already have a collab in the works about a public human skip figure and we don't need two of those. But this was a concept I wanted to do that I felt could be frightening in this setting, and I realized I could accomplish some two-in-one action on my canonical to-do list.

Thank you to DarkStuff for post-posting feedback.

Image source is all me  took a picture outside and did the rest with the paintbrush in Photoshop. CC-by-SA-3.0 and all that.

See Ad Astra Per Aspera for more of what all this nobody and 3848 business is about.

I'm upvoting because this is written very well, and the dialogue is great, but I don't feel like much happens. I clicked that Nobody link, and I imagine that if I read that tale I would get some good context for this piece, but as is it just doesn't hit many notable notes.

We have a sphere that gives people powers, and clearly attempts to touch as many people as possible, and we have it set in a broken masquerade scenario, and the link (and tags) tell me that Nobody is involved. The most mystery we really get let onto is the fact that he feels like there's a hole in his head when he thinks about the sphere

But that plot point feels like the beginning of a big mystery, and this is certainly written to feel like it sets that up, but instead it ends then and there. I'm left wanting more, but not in a good way. And thus well, still upvote, because damn you're good at dialogue, but you know. I felt the need to leave a comment.
