This one's a very mixed bag, so I'm novoting for now. There were quite a few spelling errors and minor formatting issues, which I did my best to fix, feel free to roll it back if I made any changes in error.

Showing an alt-history can be fruitful, and you do some good things with it, but the migration from  doesn't make a ton of sense.

Notable Good Bits:

The picture is nice, compliments to Cyan.
The original timeline does paint a pretty interesting image of the world.
The Excerpts at the end are, in general, pretty well-written, and avoid narm for the most part. My favorite was the testimony from the Brazil resident.
The conclusion is relatively strong.

Notable Bad Bits:

Inclusion of 096 is a bit whack. Killing six-figure numbers of civilians is patently ridiculous, as well as off-handedly amnesticizing an entire country in five days.
Detaining over a thousand staff members over something obviously not from our timeline does not reflect well on the Foundation. It is very hard for a piece to be convincing when it makes the Foundation look incompetent.
The lack of information on what happens post-1946 or post-occult-war is a bit frustrating. Surely the Foundation could infer from the narration at least a few things about what the present is like in this world? For example,

I absolutely love, love, love the topic of alternate history, so I had high hopes for this article coming in, but there were just a bunch of things that didn't quite make sense to me (similarily to what TyGently and Cyantreuse both mentioned) that made it hard for me to stomach, such as:

Somehow most of central/southern/eastern Europe experiences little change-which doesn't seem to make sense if Napoleon conquered their governments and replaced them. Even if they were conquered, things like Italy's unification and the formation of Poland were highly highly dependent on events in Austria and Germany that presumably would have never happened in this timeline.
France goes from  to somehow being in control over most of Europe with no explanation, which is inconsistent.
For some reason France invades Great Britain and the Soviet Union for oil of all things, which makes little to no sense.

Things like this really hurt the immersion of the article, and the randomness of 096 and a bunch of other skips being tossed in for what seems like name dropping reasons kind of kills it for me.
