SCP-824 is a species of tree that exhibits significant mobility and consumes plant matter.
附加记录：    

Does it consume things made out of plant matter, like pencils, paper and cotton cloth?

In 2011, several specimens of SCP-824 were found outside of containment on ████████████ Island, Japan. The behavior of these specimens has led to them being classified as a separate subspecies of SCP-824 (hereby SCP-824-1).
附加记录：    

What exactly is the point of having two different types of the same tree? Why not just focus on either the ones from the extra-dimensional pocket or from the Japanese island?

Residents of ████████████ Island have not been cooperative with Foundation personnel, and generally react with suspicion to inquiries about or studies of SCP-824-1.

The part with the extradimensional ones is to sort of give them an origin story. The ones on the island are to show that they can somehow be turned into something suitable for agriculture.

Why has the Foundation not simply seized these trees and administered amnesiacs?
附加记录：    

Because A) They've been living with these trees for a long time, and so long as they maintain secrecy operations, they don't have to disrupt normalcy for the people on the island. Much like the existence of anomalies would turn most of the world on its head, the lack of these trees would turn these people's lives over. And, B) They want to try and figure out how the trees got here and how they got turned from indiscriminate nomming machines to apple-picking trees.
