Fold

04 Apr 2011 17:38

And how many people do you think would be able to make "gentle and careful movements" if they spotted a huge spider crawling on their shadow? Those directly assigned to 538, maybe, but others elsewhere in the facility? You're fighting against millions of years of instinctive arachnophobia and swatting reflexes.

Even a small-scale containment breach could be extremely messy to try to clean up (did you really check every single shadow?), and it seems like an escape into the outside environment would be almost impossible to recapture. The confusion and aggression caused by the bite (which I think is unnecessary, btw) would also be a major headache.

The 538 spiders might not be going anywhere if containment procedures are maintained, but the complexity of the procedures themselves are a good indication that these things need a higher classification. Safe SCPs don't generally require an airlock and hazmat suits.
