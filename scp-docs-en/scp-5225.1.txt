Hey! Thanks for the feedback, and I'm happy you enjoyed the article. I'm happy to adress some of this as best I can, but as you said yourself, this is a bit hard to describe in words. I'll try to go through line by line.

Collapsibleconvenience

:)

The reason the anomaly is Safe is us really trying to lean into it as a containment class and a containment class only. We tried to allude to it in the article a bit, but it's not that just any artificial consciousness could stumble into the anomaly. It's things that are wrong or unhinged in some way. MARDUK.AIC was infected with a memetic hazard that it got from Foundation duties, the unknown was a pattern screamer (big reveal), and the third was made of a CotBG hive mind. Also it would require that consciousness to be powerful enough to enter the Noosphere in the first place. If you can't chuck yourself into the Noosphere using anomalous science, this thing can't really effect you.

I definitely understand confusion regarding the first sentence, because it was literally the first sentence that was written of this.  is a fancy way of describing what the anomaly does. It takes artistic concepts and uses them in physical constructions. It's biologically contained in the Noosphere because only unhinged technology can access it without assistance. Yes, a person could just physically enter the Noosphere through a Monolith, but that would first require a Monolith to be constructed and accessible. The three Monoliths in the log through are the only ones that were able to reach the Monolith stage, and its only because they were fast enough.

As for the confusion regarding what is affected, I think this is the best way to put it. The concepts that are mainly affected are ones that  the person and their idea of creativity. 5000 wouldn't be able to spread to you because you aren't in the Noosphere.

Yes, this thing could cause some serious damage. But for the efforts of containment, there's not much you need to do. It's not like everyday people can shoot their consciousness into the Noosphere.

Hope this helped clear up things in one way or another!

24 Mar 2020 04:43

Technobabble requires a very specific finesse to pull off  the ability to juggle complex concepts, to reduce them to terms whose meaning can still be inferred or quickly explained, and to apply them in ways which are understandable in the context of the story. Am I going to claim I know how to achieve this perfectly? No. I've fucked up plenty of times, and for as long as I'm practicing I'll be fucking up in different ways.

However, there still comes a point when I'm reading something where, if it becomes so dense I lose track of what it is I'm supposed to be focusing on, I am going to be completely lost in the story. Not because I'm necessarily confused  but because I'm lost on what it is I'm supposed to care about. The technobabble here is layered so thick and so without relent that I don't know why I should care about any of what's happening. I tried to search for it, but by the halfway point I lost my patience.

I get that the intent here is for the mystery to be in deciphering what the terminology all means. That isn't something I inherently dislike. SCP-2719, one of my favorite articles, has an understanding which hinges on five words of technobabble. However, the difference between this and 2719 is that I could read 2719 without knowing the meaning and still be viscerally horrified. The surreal, apparent logic and the small details instill a reaction of  everything which made me care about what the terminology meant.

This article is the technobabble and the technobabble alone. I couldn't find a single reason for me to decipher a meaning through all of it.

-1.
