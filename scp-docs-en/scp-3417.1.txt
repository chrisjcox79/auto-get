Round 2

28 Jan 2018 01:49

So a month or so ago I posted SCP-3417, and after seeing it get mixed reviews I decided to rewrite this. The whole article's been overhauled from it's original version, and aside from central ideas of the anomaly and Ortothan extraterrestrial stuff everything's different now.

Thanks to MaliceAforethought for reading and giving crit, and thanks to everyone that left feedback in the original discussion thread.

Fun fact that isn't directly stated in the article because I couldn't think of a good place to put it: Those transmissions in the image traveled 28 kilolight-years (28,000 light-years) before reaching Earth and getting detected. This is the earliest transmission from the anomaly that was detected, which means that SCP-3417 has been preaching for at least 28,000 years, and has been alive for longer.

Original Version of SCP-3417

+Post

-Post

Put on your Sunday clothes, there's lots of world out there! 

The universe is a busy place.

This is the first of more planned articles that involve the large presence of aliens in Ortothan groups, the mythos, and the Church of the Second Hytoth itself (Note that not all Ortothans are a part of the CotSH, that's just the largest Ortothan group on Earth, which is mainly human. There's a lot more groups and lone Ortothans out there.). This will also the first of more planned articles featuring the Extrasolar Activities Department, which should a lot of fun to write.

Thanks to taylor_itkin for crit!

Fun fact that isn't directly stated in the article because I couldn't think of a good place to put it: Those transmissions in the image traveled 28 kilolight-years (28,000 light-years) before reaching Earth and getting detected. This is the earliest transmission from the anomaly that was detected, which means that SCP-3417 has been preaching for at least 28,000 years, and has been alive for longer.

29 Mar 2019 20:14

So, people may be noticed that I've removed the Ad Astra Per Aspera theme from this and every other AAPA tale I've written that has used it. For a while now I've become increasingly aware that the theme has been causing heavy eyestrain issues, ranging from the high contrast between the light gray and dark gray text and the blaring neon colors. I've tried to make improvements, some of which have worked, but by now I've decided that the theme (sadly) won't work out.

Does this mean AAPA will go without a cool theme? Nope! I've started work on a completely new theme for the canon, which you can find test pages for Interlopers). No other CSS themes will go on my pages for the canon until this one is done and is made to be the best it can be.

And to address the elephant in the room, a canon hub isn't ready yet. Just a bit more left to do before AAPA can go live.

EDIT:  The new theme is live!
