04 Jun 2017 02:03

Firstly, the quote that inspired this concept:

I can draw out causes of death from the residual information of the dead, store them in existing weapons, and swap out the causes of death. I can strangle you with a sword, drown you with a train, crush you with a blowtorch, and burn you with a handgun. That is a suitable reason to bear the name of Hel, the queen of Niflheimr who rules over the dead. – Hel, A Certain Magical Index
附加记录：    

Despite inspiration, I made up everything other than the super general premises of it.

Next, allow me to express thank you to the many people who've seen this through: DrMagnus, SunnyClockwork, ARD, and many others on the chat.

Other things:

+block

–block

The SCP is originally an analogue to a spacesuit for the mer-person in the addendum. She basically altered the SCP to cover the entire train and then offed herself.

As for the nature of the mer-person, she takes influence from both the merrow and selkie.

The red feathers are analogues to guns for the mer-person, and references the myths on merrows.

The map actually denotes manifestation of SCP-3456, which usually appear in places where war and human suffering occurs.

As for motive behind this madness, the mer-person is affiliated with some group that monitors 3456 and she was mortally wounded by 3456's attack. Unable to report back to her superiors underneath the sea, she chose to deliver her findings to the next best thing - the Foundation. But she did not know where to find them so she created a scene to make them show up.

EDIT: After some thought, I will not impose the mer-person's motives. She is still monitoring 3456's activities on the surface, and was attacked by one. Her 'sea-suit' ultimately unravelled while she was travelling in a train.

If you're going to nitpick about linking to Wikipedia, there have been other articles that also does that exact same thing (SCP-3872 come into mind).

In my opinion, these in-line links are out-of-universe and serve for the out-of-universe reader's understanding, since some readers express the opinion that they do not understand the significance of those places. I don't see why there would be in-universe link for the SCP articles themselves, since I would perceive them to be isolated files for the sake of security. We (readers) are likely the only ones who will gush and care and react towards cross-referencing, while the .

After all, while they are places where tragedies occurred, they are not as ingrained into the collective psyche of our species as say, Auschwitz or Nanking (as far as contemporary humans of the more developed world go by).

I really wish there was another way, but this is the only preferable option.

附加记录：    

That portion came in the idea process at the last leg. My intent with this article was from the beginning, always about a train that drowns one to death. The problem lies in why would it happen.

At first, I thought of the  route if I have nothing, but that is a meaningless layer of characterisation. The mer-person needs something firmer to be grounded upon.

I then drew parallels with the selkie and merrow, which first introduced the Ogham as text to outright state the  for me-people part. But that was telling, not showing.

And along the way, I decided that SCP-3456 can be thematically suitable (since it's in the North Sea and all). And I went about with the mer-person tracking down an 3456 instance. It provided a situation wherein the mer-person can be in great injury to the point that it will not mind implicating hundreds of lives.

What I enjoyed:  I like the premise. I like the connection to SCP-3456 and the greater Orcadia canon. I'm intrigued by the background details given and discussed in this discussion thread. I find the merwoman, and portions of the article which relate to her, super interesting.

What I didn't enjoy:  I don't like how the parts of this article come together; I think the writing leaves much to be desired. I didn't understand the distinctions among the types of animals; why they were of significance; why lines on the ground could make animals not drown;1 or, why the article delved so deep into their properties but never explained their connection to the merwoman.

I also didn't think the merwoman's intent came through in the article as well as it could have. I'm inferring she used the bullet-feathers to battle the Nuckelavee in those three sites of historical tragedy and returned gravely wounded. I couldn't infer why she decided to kill herself and the other train passengers, especially when it seems she was already dying from her injuries? There really wasn't a connection there, and I wish there were, because again  the premise has me really intrigued.

What I would love is an SCP about the merwoman, her effects of personal/cultural significance, and what she does. But I don't think it works too well here because it seems presented as an after-thought. (Then again, I'm still reading through the Orcadia canon, so maybe things will more sense later).

Footnotes
1. If it's some magical working, or of ritual significance in the article's context  I would find that cool! But there isn't any further information, and I'm not comfortable just assuming.
