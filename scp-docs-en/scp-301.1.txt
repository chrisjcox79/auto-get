"SCP-301 is an energy anomaly roughly three meters in diameter located in the middle of ██████████ National Park."

Well, I'd avoid calling it an "energy anomaly" or "space time rift".

"SCP-301 is a region roughly…" for example, serves the same purpose without the chintzy language.

If you're looking for that psuedophysics goodness, though

"The prevailing model of the effects displayed by SCP-301 suggest that disappearance/appearance events are connected by a spacelike geodesic lying in a frame of reference attached to center of the Earth but otherwise displaying none of the planet's rotational motion. No adequate explanation for the mechanism underlying the continued existence of this unique curvature of space time has yet been proposed."

Since this make sense as general relativistic effect, I'd change the language of

"By correlating the time the the subject disappears with the distance it travels, it is almost a perfect match with the velocity of the Earth's rotational motion about its axis. Based from witness testimony, as well as data provided D-Class personnel, time spent inside SCP-301 is relative. While time outside SCP-301 passes normally, the subject inside percieves no passage of time at all. Mechanical and digital timers sent into SCP-301 can still function, but show the noticeable time discrepancy between initial entrance and reappearance"

to

"A relativistic analysis of the proposed model for 301 has generated predictions of travel time consistent with observational data. Consistent with it's seemingly spacelike nature, travellers and data collection devices sent through 301 have reported near instantaneous travel from point to point."

Does the effect extend to air? If not what's the minimum size of an object that 301 will accept?

28 Apr 2009 03:48

I did mention that idea to Spoon in chat when he first brought the SCP up, but he felt that it was okay to say the SCP doesn't work that way.

I think this is partially justified — you could argue that the effect is somehow gravitationally locked to the planet. The big leap is then the fact that it's stationary in a nonrotating frame of reference attached to the center of the Earth. As a curvature in spacetime the SCP should probably move or dissipate in some fashion in that frame.

In a way, you can tie the mystery of its rock solid position to the mystery of what keeps the SCP from changing shape or dissolving apart in gravitational waves in the first place pretty nicely. There's some pretty strange exotic matter things going on behind the scenes.

"Anomalous region" Why you cheeky —!

On further thought, I'd suggest that 301 be triggered by density — density of mass/energy basically. The benefit a density trigger gives you is it provides a filter which keeps 301 from going off constantly.

Yeah, I just now noticed the bit about how 301 closes itself up when it has matter in it. Sorry for being dense — I still think you should consider a density trigger, though.
