Author Post

16 Mar 2020 14:07

Thank you to my critters: DrMagnus.

This represents a significant amount of research into the American Civil War as well as the impact of that conflict upon the mental health of the people that participated. As one of the bloodiest periods of my nation's history, the impact of that war has etched itself deeply upon the collective psyche of the people that survived it and the descendants.

I am a first generation American. Both sides of my parental lineage stretch far back through centuries of conflict and death, so while I cannot say that the echoes of the American Civil War still ring in my blood, I see the horrors of my own cultural legacy mirrored in it.

While I am excited to write fiction about these events, I think it is tremendously important to keep in mind that these events happened. There is no horror that I can craft in the comfort of my office chair that will ever match the horror experienced by the people that fought and died in that war, or any war. Out of respect, I have kept as close to the source material as possible.

All of the officers mentioned here are real people. The battles and dates of the battles mentioned here are real. The only one that wasn't is , that is entirely of my creation. The horror of The Battle of The Crater is real, and the racism that added to that needless slaughter was also real.

Let us not forget that this war was fought, and a great many people died, for the  of the Southern States to own slaves. This horrific stain on my Nation's history must never be forgotten lest we become complacent, and thus complicit, in the practice of trafficking and sale of human beings all around the globe.

If you liked this piece and would like to read more, please visit my author page.
