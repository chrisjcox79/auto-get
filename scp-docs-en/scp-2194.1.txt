I give it a +1 for now for its cool concept and clear writing, but I am curious about why/how it can't be destroyed. The article would suggest that this SCiP is capable of standing up to incineration, acid emersion(and base emersion too for that matter), any number of chemical solvents, and anything else the Foundation could throw at it. I'd say that only acknowledgement of the SCiP's indestructibility is wasted potential. You could write so much about how it just keeps spreading no matter what the Foundation dose, and that could expand to how the SCiP propagates. I would love to hear a technical breakdown of its capacities. You could even write that a Site was condemned because the Foundation brought it to one, and consequently, in their attempts to destroy it, couldn't stop it from spreading at all.

Edit: As an after thought, what if you just buried the SCiP under like a meter of concrete?

Fold

04 Sep 2015 20:20

With you on the potentially interesting test logs thing, but incineration might really be the best answer for a couple reasons:

Organic just means  some normal organic compounds are otherwise stable and mostly harmless until you put them into solution, at which point they become highly toxic or explode violently at the slightest impact.

The foundation probably uses plasma arc incinerators for just this sort of reason: they literally vaporize things down to a hot plasma soup of component atoms and electrons at a temperature several times hotter than the surface of the sun. In the real world they use them for disposal of medical waste and toxic industrial byproducts because it's the only way to be completely sure you've annihilated all molecules of something.
