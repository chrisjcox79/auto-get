Images here. All from wiki-commons.
Based around the loss of the Deepwater Horizon oil spill.

FEEDBACK: 

SCP-3850] - I Can't Drown My Demons ) helped me come up with the name 'Gambheer Bakshi'.

AUTHORNOTES

–block

Odo Island is a reference to Godzilla.
This is the same Browning from Counting Station. Rest in peace, you gigantic asshole.
This is the same O5-5 from Murphy Law. She drinks her tea from a coffee mug that says .

She takes her tea black. No sugar. She's sweet enough as it is. ~

DeVorn left Prometheus way before the bubble burst. He saw it coming. But he didn't see that coming.
This story is based on my experiences in industrial safety, the Deepwater Horizon oil spill, and my desire to write an article that engages in serious lore-building.
This story is also based around my fascination with reconciling the newer Foundation with the lolFoundation.
This story is also my stab at fixing some of the narrative problems with SRAs. I'm particularly proud of the idea that the more you build, the more unstable they become. And the idea that DeVorn would try to fix this just by using more SRAs to suppress the effects of unstable SRAs.
The joke at the end is that invented Scranton Reality Anchors. So hey! Maybe he really does just want to share notes. xD
My one regret is that I couldn't find a way to work in a line by O5-5: 
The final addendum's passcode (Nemo me impune lacessit) and final hidden text (in pace requiescat) are both references to Edgar Allan Poe's The Cask of Amontillado. The first is the main character's family motto (No one injures me with impunity), and the last are the lines he closes the story with (Rest in peace).

I've made a few significant (though small) tweaks to the article:

The SS Sommerfeld was now constructed to transport low-risk anomalies and anesthesized ontokineticists (reality-benders). This is to better fit into the lore theme of Scranton Reality Anchors being a way of addressing reality-benders.
I modified the discussion throughout the article regarding SRAs to better reflect this lore.
I made the footnotes visible (mobile users reported having difficulty sometimes seeing them), and displaced them above the O5-5 note.
Changed the O5-5's passcode to something a little more literary (and, in my mind, far more menacing). Also tweaked a few bits of her note that always bugged the heck out of me.
