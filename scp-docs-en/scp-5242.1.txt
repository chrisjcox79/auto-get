Iguess...

credits to popsioak for this abomination

This is my first skip, and I put a lot of heart and soul into it. It went through a lot of edits and rewrites. Thanks to everyone on the SCPD Discord, IRC, and Idea Crit who gave feedback; they were all super crucial in helping me focus my idea and develop a narrative.

Special thanks to 4703 are fantastic and I hope my contribution to this larger story is worthy!

EDIT: Extremely special thanks to Calibri Bold for designing and coding the custom ACS icons!

+addenda:

-skips):

A Foundation researcher did some bureaucratohazardous anti-math to derive the existence of LOGICIAN, an antilogical abstract entity that rules over the forest that's so damn hard to talk about  (and is worshipped by the Fae). He said LOGICIAN's name, not aware of its ties to name-related phenomena, and subsequently took on a bit of its identity. However, it's sheer abstractness and unreason caused his own identity to become abstracted by LOGICIAN, as a PUNISHMENT for trying to speak about it. Thus, reality was adjusted so that he was cursed with the name Dr. Placeholder. Anyone else who attempts to communicate about it meets a similar fate. The LOGICIAN is the bureaucratohazard complex, but only those who understand that it's really an entity are forced to live with that knowledge, as Dr. Placeholder was.

The idea of an anomaly is inconsistent here. All anomalies are things that the universe says is true, even thought it shouldn't  the universe says that a particular statue can move, or there's a number between █ and █, or that SCP-055 is a self-keeping secret. The whole point of the Foundation is that these things exist despite contradicting what we formerly presumed to be true.

Such things are not anomalous, as they are a fact of baseline reality; they always have been, and always will be, true
附加记录：    

And this isn't what  to encompass pretty much everything anomalous, particularly when you go out of your way to wax philosophical about how actually, no, this one is special.

But in that description, you've basically stated the core flaw  hell, at that point I'd agree that 'anomaly' is maybe an invalid term. But a flawed version of the same system, to me at least, doesn't seem to be different to any other given anomaly. If it's possible to detect this bureaucratohazard, it means by definition that there must be an alternative normality with which to compare it, which means that it can still be classified as abnormal, and is therefore just an anomaly with a fancy new name. If there wasn't an alternative normality then I could see your point, but that's clearly not what's happening here.

A little further down in that same memo is the line

even if anomalies are irrational, they are factual
附加记录：    

which should help to outline my problem here. A bureaucratohazard is still an anomaly. And so are any other anomalies which conflate the real with the abstract, and the physical with the metaphysical.
