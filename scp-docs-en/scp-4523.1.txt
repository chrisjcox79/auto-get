Okay, but derivative

22 Jun 2020 22:42

I just couldn't like this SCP. The 'emaciated humanoid', the autopsies with nonsensical injuries, the camera feed cutting out immediately before the monster tosses the MTF's saladIt really just felt like an underwater SCP-087, except 087 at least gave the reader a sense of tension by providing the opportunity for the D-class to escape from danger.

(Alsomorse code? I get that encoding messages is a trope as old as time, but I could not keep a straight face imagining 4523-01 (who I imagined as 096 with flippers on) clicking his tongue for the viewing and listening pleasure of the Foundation.)

Granted, it PASSED as an SCP! The page layout was tit-for-tat, it mixed thalassophobia with dysmorphophobia well enough to embed itself into the nightmares of any 15 year old, and honestly, I was a little unsettled by it going through my first organic read-through. If it was 2008 and this was competing for a Series I spot, it definitely would have made it, of this I have no doubt.
