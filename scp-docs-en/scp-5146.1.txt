[] The more accurate the map, the more it resembles the territory. The most accurate map possible would be the territory, and thus would be perfectly accurate and perfectly useless. The tale is the map that is the territory.
附加记录：    

- Neil Gaiman, American Gods

In late 2007, the people of Bikini Atoll lost a lawsuit against the U.S. Government for its refusal to pay reparations for their displacement due to American nuclear testing on their land. This is about that, sort of. More broadly, it's about homesickness, and immigration, and not having somewhere to return to- but it's also under 500 words long, so I'd rather not get too far ahead of myself there.

This piece took about three hours from start to finish, and was written on impulse in a single sitting. Thanks for reading!

Edit: It seems some key elements of the anomaly aren't quite as clear to some readers as I would have hoped they'd be- if you're one if them, hopefully my replies to the earlier comments in the discussion will clear some of that up for you!

The island is the map, and the map is the island. The quote I've stuck up at the start of my author post actually explains the idea pretty well- It's a play on the concept of map-territory relation, where in this case what makes the object anomalous is the fact that it is a map so ideally capturing the thing it was supposed to represent that it has become the territory itself. As this isn't something that's actually possible to do in real life and constitutes something of a paradox, therein lies the anomaly.
