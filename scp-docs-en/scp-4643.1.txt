Junior Staff - Site Criticism

30 Aug 2019 19:56

I really enjoyed this.

+rambles

-OFF

I'm a big fan of media-based skips, and the time period you chose to set it in lends a really nice atmosphere to the interview too. I can really imagine this cheesy, hokey interview slowly turning uncomfortable and then horrifying as Wells rants on and on. Something that I found especially interesting is the fact that the interview itself is designated as the SCP, not because it itself is anomalous, but because it exists as the only evidence of this anomaly. That's a nice touch - it really emphasizes how the Foundation sees even this second-hand source as a threat to normalcy.

The appearance of the shadow entity and what happens to Wells at the end, along with what's said during the interview, along with the way he talks about his life and what happened in Borneo, hints at this bigger story that we don't quite see the entirety of - there's a great sense of mystery there without things feeling too deliberately obfuscated.

Something that sort of bugs me is we don't get much sense of the aftermath here - a lot of people disappeared, and some real shit went down, but we stop before we see how people respond to that. That's not necessarily bad, though, as it does a good job of leaving you wanting more, but I just thought I'd comment on it.

Also, just a really nitpicky thing:

However, when Robards had to cancel due to illness show producer Jack Coffman contacted American evangelical Christian missionary Reverend William  Wells to come on the show instead.
附加记录：    

This sentence feels like it kind of runs on a bit. I feel like an additional comma after 'illness' here would help with that, maybe.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Jason Robards, who was in the middle of a press tour for the film A Thousand Clowns.
附加记录：    

I was so sure this was a made-up movie name. It's nice to know that there's so much beauty in our real human earth history.

+1

31 Aug 2019 18:31

SCPs can be anything, really: events, people, objects. For example, SCP-186 is simply a historical battle that the Foundation covered up. The log of extranormal events is meant for far briefer descriptions than a typical SCP article is, so if a writer wants to delve into their extranormal event for more than a paragraph’s worth of text, it’s totally reasonable to commandeer an SCP slot for it.

This one stewed with me for a few hours. I hope my ultimate reason for upvoting isn't interpreted as denigrating; your work is an inspiration, kaktus. I wasn't really touched by this one because I don't know, for the subject matter and message it felt hollow, or too easy. But it's still a very worthy message to convey. I've given it a +1 just for that.

I do feel like the idea itself, as an SCP, is good, but the execution is unclear in its details, and it's muddied by what's left unsaid. Sometimes that's great, but in this case I feel like being overly blunt isn't so bad when you've taken the step of literally damning someone to hell (deservedly). For the message itself, in any form, this should succeed. Too few people think about what has been done to too many in such clothing.

Again, I love what you write and hope this isn't construed as too negative :)
