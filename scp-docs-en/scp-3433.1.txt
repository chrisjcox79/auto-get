13 Nov 2017 10:37

So the ideas here are really cool, I like the concept of the Foundation being in possession of something that, if ownership isnt regularly cycled, will cause their employees to completely forget them.

There are a lot of issues though, starting with imperial measures (should be metric) and a [REDACTED] in the containment procedures, which is normally enough for an instant downvote from me.

You say that the effects of the painting are permanent, unless ownership of the paintings changes, which means the effects ARENT permanent, and yet the two missing superstars whose paintings the Foundation owns don't seem to have reappeared.

Aaannnd, sorry, but that interview log was pretty painful to read. The Doctor just seemed to be wilfully ignorant to the point of farce. Everyone knows the whole  deal, it's not a difficult concept, and he seemed to be ignorant of the fact that it was even an anomaly (demanding what the superstar means and how it works, etc.)

Oh, and the numbering system, with n-n and all that is unnecessarily complicated. Seems like different paintings all have the same effect, so you could simplify that a lot - no reason to have classifications specific to which person was affected by which painting. Seems like you posted this without getting any feedback first, which would have helped improve a lot of these issues.

Still, the ideas here are really good, so I'll leave it at a no-vote.
