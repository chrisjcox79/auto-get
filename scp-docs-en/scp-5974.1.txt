-1

I feel like there's a good core story here and I like the physical depiction and concept, but it feels like you've made a lot of incorrect assumptions that readers will be keeping up with you, resulting in a lot of basic gaps in the telling between big, swinging story moments. Much like Croquembouche says. For example, in the logs I think I as able to piece together what was going on in terms of people's locations and actions, but you omitted a lot of basic description and included some things that just made it more confusing. Ditto the line between conceptual/noosphere and reality, I never really got what was where.

A small example of a seeming disconnect: . isn't that us, reading this file, as well as all the people discussing it in the meetings and logs?

Another example was you describing Reynes in plain terms as real, then saying that the entity looks like Reynes, and then saying they never existed. Again, I think i see what you were going for, but that kind of retrocausal fuckery is something that you have to build up and pay off, whereas here it just feels like you're saying at least three contradictory facts in the same 

There's leaving room for mystery and then there's just being vague, and this is the latter. I believe you want me to be scratching my head about the Azrael connection, but I'm scratching my head about basic story elements and concepts.

I'm only downvoting on the hopes that you'll fix it. It feels like this is something you could iron out with another pass or two through it.
