Fold

20 Aug 2020 16:27

Thank you for reading and commenting!

Avoiding cliches was definitely a pressing concern while writing. The original idea was “an infohazard that gets more dangerous as you know more about it.” Not terribly new. So the hook that seemed novel was “well, what if it’s such a big chunk of data that only computers could absorb it?” It evolved away from that original concept gradually, maybe to its peril.

The self-aware references to “I Have No Mouth, and I Must Scream,” while those might not work for everyone, was how I hoped to signal awareness of this challenge. Certainly omnicidal AI is played out. SCP-079 is the archetype; it’d dominate the planet if it lived outside of a toaster. Depressed AI has been done (Hitchhiker’s Guide). Benevolent/benign AI is explored in the AIC canon pretty well. I had hoped that “powerful but anxious and only concerned with self-destructive patricide” would have been uncharted territory. It may be one of those cases where that’s not enough for some, too much for others. Such is life. Again, I appreciate your comment as a reminder to aim for more novel, less cliche targets in the future.
