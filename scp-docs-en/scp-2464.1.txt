13 Aug 2014 12:02

Firstly: +1. This is very good.

However, it does have a few issues, which I will highlight on an individual basis.

Showline-by-line

Close

The image - I cannot for the life of me figure out how it relates to the SCP. It appears to be an underground, concrete-walled building like a garage. If it's meant to be the entrance in the limestone mine, it looks nothing like that.

as one travels further and further away from SCP-2464-2
附加记录：    

Is this meant to be -1? It could easily be either, but I wanted to check.

SCP-2464-4 is a machine designed and constructed by the late Dr. Michael Banning
附加记录：    

As this is your first mention of Banning, I think you should add a brief description of who he is - either here, or earlier, perhaps when you first mention the house (it's his house, right?). I was very confused when I read this - is he a Foundation doctor? He is mentioned like someone I should already know.

The apparent purpose of SCP-2464-4 is as a means of extending the lifespan of SCP-2464-3 by means of preventing SCP-2464-3 from aging
附加记录：    

This sentence feels a bit redundant - the repetition of 'means of' highlighting this. You should consider shortening it.

entirely because of the difficulty in finding and containing these entities. It is entirely possible that,
附加记录：    

Might want to consider a re-write to address the repetition of 'entirely'.

Finally, regarding the final addendum: I don't like it. It tells us nothing that the rest of the article doesn't tell us, and it just feels like a note directly from the author (not helped by the name being an obvious reference to your username) telling us that we should have feels about this. Feels would already be there - especially if you included, as I suggested earlier, a quick note indicating that the farmhouse is believed to be the property of Dr Banning, the father of -2 and -3.

But yeah I still really like it and my upvote stands even as-is.

Fold

13 Aug 2014 14:14

This one is okay, but really just okay in my opinion. I think the problem is that there's altogether too much fluff. The infinite space under the cavern is pretty well pointless; its connection to -2, -3, and -4 is tenuous and underdeveloped, which makes it look like filler.

You have, however, managed a really interesting mental image. Strange device with copper pipes feeding into a young girl. Viscous black fluid pouring from her mouth into her sister's mouth. Identical twins (which is really just a short-cut to creepy town), a poorly repaired farm house, constant dusk. It's reasonably surreal, I'll give it that. Some kind of half-remembered dream or something.

But there are flaws. It doesn't cash the emotional check, for one (an angle it's pretty clear you were trying hard for through the last addendum). I'm a dad, even, and I find it hard to relate for some reason. Maybe it's because you wrote the letter from the POV of a Foundation crony and not the POV of Dr. Banning. Maybe it's because  is also the name of the Incredible Hulk. Maybe I'm being a fudd. Nonetheless, it doesn't strike me.

Second major flaw I see is the restricted access. Perhaps I'm jaded or too used to looking at the Foundation from a Level 4+ perspective, but I don't see the need to keep that information secret. It seems to me that it would stress the importance of the classification and the research into repairing SCP-2464-4. Maybe it would make the assignment a bit more stressful, but considering the other kinds of Keter threats the Foundation deals with I don't think that would be a concern.

Despite my misgivings, the prose is solid, the narrative is clear, and most of the choices seem self-consistent and justified. You'll get a 0 from me, but it's an optimistic one.
