Had to downvote this, reasoning below:

+block

–block

I feel like I'm missing something here. At first I thought it was some other article that'd contextualize this but Deci says this stands on its own so I maybe I'm missing something in the article itself. I'm willing to accept that maybe it's me if someone can clue me in on what I'm not getting.

I don't, for instance, understand why the last line has a strong impact on people. There's no aha moment there, since one can tell by the middle of the article that the thing has an environmental bent. The conclusion just seems like a natural direction to take it with no real subversion of expectations (and even if it was subverting the expectations that you set up one sentence prior; that's a weak execution).

There's also a lot here that just goes unexplained, and not in a 

What's the significance of ?

What's the significance of Ashworth Moor Reservoir?

What can be gleaned from the details available on the exaggerated earths?

Also: I could excuse the Foundation not making a direct connection to the Fifthists in the article if it was longer. But when so many of the very limited details point to Fives, the Foundation probably oughta notice. Five earths, .5 they didn't notice any of that and at least deem it worthy of investigation or mention?

Summary and TL:DR:

What's here is impeccably written and has incredibly potential, but doesn't capitalize on that potential. I don't need every article to be a 3,000+ worder, but if you're going for something this short it may behoove you to avoid including so many unfired guns. By the end I was just left unsatisfied with the article, and that's not the reaction I imagine I was supposed to have.
