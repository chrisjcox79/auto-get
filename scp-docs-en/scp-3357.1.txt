08 Jul 2017 08:00

I enjoyed this quite a bit; the writing is clean, the concept intriguing, and the dialogue extremely well-polished. However (you could tell that was coming, probably ):

I feel like Dr. Cadence's insistence that SCP-3357-1 is responsible for the containment breach (and therefore the deaths) is both deeply unfair and highly manipulative. If you stick somebody in a cage and drop that cage in a mine-field, you can't really yell at them when they sneak free and something blows up. Especially if you never even told them about the mines!

That being said, one way to take it (and the way I took it) is that Dr. Cadence was just using SCP-3357-1's guilt against them to get more info. Even if this wasn't intended to be the case, the article's good enough otherwise to merit an upvote.

It makes me happy to know that you enjoyed the article.

The way I intended Dr. Cadence's blame of SCP-3357-1 to be taken was both as unfairly manipulative and as information-gathering. In my mind, she genuinely holds SCP-3357 partially at fault for the incident (she blames herself just as much, and she really is meant to be on psychiatric leave after what happened), but when Cadence realizes that the anomaly feels guilty, she takes advantage of that to squeeze something useful out of the interaction.
