It is a good article, but there are some glaring problems.

The Containment makes it clear and all-too-relevant that gravestones and coffins are transplanted from somewhere else to some other place, but the description gives nothing about it. Although the log does include a coffin it doesn't disappear either, so there is no relation whatsoever between the article and its containment procedures (apart from keeping people from entering).

Also it is said that the con artist evaded the Foundation in the past, but stops on that and it doesn't have any impact in the story whatsoever, so it must either be elaborated on or removed, IMHO, 'cause it does stand out.

Some info on the prices at the bar itself would be good, although it already made it clear that it costs actual common money. I would argue for a more thorough investigation on all the things, but it doesn't really need it.

16 Dec 2020 17:00

I like the play on the honey panacea scam and the funk. Monteparnas seems to be saying the gravestone/coffin transportation is a Chekhov's gun situation, but I disagree. The ending of the story clearly reveals one mysterious way in which the bodies are sourced. I also like that this is not 100% doom and gloom, since the effects are seemingly positive and these entities may be content immortals enjoying a benign afterlife/second life. There's still that menace/sickening factor in the cannibalistic element though, which gives it an appropriate SCP flavor.

One tweak I'd humbly recommend since Dr Calder described a similar issue: when the log starts, I was a bit thrown off by the names. It may just be me, but it seemed at first like the agents were listening in on a conversation in the house due to the casual tone and use of pronouns. Shortly after, I realized the two speakers were the agents, but it briefly took me out of the article. Maybe put Agent in front of their names or something like that? It would also help fix the issue Dr Calder had with similar names.

I'll +1 anyway, just some thoughts.
