18 Aug 2014 19:14

This is a great example of how to do story in the SCP format. The log is pleasingly weird and provides enough storytelling to generate interest in the reader, without resorting to a Dr. Author figure explaining things directly or a convenient note mysteriously left behind. I enjoyed how the entries in the list progressed, and I really liked the idea that somewhere out there, the future prime minister of Montenegro is blissfully unaware that a corpse identical to them is buried in a pet cemetery in Florida.

That all said, I would enjoy this even more if the background stuff was pared down a bit. I found the idea of the Foundation intervening in a situation just because someone decided to bury humans in a pet cemetery a little belief-stretching, and the notion that everyone's genetic information is on file at the Foundation even more so. For me, that stuff didn't really add much to the article.

On balance, though, this was good. Upvoted.

[beware: headcanon ahead]

LMTF 352 is responsible for investigating weird news stories they can get to in an eleven-county area of North Florida. They're not omnipresent, but this was only a few miles from their Gainesville headquarters and Wallace was tired of losing at ping pong when the Sun story came down the wire. Lucky catch, but they probably would've got to it after the Sheriff found out about the  thing.

And the Foundation are packrats, and have a lot of weird/anomalous materials with DNA in them. They don't keep everyone's genetic information, but the Foundation (and its giant, lumbering, well-funded bureaucracy) have made it a priority since the late 80's to keep as much genetic info as possible, starting with world leaders. Who wouldn't want to know that the flesh lump they found bouncing around a racquetball court in Amsterdam appeared to come from former Mexican president Vincente Fox?
