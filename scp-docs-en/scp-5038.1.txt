I want to chime in here and say something.

When Riemann and I discussed this during critique, he raised all these very valid points. I considered them and had a few lengthy discussions with other critiquers afterwards.

+Arguments

–block

In the end I made the conscious choice to disregard several of these points, not because I didn't feel like they were legitimate, but that they simply didn't outweigh my opinions on how this piece stands. There are several things that we accept exist in a kind of . The Foundation is a giant bureaucratic entity that exists to protect normalcy. The Foundation is a global entity and for the most part tries to act solely to protect normalcy, no matter the cost. The Foundation generally has the technology/financial capacity/manpower/facilities necessary to execute the special containment procedures listed within the first part of the document.

Now, each of these don't need to be explained in order for the piece to stand alone. I think that we would RAPIDLY lose readership if we included banal things like financial statements or a cost/benefit analysis for every single SCP as a part of the standard.

Since I am very much including this within the context of a pre-existing canon, similar understood things must apply. Some of my favorite authors and stories exist within the context of  the reader immediately into the pre-existing lives of the characters within, then allowing contextual clues provided along the way to showcase the backstory.

I have tried to encapsulate that idea here. Yes, there is pre-existing architecture here for the reader to look back on, should they choose. We don't require readers to go back and check up on any of the History of the Foundation documentation in order to read and understand whatever the current piece is. Hell, when 173 was written, none of that existed.

So, I chose to allow the curiosity of the reader guide their eye. Should they read this and wish to know more, the links exist to allow that. If they do not, then they are welcome to enjoy or dislike this piece on its own merits.
