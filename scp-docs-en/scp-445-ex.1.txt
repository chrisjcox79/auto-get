Site Criticism Team - Review

08 Jul 2019 05:14

I have thoughts about this that I've been dwelling on since I read this last night.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

+[Critique]Access

-[ClickText]

I so badly want to like this article. I want to upvote this. But I can't bring myself to.

Echoing from what Chandra said, I don't think real-world problems would have the same weight that they do on the rest of the world. They just seem so minor compared to the work the Foundation is doing, especially when many articles make the Foundation out to having a lot of weight to throw around. It's also weird to see such things affect Wondertainment as a company.

But I get it. The Wonder World!™ canon is about taking in real-life for what it is. It's about realizing that there comes a day when you can't be a child anymore and your actions have meaning and you have to grow up or the world rejects you. And this really hits that in how Holly rants forever about how little hope she has left and how much of a burden looking at the world outside of Wonder World!™ is. I get it. The world is unfriendly and there's only so much magic can do. I didn't need Holly talking for half of the skip to get that information, but I understand.

Normally, I would upvote on the sentiments presented alone because that's heartbreaking and I'm really feeling that, but Wondertainment giving in is such a jarring thing to see. I find it incredibly hard to believe that they would settle for working with the Foundation, and I find it even more unlikely that the Foundation would give Wondertainment the time of day and compromise on manufacturing their toys.

I want to like this, but I'm conflicted. So this is a no-vote.

SoullessSingularity, for any questions or concerns relating to this review.

Re: Site Criticism Team - Review

08 Jul 2019 15:36

I think this is an area in which we simply fundamentally disagree with our headcanons. After 133 years of existence, Dr. Wondertainment the company is not giving up, they are realising that they are failing their purpose because they need to be reaching everyone, not just the anomalous community.

The Foundation is giving them the time of day because the Foundation’s purpose is to maintain and preserve normalcy while studying abnormalcy. They are more than willing to push an anomalous company into collapsing back to relative normalcy, especially in exchange for close contact with one of the largest anomalous manufacturers in the world (I’m sure there’s a lot of knowledge to be gained through studying how they make “toys”). That’s where my mind is.

The “real world problems affecting the SCP universe” is personal taste that I can’t get around. There are some ideas I have about that that I will write more so I’ll save them for another time. In any case, I see where you’re coming from, I think we have different views on how certain organisations would act.
