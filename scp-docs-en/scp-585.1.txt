Fold

03 Aug 2012 06:08

I know this post is quite old, but I wanted to expand on this a little.

The penetration depth is actually completely independent of the sharpness, and actually depends on the amount of energy put in. (Consider that a bullet isn't sharp, but will penetrate very far through materials softer than it's own because of its high energy). Regardless of how sharp the pencil is, tearing apart 5 metres of steel takes a definite and very large amount of energy, certainly more than the pencil would have just from being dropped. So, the sharpener actually very seriously violating the conservation of energy.

There's really no reason why some device couldn't extract this free energy, and provide unlimited free electricity for the Foundation.

Knowing this doesn't really detract from the article. It's just a thought.

Edit: Also, I'm no nuclear fission expert, but I'm pretty sure splitting carbon atoms will absorb energy, not produce it, so there wouldn't be any risk of explosions. The sharpener would become virtually impossible to turn due to the energy needed.
