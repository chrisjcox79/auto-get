Many thanks to everyone in chat who reviewed this and gave me critique, especially Scantron, Kens, Nusquam (Adam?), Vivax, Jekeled, and Bright. (and Moose.)

If you don’t understand the SCP, perhaps look at the small hints for context?

Spoilers?

Spoilerrssss

Do you have an idea of who the parents are? If not, consider this: drawing from details in the addenda (the reasons for the father's love, and why the mother stayed while the child left), what changes with the seasons and is rooted to the spot?

The answer is what the family members are, and explains why the father was taken away from the mother and child. Also, what can both boats and wagons be made of? That’s what happened to the father.

The child himself is attempting to learn to heal others so he can bring back his father, from whatever’s left of him. He heals anything nearby when he can, not out of generosity, but because he needs to practice. The reason for those rectangular shaped cells? Plant cells. His healing isn’t exactly suited for animals, and if anything, he doesn’t particularly care for them.

Also, the reason he can’t be contained in a place outside his forest is the same reason my potted bamboo died right after I brought it home for winter break.

Incidentally, a few people I showed this to initially thought that the parents were both deities.

Also, in regards to the Blue Ridge mountains

MOAR!Spoilers

hide

The reason I picked that location was because of its relative proximity to the east coast of the United States (as opposed to, say the Rocky Mountains). The other location SCP-1338 was seen in, a forest in England is also significant, in that the family originally lived together in one of those locations before humans interfered. Putting together the backstory reveals for how long now the child has tried to take care of his father. Also, in regards to that, SCP-1338 appears as a child because his aging process is so slow, due to his being, well, not human.
