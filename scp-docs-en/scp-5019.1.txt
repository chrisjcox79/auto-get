15 Feb 2020 05:25

So it's been awhile since I posted a scip, eh? I guess it's just hard to find what works. This idea originally was something I had in the format of a parawatch tale, but Calibri Bold confirmed that it didn't work in that format. So I changed up some things, and now here it is as an SCP!

This scip has my feelings in it in some places, so I guess it means a good bit to me

All art is done by me. All profile pictures used are either art pieces I did, or pictures I took. One is of my cat, one is of ice-cream I had one time. If you want to see the full images I'll show you, but they're mine lol.

Special thanks to my reviwers/betareaders:

MalyceGraves-Gave me the idea for the AWCY bit and final addendum, and with wording and flow in general.

 Hope y'all enjoyed! It only took like a week to whip up, but it also took some hard work and dedication. Until next time!~

15 Feb 2020 09:54

So, all the media bits didn't really do that much for me. They only really existed to reiterate what had been set out in the paragraph before, and as an excuse to put some art in. Since the characters set out in them didn't really show up again, they went on far longer than they deserved. There's also a fair few grammatical/spelling/tonal errors throughout the piece.

The final messages were entirely lacking in any form of subtlety. The Twitter thread only served to tell the reader about this character and why she made the episode, without leaving anything up to the reader to work it out. Being practically hit on the head with the intentions without any room for interpretations made the entire message feel extremely forced.

Finally, as is extremely common for this group of interest, the AWCY? link at the end felt extremely tacked on. It attempts to introduce a new character with new motives which aren't developed on much at all in the time, for what felt like just an excuse to jam in the final image and the connection to the group. I understand that it serves as an explanation for the anomalous effects; however, it's both unclear how or why these specific effects have manifested, something which is only explained with a 'they don't know'.

I'd go further than tacked on and say that the AWCY connection felt out of character for the group. To my mind, AWCY are mainly defined by a culture of 'high art' - appreciation for classical styles and postmodern innovation, installations displayed in exclusive galleries, making a statement with their work. Working for an animated television series aimed at a youth audience feels very far from that scene. I think distilling things down to  is an oversimplification, without consideration for the mediums or culture.

That it comes out of nowhere right at the end kind of compounds the feeling that the mention lacks much justification for being there at all.

15 Feb 2020 13:07

Overall, I found this article to be enjoyable, and it has quite the potential indeed. I don’t think this is a bad piece, but I feel it still has some stuff that needs addressing.

Firstly, I don’t really get the object classification. I don’t see a reason for this SCP to be a Keter, and for the Foundation to have such a hard time redacting and censoring it. The risk class and notice class also seem a bit off; In my humble reader opinion, I think you should either make the object more containable or make the relation between the whole “entitlement and censorship” theme and the anomalous properties more obvious and logical to us readers.

Also, just like gee0765 pointed out earlier, the ending is quite underwhelming. The AWCY? bit seems really artificial and lame. Maybe you could change the ending to focus more on the relationship between the two PoI, and an eventual fallout and maybe suicide as cause of the anomalous behavior. ( Note: this is just something I came up with on a whim. I’m certain you can probably figure something that better fits your style and objectives)

In conclusion, I must say that while this article is still not SCP Foundation quality, its not THAT far from it. Anyway, this is all just this newbie’s opinion. I really hope your SCP grows and develops exponentially, DianaBerry!
