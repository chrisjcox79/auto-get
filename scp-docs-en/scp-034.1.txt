Its a clever and inventive idea- an army of ables, with all his ability but non of the sociopathy. We would only turn people into him that we knew were 100 percent loyal, and we could control them the same way we control the original; with an explosive collar just in case. Yes, he probably would freak out and try to kill them all if he knew, but who's to say he has to be told? And think, we can flay him all we want, but eventually he'll just regenerate, so the number of troops is limitless. Hey! Maybe that's why the guy tried to infiltrate the site! To steal skin. Makes sense.

Of course, going behind his back like that would be dangerous, but definitely interesting. I can imagine The Foundation keeping that secret from him, covering it up, hiding the different versions when he walks by.

Maybe they have a couple strips of skin stocked up just in case he goes completely rogue and somehow escapes?

This kind of ties into an idea i was thinking about. We're given the perception that the Foundation is extremely powerful, especially by the implication that they successfully capture all these keter class objects and foil their escape attempts. Like 682 for example; it's not really said exactly how it's stopped every time, but they do it. How? The Foundation doesn't have access to super science, so what sets them apart from normal soldiers and commandos? Obviously they use SCPs to their fullest advantage. I think this would be one of them.

Two things though Lacynt:

It couldn't be used on 682, it doesn't work on animals. On that line of thought, maybe it wouldn't work of able either; i mean he's not exactly human is he? didn't the article say something about his organs being different.

Secondly, not every object has to have a use or be destroyed. Thats a really bad misconception. The Foundation contains almost everything, even if it costs money or has no purpose. That's its job. If it was all about the benefits it wouldn't need funding. But its not a business or something thats completely self-sufficient. I bet governments pump money into this organization. So it doesn't have to do anything helpful. It just has to be special. I really dislike the other point of view.

Except for name who directly mentions this in his post.

Is Able classified as non-human? Just because he is ever-respawning doesn't necessarily make it so that he is non-human. Although it is a good indication. The series canon with the interaction of Cain and Able in the Wanderlust points to emotions similar to what would come from the Bible story of the two brothers of the first human family.

Anyway, the testing of such an experiment would be tricky, as it would have to be an unquestioningly loyal Agent or Doctor to not abuse the powers granted to him. Also, if we killed Able while someone was disguised as him, would the disguise fade because Able isn't technically dead, just not entirely alive? Another consideration, if the 'death' of Able ends the disguise, will his reincarnation restart it if the time hasn't expired? What about samples of flesh harvested from Ables that have 'died' before?

However, with the assertion that 034 "ONLY WORKS ON HUMANS" is contradicted quickly by saying "the results were… well… unexpected" meaning that 034 does work, just not in a manner that the Foundation likes. Further clarification should be done in the form of releasing of test files of animal/non-human species, or trying more experiments, preferably weaker easily controlled species selected.

Does the limitation of 034 apply opposite the above proposition, with animals being tried to change into others of the same species and different species, before attempting making an animal into a human form.
