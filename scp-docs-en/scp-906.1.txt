Fold

12 Apr 2011 21:48

More nitpicks:

-The phrase  is redundant. If it's not bipedal, it's not humanoid.

-Caustic and corrosive are not synonymous: caustic refers to the actions of strong bases, while corrosive refers to acids. So is their secretion acidic or basic? Also, titanium is already corrosion-resistant, so saying the secretion doesn't significantly affect Ti doesn't really say much. And, if it doesn't affect glass, then its effect is not comparable to hydrofluoric acid. (Also, what the heck is acid-treated glass? A Google search found that  was just made translucent, or had translucent patterns put in, via etching.)

-Whether the gunk is caustic or corrosive, either way it's not going to melt anything. The word you want is dissolve.

-If you cut an earthworm, all you'll get is a dead earthworm. You're thinking flatworms, not earthworms.

Too many little problems like this in the article. The concept itself seems okay, but downvoting on execution.
