Hey wait a minute, this isn't one of my articles! 

No, but honestly, fantastic work with this. You use the conprocs to effectively hook the reader, the description elaborates on a lot without beating around the bush, and the exploration / interview logs tell an engaging story.

My crit would be that the  addendum doesn't really do anything for the narrative. It just states facts we don't necessarily need. Also, I could have used a bit more prose and emotional resonance in the final interview. This person has had an intensely therapeutic experience, letting go of pain from the past and moving forward towards healthier relationships. I just don't feel that change in their tone and body language right now. It could be easily remedied with some quick changes to the dialogue, and a few additional notes on their movement / speech patterns. Illustrate for us!

Either way, +1. I'm very impressed, and hope to see more!

Yeah, the discovery addendum is kind of a leftover from an earlier draft where I included this whole storyline about a Person of Interest, with the addendum setting that storyline up for later. I do think that removing the discovery addendum altogether wouldn't work for me, however. I do feel that including the addendum acts as a little bit of a breather for what happens next (i.e. the exploration log), and that excluding it altogether would be a little bit abrupt. However, that's just my pretentious side speaking there.

As for the final interview stuff? I'm going to make it a little more emotionally impactful at some point, although I'm probably going to leave it be for the time being.

Either way, thank you very much for your insight, T Rutherford!
