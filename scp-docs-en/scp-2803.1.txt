Fold

19 Feb 2016 08:20

There's a limit to how much comedically misspelled, Spanko-style interviews I can enjoy, and I think I've reached it. It feels as though there are two dave-templates operating at the moment, one of which finishes with a short interview log which establishes a single character trait, usually for comedic effect (this one, 2324, 4055-J, 2527, 3V1L-J, TTKU-J, SCP-J) and the other of which uses escalating lists for comedic effect (WTF-J, 7000-J). 2219 and 2416 combine both templates. Many of the shorter-lived SCPs also fall into one of these camps.

That's not to say that the templates aren't effective. They are, and there are some good writing techniques at work in each of them. And they clearly work for the up-voting audience. It's just become a bit predictable for me - there's only so many times I can see the same trick done well and still be impressed.

I'm going to agree with what psul said, and add this: this explanation is basically the least satisfying explanation that could be offered, even more unsatisfying, I'd argue, than never answering the question at all.

I wasn't a big fan of the original video game articles, but I at least thought that there were intriguing questions to be had from them. Why would a modern gaming company emulate an Assyrian god? Why does it resemble bootleg software? Is there a better version out there?

The answer to every question, this article shows, is .

I will grant this article a few things: the description of the entity is rather nice, and the foundation pleading for it to continue making video games is somewhat cute. But beyond that, this article obliterates the only things, in my view, the previous articles had going for them, and psul's comment sadly made me realize how formulaic this all is. There isn't a deeper story because the formula barely allows for a basic story.

So, all in all: downvote.
