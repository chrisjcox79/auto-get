20 Jun 2018 06:04

I just can't take this seriously. We've had two, if I can remember correctly, SCPs that play strongly on the body horror of being infected by bees or wasps, but you spend virtually no time cultivating the necessary sense of disgust for that to work. You have some implications that things are getting weirder (bodies/DNA/offspring changing), but only implications, and they're lightly sprinkled through a sea of bland exposition, bland expository dialogue, and difficult-to-believe plot turns.

- Like, having your characters point out how ridiculous it is to wear bee suits all the time just made me go, 

- Also it's odd that you dedicated a huge table to flowers and nothing to the crazier (body/identity horror) stuff.

- Another example, you log is full of a: a few items of actual necessary plot + a whole lot of nothing + One body horror reference + Bear friggin Bond-villain level monologue-ing exposition. The O5 logs similarly lacked unique voices or interesting content.

I'm guessing a lot of this could be due to this being a contest entry. I haven't read the other articles, and this didn't really make me want to click any of the crosslinks.

My biggest takeaway, you've got a base/starting SCP that still feels like it needs some polish before making the mainlist (in terms of how and when effects are discussed), but you're also asking it to support an end-of-the-world story that often beggars suspension of disbelief and is weighed down with dry story mechanisms. That ship doesn't float.

-1

+Block

-Block

I wasn't trying to go for body horror. Body horror isn't something that I care about. I'm more interested in the Foundation's philosophy. In particular, I want bee people to be on an equal footing with humanity, with only mild amounts of body horror and weirdness so that SCP-2401, in which bees turn a person into some weird hive mind I guess. That one has plenty of body horror. I feel that if I took a U-turn and capitalized on the body horror of bees making hives out of people, I would end up with a replica of SCP-2401 and the entire SCP would be taken down due to being too similar.

The characters decided to wear something akin to beekeeper suits to prevent infection. It didn't work obviously, but it's the best thing they had. Of course it's stupid, but that's because there is no miracle cure, there are only poor inventions that prolong the inevitable. It's similar to the scenario in the Left 4 Dead franchise, where government agents wore biohazard suits and still got zombified. That said, I suppose I should make it so that they don't wear the suits 24/7, only when outside a Foundation building.

The flower table exists to show off the potential of the flowers effects on SCP-3368-1. I've kept it thus far because I believe that it's important to show the scope of these effects. The scope is used as a talking point in a couple places, both later on in this SCP and SCP-2000-B. I used to have an experiment log that described the effects of the mind honey, but it was poorly written and I was coerced to remove it to reduce the article's length. I suppose that I could remove the flower table as well (you aren't the first person to talk to me about it). I personally like the extra world building that it provides, but I can see why others don't. As for the crazier identity horror stuff, my teammate Casponaut wrote an article for that.

I can't say much of the issues in your third point without more details/suggestions. Personally, I feel that the story is already pretty condensed. I could, for example, take out the CI log and references to the GOC completely and just have the special containment procedures increase be something of the Foundation's own decision but that sounds even worse than what I have written. The GOC exists in this SCP to pigeonhole the Foundation into making poor decisions and to explain their increasingly militant behavior. As for the O5's, I gave them each a headcannon from the command dossier web page. With the exception of O5-2 and O5-6, I did not spend a lot of time giving them additional characterization, because I believe that would increase the length of this SCP article even more. I'm surprised that you found them to lack interesting content. I personally had a lot of fun writing the votes, which have nods to each of the O5's chosen headcannons. As for Bear, I had a lot of trouble writing his exposition bit, while also having him order the deaths of three people. I'm not sure how to make that part more palatable.

I honestly can't make sense of  in the final paragraph. I'm not entirely sure where I lost your sense of disbelief and how I could make this SCP article anything but dry. I also don't understand the connection that you propose about dry writing and sense of disbelief. I mean, most SCPs are written in dry language.

While I do have to agree with some of the criticisms proposed by the users above, I ultimately found this story to be engaging throughout most of the read. Like, the O5 correspondences for instance. I found those mostly unnecessary due to the large volume of things the Overseers spoke about, but I ultimately enjoyed them because we don't often see how the O5 will go through and deal with such an anomaly in its various stages.

The way this scip built on itself throughout was insane, even if some parts felt like a cheesy action flick (the initial raid on the Chaos Insurgency outpost, for instance). You took the time to explore nearly every avenue this scip offered, even if some parts weren't particularly interesting, and I find it commendable.

One final thing: I was pretty ambivalent about the final note. It seems more like a place to cram in bee references than a necessary addition to the story. I'd say the parts that pertain directly to O5-2 and MTF-Mu-Pi-6 are the most in need of work. The rest is enjoyable.
