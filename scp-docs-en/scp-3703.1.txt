This is a continuation of the Orcadian Saga, and something I spent the last two full months working on. It started off as crystals made from Finnfolk sacrificing themselves, and evolved into clusters of corpses, where the original themes were unclear it evolved to focus on sacrifice, loss, and desperation. This is one step closer to my 001 proposal. Thanks for all the help.

OtherCanon

HideStuff

SCP-3456 The Orcadian Horseme
SCP-3047 To Be Drowned By A Train
SCP-3700 Tides of War
SCP-3702 I Walk Through The Desert, And I Have No Name
The Seas of Orcadia Part 1: How I Met Your Mither
SCP-2368 Across The Water Related but as of yet not directly connected.

Warningexplanation

hidespoilers

The corpse blobs are being partially controlled by the finnwoman, whose name is Triemides. She is able to remotely control some aspects of the corpses, mostly relating to the subliminal messaging and communication attempts via the carvings on various objects. She's using this to send cries for help in whatever way she can, due to the fact that she's losing a 5000 year fight against Teran, partially because of human inflicted damage on the sites that the corpse blobs are located at. This is seen at the end of the SCP. As for the purpose of the corpse balls themselves, they are holding open a trans-planar tunnel which is being used to power my 001.

It's implied at the end that Alva is not actually who she seems to be. In Orcadian myth, there is a deity know as The Mither o' Sea, and another Deity known as Teran. They fight an endless cycle of dominance with one another (A theme which is heavily prevalent in 3700) and this is another piece where that theme is coming into play. Alva, is the Mither in human form, and the Fishwoman's message at the end reveals this, and the large humanoid entity is Teran.

There are two themes running in this piece, which consist of Triemides desperation, and the sacrifices that the Finnfolk made to keep this and 5 other deities sealed (I.E. literally sacrificing themselves in large numbers to accomplish this goal.)

There are a lot of things I deeply enjoy about this (e.g., the mental image of 3703/the finfolk, the implications about Alva/history/the Foundation, the carved dialogue, the mythological beasts), and some things that didn't engage me much (the war of the Gods), but there are two major problems that honestly are leading me all the way to a downvote.

+block

–block

First, the description is a mess. The base anomaly is thoroughly complicated, and not broken down in a clear and understandable manner. I had to read the entire article and then reread the description twice before I got the full picture.

- Describing 3703s as structures before talking about the structure they are found in (dash1) gave me the complete wrong initial mental image.

- Describing their geometric/thaumaturgical shape and the presence of extradimensional portals, THEN their ability to interact with things, THEN the plasma generation caused confusion about what was important to know/remember, as did not designating the plasma with a letter or number.

- (Also having both -A and -1 was not a major problem, but it didn't help me zoning out a couple paragraphs in, especially as the list of effects stacked higher and higher.)

- It feels like a lot of the effects (the manipulating appendages and cognitohazards especially) were basically typed in in the order that they showed up in the later story, and thus it feels like they had too much detail or were largely superfluous. Yes, the Foundation would document them like you have, but you aren't the Foundation, and you've got a bigger story to tell.

Second big problem was Document A. This was a gigantic failure of show-don't-tell, and left me rolling my eyes for the entire duration. It was basically an attempt to explain the entire premise to the reader, and isn't even remotely an interesting interpretation. I would've been far happier with my own theories.

Look this is already a decadent exercise in rich, mythological storytelling, using a complex narrative with many subtle references. That is absolutely not a problem, but you can't afford to do both that and be indulgent or loose in other areas. -1

Improving the description would get me a fair distance towards upvoting.
