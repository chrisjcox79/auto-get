Re:

The problem isn't so much the size. It'd be physically impossible for a mechanical device to replicate the speed of a electronic computer. That's why the compression in space and time.

It's a little bit of a cheat and a conceit, but it's some of the only ways you could possibly make mechanical objects that replicate the capabilities of common consumer electronics. I justify it by it explaining that the complexity of 914 means sometimes unintended consequences happen. The presence of another powerful SCP (808) changes the mix enough that 914 decides to skip electronic principles and create a computer using general relativistic ones.

Sometimes, the 1:1 correspondence can be a little bit more of conceptual, rather than a physical match.

Besides, it's kinda neat to try to figure out how the contortions in space/time allows 915 to be the same mass as the input computer.

808's aura would probably want it to be pushed towards increased reliance on electron quantum states, since she's a conduit for, as Fish puts it, a "universe of pure electronic order".

914, however, wasn't having any of that. I'm still divided as to whether 914 was afraid of 808 the conduit, or trying to help out 808 the person. Or maybe 914 just got bored and decided introducing extradimensional space would be a nifty thing to try.
