Also:

constructed from an unknown material
附加记录：    

Here we go

proven to be completely impervious to damage
附加记录：    

No thank you.

impossible dimensions
附加记录：    

There is already an SCP that does this, and is probably better than this.

the doors of the container act as a portal to the interior
附加记录：    

There are already a city full of portal SCP's that do various weird and odd things, they are pretty much better than this

the entire Experimentation Log
附加记录：    

Look, this does't make it more interesting nor does it tell us more about it than we already know.

To be frank, the idea of being alone and confused and frightend in a room for all eternity, isolated so that no matter how long a person screams no one can hear him, whatnot, sounds great. However, all the OTHER things tacked on detract from that potential horror, until you just have a mess of 'interesting' details that lose the core of the SCP- a box that messes with time when you shove people in it. It also brings up the question do they just sit there, do they scream or cry or scrape against the walls so hard their nails break? If it were mere after a time i would be writing 'time time time' and various clocks in various positions with my own blood evrywhere because isolation does horrible things to a psyche. If a person became a child would they remember everything that had happened to them prior to their age-change or do their memories revert? If a person stays there long enough, what happens if they need to poop or pee? What if they're hungry or thirsty?

Final Report: 

SCP that buries its hook with a buncha unnecessary details. Downvoted.

Living the dream, or dreaming the life?

I can fix this. I'll strain out all the clichés I can, but a few of them are necessary.

1: The interior has to be indestructible, otherwise someone would punch a hole in it and take a peek outside.

2: If it's indestructible, you can't really test it thoroughly, so it's going to be unknown. What if I added a few more details about the material from non-invasive tests/observations?

3: It also has to be some kind of portal, otherwise you could punch a hole in the exterior and take a peek inside.

The impossible dimensions thing can be taken out, you're right about that.
