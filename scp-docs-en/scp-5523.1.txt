27 Mar 2020 09:34

I feel like this is missing some story development, and has some writing issues.

On the story development side, this is basically a thing that compels deer to eat it, then die. The twist is that this is a good thing, apparently. I don't really get why that should be. As described, the deer are going extinct very rapidly, because they die less than 24 hours after birth, as do the mothers. More exposition is needed to show why this rapid extinction of the deer is good, ecologically speaking. Or to show why the deer don't go extinct.

On the writing side:

Fawn should be fawns, I think.

Footnote 5 needs a -1

Saying  all over the place seems a bit non-clinical.

SCP-5523 seems to be much more than just a behavioral change in female deer - other anomalous behavior includes the seeds in the breast milk, the rapid decomposition, the rapid growth, and the hallucinations experienced by Sid.
