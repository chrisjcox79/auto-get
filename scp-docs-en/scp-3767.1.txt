Oh, I thought it was pretty clear.

First, an extra passenger gets put on board. This passenger is a fake person.

In almost all cases, when there isn't outside interference, someone gets thrown off the plane. The plane then ascends, and lands safely. If the passenger that got thrown off was the fake person, no issue. Otherwise (63% of the time) a real person has gone missing And fake person goes on living somewhere, presumably. And in all cases of someone being thrown off, there is no second plane.

In the particular case, they did not throw anyone off the plane before hitting the ground. All the real people, and the fake kid, died. New plane was created. The fact the d-3114 from the new plane was fake, implies EVERYONE on the new plane is fake. Which is also implied by all of the real versions being dead in the plane that hit the ground.

The only issue is, at 1 meter per second, they should have had 3 hours before crashing, which does not seem like the amount of time that passed.
