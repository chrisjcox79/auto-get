Fold

That's not actually true; fortune cookies are originally Japanese, all the way back to the 19th Century. There are a few differences:

The fortunes were originally in the bend, not folded into the cookie.
The cookies were soy miso based, not vanilla and butter.
They are called 辻占煎餅: fortune-telling rice crackers.
They're larger and darker in color.

You can still buy them near the Fushimi Inari-taisha shrine in Kyoto.
