This feels like the anomaly is contrived to deliver a story that never needed an anomaly to exist in the first place. I like the story itself, but I often tell people that if you're going to write an SCP (or a Critter Profile, as I critique those a lot and the advice remains the same), then the story you tell should have the anomaly be a central element  something that the story would not exist without. Otherwise, you should make it a tale, in my opinion. And this story does not need the heart in a backpack.

Formats in which you can start with an anomaly as a result of a story that doesn't need the anomaly to exist include Are We Cool Yet? projects, because the artist is creating something that pulls from other experiences they have had. But then again, I don't particularly like Are We Cool Yet? GoI formats for that very reason. I dunno.

Anyways, I did like the story. I wish it was a tale. You could even have titled it, , which would have been adorable. As is, novote.

So, I see some more effort was put here since the time I saw it as a draft (I remember stating what my problems were on an earlier stage of this, even though I'm not mentioned in the author's post. Dunno if it was done deliberately or not, but okay. The point is that I remember seeing the earlier version of this). You've definitely attempted to put more story into the piece, so it doesn't feel barebones as it did initially.

However, I still didn't enjoy this. The story, while it adds some weight to the article, describes a very cliched romance that we're used to seeing in media: a shy person meets a confident, cool person whom he/she likes very much and learns that the cool person likes them back pretty much instantly. Then they fall in love unrealistically quick, even though their characters don't really match that well. That's very typical turn of events, and tropes like this are frequently used, with a difference that here the characters are gay. Regarding that, I can understand that them being gay makes the article more relatable for some wiki members, but it didn't do so for me and this fact doesn't directly influence the writing quality, so I'm viewing this as neither an upside or a downside and judging the narrative from a more general standpoint.

The ending twist isn't bad at all, but it wasn't as effective as it could've been, because the narrative and the characters that were presented before it weren't that interesting or engaging.
