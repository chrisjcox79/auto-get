Some detail about this SCP

04 Jan 2012 02:22

I always do one of these for an SCP, so I figured I'd do one for this one.

This SCP was inspired by a dream I had while I was sick a few weeks ago. Vaguely like Dyson Trees? I looked it up, and was shocked, since I've never seen anything like it before. I decided to keep working on the thing, because I liked the mystery of the idea. What is it? Who made it? Where did it come from? Why is it trying to do something with Earth? I chose the number 1923, since that Dyson guy was born in 1923.

The asteroid I used was 951 Gaspra, which coincidentally is in the 'Flora' grouping.

04 Jan 2012 07:14

I really like this one. The fact that quite a lot is unknown simply because it is hard to get to is very plausible.

Some suggestions

As pro-mole said, fauna is animals, flora is plant like

Redacting the actual ID of the asteroids involved makes perfect sense but does make it hard to follow . For example the sentence 'All signals originating from ███ ██████ are to be recorded and analyzed.' is ambiguous, unless that is your intention given addendum 2. Have you considered giving the different asteroid sub SCP designations e.g. SCP-1923-1,- 2, -3?

While you have indicated all conclusions are tenuous given the quality of photos, if the foundation can get a camera close enough to determine colours and relatively modest scale objects (leaves on the shedding 'tree', movement of the newer object) they should be collecting enough data to perform some basic spectroscopy. That would let someone say with more confidence if the growth is reflecting light in the same way as Earth chlorophyll does. That doesn't mean though that the actual results wouldn't be classified, redacted or informative but the foundation should be able to determine slightly more than they have. If the infection's metabolism is in anyway analogous to the chlorophyll cycle then being able to reach that height in the limited amounts of light (good old inverse square law) and free carbon where they are, with enough excess energy to power movement is pretty damn terrifying and the foundation would be right be be concerned about it targeting earth.

The fact that the foundation have before and after shots to estimate the height of the growth suggests they are monitoring lots and lots of asteroids on an ongoing basis which is itself pretty cool.

I am happy to up vote this and am interested in seeing how this will develop.
