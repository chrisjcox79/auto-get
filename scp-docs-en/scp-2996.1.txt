19 Aug 2014 22:22

I went back and forth. I'm not one who needs fifty paragraphs of containment, as I feel that excessive technical detail can ruin articles. But I would have liked a little more knowledge on how it is the Foundation can contain ghosts. As it stands, that part feels very handwaved, and for all we know it's a bunch of technicians in beige jumpsuits with proton packs.

However, I was also intrigued by the fact that the Foundation encounters enough of these kinds of things that there's a formal classification system. I like ghost stories, so I found that to be kind of a cool bit.

Then we're doing psychological wellness checks on a ghost, and I'm like , but that got a lot less ridiculous when it turned out that the Foundation can bring people back from that state. So that ended up okay on balance.

The initial ghost dialogueI mean, the narm burned in a lot of places, which I understand was probably intended, but I still caught myself cringing a few times. It got better, though.

I actually didn't mind the blackboxed reveal that the ghost was human again. I was okay with that. I was not okay with the needless vivisection business (The Horrible Evil Foundation is not a favored trope of mine). It turns out to have been a mistake, which is less bad, but still kind of an unwelcome development.

And then the ending. Scary infiltrating doctor-monster thing completely out of nowhere. I can envision a scenario where I'm prepared with some sort of reason for that development and end up not disliking it, but it just sort of shows up and BAM. On an article where my verdict was wobbling, that sealed my downvote.
