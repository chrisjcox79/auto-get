This is the final entry of Team 

I'd like to thank the members of my team, first and foremost. rumetzen, you will always be our spirit animal. Writing (and complaining) with all of you has been a blast.

Secondary thanks goes out to Grigori Karpin for their invaluable crit, without which this would have looked very different.

Writing this thing took a few different approaches entirely, and was compounded by my IRL schedule suddenly being hit by a freight train. Still, I enjoyed writing the different narrative segments of this work a lot, and I hope you enjoy the conclusion of our story about the Hanged King.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

On a different note, this is the 50th work that I've posted to this site. When I first joined here (almost) seven years ago, I was so anxious about getting a single entry up that I lost sleep about it the first time I posted something. Getting to 50 was unimaginable at the time, but I can genuinely say that I had a blast writing all of them. Thank you to everyone here who has made this place so special for me. Here's to (maybe) 50 more.

Given this as the last entry in The World Has Progressed Past The Need For Other Games In Town's entries, I was expecting this to be a big climax to the whole thing. This having much smaller stakes, focusing on the two characters made it all much better. In contrast to minmin and ARD, the last collapsible actually worked really well for me: I was expecting to open the collapsible and get more content. Perhaps something about how Zhou wasn't successful in the end, or some form of extended detail about the Hanged King. Instead, I got a short perfunctory note that accentuated the bittersweet ending. I'm not sure how well that reading will hold up outside of the context of the contest or when reading in the order of the canon, but it was personally effective for me.
