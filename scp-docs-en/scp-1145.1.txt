I just don't like this one. In essence, it's the old man, but radioactive and teddybear.

I used the Sv measurement as I figured that would be the easiest for non-nuclear folks to understand. If the consensus believes I should be using a different unit of measurement, I'll consider changing it.

It makes no fucking sense.

A Sievert is a measure of energy received from radiation per kilogram of living tissue, scaled by type of radiation.

This thing is emitting radiation.

You are either a) abusing the unit to mean energy emitted per unit mass, which is not what Sievert means, and as such won't mean anything for non-nuclear folks either.

b) using Sievert correctly, which leads to absurd conclusions like that when someone will enter the enclosure, they will receive a dose of radiation dependant on their mass and independant eg of the spatial angle they intersect, or distance from the radiating bear. Hell you can have two people with equal height but different height receive differing doses at the same time! Bwee!

tl;dr : Epic fail explanation.
