11 Dec 2017 17:46

I'll add a line or two in the intelligence memo to suggest a broader history of conflict/escalation since that's clearly been a point of concern with multiple people.

As to the point about O5s having absolute power over the Foundation, I think that would be extremely infeasible in any iteration of the organization that wasn't extremely small. Even if they intend to execute unilateral authority, they need to delegate to accomplish complex tasks (especially when they involve incredibly specific fields of research taking place in locations across the world.) That means the creation of power centers that may prioritize their interests differently and may be willing to make life difficult for their bosses. This is particularly difficult in membership-voluntary organizations, as preserving employee morale and satisfaction is necessary to maintain a continued workforce, especially one consisting of very smart people who could probably find jobs elsewhere (protecting reality is important, but at the end of the day people are still going to be people.)

All of that is to say that O5s probably love to think that they can order everyone else around, but I don't think the Foundation could function without other powerful bodies forming and eventually coming into some mode of conflict with them.

This is pretty much how I imagine the O5's relationship with the rest of the Foundation, based on my understanding of how complex organizations work - especially ones where day-to-day business involves making decisions that affect people's lives.

There's an official hierarchy to every organization, but as things get larger they have to decentralize, so enclaves form, and then power and influence get redistributed little by little. Eventually the authority of the leaders effectively becomes limited to courses of action they can get a majority of their subordinates to approve of.

That's usually about the time everything falls apart.
