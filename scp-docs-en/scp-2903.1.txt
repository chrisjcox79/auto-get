Vacuum Lethality

05 Dec 2018 01:45

The 2e effect stands out to me

A vacuum temporarily  forms around the consumer, resulting in near-immediate  death.
附加记录：    

The wording is fairly vague compared to the previous descriptions. How  is this vacuum? Is it just around the subject's head or does it surround their body? What shape is it? Does the vacuum's formation remove the air in the subject's lungs? (this last one is important)

Brief exposure to hard vacuum is not lethal. The primary hazard of sudden decompression is air in the subject's lungs causing ruptures as it expands, which can be avoided by exhaling. After that, it's estimated that a human can survive over a minute in hard vacuum. If the vacuum persists that long, cause of death would be asphyxiation.
