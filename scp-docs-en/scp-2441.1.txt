The sudden, perfect understanding of how to manipulate reality was where you lost me. That is too convenient to be readily believable, and there isn't a reason for it to arise in the context of the original anomaly (why don't we see how this has affected the wife? Is she in fact dreading her husband's return, and thereby keeping him away, which would be a nicely ironic ending?). And the newly omnipotent D-Class doesn't indicate that he is using his power to avoid an impending undesirable experience, so the point is kinda lost.

I would have preferred an exploration of the original effect - how has it made the family operate for three months, always thinking it's almost 8pm?

One last nitpick - I'm not convinced that  is genuine German colloquialism.
