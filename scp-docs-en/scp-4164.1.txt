I was slightly disappointed with the ending, but only because when it started directly referencing the Foundation, I thought it was going in a much different direction. And that (imagined) plot thread never paid off.

My thought was:

What if there's no actual doomsday cult out there trying to immanentize ?

What if there's only one manual, and the Foundation already has it?

What if the text on the document is a total red herring, and the reason the kill count keeps going up so rapidly is because every guard killed in a containment breach, every terminated D-class, and every experimental casualty happening within a certain radius of the manual counts towards the total?

OP is welcome to use or modify this idea if they see fit. ^^

03 Jun 2019 19:13

You were actually spot on with most of these! This SCP is the second entry in my Pennsylvania Dark series, about a secretive cult in rural Pennsylvania called the Nachtkarich  but they're not a doomsday cult. They have access to some rudimentary occult techniques, mostly around long-distance telepathy and information transfer, but their primary weapons are lots and lots of guns and home-made pipe bombs. They're fanatics, ready to fight to the deaths of every man, woman, and child in their village against the evils of the Foundation/United States government/any authority figure whom their leader tells them to hate.

They're aware of the Foundation, and allowed the Instruction Manual (of which this is, indeed, the only copy) to fall into the Foundation's hands. They're using it as a kind of psychological warfare, sending the Foundation on a wild goose chase after fictional copies of an instruction manual for a fictional dark god.

The kill count is made up, as well, and is an attempt to make the Foundation fearful and uncertain. The only thing that was real is that they hacked a Foundation computer terminal, and killed a Foundation employee in a drive-by shooting.

I'm curious to know what plot thread you were imagining?
