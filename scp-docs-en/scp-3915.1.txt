I couldn't get invested in this, primarily because I felt the whole things was rather shallow, We're only offered the vaguest glimpse of this person and what happened between them and their partner, and his motivations don't come off as poignant as they could. It feels far too direct, to the point of being almost cloying in the first and third collapsibles.

I think diving deeper into this, giving us more of their story, show how he grows and adapts over time, show his attitude and feelings (instead of blatantly  throughout the logs) could give this more weight than it currently has.

As it stands, I can't feel anything for the character. I could see myself upvoting a strong character piece in this vein, but this is't it.

This is the second article I’ve read today where as a reader I’m expected to feel something for a character that is only barely presented through the story.

“This exists and it’s sad/heartwarming/tragic/etc. because it exists” has never been effective for me. The most insight we get into the protagonist is through a brief, surly interview, which makes me completely numb to his romantic plight, which I’m led to believe is the whole reason behind this.

I feel like if you’re going to expect the reader to identify and feel something, you’ve got to do more to forge that connection than what’s presented here. Notes at the end aren’t cutting it.

08 Dec 2017 08:43

+nitpicks

-nitpicks

SCP-3915 was virtually identical to an ordinary human male except for its incorporeal nature
附加记录：    

If it's still only 'virtually' identical without its anomalous properties, surely that'd mean it had something else abnormal about it. Why not just say 'SCP-3915 was identical [] except for its incorporeal nature'?

.
附加记录：    

I do not like this. This, combined with the  later on, just feels like a lazy excuse to avoid having to convey certain parts of the story. That may just be me, but it's quite a large downside to an otherwise adequate article. Sorry :/

a written document that read 'gone to do what should have been done years ago'
附加记录：    

Spooky ghost leaves cryptic clues that are still blindingly obvious, making me wonder what the point of making it an un-capitalised sentence fragment even was.

okay. i know you're not coast guard. it's fine.
附加记录：    

Again with this. Why does this person go into the trouble of dotting capital Is incorrectly, but still uses apostrophes in the correct places. It seems like you're making it more 'realistic' by making this person seem stupid (in a weirdly specific way).

Overall I agree with Kalinin above: there's nothing that makes me feel for the character, and the way the letters were written distracted me from their contents. The idea doesn't seem distinct from any other incorporeal humanoids we have on the site, and I never found myself 'involved' with the narrative. The core ideas are good, but I think you could come up with a much deeper backstory than  and a much better anomaly than 'ghost (actually not a ghost)'.
