The core idea here is interesting but isn't really explored beyond the very base concept - ultimately it's a series of chess moves that make you swap bodies and nothing else. The biggest problem here though is the repetition of information. Almost every piece of info given to the reader is repeated at least once, and in most cases multiple times.

Feedback

–block

For example; the bullet-pointed list in the description just repeats the same information from the previous paragraph about how the anomaly works, just longer. The concept wasn't so complicated that it was unclear from the initial description, rendering the rest redundant. Your recovery log then repeats the information again - they make the moves, swap minds and their rankings change. Nothing else of interest is added. Your interviews basically duplicate the same interview twice, which itself duplicates already available info (people swap bodies), and then your testing logs do it again - the only piece of new info (which was already alluded to before in the containment procedures) is that it can affect more than two people at once.

The very first test (assuming they don't know anything about this process at this point) immediately getting to the same point and getting a result strains credulity a bit, though that could be how you worded it. Simply saying that the D-Class repeated the moves made by the players in the original game would have made that a bit neater. And, as mentioned, the cross-testing note at the end just feels tacked on.

There are a lot of places you could take a concept like this to expand on it and make it work; the most obvious one off the top of my head is that all famous/talented chess players have actually been affected by this, and aren't who they appear to be, or have had their talents artificially inflated by the process, but that's just the obvious angle. Right now though, there's just not a lot to hook someone in.

Specific issues:

If an instance of SCP-3751 manifests at any event Mobile Task Force Theta-3  is to be deployed and secure all instances present.
附加记录：    

SCP-3751 is the move set here; nothing actually manifests and affected people aren't instances of SCP-3751. Your wording here is a bit odd.

For example, if player A and player B are the only two players who have made in a game of chess that reaches SCP-3751, the following will happen:
附加记录：    

There's a word missing here somewhere, I suspect

I really enjoyed this. It’s an interesting concept and I personally really enjoy the narrative of switching bodies suddenly. The effect of having bystanders not notice as well aids to the storytelling. I really loved how fleshed out the discovery logs were. I was a kid in a candy store when I found the interview logs. As for the last Addendum, it doesn’t add much to the article, but adding a little more meat could make it interesting to people who don’t know what those skips are. For example, adding something like “Research personnel theorize that a crosstest with SCP- would result in [vague description of results].” Something like that could entice readers to click on the links and learn about them, which would interest them about crosstesting as much as it interests you, which would add to the article.

Overall, upvote from me, since it’s really got enough to stand on its own for me. I enjoyed it.
