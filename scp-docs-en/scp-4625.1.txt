Site Crit Team

27 Mar 2020 13:08

Hello author, I have some words for your words

Someline-by-line

Okayhere

and a Hume level detector
附加记录：    

A bit nit-picky but this is redundant if you're using SRA's. Or, it could be a default part of the package of a humanoid containment cell. Either way I feel this is superfluous.

French Civil Registration indicate that the subject is at least 95 years old.
附加记录：    

This is not good technical language. A government isn't going to say this and a technical document isn't going to do this.  how bout? Or whenever you want to set this.

counselling with Commander Idani Daniels on a weekly basis.
附加记录：    

Unless there is a super good reason to name an explicit person, it's always better to be vague.  is perfectly fine. Furthermore what the heck is a Commander doing giving counseling? This is weird.

low level
附加记录：    

Unless you mean that there are a lot of reality benders stacked above it physically, this would be 

Type Green, Colloquially referred to as 'reality benders'
附加记录：    

Why are you adding a colloquial description to a technical term?

Testing has determined that the subject's abilties have a radius of 40m from the subject.
附加记录：    

Subject/subject word repetition. You're also missing an 'i' in abilities.

who was startled by the group and promptly used its abilities to render itself invisible
附加记录：    

'Promptly' has no place here, and you dont have to say it used its abilities. It's evident by what happened. And the footnote explaining AGAIN that it used its abilities to bend light is redundant and poor form.

encountered forest wardens whom were
附加记录：    

'Who' were is fine. This is very unnatural.

MTF Omega-12 (Achilles' Heels) were deployed
附加记录：    

'Was' deployed. The MTF is a single unit.

Upon arrival at the cabin, they located SCP-4625 within the cabin.
附加记录：    

Cabin/cabin repetition.

SCP-4625 did not resist containment, and was brought to Site-37.
附加记录：    

Containment doesn't happen in the field, so you need a different word. He was apprehended. Furthermore, get that comma out of there.

I'm going to stop my crit there because we didn't even make it to your addenda and this is a SPG trainwreck. I cannot support a piece with so little polish which is too bad because it looks like you put a lot of work into the rest of this narrative.

28 Mar 2020 11:11

I'm not a big fan of this. It feels like the article can still use some polish, as in sentences like

SCP-4625 is a male humanoid of French descent. Although the subject appears to be 25 years of age physically and biologically, the subject has said that it was born in 1915, making the subject at least 105 years old.
附加记录：    

Which come pretty early into the skip and thus affect the overall impression going forward.

The dialogue doesn't have that many SPaG problems, but it's not perfect nonetheless. It does feel like it's dragging in many places, and many things stated by the characters could use some subtlety. I don't really need the piece to tell me what the implication and/or overall idea is, I need it to convey it in such a way so I'd be able to come into that conclusion myself.

Here, the most interesting interview (the one about him trying to rescue people from the nazis) takes less space than the one which discusses mental trauma. The subject matter isn't a problem here, but the interview handles it in a way that seems very forced and expository. I do feel like many sections could've been significantly shorter, and the whole trauma discussion wasn't that captivating to keep me engaged for so long.

Admittedly, I didn't turn on the music before reading the skip (I prefer to read in silence because things like music distract me a lot). I briefly listened to it after, and while I like it, I don't think it's an upside for the SCP itself. As a matter of fact, if an article has to rely on certain music to create an atmosphere, then it fails its purpose of creating it on its own.
