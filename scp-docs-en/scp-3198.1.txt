16 Feb 2018 08:12

Not to mention that written materials should be pretty easy to read. A Jenga block blown up to 64x its normal size would have letters on it approximately 1m high. I can read letters that are 1m high. Why can't the task force read the equivalent size letters on the blocks?

One of the other things that bugs me: I can't imagine that the MTF is so slow on the uptake that they wouldn't have realised what was going on if they knew that 682 was being cross-tested with 1056. But if they didn't know, that reflects awfully on Foundation Command that is fully aware of 682's adaptability, but didn't think to warn the containment team of what it might be adapting to?

Speaking of which, 682 typically adapts to and regenerates from environmental stresses - it doesn't tend to take on their properties. As such, I struggled with the premise that 682 was able to replicate the effect of 1056, but more effectively and on a higher scale.
