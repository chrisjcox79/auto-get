Remember Rudy Benson

29 Nov 2019 20:22

The inspiration for this article, is, in the article. The second image inspired the rest - the story of Rudy Benson (which was told to me by PeppersGhost) stuck with me for a while and bounced in my head. At first, it was the Chicago Spirit's version of a memetic kill-hazard on their 001 proposal (which I abandoned writing). Then, it became a simple warning. Finally, it was this. The second image is the source of all this, and really boils down to the following: What if  was an imperative and subject, rather than imperative and object?

This SCP has been a long time coming. The first version of the ending was a very dry and clinical Foundation experiment in which they brought Rudy back to sapience, and it was then a series of logs spotting Rudy over the years. Only this past week did I have the idea of bringing Parawatch into this, which practically wrote itself. The Exploration log followed, since Rudy needed to make another appearance in his article.

A massive thanks to PeppersGhost for talking to me about Rudy Benson initially. The information that Chappell gives in his log is inspired by what Peppers told me about Rudy Benson, and that story was originally conceived of by him. Everything after Rudy being exsanguinated (and his subsequent turn to swamp thing) is me going off that. Peppers also looked this over at various points, so many thanks for that crit.

Additional thanks to Rounderhouse (who crit this twice), Gee and rattles, who also looked at this.
Attributions:

Bubbly.jpg is from here and was released into the public domain by the US Geological Survey, a US Government Branch.
benson.jpg is initially from PeppersGhost (who added the typewriter text).
In the interest of clarity, the quote from The Jungle by Upton Sinclair is, indeed, from The Jungle by Upton Sinclair, which is in the public domain, as it was published in 1905.

Site Criticism Team - Staff Review

29 Nov 2019 23:13

Overall, I found myself enjoying this piece. Your creature design is incredibly gross, and I think is the strongest aspect of the piece. A self-hating entity made of blood and trimmings is supremely gross and holy shit I wish I had come up with it myself. Additionally, adding the irony that Chappell's murder of Rudy turned his existence into sheer torture every time his name was used to incite fear was a really interesting choice that I enjoyed from a literary perspective.

That being said, I do have to agree with ARD that the Parawatch post overstepped a touch. Ultimately, the author knew way too much about things they shouldn't have, and it conflicted with my current understanding of Parawatch as a GoI. I think this is easily remedied by changing just a few details. If they didn't reference the Spirit and Chappell, that'd be an excellent start. Additionally, simplifying the creature design to one that aligns closer to a more cliché horror monster would up the amateur message board feeling of the piece and better justify the Foundation not taking down such a targeted post.

Despite my issues with the Parawatch section, I still enjoyed this enough to upvote.

SoullessSingularity, for any questions or concerns relating to this review.
