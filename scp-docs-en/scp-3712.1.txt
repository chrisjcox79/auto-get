I enjoyed this, it's fun and creepy in that way that is unique to animated puppets, and the escalating weirdness and then impossibility of its advice is a good hook. I'm only sad that we didn't get to see more of it - I usually avoid giving the  advice, but this is one case where I think a table of its advice would have been cool.

My main issue with the article is that I don't care for the frequent use of examples to make your points. It feels a little jarring and generally shouldn't be necessary to communicate the info you're trying to get across - the stuff being presented isn't complex, and was easily understandable before the example. My personal feeling is that if these example sections were removed and replaced with a log of some examples instead, this article would be much stronger.

Despite that though, I did enjoy this, so +1 from me.

Minorissue

Two, English should not be assumed as the default (despite this being an English language site, the Foundation is global.) You could better describe the phenomena as something like  if that's the case, it's unclear.
