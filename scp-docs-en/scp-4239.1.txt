-Star.Ships: A Prehistory Of The Spirits, Gordon White

This was a long piece to finish. I think I had the idea basically in 2016? The earliest concept of this didn't involve Gamers Against Weed at all and would not have been remotely this long, or involving the same anomaly (in all but one aspect). Some things changed steadily over time as I'd set aside the concept, do a little work, and rethink how I wanted to approach this. After one particularly heavy reformatting following submitting a 'just finished enough to be at least looked at' draft to the drafts forum for critique I found myself looking at something that now had the basis for being something I really liked, but that would need far more to be completed. I chose at that time to leave writing it alone for a while so as not to burn out. That continued for a significant period of time (which was all very fine and lovely time; I was entirely pleased to leave an unfinished draft in my sandbox and do unrelated activities for a period of several months) until I, inspired by some of the things DolphinSlugchugger's excellent and significant work I feel we were together able to write an article we can both be proud of.

I have a lot of people to thank, and this post is already of significant length, so that's all going under a collapsible.

Thanksdedications.

Thanksdedications.

Thank's 

Firstly, I want to thank DolphinSlugchugger for being at the same time my coauthor and muse to continue writing this until the end. Every piece of this it wrote impressed me and made me want to write another. It really worked for me, as a system.

Secondly, I want to thank our sister article, Ms. Zapatista, but also through encouraging me via both cheerleading and cyberbullying (by making oblique references to my unfinished draft in published pieces on the site) to continue on with this article through, like, all stages of that process. They were the first one I talked to about the concept and the first person who got excited about it and told me I had to make it. They gave me any piece of advice I asked for when I wasn't sure how to carry on with it. I like to think of them as this article's guardian angel.

Thirdly, I want to thank user Decibelles for unflagging support of the article, crucial critique, and allowing me to make constant 

Fourthly, I want to thank Communism will win for taking it in good spirit when I said to them 

To me, this article never would have happened in any form without those four.

I also want to thank users DolphinSlugchugger came in. I didn't end up using all of your advice, or maybe even most of it, but a great deal of it was extremely helpful for me in focusing the article and re-evaluating what I wanted it to convey as a message. Thank you all.

Thank you also to Uncle Nicolini for some really useful last-minute criticism, like, last night that I think really helped us get this where it needed to be.

Dedication's 

First, the core of this article, the parts of it I wrote earliest, are dedicated to somebody I used to be really infatuated with and who I found to be very sweet to me during that period of time and although we did eventually decide that circumstances were such that not making anything of that infatuation was best, that infatuation did shape and inspire a lot of the earliest parts. I'm reasonably sure that Mr. Destiny has a dog head because I was thinking of them while writing. The island is in Lake Michigan because I asked them to choose which lake it should be (there were contextual reasons why this made sense for them to decide). The island is near Kenosha and called Kenowhere because they thought it should be because according to them 'people call it Kenowhere because there is nothing to do' and I thought that was great and I still think it's great.

Everything I achieved in my later edits before dolphinslugchugger came in are dedicated to Lady Gaga because I made a lot of those edits while listening to Lady Gaga and crying, frequently while high (I wrote no other major sections of this article while high). I wasn't crying because I was sad or anything I was just crying because I love Lady Gaga and I think she's an excellent artist and performer and I had recently seen her documentary which I also cried during several times and I also have a crush on Lady Gaga a little bit.

All the parts written after dolphinslugchugger came in as co-author that could be attributed to me and therefore I am allowed to decide who they're dedicated to are dedicated to Peter Murphy, who I only had a crush on when I was some years younger and I had recently been introduced to the man through Bauhaus (as most are) by my friend who was trying to get me into classic post punk/goth music and got me to make a vampirefreaks.com account. I got really excited about Peter Murphy and stopped listening to what this friend was trying to tell me about goth music when I found out after he left Bauhaus, Peter Murphy's solo career was almost entirely songs about the search for spiritual truth in weird pretentious ways and I immediately had a crush on him from then until I was maybe 20 because that's never not been or going to be my shit. Listening him sing about doubt on the path to enlightenment was essential to (my part of) turning this into a finished piece.

Finally, as a whole, this article is dedicated to, well, that friend I once had who got me to make a vampirefreaks.com account, and to every other friend I've ever had online who not only made the internet a fun and safe place for me, but who inspired me to get out and do real things in the real world from time to time.
