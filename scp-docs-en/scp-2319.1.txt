Commentary

10 May 2015 07:41

This one has a central basis and a few side-inspirations:

- Obviously, the Titanic was the central inspiration. Funny thing is, I've been mulling over a vaguely Titanic-themed idea for a while now, but as a story. It was supposed to be this story about some random guy tasked with delivering a strange object to some strange Foundation, and how excited he was that they were paying for the trip, only revealing at the end that the ship is the Titanic and the Strange Object is an SCP that attracts ice to it. Hell, if this SCP tanks, I might just still write it.

- Another inspiration was a book I read long ago, as a kid. It involved characters crossing dimensions/locations through a process that one of the users compares to being a 2D cartoon character getting past a solid line by lifting themselves off the page, hopping over the line in 3D, and then falling back into 2D mode. The bit I most remember is that the above analogy wasn't perfect, and one (or more) characters had everything on them flipped/mirrored at times. That, most of all, tied into this final SCP, with a lot of mirroring and flipping to give a sense of something weird happened here.

- This one is the least important, but it still sticks out the most in my writing this SCP. There was an in-universe special feature related to the first Blair Witch film in which one of the Talking Heads discusses at length how impossible it was to have found the Supposedly Lost Footage that made up the movie where it was. X Y and Z make it impossible, but that's where it was found. I really wanted to do that with both the SCP itself (it wasn't there, but now it is, and it's always been there given the wear-and-tear) and the final safe (it's there, it's been there for over 100 years, its covered in 100 years of disrepair, but it's a cira-2015 safe). I'll be honest, this part of it was most exciting for me as a SCP writer, because I tend to write from a Everything-Plotted-Out perspective and I remove things to give the reader stuff to ponder over. This time, I intentionally wrote things without an explanation in my head.

What Titanic reference are you seeing? The point is that it's the Titanic. Sorta.

As for it , I encourage you to think about it a little bit more. Mental excercise: take a piece of blank paper and draw the Titanic wreckage from either memory or from online. You've got the front half of the boat at the top, the busted-to-all-hell backside at the bottom, and in the middle-right of the page you've got the major debris field.

Now turn it over and look at what you've drawn from underneath, through a bright light. That's SCP-2319. Whatever force at play either 'saw' the ship like this from 'underneath the paper', so to speak, or was trying to do something that went wrong and left it like this. Even the location of where 2319 lays speaks greatly to what-the-fuck-happened-here tone I was trying to convey.

This goes beyond the location and existence of the wreckage. Like a piece of metal becoming mildly magnet after being exposed to a big magnetic source, everything involving 2319 has this residue of whatever happened to it. It's fucked with the sealife in the area. Sometimes things only catch a whiff of it (and their controls go all screwy and inverted), sometimes people seeped in it go a little weird for a while and need to step back.

The central theme of 2319 for me is that it's a series of what-the-hell-this-is-impossibles. There was never a Titanic south of Australia, but here's one. It's flipped left-to-write, down to the lettering on everything, which would never be done, but it's done here. And whatever happened, every single bit of it has an echo/residue of whatever the hell caused it in the first place.

Some comments:

+block

At this time, the Foundation does not agree on the long-term survivability of SCP-2319, owing to the environment being hostile to the materials composing SCP-2319.
附加记录：    

Awkward sentence. Maybe change it to something along the lines of .

Historical maps show no hints of SCP-2319 dating back decades, but the current surface terrain is as identical to the ones found at the Titanic wreckage.
附加记录：    

It makes sense that /maps/ wouldn't show a shipwreck, if a shipwreck has just been discovered, historical maps wouldn't have anything on it. However, you could achieve the same effect by saying that there is no record of such a ship ever sailing, as that is something that would be recorded historically.

Also,  is very grammatically incorrect, and also wrong in the context of the skip. It's not identical, it's mirrored.

SCP-2319 is mirrored along the X axis
附加记录：    

As far as I know, there is no commonly accepted definition of the  used geographically. If I'm right, that means that this statement means pretty much nothing.

remaining paintworks on the hull hint
附加记录：    

Hint isn't very clinical, and you use it three times. I would go with .

As with the Titanic wreckage, a unique ecosystem of anemones, crabs, shrimp, starfish, and rattail thrive within the wreckage of SCP-2319
附加记录：    

Are they identical or mirrored from the ecosystem at the original record?

upwards translating to downward motion
附加记录：    

I get the port/starboard thing, because it's flipped horizontally, but if directions are flipping vertically, why didn't Titanic show up upside-down?

air-tight safe, it contained
附加记录：    

That should be  or replace the comma with a semicolon.

Okay, so, final comments. First of all, what about the corpses? Are they identical to corpses found in the real record, DNA wise? Or can they not be analyzed?

Also, on the subject of importing animals from the mirrorverse, there's actually an interesting chemical principle that changes things when you mirror them. Certain molecules exist in a way that they are not symmetrical, this is called  how does that work in the mirror-verse? Is biology slightly different to work with the different optical isomers? Or are the functions of isomers changed? Like, if isomer A is harmless and isomer B of the same thing is poison, you could say that the corpses contained high levels of the deadly isomer or something, because in their universe that's the safe one. Not really a major thing, just something relevant you might want to be aware of.

All in all, I'm a bit iffy on this concept. It seems rather simple, and I'm not entirely sure I get what implications are coming from the last note. My thought is that some extradimensional entity tried to save the mirrorverse Titanic, but ended up just moving it to our universe and screwing up the fabric of both universes. Anyways, that's just my two cents. I'll novote for now, but I'm confident that with some tweaking you could change that.

Honestly, that was the singular problem I had when describing the effect.

The only way I can explain it, as I did above, is that it's like looking at something you've written on a piece of paper, from the back side. Everything is flipped.

Like the name of the ship. Sure, it says TITANIC on it, but it's written as if someone wrote it through the inside of the ship. Every piece of paper recovered from the ship is illegible unless you hold it up against a light and read it from the back. Even the goddamned ecosystem that lives in the wreckage is flipped around. It's not just that we've discovered another Titanic, but where we found it and what it looks like.
