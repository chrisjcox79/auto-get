Fold

14 Oct 2020 21:09

out of deference to the mods.

+block

–block

I don't really know how to express my distaste for this article. I think the first things that strikes me is the writing and tone. At best it's weird, things like

SCP-5025-1's cadaver is stored in the Cryogenic Biovault at Site-17.
附加记录：    

does the pet peeve of adding possessive apostrophes to SCP designations and implies that 5025-1 is a concept that has a cadaver. Is 5025-1 now not the body?

In the late ████'s, Bowe presided over the MTF Omega-7 ( in ████, Bowe somehow attained an anomalous immunity to death. He became the primary figure behind the Foundation Elimination Coalition in late 2020.
附加记录：    

Why is the year being blackboxed? What purpose does this serve, especially given the latter half of the article is a 'mea culpa' over the whole hagiography of Bowe. The tone here is also wack. He  attained an anomalous immunity to death. What does that even mean? Death is a concept, not a disease. This is not a description, it's editorialising.

SCP-5025-2 is an anomalous mechanical device created by SCP-5025-1's scientific personnel. Details of SCP-5025-2's internal functions are unclear. However, it is known that SCP-5025-2 is capable of killing a wide variety of living entities.
附加记录：    

5025-1 has scientific personnel? How are the internal functions unclear? It was dissassembled. Did people even try? What does  even mean?

And oh man, Cimmerian boxes. We'll get to this red herring of a Macguffin in a bit, but 'shooting extremely powerful beams of energy' is not something I want to see delivered straight.  is a relative term.

SCP-5025-1 resurfaced at some point in late 2020. He contacted the Chaos Insurgency and used its influence as a catalyst to form the Foundation Elimination Coalition.
附加记录：    

I don't know what bothers me about this passage, other than it's so vague and undescriptive. Surely there was a point of first contact? Why does this feel like a plot summary on wikipedia?

In response to this, SCP-5025-1 began constructing SCP-5025-2.
附加记录：    

I feel like this is supposed to be an 'oh shit' moment, but again, I have no idea what -2 is, and it's named a , which sounds like something I'd get out of a pachinko machine.

Where is the bridge between the last sentence and the addendum? It just happens, and I don't know why.

SCP-5025-1: Yes, just a minor annoyance. Assassins, and all. I think I'm going to try the plan we originally discussed.
附加记录：    

This is not how anyone has ever talked. And since there's no interstitials to describe Bowe's reaction, my mental image is .

This speaks to a larger point that the dialogue is stilted and awkward. I'm never really getting the sense that there's any urgency, or than anyone is really speaking to anyone else. People are just saying stuff, sort of in a vacuum, and the next response happens to be related.

Dr. Moose: That segues nicely into my idea. Dr. Dan, could you please throw a cream pie in my face?
附加记录：    

I I don't know what the tone is supposed to be. This is slapstick comedy, and it just clashes so much with what's probably supposed to be a tense moment and the ostensible climax of the entire series? Why? Who asked for this?

The SCP-050 idea is clever though. And I like Dr. Dan.

Similarly in Addendum 4 there's no sense of urgency. I keep expecting to hear a laugh track, or the characters to turn to each other and shrug genially.

Dr. Crow: Not that. He and his team of scientists are working on some kind of god-killer gun. From what I've overheard, it's supposed to be able to kill anything. Just shoots a beam and, whoops, you're eliminated from the universe.
附加记录：    

You know what else is dangerous? A gun. Why is Bowe building -2? Why should I care? Why does it pose a danger? The article assumes that I know why it's dangerous, but it's described as a scifi laser gun that we've never seen work. Why is it a threat? Why is it more dangerous than taking site 19? And why does Crow seem like he's just been vibing with snacks?

Someone offhandedly says , but then when asked to explain, shrugs it off. And why is Bowe even doing this? What is his motivation anymore?

附加记录：    

This is the most anticlimactic action and pervades through the article. The entirety of the tension and violence and danger I'm supposed to be feeling is summed up in a throwaway sentence.

SCP-2273: Bowe's bluffing. This all sounds ridiculous. He's going to kill the gods? And that somehow makes everything anomalous stop being anomalous? It's absurd!
附加记录：    

This is lampshading. It is absurd. But calling attention to it doesn't make it less absurd.

SCP-5025-1: Oh, hell, what am I saying? I'm not a god. I'm barely even a man at this point. I'm a ghost! I'm a god damned ghost!
附加记录：    

Apparently Bowe's gone crazy between the start of the article and this point. When did this happen? Why? Who knows. He's just a goof now.

SCP-5025-1: They're going to kill me. There's a special level of Hell for people like me, I think. My gut's telling me there's only one way out of there. I'm not giving you the catharsis of finding out.
附加记录：    

What is his motivation??? Why did all this character development happen off screen? What is happening???

And finally we get to the 'twist', which does not work on an impressive amount of levels.

So. Bowe was human the whole time, but turns out that he was just hoarding water bottles, which didn't seem important to anyone until much later, but those bottles also contain 006, which we know because some rando agent taste tested it, even though canonically it is indistinguishable form regular water.

On top of that, what was the point of -2? You could remove it entirely from the article and it would not change anything. So what was the point of this MacGuffin? Nothing! It was a red herring, for reasons?

Then there's some aside about how 'we overplayed his immortality, and now we need to change course' Except the entire article seems to be a monument to mythologising Bowe for unknown reasons.

I felt lost and confused throughout most of this, frustrated by a red herring of a macguffin, wooden dialogue, weird tone, and an entire and aggressive lack of thematic or tonal consistency. I don't know if I should be on the edge of my seat, tense, laughing, or treating this as a sitcom episode.

-1.
