The anomaly itself is honestly too simple to carry the entire article on its shoulders. It could potentially work if there was some interesting story tied into it, but the addendum merely reiterates the object's properties for some time before delving into two final variations. Variations that are admittedly fun but fail to pull my strings of imagination enough to justify a lack of interesting details before them.

I have to agree with the people before me, the concept is simple. I also think that is can be made more interesting by adding an incident report when the Foundation tried to stop a murder from happening, or by adding different consequences to cheating, not playing, different methods of cheating or accusing the wrong suspect in the game. I feel like the effect being an increase in violent crime rate is too intangible.

Otherwise an interesting concept. It might be worth exploring the idea of this being one of multiple anomalous board games (some may already exist but I'm not aware of any)
