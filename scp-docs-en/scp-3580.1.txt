01 Oct 2017 09:52

This has a very compelling setup, but I was waiting for a deeper narrative-some kind of reasoning behind the behavioral changes. Without this, I'm left to make my own with what little we're given. Is it that the jewelry contains some essence of the woman in the photo, which leads to the dissociation? The interview with the afflicted doesn't seem to support that-it points to something more alien, something that views any human form as wrong.

Also concur that the autopsy results, while technically extensive, didn't seem to add much beyond clarifying that the individual wasn't a victim of a homicide. That could have been done in a sentence or two.

Cut out the autopsy and work on the narrative. You don't have to give us everything wrapped up in a bow, but I think this is interesting enough that if you were to re-work it with more focus on the story and less on the effects, it could be quite successful.
