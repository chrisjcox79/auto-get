Dive Depth

15 Jun 2017 03:46

3.5km is a nigh impossible depth for someone using only scuba gear. At depths much lower than that, the pressure can cause the air the person is breathing to become toxic. Nitrogen can induce an intoxicated state at just 30 meters. Oxygen can become acutely toxicity at 60 meters. Even inert helium, used by divers to dilute their air mixtures, can cause convulsions at 150 meters. The deepest anyone has dived using scuba gear is 332 meters.

Re: D-Class Names

01 Sep 2017 21:58

In-universe: pretty much what Zyn said. I like to think that the Foundation has at least decent privacy protocols, so in this case it's extended to D-Class.

Out-of-universe: it's a lot easier to quickly differentiate between D-5███ and D-7███ than it is to differentiate between D-5923 and D-7448. It's a bit of a handwave, sure, but I think it does enough to help the flow of the narrative to justify its inclusion.

Glad you enjoyed!
