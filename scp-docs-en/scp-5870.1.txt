Hello! Veralta's here with a new SCP about koalas, nature, the future of Australia, and intricate worldbuilding based on the beliefs of aboriginal Australian people like the Bundjalung and the Eora. Consider this SCP a free New Year trip through Australia, and a little bit of a crash course on the Australian condition.

Also, it's linked to Ralliston's Obearwatch Command; fun fact, all of these started when he threw the word . I initially planned out a short article with a gimmick and a punchline.

But then, I realized I had to dive deep into the waterhole.

The aboriginal Australian belief system, also called Dreamtime1, is very complex and interesting. It would be a shame if I just went with a silly punchline when aboriginal culture, with its mix of hopes and dreams for Australia, needs a chance to shine here.

Lastly, consider this article as a piece dedicated to Australians and wildlife - such as the koalas themselves - who have been affected by the severe bushfires. As a Filipino youth, I couldn't do much to be honest, aside from raising awareness. I tried to stay true to the myths' essence while adding my style of writing to them, while I researched news about the bushfires.

All in all, though, please enjoy this SCP, and I wish a Happy New Year - and more happy years - to come for all of us!

Special thanks to Dr Balthazaar for providing guidance on Australian beliefs and culture. You guys are amazing!

Footnotes
1. By the way, this word does not cover what the beliefs really mean, and while dreaming is a part of the culture, it is not just that. Additionally, the word  may even be just a mistranslation of words meant for deities.
2. Bear goes rawr :p

Offering my two cents as the author, I believe you unfortunately misread the whole article.

So, the first point. It's not anomalous, you say. For context, this article takes place in 2022, two years after the bushfires have occurred. Safe to say, if thousands of koalas1 still disappear at that point, without any fires, that would be utterly anomalous, right? I mean, it's the symbol of Australia, and someone's bound to notice the fact that they're disappearing.

Plus, I don't think large, flying serpents are a species native to Australia, because if that's the case, then I would be saving up in order to ride on those. Second, koalas are cute, and I won't trade those for snakes.

Second, I'm not sure on your thoughts about the pacing. You say  at the start but then dismiss my article's pacing and narrative as a joke.

Choose one - it's ok if you found the article unsatisfying, but I'm just really confused about what you said.

Additionally, all people I asked did not point out that my article was too sparse and unnaturally progressing, and I'm against the idea that environmental action against climate change is a joke, so I have my doubts on everything you said.

Lastly, the ending (Incident DROPBEAR) was crafted in order to suit the New Year. Look, I don't want to shoehorn drama, politics2 and angst into this whole thing just to reduce the possible moment, because that would detract from the whole koala and aboriginal culture angle.

Plus, I'm simply basing off the fact that Dreamtime is rooted in the concept of an everywhen - the fact that it is timeless, so I actually don't feel good about you saying that this is a joke.
