As much as I enjoy the concept, the clinical tone isn't quite there and there are too many errors to warrant an upvote from me right now.

But for real though, predatory pianos are awesome.

EDIT: I'll clip some bits that stand out the most shortly.

+Open

-Close

Any music played by SCP-4088 is to be recorded and sent to Head Researcher Zarakolv
附加记录：    

Missing a full stop.

SCP-4088 is mobile, moving around
附加记录：    

then will lurch towards the prey and attempt to slam its lid shut on the prey. SCP-4088 will continue to do this until the prey is deceased, then it will drop the prey into its interior where the body will disappear.
附加记录：    

the word 'prey' is used four times with less than ten words between each use, which creates a semantic sensation.

The way that this occurs is currently unknown, as it only appears to occur when no humans are observing the room though any means
附加记录：    

The way that SCP-4088-1 is able to support SCP-4088
附加记录：    

The subject that is standing under SCP-4088 when it is falling will not attempt to retreat, and only looks up at SCP-4088 with a terrified expression. SCP-4088 falling onto the subject almost always ends in death,
附加记录：    

This appears to be the song SCP-4088 uses the most to draw in prey.
附加记录：    

Now that I read this again, there's an awful lot of semantic sensation on top of the tonal issues. It also feels as if the 'amounts played' part of the table doesn't add much as we can infer how often it would be heard to be playing the songs based on its established behaviors as a predator.

I will probably regret coming down so hard here

+block

–block

but this is sub-draft-level writing. And I want to like this because I am a fan of classical music, pianos, and Mr. Joel.

In addition to the errors and issues Uncle Nic pointed out

SCP-4088 is mobile, bending it's legs
附加记录：    

its, not it's

SCP-4088-1's anomalous properties only manifest when left in a closed room with SCP-4088 and not observedThe way that this occurs is currently unknown, as this behavior only manifests when no humans are observing the room though any means.
附加记录：    

This is inexcusably redundant, you say the exact same thing two sentences apart from one another.

Additional instances of repeated words:

SCP-4088 then will wait wait until
附加记录：    

The damage caused to the subject will almost always end in death, with the cause
附加记录：    

to regrow the length of its rope through unknown means, at a rate of 2 meters regrown an hour.
附加记录：    

Both visually and compositionally, the second paragraph needs to be split into two; it is too long as is.

The installment of the victim's sudden inability to run away, left instead in helplessness to only look up with a scared expression introduces a strikingly powerful psychological/neuro/musculoskeletal component to this anomaly's abilities, yet it is never detailed and is only placed compositionally to ensure that people die. It also doesn't make any sense that it isn't used by the anomaly at other times. You've already mentioned that the piano can move at remarkable speed, why can't it simply drop at similar speeds too?

I'm sorry, I either upvote or novote 98% of the time, but I just have to downvote this.

I do love the imagery of a predatorily-toothed piano.
