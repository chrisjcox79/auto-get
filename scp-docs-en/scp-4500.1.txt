Author post

02 Jul 2018 22:59

I got the idea for this a few months ago ().

Dr. Cimmerian (especially), MsMerry, and HumidHermit all helped me out in the forums. Thanks for all the support!

The multiple collapsibles are only possible because of Mortos's Nested Collapsible Demo (http://scpsandbox2.wikidot.com/mortos-collapsible).

+Explanations

-explanations

This entry is based off of Plato's theory of ideals, the idea that every concept that we see in the real world is a dark, shadowy reflection of a truer ideal.

The overall layout is based on a Project Gutenberg version of Plato's Republic.

The threat levels are based on the ages of Greek mythology: Gold, Silver, Bronze, Heroes, and Iron. Gold corresponds to Keter, and Heroes corresponds to Thaumiel in my version.

Pi Alpha Nu (PAN) is of course Pandora's Box. Pi Upsilon Theta (PYTH) is the discovery of irrational numbers, a real-life controversy and cognitohazard. Omega Omega Omega is Typhon, who was the greatest of all threats to the Greek Gods. Pi Lambda Alpha (PLA) is the allegory of the cave from Plato's Republic, and is the way that the whole facility became ideal.

The discovery section is a reference to Russel and Whitehead's work on Principia Mathematica, the famous book on the foundation of mathematics that takes 300 pages to prove 1+1=2. Their work was made completely pointless shortly after by Godel's incompleteness theorem.

Finally, Hercules being on Gold/Keter duty after killing Megara is a reference to Hercules' Twelve Labors.

Being 1/4 Greek IRL:

I got the  1 immediately (bonus points for making it a Píthos-type storage jar like in the original story).
I was unsure about Typhoeús/Typhõn/Typhós/Typháon, because I recognized the physical description but couldn't remember the character's name until I was halfway in.
I can see why irrational numbers would be considered a real-life cognitohazard, seeing as I hurt my head trying to wrap it around the concept when I myself was introduced to it (I was wondering why they would be in an ancient SCP analogue run by the Gods, though)
I recognized Plátōn's Allegory of the Cave, but I was wondering how it tied in (thanks for the clarification). I didn't get the reference to Plátōn's Republic in the article layout, since I had no familiarity with that work outside of the Allegory of the Cave.
