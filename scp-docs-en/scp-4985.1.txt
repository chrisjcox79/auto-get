02 Feb 2019 08:36

Feudalism to kleptocracy is no long walk so there are amusing themes here, but the implementation is problematic. This is a completely uncontained Keter that kills people — at the very least its classification as Euclid is wrong. I get that the point is that it’s amusing for the cliche contest, but it’s a gaping wound in reality that the Foundation would either contain or be forced to neutralize, and that’s a bigger deal than the central gag.

Keep in mind, not everyone is in on the gag. If the feudalists try to employ violence against someone not infected by the cognitohazard and the victim fights back successfully, you’ll have a lot of dead bodies and complete containment breach, followed quickly by more dead bodies. And how have authorities not arrested all concerned yet?

For me, the diplomacy log is far less important than those plot holes, so when I got that instead of an explanation, I became distinctly unhappy with the writing.

I was about to try and defend my object class choice as I have a more lax/flexible definition of the classes than a lot of others, but I realised that even by my standards this leans more towards Keter than Euclid, so I've changed it.

The lack of response from authorities or organised response from others is intended to be covered by the line about secondary anomalous properties which suppress the response of others, as an extension of the compulsion cliche. If you think this is an unsatisfying cop-out that's entirely fair, but I did see that plot hole and try to paper it over.
