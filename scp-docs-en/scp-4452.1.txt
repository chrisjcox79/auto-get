I'm not sure whether I buy the official story that the boy did this to himself - it seem highly unlikely that an 11-year-old would know to do this or would have the resolve to do it. Given that this was an attack on a Foundation official's home while they were absent, I would suggest that the husband was also probably a Foundation official. I think it's at least plausible that husband, as a Foundation official might have known enough about the infohazard to perform the procedure (or instruct the boy to perform the procedure) before succumbing to its effects.

I agree with other commenters that the twist doesn't land as hard as the article seems to expect, but the initial set-up and execution, along with ethics back-and-forth, had already won an upvote from me so it's hardly an issue.
