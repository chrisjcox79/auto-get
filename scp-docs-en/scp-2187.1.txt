23 Nov 2013 00:38

So, I haven't played Bioshock, but even I knew that this is heavily reminiscent of it. That's not the reason I downvoted, but it didn't make me favorably inclined towards the article, either.

Points in favor of this one is that it's an ambitious effort and there's obviously a cohesive story that you want to tell. I respect that and the attempt to build something of complexity.

Weighing that down, however, is how convoluted this article was as I read it. I feel like there wasn't enough in the first part of the article to set me up for what was coming, and as a result the front end feels disjointed and unnecessarily complicated. Later on you drop some hints, but I feel like I've slogged to get there. The supplemental section is a little scattered, though I acknowledge how difficult that style of exposition can be. It just felt a littlerandom, I guess. Also, I find it hard to believe that no one remembered to bring food to the New Utopia Paradise. A little too far from reality and too close to a Bob the Angry Flower comic.

This is a valiant attempt, but ultimately this was just too clunky in the execution for me.

The Foundation is 5% more evil than D-Class. I groaned at that part.
