A 'cliche' is a type of 'trope' that is so repetitive and overused, it's tedious and actually a little painful to read. I saw little to no cliches here, only tropes.

Tropes are necessary in keeping short stories short. We don't need to know where Bagel grew up and how he got into the business, all we need to start is 'gangster' and a tiny detail to make him unique. The author then takes it from there.

An even better author will also drop hints of a greater world with even more adventures. But this right here is the adventure we're reading about today. @Rounderhouse also did this by hinting about the Big Bad Guy's greater plans.

+1 all around.

18 Jul 2020 14:47

This came out a bit long, unfortunately, but the following is my explanation as to why I gave this article a -1.

Show

Hide

I'll start by clarifying that, when I read this page for the first time, I gave it a +1. That's because this is a good page - there's a good plot with a nice twist, all of the characters feel believable, and the setting and backstory contribute as well.

The reason I now changed my vote to a -1 is because, while I feel like this is a great contribution to the site, it's not one I feel should be in the format of an SCP article. SCPs are things the Foundation either contains or has contained, for an indefinite amount of time. This is why the articles have Containment Procedures right at the top - they're supposed to help Foundationers continue the potentially perpetual containment of the anomaly.

This article discusses a group of anomalous humans, which means the Foundation would've certainly had an interest in them, and would indeed have apprehended them as described in the article. The problem here is that, as far as I can tell, all of the events of this article take place within 2 days. The Foundation  here mentions multiple times that the Foundation has no interest in indefinitely arresting these guys, and that they will be released into the regular justice system once the investigation is over.

This doesn't feel like something the Foundation would write an SCP document about. Honestly, it feels like it would've taken the Foundation a longer time to write this article than the amount of time these guys were in containment. In my opinion, this story would better fit into the world as a tale - an in-universe documentation of the investigation, perhaps. Unfortunately, this means that for now, I'm giving it a -1.
