The Foundation hasn't tried to kill it because Secure, Contain, Protect. Not Slaughter, Cut, Perforate. That said

I downvoted for two reasons: one, redactions and blackboxing in the containment. How is someone supposed to know where to contain it if that information has been hidden? Two, it breaks suspension of disbelief in that this creature feeds exclusively on humans. People haven't been around that long. Even less time have people been in places where this creature would feed. It would be a truly breathtaking series of circumstances that caused evolution to select for a humans-only diet when it lives in areas that A) have hot springs and B) have humans near those hot springs. Then there is the numbers thing. In order for it to get enough energy from humans to drive its 300 meters of body, it would need thousands, possibly hundreds of thousands of us each day.

Although, I do like the implication that the people it eats are living it up in a sunny paradise inside it.

+block

[LOCATIONS REDACTED].

Why not , which is one word less? Why not blackboxes, which do not even count as words on wordcounter.net? Minor nitpick, but I'm highlighting that.

Should there be a shortage of D-Class during feeding episodes then civilians are permitted to be used at the discretion of two members of Level 3 personnel.
附加记录：    

While I think you're going for a horror moment, it's not really working for me since there is a certain protocol (depends on headcanon) where civilians can be inducted to D-class status if the need arise (its name escapes me currently). But I feel it will more professional if said protocol is mentioned.

Of course, name-dropping a protocol not everyone knows may not be the best thing. So make of it as you will.

Pros:

This SCP really harkens back to the older stylistics and SCP-2086, which go all out to describe a creature's habits and all that. Nostalgic value, I suppose.

Cons:

The horror moment that one can be fed to a monster is not that well-done. Why must humans be sacrificed? Can the Foundation not force it to adapt to the taste of a cow?

* I do agree with Whatopercy2 on the excessive loss of lives to maintain just one thing, that may not be that all important. It's quite a fallacy since one writes SCPs under the assumption that one has all the resources needed, but a real organisation like the Foundation would be eternally strapped for resources. EDIT: In light of edit, switch to neutrality.

Verdict: Neutral vote
