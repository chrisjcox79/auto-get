This just doesn't really do much for me. A little bit sad, but that's about it. SCPs need to be either creepy, very interesting, or very very witty. This is slightly creepy and slightly interesting but not enough of either to really grab me.

Also, why hasn't it been picked up and thrown into a containment cell somewhere to make sure it can't be moved? Leaving it out in the ocean just seems a little…silly.

Agent reported similar feelings returned, as well as images of whatever." Maybe better worded than that, but you get the idea.

So yeah, all in all a neat idea that just didn't go very far.

I can see why you'd want to make it more emotionally involving, but the first version was better. This isn't really a site where you'd write up a sob story like that. You need to enhance the horror and the creep, not the heart-wrenching.

An idea: the statue is not intelligent enough to deduce that humans cannot survive underwater, and is so desperate for company that it will capture and drown anyone who approaches. To this end, it broadcasts a psychic 'beacon' that draws humans there to investigate, at which point they are caught and drowned.

Second idea: the statue is intelligent, and malevolent. The coral it is composed of can actively colonize others on contact, and convert them to a new instance. With each added instance, the statue can remain active longer when out of contact with the sea floor. Extrapolated, with enough converted humans, the group can remain active for weeks, months, years, and free to roam on dry land. Psychic beacon optional, but would probably be pushing it.
