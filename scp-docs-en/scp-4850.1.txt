My entry to the 2019 Cliche Contest! This is my first solo skip and by far the most ambitious piece of writing I've ever done. I wanted to explore how science is done in the foundation: Endless cross-tests never answer the questions at the core of the anomaly. I wanted to show how Foundation scientists would go about answering these questions, and what happens when there isn't a single clear result. I'm really proud to have created this and I couldn't have done it without the help of these awesome people:

01 Feb 2019 16:42

It's nice to see your writing character independent of co-authors (as nice as SCP-4003 is).

Thoughts

–block

You have a command of appropriate tone that is developed beyond your years, and you swap from a sharp clinical one to a more congenial, human one well and with good effect here. My favorite part of the article is the ability log, which dazzles with delightful physics concepts and is very clever.

I think you've achieved your goal of communicating the raw nitty-gritty of Foundation research; this offers a perspective usually taken for granted, and reminds us that any given science textbook's sentence can require a mountain of hard work (and sometimes suffering).

I was glad to see the text blinking effect justified in-article. But the bottom line about it from me is that it is very distracting. I had to highlight any given entry featuring it as well as any surrounding entry just to keep my eyes focused enough. Maybe that's a me thing. Another formatting nitpick would be that it seems strange that the the last note is center aligned.

The line where the researcher recognizes the wormhole strikes me as odd (per the article, they were up until this point theoretical, no?). Also, I feel as though the threat at the end is somewhat stuck on. I think the article would have been more effective with a bit more of a modest reach, ending at the researcher losing her arm (there's a pun in there somewhere).

Novote leaning towards upvote for now, but I will check back and see if some time or any changes push me in that direction. Good work imo.
