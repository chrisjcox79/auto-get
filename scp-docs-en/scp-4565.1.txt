Junior Staff - Site Criticism

28 Aug 2019 19:59

I really loved this.

+rambles

-rambling

Anomalous history is one of my favourite themes on the site, and this was pulled off really well - you really get the feeling that this is the Foundation working backwards to dig up and analyze events from long ago without actually having access to those first hand sources.

I was already on board from the premise of a historical event being designated as an SCP just from how damn weird it was, and as I read on and you just went into more and more detail (especially with the descriptions of the various anomalous soldiers and phonomena) I just got more and more hooked. Tying in all the weird medieval art as being part of the records of this conflict was a lovely touch, as well. You’ve got me intrigued with the mentions of the grail and the ark, too - excuse me if I’m wrong, but those felt like definite hooks for future stuff, and I’d love to see more in this style.

One little touch I really appreciated was:

In the letters of Joshua the Clean (Footnote: A soldier that fought alongside Raymond the V in 1218 and died in 1220.)
附加记录：    

It’s a really small detail, but just going into that tiny bit of description about the soldier who recorded the knowledge makes it feel like real history beyond just the confines of the article and lends it a lot of verisimilitude.

One thing I’m not 100% sure on is the use of the Sæfernmúða Edlæcan. The intiial mention of it and the actual transcript are pretty far apart, so it’s easy to forget about that specific anomalous device after it’s first mentioned and get confused as to how the Foundation got this transcript. Maybe have another mention of it a little later, just before the transcript, to keep it in the reader’s mind? It’s a pretty tiny nitpick, all things considered, but it’s just something that I thought I should mention.

Still though, definite +1 from me!

While I love the high concept, there are some issues:

Special containment procedures:

 This is simply assembling a library.

+block

–block

 means- if what you mean is that the Foundation has come up with a cover narrative of the Albigensian Crusade and wishes to alter extra-Foundation accounts, then clearly say so.

Seems weird (but not unprecedented) for a historical period to be an SCP subject as opposed to a tangible artifact relating to that period being the SCP.

The old-timey grammar in  is cute but contains numerous distracting errors- here’s a non-exhaustive list of a few that jumped out at me:

 is the third person singular. You use them wrong. (Also, this is a usage from the King James Bible/Shakespeare era, which is an understandable stylistic choice on your part since true period Middle English would be pretty impenetrable except to Chaucer scholars.)
Why is Raymond named a baroness? And if he's a baron rather than an actual king, he wouldn't be addressed as 
.
. Not sure how this is supposed to be parsed.

I think the article could be dramatically improved with some grammatical proofreading of the faux-historical documents. (It’s also not clear in-universe why they’d be written this way- if the Foundation has translated the documents from medieval French, why would the output be in Elizabethan English?)

No issue with the substantive content of the documents, though - you're telling a cool story and I like it. (I especially like your in-universe explanation for why there are so many medieval manuscript doodles of knights battling snails.)
