The first image image is set to be displayed as only 300px wide, but is actually 2300px wide. An image that big takes a while to download, even over a reasonable speed connection. You should use an image editing program to shrink the image to 300px wide.

+comments

-comments

SCP-3000-V refers to an unknown chemical or biological vector responsible for the onset of several cognitohazardous effects in the Area surrounding SCP-3000.
附加记录：    

Cognitohazardous effects are induced by seeing/hearing/etc something, not by chemicals or microbes. Perhaps you mean it caused vulnerability to the cognitohazardous effects, such that if you aren't first exposed to the vector then the perception of [whatever] then you aren't affected?

Olea europaea trees within the active zone appear with the same frequency as on the rest of the property, and may outnumber non-anomalous trees present.
附加记录：    

Olea europaea trees are non-anomalous. Do you mean SCP-3000-1?

Camera view turns and proceeds through several normal olive trees.
附加记录：    

Do you mean , as in the trees acted like they were holograms?

After approx 15 seconds, a pair of trees at the edge of the visible field appear to stop moving as several more trees manifest through them and move past.
附加记录：    

I think that what you're trying to convey here is that, due to the non-Euclidean nature of the space in SCP-3000, some of the trees appear to moving relative to an observer at rest, but the description is ambiguous due to the fact that you're also describing the movement of images across a video screen. That is, the images can be moving across a screen because they're moving relative to the ground, because the camera is moving relative to the ground, because the camera's view is rotating, or some combination of the above. Is a tree moving relative to the ground, with its roots moving through the soil as if the soil was water? If a tree and the soil it's in moving relative to the rest of the ground in the view? Are most of the trees moving across the screen because the camera is changing position, but one of the trees isn't moving across the screen because it's somehow keeping pace with the camera?

Trees that were formerly stationary at the edge of visibility continue moving forward.
附加记录：    

Again, its unclear which trees are doing what.

You've been walking through them for the past twenty minutes
附加记录：    

Past them, or literally through them?

Vector appears to apply not only to live observation, but also to video and audio recordings.
附加记录：    

That sounds like it's antimemetic, not cognitohazardous.

04 Apr 2017 14:15

I'm incorporated some of the feedback you've provided, thank you. Some of it comes down to colloquial differences in language usage, but the changes I've made should clear it up.

Regarding cognitohazards, however, you're mistaken. Memetic hazards are specific to hazards which are perceived via some form of media, or are otherwise infectious from one mind to another. Cognitohazards can have any vector (visual, informational, auditory, tactile, or chemical), so long as the alterations to thought and perception are anomalous.

And with regards to antimemetics, the on-site material is written in such a way that antimemetics are not widely known or discussed throughout the rest of the Foundation, and this document was most recently updated by Dr. Marshall Grant, former HMCL of SCP-400. He'll necessarily see it through that lens.
