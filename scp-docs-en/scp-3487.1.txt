02 Dec 2018 07:36

Author, you have a very solid concept here. I personally enjoy it. However, I think I'm going to leave this at a no-vote for now. There are a few things that you could do to polish up this work.

+block

–block

Though you don't have any run-on sentences, there are a few sentences that are incredibly long. Your last paragraph for example:

These historical changes cause the butterfly effect, and though this effect appears to be somewhat muted (considering the expected results of some of these changes), there remains a significant risk of a CK-class restructuring event caused by SCP-3487, with an immeasurable number assumed to have already taken place before containment to establish present-day consensus reality.
附加记录：    

is all one sentence. It can be a bit of a slog to read, and breaking it up would help your article flow easier.

SCP-3487’s primary anomalous property manifests when it moves, whether by walking or by utilising man-made transport (such as cars and elevators).
附加记录：    

This passage confused me a bit, particularly the latter part. Especially since at the bottom you say

The anomalous effects of SCP-3487 do not manifest if it is not moving of its own volition, and thus it can be transported without issue.
附加记录：    

which is a bit contradictory, from what I can tell.

SCP-3487 can be harmed in a similar manner to other house cats, though it heals at a slightly accelerated rate producing no scar tissue and is able to slowly regrow parts of itself (including vital organs), resulting in it being effectively immortal.
附加记录：    

While this gives me the impression that the Foundation took apart this poor lazy kitty, it is a little loose on its tone. A suggestion I might make:

SCP-3487 is not impervious to physical harm, although it is capable of accelerated regeneration. Therefore, it is theorized that SCP-3487 cannot die.
附加记录：    

Out of curiosity, did you get this looked over in the Drafts and Critique forums? It may take a few days for people to respond, but someone always responds and it is a useful resource for getting work to its best possible state. It may be worth your time to consider this in the event that your article doesn't succeed. Again, I think you have a novel concept here and some of the writing isn't bad, but it is just a little ways out of upvote territory for me.

29 Dec 2018 03:46

I absolutely love this piece! The concept is awesome (and as a cat owner I absolutely relate), and the execution lives up to it!

One thing that might be cool to include is some examples in the movement logs of more significant changes made to human history. The fact that this cat can alter geologic history by throwing a meteor at the Earth millions of years in the past is both hilarious and a great way to demonstrate how powerful this thing is, but it would be cool to see it move itself by impacting significant human events as well- changing the results of elections, that sort of thing. A cat that can move tectonic plates is scary one one level because it's so powerful, but tectonic plates are pretty removed from everyday experience. A cat that changes who won WWII is scary in a very different and also interesting way. Just my thoughts though - don't mean to stick my nose into the creative aspects of someone else's story when it doesn't have any problems in the first place.

Again, super creative and well-done SCP! Definitely a new favorite!
