I love this piece's energy, and I love the execution  but I also feel like it could use a couple more proof-reads.

FEEDBACK

–block

As SCP-5243 is a recurrent catastrophic containment failure, it is  through amelioration of its effects.

Every year, on the eighth day of September, the following actions must be taken at Site-43:
附加记录：    

This is a great opening hook, but I feel like it could be even stronger (and simultaneously more clinical):

The effects of Site-43's recurrent catastrophic containment failure are to be ameliorated at all costs. On September 8th of every calendar year, the following actions must be taken:
附加记录：    

Shorter and punchier. But YMMV here, this isn't a strong issue for me; I think the hook is great even as-is. The bit where you end it with the personnel who must perish is also great.

I do feel like you can make this more WTF. Everything you write in this table involves what people must do. But I'd throw in things which objects must do. .

The  of these facilities allows waste products from supernatural subjects to proceed vertically from their original sources, to study in Applied Occultism, to neutralization in Acroamatic Abatement.
附加记录：    

I love the whole weird industrial vibe you give this, but I'd change 'supernatural' to 'anomalous' .

At 18:21 hours, undetected emissions from an anomalous subject in the Security and Containment Section seeped into Acroamatic Abatement Facility AAF-D via Applied Occultism, triggering a catastrophic overload. Orphic outflow reached critical recondicity, and a materials containment breach occurred.1
附加记录：    

This is some primo technobabble shit, which is what I think gives the article its great energy. The footnote isn't necessary, but I don't think hurts the piece either  but I'd consider cutting the 'Euclidean geometry' (it's become a bit of a cliche) and maybe replace it with 'terrestrial geometry' or 'local space'. Again, YMMV.

A brief torrent of supernatural activity followed, much of it counter-chronological in nature, with effects including but not limited to:
附加记录：    

Again, I'd avoid words like 'supernatural'  you want this to feel like Ghostbusters, I think.

The retroactive and asymmetrical erasure of the above-mentioned entity2
附加记录：    

I *love* this bit, but I also think the footnote goes a step too far. I'd dump everything after 'save some cryptic references'.

He therefore did not receive a telephone call directed to his redline receiver at 18:21 by Janitorial and Maintenance Section technician R. Ambrogi, requesting instructions for the handling of an emergent situation in AAF-D. (Established protocol calls for an immediate activation of the Site-43 Containment Breach Warning System in such a scenario. Technician Ambrogi was either improperly trained, or had forgotten his training.)
附加记录：    

Parentheticals like this should be contained within the punctuation of a sentence, not outside of it. IE:

).
附加记录：    

If you want a whole set of sentences like this as an aside, you should probably just make it a footnote.

but was struck by a burst of orphic outflow and biologically inverted.
附加记录：    

'biologically inverted' is a great phrase.

At 18:22, fourteen processing tanks ruptured in AAF-D. Dr. H. Blank heard the ruptures in his research laboratory, and remarked upon them. Associate researcher Dr. R. Wirth exited the laboratory in search of the origin of the disruption.
附加记录：    

Here's where I'm going to start getting a little critical: I feel like a lot of these sentences are belabored? They feel like they can be cut down, cleaned up, and made punchier. For instance:

At 18:22, fourteen processing tanks exited to investigate.
附加记录：    

Much shorter, much quicker, much punchier. There's a number of points in this article where I feel like you use 10 words when you could use 5. And this is important, 'cuz it's a bit wordy  and you want this to feel as much like an industrial disaster report as possible.

(If you haven't already, I recommend checking out this channel and the way that a lot of these industrial incidents are described  my main gripe is with some of the wording and a number of cases where you use a few too many words IMO)

(Also, the most important words in a sentence ought to either be as close to the beginning or close to the end as possible. Hence swapping 'AFF-D' with 'ruptured'  ruptured is the important bit, which leads to Dr. H. Blank hearing the rupture.)

Wirth was pulled into AAF-D by a translucent orange tentacle and disappeared.
附加记录：    

I'd take out 'disappeared'; it doesn't sound clinical. You want this to sound as much like a tedious business report as possible  make the impossible/supernatural sound ordinary, and it actually heightens the tension and dread.

Wirth was pulled into AAF-D by an orange translucent tentacle; both he and the appendage dematerialized.
附加记录：    

Also, we don't know that Wirth perished here! Which means you might want to modify the containment procedure where you say these personnel must perish  YMMV.

At 18:24 he called out to any personnel still alive within AAF-D, simultaneous to the appearance of technician Markey at the end of the airlock approach hallway.
附加记录：    

At 18:24. This occurred simultaneous to technician Markey's arrival at the hallway approaching the airlock.
附加记录：    

It sounds like Markey magically 'appears' in this hallway, otherwise. Also, 'airlock approach hallway' is a weird way to phrase 'the hallway approaching the airlock'.

For unknown reasons, upon hearing Chief Nascimbeni's voice technician Markey stopped running and turned around.
附加记录：    

ceased running and turned to look at an unidentified subject.
附加记录：    

'technician Markey' also sounds very weird. Maybe 'Chief Technical Officer Markey', or 'Chief Technician Markey'?

A wave of steam passed over him and he collapsed, converting to coloured droplets as he fell. An inexplicable gust of air pulled these droplets deeper into the facility, from which screaming could now be heard.
附加记录：    

A wave of steam passed through him, converting him to coloured droplets. A gust of air then pulled these droplets deeper into the facility.
附加记录：    

Yeah, this is where things start to get increasingly clunky for me. A few random points:

'inexplicable gust of air'? Is anything going on 'explicable', right now? Was the orange tentacle 'explicable'?  etc.
A lot of this is starting to feel like you're trying to write a novel instead of a technical report. Nothing against novels, and I think your descriptions are pretty good! But the aim of an SCP article is to describe the horrifying in technical terms. Remember, I shouldn't feel like you're trying to be scary. I shouldn't feel like you're trying to be cinematic. I should feel like you're just telling me precisely what happened.
The 'from which screaming could now be heard' is very much the move a novel would make a camera? A person?)

In fact, a thought I just had: I think this would feel more clinical and horrifying if you could find a way to present it as more of a 'log of events' rather than this sort of descriptive account. Similar, though not identical, to what you had in the containment procedures. Imagine something like this:

]  Chief N. Nascimbeni is automatically paged by the alarm system. He proceeds to the airlock at AAF-D alongside researcher Wirth.

]  Chief N. Nascimbeni and researcher Wirth arrive at the airlock. A translucent orange tentacle seizes Wirth, retracts into AAF-D, then dematerializes with the researcher.

]  Technician Markey arrives in the hallway that approaches the AAF-D airlock.

]  Chief N. Nascimbeni calls out to any remaining personnel within AAF-D.

]  Technician Markey stops running and turns to face an unidentified subject.

]  A wave of steam passes through technician Markey, converting him into coloured droplets. These droplets are carried deeper into AAF-D.

]  Screams can be heard from several points throughout AAF-D.
附加记录：    

See how this lets me throw the screaming in there as just a random detail, instead of trying to make it feel linked to Markey somehow? This is just an example of how you might do something like this, though  like it's not designed to scare us, but something that's just happening).

(You could also use this approach to show us several events in different areas occurring simultaneously  but because of the nature of the presentation, it doesn't feel like you're trying to horrify us.)

I'm not going to go through this whole thing like this, so I'll just throw out a few more random asides:

Technician Ambrogi's inside-out corpse
附加记录：    

Given the lovely phrase of 'biologically inverted' from earlier, I feel like this is a missed opportunity. 'inside-out corpse' sounds boring:  sounds way creepier (and evocative).

Five hundred and seventy-two life-sized, photorealistic, two-dimensional representations of agent Mukami frozen in a state of extreme distress
附加记录：    

This is fantastic, but I'd cut 'extreme' and 'frozen' (just 'agent Mukami in a state of distress') is more than enough).

An inescapable sense of  in a third-floor washroom facility experienced by all searching personnel, which dissipated after twenty-four hours
附加记录：    

'inescapable' is a cinematic word.  which implies it was temporary).

But, yeah  I really like this piece, but I also feel like this piece could have way more polish and feel way more 'real'.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

One more thought: Your 'stinger' here is that the anomaly 'grows' to include any new deaths that must then be included in the containment of it. You can actually hide this from us by the very end (without cheating) with just some careful phrasings.

I feel like I've already written way too much feedback on this piece, but there's still way more stuff I could touch on. Either way, I think this has a lot of great moves, and  the energy of it (an anomalous temporal industrial disaster that has the potential of getting bigger as we continue to make sure everything happens precisely as it did) is great, and enough to win me over despite the fact that it isn't precisely what I think it should be.

Good job, and good luck with the article!

I actually had a line about that in an older version of the skip!
