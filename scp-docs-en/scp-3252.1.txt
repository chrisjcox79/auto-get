So, the thing is, this doesn't imply that time didn't exist Just that certain things weren't happening during that time.

For us to not see the stars in that distance range, but see the ones before and after, they must have not been producing light But, light must have kept moving. Otherwise, we'd still see those stars' light, since it was frozen, and then started moving again.

And for the fossils not to be dated to that period, no fossils could have been created at the time would just look younger and there'd be no apparent problem.

Basically, for us to notice missing time, there must be something to compare to that's Outside time, I guess. And you have provided things to compare to, but they're within time.

So, unless you come up with some different way for the Foundation to notice this, your current setup describes a time period during which some thinhs, but not all things, stopped happening.
