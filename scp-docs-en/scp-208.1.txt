"Acting on a wavelength previously unknown to physics" grates on the scientist in me.

It'd be much more plausible as superscience if the electromagnetic radiation was oscillating with four degrees of freedom rather then three. This would detectable, and might be interpreted as a unique "resonance" or "beat" to the waves. On first glance, physicists might interpret it as the result of the superposition of several complicated waveforms, though if they tested the radiation's range they'd notice it would diminish must quick then normal EM radiation.
