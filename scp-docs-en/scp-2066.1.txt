This is a good skip, and I'm gonna tell you why.

It's the human element. This skip is excellent at describing the anomaly through interview. It's a risky choice, not a lot of people can pull it off anymore, but this skip does an excellent job at building suspense with a proper payoff at the end. And it does so by good characterization of the anomaly and the situation. It's the subtle things, like the building urgency and mania in -6's voice. The increased coldness and distance of the researchers over time. 2066 get

Well I should probably not spoil it.

SOMEONE ELSE READ THIS THING AND UPVOTE IT. I haven't read good unsettling horror for some time, it deserves recognition.

The structuring is bold. You're going against a lot of the established norms of humanoid SCP characterization, by ignoring most of the standard format, and using the description to set up the interview instead of really describing the object.
