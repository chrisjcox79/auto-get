15 May 2018 01:43

I thought this was pretty nice but had some issues. Minor formatting ones included the blackboxing of the number of pages of sheet music and the time of unconsciousness - not sure why that would need to be blackboxed - and the first Japanese text (樺岡) should probably have a translation, since all the song names did, although I know it got explained later.

I agree with PeppersGhost that the compulsion effect is unnecessary and I also disliked the note, which I thought was tonally jarring. A more nebulous criticism of mine is that this feels like something which ought to have more meaning and backstory than it does. Reading through this, I want to find some sort of reasoning or internal logic behind this, but it doesn't seem to be there unless I'm being obtuse. I'm not saying it needs lots of extra details, since its simplicity and brevity is part of the charm, but I would have preferred some more hints to what is going on, or at least an indication that there is something more going on here. The note at the end is also a problem with this as the 'twist' (that people get trapped in the historical world) just comes out of nowhere - it doesn't tie into any information presented before.

I novoted, as the charm of the piece mitigated my complaints.
