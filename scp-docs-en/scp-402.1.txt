Somethoughts

close

All known SCP-402-A
附加记录：    

I realize that this refers to a singular volume of water, but the use of singular here feels odd to me.

Mobile Task Force Tau-4  is tasked
附加记录：    

Typically, the MTF nicknames are also written (in parentheses), but I supposed it's just stylistic.

is tasked with performing all testing
附加记录：    

I usually associate MTFs with field work, as opposed to testing or other lab work.

Any water that comes in contact with SCP-402 (hereby SCP-402-A) develops antimemetic properties, effectively rendering it imperceptible by any direct means.
附加记录：    

How does the Foundation know this? How do you perceive the imperceptible?

Safe handling of SCP-402-A can only be performed by trained personnel.
附加记录：    

By , because I'd agree with the first, but not necessarily the second.

resulting in the illusion that it did not possess any body fluids and giving it a desiccated appearance.
附加记录：    

Why would he look dessicated? Presumably the anomalous water still acts and moves like water, so it's not as if he'd look like a mummy. Also: if the body can't recognize the water and trigger laryngospasm, how are his cells still able to recognize and use it as water?

SCP-402-1's blood, when composed of SCP-402-A, possesses cognitohazardous effects when consumed in any quantity. Subjects will become convinced that any water they encounter is an extension of an effectively infinite subterranean body of water, in which they risk being trapped should they come into contact with the water. This results in severe aquaphobia, as well as a fear of drowning that persists even in situations where no water is present
附加记录：    

This phenomenon seems sort of tacked on and underdeveloped.

Testing the anomalous properties of SCP-402-1's blood has been deemed cost-ineffective
附加记录：    

This reads like a cop-out. For the Foundation to exist in any reasonable capacity it would need a functionally infinite operating budget, and I doubt they'd let money get in the way of further investigating the nature of an anomaly.

Also: While I feel the crosslinks feel a little forced, I don't really have an issue with them both due to the ongoing crosslinking effort, and due to a recognition of deeply ingrained site-culture bias.

I appreciate the attempt made to rewrite a fairly bare-bones scip in a style reminiscent of some of the older articles, but I feel like it leans more towards dated than classic. No vote for now.

+block

–block

(miscellaneous stylistic concerns)
附加记录：    

Appreciated, but I think those are all fine.

How does the Foundation know this? How do you perceive the imperceptible?
附加记录：    

Indirect means, which the paragraph then goes on to list examples of.

By , because I'd agree with the first, but not necessarily the second.
附加记录：    

I do mean the second. People who can't exclude as much moisture as possible from the surrounding area, keep a close eye on indirect signs of where water might be, and recognize the effects of oxygen deprivation quickly all risk a 402-A-related mishap.

Why would he look dessicated? Presumably the anomalous water still acts and moves like water, so it's not as if he'd look like a mummy.
附加记录：    

I could BS something about refractive indices and how much of cells are water or whatever, but quite honestly I just want it to be this way and it's within the realm of possibility, so I took the creepier option.

Also: if the body can't recognize the water and trigger laryngospasm, how are his cells still able to recognize and use it as water?
附加记录：    

Laryngospasm is a response to a tactile sensation, whereas the body's cells react to the chemical properties of water.

This phenomenon seems sort of tacked on and underdeveloped.
附加记录：    

In conjunction with the 109 and 812 crosslinks (which already linked up with 402, incidentally), it's worldbuilding. Something fishy is going on with a possibly-imaginary underground sea that may or may not be leaking into the rest of the world. Delving too deeply into this would be too distracting from the original premise, IMO  overdevelopment, basically.

This reads like a cop-out. For the Foundation to exist in any reasonable capacity it would need a functionally infinite operating budget, and I doubt they'd let money get in the way of further investigating the nature of an anomaly.
附加记录：    

High resources overall does not imply high resources earmarked for testing anomalous properties on human subjects when it's totally unnecessary for containment. There are places those researchers can be assigned where they'll be keeping things contained and saving lives. The Foundation doesn't always care about everything.
