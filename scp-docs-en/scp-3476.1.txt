So now they both dead.

And you slash your own throat.

So now it's double homicide and suicide—with no note!

I shoulda known better, when you started to act weird

We coulda— hey, where you going? Get back here!

附加记录：    

This is my gift to the lovely Gabriel McQueen, whos request was:

I'd love a tale, article, or poem about a location made strange, lonely, or haunted. Something has happened here, it may be unclear just what, before the time that the story picks up, that has changed a once innocuous place into something…else.

Here was my review from the Art Exchange forum:

This is a delightfully disturbing real-feeling story. I want to say very little about it so as not to spoil it. I do think that in the climax, the laughing felt out of place and out of character. There was also a description of something becoming louder despite the fact it's established the cameras don't have sound detection. But, yeah. Captivating. Disturbing. I will have more to say when this is posted  and it had better be posted, because damn. Oh, and while I don't think the SCP Foundation would ever have content warnings, I support the execution the author used here, keeping the content warning in-universe. Despite it feeling out-of-character for the Foundation, it aids in immersion, so if you must have a content warning in an SCP article, I do recommend this method.

Alright fine I decided I have more to say. This almost felt like one of those skips that was a glorified tale, where the anomaly isn't really necessary for the story and in fact is just a framework so that the author can write their tale idea in clinical tone and on the mainlist where it will get more attention. However, the ending pulled it back around, and I really appreciate that. Okay, now I'm done.
附加记录：    

My opinion remains unchanged. Good, disturbing shit. Oh, and thanks for taking my critique into consideration!

30 Dec 2020 16:11

This really did very little for me, despite my expectations going in. The central story behind this article is one that's been told many, many times before  he's just a walking mental-illness stereotype whose descent into murderous frenzy is never really built up or justified until a paragraph before shit goes down. The scenes of incredible violence in this are all too gratuitous and over-the-top to take seriously, and the actions and behaviour of McQueen in those scenes are reminiscent of a b-list slasher movie.

Also an issue for me are the multiple errors in grammar (for example - the apostrophe in ), style (always referring to characters by their first names in the recording logs), and the choppy and somewhat stilted structure of the syntax, which along with the over-the-top scenes of unnecessary and graphic violence made this hard to get through.

Lastly (and this may not be up to me to decide, but it's only my opinion), the content of this article seems to sharply contrast with the initial prompt behind it. While the initial anomaly of the article follows the subtlety and quiet emotion suggested by the prompt quite well, it quickly becomes an afterthought as the story of the article devolves into a slasher movie in SCP form.

I started reading this article expecting something incredible, and left sorely disappointed. Downvote.

The only thing readers get to see is purely the visual feed, which in my opinion seems pretty realistic? I don't understand where it falls from feeling real to  I can almost see the mention of Laura in a bath towel being a potential source of the problem, so I have removed it. However, I would like to ask what you'd do in this situation presented? How could I detract it less from sounding like this?

walking mental-illness stereotype : Frankly I don't necessarily know where this stems from. The only part of McQueen where I could potentially see potential stereotyping is the mention of medication for anger-related problems. I have since removed this, but otherwise I'm unsure of where else this occurs. I tried as best as I could to keep mentions or anything of explicit mental health problems at a minimum to avoid this. As to the part where you mentioned that his killings were unjustified: I'd have to argue against this. Its hinted several times that McQueen was somewhat unstable, even directly admitting to torturing animals (which is an indication of underlying tendencies). Also, just logically speaking, how fast do you think you'd confront a cheating spouse? I feel as if it would be unrealistic to elongate McQueens confrontation, especially given the context that he had hidden cameras all over the house. In this regard, I simply have no solution.

multiple errors in grammar:  These are being corrected, but currently I'm working ;-;

article devolves:  I cite my earlier comment of the ] that has changed a once innocuous place into something…else). I specifically wanted there to be a sharp contrast in order for the ending to be more surprising for the reader. But, still, this is a valid critique. I'm unsure of how to fix it though.

despite my expectations going in:  this doesn't necessarily seem like a problem tied to the article. In truth, this seems a bit unfair. I have no way to defend against pre-conceived expectations, especially considering that this story wasn't meant to  It was intended to be personal, to have an emotional connection amongst a small group of people. In my opinion, downvoting an article based off prior expectations seems somewhat unreasonable. (This isn't to suggest that that's what you did, just in general)

Hey, recipient of the story here! I wanna say thanks, of course, for putting the time and effort into making this gift for me. I very much appreciated the content warning, as I didn't really expect such a violent story resulting from my prompt, but, having been warned, I was able to put myself into a mindset where I wasn't unpleasantly surprised by it. I'm intrigued by the underlying mystery here, and I think tales could spin off from this piece about the location, the McQueen family, and how the magic of the place and the actions of its inhabitants feed off of each other. That would be one way to side-step the violent mentally ill person stereotype as well, if it's hinted that Gabriel's violent tendencies are seeded by something anomalous in this place of his ancestry, something that influenced his father's connection to magic as well.

While I would be interested in seeing more stories branch off from this, I do have one request that I think I'm entitled to make: if Gabriel McQueen becomes a recurring character on the site, even just in one more piece, I would prefer if his first name were changed to something else. It was fun to read a story with  I'm fine with keeping it! But my username becoming a recurring character out of my control would be almost weirder to me than reading my actual legal name in an SCP. Hope you understand!
