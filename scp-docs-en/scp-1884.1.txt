That was intentional. Rezarta and Luana were treated differently by the Circus, and their differing experiences affected their personalities. As the mouthpiece and show personality, Rezarta was conditioned to speak and act in a very specific way. Luana had much more freedom since people didn't expect as much from an apparent monster. Thus, Rezarta grew up to be very submissive and reserved whereas Luana is proactive and candid. You see this in their respective interviews, particularly in how they end: Rezarta focuses on buttering up the people holding her captive even though she's not entirely pleased with the situation, but Luana is outspoken and makes an effort to improve her quality of life.

Basically, I thought it would be interesting to have a human/monster duo where the monster seemed more human than the human did. One's outward human appearance led to her losing some of her internal humanity. One's monstrous appearance afforded her much more leeway in how she developed. And yet, in the end, one gets to stay in a residential chamber with an attendant while the other ends up alone in a cage, all because of their outward appearances.
