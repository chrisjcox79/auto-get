I am going to be a bit harsh here because I think, given this is your second try, you deserve to know: you REALLY need to work on using a clinical tone.

Some hints on maintaining a clinical tone.

1. Do not draw conclusions: present the evidence instead. For instance, you don't need to comment that a guy whose birth and death dates are 300 years apart is weird: people should get that just by reading. You don't need to speculate that the item is gloating, let the reader draw their own conclusion.

2. Do not use run-on sentences: each sentence should present one, or at most two, facts, FULL STOP. Then start a new sentence. If you have a list of similar things to talk about, use bullet points.

3. Do not go into details on side situations such as other SCPs involved: refer to the other page, or just say Data Expunged.

4. Each paragraph should be based on a single thesis.

5. Check your damn spelling and capitalization.

6. Finally, one issue I've found with all your SCPs to date: STICK TO ONE IDEA. Don't toss everything in there just because it's scary. SCP 173 is so effective because it's simple; a statue that kills you if no one looks at it. Try to be like that.

This article is seriously under-par. I am downvoting it until it becomes better.

"I don't think theres anything wrong with my sentence structure besides being longer( and can someone tell me if there is? from what I've seen from the comments, a lot of people say they like the idea in a strange way "from what i could gather" "from what i saw". Is that the same as saying "from what i understood"? is my writing complete gibberish? i can handle my formatting not being appropriate for this site, but it would really disturb me if my writing is universally bad) but see now that the bullets are a much better way to do things."

I know you say this is your informal writing, but ironically, your question is the best example of what's wrong with your overall sentence structure (as per the article pre-rewrite). You miss commas, and often string many complex phrases together before ending a statement. Also, the way you order your ideas can be confusing at times. Here's a quick rewrite:

"The rewrite's example showed me that a bulleted list is a much better way to do things. Before, I didn't think there was anything wrong with my sentence structure, though I do tend to write longer sentences than others. However, from what I've seen of these comments, even those who liked the idea use phrases like "from what I could gather", or "from what I saw". Is that like saying "from what I understood", as if it were hard to decipher? Is my writing complete gibberish? I can handle the idea that my formatting wasn't appropriate for this site, but it would really disturb me if my writing was universally bad."

Now, I don't mean to insult you, here. The reason I took the time to edit this article is because your idea was excellent; it was just that the grammar, organization, and style was right-out. So, since I'm a grammar nazi and hate to see a great idea downrated and gone to waste, I did the best I could.

Many of your ideas - especially your color and suppositions - took up too much space in each sentence. I didn't completely remove them, however: many I simply compressed into analytical adjectives, reducing clutter and improving the scientific tone without removing information. For example, "the team ventures to guess that this is it's way of gloating" was compressed within the statement that the inscriber "eagerly takes responsibility" for the deaths of its victims. I'm sorry if it seems like the color was drained out of the article, but we could always add an incident report of a grave being added. That would add plenty of colorful dialogue and [DATA EXPUNGED].

Your fake hero "Calothisosi of Britannica" seems kind of random, especially since he's fake. If you don't mind, I'm going to go ahead and replace him with Achilles. And by "Christ's two graves", I meant an empty one inscribed "not even you are above me", and a full one inscribed something like "oops, i forgot to lock the first one". The significance would be that it involves the only grave without a corpse in it, giving us the perfect place to insert it in the article. Again, it's more funny than scary, so never mind.
