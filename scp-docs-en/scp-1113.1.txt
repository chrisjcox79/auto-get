Fold

18 Apr 2012 22:16

I really wanted at least some of it left up to the reader's imagination, but I think I'm going to have to go with writing the guy's diary to save this SCP. Something like this:

He gets the spinning top when he's young. Idle spins it a lot, maybe uses it as a desk toy.

He gets something like osteoarthritis or a similar affliction, feels better when he's spinning it without realising, Pavlov's dog kind of thing.

He starts to grasp that there's some connection between him feeling better than many of his companions and the top. Finds out from crazy gypsy lady a folk tale about it.

Starts using it more often, uses it all the time.

Now this bit is tricky as he wouldn't be able to diarize it:

Realises that he's reached the end of his natural life and that he's dying having used up about 70 non-spinning years. He keeps the top spinning constantly to hold back death, but it gets harder and harder to spin it and it spins for less time as he gets closer to his natural end. Then he spins it too hard, causing a huge tear in the already blistered and torn palm. It spins under the sofa and stops against the back wall. In a panic he jumps down onto the floor and starts desperately scrabbling for it, dislocating his frail shoulder (he's 70 after all). His frantic actions may even speed up his descent into death (Oh, the irony!) and he passes away on the floor, the top just out of reach.

Now I've removed any possibility of the reader having to imagine anything at all, I hope it makes sense.
