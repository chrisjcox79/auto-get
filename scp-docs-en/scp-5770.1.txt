There are quite a few spelling and grammar errors that need correcting:

*During the second log there's a bit which goes: 'The gestures it's tail'.

*In SCP-5770 Events - 16.2.21 the word  is used where 'were' should be used.

*In SCP-5770 Events - 17.2.21, I don't really understand the meaning behind 'engage with an alligator'. What does 'engage' mean here?

*'the bodies of which have been covered by brown sheet'.

*'Dr. Pasqua is staging in front' should probably be 'standing'

*'cricified'

*'d-class' where it should probably be D-class or D-Class.

I think there are other times where the tone and style of writing can definitely be improved. For instance this sentence:

'How in the hell did they manage this and of course I didn't have the camera set up but it would have been pointless'

It could really do with being broken up with some punctuation. There are times when punctuation should be used, but isn't. Sometimes the tone of the article doesn't sound appropriate to a Foundation report, in my personal opinion. It's hard to lay my finger on exactly. Also, in the logs I feel there's sometimes an inconsistency where sometimes past tense is used (e.g. 'raised') and other times it isn't (e.g. 'walks'). It's definitely not a bad article though, I just think it needs cleaning up a bit.

27 Apr 2020 22:11

I like the concept of the article. An anomalous festival is an interesting idea as well as the concepts of SCP-5770-1 and -2. I also like the references to Italian culture.

In my opinion, certain parts of the article don't sound clinical enough mainly due to word choice and the sentences not being 'long winded' enough. For example the sentence  This extends to the research journals and logs in the addenda. While, again in my opinion, depending on who is writing the log or journal, they may be a bit less formal than the body of the article, they should still be clinical and detail oriented. Especially if it is written by a doctor or a researcher who have been trained to write reports in such a manner.

I would also suggest including more information in the description of the object and then expanding on it in the addenda instead of including the information only in the addenda. For example, it doesn't mention in the description how people react to seeing animate statues walking around, how they react to seeing people killed live on stage or any effects that the festival has on those who attend it.

There are also some inconsistencies in the article. Like if The Foundation knows that SCP-5770 takes place on the 7th Sunday of the year every 70 years in a specific plaza and hasn't been observed occurring outside at any other time or place, why do they have to monitor metropolitan areas in Italy between January and March every year? You also mention a  in the containment procedures and then never bring it up again to explain it further. You mention SCP-5770-1 and -2 might need to be forcibly removed or detained but you don't go on to elaborate about the circumstances when this is required, procedures to contain the entities or their reaction to people attempting to detain them.

Again I like the idea and I think this can become a great article with some more work.
