20 Oct 2013 23:06

I'll start out by saying that I really like the concept for this SCP so that the rest of this post doesn't immediately sound like a mindless hate train, but there are a lot of things wrong with it at present.

There's sentences which don't make sense, be it from missing words or confusing structure.

The SCP's sapience isn't referred to in the description; only its ability for telephatic communication. I get that it's implied and obvious, but an SCP's sapience is always something the Foundation would note. Maybe it should be tied it with the mentioning of its ability to communicate.

SCP-1485-2 is mentioned in the primary picture but never again. Is it supposed to be SCP-1485-1?

How do we know about SCP-1485's effects if only one child has been observed under its effects? The only way we'd know that it had the ability to imprint itself on children as it does is with testing involving more kids, the repercussions and results of which don't seem to be present.

The paragraph involving the SCP's capture is very poorly written and how does a hu-manatee (see what I did there?) manage to get into a shopping mall?

There's more, but you get the gist. Good concept, but with critical flaws.
