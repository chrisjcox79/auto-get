It'snot a format screw? Criticizing an article for having the narrative mainly in the form of exploration logs is like criticizing a novel for having a story told mainly through flashbacks - it's a storytelling device. It's one way in which you can convey information. Many of djkaktus' works, for example, rely on this way of moving the plot forward.

There are also several SCPs for which the point is not the effect in itself - they act more as a vehicle for the narrative. This is one of those where it doesn't revolve centrally around the nature of the SCP itself - it is more about the discovery and escape from it.

I see where you're coming from; as you said, the focus of this article is on the people involved and their exploration, not on the SCP itself. But I just feel that the magic of an SCP article is writing in such a way as to allow the reader to fill in the terrifying blanks themselves. My contention isn't that the presentation is bad. The presentation is what makes it good, in fact. I'm saying that the core concept of this article wouldn't work unless it was presented in the exact way you did it, but, it's also in the wrong format for me to appreciate the presentation, too. There aren't really any blanks for me to fill in, because the two survivors verbally expressed that  moment before I got around to doing it myself. I felt kind of cheated. For that reason, I feel this would have worked far better as a tale, where I walk in with a different set of expectations.
