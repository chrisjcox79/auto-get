Spiritual Successor.

22 Feb 2017 23:56

This began as a rewrite of the old SCP-948, “HOPE”, a hospital which allowed one within its walls to recover their mental scars and illnesses. It was rewritten to be as such by me back in 2010, with minor edits here and there until about 2014.

The original SCP was ported over from the old site by Far2, and was an orphaned article, as many Old Site relics are. With its rewrite, I became sole owner of the article.

Recently 948 fell below -10 and Rimple came up to rewrite it. He came up with the current draft - I’m a pretty big fan of it, as it keeps the basest essence of the idea, while going in a much more modern direction. It’s not perfect, no, but I like its direction.

The Rewrite Team decided that Rimples new draft here is not close enough to SCP-948 “Hope” as it was to count as a proper rewrite, so we reached a compromise. I deleted the old article, keeping a draft on Google Docs of both my most recent interpretation, and the oldest possible draft ported over by Far2. Rimple’s article here takes the slot as a spiritual successor.

It’s unorthodox, but the Rewrite team was more or less on board, and suggested this course of action.

NOW, WITHOUT FURTHER ADO, please enjoy Rimple's article here.  In the future, I shall redo SCP-948 “Hope” as a hospital again, but not here. Needs to see a rebirth, not a rewrite.

It's all Rimple's now.

I first read 948 back in 2013 or 2014, before I was a site member. I didn't like it, exactly, but something about it stuck with me for ages. The desperation, the frustration of stifled communication, that central question of what it means to 'cure' mental illness. When I saw it in deletion threshold a week or so ago, all that came rushing back, and it felt right to see how I could tackle those parts of it that had meant something to me, charge them with my own particular perspective.

In my own writing, I priotitise the themes and emotions I want to explore much more than the framing of those ideas, and I decided early on that it was right to treat this rewrite in the same way. Some might disagree, but that feels more respectful to me. Long story short, that meant dialogue, and that meant a humanoid. Ultimately, I hope that I retained the soul of the original, despite the recontextualisation.

A bunch of thanks are in order for this - to Dex for supporting this more abstract take and working with me to make it happen, to the many many people who gave crit on this1, to my parents for inspiring certain elements, to the Rewrite team for talking me through the process and being open about their thoughts on it. Thanks, as always, to AidenEldritch for being a fucking dork.

I hope you enjoyed!

Overall an interesting and compelling story. There were times when Eleanor didn't exactly speak like a doctor (I mean in terms of expertise, not of formality), although I guess she might not be an MD.

(Edit: The reason this is a minor problem is that her lack of expertise and somewhat her informality make her final breakdown to 948 less believable. I know that such emotions are universal and that MDs and PhDs are people like everyone else, but she talks as if she had no more idea of what she's asking him to do than a teenager with some online resources printed out. I mean, I upvoted, so it wasn't a huge problem, but it was there.)
