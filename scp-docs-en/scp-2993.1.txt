I'd just like to point out that crosstesting isn't something that's done on a  basis these days. Interactions between two anomalous things, neither of which you understand completely, can be unpredictable.

Basically, trying learn something about two SCP's by crosstesting them is on par with trying to learn something about two unlabeled reagents you found in the supply room of a chemistry lab by dumping them together in a beaker. Best case scenario they react in some radical way that tells you something, worst case scenario you just blew yourself up and took the east wing of the building with you.

Ok, you seem eager for advice, so I'm gonna go over some of the reasons I downvoted. I read the containment procedures, and they're pretty good. Brief, basic, clear. So nice job. The skip then however, didn't have the same pleasure. The effect itself is mildly interesting. The type of thing that if given it's own twist could work well as an SCP, but otherwise could not. Personally, I don't really see any twist that persuaded me towards maybe upvoting.

+block

–block

The recovery log doesn't feel necessary. Your redactions here seem fine to me, but I'm not particularly interested in what happened because there's no real narrative or conflict.

The addendum-1 could be potentially interesting as an introduction into a more expansive anomaly, but it seems bogged down in the story with the breach, and this:

It should be noted that both sentences Junior Researcher B█████ wrote contain all twenty-six letters of the alphabet.
附加记录：    

Eh, I don't care all that much. It feels random and out of place. Why should this be noted? Overall, I feel like the second paragraph of addendum one was the only thing with any real value. I also disapprove of an O5 being needed to approve testing with such a basic object. It also makes me question why they were allowed to view video footage when they weren't allowed to test.

Addendum-2 is where expungement gets a bit out of hand for me. Potentially interesting information like what changed, and what could lead to and XK event, are simply expunged. The doctor asks permission again for some reason, even though he already has permission, and then you go into trying to destroy the object. This is something I have a big issue with. What if the object does get destroyed? Considering it's supposed to look into another universe, one with XK class scenarios in place, what happens if you break connection with this universe? The fact is, we don't know, if anything something terrible. I would never permit these tests.

Finally, I think the theorizations (both of them) about the parallel universes are a slight break in clinical tone. Not only that, but even without them the reader can easily make similar conclusions without it being said outright. The second time I saw the doctor's quote concerning this idea, it felt really redundant.

Anyways, my opinions. I would have no-voted if only one or two of these were present, but since I can identify things that I dislike in almost all of the portions in the skip I decided to downvote.

Almost there, and I'd ordinarily novote, but there are multiple issues I have with this.

+Feedback!

-Feedback!

First off, wording and tone. When you say stuff like:

SCP-2993 appears to be an unremarkable standard pair of aviator sunglasses with no visible markings or logos and standard durability.
附加记录：    

Unremarkable is fine depending on how you use it; it's not good here, as it doesn't read nicely with the rest of the sentence and is padding.  is also something I have issue with. You wouldn't use it in this kind of context. If something is one thing but actually is another, then you'd use appears.

No form of radiation is able to pass through SCP-2993.
附加记录：    

No form of radiation? So not even visible light?

The fact that this breaks the laws of thermodynamics is pending investigation.
附加记录：    

This is silly. You don't need this sentence at all. You can let the reader figure that out on their own and imply it like you're doing. No need to state it. This also disrupts the flow of the rest of the description.

6 hours 17 minutes and 32 seconds
附加记录：    

You repeat this often to the point where the article gets long to read and is tiring to see over and over. I'm not exactly sure if there's a better way to word it, but wouldn't it be better to simplify and make it ?

Secondly, your redaction and expungement. It's absolutely sloppy and drags the article down considerably. You don't need to expunge to the length you do.

SCP-2993 was recovered on ██/██/2005 in the home of Mr. ████████ ████, a ██████ Professor at [DATA EXPUNGED].
附加记录：    

For example, this is really unneeded.  is supposed to be. And why expunge the location? Why not blackbox that out either?

You also repeatedly blackbox researcher and doctor names. Again, this is alright depending on the situation, but when all you can really see are blackboxes, you need to find a way to work around this. Either tell us who these people are, or limit the people you're mentioning. I recommend looking over the Zen and the Art of DATA EXPUNGED guide to get a better idea of how to work at it.

Third, you can easily cut down on the text of a lot of this. The recovery section in particular uses a lot of words to say nothing interesting or of importance, and you do the same thing in the addenda too. All the doctors' notes don't really need to be there, especially not this:

-I am now questioning whether SCP-2993 is viewing into another universe as had been first theorized, or if, instead of showing a parallel universe, SCP-2993 shows the past of our universe in an imperfect manner with some minor differences. - Dr. ████████
附加记录：    

Again, this is something you would imply and let the reader try to figure out on their own. Don't spoonfeed it to them.

Finally, there's not much of a story here. True, you leave implications (even if you outright tell the reader what to assume at times) and they're interesting, but it doesn't really lead me to care much beyond that. Give the readers more, give them breadcrumbs and a story, even if it's subtle, to let them know the larger implications of this. Because otherwise, these are just sunglasses that let the user see into a parallel universe.

-Feedback!

I like this idea. I think it can be done well, and there's potential for it to be interesting. But there's too much here that isn't done well that makes me downvote.

The object has promise, but here's what I'd improve:

+vision...

-hindsight.

SCP-2993 appears to be an unremarkable standard pair of aviator sunglasses with no visible markings or logos and standard durability.
附加记录：    

No need to say . Remove.

When viewed from the front, SCP-2993 is opaque and acts as a mirror. No form of radiation is able to pass through SCP-2993. The fact that this breaks the laws of thermodynamics is pending investigation.
附加记录：    

That doesn't actually break the laws of thermodynamics. Radiation can still be absorbed and reflected just fine. It's unexpected, but not thermo-breaking. Furthermore, given that the view from the other side isn't what's being shown in front of you, then no, light isn't getting through. Remove the thermo-breaking sentence.

SCP-2993 was recovered on ██/██/2005 in the home of Mr. ████████ ████, a ██████ Professor at [DATA EXPUNGED]. Foundation personnel were dispatched when Professor ██████ triggered multiple keywords on online forums under the pen name [DATA EXPUNGED].
附加记录：    

Too much expunging. So a professor said something somewhere as someone. This is too non-informative. Furthermore, he's Professor ██████ elsewhere; shouldn't he be introduced as Professor ██████ and not Mr. ████████ ████? It's clearly not the same name, by the way. All in all, the Recovery section doesn't really give us enough. The guy bought it at a yard sale. Does the fact he's a professor matter? Or that he teaches something at something U? None of that needs expunging. Or recording.

Similarly, no need to mention names and then expunge them.  later in the paragraph without multiple expungements.

APPROVED - O5-█
附加记录：    

Put the number there. It's okay. The O5 is still unknowable.

The notes on SCP-████ appeared to be minimally different, excluding [DATA EXPUNGED].
附加记录：    

This is one sentence where I don't mind the censorship. It's not relevant to SCP-2993, and is in keeping with the SCP theme.

What I love is that it seems that first, the glasses are showing the past. Then, the glasses are showing a parallel universe. Then, the glasses are just lying. Each theory requires different responses, and the ambiguity makes them all the more interesting.

No vote for now. It's not ready as is, but it has lots of potential with some revision.
