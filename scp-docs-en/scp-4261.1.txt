I'm oddly torn on this.

I don't really dig the whole thaumaturgic genre of SCPs. Throwing straight-up magic, runes, protective wards etc. into the mix has never been my cup of tea.

Similarly, I've never been big on religion-centric SCPs. It always feels a little on-the-nose to me to have SCP objects that just about canonise IRL religions documents  events in-universe.

Despite all of that, you wrote about a straight-up horn from the Battle of Jericho, kept secured with the use of thaumaturgic seals, and I actually kinda liked it. The hell did you do?

One of the main things I liked is that - similarly to Clef's 001 proposal having no instances of the words  is completely absent here, leaving that specific significance alluded but not outright stated.

I liked how the Foundation had to escalate the interrogations to circumvent the prisoners tricks, though I find the second one egregious in that surely the Foundation ought to have cuffed the guy to a chair? You go as far as to mention  but the Foundation didn't think to physically restrain them?

Even with cuffed hands you could still have the angle of him yanking his wrist through the cuff to do the same thing while missing the odd logic of having this clearly dangerous individual's hands free.

One more nitpick I have:

First interrogation:  would be a more appropriate term.

While I do agree with the criticisms posted above, I'm going to tentatively upvote because I still kind of enjoyed it against all odds.
