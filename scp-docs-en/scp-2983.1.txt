21 Oct 2018 15:28

Had this ended with the log of 'mallows, I would have been tempted to upvote. It's a cute base idea with a flair of detail to help bring it to life, and the references in the table are an example of good, fun, crosslinking (and bonus points for the handling of Headless!). Doubly happy that the newer take on Wondertainment is seeing presence in the mainlist.

However, the story has a few elements that, taken altogether, make it an unsatisfying read overall. It feels like some parts are introduced without much thought in how they fit into the whole.

+block

–block

First, and this is something I'm typically not pendantic about, but Safe class being used for something that requires ongoing, active monitoring and retrieval - something that can appear with little forewarning in public places - doesn't fit with how that class is typically used.

Second, while the first set of crosslinks is sensible (and good product placement!), it doesn't make a lick of sense to link 3203. In the context of that article, it is not even formally recognized as an SCP, and beyond that, it's something the higher-ups try to brush under the rug; it's not exactly something I can imagine the O5s calling attention to elsewhere in the database. I would try to find something more appropriate (perhaps Mr Money? that'd be keeping in with the rest of the references, but I cannot recall if his ability is to actually generate money off the top of my head).

Third, it's a little strange from an in-universe perspective for there to have been no mention of earlier prizes showing up prior to the final log, almost as an aside. Did they all appear at once? If so, It doesn't feel sensible for the first prize to not have manifested prior to the release of the other cereals. It feels like there's a hole in progression.

Finally, and most importantly: The sudden introduction of Waters at the ass-end of the article makes the final message ring hollow. It feels entirely arbitrary for the prizes to be directed at her, considering that she has no presence elsewhere in the work. The easiest fix, I feel, to give it a bit more coherence, would be to simply edit her in earlier on, replacing stuff like . Or mention her as the research head or somesuch.

Ideally though, I think she should have more prominence than that. Minor, clinical updates and addenda and proposals (like the O5 vote) could instead be infused with her voice, making her a character in her own right instead of a one-off vehicle for resolution. This should help the ending feel more natural, and earned.

-1, it's not quite as strong as it can be.
