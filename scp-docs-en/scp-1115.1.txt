You're assuming their number system works the same as ours, being read from one side to the other and strictly increasing by value. Human numbering systems, let alone alien ones, are often much more complicated. Just look at French with it's  to encode 91, or Roman Numerals where the location of the changing digits varies.

17 Jun 2013 17:07

Even in an intentionaly obtuse system (messages coded to prevent enemy eavesdropping), a counting system makes for an easily spotted pattern. This is why numbers are usually the first thing deciphered, long before the text content of messages can be understood. Counting up just makes it all the simpler, that's the sort of thing they put on deep space probes to be sure that aliens can figure out what we are trying to communicate.

If the probes used a number system where every single digit had its own unique name, with no relationship to any other number before or after (1,2,3,4), then the experts could be excused for not spotting it early on. But such a code is literally impossible to crack, and that's obviously not the case here.
