It might make a little more sense to me if the anomaly is that a small number of people perceive something fake to be real, instead of the anomaly being that a large number of people perceive something real to be fake.
附加记录：    

I had pretty much the same reaction though, which is why I downvoted. Maybe if that ambiguity were introduced, it could have been more interesting, but as it stands now I can't buy the premise of this; it wasn't really humorous or strange or horrifying enough to get over this hurdle. Structurally, the way it's handled leads to quite a bit of repetition that bloats the text to an unnecessary degree which left me skimming for the interesting bits, which were scant.

'Keyfabe as an SCP' feels like a great idea, but the way it's executed here leaves much to be desired. It feels like a waste of a perfectly good topic. I think a shift in the idea may help this to be more than a mild success. Depending on the type of reaction you were aiming for, I would suggest one of the following:

Keyfabe is merely complex containment procedures or ritual that keeps some epic being at bay. Now, this is kinda SCP-2090's shtick, but I think there's potential to twist this in enough ways so that it's sufficiently original.
Keyfabe is involuntary, and the SCPF is struggling to figure out how to free wrestlers from their internal hell. It could very well be that something is manipulating people akin to action figures.
Keyfabe is a trace-like state wrestlers abuse as a form of escapism.

Or hell, maybe all three of those things combined.
