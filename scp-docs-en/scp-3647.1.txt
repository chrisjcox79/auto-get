Well, my second SCP, also Roman themed, is done. I hope you like it, and hopefully it doesn't fall into the  stereotype. Thank you to everyone who reviewed this for your feedback!

+History

-History

The  referred to several times in this SCP was an important woman in late-Republican Rome, having been married to the scandalous tribune Clodius, orator and praetor Scribonius, and the famous triumvir Mark Antony. All three supported Julius Caesar and helped to bring about the rise of the Roman Empire. Oligarchical rule was not without its detractors, however - perhaps one of the most famous is the orator Cicero, a former consul and one of the most influential politicians of all time.

On the order of Mark Antony, Cicero was killed and beheaded. When Fulvia received his head, according to some historians she pulled out his tongue and stabbed it repeatedly with her hairpin, a symbolic revenge against his power of speech.
