This feelsoverly perfunctory perhaps? It feels like there's a really good idea in here but there's not quite enough of it for it to feel really satisfying as a read: possibly the ending being so open-ended and like, unthreatening is what is making this miss for me but I'm not sure.

It kinda feels like it ends up just really lacking conflict: there's a story, or at least a series of events, but there's no feeling of there being stakes to them or any quantifiable risk of failure; so it falls a little flat for me.

For example:

Throughout most of 1915, SCP-4956 was rolling along the bottom of the Black Sea before emerging on the shores of Anatolia and causing widespread destruction. SCP-4956 was subsequently launched into Earth orbit by the Ottoman Empire, with assistance of the Imperial German Army in 1916.
附加记录：    

This is a great opportunity for like a really tense moment where there's uncertainty about whether the SCP can be dealt with before it causes too much destruction; but instead it feels but there's also no triumphant moment when it's prevented and launched into space because there's no sense of it being challenging or the real cost of them taking so long or anything.

I think this is a novote from me as a result. There's definitely potential and with better execution, I could really see me digging this.

For my first comment on the SCP wiki, I'mdownvoting this. Execution was poor, and some lines made it feel less like an SCP article. It could've been structured better with more valuable sorting of data and filler. Maybe something like a discovery section below description, and various addendum dated around various events.

Now onto the actual content. Some actions by the Foundation presented do not comply with what the Foundation actually does. They don't just launch an anomalous rock into space and hope that fixes things. They have to be precise and know what they're doing, as well as how the anomaly would respond. Plus, I'm sure Saturn also hasROCKS on it. It's theorized to have DIAMOND RAIN, come on! At least add some addendum where they go and record the current state of the anomaly, and attempt to add additional containment procedures to make EXTRA SURE it don't bite them.

All in all, this needs a LOT of work to make a good SCP article. I get the point you're trying to pass with this one, but it needs a lot more editing and directive filler to make it more clear.

I'm afraid I'm no-voting as well, but I would like to add something. It's been said above that the theme of this piece is pointless destruction, and I'm super down for that. But I think something that would hit the emotional core that idea evokes would be go to into more detail with some of the destruction caused. Right now it's only described with a single line, and I supposed alluded to with the mention of the Paris Gun; however, imagine something, anything really, that got across the sheer damage caused by this giant boulder.
