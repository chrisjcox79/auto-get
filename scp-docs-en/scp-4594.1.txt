Fold

06 Jan 2020 10:46

Hello! Thanks for the critique, I really appreciate it.

I was intending to convey the weird and often repulsive relationship between humans and synanthropes through the imagery of a giant human esophagus which serves as a kind of express highway for rodents. In that sense, it's meant to be treated as somewhat innocuous, in a sense fitting in with the rest of the city's infrastructure. Disgust is what I was aiming for here.

+block

–block

I'll implement changes based on your comments, but I'll also explain my thought process behind some of the things you pointed out.

Small mammals using it as a means of travel but remaining limp while within the SCP feels a little weird here? Are you intending to convey that it's kinda like a futurama style people tube rather than like ?
附加记录：    

Yeah, the Futurama-style people tube is an apt analogy. The rodents are taking advantage of the muscle contractions( i.e. peristalsis) of the skip for movement. So they themselves don't need to move.

It feels kinda weird that for about 30 years, the Foundation knew this existed but didn't use radar to track it? From what I can tell, ground-penetrating radar has existed since 1910 and like, there were developments in it during the 70s which meant it really started to take off then?
附加记录：    

You're right, but as far as I know, it would probably still be pretty difficult to use modern GPR to track something with the diameter of a human esophagus in an area already saturated with all sorts of piping and tunnels and whatnot. That's why I chose 2003 as a date when the Foundation had effectively developed specialised GPR allowing them to track the skip. But I realise it's not made clear at all here.

….Honestly part of me feels like something more evocative should be used here? This is very, dry and factual and it feels like an opportunity to really convey…something.
附加记录：    

Fair enough. I'll think about writing a weirder discovery log. I was attempting to imply that a concentration of rodents in one location, in this case the guy's absurd number of pet mice, attracts the skip to that location. Mayhaps that's not that interesting of an implication though. I'll think of something else.

Thanks again for the feedback.

Edit: I've implemented several changes based on your feedback. I hope it makes for clearer and more impactful reading.
