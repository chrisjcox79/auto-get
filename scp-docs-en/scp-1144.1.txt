Fold

18 May 2012 12:54

It's a common sign of a lazy SCP to use the quality of other SCPs to bolster it up. The best way to look at it is would this still be the same quality if you changed every SCP in it to a different type of dwarf hamster. In this case, I think it would, and so you're not gaining anything by using SCPs, and the researchers wouldn't be learning anything by using the different SCPs either. Maybe replace the SCPs with something that would be more likely to ascertain what the scale is detecting.

Also, and I would consider this a big one for developing this into a good SCP - do YOU know what it's detecting? I would make sure you do, and also make it so a clever reader could work it out too. Experimental logs will be good for that.
