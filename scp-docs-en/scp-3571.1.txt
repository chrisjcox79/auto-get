+1

I think this skip has an undeniably strong start, but that dithers as you hit the Interview Log. The dialogue was just not up-to-scratch as far as realism goes- not to mention what a drag it was to read. The whole time I just kept thinking to myself, 'Is this really adding anything?' I felt as though the description did sufficiently well at explaining the concept without needing this elaboration that the interview provided.

Barring that, the ending was wonderfully ominous, and I certainly didn't see it coming. It was a little reminiscent of SCP-3000's first addendum, but ultimately not enough to detract from my reading experience. Good work.

I like the idea, but I feel like the addenda overstayed their welcome and generally overlapped. They didn't exactly repeat themselves, but it felt like they retreaded a lot of the same conceptual territory (for instance, the interview and testing logs are both seem to be depicting the same general sequence of events multiple times with only a few differences.) I think this piece would be a lot stronger if you condensed the addenda, maybe into a few shorter tests (with a focus on an interesting facet instead of trying to capture the entire test) and then the exploration log? I think that's how I would go about it at least.

I'm at -1 until that whole section is tightened up.
