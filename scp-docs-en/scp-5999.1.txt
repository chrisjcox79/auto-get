This is a passion project that is well over a year in the making. I would like to thank everyone who has taken the time to review this since its inception. I have to note, however, that I was fairly deceptive in shopping this around in the early stages  many people only read a small piece of the article at any given time not knowing the larger work it was attached to. The actual html file is here.

This is where ALL  of the translation is done. The Javascript and CSS files do not include any text needing to be translated. If you have previously translated Seven prior to the format change, the actual content has largely gone unchanged and you can likely just copy paste your translated text into this HTML.

Figure I should just update this comment as I keep stringing together random theories about this piece, since making a new comment each time would be obnoxious.

First thing, I'm pretty deeply convinced that the doll and Cindel Shields are effectively the same person. Although that's probably not a huge surprise.

I am also pretty convinced the thing in the video is the FALSE BEAST from the exploration log.

Finally, with the addition of the new image to the video game timeline, I think that SCP-5000-A is the prodigy, SCP-5000-B is the priest, and SCP-5000-C is the king. Unfortunately I'm not sure about their relationship to the rest of the skip, or the symbolism behind the Sword, Jewel and Mirror.

Ok, so we also see that each of the sections is prepared by a different character associated with a pataphysics article. We have, in order:

Swann, who is known for the  and burning them. Possibly related to the 001 idea of killing the readers, we the readers may be the witnesses. In this case I eagerly await incineration because it is cold as balls in my room.

Next we have Talloran, whose section is dedicated to the rape and murder of Jacqueline Holcroft. If there was any particular reasoning for this pairing I'd have to say maybe some sort analogy between SCP-3999's treatment of Talloran and what happened to Holcroft? Although I personally believe that a stretch.

Following that we have Dr. Panagiotopolous, who is the director of the OG pataphysics department from Rimple's author page. No strong idea why this character was chosen for this segment.

Then we have the exorcism of Agent Grace created by another member of the OG pataphysics department, agent Sullivan. Sullivan is able to sneak their way into narratives, and possibly alter them, so it's possible that Agent Grace's possession was not intended to happen, so Sullivan had to sneak into to  by becoming Doctor Macneil. Although this sort of is dependent on a theory I'll present later.

Next is the  game prepared by Researcher Smalls. Researcher Smalls is known for being deleted from the SCP-verse and attempted to seek refuge in the forums, which may be connected to the fact that a lot of what we hear about the game is through an online forum.

After that we have the Doll Interview prepared by I.H. Pickman. I don't have any particularly strong working theories about this pairing, but then I again I don't remember ihp's proposal super well.

Lastly, we have Dr. Menard. I don't have a super strong theory about why Menard was chosen for this last section either.

But one thing that I want to draw attention to is that.

Now let's take a step back. Consider some of the things noticed so far. There was mention of  or a classical creepypasta content.

Finally, I'd like to look at the chain.

If you look at the mentions of the chains at first they're described as taut. They're holding something back. But then they're weakening, and by the end they're broken, and useless. The chains can't do anything more.

You know, in that parlor game, it's really just a way to make telling scary stories even scarier. You start with 100 candles, and you put them in a room. Then each person playing tells a scary story, and then goes into the next room and puts out one of the candles, and then they come back. You keep going until either everyone chickens out, or you extinguish all of the candles, creating a place for the evocation of spirits. According to wikipedia, most games end at 99.

But in that last image there are no candles left.

You know how in SCPs there's thing that tell you  but really you're supposed to read it because like, that's the point of the story?

I think the point of this story is we really weren't supposed to read these. They're designed to be scary as to discourage the witnesses from finishing. Because you see, when we ran out of stories, we were finally told to read on, except for the candle picture. Which told us to stop.

We extinguished the last candle, and released the anomaly.

But is that all?

Of fucking course not.

Because you see book. There's hidden text throughout the whole damn thing. Most of it is chapter titles which, while important, I think mostly just relate to the section they're about? The chapters are, in order:

If this was all the white text, I wouldn't feel like I've missed something as much. But no. THERES MORE.

In the last entry of the Recorded Evidence collapsible, along the side of all of the repeated bullets about the 100 candles, there is a question laid out for us in hidden text:

And like BUT THEN WHAT DO YOU MEAN BY WHAT HAPPENED TO THEIR BODIES???

Alright, it's been about a day. Time for round two.

Andhole...

...go!

As you said, I was almost there. And in that spirit, I'm not throwing out everything from my last post. As of right now, the most important parts of the previous theory that carry over are the following:

This article is just the ending to a game of .
The doll in the testing log is just the merged version of Cindel and Mandy Shields.
By the end of the article, something got out.
If we wanted to survive, we should've heeded the warning and not read the article.

Now, important new points that will be appropriately factored into my new theory:

The blackboxes line up nicely with the kill agent protocol name from Swann's proposal, meaning that the only article directly referenced by this one is Swann's proposal
Everything in teletype was not supposed to be there

Let us begin the new theory, shall we?

My dear Watson, let's examine exhibit A: the teletype. The teletype throughout the article does a few things. One of the most notable ones is that it details the weakening of the chains as we continue to read the article. But they also tell us a few other things. They tell us about Cindel and Mandy. They tell us about the witnesses. And they tell us about spirals.

Given the way that the teletype uniformly escalates throughout the article, I still hold my belief that by reading the article, we're freeing something. And by finishing the final story, we've unleashed whatever this thing is upon ourselves.

I want to also note that this thing is most like a pataphysical entity of some type, given how it infiltrates each article in a way. Most of the times it appears it comes out as something that's not really grounded in the chapter itself. It's always in a category of its own. The mentions of chains are not really tied to any one story. Cindel and Mandy are both purple, not one of the empathic profile colors. Spirals comes out of nowhere.

Except.

I don't have a lot of connective tissue for this jump, but I'm going to continue on the premise that this is the name for the anomaly that is contained by the stories. It's the thing that's breaking the chains. It's the monster that smiles in the video at the end.

But but notice. I'm not calling it SCP-5000. Because it isn't. No, the FALSE BEAST is just part of the con procs for SCP-5000.

The spirals line feels a bit out of nowhere, no? Well, let me ask you this, with Swann's proposal in mind: What does the fractal memetic kill agent on the SCP-001 page have on it?

Spirals.

You know, there was a time when shaggy was joking about not naming this article . I wondered why, and I thought I had a reasonable explanation for it in my first theory, but I think I was off.

We aren't releasing SCP-5000.

We are SCP-5000.

And this is the Foundation deciding we need to be contained.

Before you open this document, each of these are just foundation-ized versions of classic horror story tropes. Classic ghost stories. As we read the article the embodiment of the memetic kill agent, the FALSE BEAST, is just tearing through the stories, trying to get to us. It invades and contorts what were originally standalone stories built to keep it bound, and meshes them all together as we go, bringing pieces from one into the next into the next.

Until we reach the end. And the kill agent greets us with a smile.
