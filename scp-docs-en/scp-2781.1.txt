+block

–block

SCP-2781-969 is the late Agent Kata, returned as a 'bodhisattva of the high seas'. To him, he is trying to relieve the suffering of the Foundation by helping them contain SCP-2781 as a whole as a Middle Way between his duties as a bodhisattva and a Foundation member.

And the other instances are essentially trapped at two points (Site-2781 and their respective original places) while being translucent. If they are translucent and not opaque, they cannot interfere with physical matter and cannot 'attack' people anymore.

That's because the editor was formerly in the Foundation, hence why he decides to edit the SCP file. I suppose that when he got back to the stuff he knew from life, he adopted the stylistic of the Foundation.

'Hypothesized'? If the boddhisatvas aren't even certain of it themselves, why would they exert so much effort to attract people to the 'pure land'?
附加记录：    

I will chalk that to that particular instance's (SCP-2781-969/Kata) insistence of scientific methodology. For now, it is only its word that indicate any existence of SCP-2781-A. The other instances are unlikely to confirm it (and the Foundation would not deem it to be strong alibi, unless they explore the place with people who come back to tell the tale).

So 'hypothesised' was a compromise to show that the 2781-969/Kata is aware of how the Foundation would tread on the matter.
