“Squeamish” added to D-0892’s file, to be reviewed as their reaction is odd considering the acts committed which led to them being acquired by the Foundation as D-class personnel.
附加记录：    

I don't really like this. Any instance of Foundation researchers taking shots at D-Class personnel for what they did prior to becoming D-Class, even vague and offhanded instances like this, just rub me the wrong way. It just seems out of place and a bit unprofessional to me.

That's a minor SCPeeve, though. Overall, it's not bad, but it's not particular gripping for me. It has a curious effect that's thoroughly explored, and while I admittedly have a soft spot somewhere for things that do a weird thing with a test log demonstrating said weirdness, nowadays, that soft spot doesn't really do all that much for me unless what it does is really mind-blowing, or there's some kind of other hook that leads into something deeper.

For what it is, though, I think it's alright. Novote.

I personally love these. Also, this is the sort of thing that really calls for a collab testing log.

Item: Dr. Harris's antique nickel-plated wristwatch

Subject: Researcher Jobes, 29 years of age

Procedure: Subject placed item inside box and then closed it.

Results: Small metal and scraping sounds observed for 2-3 minutes after box was closed. Dr. Harris discovered his watch was missing during the testing procedure and attempted unsuccessfully to gain entry to the box for the remainder of the 5 minute period. Upon recovery and examination, the movement gears and other previously internal workings of the watch were now situated outside the case, while the leather of the strap was reshaped and now contained inside the case. Additionally, the watch no longer functioned.

Analysis: Researcher Jobes has been placed on probation (again).
