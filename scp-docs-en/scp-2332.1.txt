Authors Notes

21 May 2014 06:03

You had one job, butterfly.

This is what would’ve been my SCP-2000 contest entry, had I gotten past the brainstorming/scrawl notes in my notebook stage.

Showspoilers

hidespoilers

Basically, the Foundation in some other time/space/whatever managed to manufacture a means to send messages between parallel dimensions. The messengers are these butterflies made of light and human brain cells, who have enough embedded intelligence to communicate as well as relay the messages they were created to store.

Mark Kiryu in one dimension (where these butterfly messengers are commonplace) was trying to send a message to Mark Kiryu in another dimension but some funky space stuff happened and another butterfly smacked into it. Basically, two of them en route to the same message delivery spot got mushed up by accident and ended up in the wrong time/space/dimension. The resulting butterfly is basically waiting for the parallel universe it came from to right itself so it can go home.

Different kinds of light are like food to these butterflies. You are what you eat. d: This one happens to like ultraviolet light while blue light makes it feel nauseous.

Many thanks to all these nice people who read this over in chat! /deep breath ProcyonLotor, Riemann, Gaffsey, MisterFlames, Zolgamax, Eskobear, Devereaux, Gamerkid2345, TheTyrantis, TroyL, TwistedGears, Athena_Grey, Kalinin, Ihp(kmn) Sorry if I forgot anyone.

Some easy easter eggs, look up the NGC 6302 nebula and the etymology of the word “angel”. ;)

Showquestions

hide

What is happening over there?
附加记录：    

Business as usual.

Can the Foundation contact them?
附加记录：    

This dimension's Foundation cannot.

Are they trying to contact our universe's Foundation?
附加记录：    

No. The butterfly was never supposed to end up in this dimension.

What was the message they were trying to send?
附加记录：    

Something that would possibly alter this dimension's timestream if it was passed on to the wrong Mark Kiryu.

My initial take on this was that it's inherently not that big of a deal to see these light butterflies flying around in other dimensions, but they're not supposed to exist at all in the current dimension, at least not for a hundred years or whatever. The Foundation wanted something fast (hence the light) and smart, to a degree (hence the nerve cells and the passage of words directly from one iteration of a Foundation staff member to another).

Potentialspoilers

hide

This dimension's Foundation finding it was entirely a mistake. Two messenger butterflies got smashed together en route to the same place, crossed the streams, quantum entanglement, what have you. Admittedly, I'm not sure if that got through in the interview, but I wanted to leave enough room for the reader to put together their own comprehension of the little dimension hopper messenger butterfly.

As stated previously in chat, I'm close to an upvote on this one. I enjoyed the interview, which is a neat trick because interviews are difficult to write successfully, both from the proper execution and article enhancement angles. As a whole, there's a pleasant degree of puzzlement that I get from this article; not enough is given away to make things immediately apparent, but it's not so veiled as to be meaningless nonsense.

Not much more, because I don't want this clearly explained and in a sense disenchanted, but maybe just a little more suggestion. I mean, what kind of message is this?
