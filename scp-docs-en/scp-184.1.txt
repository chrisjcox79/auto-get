06 Aug 2008 21:19

I have a few questions, if nobody minds:

1) Will 184 internally expand things that are not accounted structures, but are enclosed?

For example, would it internally expand pillowcases and sleeping bags?

2) If 184 is placed in a wardrobe within a 184-ed house, will it continue internally expanding the house or begin work on the wardrobe? If the latter, can this nesting be done indefinitely?

3) Is the extra space being taken from other structures, or some extra-dimensional space? Are there any hazards inherent in this?

4) Do 184-ed structures have their own gravity? i.e., would turning a 184-ed box upside down affect anything inside?

I think that's all for now.

And yeah, this is something I'd have on my wishlist. :)

Fold

08 Aug 2008 01:33

1: If it was enclosed, yes, it would. eventually you'd get pillowcase hallways, and sleeping bag tunnels that open into soft rooms and corkscrewing shafts that go down and down…

2: Yes, it will start to expand the wardrobe. As this thing is unstable, i think if you nested it too much, it'd start to leak into one another…or into the outside.

3: Extra space is taken from a unknown source. Unknown if there is any danger to this process, besides the more strange and potently dangerous duplicates: carpet made of glass chunks, or a operating blender that's turned inside out, is in a random hallway, and is six feet tall, for example.

4:Turn a 184-ed box, and everything inside turns with it. Think of shaking one with a team inside…or burning it…

I glad you like it, but i won't be hunting for it on ebay quite yet. i like being able to go to the bathroom without having to secure a safety rope.
