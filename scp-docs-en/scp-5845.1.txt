Fold

11 Jun 2020 22:54

Department of Abnormalities skips aren't very blatant and that mysteriousness is part of their charm which I have had some trouble balancing with understanding during the drafting of this. I eventually settled on wanting the reader to walk away from this article understanding two things about the DoA's actions here, what this place contained and what they are trying to hide. Those two questions don't have the same answer but they shared a history.

Per other DoA articles, this often requires some knowledge of other SCPs so popular ones are often selected by the author. What this place is built for isn't as well known as some, like how SCP-3220 re-imagines 173s containment or SCP-5832 with the Scarlet King, but chances are you have probably heard of this one. I only hope I'm pulling this off with even half the ability as those authors.

23 Jun 2020 04:19

I gather that this has something to do with 2845 but that's about as far as I can figure out.

+thoughts

-thoughts

Given the (needlessly?) esoteric method for entering 5845 I have to wonder how the Foundation even discovered it in the first place. Are they just shooting people into random celestial bodies and seeing what happens?

 term

The 1km sphere completely baffles me. Was it the containment cell? Is the 'square pyramidal hole' a spot for one of the frequently mentioned nails? Does the lack of anything in the hole mean that containment has failed and whatever was within has escaped and if so why aren't there any signs of something escaping?

I really enjoy the containment consultant's document but the physical document is left too vague for me. Without a physical description of the document there's no clue as to when or how it was written. Is it written with ink on parchment? Typed up on an modern sheet of 8.5x11 paper? And what language is it in? Renaissance Latin? Italian? English? I feel like these kind of details could help give us more clues as to what is going on here. Ditto for the writing on the silver plaque.

The murals/reliefs have some incredible imagery in them, they're probably my favorite part of the article. No idea what they mean, but they're very evocative.

Overall I think it's very well written but the puzzle pieces are just too vague to make a cohesive whole for me.
