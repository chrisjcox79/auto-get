It's a good central idea, but far too explicitly explainedand too obviously emotional.

I'm sure others will disagree, but I think the emotional impact of a skip should come from the story pieced together by the reader from clues in the text. The reader is the one who does all the interpretative work, and the text should provide a bare minimum of clues compatible with allowing them to do it.

There's an interesting central idea here, with the personalised museum and everything, but I don't like the test logs - with the exception of the drowning one and the shooting, they all seemed like overly cheesy attempts to wring feels out of the audience. I'm also not an enormous fan of the entity behind this being quite so personable - it's refreshing to have an SCP be forthcoming about their origins, effects, etc. but seeing as it's a genius loci I was expecting a something a little less human. That's probably more a matter of taste than a real problem with your article, though.
