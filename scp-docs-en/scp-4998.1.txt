The history of advertisements is the story of their continual expansion into newly created spaces. Those spaces are becoming increasingly proximal and yet invasive by the same measure. This is a story about that trajectory mixed with the anomalous, and is a horror piece imo.

This is also in sort of thanks that even after 10 years, we don't have to suffer through ads on this site. Could be much worse folks.

Honestly, I'm not sure, and I'm loathe to push you either way  but I'm not at all sure how I would have done it is any better than how you already did it, so take the following with prescription-grade salt:

+block

–block

I think the meandering style of the dialogue is great, in that it works to convince us that this guy is 1) An idiot, 2) Very powerful, important, and unchallenged in his time, and 3) The natural consequence of a culture where corporatism is our culture.

(Aside: The bit where the O5 starts talking about historical figures in marketing  but it was still such a clever way to determine/demonstrate that. Clever enough that I'm loathe to say you should touch it or cut it.)

If I were writing this, I would keep that style, but remove all overt Trumpisms. I'd keep him talking like Trump, but I would write it in such a way that the reader isn't going to recognize it as Trump unless the reader is trying to recognize it as Trump (IE, nothing Trump has ever actually said, and no direct  to me, that would be an Easter Egg for the reader astute enough to bother and count the number of boxes to match them to Donald Trump's name.

My reasoning for this: I don't think you need the reader to directly connect this to Trump. This article is definitely about a form of Trumpism, but (as counter-intuitive as this might sound) I don't think you actually need to invoke Trump to get across Trumpism. Trumpism is something that probably predates Trump by quite a large margin; he's just the most blatant and obvious example of it in our time.

Again, let me stress  writing is basically alchemy, not science. I think this article is great as-is, and I'm not sure if the way I would have done it is actually any better.
