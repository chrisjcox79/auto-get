Overall, the core anomaly and general narrative are far too dull and short-lived to be enjoyable. The anomalous attributes exhibited by SCP-4273 aren't detailed particularly well, and from what I can get out of the description, what it does do isn't very interesting; something more of an AI (anomalous item) than something that would be given its own SCP № designation.

As for the narrative, it's quite bland in all honesty. An individual in power binding their soul to an object to maintain power even after death isn't very original; bordering on a cliché at this point. Furthermore, even if the narrative was something of grand uniqueness and intrigue, the article itself is far too short to convey much meaning and makes it hard for me to have any notable feelings for what's going on here.
