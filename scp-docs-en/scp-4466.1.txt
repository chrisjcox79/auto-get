This is art

06 Nov 2018 22:47

One of my favourite things in SCPs is when something exactly rides the line between being inexplicable and having an underlying associative logic, and this is frankly a masterpiece at just that.

Gonna do a spoiler because this might spoil the magic for some folks:

Analyticalspoilers

–block

The idea of the article is that the steeds are almost but not quite horses, in an uncanny way that never quite resolves in your mind (hence the headaches it gives you, and all the more so the more you know about horses).

So how do you sell the idea of something that's almost but not quite horse? Several ways, some more obvious than others. Obviously we scrupulously avoid mention of horses directly; we call them .

But the real engine making this work is associative logic: the article takes concepts that are themselves almost but not quite related to horses, and has them orbit the  in ways that also don't quite make immediate sense:

Salt is the most used concept of this kind, and it returns throughout the article. Salt is an expertly chosen material for this purpose, because it has a weak but present associative link to horses (because people give salt licks to their horses), and it's a pleasingly mundane material (this fitting with the overall agricultural milieu) but it's also rich with other associations (e.g. its used often in many occult traditions in rituals involving the binding of banishment of demons - which is why we see the cowboy laying a circle of it). Salt is persistently referred to so we recognise it as a non-arbitrary detail,but also persistently refuses to be associated with horses in an explicable and expected manner, so encouraging us to dwell on the other associations.
The trowel in the second part of the video also operates this way; it's dimly associated with horses as a recognisably agricultural implement, but its actual use on a horse-like creature is not immediately clear - what can you dig in a horse? and we cut away before any further detail is seen).
The cattle prod in the final bit of video works the same way: cattle prods are almost but not quite something you use on horses, and we cut away before seeing how its used. But also note how its appearance at that juncture in  represents a resurgence of the agricultural logic in what was otherwise a very occult scene of magical body parts coalescing.

I could honestly write a whole essay. I think I might. I wish I could +1 twice.
