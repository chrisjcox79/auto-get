Some science issues with this:

Metal ions integrated into these proteins
附加记录：    

Carbon is not a metal, and I don't think you can have an ion of an alloy, which is a mixture of metals rather than a single element or molecule.

most SCP-3184 instances posses few to no chloroplasts within their cells. It is unclear how the process of photosynthesis is circumnavigated in SCP-3184 species
附加记录：    

There are parasitic plants that operate just fine without chlorophyll. It may be reasonable to say that an anomalous, carnivorous plant gets enough nutrients from its prey that it doesn't require photosynthesis.

an exterior shellwhich is keratinous in nature
附加记录：    

I believe keratin is found exclusively in animals. I might suggest chitin, which is a comparable protein found in, among other things, the cell walls of fungi. Considering the re-structuring of the Kingdom classifications of organisms that seems to happen frequently in biology, I'm not even sure this really qualifies as a plant, though I'm not an expert to tell you what kingdom it might actually fall into.

im glad you've mentioned this stuff actually. im always up for a science-y chat. the things you've mentioned are probably not explicitly clear due to my writing at the time (i'd like to think i've improved), however they are supposed to conjure up questions from those with a scientific background. the reference to metallic carbon relates to some papers i've read of attempts to develop carbon with metallic properties, of which allotropes are theoretically possible.

as you have said, keratin does not naturally exist in plants in quantities enough to create a tissue. however the fact that these organisms are made by amateur scientists in a house in the middle of nowhere lends credence that they were trying to work with experimental properties of their organism.

also, i would be interested to see evidence of actual plants that lack any chlorophyll. even carnivorous and parasitic plants rely on photosynthesis for respiratory substrates.

thank you for the comment though. this scp was genuinely supposed to a throwback to the piece of shit forgotten about scps from season one; an attempt to show that not all scps have to be some reality altering, end-of-the-world causing super human phenomenon. and as a result, it is boring and inconsistent.
