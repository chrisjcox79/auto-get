A solid improvement from your earlier draft! Adding the summary of the anomalous Book of Mormon gives it enough background to make it feel like there's a larger pattern that this SCP fits into, but maintains the mystery of what this place really is, why it exists, and what's actually going on with it. I like that the exploration starts off mundane (for an extradimensional portal), and I like that the weirdest effects of this place is on the people exploring it. Creepy without feeling the need to resort to people turning into bone-monsters or pools of blood or whatever. The whole piece has a great sense of things being just not quite right.

+1!

I actually got in-person feedback on this from somebody today! What a small world :). After talking to them, I changed the titles of Dr Arcia's research note and the literary analysis. 18 Apr 2020 02:47

At the heart of it, this is a flawed article. There are some pretty glaring SPaG issues and formatting quirks that were major enough to detract from my ability to read the article. The most notable of those is the placement of lines in the exploration log right next to each other without breaks, which causes a wall of text appearance that's hard to read.

The dialogue is also a weak point, and there were a few pieces of dialogue that I disliked. It was a bit hard to follow the dialogue at points, particularly at the end of the second exploration log, where I read it a few times but still wasn't sure of what was happening.

However, I did still enjoy this despite the flaws. I read through to the end, despite a lot of the above flaws being enough to make me stop reading in most. It's a nice atmospheric article, and when I was able to follow the action it was engaging. Although it could be improved, I did like this enough to upvote.
