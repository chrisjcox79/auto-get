I enjoyed this one a lot. I liked how it just kind of kept jack-knifing back and forth, with little predictability. Generally, I really dislike separate addenda pages these days, but these were used rather well.

If I have any complaint

+block

–block

It's that I found the punchline in the Black Eagle log cheesy. Looking back at it in the context of the visual distortion on the -1s, it makes sense, but it's still

Bear with me, I'm having trouble articulating this. The article spends a lot of time being very tense and serious due to the deadly-mountain-climbing subject matter, then the tone very much flips to  in Log C, especially where, after getting real weird, the scip basically saves the remaining team members. So far so good, enjoying all of this.

The Orpheus log flips tone again, ending up on the amped-up-superpowered-conspiracy-theory style I've only really seen in Warren Ellis comics. (Giving the astronaut a name was an excellent touch, by the way. It really tied down the rest of the Black Eagle stuff to something , and distinguished the person being interviewed from the Foundation.) Anyway, about halfway through this log, I've sufficiently switched gears enough to buy into this.

Then the phone call comes in, and the buildup is intense but the contents of the phone call have flipped tone again. Now we're back to wtf territory, only now it seems pretty arcane and somewhat mystical/magical. I wasn't ready for a hard right turn like that, and compared to log C it left me kind of unsatisfied. I can't tell if I'm sad that it didn't answer enough of my questions or if it answered too many of them (by inserting -possibly- some kind of escaped world-ending super-weapon behind everything else that was going on).

But, despite my writing four paragraphs about it, it's not meant to be a huge complaint. Definitely +1 in any case!
