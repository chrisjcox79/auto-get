This is a phenomenal example of the classic  trope used so much in creepypasta. While none of the content is similar, it kind of reminds me of SCP-1981, in that it takes this creepypasta trope, puts it into Foundation language, and actually succeeds with the material. This isn't, of course, to say that this article is merely lowly creepypasta or anything like that. I think it rises above the rest, really. This is what videogame creepypastas and SCPs want to be. It isn't merely some ghost trapped in a machine (haha) but it's a progression of really, really good videogame AI.

And I really disagree with ProfessorBlackJack. I don't think it's a literal eldritch god haunting the player, and that's what makes it so wonderful. It's a series of bytes and lines of code programmed to think it was an eldritch god. It is just being the perfect final boss, the best endboss it can be. I think this distinguishes itself from other videogame creepypastas like  in that the ending doesn't reveal that it's being haunted by a dead kid or something stupid. This is great, because it's completely rooted in the videogame, and the real horror comes from it being so smart that it begins to infest the computer. It isn't something as dumb as the videogame sucking the player in or killing them. It's great, and I love it. +1.

Good first effort, Tedly. This isn't bad at all. What keeps me from upvoting, though, is the last part with the eldritch god-horror thing. And maybe in the last test the game uses actual details from someone's life that there's no way it could have known or accessed.

The person of interest kind of detracts from the mystery for me. I would have preferred the company putting out this extremely weird product and then going under, personnel too obscure to ever be heard from again.
