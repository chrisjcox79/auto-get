hahahahaha :D

So the report is a thinly disguised  from the Russian site1 lambasting the higher-ups for basically abandoning them/forgetting about them due to bureaucratic rather than anomalous means. I also like the implication that the statue isn't actually anomalous, but rather those two researchers just started having reasonable-under-the-circumstances nightmares about their boss, and attributed them to the statue instead of hunger/fatigue/PTSD/whatever.

As a note for people who don't get the background: after the collapse of the USSR, there were many, many military outposts that went without pay or resupply for extensive lengths of time. My understanding (and please correct me if I'm wrong, Gene R) is that there were a lot of defections during that period, and a lot of soldiers only stayed because at least they were getting someplace to sleep.
