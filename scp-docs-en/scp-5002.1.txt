5000 contest - psuls entry

14 Jan 2020 06:03

For the SCP-5000 contest, I have written a murder mystery. A skip is neutralised in a locked containment chamber. There's a long list of suspects. Whodunnit?

Fair warning - this is long, and it refers to (but does not depict) sexual abuse. I have tried to do justice to the genre, and to be fair with the information given to the audience. Let me know if I've made the clues too obscure / too obvious - I may be able to tweak this a little for future readers. Similarly, if you think the very last section doesn't belong, I'm happy to discuss removal. Spoilers in collapsibles, please!

A huge vote of thanks must go to Pedantique, who provided several rounds of very useful feedback on the forums. Thanks also to Kid F for their review, and to OBot321 for the vote of confidence! I also have to acknowledge my indebtedness to some of the masters of the genre - in particular Dorothy Sayers and Georgette Heyer, whose work you should really read.

If you like the intrepid investigator, Agent Ellen O'Connor, you may want to read about how she got her start at the Department of Analytics: Exit Interview.

I hope you enjoy this, and please don't forget to vote. Good luck everyone for the contest!

I'll start with the positive: the scope and length here are more than enough to make this a good SCP-5000, in my opinion. Thanks for your comments, planet Jane, and I'm pleased that you liked it overall. I'll appreciate the detailed points you make, and I've responded below.

–block

I really do not think the rape subplot  I don't think simple shock value (which it strikes me as here) justifies its inclusion. I really think you can write that out and make the whole thing a lot more digestible.
附加记录：    

You make the fair point that this may be a failure of imagination on my part. I wanted the SCP to be a threat (because it justifies the Site Director's use as a tool of revenge, and also because the SCP doesn't get much screentime, so needs to be an obvious threat), and I wanted the nature of that threat to be something other than murder (because I wanted variety among my characters, and the D-Class murderer was already there). Choosing sexual assault was probably a cheap decision in that respect, and it felt like it fit the  anomaly in terms of invasion of space.

I'm open to discuss another way of providing the SCP with the same level of threat, and I'll think about it further myself. I don't know that I can remove them entirely without making major changes to the story, as the Site Director and doctor's plots depend indirectly on this SCP's role in proceedings. But let me know any thoughts.

Going farther and involving the Pataphysics Dept. just strikes me as serious overkill, it's kind of the same fundamental problem as ending a story with someone waking up from sleeping.
附加记录：    

As noted to Taffeta above, I agree with this, and I may well decide to nix this ending. But I do have some affection for it, in part for the very reason you identify - this is a huge anticlimax. I like that fact, as a playful subversion of expectations. My first two endings are a typical murder mystery ending, followed by a typical Foundation story ending. The Pataphysics ending is exactly the thing that SCP-5000 herself warned against - it's effectively .

The other reason I like this ending is that I think it adds to the contest theme, and adds some fridge logic to the piece. While  resolves one mystery, it begs more questions (as SCP-5000 also alluded to) - exactly how and when did they substitute the clone, and how could the events as we've seen them play out with a clone in the containment chamber?

I may yet change the ending (and perhaps beef up O'Connor's note in compensation), but I wanted to highlight why I put it there in the first place, just for discussion purposes.

God, most of this is absolutely amazing and I was hooked all the way through. Yes, it's a murder mystery played fairly straight, but is that an issue? Not for me. Today, with one exception, this room contains all of the people who were present in Wing G on the night that Ms Hastings was killed.

Though (and I've checked this) opinions on whether to add a period after Ms do very, due in part to the fact that it's not actually an abbreviation of anything, the majority of sources I checked (and my personal preference) seem to favour adding one. It seems to help with consistency, especially when it's so close to the mentions of  I definitely wouldn't hold it against you if you were to keep it without one though.

SCP-5000 was killed between 1am and 3:00am last night.
附加记录：    

This is the most apparent offender of the minor issues with consistency in how you've written time in this. I have no real preference for how it's written, though maybe look through a little to check it's consistent.

Agent O'Connor: This is the Foundation, doctor. Everyone has secrets.
附加记录：    

This is the one line of dialogue that left a slightly sour taste in my mouth. It's both extremely cliché and overly foreshadowing the end sequence of this; honestly I feel it only detracts from the piece.

I admire your character development, no two characters felt overly similar and none were especially shallow. While I've seen contrasting opinions on the rape part, I feel that the interviewer's emotional reaction to it added a whole layer of depth to the character. I think it maybe went on about one interview too long, though that's not especially helpful as I can't think of one that could really be cut.

As for the controversial pataphysical section. I'll admit that I somehow managed to miss it on my first runthrough, and didn't feel the piece was missing anything at the time. Therefore, I agree with some of the other commenters that it came across as unnecessary and may have dulled the ending for me a little. Not enough to change my vote, but enough that I noticed.
