I have some nitpicks and critiques for this article though:

Follow up with D-1526  D-1526 expressed that, from their perspective, 
附加记录：    

I think there's supposed to be a colon there.

I'd suggest having a blank line between some paragraphs at certain points. An example of what I mean:

D-1527
The device acted on D-1527 as observed in prior testing and presented the number 0 after completing the quantization process. Upon being returned to the room by SCP-5367-1, D-1527’s epidermal layer sloughed off and subject began to seize. D-1527 expired after 14 minutes on continuous seizing and blood loss.

Researcher note:  It appears, after re-watching security footage during Test 4, that a fruit fly entered the space between the aperture and D-1527. Based on the disastrous outcome of test 4, we ask that all further testing with SCP-5367-1 be ensured to have a free field of view between the subject and SCP-5367-1.

This is, on the whole, competently done. There are no glaring tonal breaks (we'll get to the test logs), and I've left with a good understanding of what is going on and what you wanted to get across.

+upvote.

-text

1

The testing logs drag. I found all of them difficult to get through, and they didn't particularly add anything after the first example. I gather I'm supposed to be left wondering about the quantiser, for instance, and what its internal logic is, but I don't, I'm afraid.

Four steps after walking through the door, D-2413, D-1211, and D-2834 all stop walking and form a triangle facing in the same direction. D-2413, D-1211, and D-2834 remain silent for 60 seconds before the research team prompts them with a question. D-2413, D-1211, and D-2834
附加记录：    

The repetition of the D-class make this entire thing difficult to parse. The interview log format for -3 is genuinely clever - but again, it's difficult to parse.

And the descriptions in -2 are weird. It feels like an attempt to balance formal tone with conveying something fantastical, but it ends up being stilted and odd.

The space inside the room appeared to fracture, reminiscent of glass breaking, and then returned to the appearance of the room as it was before activation of SCP-5367-2.
附加记录：    

This is a wild sentence and is exemplary of the overall reliance on  and what is clearly an artistic description.
2

This doesn't feel like a Wondertainment product to me. These can't really be construed as  toy that superpositions the child using it in some horrifying and disorienting manner. But as it is this is almost more of a Factory toy to me.
3

This is relatively minor, but this toy interacts with Quantum Mechanics principles on such a surface level. I do enjoy the Quantiser - I think it's a cute joke and feels fairly appropriate for Wondertainment. But the Entangler and Diffractor, so much less so. . I know this is my bugbear, I do, but there's so much more that could be done with these.
4

The discovery log and blog post at the end are extraneous. You spent the whole article not talking about this teacher, so for them to just show up again breaks the flow.

Hi Riemann,

Thank you for your insight and suggestions!

I have tried to address as many of your tonal comments as possible in the current draft of the article (D-2413, D-1211, and D-2834 becoming subjects, glass breaking to glass fracturing, etc.) but have left the main thread of the tests the same. I received a lot of feedback that the way the logs were written let the ideas become accessible without a defined description of the properties themselves.

I had not considered the Factory model and it had not come up in critique before! I have added it in in the way of http://www.scp-wiki.net/scp-4876 as a Wondertainment product manufactured by the Factory. I really like this suggestion and thank you for it!

Thank you so much for your feedback!
