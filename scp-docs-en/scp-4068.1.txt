This is missing a rating module.

I don't dislike this enough to downvote, but I will say this is dense and difficult to read, especially considering how straight-forward the anomaly is. I feel like with some tweaking this could be a -J. It's an anomaly that forces people to use corporate jargon, and it can be written with corporate jargon, like in the interview at the end. If it's going to be on the main list, it just needs more to it than .

Also, if this anomaly actually has the capability of affecting the top 500 corporations, then it is Keter. That is a lot of data to track and contain. Think of the amount of resources it would take to monitor ALL of the corporate communications at, say, Wal-Mart. Then add 499 more corporations of that size to that list. We're talking about an anomaly that affects the vast majority of the American work force plus a huge chunk of overseas workers too.

On a second reading, it's not that bad. Thanks for taking my feedback into account though. It makes me feel special.

Excuse my language.

+block

–block

FUCKING. NIGHTMARE. FUEL. +1

It is possible that SCP-4068-1-B instances are at least partially aware of their condition.
附加记录：    

Poor performance reviews result in the [DATA EXPUNGED] of employees.
附加记录：    

SCP-4068-1 instances show no apparent signs of discomfort.
附加记录：    

Moving the needle to my bleeding-edge pain points by the out-of-pocket disruptor drilling down from outside the box in a lovely little hotel in the Rockies.
附加记录：    

Mind effecting SCP done fucking RIGHT. The corporate jab is alright but that's the foil for this horror show, it's what brings it down to earth relatably. If you let yourself believe that office suits have actually been co-opted by a memetic virus and are imprisoned in their own minds just remember, some day you'll be old and wearing that suit too.
