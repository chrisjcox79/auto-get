Offhand, fron reading the article, I'd say:

If the epidermis is technically dead skin, could 293's effect take place on your skin?
附加记录：    

No/very unlikely. It's still part of a living being.

If the you have a prosthetic, could you get attached to it?
附加记录：    

Sure.

If you leave two object's with 293's presence, would both of them get effected?
附加记录：    

Possibly/probably. I don't think we have any good tests on it's transmission.

If so, would an effected person get attached to BOTH of them and think that they are trying to compete for attention?
附加记录：    

I think not. I suspect you'd fixate on just one item. No doubt the first few to be exposed to this had a number of items around to potentially fixate on.

Could you become obsessed with part of someone that isn't alive (like carotin or feces)?

I have no idea. I want to say no, but I honestly don't know if it's logic or squick making me say that.
