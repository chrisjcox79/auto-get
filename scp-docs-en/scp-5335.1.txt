Site Criticism Team - Staff Review

19 Jan 2020 22:34

Alright, I promised you guys a site crit once I got around to rereading, so here it is.

So, while the article is definitely better than when I critted it last week, I'm still finding myself rather unexcited by it. The entire concept of Spook, Mack, and Mick being three facets of the same personality is honestly reall cool, and your title tips me off about exactly how they were split by Fuller. That being said, this was just entirely too long. There was so much information here that I found myself skimming by the time I hit the UIU file. Really, the story that you guys tell here isn't all that complex, but there's a real slog of getting through minutiae and retreading old ground that stands in the reader's way. The pacing of this is really nonexistent, unfortunately.

The writing here was excellent and the concept is awesome, but the length really killed this one for me. I'm settled on a novote, but if the writing was any weaker, it'd have been a downvote.

SoullessSingularity, for any questions or concerns relating to this review.

16 Apr 2020 23:13

+Crit

-1

My primary problem with this starts with the Special Containment Procedures, and is the same as TL333s. The first paragraph of those is firmly Description, and it's both jarring and hard to read as formatted. As much as we want to protest that you should read the ConProcs first, the fact of the matter is that most people don't, and leaving Description info up there can result in a very confusing read. Even if you stick to the ideal of reading ConProcs first, their purpose is to act as a teaser and hint at various aspects of the SCP  which is defeated if you give everything away immediately.

SCP-AI webcrawler ΛΞ-207  has been reassigned to trawl for any forum or social media postings indicating the current or prospective location of GoI-233.
附加记录：    

This is an unnecessarily long clause, with two , since forums are social media. Also, I don't really see the Circus advertising on social media?

Due to the anomalous nature of GoI-233's mode of travel, additional predictive analytics have been added to ΛΞ-207's codebase in an attempt to more successfully ascertain where they will appear next.
附加记录：    

As with the previous, this is a bit unnecessarily long and could be easily made more concise. Consider that  has all the relevant information and is half the length. It seems this is a recurrent problem throughout the work. A lot of the times it's just two or three words that could be cut, but it adds up to bog the article down.

As a minor note, Black Highlighter doesn't handle Greek characters well, and they don't appear at the same text-size. It's best to avoid one of the two.

In the event that one or more of the SCP-5335 instances be contained, these procedures should be updated to include the contained instance's archived containment procedures.
附加记录：    

This doesn't need to be said, it should be assumed.

This massive cellular regeneration produces a fully-grown adult human and appears to select physical characteristics at random.
附加记录：    

What  massive cellular regeneration? This is the first time you talk about it, you can't introduce a concept with the pronoun .

SCP-5335 was originally brought to the Foundation's attention after the ongoing mutual exchange of files between the Foundation and the FBI:Unusual Incidents Unit began in 2███.1
附加记录：    

Here's an egregious example of redundancy. You don't need to say  twice in this sentence. You need to rephrase this to only say it once.

Also, what is the point of only redacting the first digit? You explicitly say that further action is undertaken in 2012, so there's literally no ambiguity in the date. Did these files com from a hundred years in the future? Either redact the full date or don't redact the second digit.

Dr. Stewart: You are no longer an Agent of the FBI, SCP-5335-2.
附加记录：    

The dialogue is a bit weak and sounds unnatural. . This continues throughout the article: I'm not a fan of much of the interplay, it just doesn't feel realistic to me.

Dr. Stewart: Ok, McNeal then. I'd like to ask you some questions about how you came to be… like you are.
附加记录：    

This is really rather unprofessional dialogue: a doctor should have the ability to phrase a question better than this. It's just a hamfisted way for the anomaly to get in a joke.

Interview was terminated shortly after that.
附加记录：    

Terribly nonclinical. Just put the Closing Statement after your [END LOG] and drop this sentence: it'll do all the work for you by itself. It's already evident the interview ends by the fact we're no longer in an interview.

Interviewed: Retired FBI:UIU Agent Jackson Hooper
附加记录：    

The second interview is a completely different format from the first. The header information and the Begin/End tags look completely different.

File selections
附加记录：    

All of the images in the image carousel in the UIU format broke and don't display.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

With a word on formatting: I'm very strongly not a fan of the combination of the Magnus Format with Black Highlighter. I don't think it looks good, especially the collapsible link colors.

Further, a lot of the design choices put no consideration towards margins/lost space. Both of the interviews nest the text within three borders, the last of which is firmly superfluous (there's nothing between it and the second border other than the link to close the collapsible). In parts of the UIU files, this gets as egregious as four layers deep when it goes Main Div - Second div. This clots up space on screen. I thought this was a problem on desktop, on mobile it looks like you're using nearly half the screen space to needless gutter.

With the image/page divs, this is also a large problem. The pages are quite compressed on the page, even viewing on desktop. I'd suggest moving the images into the class= divs, which would greatly alleviate this problem, by letting the pages have their full width, rather than the smooshed width they need from the photos.
