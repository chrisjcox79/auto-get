im pretty torn on this, for one i really enjoy the references to 317, but the footnote makes no sense out of and in universe.

rant warning!

can people please just grow up and accept that most, if not all theropods had feathers? dinosaurs aren't movie monsters, they were living animals. they were birds. end of story. there is a numerous amount of evidence of theropods possessing feathers. even trying to piece together a living creature out of just its bones is difficult but please don't use that difficulty as an excuse to make them whatever you want, especially when it should be common knowledge. i know this has little to do with the article but i needed to get this off my chest.

11 Feb 2020 02:35

its not that its harder to believe, it's just that the reasoning behind it kinda makes me mad. it's almost circlejerking at this point. and yeah i don't like 4041 for the same reasons. making a story about dinosaurs building a spaceship is technically feasible, we don't have evidence that they didn't, but we do have evidence that they had feathers. that's the difference. i just think its a bit disrespectful to the people who work hard putting together these animals and trying to get rid of the idea that dinosaurs were big lizards when stories about them not having feathers and making the people who made those discoveries 'wrong'.

4041 comes across as almost cynical. almost like a tongue in cheek story about how the author doesn't like the idea of dinosaurs with feathers. 'dinosaur fans' treat paleontology like a franchise, like making a discovery could be a wrong decision or something that the 'fanbase' doesn't like, but science doesn't work that way.
