this is one of those rare situations where i feel that the gaps in the construction here work in its favor rather than against it. the situation that these people are in is ambiguous, their fate is ambiguous, the skip's actual mechanism is ambiguous. but it's couched in solemnity, and resentment, and helplessness, all of which are portrayed realistically and effectively and combine with the rest to make something haunting. this article isn't a stab in the chest, or a blow to the head, but an ache, and i think it's all the more effective for it.
