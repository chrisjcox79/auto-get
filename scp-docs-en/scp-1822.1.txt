09 Oct 2019 11:53

The Foundation broke a fundamental constant! And is trying to cover it up like a kid with the shards of mom's favorite lamp.

I was shocked to see this was only +5, but then I paused for a second and realized why.

The technobabble is strong with this one, and I love that. Particularly because there is no  involved. The fact that 80/20 shows up so often irl is weird, and more than one academic has made a career of studying some particular instance of it.

But most of the readership here prefer their technobabble ST:TNG style. No more than six sentences followed by a one sentence analogy that a child or commanding officer could understand.

So how to tweak this so that the average wiki-member can appreciate it?

I'd say start by not censoring the ratio itself. The slow reveal will still happen because most people won't realize the significance of the variation from 80/20 until they've read further. I see upthread that you had good in-universe reasons for the redaction, but this seems like one of those times where the realities of writing fiction need to take precedent over in-universe consistency.

I also don't see any equivalent of that Star Trek executive analogy. One more sentence at the end of the description along the lines of , and also underline the punchline of how catastrophic it is when they screw it up later.
