Thanks!

All the technobabble related to physics actually has meaning, although it might describe impossible things but I tried to make it scientifically accurate (as long as you accept there can be structure within quarks, which I think doesn't violate any laws of physics as long as the patterns  so the quark flavour stays the same). The maths is not arbitrary either, the 'Monster Group' is a fascinating real thing.

The containment procedures are somewhat random though I have to admit, especially the part about the protective tiling. I just like the idea of a readily available supply of cheap 'multipurpose' deflection panels that includes a bunch of protective features, just for anomalies that are not completely figured out yet, and it seemed like a good way to drive the point home that resources are primarily allocated to dangerous items, leaving the harmless items a bit neglected.

I might have overused precise numerical values though (I felt like giving the number of bits in the message and the durations was something you would find in a technical description, but of course it glosses over many other stuff that could have numerical values so maybe I could ditch a few more specific numbers).

The document references are meant to show how the two competing schools of thought try to make their revisions hard to reject, and to avoid giving the impression that the Foundation would allow revisions that are not supported by facts But I see how it can be annoying to read. That's how real-life scientists fight each other though as far as I know!

There's a lot going on, I'm not sure what exactly you don't get so I'll try to restate the main points a bit more clearly and with less technical jargon:

The rock happens to contain a quartz crystal which, through random chance, crystallized in very homogeneous conditions. The result is that at a sub-atomic level, all the atoms are extremely similar. In this fiction, quarks (which are subatomic particles) have a chance of containing something similar to our universe at a very small scale (because within a quark, there can be tiny variations just like our galaxies and stars are just tiny pockets of energy in the void of space). I tried to make sure all that stuff is somewhat scientifically plausible, no  involved!

Since an atom can contain a tiny universe, neighbouring atoms that are very similar can all individually generate a universe and the result will be very similar! So like parallel universes or alternate realities. This is what happened inside this rock.

There is an extremely small chance of life being born, and then civilisation being born, and then this civilisation becoming extremely advanced, and then this civilisation trying and succeeding to send a message to an higher plane of existence in a given universe. But since there are trillions of trillions of atoms in the Earth, it was bound to happen eventually somewhere! This rock is amongst the ones in which it happened, and since there are a lot of  of the universe, the message is detectable even though a message from a single atom would of course be undetectable.

This civilisation has a lot figured out about reality, and they understand that beyond their two dimensions, and beyond OUR three dimensions, the plane of existence that is really special and really matters has 196,884 dimensions (this is supported by actual math! There is something about this amount of dimensions which is different and nobody understands exactly why, it just IS, embedded in the way numbers work. Spooky stuff.) Since they're a post-scarcity civilisation that has been freed long ago from animalistic desires and has explored and discovered everything, they want to contact those high-dimensional beings of course. But since they're only two-dimensional and trapped inside a tiny quark, they need US to carry their message across. Of course the only way would be to alter our universe in such a way that it would be visible to higher, presumably four-dimensional beings. So just destroy our universe to make it vibrate in the right pattern, exactly like they did to send us electromagnetic waves!

The Foundation is starting to understand this. Of course this is a , harmless item. But think of all the knowledge it contains! Unfortunately it is quite fragile since the tiniest disturbance has an effect on every atom and obviously the extremely small patterns that are their universes cannot withstand perturbations at our huge scale. Since the crystal is inside a thick layer of granite a few billions managed to survive anyway, but the signal is decreasing and the rock requires a lot of space and maintenance to ensure it decays as slowly as possible to keep recording the message.

The Foundation has a bunch of eldritch abominations to take care of though, and sinking a lot of money into maintaining this boring rock might seem a tad wasteful to some people. Thus two opposite sides arise: those who want to keep studying the rock and learn stuff from an extremely advanced civilisation that seems to have solved the fundamental nature of reality, and those who think the Foundation should use resources to stop Cthulhu instead of keeping a chunk of stone well-insulated for decades (not to mention that everything in the message seems aimed at getting us to destroy our universe to transmit the message to a higher realm).

The controversy is so deep that both sides start using  of sorts (while staying within the rules of the Foundation) to advance their agenda. Since technical documentation should stay neutral, but pretty much nobody amongst those who contribute to SCP documents is neutral on this issue, you're getting a bunch of edits from both points of view within the document (Supported by facts, as technical documentation should! But both  sides are backed by undeniable facts.). I intended the reader to piece together the whole story through those glimpses at the internal conflict that pollute the factual, scientific core of the document.

Thanks!

In retrospect I agree I might have got carried away with a few ideas and tried too hard to make them work together. The whole  thing is not that important, but it started organically and I got attached to it :

I tried to make the  section more intriguing with the backstory about the disastrous excavation process that lead them to just condemn a whole area to stop disturbing it ASAP

I was so glad with the way it explored the  side of managing anomalous items that I doubled down on the attempted realism and tried to develop what decades of containment of a harmless thing would entail

I started to add a bunch of addenda to create a feeling of accumulated changes in containment policies over decades

I got interested in the idea that it would start being neglected, and felt like it would be really different to make the scariness come from the Foundation ignoring important stuff

From then on I needed another point of view to emphasize that the item is very important despite being just a harmless rock

I got caught up into the side-story about the in-fighting within the Foundation, and started putting as much effort into developing both the item itself and the side-story.

So this whole process made me way too attached to the  aspect, maybe I should strongly reduce it and keep it aside for another entry. I'll think about it!
