This is my best writing on the site simply because it expresses a lot of what I want to tell about the Foundation in a simple, streamlined way. The interactions between humanoid anomaly and researcher, the line of treating the anomaly like an object versus a person, mental health, video games, being gay, being trans, etc.. I could (and absolutely can) write an entire article on each of these subjects alone, but figured each of them can make an interesting part of a whole story in one article. Mental health is a big thing for me, something I'm both hurt by and fascinated by. How does one react under certain situations? The whole mantra of showing and not telling applies here, but I wanted to tell a bit to get it across. Heather escapes in video games in a sort of real literal sense, while alive and while an anomaly. It's a good way to escape anxiety and depression, and I feel much the same way.

Plus, any article people will cite it as an example of the SCP Wiki failing, being shitty as a whole merely by existing, being too SJW, or act like immature babies over is a real successful one. It weeds out the people who read fiction for the wrong reasons and don't want to look closer into. Plus, I feel like I've succeeded in creating a character I genuinely love with this. I've never written a character before that I was really into, either merely liking or writing only in service to the story. So, yeah, this is my favorite thing of mine and maybe the best thing I've written.

There's also a contingent of people who hate this solely because it has any association with Gamers Against Weed, to which I say, come the fuck on. The main thrust and core of this article only uses that as a launchpad to run with something else altogether. Anyone who read this, saw the GoI, and downvoted never had an interest in connecting to or reading or analyzing this article on any sort of scale IMO. It'd almost be entirely the same without the GAW connection. The only major things I'd lose are the sense of community and the theme of minorities carving their way out in the world of anomalous happenings, both of which I think provide necessary background to make this more than . It adds a layer to this that makes it more human. I will admit that not every reader is gonna be connected to someone who's trans and gay and is a girl, and who has a defensive guard surrounding that to dictate their actions. But also, nearly everything I write on this site now is filled with LGBT themes, so it's just par for the course if you're reading my stuff.

Dahlia and Andressa asked chat if they could make Mr. MAVG a girl instead. Everyone was fine with it. They made it. This was after some of the Misters1 had already been made. Regardless of what the other lists said, GAW was fine with it, and so it came to be.

If someone wanted to write Ms. Zapatista as a male, well, they could, and they could technically use the same reasoning as above, but it sort of ties into how popular media and culture doesn't have nearly as many girls as it does boys, with how severely underrepresented they are. That's why I made a Mr. a Ms.; because I thought there could be more female representation as far as the series list went. It's more deep than the creators in-universe gave it, because I want to make my writings more representative and less  (she's obviously none of those) than what sci-fi/horror/fantasy writing usually comprises of. I think MAVG's new gender suits her better. The only reason I didn't consider it before was because I didn't feel comfortable taking too many liberties with things that were previously established.

Similar to how only Mr. Hax's list has the Humble Waterfilter Merchant and how that can't be added to existing articles without permission of the original authors, I don't want other people to edit current lists to change Mr. to Ms. unless they are the authors of the articles. Did jockjamsvol6, creator of Mr. Bernie Sanders, left GAW before Ms. MAVG was finalized? I don't know. I don't feel like it's my choice to make. I just know that the list Mr. Ominous made had it as Mr. Mad About Video Games

I did change the list on Mr. Literal Serial Killer, however. None of the names on that list matched the original in the first place so I felt like that list was beyond such a restriction.
