I really like this Scp, but its length makes it tedious, I appreciate that you recognized this and made differences in the words blue but in the end it makes reading a confusing mess, because most of the important words are to most of the public jargon, this makes reading the first one a chore because you have to either google or try and remember what some of the words mean. This makes highlighting the blue words ineffective because by the time you on to reading the new page you have forgotten the last one and the whole thing loses its context. May I just recommend reducing the size because half of the pages, while I can appreciate the work you put into them, do not add anything to this Scp.

Regardless it is a good concept and you definitely proved yourself as a good writer with a large vocab and technical skills especially considering it is your first Scp. Just have a look at some of the more popular Scp's though, almost all of them do not have half as many pages as this one, because unfortunately and I wish it wasn't the case but no everyone can be bothered to read through so many pages that uses jargon they do not understand so consistently. Definitely a +1 though, not often you find a Scp with the main problem being too long problem is being to long.

Man, I really love this. It's a simple concept executed beautifully, with a very believable progression in both the Foundation's understanding of it and their response. I enjoy biology-based SCPs, and this one is done really well. The ethics question at the end - can they wipe out over half of the world's marsupials to enact effective containment - is a compelling one.

The effect itself, of making you see a big scary monster, is kind of simple/bland, but I think it works because that's not really the focus of the SCP, and isn't really relevant to the core message.

Great article, thoroughly enjoyed, easy +1
