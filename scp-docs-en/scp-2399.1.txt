27 May 2014 08:07

This has a nice tone and feel to it but it has two of my biggest problems with SCPs:

First, it relies on handwaving something done by the foundation that would take preposterous amounts of manpower and resources, would be ridiculously unethical, and also still wouldn't work. In this case, brainwashing every single person who has ever heard of the moons of Jupiter to forget the existence of one of them, and then erasing records of it in every single written account going back to the 1600s, three separate times in the span of 20 years. No. That isn't happening, no organization could possibly have that level of resources, and the number of people who would be required to cooperate would not cooperate. The cover-up you describe is utterly impossible on any world that resembles our own, without employing plot devices that make all drama and humanity meaningless.

Second, it's gonna getcha. It's of unfathomable scale, it's from space, we don't know where it came from or why, but like every  other thing we don't understand, it is *gonna getcha*. It's hard to buy that we're insignificant in the cosmos when one third of all objects in space were built for the purpose of killing us, and us specifically  to not get getcha'd by. Hell, looking at how important we are, and how many things are aimed at us, and how something coincidentally stops all of them from killing us, the best conclusion to draw is that YHWH or someone an awful like Him actually exists and He's got our backs. Probably not the implication you want to give in horror fiction.

I would cut everything about it eating moons and the Foundation covering it up. That's completely unbelievable and eye-rolling. Jupiter has a dickton of minor moons  But as our technology gets better, people will be able to see that the moons are vanishing, and the Foundation doesn't know how to deal with that.

Second, I'd take out any reference to it being out to get us. No targeting the third planet. Third planet isn't important, has never been important. It's getting orders, we have observed the orders correspond to changes in its behavior, but maybe we cannot decipher them. We're afraid what they might be, so we made the BARRIER array; but it wasn't by unanimous verdict  and its program when cut off from orders is to assume it is under attack and neutralize the source of jamming.

Also, how is it getting relevant command from 3 million light years away? Those are 3 million years old; even if it did crash more than 6 million years ago and transmit, why bother sending orders that will be responses 6 million years out of date? Furthermore, why have it RELY on up-to-the-minute commands that are six million years out of date  why wouldn't its actions be preprogrammed? You can go for a big time-scale, but 3 million LY is totally out of line. 175 LY or thereabouts is still unimaginably vast to a human mind, but it makes it such that Central Space Control is just now responding to what was happening around the time Cassini first spotted it. That creates a nice little parallel. It still doesn't quite solve the problem of it relying on constant new orders that are hundreds of years out of date, maybe that whole angle just has to be scrapped.

MAYBE, it was transmitting sensor data back to its home planet, which was only broadcasting back periodic messages just to aid in targeting where to send the sensor data too, and we figured out that it couldn't see Earth yet  and if they might send something to go check on a probe that stopped communicating.

Due to application of amnestics, and an ongoing misinformation campaign, current information maintains that Jupiter has only ever had 4 moons. This program will remain in effect indefinitely.
附加记录：    

This is breaking all credibility I could be building up to it. Just one of these is far too much. Amnestics erase memories, they don't erase the fact that anyone who looks at the Jovian system is going to see its moons with commercially available courtyard telescopes. We can't hope to take them all down, the amount of personnel required would be absurdly huge. It's an Ennui-5 Protocol in all its glory, being implemented every time a moon goes missing. It's too big. : /

Otherwise, I don't dislike it. Space things are nice, and this one has a potential (and accidental) connection with an article I'm currently rewriting for the main list, so okay. Neutral vote for now. May come back to read it again.

Edit: Also, the entire BARRIER, Gigas and LEGIONNAIRE programs are simply excessive. This SCP is probably staged in the not-so-far future, 'cause even if every government in the planet bankrupts itself over and over again, we wouldn't be able to send a mass of projectiles such as Gigas or LEGIONNAIRE require. BARRIER well, I'm not certain of how many satellites BARRIER implies, but I'm thinking less than a hundred. In any case, it's too many, I think. Dunno. Keeping the Neutral.
