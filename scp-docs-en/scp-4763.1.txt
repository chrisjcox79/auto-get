This is a perfectly valid criticism. While writing, I mentally flipped a coin over whether to have the crux of the article be the reveal of pathos, or the pathos itself. It metaphorically came up tails, so I went with the latter.

Also, I've been writing a lot of  it's a departure from most other conceptual scips that either I or others have written, which tend to explain these things in more detail.

Either way, it was an experiment, so I expected some negative reactions. Thanks for taking the time to look it over, and doubly so for posting in the discussion page; I appreciate comments much, much more than upvotes :)

23 Sep 2019 23:55

In my mind, this only works as a piece of fiction because it's short. If I strung it out longer, the concerns of people like Pedantique would become much more prevalent  the absurd quantity of pathos only works once. If I strung it out more, it would become even more predictable than it is already.

I understand that it's fairly bare-bones and short, and it's perfectly fair to want some expansion on the anomaly; I don't think that makes it unfinished.

EDIT:  Also, just for the record: I wasn't originally aiming to write this quickly. I just wanted to pin the idea to metaphorical paper as soon as possible. As I was finishing I realised , so I posted. Speed and brevity were both happy side-effects of the initial inspiration, but not the ultimate goal at any stage.
