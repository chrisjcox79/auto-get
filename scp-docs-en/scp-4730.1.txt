SCP-4730 is a long SCP with an interesting premise that, ultimately, collapses under the weight of several, minor issues. Most of them are lapses in tone which make believing that this was written by professionals difficult.

+Examples

-some

All containment areas at Site-70 are to be kept at least 300 meters away from any door requiring level 4 or higher security clearance and have at least one 50cm thick steel wall obstructing direct line of sight between them.
附加记录：    

These exact numbers, 300 meters, 50cm, never appear again. They are arbitary. If a wall is 299 meters away from a door does containment fail automatically? No. It's the X by X by X containment cell issue but in a different format.

A MTF Nu-7 battery of at least 120 members are to remained stationed at Site-70 in compliment to its standing armed guard force of 1000.
附加记录：    

Again why is 120 the magic number? Furthermore, anyone reading this wont know what's contained (because that's blacked out below), but will know exactly how many armed guards are on site by default: vital intel for most saboteurs.

SCP-4730 is an anomaly that affects a randomly chosen level 4 or higher security card at Site-70 every 2 to 40 days and activates upon use on a level 4 door.
附加记录：    

So there are leveled cards AND doors? If containment is an issuethen why keep using the system that causes issues? Why not use that MTF battery to screen clearance levels in different ways? Why not find an alternative method to the card+door system that causes hostiles with gauss weaponry to invade?

SCP-4730-3 instance covers Researcher Mochizuka's mouth with their hand. Researcher Mochizuka is escorted through various corridors before being deposited in a stark white interrogation room marked 75T. Mochizuka is handcuffed to a metal chair in front of a metallic table and left alone for two minutes and 20 seconds before someone enters the room and sits across the table from Mochizuka. Person is of masculine appearance and designated as Interviewer.
附加记录：    

This doesn't read as clinical, official notes. This reads as pure narrative description, as though from a novel. The room isn't white it's 'stark white.' Why do we need to know that the interviewer is of 'masculine appearance' or that the furniture is 'metallic?'

You better damn be, I do NOT enjoy being woken up by a guard ordering me to my computer with no clue as to why.
附加记录：    

The dialogue suffers as well. This doesn't sound like one of the people responsible for managing global Foundation operations, this sounds like an unprofessional kid who's never experienced a sudden emergency or meeting before, which is impossible considering Foundation operations in general.

The concept is promising. The execution was not, unfortunately.

Sorry, this is a downvote for me. It was a neutral vote awaiting some improvements, but it seemed to get worse the further I went.

First, I would remove the second picture entirely. It in no way looks like footage from a security camera. It's also clearly a picture of a US military exercise, using equipment that would clearly be outdated considering the nature of the -3 MTF instance.

+block

–block

The method of accessing our universe makes no sense. If they've done this dozens of realities, why is it that they can only enter ours through a randomly selected level 4 or higher keycard at one site? That seems a pretty poor and uncontrollable method of entry.

The properties of the Gauss rifles are never explained, but I'm not sure you have your principles correct. Popular conception of a Gauss rifle has a lightweight piece of metal accelerated to high speed. Conventionally this would mean that there is no such thing as a  Gauss rifle as the projectiles are typically theorized to be several factors smaller than modern rifle projectiles.

I really didn't like that I felt like 4730-3 could have been replaced with Fallout's Brotherhood of Steel seamlessly.

There were many elements that were thrown in out of nowhere that were disorienting. I have no idea what was going on with the giant crosses. The evil space baddies mining everybody's reality for resources is very cliche.

The whole lot with the captured researcher was bewildering to me and I don't know what I was meant to get out of it. I'm also skeptical of the preservation of all of this conveniently important dialog over a video stream that is sending data to a different reality.

The -3 interviewed can't decide if he's a member of a supreme reality, a thoughtless bruiser for hire, or an imbecile who doesn't understand the most basic functioning of the organization he works for and proclaims dimensionally superior.

The portrayal of the O5 council largely felt like a throwaway gag with little to no thought into how members in that position would react or even much research into their communication in other well established tales.

I like the idea of a decoy site containing an anomalous entity that attacks it for the purpose of securing the SCPs. I absolutely hate the method (since when is blowing things up containment? Why does this nearly omniscient Foundation only enter one site at specified doors? Etc.)

I do like the idea of a competing Foundation reality that has antagonistic goals to ours and is possibly under the persuasion or a power they themselves don't understand.

As a courtesy to our readers on mobile devices, please collapse long posts. ~Zyn

I think this SCP is all over the place. My downvote is in hopes of it being removed and going back to the drafts forum where I think it should have spent more time and far more criticism.

If you're attached to this universe, my suggestion is to strip away all of the logs and spend some time picking it apart. Write it as an SCP and leave out the section that's trying to be a tale. Put your work into establishing your baseline, create a really solid foundation (pun intended) and create it as an SCP. If that's accepted by the community, that's when I would begin trying to expand the story via tales

I also saw at least one instance of SCP-XXXX-3 that should be replaced.
