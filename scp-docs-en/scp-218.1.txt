Re: I have a problem with this.

18 Jul 2015 23:03

I fundamentally disagree with the concept that this is a iazy rewrite, because I think that does a disservice to those who do rewrites and cleanups.

As the captain of the Rewrite Team, I can confidently state that one of the most difficult things in doing a rewrite is balancing how much you need to maintain in the original article and how much you can add of your own style. Far too many rewrites of classic articles try to take the original material and spin it off into some piece with so much of an author's personal style that the original article is barely present at all.

This is delving somewhat into personal opinion, but rewrites should not be a space for the author to completely dismantle an article and make it unrecognizable. Rewrites are where an author examines the best parts of an article, absorbs the original's finest points, and then uses those to highlight the article's strengths. The concept of a  only exists when you can't appreciate an article written with a classic flair.

Also, there is absolutely no reason for an SCP researcher to ever make logical jumps just because it  That's just not how science works.
