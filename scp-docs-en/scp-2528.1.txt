My understanding of what's going on here:

+Spoilers

-Spoilers

I don't think they're 'literally' computers, so much as the fact that their current structure easily lends itself as a comparison to one.

Essentially, they're not so much an entity from the previous universe so much as the leftovers of an entity from the previous universe. Basically the prior entities embody concepts and bits that simply can't exist in the current one. Most of the prior entities reduced themselves to some very core concepts that could exist, but in doing so lost all their prior knowledge and identities, but at the core retained the essence of themselves. (It's suggested that they're now 'us' in the sense of stuff that's alive on the planet)

But the ones here were too prideful or fearful to sacrifice that much of themselves and tried to fit themselves in via a loophole. Exceepppt, they didn't to a great job and in fitting through said loophole, great chunks of 'self' were annihilated to fit. It's also why they're so weird and basically a kludged together bunch of identities that exist in the interaction between Pandas and Bamboo, and why they're so maimed. But it's also why they remember the previous universe.

Note, I do think the prose is too purple and somewhat impenetrable - but that's my understanding of what's going on.

Elaboration

–block

I can see two main approaches to conveying an alien mind through dialog, I call the Kosh and the Kosh2 methods:

Kosh2: Simple, direct statements. Yes. No. Irrelevant. Go here. Do this.

Alien is seeking simplest common reference frame. Communication is objective oriented. Alternately, alien has a machinelike mind.

Kosh: Metaphor and purple prose.

Alien is trying to find common ground despite have different perceptions. Alternately, alien has fey mind. Or is just dicking with you.

-C wants something, yes, but their mind is more on the fey side of the scale. They don't fully understand themselves what they are talking about. Purple seemed the way to go.

One of my original ideas, which can be seen in my sandbox, has a much more personable -C-1 with a more histrionic -D providing the purple bits, with -C-1 explaining as best it could. That ended up being too blunt.

Cybernetics, dude- it'll blow your mind. X3

Basically, anything that can receive input, process that input, and respond in some way is a control system: any network connecting enough control systems to achieve some level of recursive feedback is a cybernetic system. Network enough of those together, and you have a computer. There is technically no meaningful difference between  its simply a matter of words: whether you mean the box on your desk, or the network of connections that it holds and links to the global network of connections operated by organic networks of connections.
