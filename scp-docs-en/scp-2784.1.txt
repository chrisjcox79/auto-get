15 Oct 2016 16:54

I think there's a lot that's good about this, but it doesn't quite gel for me. The fact that you're really subtle about the suicide is appropriate and realistic, but it also muddles the story and leaves a lot of room for confusion.

Also, overall, it's very in the note.

I guess what I'm getting at here is that the only thing ambiguous about this entire article is the fact that it was a suicide. Everything else is spelled out, and I think that choice hurts both sides of the equation.

-1

Edit: I should say that you also include a lot of hesitation on the Foundation's part about whether the apparition or body are, in fact Andrew's, but again, this is a mystery that doesn't enhance your story. There's never any other alternative and already a very clear explanation, so it's a lot of hemming and hawing over nothing. Is it a realistic amount of skepticism for the Foundation to hold? Yes, but it's not interesting to read.
