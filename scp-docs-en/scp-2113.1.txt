27 Nov 2014 18:58

SCPs that force historical experience have been done before. There really isn't anything that feels mysterious about this one either: somehow it was found, it came into Foundation possession, we learned everything about it, and then we re-buried it(?) No containment measures have been undertaken to secure the ghost, which feels a little lax for Foundation procedure, and the effects are just really predictable.

There was one thing I did find a little creepy/interesting was the account of the fossilization experience.  was a bit interesting, as one rarely considers what fossilization would feel like, and the ambiguous descriptions of scraping and deserts definitely caught my eye. It might be better if the SCP were centered around this theme.
