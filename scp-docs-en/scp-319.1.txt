Here's a rough analogy:

Imagine our universe is a pool of water in a crater. What we don't know though, is that the crater is at the top of a mountain. As far as we are concerned, the universe is stable and comfortable, but in fact, the only thing keeping our universe from flowing down the mountain is that it can't get over the rim of the crater.

Right? So this guy managed to drill a hole in the side of the crater, and our universe started to leak out. The vacuum bubble inside the machine is the portion of the universe that has already fallen down the mountain. Unfortunately physics works differently at the bottom of the mountain than at the top, so everything that goes into the bubble get destroyed.

Very late to the party, but wanted to put my grain of sand. Mind you that this is all real physics.

This is considered within the multiverse hypothesis.

A small variation on the properties of matter, or a slight variation on the amounts of the ingredients of the universe (read matter, radiation, dark energy) can result on a universe with entirely different physics or, well, chemistry.

Imagine this entropy thingy inside the structure. Entropy is fancy word for saying that everything wants to go to a lower energy state and stay that way.

A heavy atom being put together takes energy, therefore, at some point, it will decay (see uranium).

Our universe is theorized to be electrically neutral, as in for every electron, theres a proton. This means that a non electrically neutral state of the universe would take energy to sustain, and as entropy means that everything wants to be in a plain state with little or nothing going on, its thought that protons and electrons are stable. This means that atoms themselves will likely be stable forever, as in, they wont break down into free protons and free neutrons.

+block

–block

A  is hypothesized, it could radically change our understanding of this, buuut theres not enough evidence that it happens.

But now lets imagine another universe. Let's say that in this universe, for practical purposes, say it has nothing, nothing but space-time itself. No matter, no radiation, no nothing. Therefore, we can assume that this universe's lowest energy state, is one where also nothing  exists.

Right now, if such thing happened, it would be called a 'false vacuum', because its 1 tiney tiny step before reaching its absolute low energy, which then would be called a 'true vacuum'. A true vacuum will always expand, sorta because of a . Like if we were communist and you had an iphone (analogy for the iphone being a zero energy state) and no one knew, but as soon as i find out (break containment) i also want one too and as more and more people find out, they also want one too. All want the same.

The space inside this SCP is a 'true vacuum' which is being contained by some magical means. This means that i you cross that barrier inside this structure containing that space, your particles also want to join it and reach a zero energy state, efectively obliterating you from existence.

That also means that if the SCP there overcomes its containment mechanism, then all the surrounding atoms it is now touching will want to reach that level of zero energy to, meaning that the universe will be effectively obliterated.

In fact, The fear of creating particle accelerators like the LHC was in part because its possible to create a true vacuum inside of it, the thing is that it has not happened (we can tell because we are alive) despite cosmic rays reaching and hitting earth at energy densities orders of magnitude more powerful that makes the LHC look like childs play.
