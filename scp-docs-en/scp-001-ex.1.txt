Dr. Good Boy, or: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love my ROM

01 Jul 2018 04:02

000 INTERNAL SERVER ERROR: UNCONTAINED BAD BOYS DETECTED 

You are AK9. You are a Good Boy. A universe full of uncontained Bad Boys approaches.

NOTE: This fills you with

D E T E R M I N A T I O N

AUTHORNOTES

–block

Thank you very much, Mr. Roboto

For doing the job that nobody wants to

And thank you very much, Mr. Roboto

For helping me escape, when I needed to

This is a story about that time the Foundation accidentally trained a neural net to become a wizard.

It's born from Peppersghost's obsession with predictive text, my fascination with neural nets in general, and our overwhelming desire to tell (what I hope is) a compelling story using pretty much nothing but the narrative equivalent of Excel spreadsheets.

Also? It's an SCP-001 that's classified as . So, there's that.

Special thanks to The Things Left Unsaid) for helping us realize that this absolutely just had  to be an -EX.

And finally, special extra thanks to botnik for being the best little predictive text generator a writer could ask for. Who'sha good bot? Whooosha good bot?! You are! Yesh you are!

A few final notes:

Perceptrons are real. They're not a word I just made up (even though they totally sound like a word I just made up). Think of them as really, really early versions of the data-structure that would eventually become neural nets.
Otherwise, I took a lot of, uh, liberties with how perceptrons work (they're actually not really that good at the sort of thing they're being used for here).
We left a credible timeline out of this skip on purpose, because we wanted the reader to be able to construct their own timeline. That being said, I noticed a little too late that SCP-3199 actually was only discovered in the 2000s. Alas!
The equations I'm using are Stirling's Approximation (thanks again, Taff!) and so long as there's a part of them censored, anything should work.
A lot of the text in this entry was actually produced by botnik (and just lightly edited by us). So maybe botnik deserves collaborative credit; I dunno!
This was supposed to be posted 3 months ago. Peppersghost and I decided to wait until the contests ended, so we could have a nice, clean period without anything big going on and then everybody decided to drop an SCP-001 at once. Oops. Hopefully, this one won't be the one that breaks the camel's back.
One final kicker: At its heart, this is a story about how the Foundation got out-Foundationed and the anomalies got out-anomaly'd by something that isn't sapient, and probably isn't even anomalous .
