Maps are fucked up. Going in a cardinal direction is a lot different than going in a  around the Earth because it acts really weird at the poles. It can only be said to get to the North Pole at all because the Foundation decided that [insert distance] is close enough to count and rounded it off.

Let me put it another way  a straight line on a Mercator projection map gives you a consistent compass bearing, which is what -B has. The Mercator projection takes up more and more vertical space the closer you get to the poles, and cannot actually show the pole.

You can't proceed North, East, West, or any combination of the three from the North Pole  the only option is South, and then you have an arbitrary number of Souths to choose from. Maybe -B will stop near the North Pole and die because it can't keep going 34 degrees North of East in circles so tight. Maybe it'll just reverse course the way it came after a while. Maybe it'll pick a new South-esque direction and go that way. Maybe staying put long enough would build up enough energy to make the world asplode.

What it really boils down to is that the Foundation only knows THAT it moves and THAT it maintains a compass heading, not WHY it does those things or how important they are to it. So they just say they don't know what will happen.

Out-of-universe, we might surmise that since 2804 is basically an idea that got dislodged from Fly's mind, it travels that way because Fly thought of the direction between his house and the Wilkinson house in terms of a compass bearing. Some other things he probably thought included that decks should be sensibly constructed and stick within property lines. When 2804 couldn't adhere to those, it flipped out and made fucked up anomalies. Will it make anomalies when it can't go 34 degrees North of East any more? It seems quite likely.
