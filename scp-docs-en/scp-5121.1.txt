“A great Shadow has departed, and as he listened the thought came to Sam that he had not heard laughter, the pure sound of merriment, for days upon days without count.”

J.R.R. Tolkien, The Return of the King

附加记录：    

This is an SCP about religion, but not in the way(s) you might expect.

This is an SCP about childlike faith, and the struggle to retain it amid a vortex of depravity. It is also about what happens when you have serious, proximal encounters with that depravity in the communities you trust, and then in yourself.

Whoever you are, and whatever you believe, I hope this feeds you one way or the other.

Inspired by:  Bunyan's The Pilgrim's Progress, Marilynne Robinson's Gilead, Lewis' The Great Divorce, and various real-life experiences.

+Spoilers

-Spoilers

Paul and Silas are students who enrolled into the SS together in 2012. 4 years later, a disillusioned and hurt Silas posts one SCP spoof to the wiki each midnight, as an iconoclastic anger-venting strategy. These spoofs comprise his (and Paul’s) SS-related creative output over the years, and thus divulge his entire backstory.

Paul finds out that Silas is airing dirty laundry on the Internet, and intercepts him on the fifth day by hogging the SCP-5000 slot, urging restraint and forgiveness.

Meanwhile, in the Foundationverse, the Makarios High School undergoes events that are analogous - but not exact - to Silas’ experiences and thematic concerns. Now normally, when an author posts to the mainsite, their anomaly is realised in the Foundation’s reality (as per the SAS proposal). But Silas’ authorial intent is not to populate the SCP wiki with new anomalies, it’s to let off steam. And this steam is realised as - or “transmuted” into - a rampaging, socially-twisted Keter. A Keter that the Foundation can’t catalogue properly, because its containment files - ordained by whatever is in the Database - are Silas’ spoofs.

Paul’s intervention on Friday succeeds in consoling Silas and, in the Foundation’s reality, halting the perverse charity drive through the eagle’s sacrifice. When this fifth and final spoof gets voted off the wiki (SCP-3309), all firsthand data about, and physical evidence of, the anomaly vanishes. In real life, Silas cheers up, and gets a special gift.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

I didn’t write the WD part with the intent to require prior reading, but YMMV. Thanks for highlighting that.

Do let me know if there’s anything I can do to make it more obvious (if it needs to be)!
