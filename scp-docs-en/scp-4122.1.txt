I've gone back and forth on this one. On one hand, the effect is relatively neatly explained (although a better mental image could definitely be painted) and the ending logs are good fun (even if I didn't understand them). In addition, the testing logs didn't feel like they were longer than they needed to be and elucidated the effect relatively well.

However, my main issue is that there's no real hook. The Containment Procedures have little substance to them, and reading the description made me think that this was just going to be yet another SCP-786, even if the concept was different. I had to force myself to continue on to the actual good part.

I'm gonna leave this with a hesitant -1 and see what the rest of the site thinks of this.

I see what you mean. The central conceit here is that it’s a fun little curiosity with a horrifying twist ending, but until the twist hits all you’ve really got is “fun little curiosity”. It's designed to lull you into a false sense of security, but boring by design is still boring

I can't imagine that heavier foreshadowing of the ending would help, though. O5 overreactions are a staple of poorly thought out newbie articles: I certainly don’t want to make it look like one of those.

If I can come up with a good idea for an early hook I may yet take the whole thing back to the sandbox, but for now I'll leave it up and see whether other people have the same problem with it you did.

I think use of redactions is more down to preference and what your personal headcanon is as to why the Foundation database is available on a creative writing site. Since other articles have the user inputting O5 credentials and reading all the Things Too Terrifying For Humanity To Know, I personally don’t see the point in redacting much at all in these articles. The audio logs are transcripts of the “high pitched sound” emitted by the object every time it creates a paradox. The sound itself has to be artificially slowed down and pitched in an audible range, otherwise it just sounds like a squeak.
