This is one of my favorites so far in the Con. It really takes this one idea, this one particular idea, and gives it just enough definition and backing to make it meaningful. It doesn't drag on or throw in unnecessary information or side-plots to add more substance than needed. No fluffing, if you will. It would also be quite scary in reality, having this compulsion extend beyond the object itself and have it manifest even when just referring to the object, and beyond that being perfectly able to erase or destroy any trace of the thoughts you project under its influence, but simply not wanting to.

The cliche nature makes the fear dulled, but it's still effective at grabbing your attention and making you think what the object would make you say. +1
