My issues with this one:

I dislike compulsion effects as a general rule. I feel they are a way around doing the heavy lifting of giving characters and events proper context and motivation, and are sort of a cheap way to a tragic/sad ending. I can expound in much, much greater detail on this point if anyone wishes.
I'm so very tired of the metacommentary on how heartless the Foundation is. The business with the thing not liking being addressed by a number felt forced and unnecessary. Also, given that you have a Foundation agent just straight walking into a harmful anomaly and going , they're going to draw some line of formality about names? Really?
For that matter, why are staff put into this thing? The containment procedures state that only Foundation personnel can enter, but there's no justification for that. Which makes it read to me as purely a contrivance for that last interview.

This is very clearly an overt attempt to deliver a cat-based feels missile, which makes it feel manipulative and also means that the narrative is constructed with only that purpose in mind, leaving aside matters like how much actual sense it makes.

I feel like the concept is good, but the execution and characters are were this SCP falls flat. For instance, if they know they cannot leave in some circumstances without someone accepting a 2279-a, why would they not bring a D-class with them, and risk two agents lives? Additionally Timmons and Fredericks dialogue feels a bit lazy, it seems more like the dialogue you would see on a buddy cop detective show than a cold and clinical interview. I could understand maybe on the side of Timmons, but I still feel like they would have plenty of training in how to handle a situation like this, or at least know to keep completely quiet and allow the lead agent to do the talking. If this agent was so inexperienced, I don't believe they would have been brought on to any humanoid containment site, no matter the object class.
