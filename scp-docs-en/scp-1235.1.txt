Fold

24 Jun 2013 09:47

Reminds me a book I read as a kid, The Boy Who Reversed Himself. The titular boy is the newest member of an organization dedicated to protecting the secret of moving in n-dimensional space, and protecting those who live in lower dimensional spaces. Essentially, every n-space world has an (n-1)-space bubble/portal thing inside of it that is its own separate world (down to the 1D world and up to some vaguely defined upper limit), and every world's organization needs to protect those that live in their bubble (and thus those in the bubble's bubble and so on), or risk their world being destroyed by the higher levels as vengeance. Beings can reach into the bubble and perform 'acts of divinity' as you mentioned, and being who learn how to move along the (n+1) axis can exit the bubble, though they are still 'flat' on the (n+1) axis and are also tiny in comparison to regular beings of (n+1) dimensional space. The characters had to wear special glasses that were capable of distilling the information of the 4D world into 3D visuals to interact with their 4D counterparts. The background conflict of the book was that the house in 3D space that holds the 2D world in its basement is under threat of being seized under eminent domain laws and being destroyed (along with the 2D world and then our world by proxy) in order to build a new highway.
