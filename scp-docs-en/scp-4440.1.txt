This honestly feels like there are three different, underdeveloped ideas placed together in the same article. They serve to ramp up the scope and potential of the anomaly, yes, but shatter my suspension of disbelief in the process.

+block

–block

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

First, we learn about anomalous Nerf guns with a 1.2% chance of increasing the expansion of black holes when a consumer chooses them over another brand. Interesting, to be sure, though I was hoping to get more from that, as I was admittedly skeptical when I first saw this article pitched. But after one paragraph of that explanation, and one brief paragraph detailing discovery, the focus suddenly shifts.

Next, we learn about an alien civilization, wholly dedicated to the mass production of Nerf products (as marketing slogans printed on Nerf packaging explains), that somehow fights an intergalactic war(?)  with Nerf guns. One sentence explains how this all connects to the main anomaly:

Approximately two weeks after the 2013 Christmas season's completion, packages of Nerf guns began to print cognitohazardous phrases with effects similar to SCP-4440 when read.
附加记录：    

So, just reading the packaging expands black holes now  the focus shifts again.

Finally, we learn about a massive, 44-million-kilometer-diameter mystery object (with a height of at least three or four times that, judging by the picture) that uses conventional rocketry to escape the gravitational pull of a black hole millions of times the mass of the Sun. It accelerates above lightspeed, attempts to somehow stop the Andromeda Galaxy by ramming into the center of the Milky Way, and ultimately destroys itself. That's all Quite a bit to accept, and we learn this in a single paragraph! After this, it's revealed that the Nerf guns were constructed to halt the Andromeda galaxy all along, and the aliens admit defeat.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

This all shatters my disbelief, sure, but to be frank, it's fair to say my disbelief doesn't matter, since the Nerf guns are an anomaly anyway, and the article doesn't exactly motivate questioning.

So then, with that aside, I take issue with the article's structure. This is flash fiction, yes. But the sheer number of concepts and the way everything is worded slows down the pace and leaves me more confused than fulfilled. My confusion, specifically, lies in the following areas:

The Special Containment Procedures, which poorly explain things, aren't relevant to anything beyond the Description.
The BONZAI event, which has little-to-no connection to anything preceding it, is muddled in its wording .

I suppose I also take issue with the mood  which is troubling.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

I didn't plan on rambling so much, so as a whole, this doesn't work for me because it (A)  has a deceptively high number of moving parts despite its length, (B)  doesn't capitalize on any single mood or tone, and (C)  doesn't make much sense, in part or as a whole. I see other people enjoyed this  and that's fine! I'm a bit too particular with my tastes, I think. So I'll leave off my rambling with a suggestion: With a piece such as this, try to ensure the focus/scope is tuned carefully, so that the piece has a higher impact on the reader as they read it, and, leaves a stronger impression after they complete it.

EDIT: In hindsight, the meaning of Addendum 2 went over my head entirely, but I think some of the points I raised are still valid.
