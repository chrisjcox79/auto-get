I've been ruminating on how to phrase this post for the last ten minutes. I honestly don't know how to feel about it.

Similar to Ihp’s vote, my feelings on this are conflicted, though my problems lie more with the structure of the SCP. In terms of stuff I did like, I think the idea is very creative, and it’s well-written. I know some may have a problem with the Pataphysics Department stuff, but it never got too “wink wink, we’re not real” for me to not care about it. Part of the problem of Pataphysics is that, at some point, if you’re reminded too much that it’s all fictional, you just stop caring. This neatly side-steps this by focusing on the people between narrative dimensions, which allows for the reader to actually care about the semi-fictional entities.

My issues lie in how the skip is laid out. Firstly, the Description reveals all of its cards too early for my tastes. I’d rather the description at the beginning describe SCP-5875-1 solely (along with at least one example of an SCP-5875-1 instance besides just the last one). Then, after the description, we see the final note, then the first SCP-5875-2 instance, followed by a description of SCP-5875-2 and the remaining instances. This would be a more engaging narrative than just describing everything in Description, and it would allow you to show a bit more than tell.

Speaking of SCP-5875-2, while they are well-written, I’m not sure they escalate in an interesting way. At a certain point, I was just like “yeah, I get it”, and I don’t really have a specific understanding of SCP-5875-A culture. I’d rather the logs be in a more linear order, with earlier logs perhaps hinting at the presence of powerful entities in later logs. I’d also maybe like an insight into SCP-5875-A culture and daily life. Maybe one of the logs is a common robbery, and we learn about how SCP-5875-A stores work or whatever. As of now, they’re all a bit samey.

As I’m opposed to the no-vote, I’ll give this a downvote for now, but I think this has great potential, and you are a way confident writer, so I think you can make it work.
