Giving it teeth isn't bad, but no one's feeling afraid when they read [DATA EXPUNGED] here, they're just feeling unsatisfied. Everything in the article is pretty whimsical, so dropping in a box with [INSERT FEARS HERE] won't work. You can't just tell the reader that they should be feeling creeped out, you have to give them something to speculate on.

If you want to maintain an implicit mystery about what happens, have the Doctor get dragged into the van by an unseen force, and then the Van driving off. Maybe insert cliched banging and screaming, or how the attempt to retrieve the doctor failed. If it's something weird, like the driver (!?) getting out and doing his thing, or a ray gun popping out to turn them into ice cream, or make them violently decompress, then you should mention these things as they're important. At the very least, mention some Class D's being deployed to mop up the ice cream/blood/bloody ice cream so that the reader can figure it out for themselves.
