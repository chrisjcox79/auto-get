09 Nov 2014 19:55

Mmmm, didn't like it.

How people acted inside the rave felt too far fetched. From mysteriously protecting the DJ to not reacting to the presence of guns, which could easily start a panic where human stampede effects could happen. Though all in all it was never clarified what guns are these. Handguns could be easily concealed. I recommends further describing the equipment. It could be easily passed off as mental suggestions, but (as of the time of writing, seeing how Soully is still upgrading the skip) article clarifies nothing anomalous happens to the people themselves.

This line treats something that should be assumed of a rave as if it was worthy of mention. I recommend to simplify it to something along the lines of 

This line implies that people drink more alcohol and drugs than normal in a rave, but how much is not pointed out anywhere. Please clarify. It also leads to the following:

The anomaly is lackluster - if the creature is supposedly magical in nature, given that it used to do magic stuff before becoming the scravecrow, one would have said a rave with such anomaly would be much more crazier, however nothing truly anomalous happens beyond magical disappearance of objects. That feels like wasted potential to me. Worth of mention is the fact that everything dematerializes in front of people, yet nobody reacts. Even for parties where people is commonly high, it's ridiculous to assume none would suspect a thing.

The descriptions of objects and actions are done on an overly formal, verbose manner, which end up being more of a bore than a summary of the events. By writing it like anything regarding the rave is rare or strange, it feels like a soccer mom (with no intention to insult) that has only heard of raves wrote the procedures. I don't think the foundation researchers would be that naive/old fashioned.

The logs themselves feel too cliché. Researchers and MFTs, which we should remember are specially trained to deal with the SCPs they are assigned too, act as if they are completely unprepared to the setting they are in, which treads into the cliché of the socially inept research (It could be another cliché, it's the first one that comes to my mind). Then there's the whole ditz teenager stereotype on the hands of the girl. Dialogue and description of events on the logs felt really ugly. There's also the fact that they are called interviews, but interviews are not that from what I know, those are an event's sound recordings, not a premeditated interview. There's probably a better word, but I can't remember it.

The last log however, I liked.

I think that's all without copypasting the article to my sandbox and making a sentence by sentence analysis. Sorry if I was too rude.
