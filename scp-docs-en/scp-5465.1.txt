Don't skip leg day, you're hindering our ascension into psionic superbeings.

The image is a plate from Gray's Anatomy, which is in the public domain. I got it from https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gray432_color.png.

This was an absolutely amazing set of containment procedures, and I spent a while grinning at it like a madman before I got started. I hope the description was worthy of it.

Bonus Commentary (dead authors make exquisite corpses; the below is strictly paratextual)

+Open

-Close

The Nietzsche reference is, of course, Zarathustra's discourse addressed to the despisers of the body.
I made minor edits to the containment procedures, mostly to align the jigsaw puzzle pieces between the parts. There was no appropriate MTF that I found, so I went with the obvious name, . I think the only spiritual change is that special audio was originally used to force the hapless containment sacrifices to skip leg day, and I made it part of what gives the leg-day skipping of such a small group its efficacy.
The quadriceps femoris, or , is of course a very real muscle group which you may have an instance of in each of your legs. To my knowledge, all of its functions, including hip flexion, are non-anomalous in real life. This doesn't mean you should skip leg day.
Authors here have, in the past, advanced the idea of SCP-internal journals and referenced the titles of articles within; in my view, these are real scientists and use a preprint server, not journals. The specific scpXiv paper is an easter egg, but not a good one; you'll be disappointed.
The . Arguably, putting it in a footnote after a few already-technical paragraphs is a terrible idea. On the other hand, I like it.
Agrippa's Gym worked towards the alchemical great work of protein-heavy workout elixirs, naturally. They were very bad at alchemy, so no one cared (including them, really; it was mostly a group bonding thing). They basically stumbled on this lightning rod by chance. They aren't actually good at parakinesiology. They are very good at being a dedicated group of anomalous-active exercise buddies.
Why do the former Department of Parapsychology want to kidnap and enslave these gym weirdos? Remember, these people were deskbound nerds whose world has suddenly been upended. They are in dire need of good personal trainers.
Naturally, the Department of Parakinesiology are careful not to breach containment; they worked out how to align their not-yet-ripped quads to a secondary muscular gestalt under their control. In a grand irony, they did this using actually-competent alchemical broscience, which they worked out in probably a couple weeks. To be fair, they read some good alchemy papers on scpXiv.
Don't skip leg day.

Shame about the poor rating because I honestly think you have a fun idea here, this just needs some more polish. Here are some suggestions for if/when you rework this

1: You may want to make this slightly longer. I don't think it needs to be that much bigger but I do think there's a little room to expand.

2: Give us a bit more info on what SCP-5465 actually does and what the 'paraphysical capability' part means. You also may want to explain more on the gestalt part.

3: From my experience SCPs that try to introduce a new GOI in one go rarely work. You may want to remove the Zarathustra's Gym part and replace it with more info on SCP-5465. (I think in this case the big problem is that introducing Zarathustra's Gym introduces a new variable and plotline when the SCP is almost done).

4. To expand on that read through the story and cut out any extraneous details that don't add to the core narrative.

5. Make sure you check out the guides and get some feedback in IRC or the critique forum. I'm still noticing some SPAG (spelling and grammar) issues.

Anyway, good luck!

I'm going to mirror some similar comments here and say, ironically, you didn't  only half a routine! The con procs give a great hook, balancing critical information with a touch of intrigue and humor. Then, the description (somewhat) clearly lays out the premise, and it's a good one!

The problem is, after the first climax of redefining paraphycology to parakinesociology, it just falls flat. Give me something!

This article doesn't go anywhere, which is all the more disappointing for it's great premise. Please, take this back to draft, and make it a complete story.
