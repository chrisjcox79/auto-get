I have the same problem with this that I do with a lot of  articles. Namely, that much of the enjoyment and understanding that is derived from the specific ways in which the story is told and uses its medium. Stripped of the specific story-telling context, and not adapted to the medium of a SCP document, it loses almost all of its value as anything but a reference. Just as I wouldn't be interested in reading The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari, The SCP Article, I wasn't particularly interested in reading this.

I will say that I enjoyed the dreamy quality of the recovered footage, but the article overall did little to nothing for me.
