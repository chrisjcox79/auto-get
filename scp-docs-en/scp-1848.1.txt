07 Feb 2015 16:42

The second instance is me referencing a style of Native American myth which has the trickster let someone succeed (usually at something 'superhuman' or incredibly difficult), and then, delights in the failure of the next few. The really famous one that comes to mind is when Coyote told a tribe that he could raise their sons for them overnight. They were skeptical, but they gave him an orphan to try anyhow. He goes down to the frozen river, cuts a hole in the ice, wraps the baby up in cloth, and dunks him into the river. Leaves him there for a day, then comes back, tugs the rope, and out jumps a fully grown, handsome, smart warrior. All the women in the tribe rush to do the same thing, and they drown their babies.

This is sort of a really dark version, but nevertheless, it's the pattern I was seeking to emulate. Almost all of the instances of 'appearing to people' and 'causing trouble' are based on Native American trickster tales.

The only exception to that is Dr. Clef's entry, which I included to  got his shirt stained.

We do need to use use 'style' and 'tone' to mean what they mean in the real world; otherwise the linguistic drift allows them to mean essentially anything the user wishes, which is confusing for writers who then have to decipher that feedback.

It's like the drift with our accidental redefinition of 'hook' to mean 'literally anything interesting or entertaining in a piece'. The actual meaning of 'hook' is 'something in the beginning of the piece that fascinates the reader and 'hooks' them in'.

I've seen endless confusion and hapless flailing on what people mean by  or all sorts of other words. That's what motivates me to point out the error here.

Tone is the work's attitude towards the subject and the audience. This is the meaning of the word.

Why is it important to talk about it here? Because this is precisely the kind of SCP that ends up getting the shaft because it's different from what we're used to. This page is absolute bait for taste-based critique framed as non-taste-based.

In this case, it won't get the shaft because Troy's an admin and a top writer. Another writer might not be so lucky.

Also, this is more aimed at others reading this page. We already know what you mean because you did clarify, so you're cool.

That said is seriously awkward and should probably just be rephrased.

I probably shouldn't have been so picky when you called that particular sentence  to be honest, that's mostly me being a pedant.
