Mental age is an actual, quantifiable, concept in psychology. Basically how ones intelligence score results compare to an average person of a particular chronological age. The issue isn't with mental age, but with chronological age.

Presumably what we are taking about with 4469-A is her chronological age as assessed from her physiologic age. In other words, how old her body appears to be. They could get s measure of tht parameter from her telomere length.

Telomeres being repetitive sequences found at the ends of our chromosomes. They are how organisms with linear DNA genomes avoid the problem of 5' ends (also called the end replication problem).

+problem?

-content

I was going to describe it, but there are lots of good explanations out there so I will just include a few some

https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2819049/

https://courses.lumenlearning.com/boundless-biology/chapter/dna-replication/

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ohm-avnDYcM.

Bottom line is that telemeres get shorter as we age to the tune of about 25-28 base pairs a year and they could calculate a body chronological age from this data.

I do have to agree that the month and year is far more specific than is possible or reasonable given the error inherent in these assessments, of both mental age and chronological age.

Borrowing from SimpleCadences's suggestion, I would rephrase is this way:

UPDATE: SCP-4469-A has exhibited accelerated emotional development and mental maturation since containment. At the present rate of psychological development, her mental age is predicted to align with to her body's chronological age (estimated from telomere length) in the early 2030's.
