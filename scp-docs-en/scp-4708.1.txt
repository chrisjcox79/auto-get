This was thoroughly disappointing in terms of a celebrity-centered, comedic SCP. While intruging on its face, it quickly devolves into the simplistic gimmick of .

It seems like a missed opportunity to truly capitalize on the absurdity or surrealness of it all, ala SCPs such as SCP-4844, and I feel the piece suffers for it.

I believe it'd benefit immensely by truly leaning into its own insanity and developing the narrative a stone's throw beyond what's currently on display.

Ultimately, it's not quite creative enough for me to personally enjoy.
