Novote.... for now

26 Jul 2020 21:08

I really want to upvote this SCP It’s very well written, every single word makes its own contribution to the overall narrative, which made this SCP really difficult to put down when I started reading. I also like the use of images- they are really well incorporated into the exploration logs, as opposed to having simply been pasted into a pre-existing block of text (which I believe to be the cardinal sin of image use).

However when I reached the end, my first thought was ‘is that it?’. This SCP gives very little information about the anomaly to the reader. In fact, there is so little information here that it can even be debated whether this SCP is actually anomalous or not- while extremely unlikely, many people disappearing within a small area could have a perfectly mundane explanation, as could all the other weird things mentioned.

I get that leaving the reader in the dark about certain things can have a positive effect, but there is so much information missing here that it’s difficult to work out what this even is. That may appeal to some readers, but unfortunately doesn’t work for me.

Novote, but I constantly find myself coming back to read this, so I may change my verdict in the future.

06 Aug 2020 05:15

If you're going to provide no punchline besides  the following would make the story pop:

• A good reason why the pheonomenon was discovered in the first place, with details clearly indicating that the weird stuff/bad things were a component of the thing being studied, giving the reader a clear understanding as to how investigation began in the first place.

• If there are weird or unusal spaces, explicitly state what aspects of the space defy physical laws or don't.

I can't stress that second one enough. Rooms don't have to be on blueprints. If a house has a ton of un-enterable rooms, you'll notice the unused space if you live there  or even if you just visit it. If the house defies the laws of physics, that's something you need to state up-front, then describe the investigation into the same. If pipes do not have a clear function, there are completely mundane ways of tracking their layout and such should be described. If a substance is unidentified, it should be analyzed and the results should be given because that's the point of a report. It's hard to be made unsettled by a demon house when the incomplete report of its researchers seems far more absurd.

Downvote.

I suspect you're conflating what an SCP article is (a piece of flash-fiction) for what it simulates (a scientific report). Those familiar with reports of this nature might notice these oversights, but  not reproduce a 'genuine' report.
