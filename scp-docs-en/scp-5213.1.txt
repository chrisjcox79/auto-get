19 Apr 2020 19:15

The historical fiction here is a lot of fun, although I feel like the logs in the middle of the article go on for a bit longer than they needed to. I also found it distracting that a French aristocrat would be handwriting in English, wouldn't it make more sense for the handwritten sections to be in French and have English translations?

After the strong start, by the end of the four logs and the bit about the Watcher I was growing less and less interested, and the ending didn't do much for me at all. This lacks the character and fun that made 3862 work as well as it did.

I think if you consolidated the information presented in the notes to be maybe one long letter, with an English translation to French text, along with further consolidation of the last three log sections to keep the nice bits of visceral description for the watcher, the action of the log and the bubbling soup of the ending, it might be enough to carry a whole article. As it stands now, there's too much filler and not enough character for this to stand on its own feet.

Whether you like it or not, history is on our side. We will bury you!

mostly in agreement with roget on this one - i really liked the beginning and ending (save for the redaction, which i'll get to in a moment) but everything between that was a slog. you could condense the letters into one or two longer ones, and i don't really feel that the apocrypha contributes anything of narrative value to the reader at all. the cssmith in me also wishes you'd used a better background for the letters, it's a bit hard to read crossed with the cursive font. I will say though that the ending log was very tight, very gross and fitting. that said, the redaction is very plainly purely for narrative purposes and has no real in-universe sense. that's not to say that it needs one necessarily, it just feels a bit paper-thin to the reader when its clearly for dramatic effect. overall though, good stuff.
