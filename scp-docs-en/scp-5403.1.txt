I'm ambivalent on this one. To some degree, I do find the premise of Laraske being distressed by all this personal stuff coming from 5403 an interesting one. Unfortunately, when it comes to actually conveying that distress, it either doesn't stay long enough for me to feel enough sympathy (see: Laraske's notes in the first addendum being sidetracked by something about the redacted SCP or more unrelated fanfiction shenanigans) or overstays its welcome (see: the interview with Laraske in the second addendum).

This is a hard balance to strike, I'll admit: indeed, finding a balance between sidestepping Laraske's distress and overly delving into it while also keeping in mind the format of an SCP article is definitely a tough one. However, there are some main pieces of advice which I would say would make the piece more enjoyable. Particularly, having a better lead-in to Laraske being distressed by 5403 (as Clef x Bright shipping, while somewhat funny, doesn't do the best job at leading into Laraske's storyline) or giving Laraske's emotions some time to breathe instead of fragmenting them into researcher's notes would give me some room to empathize with this character a lot more.

Because of all the above, I've downvoted, but there is a lot of potential with an idea like this. I just think that its emotional centerpiece could have been executed a lot better.

I think my main problems with this piece ultimately come from the framing.

As I said in IRC, it's quite obvious what you're attempting to hit for; for the reader to recognize how strange and disturbing 5403's actions are. The SCP ultimately falls flat in a couple of areas in that regard, for me at least.

The length and lack of characterization of Laraske besides  It reduces the emotional connection to, in essence, a bit piece that you attempt to hammer home with the final collapsible, and due to this, I feel really nothing towards it because of this one-dimensionalness of Laraske.
The main conflict doesn't feel like a convincing portrayal. In my opinion, yes it is written well; the issue is that once the sort of  of the art and language is over, it becomes a bit of a slog to get through. It doesn't feel as if it's getting particularly worse up till the very end, where it goes from 0 to 100 really quickly and leaves no room for breathing. That motive and idea you're aiming for is hidden beneath the exact same framing and word choice you use to do humor just above, and it makes it difficult to see your message throughout the entire piece.
As barred said, the actual emotional portrayal of Laraske is iffy; stays too long, or needs to be fleshed out.
I don't have a problem with the topic you're attempting to cover, but merely the method of how you go about it; as stormbreath said, it's very, very hard to have an article pull off an impactful story when it says  in the very beginnings, and this article never recovered from that dissonance until the end, where it did so quickly, and unconvincingly.

There's certainly potential in this, but the strange pacing and lack of compelling characters reduced a piece attempting to tug at the heartstrings to an unfunny slog.
