I have issues with the conceit that each would react in precisely the same way to stimulus excepting those that involve their immortality of choice. All the +1's, this is excellent.

The more I thought about that part of the SCP, the more I liked it in particular.

My headcanon for this story solves Kakroom's concern (at least in my opinion - they may disagree) by saying that all instances of 3477 were anomalous from the start. In particular, I'm thinking they're not exact copies, but are instead imperfect and incomplete. They all independently try to mimic what the real Harold would have done. In a way, maybe they're both not very good at imitating the real Harold, and a little too good at it. They talk in unison because it's a perfect recitation of things Harold might say, even though they're behaving in a way that's completely unnatural since the real Harold would never have been confronted with dozens of duplicates of himself.

This is motivated by additional headcanon that goes along with some other stories around here that say that immortality isn't actually possible, and that every attempt to become immortal always has some undesired outcome, despite never actually granting immortality. Maybe the 3477s can only gain  because they're artificial in the first place. Perhaps the duplicates were created and directed to undertake a variety of techniques for immortality as part of some experiment directed by someone else (maybe even the original Harold, on a quest to gain immortality for himself in a way that actually works without being completely awful to endure)?

Man, I was all set to enjoy this as just a bizarre slice of weirdness but the last three logs just didn't click for me the way the logs of the instances did. It made all the disparate entities feel artificial, their interactions felt kind of forced.

I was expecting something to escalate, maybe like what SCP-1454 did with giving identical clones a fresh personality, but instead it just retreads what's already been established for another thousand words and then calls it quits.

The note at the end is very generic and it frankly feels like this article lost what was making it unique and original about halfway through. I love the idea of the former Austrailian Prime Minister being an anomaly but apart from the altered versions this just doesn't make the most of an awesome concept.

It could have been brilliant, but for now it's just a major letdown. Downvoted but I hope this gets reworked sometime. I'd love to see a version of this that fixes its flaws.

I've made a lot of the minor grammatical phrasing revisions you suggested, thank you for those.

I could link 2736 at -19, but I tried to balance the number of purely original entries with those that were derived from GoIs/other SCPs. The concepts are similar, but not exact, and I want to still have some purely original entries.

I'll consider removing -34 from the table of entries, and seeing how that would flow.

As for the escape attempt, it was originally intended as the progression of Harold's story from the interview: I thought of the interview first, then decided the best place to take the story would be them escaping.

I really like this story. It made me hypothesise that whatever he did to become immortal opened up a wormhole to 34 alternate universes and all the other immortal Harolds fell into this one. Kind of like a wormhole but a funnel to capture all quantum possibilities.

There is some fun quantum choice stuff going on here. At the point he did whatever magic he did, that congregated all the quantum choices into one reality and dumped them into our universe.

Dunno if I am explaining this right. Imagine playing roulette and performing some quantum funny business, so that suddenly 37 roulette tables appear and the ball lands on each number possible. Table 1 lands on 1, table 2 lands on 2 etc up to 37. Or opening up Schroedinger's box and finding 2 cats, one alive and one dead.
