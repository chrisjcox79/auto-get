Rounds definitely convey the full force of the payload in my head canon. (Even grazing.) This skip violates laws of physics.

RE: detection, SCP-2283-A definitely had a unique chemical signature and thus you can train dogs, or calibrate sensors, to detect it. I also imagine the ordnance itself in the form of SCP-XXXX assembled looks  unusual. Perhaps Foundation EOD personnel know what to look for. The resemblance to regular ordnance is very close.

Concealed bombs etc. obviously are undetectable. I'd assume the Foundation intelligence units have caught on to specific threats of more than *just a bomb.* is a terrorist using anomalous ordnance just gonna make the standard threat? Maybe. But likely not.
