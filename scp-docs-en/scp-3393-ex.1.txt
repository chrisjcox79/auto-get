31 Jul 2020 17:16

I'm going to radically disagree with this take. Interpersonal relationship issues get thrust into professional settings all the time. In an organization like the Foundation, I'm sure it happens even more frequently. When security clearances make an already niche sector of the work force even more isolated, it is common for interpersonal relationships to form.

I think this is an excellent take because it 100% felt like an upset and self-absorbed person was using Foundation resources to try and blame anyone else other than himself. Would this result in disciplinary action? Absolutely it would. Does that mean that it wouldn't exist? No.

Frankly, this article makes me realize that there are likely lots and lots of these kinds of things, and Capt. Kirby just put a human face on the thing that often gets forgotten.

The Foundationverse has people in it. Not just monsters and the inhuman humans that fight those monsters.

Kirby, this gets an easy +1 from me. Thank you for writing it.

03 Aug 2020 14:48

While I do understand that this requires a bit more suspension of disbelief in some places, I think you may have missed 2 things about why (at least in my opinion) this fits the format:

+block

–block

So, first of all there is something that could be considered anomalous at play. I may have been a little vague about it, but I intended to imply that Rivera did in fact wipe her own memory. So, since Verity is noticing what may appear to be anomalous symptoms, it makes some sense he would frame this in context of a report. Of course, by creating a report like this there’s already some denial at play, but it’s not like he’s actually experiencing normal communication issues. Besides, submitting an HR report for non-anomalous refusal to talk to him would require him to admit to himself that she’s doing this to him on purpose.

As for Frie’s comments, these are her writing down her thoughts while reviewing the piece for Verity (As an aside, my head canon is that she only reviews the piece because Verity asks her to). But none of them make it through at the end. Like, she removes all of her venting at the end because she realizes she shouldn’t be using the document for her passive aggressiveness anyways.

At the end of the day, it’s totally fine if the format ruins it for you. Honestly I feel bad that the framing impacts your enjoyment of the story that much but I feel like to tell the story the way I want to, this is actually the best format to use to both let the reader see through Verity’s twisted view, while also giving Frie a voice to refute him.
