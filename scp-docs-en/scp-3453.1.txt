This article needs rewriting for clinical tone and language, for example:

SCP-3453 then jumped up in the air, performed 3 loops, and moved erratically for 2 hours before going down a sewer drain.

This shouldn't be written as a first person perspective if no one from the Foundation observed it, it should take into account that this data was gathered from interviews with students, so even just adding 'Students observed' and changing the tense to reflect the change in view would be a big improvement.

Later on in the same paragraph you refer to the student who had the original orange juice as the 'wielder'. No one says that someone wields a carton or bottle or OJ they are intending to drink, maybe if they were going to beat someone to death or something, and that's still stretching it.

The article reads like one big run-on sentence, it's just basically SCP -3453 does X, SCP -3453 Y, Z and seems to exist only for the sake of the joke implied in the article title, which isn't enough to justify the existence of a skip without some meat behind it. The 'Safety Dance' is an example of an article that did it well.

-1 pending a rewrite.
