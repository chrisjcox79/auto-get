Weird type article, with a 'haunted house' feeling, even though it isn't. It was written essentially to give the D-Class a hard time. Explanation is given below, but clues are available in the article itself.

+spoiler

-hide

The child, SCP-3607-A, has reality altering capabilities. It manifested itself very recently, prior to the events of the article, and resulted in the disappearance of his family, when he got upset. He doesn't have full control over it, and he thinks there's something wrong with him, such as a split personality disorder.

He blamed himself and sealed himself inside the house.

Hope you like it.

Edit: Here's a more detailed explanation as to what the clues were,

+explanation

-text

Bedroom:  The kid created this spacial anomaly to trap himself in, out of guilt, after he caused his parents to vanish.

Corpse:  It's one of his family members.

Fluctuating Hume readings:  The indicator that they're dealing with a reality bender, causing various anomalies in what is perceived as reality. Unlike other reality benders, his fluctuate at random intervals, which cause the electrical appliances and temperature to drop randomly, as well. This indicates that he does not have complete control over his abilities.

The [REDACTED] part implies that reality is severely bent out of proportion, to the point where it is uncommon for reality benders. That explains why the second D-Class found himself inside the first's twisted guts that merged with the piping, and existed in a  where the researchers couldn't find him.

The Note:  It could have been done better, yes, but the original story was that the kid is suffering from a split personality disorder. His  side doesn't because it's constantly fighting to keep the bad side under control. The note in this version is simplified, because originally, I just laid everything bare, and I wasn't sure if that was too direct. The note is written in the kid's handwriting to confirm to the reader that he's the only one alive in the house, thus, the one bending reality.

Disappearances:  The rest of the imagery serves to paint a picture of what could happen when reality bending goes wrong, or if the bender has little to no control over his ability to alter reality.

Hope that helps to clarify what the clues were.

The article could be improved upon in terms of conveying the story, yes. It was originally much longer, where there were investigations, and an interview with a child psychologist, but I felt that it didn't quite fit in, because it was too long.

In the end, I took it out, and simplified it. True, the leeches and the corpse are simply red herrings intended to give the article a 'haunted house' feel (the corpse is one of the family members), with no real contribution to the story. Ultimately they were just byproducts of what I intended to be the main story.

The note is very ambiguous and vague, but it's that and the fluctuating hume readings that suggest it's a kid with some form of disorder and unconscious reality altering effects.

Sorry if I couldn't convey it well enough.

I'm wondering if I should put something in the article that was in the original. I left it out because I was afraid it would give away too much. Basically, at the end, the head researcher is supposed to say something like.

You know what the readings indicated. With a hume level of [REDACTED]1, half the population of █████ could wake up and find themselves inside D-10221's guts. It could happen; this thing is unstable. As is, it is contained within SCP-XXXX, probably of its own doing. Our job is to make sure it keeps itself contained.

-Head Researcher ██████.
附加记录：    

It's redacted because a reading that low is atypical, and indicates an unstable area of reality. They want to understand it better before releasing the information for general viewing. Also, they don't want a GOI such as the Chaos Insurgency knowing about it, should an information breach occur.

If it helps the reader understand that the entity is a reality bender that can't control his effect on his surroundings, I'll add it.

Footnotes
1. Maybe .15-.2?
