I am really not feeling this one. It feels very disjointed - so much of the set-up seems to be around  (c) he is taciturn. All of which is simply confirmed in the Final Investigation.

If a sapient taco is really something that needs to be written, I think this would benefit from a re-write with the taco's human identity being established immediately upon containment. That would remove a huge amount of verbiage that has no great effect, and allows you to concentrate on an old man's thoughts on aging, death and what effect being turned into a taco has on those concepts.

+1

I don't know anything about the  that is apparently reflecting badly on this skip, but I believe any feelings about the author or previous authors or whatnot should be ignored. This skip either stands on its own, or it doesn't. To me, it definitely does.

+whatever

-whatever

Good:

I definitely felt an emotional connection to a sentient taco. Any work that can do that deserves some praise.
I think part of the appeal is how good of friends the taco became with the researcher.
I think I caught the subtle hook: The Taco isn't M. Tanish, the Taco is M. Tanish' emotional half, only. Or maybe it's only his negative emotions. M. Tanish tried to put these emotions into a taco and throw it away.

Bad:

It was a little bit long-winded, and maybe a little too much  in the logs.

SCP-2848's remains were incinerated in accordance with its final wishes after approval from the O5 Council, and its ashes were spread over the beach at Cape Cod.
附加记录：    

That doesn't seem professional. I'd think they'd keep them secured.

It was recovered when the servers, who heard the voices coming from the trashcan and interpreted it as a demon, contacted a local priest for an exorcism, which in turn alerted the Global Occult Coalition, who in turn put SCP-2848 into the Foundation's care for study.
附加记录：    

That doesn't seem right. Who immediately jumps to  when they see a talking trash can? Maybe radio transmitter? And I don't think the GoC would hand this off to the Foundation, I think they would incinerate it.

When I started reading this, I thought, since this was an article about a sapient taco, that it was going to be lighthearted and cute. Then I read it.

my feels

y u do dis troyL

In all seriousness, though, I have to say this one really got to me. The fact that there's no explanation for how 2848 got the way it is, in my opinion, really strengthens it and turns it into this Kafkaesque story that's bolstered by the emotional connection with 2848 that the interview logs manage to establish. And, honestly, I don't really have a problem with the way that the Foundation treats 2848 - after all, they are still human beings, and this is a (presumably) fellow human being who's been caught in a pretty nightmarish situation. I give this a +1.
