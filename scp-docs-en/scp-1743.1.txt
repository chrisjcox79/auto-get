People have been talking about tone, and because I want this to succeed, I'm Soulesshuman-ing this where I see tone issues.

+block

–block

the sort usually used for a studio audience television program.
附加记录：    

Eh on the phrasing. Be careful with comparisons in clinical reports.

hose employed at ██████ Studio Center similarly have no memory of how it came to be
附加记录：    

Take out similarly.

which tends to happen due to a  effect
附加记录：    

I've said this before, but I dislike when people use quotes as an excuse to break tone when there is no reference to the individual who said quoted line. The rest of the paragraph needs trimming. I understand on what you want to get across to the reader, but be more subtle about it. If I were to rewrite this:

SCP-1743 enters its active state when a human subject walks onto its main stage. The main stage can only be entered from the viewing area; areas backstage are not accessible through any means. After entering SCP-1743, the subject will not be able to perceive the object or any off-stage activity, instead perceiving that they are going about their daily lives. To date, it remains impossible to remove the subject from the stage through physical means or otherwise. (See Addendum 1743-A) [I'm not happy about this rewrite, but it's not up to me to write it.]
附加记录：    

I would then go on to explain the effects of the object, instead of combining the activation procedure with the effects. I left out the 'luring' effect, mainly because it's cheesy and way too many objects rely on this to be horrifying.

which appears to be a standard television studio camera
附加记录：    

I think it is  a standard television studio camera. It's not a laser-shooting marmoset that appears to be  a television camera.

Rewrite:  SCP-1743-2 are humanoids that appear onstage during SCP-1743's active phase. These beings appear to look and act like people the subject would encounter in it's day-to-day life. However, instances of SCP-1743-2 are without defining facial characteristics such as eyes or a mouth. Subjects under the influence of SCP-1743 do not appear to be aware of the difference in facial structure, and converse with the objects normally.
附加记录：    

I don't have too many issues with the rest of the article. My rewrites are bad, and I wouldn't use them, but I was trying to demonstrate how to condense the information and avoid being too wordy. I still think this should get a chance.

Fold

13 Jul 2012 16:07

People have noted the tone issues above, and I second the concerns raised there. Conceptually, I'm having issues with this. So people get pulled onto the stage and live their normal lives, except with a mean laugh track? It doesn't seem like the SCP goes out of its way to kill people, but that's what seems to be happening in a very short time period. I guess because of the laugh track?

I see two concepts going on here; malicious laugh track, and creepy faceless people. The first I can see working in some manner (it reminds me of the  the second is very creepy, but you'll be very hard pressed to do anything with it that doesn't evoke Slenderman, fair or not. I don't really see an inherent connection between the two.

I don't like the compulsion element. At this stage in the site's development, I feel like that unless the object really needs something like that, things what compel you to do stuff are lazy writing. I also don't care for the  thing. It's impossible to look back there? Like, there's some sort of crazy, inpenetrable force field around it? We can't x-ray it? We can't poke a hole in a wall somewhere? I would prefer the backstage appearing normal when it's observed, but people who get hauled into it disappear or something.

This isn't terrible, but there's too much wrong in the execution, and the concept feels kind of weirdly mashed together.
