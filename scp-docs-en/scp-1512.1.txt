Fold

31 Jan 2013 18:12

Incorrect terms there. 1km3 = 1,000m x 1,000m x 1,000m = 1,000,000,000. 80km3 thus is 80,000,000,000, which is a cube about 4,308m per side.

But you are correct in your math. 1 cubic meter of water weighs 1 metric ton. Thus 80,000 metric tons of water takes up 80,000 cubic meters, which is a 43.08m cube, yes. But the volume is .00008km3, not 80.
