+Thoughts

-questions

With a whole planet to explore, I wish there was more to the exploration log (or, better yet, more logs). Rather than having the team find something huge and significant in what I read as the first building they even entered, build up to that. I want to read something in the logs to support the speculation in the findings report: have the team discover some sites they think are places of worship, or dwelling places, or workplaces. Let them find some texts and artworks that they can document. Or, at least, some suggestion of why that specific site was chosen for the first exploration would be a nice touch: is it the largest, or most intact city on the planet? The one with the most robot activity? Was the  detected there before the ground exploration?

I wouldn't want to bog this down with too much pseudoscience detail, but I'm very curious about how some parts of the planet's non-detection/non-interaction work. From the fact that exploration occurred, we can see that it has some kind of gravitational field, but that it cuts off at some point before it would affect earth. Yet, it's somehow tied in orbit to earth. I'd find it worth mentioning, at least, that a goal of the exploration is to look for machinery or anomalies that could be the source of the gravitational suppression, or used in keeping the planet in orbit. Re: the non-detection effect, from my reading, I interpreted that as working on a psychic level, making the planet ignorable, rather than truly invisible. Is this how I should read it? and, if so, how does that affect scientific instruments whose path the planet crosses? Does the  to be dismissed as noise, or smudges on the lens)?
