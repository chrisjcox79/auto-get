Very few things manage to incite dread properly. You need to be cognizant of the possible ending, and there can't be any subversions along the way. As a consequence, the plot often feels too obvious when not done correctly.

I am, pleased to say, however, that this piece handles dread wonderfully. We are made aware of what is to come, and yet, we remain curious enough to keep reading. That alone, in my opinion, deserves an upvote - and the article fails convince me to vote otherwise.

Great work.

I enjoyed this and upvoted right after reading it! But after thinking about it for a little while, I'm afraid it might fall into the  trap. It isn't hard for a reader to infer what happened here, and it seems like Agent Barnes has at least an inkling, too. But it seems like a criminally negligent lack of action in the Foundation's response!

The anomaly MIGHT be unique to Samantha McArthur. But what if the source of it is Kevin Cosniak? Or Derek Thompson? Why not have an undercover agent embedded to keep an eye on them?

Otherwise, there might be a string of unremembered corpses in bathrooms following one or both of them through college.

What amos is trying to say here, and what Barnes is picking up here, is that he doesn't think Cosniak or Thompson were the source of the anomaly, but that they were just lucky.

If I had to point a finger towards anything, it'd be the town itself. Them going to college would actually make ot less likely for something like this to happen, whoch i think the Foundation understands.

The Foundation has a good understanding of what happened here. They don't know how it started, but they know what appeared to be the impetus. Due to the socio-antimeme, no one's gonna talk to them, and its self-containing. So they've administered a few amnestics, and took away the body.
