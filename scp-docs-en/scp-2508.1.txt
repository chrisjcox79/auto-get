03 Mar 2016 18:04

The hook was enough for me to settle in for what I expected to be a fairly lengthy supplemental section, but the payoff didn't really match the setup.

I really like the general idea of a document that's only meant for the in-universe researcher stuck in the anomaly. That opens the way for a non-traditional execution of the SCP format without necessarily going down the gimmicky format screw path. And the mood of the house is a nicely uncanny, sort of unsettling vibe, without being over-the-top pointlessly creepy.

The note from the O5 was kind of underwhelming. The dude sounds a little stuffy for someone who's not above getting shitfaced and taking a drive through town, doubly so for someone who's writing down his thoughts on a weird transdimensional trap house because he's got nothing else to do. More than that, though, you refer to a record archive and a supplemental section recording the thoughts of multiple occupants. That would have been a good opportunity to tell an extended story; not necessarily one that explicitly details what's going on here, but this could have gone much further down the rabbit hole than it did. I frequently criticize the  attitude that we all have lately, but this really could have been fleshed out.

I had initially no-voted this one because I liked how it began, but this just feels too incomplete for me to support. The article falls considerably short of the quality that it teases.
