Hello hello!

I’m Kara from the Site Crit team!

+Doom

-...

- “To date, only one episode of SCP-5123 has ever been seen” Huh? This is a little confusing when paired with the statement after that “with variations ranging from slight dialogue changes to altered guests and sequences occurring upon each subsequent airing.” Is this trying to suggest that this is a re-run that is changing every time it airs?

- “the show will end and be replaced by the channel's usual scheduling.” So what you’re saying is that this airing just cuts into the programming that was previously scheduled? Did people notice this before the Foundation stepped in?

- “Carter:Too bad you couldn't get a job as a paper reader, huh?” There needs to be a space between the colon. Along that vein “SCP-5123-1: No, not as funny as that.It felt like a natural ending to my career, I suppose.” There should be a space between the period and 'It'.

Final notes:

This SCP is quite interesting, but I’m a little confused by the ending. What did he do? Kill the original -1 who dared to go against him? That part felt a little foggy to me and I couldn’t exactly understand what you were trying to get at beyond the kidnapping of people and making them play these characters. It’s a great story, but it feels like a little more clarity would be useful.

Great job on this, and I hope to see more from you in the future.

SoullessSingularity, for any questions or concerns relating to this review

+block

–block

The video log is (almost entirely) on-point.

The dialogue is fine, though -1's complete breakdown personally comes off as a little too on-the-nose1 and a touch on the narmy side.

There are a few minor details in the log that I think contribute very well to giving this a little extra flavor (audience members wiping their eyes/weird stain/a novel type of Eye Scream/Dave) and overall I think there's a version of this that could make a lasting impression.

My primary issue with this is that it spends several paragraphs very thoroughly describing what is essentially the entire plot without nuance regarding its trajectory. The (more effective) video doesn't grow or progress from this point; it simply showcases it.

Footnotes
1. Doubly so with the footnote - that their identity can be verified does nothing but belabor the point.
