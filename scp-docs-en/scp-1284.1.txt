I tried to cut it down, but all the info given would be important in-universe for people reading it to know. I can't just [DATA EXPUNGED FOR BEING BORING TO READ]. Well, I could, but I don't think it'd be a good idea. Yes, heavily abridging the info about the nature and behaviour of -2 would get you to the out-of-universe payoff faster because it's not actually all that important to the story, but in-universe the part that wants to gnaw your feet off is the most important thing to know about in detail.

(e: Also, articles that lack answers/implication of answers to obvious, easily-testable questions bug me. They've been containing this one for literally decades, so I expect there to be oodles and gobs of data on the things that can be tested or examined relatively easily. I've relegated the vast majority of it to the realm of not-included supplemental files, but what I've got sans the addenda is the minimum of what I would expect of the in-universe main file.)

Because it doesn't happen in two different subjects.

SCP-1284-1/8 suffers from an additional psychosis similar to Depersonalisation Disorder without successful reality-testing, ultimately forming a personal narrative in which unpleasant events and stimuli are derealised entirely .
附加记录：    

Compare to the interviews:

Dr. ████: If we didn't give you those pills, you would bleed to death.

SCP-1284-1/7: It is his right.
附加记录：    

SCP-1284-1/7 is totally aware of what should happen and eventually kills herself because of it. That's not derealising something.

Living the dream, or dreaming the life?

Fold

01 May 2013 22:46

-1/8 has a second psychosis on top of the one that comes with being a 1284-1. -1/3 and -1/7 are both exhibiting the standard 1284-1 psychosis, in which those events that should be distressing to a normal person are not; they're fully aware of what's going to happen to them, but they don't see anything wrong with the arrangement. -1/8's additional psychosis makes it so things that even a 1284-1 finds distressing get retconned out of her internal narrative. So -1/8, for example, doesn't ever remember getting her blood drawn, because needles hurt.
