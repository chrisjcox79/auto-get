Here's a long-ass article that's been sitting around in my sandboxes for far too long. I was originally planning on posting this last year, but during revisions I set about on a grandiose plan to make one of the longest SCPs on the wiki. Needless to say that didn't end up happening, and I decided to take the part of the draft I knew were solid, revise, and post. So here it is!

Thanks to Tufto for their prior crit. I can't remember every critter I had, so if you did crit just give me a shout and I'll list ya!

This is based on Church of the Second Hytoth lore and SCP-2651, but no prior knowledge of either should be necessary for reading.

An absolutely excellent piece. You really exploit the loneliness and isolation of the Antarctic setting and create one of the best examples I've seen on-site of diary entries used for a more action-heavy, thriller-esque type of narrative. The juxaposition of the past and present elements is also really good; the ending especially transforms the curated, almost fictional-seeming diaries to something that feels much more real and direct.

Very easy +1. This should be read as an example of how to do exploratory/horror-from-time-based skips properly.
