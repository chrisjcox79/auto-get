Diary entries weren't half bad, but it's a common enough vehicle for exposition that it sticks out as just that - and it's really hard to get into unless they're executed particularly well.

They're not really bad per se, but they're too matter-of-fact to be believable (especially considering how it's even recognized in the work: ).

The story you have there? It's good. Like, everything about the dissolving situation, culminating in survivors gambling for their lives? Fuck. I'd eat that shit up - just not a fan of how this is executed.

This was off to a strong start, I think; it opens up with a compelling tableau. I believe this would have been more interesting with a less-is-more approach: present (more vividly) the state of things as they are discovered after the fact, rather than give a primer, and explain everything with a conveniently-thorough diary.

The CI stuff is what cinched my downvote. Of all the possible things to have happened involving the story you've built, it seems like the least interesting angle to follow.

07 Dec 2017 07:01

If I were to rework this, I'd retool the article by focusing on the SCPF piecing together everything as they record it.:

+block

–block

So, say, first addendum would be a recording of the state and nature of all the bodies they find, and allowing the events to be inferred from that.

Several people found outside the atmospheric safe-zone, with small details that suggest what they were up to/how they attempted to escape.
Cannibalized or mutilated remains in the game area
Some poor sod that locked themselves up in the vault and died alone
Someone of importance strung up and brutalized or crucified
Some of the hired goons something something they ded

etc. etc. etc. Basically, think about the arrangement of these things, or other small details about the hotel itself, that build an outline juuust around the happenings therein. A gentle push in the right direction would be more effective (to me) than a hard shove.

Flowing from that, I would maybe follow that with more disparate logs: crude graffiti scrawled in a certain area, a snippet of an account from someone who can only imagine what's actually going on, a suicide note from a gamblerhell, even some of the diary you've got right now might work out just fine. But again, building around rather than filling completely in.

For -1? I'm not entirely sure what to do with that. Something can come from it, or rather, it can have a place, I suppose, but involving Stock Bad Guis I feel was a poor choice.

Point is, you don't really need it, unless I suppose you need it to be there just to kill everyone else off (it's just a plot point). If that's the case, I'd say it's interchangeable, and I'm certain with some brainstorming you could find something more evocative or alluring or simply just Dark that could serve just as well in that capacity.
