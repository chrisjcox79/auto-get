SCP-3029 is based upon the very real phenomena surrounding the actual star,

KIC 8462852, which experiences some seriously abnormal dips in brightness. Theories abound regarding whether or not it's comets, ice clouds, random dust - or aliens. This being SCP, my choice was pretty easy.

Of course, I had to add stuff to it. The Shkadov Thruster was entirely my idea - the real system doesn't move. That said, the base concept of such a thruster isn't mine either - it's been used a couple times in science fiction and I stumbled across it on wikipedia.

Temporal Sinks and the Peregrine missions originated in SCP-3200. Credit to weizhong for those concepts!

All images here are from Wikimedia Commons - except for the Peregrine-14 logo appearing in the addenda, which is my own work in Photoshop.

Let me know what you think!

Oh, and to monitor the actual star, the latest data is frequently posted to r/KIC8462852. Some of the astronomers watching the star are sometimes active there, too!

The Calamity called to my mind Niven's Known Space stories - in which the Milky Way is in the process of becoming a Seyfert galaxy, one whose core has exploded in a series of massive supernovae. The ensuing radiation wavefront would be more than sufficient to sterilize the remaining galaxy.

That's why the Pak began trying to colonize the outer arms (sadly for them, there's not enough thallium out here for tree-of-life root to grow properly - we know them as ), that's why someone built the Ringworld (its structure stops 40% of neutrinos, which is definitely weird), and that's why the Puppeteer Fleet of Worlds is headed for the Greater Magellanic Cloud (sane puppeteers regard hyperspace as more dangerous than five planets in a Kemplerer rosette flying at sublight velocities through interstellar space).

I imagine the Calamity is something similar. Good thing the Foundation's got an FTL system, because when that particular XK-class scenario plays out escape is the only option.
