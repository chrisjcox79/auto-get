SCP-4784: Nature Abhors a Vacuum Tube

27 Nov 2019 01:26

At its heart, this SCP is a juxtaposition of Canadiana: obscure historical texture from Ontario, fudged so far as to be unrecognizable, and David Cronenberg's .

Thanks go out to NatVoltaic for checking the final draft! Y'all are the best!
(If I've sourced it correctly,)  is descended from Incident 096-1-A.
Dr. Michelle Wilkes is a character featured in ManyMeats. I stumbled across this article while looking up pataphysics terminology, and Dr. Wilkes' expert opinion seemed like a natural fit!
The blooming rose in Unwinding's end card is visually modified from a photograph by Gayatri Priyadarshini, made available on Wikimedia Commons under CC Attribution-Share Alike 4.0.
I ran both title cards through PHOTOMOSH.

SpoilersEggs

–block

(The Artist Formerly Known As) Anna's mistake until someone else had an idea.

The Foundation doesn't know everything about this anomaly, but hey, neither does steakshift! Some of the inciting events haven't happened yet! Except they have, because it's not a time machine, it's a story, and from at least one perspective the ending/beginning is already written.

Anna Bojarski is almost as elusive as Robert Denby!)
, it's also a tell/slip: if they truly found the album at random, how would they know it depicts a killer TV?
There is an excessively subtle music reference embedded into Unwinding's aesthetic. Can you spot it?

This mirrors my feelings. The note at the end deflates anything that's built up throughout the narrative, and it isn't itself interesting enough to make up for that deflation. I don't feel particularly respected as a reader (not that I think you are personally being disrespectful) when things are just laid out neatly at the end, even if the neat note goes to lengths to describe it as something illogical and exotic. It's harder to lead a reader to the same conclusion, but I think that's the right way to go with this sort of thing.
