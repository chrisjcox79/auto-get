Viruses can't be 100% lethal, then it doesn't work as a virus. It has to spread. If it kills the host, then that breaks the point of the virus, which is to proliferate. Alongside this, 100% lethality is physically impossible, give your immune system some credit, it's been around the block for literally all of human history, it's learned how to do its job.

Lethal viruses aren't lethal because they want to be or because it's an effective strategy, diseases that are lethal are lethal because they aren't made for humans. They're made for a different animal, a different biology, and so their attempts to hijack your system kill you. Every epidemic, plague, etc. all come from animals: HIV and AIDs(Monkeys, some say Chimps), Swine Flu(pigs), rabies(juries out chief), Avian flu(birbs), Antrax(Grazing animals, sheep, goats, cows, etc.), I could go on, but I'd just be spitting wiki after this.

Why do viruses guarantee immunity(not as you postulate, only the ones we know)? Well, because that how humans develop immunity.

It's not so much that's how viruses work as that's how your immune system works. Each of us builds millions of B Lymphocytes each of whom has hundreds of membrane-bound antibodies each perfectly tailored to an antigen. Viruses will always have some kind of hallmark and should a B Lymphocyte find one that matches, it'll mass copy and go Hunter-Killer on that virus. Oversimplifying and skipping some stuff but yeah.

Chemistry nitpicking

09 Aug 2011 03:13

If this prion is of similar size to PrP, the real-world Prion Protein. then  is not a danger as implied by the picture caption, unless the SCP universe includes radically superior peptide synthesis technology than the current state of the art. The upper practical limit of completely synthetic peptides is around 30 amino acids, these days, and does not promise to get much larger anytime soon. PrP weighs in at 253 AA.

The gene that codes for SCP-008 might be spliced into a bacterium and  which happens at various points along the natural protein biosynthesis process, and even if a culture could be raised that expressed the SCP-008 perfectly on a primary amino acid sequence level, it still might not function as the infectious protein because the culture system might not correctly fold the protein or perform other post-translational modifications.

Anyway, point being: Just displaying the ribbon diagram or even actually publishing the amino acid sequence of SCP-008 really would not present a significant threat since it would be difficult to the point of impossibility to reproduce the infectious material just with that information. As with real-world PrP, the only way to mass-produce SCP-008 would be to get your hands on some that already exists and culture it. Still, you might err on the side of caution and redact the AA sequence anyway. But there really is no danger of
