It's a good freshman effort, but it falls short in several ways. The containment procedures feel surprisingly understated for such a powerful entity. That could be forgiven if there was elaboration on what Procedure 39-Mobilov later in the article, but that's never paid off. As such the containment procedures essentially communicate no information to the reader beyond the setting. There's so much more you could be doing here.

+block

–block

The description itself is also overly brief. We really don't know anything about the anomaly and it only serves the real purpose of setting up the addenda. While that isn't problematic by itself, the fact that the reader is five paragraphs into the article and the story hasn't gotten it feet under it yet is. There needs to be more description here, if not in terms of adding content than at least in making the content that's already there serve more of a purpose than bridging us to the next section.

I did enjoy the first addenda, although it was fairly predictable. We find a facility, there's Foundation personnel there we don't know about, turns out they're from the future. It's all communicated well and with enough imagery to keep things a little spicy.

The interview is put together well. Baker isn't given much to say, but at least the dialogue is crisp enough that you could imagine a human being saying it. Pride's speech struck me as a little off, she doesn't seem to be reacting to the same situation described in the previous addendum and kind of reads like a valley girl. Although that's not bad if you intended her primary character trait here to be ditziness.

The Tachyon Disruptor Engine I like this concept. However, I think it is handled poorly here. We only see it mentioned in the second-to-last addendum and the final log. Something this big should be a central part of the article and get mentioned much earlier. You could probably integrate this and the information about the Site-39 event into the main description and have the story be much stronger. When you've got all your story beats segmented up like this it causes problems. Like one of the more presumably important story elements only being introduced immediately before it gets used.

The final log itself has good dialogue, but the pacing is all over the place. They go from chatting, to encountering the monster and then immediately everyone's gone. There's no time to get to know these characters and become invested in them so when they're all disappeared almost as soon as we've met them there's no reason for the reader to feel anything.

Administrator's note didn't really do anything for me and you should probably find a stronger note to end the article on.

You've got the pieces here to make an article, if you consolidated the description and addenda information into one piece and expanded the log. Teasing time travel adventures and then not really delivering on them hurts this piece. The exploration log should have them going through different time periods at this facility, maybe even tying into the inciting incident. These guys could've caused that. It works given the reactive nature you've described here.

This is close. It just needs more development.

Long post collapsed. ~Zyn
