This style of article (description/function of anomaly, followed by prose in the form of a journal to explain everything) really only works for me when its got anything going for it besides bland exposition.

Including interesting, entertaining, or relatable characters; telling a compelling story utilizing drama, suspense, and conflict; and cultivating a strong image and atmosphere with descriptive writing or pacing are all things that can contribute to making exposition more bearable (or, at least, mask it).

Number of issues regarding tone, along with some poor exposition, led this to be a hard downvote for me.

Description/clinical tone

+block

–block

and tourist should be redirected away from the premises of SCP-3292.
附加记录：    

)

If you mean 'tourists', then this could part of the sentence could be excised, because its already mentioned how how they handle trespassers. Alternatively, you could lose this sentence:

Trespassers are to be taken to Site-990 for questioning, administered Class-B amnestics, and released.
附加记录：    

Because it doesn't really add anything - so long as they keep people away, they shouldn't need to worry about trespassers to begin with.

Basically, some of this could be shortened without sacrificing much of anything to the overall article - it's fluff.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Two level-2 guards are to be stationed outside of the entrance to SCP-3292
附加记录：    

Figure I should note that it never actually states what or where these entrances are at any point. Reading the article it doesn't seem it matters much - if SCP-3292 could be entered from any direction, nothing would really need to be changed aside from the one-or-two vague mentions. Another detail that hangs without going anywhere, detracting from the article. It could be cut.

Alternatively, this could be used as an opportunity to include interesting or expository details (take some of the load off the journal) if it does have an entrance, what does it look like? Is it simply an invisible barrier between some trees? Is it something built into the forest? Are there inscriptions, or other small, subtle details that may convey some form of meaning or intent?
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

perpetual state of the spring season, regardless of meteorological changes in baseline reality.
附加记录：    

 would be sufficient, I think.

I would say you don't even need anything after the comma here; simply state what it is - it doesn't need to be reinforced with that comparison, it's clear from the statement itself that it's distinct from the outside area.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Twenty-four hour day and night cycles have been observed coinciding with baseline reality in SCP-3292,
附加记录：    

This could be  without losing any meaning. This whole section is rather clunky, actually.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

SCP-3292 is theorized to be orders of magnitude times larger than the forest that encompasses it.
附加记录：    

Another thing that would not seem to bear mentioning due to where it comes in the paragraph and how much it conveys vs the later sentence about mapping it out. I would maybe rework this some.

Exploration of SCP-3292 has proven to be exceptionally difficult due to effects on technology. Anything more complex than simple lever contraptions cease all functionality upon being brought into SCP-3292, prohibiting the use of drones. Currently, Foundation agents have mapped out approximately 35km² from SCP-3292-2.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

All of SCP-3292-4 have been observed to be organisms thought to be extinct or in numbers of 100.
附加记录：    

Should maybe be ). This could actually stand to be more concise.

All flora and fauna within SCP-3292 are species which are either critically endangered or extinct in baseline.

(eh not that great, but whatever)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

As for the addendaI don't think it does much that couldn't have been covered in the description somewhere, doesn't seem significant or important enough to merit its own little subsection.

I didn't cover everything here, but those are some of the things that stood out as imprecise or needlessly wordy - both of which are things that don't make for good technical writing, which should aim to be clear and direct.

Journals

+block

–block

In my hunt for the fabled Scottish viking artifacts of Preishnire, my ventures brought to me to the outskirts of a small fishing port town just outside of Grangemout, where the locals told of a forbidden forest, watched over by a mythic guardian of indisputable power.
附加记录：    

I didn't think much of this on my first read through, but now that I know it's supposed to be coming from Lord Blackwood, this seems really bland. Blackwood is a terrible braggart and embellisher.  is one of the most basic and vague things that would be driving him towards adventure. Surely something more significant or grand or interesting could be here.

I also feel tumbling through so much in this sentence takes away from giving any sort of life to the scene - it's just kinda rushed off.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

After a small argy-wargy
附加记录：    

Should be 'argy-bargy', because as-is it looks like a cutesy way of saying 'argument' which is really jarring.

Also, what was being disputed? This is a missed opportunity to tell more story and continue to paint the scene or provide characterization- contributing to the overall effect of making the journal seem like poorly-concealed exposition.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The journey was ubiquitously strenuous.
附加记录：    

The adjective here just weakens the sentence.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Horrid rains battered against my back, and the winds were so powerful I was nearly blown away!
附加记录：    

Another thing that did not really stand out until I found out it's Blackwood. I figure this would be a good opportunity to compare this situation to a previous conquest. Blackwood's the type to bring up feats in this way. It's part of the fun of the character.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

by the very gods themselves
附加记录：    

Even if this weren't Blackwood, I would figure an English explorer from his time would be Christian, so this seemed out of place.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

I was so collywobbled, I had nearly forgotten my homburg
附加记录：    

This feels like it's straight giving up on characterization and stuffing in British-sounding words. To be  feeling would be a better fit. A homburg is a formal hat, which is something I wouldn't think you'd wear travelling the wilderness. A pith helmet would be more functional and fitting.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

I've come to believe that this forest is in some kind of loop, or something similar anyhow. I can't quite put it into words, but I believe that I am still in yesterday, today.
附加记录：    

This is, to be absolutely blunt, just plain bad storytelling. There's no effort in providing anything in the narrative to clue the character in, just magic information because reasons. It stops being a story at this point, with how obvious the author's hand is.

Actually going to cut this short, as it's the second time I've lost interest in reading past this log. I'd love more Blackwood, but this doesn't cut it for me.

03 May 2018 20:02

Both of these paragraphs contain the same sentence almost verbatim. It also seems odd that a purely mechanical device such as a spring-driven clock would be susceptible to electromagnetic interference. It also wouldn't explain why something like a diesel engine, which has no electrical components, so something else would have to explain why the fuel isn't combusting properly.

In this case, I'd recommend changing it to something along these lines.

Exploration of SCP-3292 has proven to be exceptionally difficult due to effects on technology. Electronics brought into SCP-3292 cease functionality immediately upon entry, prohibiting the use of drones. It is hypothesized that this is due to the electromagnetic fields present throughout SCP-3292. Currently, Foundation agents have mapped out approximately 35km² from SCP-3292-2.
附加记录：    

Or, if you still want complex machinery to fail:

Exploration of SCP-3292 has proven to be exceptionally difficult due to effects on technology. Electronics and complex machinery brought into SCP-3292 cease functionality immediately upon entry, prohibiting the use of drones. It is hypothesized electronics fail due to the electromagnetic fields present throughout SCP-3292, though there is no current explanation for non-electrical machinery. Currently, Foundation agents have mapped out approximately 35km² from SCP-3292-2.
附加记录：    

I hope this helps some. Beyond this, I think what you have is very interesting.
