This was somewhat of a mixed upvote for me. Halfway through the article, I was sure that I was going to downvote it, but the ending changed my mind.

The primary problem that I see is in maintaining interest through the halfway point of the piece. The hallucination log, while neat in hindsight, doesn't make much sense for the reader at that point, since we have no idea what it means yet. Given how long it is, I found myself semi-skimming through that portion precisely because I wasn't quite sure what it represented, and it didn't have any grounding for me to care about what it represented.

I'm sure this will be fine moving forward in terms of rating, but I almost wish that the true story behind the piece wasn't all presented at the end. It felt a little rushed to have the entire story told to me within the confines of one or two logs, and I think that it somewhat muddied a very cool background and story behind this work.

Still, the story, no matter how it was presented, was a good one, so it's a +1 from me.

08 Jul 2018 17:17

I want to like this, but there are a lot of pieces of this that are just confusingly written, and, having read an earlier version of the draft of this, some places where things were only partially changed from a previous version.

Issues

–block

In a report to the Sultan, members of the Islamic Artifact Expeditionary Corps spoke of a green glass room which would float upon the surface if an offering was given.
附加记录：    

There's nothing about an offering required to active this mentioned; you just need a person to kneel in the centre.

Each waypoint has also been shown to have gravestones buried beneath the center of the circle. Due to the destruction of most of these locations, the tablets were able to be recovered in a severely degraded state.
附加记录：    

The switch from gravestone to tablet here took me far longer than I'd care to admit to realise you were talking about the same thing.

Just a flowing white robe. that seemed to always be ready to be flung away by the wind.
附加记录：    

Is this supposed to be a new sentence or a comma here?

We were escorted inside. We received a gift. They didn't know what they had, and it frightened them. I never saw it but they showed us men turned to hair. They still lived, but what was living then?
附加记录：    

I can barely parse this at all. Who didn't know what they had? This all feels very scattershot.

There were this amazing, huge green domes.
附加记录：    

Is  here?

He died the first time when we went under. But when we got to his stop, he knew he was going to die again. Had to. That's why he gave us the star.
附加记录：    

I feel like I'm missing some piece of information here. How is this person dying multiple times? What is this star? Also, the footnote on this sentence seems utterly unrelated to the rest of the paragraph.

If anyone said we were brothers now they could have it.
附加记录：    

They were building the way now
附加记录：    

They all had their manuals with them, guns and ammunition.
附加记录：    

None of these lines make any sense to me, in the context of the rest of their paragraphs.

This isn't an exhaustive list, just the ones I picked out during a second skim through the article.

Honestly, to me it reads like a lot of these are trying to be intentionally vague, but that makes no sense to me. They're supposed to be accounts of hallucinations experienced by Foundation people using SCP-4000 right? So why would they be so obtuse about describing what they saw?

The exploration log and summary both suffer a lot of the same issues. Additionally, while I can sort of get what you are going for, the habit of the people in the log to go off on tangents completely unrelated to the previous line made reading that difficult. And, as others have stated, the summary seems to be just recounting the exact same info from the log without adding any insight or new info.

The idea here is cool, but I found this far too difficult a read to be able to upvote it.

This feels like it's been padded out arbitrarily, and it was really difficult to avoid skimming large sections. Structurally, I feel like the glass cube itself ends up being kind of superfluous to the article, serving mostly to bury the lede of the Atlantia settlement in an addendum. I'll second weizhong and TL333s' comments: this takes a really long time to get to the punch and much of that padding is overly vague and not all that interesting. The hallucinations log is too long and too vague to keep my interest (and really the only hallucination that grabbed me on its own terms was the Severan dynasty entry), and the exploration log is redundant with the report that follows it. All this means that by the time I get to what's really going on, I've lost interest. This material would, I think, be better served by having the Atlantia settlement as the main subject.
