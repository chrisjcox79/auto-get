09 Nov 2012 17:20

I was pleasantly surprised by the fact that the skip doesn't simply kill you if you go to the party, and overall the idea has merit.

However, I'm having some issues with the execution.

+block

–block

The typical path an SCP-562 incident takes is as follows. First,
附加记录：    

The construction of this paragraph feels a little clunky to me. You could set it up like , etc.

Persons who agree to go to the party vanish
附加记录：    

electronic monitoring reports them simply vanishing
附加记录：    

I don't care for the word  in this context as it feels unclinical to me, but whatever, I think I might be in the minority on that. Nevertheless, I'd suggest using a different word in the second sentence to avoid repetition.

Human observers report seeing bright, multicolored lights and hearing unidentifiable speech and music, as well as a lingering smell of nutmeg.
附加记录：    

My assumption is that the human observers in this case are people who watch the test subject enter the door after agreeing to go to the party, and what they see is what's on the other side of the door. If this is so, you may want to specify that this is the case. If it's not so, you'll want to clarify how this works.

Rarely, the subject retains some item they possessed at the time of the 562-Claim event. Such objects show wear consistent with decades of use. These corpses are found dressed in ornate and unlabeled clothing of extremely high quality.
附加记录：    

What kind of items? I'm curious, and you're giving me nothing here. Also, is it only the people who've brought something back that are found in fancy clothes? Or are the rest of the subjects found naked in the woods?

One exchange in the release is between a Lady Marchgrove and a Dick O’Dale, in which the latter attempts to convince the former to attend a ball he is throwing. Lady Marchgrove agrees to go, and historical records note her disappearance at about the same time as the last letter is believed to have been written.
附加记录：    

I like this addendum, and I think it adds a little bit to the narrative. I'm not formally suggesting that you do anything to this, but I for one would like to see this as an actual letter (or a fragment of a letter) appended to the SCP entry, with a note following that says that  or something. I'd wait to see if anyone else would like something like this, though. It's more of a preference thing.

Conceptually, I'm wondering how we test this thing. If it shows up randomly, and we have reason to suspect that it happens less than a thousand times annually across the world, the chances that the Foundation would get to observe this in a controlled manner seem vanishgly small. This isn't enough for me to downvote, and I would upvote this article if it ends up being executed more smoothly. But this is a point that may be worth considering.
