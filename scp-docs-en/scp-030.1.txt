+block

–block

SCP-030 is confined to Site 62 with a special security grade (Class F) allowing it access to all unsecured research labs, records facilities and communal social areas (mess halls, dormitories, gymnasiums) when accompanied by at least one (1) staff member Class 1 or higher.
附加记录：    

So, it's a roamer.

With the written consent of Level 4 personnel, SCP-030 may be granted temporary access to secured research labs or Euclid-class SCP containment facilities to act as a research assistant under the direct supervision of assigned laboratory staff.
附加记录：    

And also participates in the laboratory. Even though it's an anomalous entity. Hm.

SCP-030 was discovered 6/12/████ on the grounds of the cemetery of ██ ████ █████████'s Church in London's Mortlake District. It was buried in an unmarked plot approximately 2.7 meters (9 feet) in depth, contained in a small stone sarcophagus.
附加记录：    

How? Who the hell was just digging up a cemetery and happened to find this?

The sarcophagus lid was shattered by a careless local historian during the excavation, exposing SCP-030 to daylight.
附加记录：    

I didn't know that historians were also graverobbers.

Its solid blue eyes lack discernible irises or pupils, rather resembling two small cut sapphires.
附加记录：    

Tone, I think.

To date, SCP-030 has been responsible for no less than 6000 hours of research assistance to Site 62 staff across multiple departments and command levels. Despite the opportunity, SCP-030 has shown no desire to escape the confines of Site 62, regularly reporting that it 
附加记录：    

I really, really don't like the idea of this.

6/16/████: SCP-030 has requested materials and lab space for independent research. Approval pending.
附加记录：    

Why has it been put in such a position that it is even possible for it to request this?

I personally dislike this article, due to the fact that I feel the procedures described are uncharacteristic of the Foundation. -1

Modernized the fossil a bit. I feel he's lost some charm, but hopefully the Foundation is better represented to current standards as requested.

I imagined I'd still get the question , so I attempted to address that. It is permitted to do (strictly monitored) research because occasionally, it researches using methodologies that are either completely unknown or extensions of theories that were supposedly abandoned.

It may also be (again, under very strict circumstances)  like a tiny, outdated, and man-shaped encyclopedia to look at things from its unique perspective and offer insight. This is the accommodation made to eliminate its much-maligned formerly free-roaming nature.
