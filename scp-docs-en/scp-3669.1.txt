So this is like a reality-bending system of mathematics? An interesting idea, if not a little overdone.

However, there are many errors throughout your scip, mostly tonal errors.

Here's a quick few I picked up on-

+block

–block

every sapient person that entered
附加记录：    

 seems rather redundant here

SCP-3669 is a book teaching the reader
附加记录：    

You just stated that it was a book at the beginning of the previous paragraph

more simply
附加记录：    

should be substituted for something such as 

based around arrows pointing in 1 of 8 directions
附加记录：    

I'm not really sure what this means, and this in turn led the testing logs (and the entire SCP, really) to be rather confusing. More elaboration on how the system works would be nice.

SCP-3669-2 exclusively uses arrows until the solution, which is rendered numerically.
附加记录：    

This sentence needs to be worded better. I suggest something along the lines of 

Also, there's a lot missing from your description that's covered in your logs. I recommend you cover more of the anomalous effects, primarily those outlined in your incident log and the effects and significance of SCP-3669-3.

Please collapse long posts. ~Zyn

-1 for now, until it's cleaned up and explained further.

+1

I generally like mathematical skips and this was entertaining and well done. The test logs were a tiny bit dragged out you probably could've removed one or two of them in the middle there. (Do we really need to know it works on Children? Or see it out in the wild?)

My only other negative comment is either the ending or the containment procedures need to be tweaked. Narratively, it was a little bit of a letdown. I'm not saying it wasn't imaginative and neat, it was, and I liked it. But given the absurdly overwhelming Containment Procedures (O5 approval, 50km perimeters, and requiring amnestics of EVERYONE in that huge area) I thought the ending was going to have a lot more oomph, and have a lot wider impact.
