Fold

09 Apr 2012 17:32

This bothers me. The core aspect of the SCP - that the window shows something not corresponding to the other side, the image of which isn't carried by light - is mentioned once offhandedly and then never given consideration beyond that. Recording devices see nothing, and lenses block the effect, as is demonstrated by glasses and contacts, yet the thermoplastic barrier doesn't inhibit it at all.

That would be the core focus of the Foundation's investigation of this SCP, I think. This is potentially directly stimulating the optic nerve, or somehow directly implanting memories into observers. Instead, it's just used as a handwave to avoid having to elaborate on anything, as would be required if we could view the other side with clarity, and the ramifications of this thing being a possible cognitohazard are totally ignored. The entire thing is just a vehicle to give a bare-bones description of the aftermath of some war we're supposed to care about for some reason that may or may not actually have been a thing - we don't know. It's not logical to assume we're actually seeing real things on the other side, or that there even is an other side, as it's not light coming through.
