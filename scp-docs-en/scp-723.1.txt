For the record, I can totally buy a staircase having a memetic compulsion to climb it. Curiosity is a memetic compulsion. People see stairs, and there's only one way to know where they go.

The catch is determining if there is a stronger memetic compulsion than there should be normally. In all honesty I figure the Foundation tested this by waiting to see if the local staff tried to climb the stairs themselves as opposed to sending up test subjects, or if they were bizarrely forceful in demanding more test subjects for unnecessary repetition of the experiments.

07 Jun 2011 23:54

The original concept actually didn't include the fact that people didn't want to return. Instead, people would de-age as they came back down the stairs, but were left with a lingering curiosity about what would happen if they went a few steps more. I ended up with this approach because it allowed for more mystery, and didn't feel so heavy-handed.

I may still try and improve the memetic/compulsion aspect of this, I think the fact that there's a small chance people are climbing under their own volition could be explored more, but at the same time I don't want to ruin the mystery.
