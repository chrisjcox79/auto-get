This. Especially when the blacked out numbers are all in the format #█. You already give us the information that there are between 70 and 79 (inclusive) instances; why is it so critical that we not know the exact number at that point?

Although the concept is passably interesting, it's been explored a lot in fiction, most recently in that Will Farrell film (Stranger Than Fiction, I think?). Historical documentation of anomalies has potential, but this just seems ridiculous: , which is actually a real profession, you know). How the fuck would that translate to a location on Earth? Or what if the character was a crystal-spellsinger on the sky-island of Rhee floating high above the Plains of Battle in the Realm of Fantasyland.

And given these constraints, you state that they were able to identify and capture one of these character/people as a newborn infant apparently less than a week after the book's publication. A) How was the Foundation able to identify that book as a trigger for the phenomenon? B) How were they able to identify and locate the infant in that short a time?

For starters, it's statistically unlikely for even two books with the same ritual to appear when the authors have had no contact nor read each others' books; it's statistically impossible for 7█ such books to be published, even across the space of 500 years.
附加记录：    

Bullshit. Do you have any idea how many books are published by major printing presses this year alone? Much less how many have been printed over the last 5, 20, 100 years? Even assuming that an author is able to read a brand new book every single day of the year (which is impossible since they have to, you know, write), they wouldn't be able to make a dent in the number of published books. You even explicitly state that the trigger books are in a wide variety of genres, which reduces the likelihood even more! How many true crime writers are going to be reading enough literary fiction or hard scifi or fluff fantasy or historical romances to be able to definitely come across a previous instance of the trigger?

As I said, the core idea is passable, but the execution is so horrible that it's fully unbelievable, even with a healthy dose of suspension of disbelief. Downvoted hard.
