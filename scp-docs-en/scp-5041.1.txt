The earliest version of this article looked radically, radically different. It was initially inspired by the case of , a Canadian prisoner who is not actually named that. His real name and identity is unknown. The idea of a prisoner without an identity or reason to be in prison fascinated me, and I started a draft along those lines.

Some time later, I learned of the Man in the the Iron Mask, a French prisoner from the 1600s with both unknown identity and crime. This fascinated me, and I started working from there again, scrapping everything I had before.

I've written a few mystery SCPs in the past without a clear explanation or authorial answer, and I wanted to avoid that this time. I thought of an anomaly with a good reason for the Foundation to be unaware, and went from there, as well as putting a few bureaucratic reasons in (I've been a fan of the Foundation inheriting anomalies from precursors and not fully understanding them for a while).

The second draft of this looked rather different. It had the same ConProcs and Description, and two addenda  the Estate noir letter and the Interview. But it didn't have any mention of Dr. Cocteau, or his story.

MaliceAforethought looked at it, and told me that while the story was put together in a new way, there wasn't really anything there he hadn't seen before. It was a collection of familiar tropes with a new coat of paint. I was already feeling that the draft was missing something, and wasn't fond of it. Back to the drawing board.

Enter Dr. Clouzot. As I was thinking, I came up with the idea for a Foundation researcher doing the investigation into SCP-5041 and in the process killing it. The cause of death went through a few variations, but settled on the diabetes: firmly Clouzot's fault, truly accidental and oh so mundane.

Well, see, the thing is that researching the anomaly was not actually his job. Clouzot's job as the HMCL is to supervise containment and make sure that is carried out well. Research is only necessary as a means to refine the Containment Procedures, which he notes have been in place for a while and are unlikely to be amended. Doctor Lebeau was on the job for decades and never felt it necessary to do all this research and form a theory.

Further, his actions are in violation of the spirit, if not the exact letter of the Containment Procedures. While there's nothing that prevented him, he should have picked up on this sooner. Further, a review of his logs  would indicate he wasn't doing this for the benefit of containment, but because he was angling for a promotion, going so far as to ignore the anomaly when it begged him to stop. That's the malicious intent right there, a reckless disregard for the person's concerns. He brushes them away immediately without really considering why he was asked to stop.

Considering the nature of the Foundation's work, an anomaly dying because of complete lack of knowledge, and no malicious intent or mala praxis, is one of those things that would happen eventually.
