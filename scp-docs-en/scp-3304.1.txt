I’m torn here. On one hand, this does have a nice sense of humour to it, and that final little note is a nice little ‘oomph’ as it were—and honestly, I do like in a twisted how the SCP Foundation is on speaking terms and generally friendly relations with literally Hell itself.

On the other hand though, it feels a bit too silly, at least for a Mainline article. Like, this has all the kind if set up I feel would make for a good Tale or so, but some part of me—maybe some ancient part that still insists that the SCP is a “Seriosus business of Horror and Inexplicable Spooky stuff”—can’t come to grips with this.

I may change this opinion in the future, but for now, I’m begrudgingly staying Neutral on this. It’s good, but it feels just too Silly—a good kind of Silly, but one I’m torn on supporting. Goos job all the same, however.

Ngl I really don’t like when people say stuff like “I would have upvoted if it was a J” because that kinda misses the point. Articles on this site are about the writing, and if that writing entertained or impressed you. The arbitrary classification of an article itself shouldn’t matter for the most part. If you thought this wasn’t funny or wasn’t good, by all means downvote. I’m well aware my comedic style is divisive to say the least. But if you liked the article, if you laughed, evaluate it for what it is rather than if it has a -J next to its title. Trying to restrict all humorous writing to Js just limits creativity.

Articles on this site are about the writing, and if that writing entertained or impressed you.
附加记录：    

That's not 100% accurate, though. There are plenty of tales on this site that are entertaining and impressive writing, but if they were uploaded (unedited) in a mainlist SCP slot, they would be downvoted.

My point is: there are different expectations for different types of submissions to the site.  is (hopefully) necessary  for success, but it is not sufficient .

In my example above of tales posted on the mainlist, one of the other necessary requirements - the standard mainlist format - is missing, and therefore downvotes accrue.

In the case of this article, I downvoted it because the Foundation's unserious attitude, and that of the demons, destroyed my suspension of disbelief. I'm fine with humour in mainlist articles (and have upvoted many examples), provided that suspension of belief remains largely intact. Suspension of disbelief in the shared setting is (for me) another necessary element of a successful mainlist skip.

That same suspension of disbelief is not necessary (in my mind) when it comes to -J articles, which serve a different purpose, and are assessed against different criteria. As such, I have in the past downvoted articles on the mainlist that I might upvote as -Js. I suspect that others apply a similar criteria, with similar results.

As such, I don't agree with you that we're looking to  mean in the first place! Otherwise we could just have one big list of articles on the site, without differentiating mainlisters from tales from -Js from -EXs from GoI formats - and I don't think you're suggesting that.
