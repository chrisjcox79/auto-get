It feels like there are so many angles you could take this idea (eg what happens if a subject sees its rabbit breeding? does each affected subject pick a different rabbit and if not, then what? what if their pet dog kills the rabbit? what if the happy couple leaves Chattanooga valley? what if the rabbit has baby rabbits? not all marriages are happy - how does that work with rabbits?) and the choice to go with  seems like a really unproductive one. It doesn't really lead anywhere, and I think that is evidenced by the third log which takes a very long time to make a carrot joke.

There's also the implication that amnestics don't work effectively when it comes to the wiping of any long-term partner, which slightly undermines how they're treated elsewhere. And why do the Containment Procedures make no attempt to (a) contain the rabbit population; or (b) restrict access / habitation in the area of effect?

Fold

16 Feb 2018 17:23

I can see where you are coming from. But this skip is ultimately about (ordinary human) relationships  and how, despite the artifice of it all, the loss of the fictions we tell ourselves can be tragic. Because this is the main point here, I didn't really want to explore the various permutations of what can happen with this anomaly.

I have answers in my head about attempting to contain the rabbit population [1], but I'm not sure if they should be edited in, for the same reason as above  let's see if other readers have the same reaction.

[1] Rabbits can migrate, you miss one or two and they breed like, well, rabbits, so in a few years you've got the same problem again; dealing with this requires a large scale effort (you've got to comb every inch of half of Georgia), and the Foundation finds it easier to amnesticize ~20 people per year affected by this.
