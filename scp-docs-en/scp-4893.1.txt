Fold

30 Aug 2019 02:52

I'm glad you enjoyed it!

And yes the last letter is hard to read. Bellow is the spoiler.

+block

–block

First of all, congratulations on the promotion.

From today, you are the head researcher of SCP-4893, and thus SCP-4893 itself. It'll be very difficult to read this letter, but you'll get used to it soon.

Well, you might be wondering. Why do I have to become SCP-4893? Why not use D-Class personnel?

It was like that at first when SCP-4893 was discovered. In fact, SCP-4893 has been inherited for many years by healthy, young D-Class personnel. But there's something very different now: the literacy rate. Now many people can read and write texts in developed and underdeveloped countries. Inevitably, the possibility of exposure to SCP-4893's capabilities has increased. Any textual medium written or typed by SCP-4893 is a source of infection. There have been cases in which a D-Class personnel who have followed SCP-4893 in the past carved words on the wall and made many of the staff become SCP-4893.

Our philosophy is . It is not good enough to just secure and contain any SCP. It is therefore best to choose SCP-4893's successors from among Foundation personnel who can understand the Foundation's philosophy and carry it out. Inherit what we have contained and protected. And someday you will entrust the mission to someone else. Good luck.

Former SCP-4893 Head Researcher
