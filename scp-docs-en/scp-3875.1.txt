Thanks to The Roboticon for a more in-depth one. If this skip is less than it could be cause I didn't stop to get more crits, I take full responsibility!

Looking forward to writing more tales where characters visit the Wanderers' Library, I came up against what's probably an intrinsic difficulty of the nexus. While all the knowledge in the multiverse is in there, no one really knows where to find any particular piece of it. Surely somewhere in the worlds there had to be someone perfectly suited to fill this niche. I figure TentedBeams (with the help of her many children) is that someone.

Image source here on Wikimedia.

Additionalinspiration

Hidefangirling

I'm a huge fan of Sharon Lee and Steve Miller's this novel from the series, there's a game called Aequitas which traders use to exchange information while also letting each other know the relative value of that information.

I was pondering what could be another way to approach this concept within the SCP universe, and got the mental picture of an adorable spider deep in thought while knitting webs. This skip evolved out of that thought.

Edit:  Also a cameo appearance from the campus library at my alma mater, at which I spent many happy hours back in the day.

Edit:  Holy toledo. Re-reading There Are No Strings On Me, I came across this line:

Honestly, those spider people are running the length of the Library all day. Would it kill them to bring me a Monte Cristo while they were at it?
附加记录：    

3875 crawled up out of my subconscious and demanded to be written while I was working on the Herman Fuller backstory series for Dread  Circuses. Now I know how the image of Herman Fuller, in the Library, glowering at a friendly but insufficiently obedient spider person got in my brain in the first place. Truly, the machinery of inspiration is a strange and wondrous thing.

Edit 12/22/18:  Polished up some rough spots in the containment procedures and description. Significantly expanded the recovery log. Hopefully now the dialogue is more natural and the crosslinks more relevant. Thanks to shoujoq for taking a look at the revisions.
