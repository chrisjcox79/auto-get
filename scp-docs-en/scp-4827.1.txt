I'm upvoting, primarily for the masterful use of the ConProcs here to build suspense but there's a few things here that I don't think work as well:

Why build a space station orbiting Mercury to study a black hole 5,000 ly away?
I'm not sure why this needs to be a space station or have a contemporary date. If this was either a remote terrestrial radio observatory, or set in the future you wouldn't need to stretch disbelief with this space station, and the astronomers could have been civilians which would IMO be more poignant.
The ending is significantly heavy-handed and comes across as campy to me. FWIW I also hate the ending of Event Horizon for similar reasons, which this reminds me of. I feel like this could have been much more subtle.

+1 because it's a fun story as it is, but seconding all of SirPudding's comments above.1 This could be much, much stronger as both scifi and pulpy old-school horror with some tinkering.

For instance, on the technical side it's easier to get into orbit around Jupiter than around Mercury (short version is that Mercury doesn't help you brake), and the farther you get from the Sun - and to a lesser extent, planets - the better readings you're going to get from any sort of deep-space observatory. A station in solar orbit between Jupiter and Mars would be relatively easy to set up with the help of some lagrangian points, and put your station in empty space, any planets just distant points of light. (That's good for both observatories and for creep factor)

On the narrative side, I think a slower and more subtle buildup would amplify the creep factor exponentially. On this front, I could actually see some really interesting potential if you were to change it to be affecting the ground crew of a probe- say, they're operating the thing from an isolated antenna station in the middle of nowhere, and events gradually escalate from cafeteria discussions about weird signals and if anybody heard a dog barking last night, to security cam footage of a hallway door acting like someone opened an airlock.

1. With the exception that Dr. Weir in Event Horizon is artisan ham of the finest cut.
