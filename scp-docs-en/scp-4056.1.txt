Upvoted because this proves that there exists a universe where Dr. Gears is a weeb.

Butseriously

Ibecause...

It's a really REALLY good take on a very, dare I say, overused sci-fi trope, and especially considering the 300 Brights scip, this one would have had to be extremely well written to pass.

LUCKILY, the power team of Nicolini and Kirby was up to the challenge, and this piece is both charmingly fun, and exceptionally well executed, to the point that I feel bad giving any critique at all.

However, there is one thing I think could use just a bit of work, which is the pacing of the dialogue, particularly at the end. It feels just a bit rushed, and I think having a slightly slower incline towards the  would be somewhat preferable.

But again, having read this before it was posted, I can say that it was and still is a work of art, and I'm happy the concept was pursued.

My Science Jimmies are so incredibly rustled right now.

Cold is not an energy.

Cold is defined as an ABSENCE of certain kind of energy.

Extremely fundamentally basic stuff.

Any/all parts of the skip that have to do with  nuclear-blasted my suspension of disbelief into sub-atomic particles and gave me a scientific rage boner.

It's OK to write skips that have different laws of physics, but if you do, you cannot change the established definition of something in the real world physics system without creating and describing a new definition for the word  in your own radically different fictional physics system. Just make up a new word arbitrarily and define that as the concept of coldness in your skip physics. Please don't use the established definitions incorrectly.

Edit :  as our physics systems defines it. Maybe I'm just over sensitive to science things, and that's fair enough. No-vote for now.

Read it all, +1. Didn't like it though.

PoI 4056 should be their own anomaly, separate from the location of 4056 itself. In fact, 4056 itself isn't anomalous, simply the equipment and energy source within it. In fact since the focus of the piece is the character development of the Black Queen, is it really necessary to have indestructible brain vats and energy from cold?

The more I think about this the less I like it, even. Really, what's the anomaly here? Foundation guards need to be stationed in northern Alaska? What civilian is going to be wandering the arctic circle? I could understand if they needed to be there in order to capture additional PoI-4056 but that contingency isn't mentioned, that's my brain doing the work to make sense where there is none.
