Sonata - In Midnight

10 May 2019 02:55

The night is long

My will is strong

The night is cold

But I grow old.

The night is dark,

My mind is stark.

The night is done.

I am gone. 

I'll keep this brief. This is a very personal skip for me, and I hope you guys enjoy.

This skip was intended to voice a problem I had. The inability to properly voice how I felt about certain people's passings, and how i wish it could've gone. Each log here is crafted to attempt to be as close to a family member as possible.

Music has been something to always make me feel things, and the idea of music that could open a grieving person's shell made me wish I had something like it in my life.

Thanks to Kreiner, Rura, UncannyClown, Brew, FloppyPhoenix, KnightAlaska, Nico, and Laneous for lookin at it.

This was a tough vote for me for a few reasons.

I enjoy the writing here. It's very expressive, the way you detail everyone's manner of speech, their composure, and how driven by the circumstances of their unique loss. It's very touching at times. Knowing what its like to lose someone that has always been in your life, I can resonate with some of these people. It's good.

However, it's mostly fluff. I only feel this connection because I understand the pain in the way they write it out, but it's because of the way they write it out that I can't truly empathize with them. They aren't real. No one talks like this, not even in severe mental degradation. No one just spouts about their tragic loss when locked in a room for 15 minutes. I think this could have been improved upon if there wasn't so much being written or said by the characters. Less is more.

In short, I guess what I'm saying is that I downvoted because the compulsion didn't make these people real.
