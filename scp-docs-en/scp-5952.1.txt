Pastor Warbler's dialogue was intended as an emulation of the archetypal southern US hellfire and salvation preacher you'd see at outdoor revivals, while the Unknown Man/Elder Rockwell is the archetypal  preacher. Feel free to explore your own language's quirks wrt such things.
Researcher Ford is referred to with gender neutral pronouns in the non-dialogue logs. I intended this because I saw her as a trans woman who hadn't realized she's trans/come out and just wanted to avoid dysphoria. Make of it however your language does that kind of thing.
This skip is meant to explore the American Religious Right, the  industry, and the contradictory use of brutality in pursuit of propriety. Absolutely feel free to add or cut stuff to get the point across to non-American audiences.

My favorite SCPs are those that get under my skin but never offer any final resolution. They itch. I'll spend days after reading worrying over them, trying to puzzle out a solution. Sometimes I'll have some insight, but the best ones leave me perplexed. Sometimes, years later, an image from these will pop into my head in a quiet moment - as I'm waking up, as I'm spacing out at my desk - and I'll wonder what, exactly, was up with that really weird SCP. You know. The one with the kid heads. The one with dead girl and the politician appearing in dreams. The ones that almost but don't quite form a coherent image. That feel like they should. There are alarming details but no obvious connections between them.

How does it all fit together? What am I missing? Would knowing ease the unease or chill it into full-on dread?

This has that feeling. As you continue this, please withhold explanations and connections. Resist the temptation to show off elaborate dioramas. It will be much better that way.
