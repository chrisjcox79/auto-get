I think that's getting a bit too in-universe. I suggest that anyone wishing to use the alloy bring it up in chat and get a consensus from whoever happens to be online. Or, you know, if you're planning to write an SCP involving it, just do a bit of thinking on the subject. "Does this SCP really need their utensils made out of Telekill? Is it really necessary to build the SCP space station out of Telekill to fend off attacks by psychic aliens?"

I know SCPs are supposed to stand up on their own, but it there are quite a few articles and stories that put 148 to good use (the tragic long-range empathy girl is a perfect example)

It's something that makes the Foundation's continued existence and basic operations seem a little more plausible. In addition to the hundreds of weird and awful horrors they keep locked up, they also have a few useful (but not TOO useful) tools to deal with otherwise insurmountable challenges.

It's also a "success story"- an anomalous object with an actual use, supplying a motive for keeping all the other dubiously useful stuff around in case they've got some terribly helpful properties waiting to be discovered.

This and the healing pills would definitely be overkill if they weren't in limited supply. As they are, they offer a little variety to the series. Of all the SCPs to cross-reference in another article, 148 is one of the better ones.. provided it isn't abused (the memetic tiger kitten requiring "armor made of 148" in the containment procedures springs to mind)

18 Feb 2011 17:22

So, a thought to remove some of the annoyingly high utility: Make 148 ablative. Maybe for just screening out "background noise" (i.e. shielding against a receptive telepath being able to sense thoughts), it works as described, but against more serious stuff like SCP-882, the Telekill gradually erodes or degrades. Alternatively, even that "background noise" degrades it, just more slowly due to the lower intensity. Thus, the already limited quantity is gradually getting smaller, and there's plenty of incentive to work out containment methods that don't require it.

As an alternative, this property could be used to (in effect) non-comically decommission/-ARC 148, by explaining that the degradation wasn't discovered until most of the stock had already been compromised, and what was left got used up during the transition to non-148-dependent containment protocols.

I have an alternate idea:

We've agreed that Telekill is a cop-out. We've agreed that Telekill has been used way too much (though I figure a lot of the articles that used them have since been deleted). Yet some to most of us think it's useful at least some of the time.

Why don't we just say that it's all been used? It still works forever, there just isn't any more of it. If some gets freed up, we can say it was automatically allocated to something else as per standing orders. If a really, REALLY good SCP comes around that uses it, we can say some was freed up as above and allocated here. Then we decom or ARC it and say it can't be used again. We can throw something into either the site rules or the How To Write guide mandating it can only be de-decommissioned in very, very isolated conditions with Clef's permission (or three senior staff votes, or something) and any other use is grounds for automatic deletion (grandfathering in all the already-accepted uses).

This doesn't preclude a rewrite, just takes care of the loose end.

How about this: instead of telekill blocking psychic energy, it scrambles psychic energy which passes through it. And while that prevents the psychic energy from having it's intended effect, scrambled psychic energy is bad. If you wear a helmet make of telekill and a psychic tries to telepathically say  to you, the scrambled energy drives you insane. Line a room with telekill to contain a reality-warper whose powers are psychic in nature, he tries to use his powers to melt a hole through the telekill, and the scrambled energy rips a hole in the fabric of reality which lets all of 682's relatives come through.

This still leaves some uses for telekill:

Line a room with it to prevent remote viewing.
Wear a helmet of it to prevent anyone from reading your mind.
Wear a helmet in circumstances where being driven insane is preferable to being mind-controlled.
A quick way to test if an SCP has any psychic component: put a helmet on a D-class and see if he goes insane.
Bullets which can punch through any shield a psychic might use.
