The concept is good, but the compound needs to be more than just Cl2F6. A variant or evolution of P680 containing an Antimony- or Bismuth-Cl2F6 halorganic metal complex would make more sense chemically and be more believable from a  standpoint.

Example 

The flowers, roots and vines of SCP-1621 secrete a compound in place of sap or nectar which, following the initial containment of a colony in C████████, R████, was identified to be a variant of P680 pigment containing dichlorine hexafluoride (Cl2F6) in a bismuth organometallic complex. The biomolecule vaporizes in the open at temperatures higher than 13 degrees Celsius, is colorless as a gas and smells sweet.
