First, only if the effects were demonstrated to be strong enough in character to reach that end level. And second, whether or not the dissociation occurred, a widespread disbelief in cooperation would doom humanity in any case. Furthermore, we aren't really presented with evidence that the widespread disbelief is actually happening; it is merely presented as a hypothesis with some circumstantial evidence that we're supposed to just take at face value as happening.

On the contrary, the entire world has coordinated its efforts at a far greater and more aligned rate since the 1960s. For a world to be sliding back from a high-cooperation state would require it to take place in a much different world from the one we're in.

So, yes, the scenario you describe does result in extinction. But I can't read that scenario (or even a risk of that scenario) as existing based on my reading of the document.
