Clickcritique

close

passerine aviary
附加记录：    

Over half of all birds are passerine, so I'm not sure this specificity is necessary.

an insect diet consistent with that of members of the species Mimus polyglottos(northern mockingbird)
附加记录：    

Mockingbirds are omnivorous, so they eat berries, seeds, fruit, many other small animals (both invertebrates and vertebrates) and even tree sap. A mockingbird fed just insects would likely be pretty malnourished.

are genetically mockingbirds
附加记录：    

So are they  or genetically mockingbirds?

SCP-2619-1 will instead turn into the subject's least preferred species of bird and become hostile
附加记录：    

How does that make sense? Presumably the bird is trying to get in good with the human, right? Why not turn into like a Little Penguin, or kakapo, or something equally non-threatening?

SCP-2619-1 contains much higher quantities of anomalous DNA than other instances of SCP-2619, with approximately 105 chromosomes, more than 5 times more than a standard mockingbird
附加记录：    

That is not how DNA works. At all.

5 chromosomes containing an unknown form of prokaryotic DNA
附加记录：    

Prokaryotes almost exclusively have only one chromosome, and maybe a few plasmids.

chromosomes containing triple-helix nucleotide chains
附加记录：    

This is very plainly not DNA then.

These chains have since been identified as trioxyribonucleic acid
附加记录：    

No, they wouldn't be. The  in DNA refers to the lack of an oxygen on the 5-carbon sugar used to construct the molecule, not how many strands are in the helix.

A black phoebe, //sayornis nigricans/
附加记录：    

By the time you read this, I will have corrected it for you, but the genus name of a species should always be capitalized.

were found to have pure mockingbird DNA in 75% of all cells
附加记录：    

Again, not really how DNA works.

suggesting the birds were actually mockingbirds genetically altered to appear as different species
附加记录：    

No, that would suggest that they were maybe close relatives, or some kind of freak hybrid. A 25% genetic difference is pretty big.

So this article is pretty well written, from a technical standpoint, but the actual object is sort of bland. It's a bird that transforms into other birds, and that's about it. There are some hints at backstory throughout the article, but I don't feel that you develop it enough to keep a reader interested.

Also: You seem to have a few misunderstandings as to how DNA and genetics actually work.

I don't know about it absolutely not working, if you used totally different bases (with more 3-D stereochemistry than our relatively flat bases) that could form trimers by hydrogen bonds I think there are probably theoretically possible geometries. It wouldn't have higher density as an information storage molecule, probably, because there would still be only one sense strand, although it might have better error correction (though it might just have more errors due to complexity; this is an information theory problem that I doubt is trivial to solve). It won't however be directly compatible with our protein synthesis mechanisms, although obviously codons are totally arbitrary and it could produce the same polypeptides as end products.

Fold

22 Jul 2016 18:58

and I'm fairly certain you could not construct a (suitably stable) triple helix with standard ACTG bases
附加记录：    

Definitely not, the stereochemistry is all wrong. Nitrogenous bases are relatively flat because they are cyclic resonance structures. This is how nucleotide base pairing even works, more complex stereochemistry would mean more entropy, which is bad for lossless data storage.

part of the idea was that it wasn't stable, thus the metamorphosis, but I got rid of the paragraph about the chromosomes
附加记录：    

It wouldn't be unstable, so much as impossible. You should take a quick look at the Wikipedia article on nucleobases and look how the base pairs form dimers by hydrogen bonds in a flat plane. There's simply no place for a third base to connect.
