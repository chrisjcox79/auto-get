It's intentional: A -EX was a real SCP that was later found not to be anomalous. So: Incorrect information from the original SCP document is in strikethrough text, information that is still relevant in normal text, and corrections and added information after the item was found not to be anomalous are in italics. I know that isn't how the other -EX's were formatted, but I think this is easier to read/follow than the  version.

I'm not going to say you're wrong, since originally I had this up as a regular SCP. My rationale was that the technology is a bit off; I'm 99.9999% sure it's not actually possible to make one of these things, and have it work as described. But a bunch of folks commented it would be better as an -EX, I assume because the effects are completely explained by technology, and the tech doesn't sound all that futuristic.

It's in the grey area of sounding like a real thing someone could make, but it actually isn't.

+here...

-trashed?

OK, I assumed a ton of things are possible here, and most of them aren't:

Dr. Bright is correct that an audio transmitter probably won't fit into a normal-sized d20.
I don't think there is really an analog of DMSO that is solid at room temperature.
You probably can't mix DMSO with plastic and still have plastic.
Mixing DMSO with hallucinogens probably doesn't really work to make you trip via skin contact  although there are rumors to the contrary: Look up Lysergic Acid Crypto-Ethylene on the Web and decide for yourself.
Psychoacoustics seems to be a somewhat dodgy area of science and I'm pretty sure you can't hypnotize someone with some weird combination of sounds.
Even if all the stuff above worked, I don't think the effect would be that consistent.
