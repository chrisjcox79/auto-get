09 Jun 2014 23:13

Nicely done. Anomalous broadcasts are a favorite of mine, so I admit some critical latitude might have been given, but overall the segments were so disturbingly weird that I felt compelled to upvote. The fact that the show keeps up with evolving video distribution technology is a nice touch. I especially like the  one, which is apparently a live-action Cyriak animation.

I do have one issue. This:

Good night! And laugh! And laugh! Just laugh! Make more laugh for laughter. Laugh with us. Laugh with us. (Studio audience joins in unison) Laugh with us. Laugh with us. We hunger. We hunger.
附加记录：    

The mangled-English title made me think the host was someone/something that kind of spoke English, which for whatever reason increased the creepiness of this, so when he shows up speaking normally, it was a bit of a disappointment for me. More of a problem, though, is that the piece depends on the conceit that everyone involved is inexplicably playing along in the spirit of normal light-hearted fun, whereas this bit is more menacing  directly threatening type stuff. It's also coming across as a little silly, given the effectiveness of the rest of the piece.

Still though, upvoted.

I also found there to be a tonal disconnect between the language used in the sign-off message, the show description, and the rest of the piece. The episode descriptions are gloriously bizarre (Thatcher was particularly unsettling), the conceit is compelling (anomalous broadcast), but this  language is just out of place. If the intended effect is to convince the reader that the things responsible are inhuman, well, that's abundantly clear from the broadcast content. The altered language just creates a spike in silliness where one isn't really appropriate, especially since the host seems perfectly capable of normal speech.
