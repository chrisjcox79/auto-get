It's always spooky when babies get involved. Babies are spooky.

In all seriousness, this is an awesome idea executed really well. I like the idea of (what appears to be) a benevolent group of entities trying to stop child abuse through anomalous communications. I can imagine the panic a mother might feel, sitting alone in a room at night with this thing as it threatens to harm her child. There wasn't too much in the article that was explicit to that effect; I'm not sure if that's good or bad, but it did force me to use my imagination.

One thing I was a little confused on: why have that point-of-sale tracking thing? Why wouldn't the Foundation order retailers to stop carrying and selling 2672? I suppose that might raise too much suspicion, but I can't tell for sure from the containment procedures. Anyway, a +1 from me!

07 May 2017 22:54

Started off strong, with a good buildup of horror - that this was subverted for something actually benevolent (however malicious it seemed) was nice.

My issues lie with the 'second' message being a little too expository (though, as they're literally trying to communicate their intentions, I guess that can't be helped?). Something simpler and less direct would have been preferable.

Second, I may be missing something, but I'm not quite getting the logic of the transmissions/threat message to begin with. How are these mothers supposed to somehow step in and prevent the abuse of another's child if there's no indication they need to take action in the first place? It seems to be an ineffective and obtuse tactic.

Fold

09 May 2017 06:11

+Answersquestions

-Questionanswers

The idea that the people behind this had was that eventually one of the mothers would recognize something in one of the transmissions that would lead back to the abused baby and maximized their chances of this happening by forcing them to listen carefully to the whole thing. Which actually did end up working at least once; in turn, I'm sure they'd say that this whole thing was worthwhile as saving one child's life justifies everything else (just trying to explain character motivation, not my own personal views etc.)

If you're asking why they didn't just hand the monitors over to the police or someone like the Foundation, it's because they were worried that the monitors would get locked in a room somewhere and ignored. And, well, look what happened.

Finally, if you still think this all sounds a bit obtuse and that there must be a better way to go about this note also that this is a purely audio-only device with no visual component whatsoever that's called Watchful Eye. And of course, the overall concept here of terrifying mothers to save babies. These people aren't nearly as clever as they think they are. In turn, I'd argue that this makes their creepy stuff even more awful - being well-intentioned, powerful, and stupid is often worse than simply being malicious.
