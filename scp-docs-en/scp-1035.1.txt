From  guide:

a thing that will liquefy bone in a quarter-mile radius is very dangerous, and would need special containment, but it’s not interesting.
附加记录：    

This is too close to that for me to upvote.

However, writing and tone are unusually good for a first-try SCP, so I can't downvote it either. My only nitpick is a little too heavy use of expungement. For example:

It is difficult to determine the precise date  effects first began at or around █ February, 19██.
附加记录：    

Knowing the date is pretty precise. You can just say  part and state exact date. Also, I hate 19██-like dates and would suggest that you just pick a date instead of blackboxing it, but that's mostly my personal reaction and other people may not agree with me on this.

Another example:

involving a case of necrotizing [REDACTED].
附加记录：    

Again, just pick a disease that causes necrosis and use it instead of [REDACTED]. Also, my spellchecker says that necrotizing isn't a word, but he says many things and I don't usually trust him.

And again, from the table:

Between █ and ██ hours of exposure [DATA EXPUNGED]
附加记录：    

If you have a whole section with the only content been [DATA EXPUNGED], you have a problem with expungement.

And yet again:

The [REDACTED] Project
附加记录：    

Just pick a name and stick with it. On average, the less expungements article has, the stronger effect and meaning each of them have.

I don't know how to vote on this. Writing is mostly good, but concept is bland. I don't like to no-vote articles, but I just may have to in this case, unless you somehow expand the idea into something more interesting, so that I can upvote without feeling guilty.

Hi. Thanks for feedback.

To be completely honest, I couldn't really think of anything all that disgusting when I hit a certain point in designing the chart, so I just expunged some of the data and left it at that. I thought that maybe someone else's imagination could think up a more horrifying sequence of events to plug the gaps, preferably involving blowfly larvae and gangrene. Maybe I will elaborate a little

Same thing with the project name. Of course, maybe it's just secret enough that hiding what it's called is required, but the Foundation isn't particularly concerned with low-level employees knowing that they've got someone working on the thing. If I ever think up a good name, I'll change that.

For the disease: I was thinking of necrotizing fasciitis when I was writing the cover story. Didn't want to go and say it outright, so I redacted that part. According to Wikipedia, Herod the Great may have had the thing in his groin regions. Isn't that a lovely way to die.

I see what you mean about the specific date. I will edit that ASAP.

If there's really a large amount of demand for it, I may add an interview log or something with young Sally ██████ and have her tell the doctor/embedded agent about how she wants her arm to be whole again. I'm not sure how it'll turn out, so unless loads of people really want to see me try I won't do it yet.

I'll argue that the item is a little more detailed than a simple  but that's really up to opinion.

The mittens work nicely. I'd ditch the second interview and keep the first; the second is pretty redundant. I also echo Anqxyr's point about excessive expungement. A case of necrotizing fasciitis, for example, isn't something the Foundation would classify, nor is it too disgusting for this site's readership. Go ahead and put it in.

In the future, if you're having trouble thinking of something sufficiently horrible, don't just expunge it. That rarely works very well. Ask the chat instead: there are experienced writers here who can help you fine-tune your expungement, and there are certainly enough depraved minds to help you come up with something cringeworthy. ;)
