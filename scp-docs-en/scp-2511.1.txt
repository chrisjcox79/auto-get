Fourthly, read below.

+block

–block

The kappa is more commonly found in Mongolia – Severus Snape, Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

The weird implication is that the kappas' event might be tied to a fictional story written by humans. The reference to the Day of Flowers (from SCP-1000) hints that humanity's triumph over Bigfoot might or might not be the work of some kappas reading bedtime stories to their kin rather than anything glorious.

As for the final addendum, if we break down the wording, kappa in kanji literally means 河童 or . A child fell into a river worked just as well, I suppose.

And of all things that must come true from fiction, why an obscure line that has no plot relevance in the book/series? God has weird ways of doing things, as they say.
