I love it. Very well written, as expected, but the articles really comes into its own with its amalgamation of themes. Fifthism, IJAMEA, and Lyle Burnley seem very obscure and incompatible (probably making it the hardest request of the Art Exchange) yet you succeed with flying colors in combining these entities' essences. I would never think to relate Fifthism's reality-bending madness to colonialism's truth-garbling horrors. Perhaps more impressive, it uses all these factors without relying on other source material for understanding.

Simply put, +1

My review from the Art Exchange forum:

This is a fantastic article about a semi-Fifthist artifact's interactions with a Viatnamese uprising against French rule in the late 1930s. It's pretty amazing how much detail the author went into, and it's extra amazing how much character he wrote in. You can feel the love for history here. It's not just an interesting conflict in its own right, but it talks about how information gets recorded in the first place, carrying a theme of  to avoid attributing everything to direct Lovecraft inspiration nowadays but this is even set in the 1930s, you gotta realize that that's really contributing to the Lovecraft vibe here) as I always love to see in these Fifthist pieces. Good fucking job. One of the most solid creations for this art exchange.
