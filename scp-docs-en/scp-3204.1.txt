I really like the idea. The concept, the backstory, and the item itself is pretty dope, and kinda scary. I don't particularly like the incident report, mostly because I don't think it actually expands on the initial concepts in a meaningful way, but it isn't enough to tank my view on the core concept itself.

However, I don't like the execution of the article. I know this is incredibly unhelpful, but I am having a hard time putting my finger on why. I don't like the way the information is laid out. There is something about the order in which we the reader receive the relevant SCP information that almost feels wrong. At least, thats me trying to figure out why I don't like that portion.

So, a novote for me. It's not horrible at all. I think i'm just being unreasonable.

It's not baaaad. It isn't. It's written and formatted decent, and the anomaly is - mildly discomforting. I don't really know what the tie is between Chernobyl, and evil horse statue. Is it just 'radiation are superpowers?' If it is - okay, that's as fine as a plot device as many, many others have used, it's just not very compelling. And that's really my primary problem is that I just don't quite feel anything about this. I'm not really sure why it has to be a horse, or a statue to be a thing that disappears people. The thing I do like is invoking that old sentiment of a thing the Foundation can't really hope to control or understand but mitigate, but that isn't really milked enough here for my taste.

I don't know, I think I could like this if the individual pieces fit better together. I'm downvoting for now, but with a little work I think this could be better than it is.

11 Nov 2018 16:09

For the most part, I enjoyed reading this! But there were a couple things that bugged me.

+Open

-Close

A few of the redactions seem unnecessary:

SCP-3204 has been described as marble, granite, limestone, concrete, and [REDACTED].
附加记录：    

Maybe it's just me, but redacting one of the possible materials SCP-3204 is made of seems superfluous, and doesn't add anything to the article. I'd recommend removing the [REDACTED] or replace it with an actual material; maybe something anomalous?

The redactions in the Level 4 log are ridiculous. Now, I understand that a level 4 researcher wouldn't be privy to particularly sensitive information that's restricted only to level 5 clearance. But why in the world would the amount of time something took be redacted? Placing blackboxes over things for no justifiable reason implies the author didn't want to bother with writing it. Which, can be understandable, but it's also lazy.

I'd recommend removing the first addendum, as the Foundation doesn't understand (and possibly can't understand due test 3204/07) what happens to sapient beings when 3204 makes them disappear.

-Close

Other than that I found the rest of the redactions to fit well.
