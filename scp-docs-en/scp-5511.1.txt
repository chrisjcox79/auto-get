Very succinct

17 Mar 2020 15:41

Short and to the point. At first, I thought the number was the number of potential future descendants but thinking about it the title's a lot more literal isn't it? With how random it was and how little information the incident gave I think the very short format fits well tone-wise, but two points kind of detracted from the tone for me.

First the milliton, it feels like an unnecessarily obtuse unit to use in this instance. It's both outside a useful scale for the unit and without clarifying that it is 'of TNT' in the text body forces the reader to either deduce out the unit or look to the footnotes. Just one kilogram of TNT, an equivalent number of Joules, or both would be more appropriate.

Secondly is the word NEET being used. This is a lot more open to debate in my opinion but it's always come across more as a slang word to me than something that you'd put in a report like this. 'unemployed man', 'Ōsaka, Japan', and 'age 23' would get the point across more elegantly and more in line with the tone.

Otherwise enjoyed the article though +1. Haven't been keeping up with new stuff the past few years and since all your stuff seems new I'll be sure to check it all out.
