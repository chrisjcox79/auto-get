It could be argued that "wrong" is relative. From the "survival of the fittest" perspective, it's not wrong at all. Many species behave just as the population of 752 does, as it's the most efficient means of ensuring that successful traits are passed on and unsuccessful ones are not. Our current society definitely does not do this. Humanity is accumulating traits that would be lethal in a natural environment. Those traits that would lead to early death before being able to reproduce in a natural population are being preserved and passed on in modern human society.

But, by our own society's standards, yes, you're wrong. But who's to say which society's standards are right?
