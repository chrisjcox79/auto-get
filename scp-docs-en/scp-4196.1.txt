I really like this. Articles that use the real life horrors human history is replete with are too scarce in my opinion. But I do have a few nitpicks.

For one thing, the third interview log still has a few XXXXs that haven’t been removed yet. The last one is missing a few line breaks towards the end. Finally, though it is far more subjectively an error, I vociferously object to using “4196” as a stand-in for the proper term “SCP-4196” in the interview logs. I mean, we wouldn’t dream of doing that in the description or containment procedures, would we?

Speaking of the containment procedures, I don’t think the idea that continuously monitoring the corpses will root SCP-4196 in one place is quite justified. Why exactly that would help is not mentioned elsewhere in the article, and it is especially strange as the rest of the containment procedures seem to say that this is the first time SCP-4196 has shifted positions and that the Foundation is just waiting it out.

Downvoted - Excellent Taste, Questionable Execution

21 Jul 2019 12:48

So, I'm generally gonna say that this is an excellent idea, and the work has good taste. In other words, I can imagine this set in the world that is otherwise mundane and see that all the interactions make logical sense broadly. The actual anomaly is fairly compelling from the outset, however, there are several mysteries that are never really explained. There's enough that makes me think the author knows what it is and that there's a coherent reason behind it, which is good enough for me.

Now, I had some frustrations reading this piece:

When I first began to read, I scrolled past the top image, hit the containment proceedures, and then had to look around for the object class. Given that this is the first thing I deal with when reading this piece, it is equivalent to a first line of prose in the story. And in this case I couldn't even read the first line of the piece because of the way it's laid out. Human English reads from left to right, with less and less attention given to the right side of the page (known from eye tracking studies), so having the object class be in tiny font on the rightmost side makes it difficult to pick out. That this is an intentional decision is rather frustrating and made me immediately sour on reading the piece overall.
The test logs- are they actually telling me anything I don't already know?
I dislike the way the last interview was laid out. I think it's the researcher's behavior paired with the sudden turn of the anomaly. Most specifically, the researcher's forceful threats were rather aggravating and I felt like they were unnecessary. On top of that, the sudden turn on the anomaly doesn't make much sense IMO, unless we assume the anomaly can read into people's pasts but can't read into other secrets like 'I am recording on a microphone'. That it shifts to something broadly dangerous is sudden, and I think a little clumsy.
The grandmother in all the interviews is very stereotypical, almost caricature. If this is intentional, this is fine, but if it's not intentional then perhaps consider leaning less on 'things grandmas say' stuff.

Living the dream, or dreaming the life?
