MrUnimport, you have surmised, in one short paragraph, the entire point i was trying to make, and a salute you.

The staff should be a background thing, like the Cthulhu Mythos was originally started: a cosmic, semi in-joke between multiple authors, which could be followed or expanded on by those who tried to look for it, or go wholly unnoticed. Reading "The Shadow over Innsmouth" with a good understanding of the Cthulhu mythos lets you see a lot of inter-connected ideas…but it can be read on it's own, with no foreknowledge, and still be a solid horror story.

I guess I've always looked at the staff as the "Mythos" of the SCP…the background "in joke" that bubbles up from time to time. That's not to say that they have no place in a starring role (see the Wanderlust series), just not in the main SCP articles as much.

Yeah, an E-class! We even forget there are E-Classe personel…

Here's the info:

"E: Temporarily affixed to the start of a security clearance (e.g. E1), this is used when a new SCP object has been discovered but is still in an unsecured or uncontrolled state. E-clearance personnel are tasked with bringing the SCP into effect and maintaining it. Once the SCP are in place and the object is secure, the E is removed and the personnel are given the appropriate number on the end of their clearance."
