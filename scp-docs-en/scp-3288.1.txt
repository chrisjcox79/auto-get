21 Nov 2017 00:15

I figured implied in this case meant not literally showing or describing it, not referencing it. But maybe I understood that wrong. I've had difficulty knowing where to place the list of deviations because anywhere else felt as though I was avoiding stating *what* the anomaly is, which is considered taboo on the site.

Experimentation is pretty much trying to understand how they work and to fully grasp their anomalous potential - I had no intention of actually detailing experiments. Basically, wanted to let the reader know that the Foundation wouldn't contain ALL of them, as that would be infeasible to contain all of them and it makes sense to eliminate those outside a contained population. Termination sometimes makes logical sense. I suppose it would be better to say that they are kept for research purposes, rather than experiments, as genetic testing is the only thing that really makes sense to me.

21 Nov 2017 01:56

He made the most sense to be used. I am often uncomfortable with inaccurate depictions of people. I will say that he was an intellectual and I did not want to paint him as initially monstrous, but a product of his time. This is the Holy Roman Empire and a kind monarch is extremely rare. So I tried to capture a developing zealotry that would come just before his death.

The Habsburgs would be infamous for their generations of inbreeding. That is entirely historical. This also led to mental instability along with intellectual and physical disabilities among its rulers. They had grown so inbred that the family was literally dying out. Wanted to play with the potential horror - you often see the  trope but I wanted to apply it to a people who really viewed themselves as somehow innately superior to all other people, as royals generally did.

I wanted to imply that Leopold I was educated (and he was, with an interest in alchemy as well) but was still afflicted by the . There isn't a lot about him besides his interests.

Edit: So, I will say it is very much a fictional take and not remotely meant to represent the figure himself. Being the emperor at the time, no one else made sense.
