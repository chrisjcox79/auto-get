11 Aug 2011 18:40

I really quite like the idea of a age-old bootstrap program that uses humans as an early stage. I think it's got a lot of potential for an interesting SCP. Although I feel you may have missed one or two tricks here.

Reading this SCP, there are two interesting stories that spring to mind. The first is who made this thing. The second is what the program is working towards, and why. Obviously you can't go and say these things, but a few more hints and correct usage of [DATA REDACTED] could give just enough information to get readers to form their own conclusions. As it stands, there isn't quite enough (carefully redacted) information to do this. Perhaps the manuscripts themselves are part of a chain of bootstrap processes, and were written down by some medieval monk by a mimetic compulsion from a earlier source.

Also, a minor point but I personally bugs me when people redact important information from the containment procedures. How are staff meant to know how to contain this thing if important information is missing? Information should only be redacted from the containment procedures if it is a less important (yet revealing) aside comment, or if it details a procedure so powerful and horrible that only a few can be permitted to know it. This use doesn't give the impression of either.

Still a good basic concept, and a further tentative up-vote.
