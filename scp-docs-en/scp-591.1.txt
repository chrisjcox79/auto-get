17 Aug 2015 19:38

Neutral vote. It's written to a degree of technical skill and thoughtfulness that it's an immersive document and a good exercise in the format, so it serves as a decent example of tone and execution. I will also note that it's not excessively long or sporting filler addenda, which is an increasing issue these days. Suffice to say, it's well-written enough and emblematic enough of the good qualities of a mainlist article that I can't downvote.

Conceptually, I'm left somewhat cold. Leaving aside the bells and whistles of the writeup, at heart it's a video game what transports the player into the game, which is kind of a predictable and well-worn trope in fiction since the 1980s. Does that disqualify something from being good? No, but I think there needs to be a little more narrative there for me to be invested in such a thing. The Wondertainment reference isn't as obnoxious as those usually are since it didn't come with all of the attendant Willy Wonka palette-swap goofiness that those usually entail, but there also really wasn't any reason for it be there either, aside from a default association between that concept and products intended for children.

Also,nitpick

The technical skill on display is considerable, and I respect it. But the content isn't quite there for me.
