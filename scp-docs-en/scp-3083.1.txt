I think I'm missing something here.

The article is constructed well enough - it's technically decent, the parts are all laid out and appear to come to a revelatory point where it's revealed Doctor Benich has a tumor in her throat, but then it just ends. I don't feel any satisfaction, any resolution or even any sense of absence or emptiness. I just don't understand this. It feels to me like there is an underlying story here - the fact that each of the alterations results in a head, the fact that most of the items are related to Benich and reek of some sort of multiversal fuckup - but I don't see the thread that's supposed to connect them all. Why is it in geosynchronous orbit? Why is it just her head? Why was there a boar in there? etc.

+0 novoting because i think there's something cool here but i'm not smart enough to divine what it is.

No, it sounds like you've got it. There's a story here, but it's not meant to be super-deep.

+it...

–block

In another universe, Foundation Magus Benich tried to cure his thyroid cancer himself. Whatever he did, it went super-wrong and not only blew his damn fool head off, but shot it into another universe.

And then, either A) this pattern continues with each Benich in the multiverse, or B) the spell starts killing all the other Beniches in the multiverse, with our Benich being saved because she lives in, y'know, a place they use medicine and not super-destructive, reality-bending magic to solve cancer. There's probably a C, but I forgot it.
