Authors notes.

02 Feb 2013 23:29

This has gone through several stages of chat review; many thanks to Dmatix, Rumetzen, Woggle (almost forgot aaaa), Voct, Troy, and Mann in particular. I guess I’ll just note that when reading, keep in mind how the original owner of the SCP passed away, and what kind of people would be likely to use the SCP.

I came up with this idea based on a quote I heard once, “Peace of mind is the best sleeping pill.” The idea to walk away with here is basically, “It’s alright to escape to dreams, as long as you always wake up willing to face the world again.”

Spoilers,them.

hide

- Dosage has nothing to do with how long a person sleeps after consuming the liquid. Consecutive use does, to an extent.

- Someone who is just satisfied with their life, not necessarily totally happy with it, wouldn’t sleep much more than normal if they took a dose. Someone in a mid-life crisis might sleep for days on end.

- Taking a dose of the liquid multiple times is an act of giving in, of trying to escape. People will always look back fondly on the past, but one shouldn’t need something like this to do so.

- Someone who drinks from the bottle will experience memories exactly as they did the first time, not being able to think back on it or pass judgment on it until after they wake up.

- As indicated by the MCD note, there definitely exist more instances of the bottle. A lot of people rely on it, but not everyone wakes up after each use.

Edit: The image belongs to me. The bottle pictured is a trinket I picked up while thrift shopping one day.
