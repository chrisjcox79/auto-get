02 Feb 2019 15:34

This article’s tragedy is that it’s a boring story about a magic mecha in space. The article clearly hews after the super robot sub-genre of mecha. Unfortunately, the clinical tone required in an SCP strips out the style and flashiness of the action sequences and replaces them with a dull sterility. I don’t get any frisson from learning about the exoskeleton’s cool weapons or how they work, because you fall back on thaumaturgy as a narrative crutch to let you have all these cool weapons without bothering to talk about them in any depth. Katrina Kuang and Nobody aren’t developed at all, and the setting—a space station near Mars—has zero consequence on the story at all. You could have implemented some cool zero-G sequences or utilized the geometry of the O’Neill cylinder for an escape sequence, but the space station reads no differently from a generic metropolis set twenty years into Broken Masquerade. The article feels like one big missed opportunity.
