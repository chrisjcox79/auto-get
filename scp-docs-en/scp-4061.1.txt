Fold

19 May 2019 07:37

Mostly it is a no-vote, pending to downvote.

It isn't bad, per se, but it isn't really interesting, and as DarkStuff points out you have more than one narrative, and neither actually contributes to the other.

The set of tests with the dog is redundant, since the gene was already isolated and tested under more controllable environment elsewhere. Also, its toll on the organisms lifespan is for pure shock value without really adding nothing to the story. It doesn't even sell them as too cold, because they didn't had to know the mutt would have the same reduction on lifespan as the bacteria.

My suggestion is that you decide what aspect of the article you want to focus on, and develop from there. The story about scientific discovery and manipulation of an anomalous gene? The story of how the anomalous effect affected the short but eventful life of this dog? The espionage drama around the new development that was stolen through a mole inside the Foundation?

All of those are interesting concepts for a story, but they all came and went too suddenly here and with no lasting effect on the reader.
