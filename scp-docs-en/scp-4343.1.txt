File 4000/031 - Yes, this is the war in heavens as described in Bible. The deputy resembles the .

File 4000/032 matches the Gun-Yu Legend.

The  may or may not be Baghdad Battery._.

And the Azores / City of Orders? Its location somehow coincides with Plato's description of Atlantis, and I am surprised to find out that it also coincides with that of Mount Buzhou in Chinese legends. To be short, according to legends, it was a mountain that supported the heavens, and when a heavenly war broke out, the mountain was smashed. As a result, skys fell and and earth cracked, causing a great flood.

The Unseen is originally an Islamic theological concept (الغيب, secrets known only to Allāh, sometimes considered to be related with magic and future-telling), but in this skip, the concept may have different meanings.

I always wonder what happened in those very ancient times. According to Book of Shang(尚书) and Book of History(史记)(both are history recorded by court officials in ancient China), ancient emperors before the flood (~4000 years ago) lived very, very long life(100). Legends say they can command magical creatures. Then I figure out that human, as described in Bible, can live 120 years before the flood. When I brainstormed this skip I thought about the legends. What if they are true? What if ancient civillizations and prehistoric civillizations are much more advanced than us?

Though flood myth exists among various cultures, the time that a flood occurred is inconsistent. At first I cannot make a proper time setting, and then I come up with the idea that end of the world has happened many times before. The Qur'an also mentioned that there are , and, here it is.

11 Aug 2018 22:58

Eh. This is kind of all over the place.

Not only do I have to agree with TL333s that this comes across as more prose than a clinical SCP article, but your characters here fall rather flat. SCP-4000 should ideally portray some kind of character. Even if they are not Satan himself, I'd imagine that the leader of a prehistoric/mythical army would be rather charismatic. Instead, this comes across as rather wooden, with choppy sentences that boil down to exposition. If this is 4000's attempt to win over the Foundation, they have utterly failed there.

That aside, you try to plot to weave several other bits of Foundation lore here into a consistent mythology and I don't think it gels as well as it might. I don't buy the Daevites locking themselves in a book as a means to escape the End, nor do I buy SCP-2000 as being created by the Clay. Nor SCP-682 as being a magical experiment.

On the whole, its an ambitious article, sure, but I feel in its ambition it sort of collapses in on itself, and crucial parts that are needed to carry the narrative are not there.

-1.
