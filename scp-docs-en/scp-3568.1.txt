Really close to an upvote, here. This is neat, but there are issues. For instance, Addendum A is overly redacted, to the point of illegibility of the second part.

If anything's keeping me from upvoting, it's the interview, which is very  and honestly rather dry. There's a lot of explaining going on here, like telling us the relationship between stage 3 and the density of osmium. Or that osmium is the densest yadda yadda, that's something people can look up and find out themselves.

And I suppose I'm a little put out that this only suggests a path forward at the end. Like, I want to know what happens with a lot of those tests, not just whether or not they've been approved. That might be outside the scope of the story you're trying to tell, though, so don't take that as criticism.

So I get that there's some sort of signal coming deliberately from Draugr to Earth that these people receive when they view the image. The vocalized number is the subject's age on Draugr, and the imprinted number is like an accession number for humans on Earth. I really like some if not most of the ideas, but some issues exist:

The interview reads more like the  feeling.

I like open endings, but this feels like a tease, I personally really want the results, not even of all of them, the pending ones could still be pending and there wouldn't have to be a analysis of the results if you want to keep them open to interpretation.

The comparison with osmium probably doesn't help most readers as not a lot of people know that, it's useful in saying it's denser than pretty much all elements, but adding a comparison to lead or water or similar would give it a tangible weight for most.

The last 2 are more persobal preference things though.
