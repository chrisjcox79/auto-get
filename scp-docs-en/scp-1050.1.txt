The ceiling of the storage facility, specifically the area directly over SCP-1050-1, must be shielded to block radio waves broadcast at an intensity of [REDACTED].
附加记录：    

I'm not an electrical engineer, but even if the obelisk is transmitting the signal straight up I don't think that shielding only the ceiling immediately above it will block all of the radio signal.

SCP-1050 appears to be parts of an interstellar “early warning” system of unknown origin. Before the invention of more modern forms of rapid communication, some societies would light bonfires atop watchtowers, which would then be observed by the next watchtower, which would then light its own bonfire, and so on.
附加记录：    

Nothing in the article suggests this to me. Now if the obelisk started sending a copy of an SCP-1050-3, that would suggest something like a series of bonfires.

SCP-1050-3 consists of a series of radio messages uncovered at different times by radio telescopes on four continents and in orbit.
附加记录：    

Might want to have a note on how embedded Foundation agents are preventing SETI from finding out about this.

The numbers have been determined to be base-8 (Octal) representations of the forty base-10 (Decimal) integers in the obelisk message, preceded by another sixty integers following a similar pattern.
附加记录：    

Hmmm. That would mean that the obelisks on other planets are using the length of Earth years to generate the numbers. It could be that when another obelisk is pointed towards our solar system it switches to using Earth years, but then wouldn't it also switch to base 10? And wouldn't the signal from our obelisk use numbers that would make sense to whoever is receiving it?

Also, repeating information that our obelisk already has isn't something that an early-warning system would do.

I am not an electrical engineer either, but I believe this should work, since radio waves are just photons (the same waves/particles - light is made of) at a particular wavelength. You can block a flashlight with a piece of cloth, and x- and gamma rays with sufficiently thick lead, so it would stand to reason that radio waves are stoppable with shielding in one direction. Your dentist's office isn't completely lead shielded to protect the technician that gives you a bitewing x-ray - just  of the emission source is blocked.
