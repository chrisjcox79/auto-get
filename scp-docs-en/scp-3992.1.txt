17 Oct 2017 19:46

This is cute, it's written well (though you have a few grammar errors that could have been caught with more proofreading). It tells a little bit of a story - it's a simple one, but this is a perspective on Wondertainment I haven't seen before.

What keeps me at neutral is, as DrSevere pointed out, that this doesn't stand alone. A test log totally reliant on cross-links isn't an idea I'm a fan of, especially when the tests are basically the same thing happening the same way in each one. In a larger sense, though, I feel like most of the backstory/context of this is reliant on the relationship between Wondertainment and the foundation/the foundation and anomalies. This is a matter of personal taste, but I prefer skips that are about the Foundation encountering things in the world, rather than centered on the Foundation itself, and I feel like this is firmly in the latter category.

Still, it's a nice read, provoked a couple thoughts, and definitely is in the spirit of Halloween.

Article suggestions

18 Oct 2017 08:14

Thankyou for the feedback so far everyone.

Taking the most common piece of feedback into account, I'd like to share a few ideas I have for changes to add to SCP-3992.

I was thinking of featuring in the testing logs a few anomalous humanoids (not SCP's, simply people in Foundation custody) as well as a few SCP-████'s. To make the logs less crosslinky.

As well as this, I was thinking of giving the masks the ability to take the wearers personal preference into account, and change the costume they appear dressed in to their personal idea of what that monster would look like.

Thoughts?
