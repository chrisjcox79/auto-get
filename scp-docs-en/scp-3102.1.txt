There are a few things which I like and a few things which I don't about this, which balances out to a neutral vote.

I think you pull off the image of fire snakes about as well as can be expected; the details of its behavior helped in that regard. I also liked the voice of Tyson a lot there's enough hinting at something bigger to be effective.

On the downside, I think this suffers from a lot of pacing issues. The first interview feels largely redundant because we already know that the snakes do these things. The Tyson interview log is similarly long; I'm not sure you need to have him go on at such length.

The idea is simple - burning snakes. By the way you tried to present it, I think you intended to give off a poetic wrath-of-the-nature vibe, climaxing at the beautiful interview log in the end of the article.

Sadly, I don't think it worked. You failed to get the clinical tone necessary for the containment procedure and description by putting in too many strange words () in place of what could have been more easily understood technical terms. It also gets too wordy and a bit exhausting to read.

Neutral vote, because of the great interview log with Tyson H████. You should write a tale.
