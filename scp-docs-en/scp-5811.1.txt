27 Mar 2020 21:54

Hi, like I said, I genuinely don't mean to cause any offense. I'm not saying you can't have such characters without it being part of the story, but when you have 2 non binary characters it draws attention.

This wouldn't be a problem except that it implies something that, as far as I can tell, isn't there. This will be difficult to explain but I'll try and do so. Non binary is one of the few accepted ways of describing people where they are described by what they aren't, rather than what they are. Describing someone as non white for example would generally be considered offensive.

I say this in order to explain why when you say non binary, my brain automatically goes to binary, rather than non. Given this is an SCP article in always looking for the second layer so if I'm assuming that layer has something to do with binary I'm going to think this either relates to the relationship if the siblings, which it doesn't appear to, or it relates to the nebula.

I've never heard of a binary nebula before, and don't know what that would even be, but as a sci fi fan I have heard of binary star systems many times. I googled binary nebula and found reference to a discovery made a couple of years ago of a binary star system found inside a nebula.

Like I said, it's not that you can't have non binary characters, but in this instance in particular, having 2 non binary characters talking about space stuff, with the explanation being a mystery just leads me down the wrong road, and that's what I meant when I said it was distracting.

Hopefully I haven't caused any offense, as none was intended, but I hope I've explained well enough why this aspect took me out of the story somewhat.
