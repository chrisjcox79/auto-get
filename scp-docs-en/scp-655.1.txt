Fold

05 Oct 2012 23:07

Genus groupings reflect similarity of biology, which is not quite the same thing as evolutionary .
附加记录：    

Um, what? While the field of taxonomy technically does not include phylogenetics or vice versa, modern taxonomists almost universally work from an evolutionary standpoint, even if their species concepts vary. While phenetics and numerical taxonomy do have a place today, data from phylogenetic systematics dominate nearly every decision made with regard to revising modern taxonomy, even tentative taxonomic placement (which would include extraterrestrial life).

I will grant that, once upon a time, clades (at the genus or any other level) were crafted based on morphological or other similarity. I will also grant that the designation of one genus from another is somewhat arbitrary, again based on gross dissimilarity from other, related genera.

However, the science of taxonomy, starting with Linneaus, is hierarchical. One of Darwin's greatest triumphs with Origin was to show why a hierarchical taxonomy made sense: because it is a natural result of common descent.

If the author of this entry wants to leave it open to interpretation as to the origin of the Gray Guy, that's his/her place to do so. If they wished to imply alien more heavily, I submit they use a new, currently nonexistant genus, as per the International Codes of Zoological and Botanical Nomenclature.* Homonoides () or similar would work.

*Though technically neither of these codes applies because an alien, not being descendent from the ancestor of Earth animals or plants, is neither.
