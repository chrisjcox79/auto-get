Firstly; Alzheimer's and strokes are not solely related to aging. They both have numerous genetic factors which could make them happen early in life. The stroke is elaborated on as being caused due to a widescale global outcry towards a drastic historical event which has little similar enough to compare to it.

Secondly; Mental illness is not quite the same as physical illness, which is being referred to in that quote in context.

Thirdly; Yes, it actually could happen in theory if his DNA no longer shortened but instead reached a point of perfectly replicating itself. It's plausible, anomalous yes but still plausible, he would no longer age in the sense that we think of it. We die of old age and the illnesses that come with it due to the telomeres at the end of our DNA being skipped over numerous times as our cells divide, it eventually gets too faulty to remain stable. He's reached cellular equilibrium.
