I'm embarrassed to say this did not even trip my radar while critting. But now that you bring it up, I can definitely see the objection.

I have a notion which would provide a fairly clean workaround. (Though it's up to rounder and dog if they are interested in using this or some other idea here  the article as-is is clearly succeeding!)

WHATIF

whatbroadly?

In the current , this could theoretically still qualify as a youth. Or at least, the most youthful person who was immediately available to the team.

Now, I'd interpreted Tests 6 and 7 to mean the swine god was displeased with the offering given to it in Test 6, that is, an already-deceased person. It retaliated against this insult by being exceptionally cruel to the person in Test 7, by making them suffer for hours before they died.

Test 9 is already pretty horrific  450 kilograms  is just under 1000 pounds of coal. Being manually shoveled by two people. That's a hellacious amount of shoveling. And after and/or during all that shoveling, one of the researchers is burned to death while the other is severely injured.

So if the change were made, one could easily interpret the nastiness of Test 9 as the swine god's retaliation for being given merely a somewhat youthful researcher rather than the actual child it demanded in Test 8.
