I'm liking this already.

And the rest of the article didn't really disappoint. I'm a little lost on how he turned brought about the anart, but then again he did manage to resurrect himself in an incorporeal state impervious to all physical harm, so maybe I should just be taking that with a grain of salt.

On the subject of the depiction of a modern artist committing suicide in the name of artistic spirit, I don't really know much about Hirst, so given the choice I don't really have much context on what he might do. It's mostly played as gallows humor, so I didn't really have much of a problem with it. For lack of a better word - a much better word - it's delightful, in its simplicity. You manage to get across a potent (albeit trod-upon) message within a relatively sparse length, and the imagery contained within the videolog presents a decently creepy counterpoint to the levity that pervades the piece.
