My problem with this is that it has so many different formats introduced that it looks like an hors d'oeuvres platter of alternative formats and logs; it is jarring to read it and have it look like a showcase of different kinds of formatting taken from the Style Resource page. Pick one (or two, at the very least) and stick with it. The inconsistency is very apparent and it just makes the entire article a messy mish-mash of formats.

It's also very distracting to put your attributions in the article. It doesn't make any sense and it ruins whatever investment any reader would've had from reading the article up until that point.

+1, but with a few format errors

08 Jul 2019 15:30

The images, voice recordings, and format in general were very well created for this scip. I also love the idea of the scip itself; an influence that controls its victims to the point where even the researchers assigned to the scip question their sanity.

Just a few minor formatting errors and typoes:

Describing the anomalies as  fits more into SCP document syntax.

In one of the logs, the question  is asked.

The .

In the special containment procedures, you write .

This sentence feels moderately repetitive. Maybe seperate it into two sentences and describe the effect the SCP has on its hosts? And remember, the hosts are instances of SCP-4965-1 before they are identified. They aren't instances only after being found, which the sentence confusingly suggests.

If you want to keep this part in one sentence, I'd suggest using something like  and so on and so forth.

Overall, good job! I look forward to seeing more scips written by you in the near future.
