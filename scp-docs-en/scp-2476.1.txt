23 Feb 2017 12:28

Well. While I would love to upvote given the overhaul on the first document alone, there's still a number of problems keeping me from doing so.

First, I still feel like the abilities of doc1 are too different from the abilities in -2. I think it would have been more logical for them to be -2's abilities perception-shifted into a MPATHG context. This goes double for the interview. I think going out of your way to tailor the -1 portion into a 'veiled by cognitohazard' version of the actuality of -2 would have made the reveal that much more satisfying.

Second, I think the second document's effects are oddly complicated: So, he sees potential weapons as safe items, but at the same time, sees them as being harmful, and still attempts to attack people with them? How exactly do knives take on the weight and texture of a cushion, still remain physically the same, yet not be harmful if you assault someone with it? I guess it's part of the joke, but the description of his effects seems weird.

Third, and this is my biggest gripe of all, the second interview. I'm just so damn disinterested in this skip's wistful memories of his creators and how lovingly thoughtful they are; and over-explaining the joke. I feel like it does nothing but reiterate for the umpteenth flipping time: 'gee, aren't these GAW guys just so sincere and funny? They sure do funny things because humor is something they get. You like humor and jokes, yes? You'll like them. Why, they are just like you and me.'. Christ, just no. The whole thing grinds to a screeching halt here.

I'm largely having the same problems as Shaggy. The description of the violent behaviors weren't particularly clear or imaginable. It seems like trying to write up the skit (which I've seen) in clinical tone with perfect detail just probably isn't going to make sense. I'd try to simplify. Don't make everything hard and fast rules (it only knows period weaponry, it can change weapons but thinks they are harmful but they aren't). It isn't interesting to read, and it doesn't add to th SCP.

The dumb-but-show-accurate tantrums also really don't fit with his tone in the conversation on page 2. I wouldn't change the conversation, because that's definitely the best part.

Other than that You're being too clever by half. The altering of the -A document immediately reminded me of the crown cognitihazard, which isn't a comparison you want to invite: you'll lose. You also never mentioned within the -A document that it alters it's own document format, so there's this vague meta level of confusion on top of the rest of the vagueness going on.

- So, you might as well make the picture match the actor, given that our expectations are 100% altered. That, or ditch the format changes, or push the format changes even further towards being Monty Python-esque, rather than just being slightly modified Foundation format. I guess the main way to do that would be, I donno, pictures, or  instated of the split lines.

No vote for now. I love the characterisation, but this needs cleanup.
