The concept here is great; the execution, not so much. The description is far from elegant - that is, it's extremely hamfisted and doesn't convey the information in a clinical or analytical manner. It's rough, there's a URL in there that feels completely unnecessary, and overall it just feels like it could with a good deal more polish. The tests were by far the most enjoyable part of the article for me, and most everything there reeks of 2875, which is a good thing. If this doesn't push through I would recommend continuing to work with just in the capacity that you shore up the writing in general and especially try to tighten that middle section.

Curious Regarding the Survivability of SCP-3579-2

27 Oct 2017 18:06

I am somewhat confused as to the longevity of SCP-3579-2. You have stated the alligator would suffer from a myocardial infarct if left to dehydrate for more than 5 minutes. However, you also record SCP-3579-2 remaining outside of water for 10 minutes before they return. I was wondering, do the alligators have some method to scan for nearby water, and if unable to find a way back to said water, suffer from the infarction? Personally, it seems a bit off that they can survive longer when they are in the know of a way back to their spawn location.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

On an unrelated and silly note, would SCP-3579-2 be able to materialise inside other liquids? Not necessarily substances such as gasoline or elements like mercury, but how about orange juice? It does contain water in itself, albeit a lower amount than just lake or tap water.
