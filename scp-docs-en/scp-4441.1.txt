Re: SCP-4441

19 Jan 2019 20:00

Thanks for the feedback and your take on the article! That repeated  was a typo, I fixed it. I'm European so I was blissfully unaware that A4 isn't the standard paper format in USA. I think I'll leave that as it is.

Regarding your questions, here's what was going on with SCP-6762 in this story-within-a-story, in my opinion:

+authorinterpretation

–block

The unindentified glyph covering the face of SCP-6762 is a powerful cognitohazard that all information regarding it will be percieved differently by everyone who sees it. The effect of this glyph extends to SCP-6762 itself. All researchers assigned to SCP-6762 therefore had a different perception of the entity, which caused conflicting Special Containment Procedures to be written for it.

One of these conflicting views caused the research staff to believe that SCP-6762 must be routinely  by feeding it dead D-class personnel. This was false - SCP-6762 would technically constitute as a Safe Object. Not only that, but 6762's antimemetic property is strengthened by consuming brain tissue. Therefore, the Containment Procedures only served to exacerbate the situation.

The Foundation kept feeding SCP-6762 bodies until its antimemetic range reached zero. If starved for brain matter, SCP-6762's antimemetic aura would shrink and eventually dissipate.

SCP-6762 being 2.5 meters tall and vocalising on a near-inaubly low frequency was a jab towards cringy anomalous humanoids that are super tall and mysterious and have a deep voice.

Meanwhile, SCP-4441-1 being small, asocial and artistically productive is a reference towards myself, and new SCP writers in general. And whadya know: SCP-4441-1 also writes skips.
