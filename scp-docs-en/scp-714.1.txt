Honestly it'd probably be gone for good if they… say… threw it into the sun. Just smashing it or grinding it down to a powder won't work though. Plus the pieces have to be put near each other. Or maybe weirder stuff happens if they are apart for a long time and/or exposed to additional portions of jade.

But, obviously, there is potential for reverse-engineering the memetic shield effect - given the number of memetic hazards the Foundation has to deal with, such shielding would be very useful. Testing what it can and can't recover from when those limits are not known and when only one example is known to exist is extremely counterproductive.

If memory serves the "it eats other bits of jade" thing was there primarily to increase the weird factor, rather than anything truly sinister. Something to suggest that there was something not quite right about it, but not (immediately?) hazardous to mankind. Perhaps if it absorbs enough it replicates itself or evolves somehow? Maybe it only absorbs green jade? If it doesn't, does this have an impact on it's appearance and effects, or is it always the same old green ring? Something to think about for experiment logs?

I like to think the Foundation is looking to do more than just Contain some of this stuff though, either for their own ends - such as developing new equipment for personnel to use when containing SCPs - or public use; for example… maybe velcro and post-it notes were actually made by aliens! The history books say otherwise because that's what the Foundation want people to believe, whilst they fund themselves with the royalties from the things they do make safe and useful.

Fold

26 Nov 2015 00:51

Interesting. I was gonna say,  Then I started reading the comments and realized this was an older skip from back when everything was still novel. Carry on! ^^

If you're seriously interested in a rewrite start by asking yourself why the ring puts people to sleep. Who made it? Why did they make it? Is it functioning as-designed or has it gone wrong somehow? If the latter, why? What was it meant to do and why is it doing this other thing instead?

You don't need to put all of this into the skip, but you should privately know the answers before you begin reverse-engineering what the Foundation may or may not have learned about it.

The secret truth only you know should ideally be something horrible or baffling or even just incredible. Then you can hint at it all throughout the skip, leading the reader's imagination towards it but not quite saying it. Since the Foundation can't prove anything, and they're above rampant speculation. But we're not. And if you tell it right, we'll either figure out what you're hinting at or else our imaginations will lead us somewhere even more horrible than what you'd planned.

Good luck! :D
