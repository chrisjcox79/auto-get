oncanon

oncanon

Stuff like PIIs and ED-K is from my Broken Masquerade stuff and more directly my Doomsday Contest stuff. It's not necessary to read that stuff to understand this  though some of that universe is crosslinked in here for good measure.

In terms of the canon this establishes/would potentially establish as 4000, I do not think that all SCPs set after 4000 need to be set in the 2040s, don't worry. The specific number 4000 could have been out of range for a number of reasons, and I emphasize 'there is no canon' when it comes to each SCP's place in the universe.

Ha-ha, Trauss took sixty-nine.

And we're in a 2040s AU where the 4000s are out of range I guess. IDK I'll think up some bullshit to explain it

onhistory

becauseconnection

The SCP-4069 experience is the history of the Foundation, experienced by individual personnel through the eyes of other individual personnel, no matter the time period. Edit: And it's also page history, which I did not even think of, so thank you TL333s for pointing that out downthread.

imageinfo

imageinfo

Images are created by me in full. Released under CC-by-SA-3.0.

spoilers

spoilers

SCP-4069-B is the Foundation. Put more clinically, it's the physical embodiment and representation of how the affected employee sees the Foundation and its influence on their life; hence, its appearance varies greatly and its behavior is neutral-to-hostile, occasionally kind, with neutrality and silent observation being the most common.

SCP-4069-A is its own place. It does its own thing. Sure, most of the time it will take the affected person to a random point in history at or near the death of a different Foundation employee, but some of 51174's logs are original events unique to SCP-4069-A. (Which ones are which is up to you to decide. Whatever forces are behind 4069 wouldn't want you to know the difference.) What does it mean that 4069-A generates Foundation-themed events? Beats me, but given the PII's implications I'd say it's all in your head.
