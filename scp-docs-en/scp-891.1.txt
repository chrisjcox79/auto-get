Fold

29 Apr 2013 04:11

This. Very much this. Not only do all the expungements and blackouts severely break the flow of the text - almost to the point where it kind of feels like you're reading completely unrelated sentence fragments - but it also ruins the potentially scary implications of the ideas behind them. I mean, yes, I get the choice of fruit, but mentioning third-degree burns and a reverse something-or-other and vague implications of an entity of some sort happening or something…All the expungement there takes it from  There is potential for a brilliant idea there, but it's too broken up by expungements to really work. As for the blackouts and such during the description of recovery and incident…Meh, it's still pretty heavy-handed, but I kind of get enough of an impression about everything for those parts to work for me.

If I may be so bold as to make a suggestion: Maybe turn the thing-that-might-be-classified-as-a-separate-SCP-that-happens-when-someone-eats-an-apple into SCP-891-1 and write a full-fledged paragraph (or mostly full-fledged paragraph) describing what it is, what it does, and so on. The fact that apple tree saplings seem to spontaneously appear in the field sometimes also suggests that the thing-that-might-be-an-SCP is a very integral part of SCP-891, further justifying turning it into SCP-891-1. I realize that would require a pretty extensive rewrite, and I have nowhere near the skill or authorization needed to do anything about that, but maybe if someone wanted to and the author gave permission…? I really do feel like this could be a great SCP, and I'm really sad that it's sort of ruined by what pretty much amounts to a cop-out (as far as I can tell). :(

All that said, the beginning concept - fruit what makes you eat yourself - is a very good start, and I already like it enough to upvote; I just think that, with some work, it could be a lot better.
