I can't quite lay my finger on it, but some bits don't read like an SCP report.

In any case, this is a nicely written first SCP.

Fold

22 Feb 2020 15:09

I feel like the richness and complexity of the world you want to convey is hindered by the fact that you seem reluctant to really describe in depth much of that world. Could you fold your interview into a longer exploration log, something that could include descriptions of the environment (is it all the same kind of architecture? Does it get weirder the further in you go?), encounters with life forms (are they all equally friendly? Can you give the reader a good creepiness pay-off, by contrasting their friendly attitude with their more gruesome behaviors? Can we learn more about their lifestyle through observation of crop-tending, or religious rituals, or family life?), or discoveries that hint or tease about the history or origin of the place? Even a chart or table detailing different kinds of  found in the place, starting with something weird, but not too weird, and ramping up gradually with a few entries, can help immerse the reader and add some flavor.

Also, I'm no biologist, but the 48% percent match for human dna isn't really doing anything for me, as I'm pretty sure most life on earth has a pretty high percentage match for human dna (I've been told we have about 61% in common with fruit flies, for example).
