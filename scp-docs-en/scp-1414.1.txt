21 May 2012 13:46

You need to place greater emphasis on the fact that it has three anomalous properties.

The first of these anomalous properties is that it picks up all broadcasts. Everywhere. Perhaps it even picks up broadcasts that can't be detected by any other means.

The second is that it responds to spoken requests in natural language. Only for requests to  and it's able to distinguish its owner's voice from high-quality recordings of its owner's voice. Although we do know how to reset its ownership matrix, so that's helpful.

Third anomaly is the deafness thing you mentioned. Eventually the owner stops being able to hear anything except the message coming from the SCP.

What I'm thinking is okay, have you read SCP-1455, the Lagging Door? In particular, have you read the backstory given on the talk page?

+1455

-1414

I'm thinking that 1414 is in the same basic predicament as 1455. Just as 1455 can be considered equivalent to Janus the God of Doors, so 1414 is clearly equivalent to Hermes, the God of Messengers.

Hermes was an unimaginably powerful entity whose abilities pertained to messages and the transmission of information, and some even MORE powerful entity lobotomized it, brainwashed it real good, and polymorphed it into this sound system.

Hermes will provide you with any message you ask for. Any message. From anywhere on Earth. The limits of its range haven't been determined, but let's say it can pick up broadcasts from other dimensions/timelines. Messages from off-planet, it gives us audio from SCP-163's habitat?

Loyalty is important, so Hermes only obeys its master. And as the God of Messages, it can easily tell the difference between a message from its master and the sound of a message from its master, which is why it doesn't respond to recordings.

The part about deafness, that's the result of Hermes being a passive-aggressive little shit. It's managed to sneak around the behavioral and mental limits imposed by the brainwashing, so as to fuck with its owner's life. What Hermes does is convey the message-of-the-broadcast to its owner as perfectly as possible, see?

And when you turn the volume up past a certain point, that must mean that you want to receive the information in the message even better, right? The louder the radio, the more you want. Obviously.

So Hermes formulates the broadcast such that you become unable to hear anything other than its message. You can't hear anything else, therefore nothing is drowning out the radio! If there's only one audio source in your world, then it's the only thing you listen to!

 so that's my idea for how to save this one. May I do a major rewrite?
