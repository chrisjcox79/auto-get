I posted this a week ago but have made some pretty major revisions since then. Thanks to everyone who reviewed both rounds of this article. Thanks especially to Modern_Erasmus for helping me understand some problems in the previous version and ARD for helping me work through this version.

+block

–block

I deleted the last version of this article despite its positive rating because I realized I wasn't hitting the points I'd wanted to as well as I could. The article's idea was born in a fit of brainstorming skip ideas inspired by the aesthetic and themes of the anime Mushishi (which is bomb and you should definitely check it out if you haven't), and so a big part of the original idea was a neutral relationship between humanity and relationship that didn't have human values attached to it. However, because I'm totally ripping off the last episode of Mushishi season 2 (and also the lion turtles from ATLA), I felt I needed something more.

The idea of a turtle which had, stored in its memory, something like a story of the entire Pacific led me down a rabbit hole of reading into storytelling's relationship in Hawaiian society-a ton of information that I flat-out did not include in the first draft of this article. For the second draft, I cut a lot of the direct depictions of Moses' family and that conflict and instead only alluded to it; in its place I decided to hone this article's focus on storytelling by actually depicting, in some detail, the culture and society in which this storytelling takes place. Hope you all enjoy reading!

I realize now that it might be a bit unclear, but the sentence talking about the perimeter indicates that one is only established when the turtle surfaces, which is rare. Plus, I think you'd only need a perimeter of 3-4 boats to actively patrol the turtle, and they're mostly there to ward off GoIs as opposed to random civilians.

I also say in the article that the turtle is in hiding, because it's smart enough to know that if it compromises consensus reality, bad things will probably happen to it. If it were to surface in a populated area, then yeah, the Foundation would have to deploy amnestics (not sure why they'd have to sink civilians boats), but this is preparing for a scenario that has not ever happened, the Foundation has good reason to believe will not happen, and that has an obvious response, so I didn't include it in the containment procedures.
