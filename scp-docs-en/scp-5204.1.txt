I am of the opinion that somethings don't really need to be overstated or grandiose to have an impact. For example, SCP-4999 is short, sweet, and extremely evocative. It's not complex, just a man who sits with you when you die. I could understand your scope complaint in that context, but not so much here. The implication is that this is where you go when you die. That this could be the afterlife. I can't really think of something much larger in scope that.

I like the effect and its significance, overall. This would be a pleasant read outside the confines of a contest.

Ack. I'm torn on this one.

For the first two logs, you really had me going there. It was a solid investigation, and I was intrigued to learn more and find out what the deal was, who the killer was, etc.

Then we got to the end and, well, I was underwhelmed. Not only did the big murder mystery turn out to simply be an accident, slipping down the stairs no less, but the fact that your big anomaly is a slightly grander flavor of ghosts left something to be desired. Which is a disappointment given how well you were doing up to that point.

No vote. When you hit your stride in the first two logs, you really hit it. But that ending just falls flat.
