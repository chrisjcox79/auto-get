As promised in chat.

Behold,Wiki

Nope

There’s very little time given to communication between the SCP and the Foundation, the addition of which I feel would give it more substance, and possibly answer some of the questions I have about them. Contrasted with this, you’ve got a whole paragraph about the process by which folks are turned into trees, which seems very well researched (assuming the terminology etc. is all accurate), but which doesn’t really bring anything other than some science-y stuff to the article. In my opinion you could ditch a lot of that without losing any real substance from the article.

On the other hand, I don’t agree with what War says about it being too fairytale-esque. I have no issue with this being based on a local fairy tale or piece of folklore, and a piece of folklore about weird forest creatures which subsequently turn out to be real works for me. YMMV of course.

A few thoughts about specific sections of the piece:

The known population of SCP-2831 is to be kept in a one square kilometer area surrounded by barbed wire topped chain link fence
附加记录：    

Do we know the actual number? If so, I think it would be mentioned.

Also, would chainlink be enough to keep them in? There are lots of creatures which are smart enough to climb or dig under a fence, and these seem to be sentient. I can see them scarpering pretty easily

…and authorities in Cahir are to be notified.
附加记录：    

Again, accuracy and effectiveness of containment procedures. Are we notifying the Gardai, the council, or a Foundation front hidden in the town?

They are completely built out of common ivy leaves, aside from the tips of the fingers, which appear to be sharpened sticks
附加记录：    

I don’t like the use of ‘built’ in here, although I’ll admit I don’t have any suggestions for what to use instead.

Colonies of SCP-2831 have, as of yet, only been found in Ireland, primarily in and around the small village of ███████, near the town of Cahir. The current known population is confined to an area █.█km outside ███████ (SEE CONTAINMENT PROCEDURES).
附加记录：    

I dislike this whole sentence. It’s long and a bit rambling, and for something with so many redactions it would be remarkably easy to locate exactly where you’re talking about, especially with local knowledge. You could probably get away with something like “The only known colonies of SCP-2831 to date have been found in the area around Cahir, Republic of Ireland” and leave it at that.

When in a forested environment, specimens of SCP-2831 are usually peaceful, and generally avoid humans. However, approximately every 3 days, SCP-2831 groups will travel out of the forest to terraform new habitat.
附加记录：    

I’m no David Attenborough, but this seems like an odd approach. Wouldn’t it make more sense, from a behavioural point of view, for them to gradually expand the boundaries of their territory? Then if they’re threatened they just disappear back into the forest.

Colonies of displaced specimens of SCP-2831 will seek out groups of humans (other species have been proposed to SCP-2831 colonies, all have been roundly rejected) and seek to transform them into a new home for the group.
附加记录：    

After a few re-reads of this sentence, I actually like it as a concept. It makes transporting them for whatever reason, or an unnoticed containment breach, a lot more hazardous. My main issue is that it seems to introduce a new behaviour, one which is more something that would happen in extremis than when they’re in their natural habitat.

I think if they were to attack lone humans near the edge of the woods as their normal behaviour, and then have this as their emergency ‘establish a safe habitat’ mode, they would be scarier and more convincing.

When the procedure is over, the human is left looking indistinguishable from a normal tree. The species of tree which the affected subject becomes varies - at the current time, no patterns have been found. Analysis of the bark of the newly created trees have revealed that only trace amounts of human biological substances are present.
附加记录：    

I don’t like the use of procedure, process fits better here in my opinion.

Species of tree is important. Are the new trees at least appropriate to the environment? I’d expand on that at the expense of some of the preceding stuff about the transformation process.

I like the concept of the new trees retaining some aspect of their human origins. Did you mean to just have human tissue present, or are you saying that the actual DNA is a combination of human and tree? I prefer the latter, but you’d need a real scientist to help you sort that bit out.

Another anomalous property noted by personnel is that animals seem to be attracted to the forests created by SCP-2831 colonies.
附加记录：    

By the time you get down here, it feels like you’re throwing this paragraph in for the sake of it. I don’t object to it as a concept, but unless you go on to explain more about the process it seems tacked on. Maybe it would fit better as an addendum, combined with the information about the transformation process and the group ‘habitat building’ behaviour when removed from the forest.

I could make a similar comment about the last paragraph. As the information about the hierarchy doesn’t seem to lend much to the description of what they are, it could be removed or dropped into an addendum.

I'll be honest, after we chatted the other day, I saw this coming back very different in my mind. It could have been something really creepy, which would have made me jumpy every time I went for a piss near a tree. As it is, I think Ramza, War and I are all saying that it's not bad, but could be a lot better.

I am inclined to upvote but will refrain - I'll list my reasons for doing so.

Okay, so I love this for the fact that it's almost whimsical with the image it builds in my mind - er, not including the whole 'turning people into trees' part.

But of course this sort of whimsicality combined with a darker side does have its appeal. I mean, small humanoids turning people into trees definitely has a kind of creep value (I'm on the Irish isles gdi).

But,

I'm also inclined to say that this could be too fairytale-esque for a scip  I'm not necessarily saying I believe that outright, but it's something that other people may believe. Scips based off of local lore and tales, I think, would be harder to format in a way as to read like a Foundation document (though I admit that this does so quite well).

Also agreeing with much of what Ramza13 said - there is a lot of questions some people might ask. Are they in fact aware that the people they are transforming might not necessarily be hostile? And why do they only target people? Are animals not as efficient at producing these tree specimens, or would animals in fact be immune to these entities and their effects? Or is it because they seek to protect animals?

Neutral-vote for the time being.

I was directed here to review from the Young and Under 30 project, which is meant to get more attention to articles less than a year old, and with a score of less than +30.
附加记录：    

I think this article is facing some moderate to major structural and clinical tone problems. The description just kind of meanders around, not really providing information about these creatures in an organized manner. For example, you make several statements that seem to involve the critters communicating (e.g. rejecting other animals), but you don't actually get into their linguistic ability until way further down.

The telling is also somewhat casual, rarely feeling like the insights shared have been substantiated or considered in any way. For example, if they go human-hunting every three days, why isn't that reflected in the containment procedures? Why would they specifically say ?

A lot of this is made worse by a lot of the imagery being pretty ho-hum. The creature description sounds much like a DD Monster Manual entry, infection by simple touch is kinda boring, stuff testing positive for human DNA is definitely boring, and I can think of probably at least two SCPs involving turned-into-a-tree body horror. This isn't a death blow to the overall story, as I still like the idea of them going hunting and the story told in the log, but you've got to limit what isn't interesting for this to float. I'd cut the stuff about the mural and myths, for instance.

-1 for now.
