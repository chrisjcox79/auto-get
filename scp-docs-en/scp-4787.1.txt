Hi there mastermindartemis, thanks for leaving your thoughts.

This is an SCP article, it’s just not written in the typical Item #, Object Class, Containment Procedures, Description, and Addendum format. I wrote the article this way because I felt that the story would be more impactful from a first person perspective.

I considered writing it as a tale, but at the end of the day, that’s not how I wanted to tell the story, because putting it in an article opposed to a tale actually does matter to the narrative.

Fold

14 Sep 2019 01:20

The anomaly itself is only remembered by Brooks. All the foundation would have to go off of is his description of it. This SCP is detailing the anomaly of the pothole from a first-person point of view by the man affected by it. I don't believe a normal article would be easy for the foundation to write because they have little information about the anomaly, and Brooks' account is all they have.

I felt that the story would be more impactful if told this way. Brooks posted it on the database because he desperately wanted people to remember him, and knew it would be seen there. That's the in-universe explanation. So in my opinion, the screw is completely justified, but I see how people have different views.

Thanks for sharing your thoughts. :)
