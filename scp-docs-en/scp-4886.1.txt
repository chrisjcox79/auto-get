23 Oct 2019 22:56

I really like this.

It's very easy, in my opinion, to do a  story. It's much harder to do one where the Foundation is trying its best but, in a very realistic way, overlooks something. The real horror here, at least to me, is not the spooky monster (though it's certainly an unpleasant anomaly to say the least), it's the fact that the SCP Foundation, rigorous and logical as they are, managed to just give this *thing* playing dress-up as an agent so much control over containment of what turned out to be, basically, itself.

And assuming this is the  iteration of the log, in the in-universe year of 2019, it's been almost 30 years and they've yet to capture the thing. Which is a solid metaphor (intentional or not) for how fuckups like the one the Foundation commits here can often have an impact that well outlasts the actual incident.
