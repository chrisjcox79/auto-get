Well this is quite horrible and inexplicable. It's a bit on the random side, but I like it. +1 for sheer creativity.

Three notes on semi-science though:

-The miniature rainforest described seems to be specifically be a tropical rainforest, so it would seem salient to explicitly specify that in the text.

- don't seem to match. In the former, the maximum is eight times the minimum, in the latter, less than twice.

-In addition, the percentage is several orders of magnitude too high to result in less than two dozen cases per year. Sources aren't quite consistent with incidence rate for ectopic pregnancies, but most state one over 1%, so we'll go with that. There's 211 million pregnancies a year (source: http://www.who.int/whr/2005/chapter3/en/index3.html ), and 0.5% of 1% of that would result in about ten thousand cases of SCP-3224 a year.
