My inspiration is 80% music. Music is my life. This article came from listening to  by The National. I fixated on the name of the song and thought it would make a good name for a skip.

The meat of the article was heavily inspired by The Great Hippo's essay as well as my tremendous fandom of Doctor Who. I tried for an overall  tone of creepy curiosity and an entity that isn't particularly evil or whatever. It just needs attention.

The image is CC compliant and was found through Wikimedia Commons. One problem though: it's John Marchione, the mayor of Redmond Washington. That might be an issue I don't know.

The article doesn't state anywhere what SCP-3129's area of effect actually is. Is it just the continental United States? Has it appeared anywhere else outside the United States? Does SCP-3129 appear more often in places where the ideas of community and togetherness are ? Or does it prefer places where those ideas are already strongly valued? How frequent are SCP-3129 infected broadcasts?

The description lead me to believe that SCP-3129-1 has specific ideas that people who already agree with make them more susceptible to infection, but the example log at the bottom seems to cite 3129-1's main ideas as security, togetherness, and community which are pretty board and popular ideas among any stock politician regardless of party, and ideas which include a pretty broad amount of potentially vulnerable infectees. Wouldn't that pool of possible cognitohazard-vectors as well as the incident report (which might suggest possible reality reconstruction or at least a perception-based effect) be a point of concern for the Foundation to at least consider a pending Keter classification?

I don't wanna downvote, but I just feel like the ideas as they are presented here aren't fully developed and realized. If can address a few of these questions in some edits, I think I could confidently upvote this. Otherwise, this feels like it's almost something good but just not quite there yet.

It doesn't actually. The commercials are vague on purpose:  doesn't actually understand politics. Its use of local politics is purely opportunistic. It saw a quick and dirty way to get what it needed (attention) and decided to emulate it. Badly. This thing's poor reproduction of actual campaign ads is entirely intentional. It doesn't even need to be actually convincing because the hazardous aspect of the transmissions is what gets it what it needs. The fact that it works on people of particular alignment at any given time is coincidental.

It doesn't really have ideas of its own as far as an actual  is concerned. Different commercials say different things. The entity just throws out what it thinks people want to hear and tries its best to carry the facade of an actual person.

As far as its classification is concerned, maybe a pending Keter designation is appropriate. This is just an unfortunate result of the end of the first addendum not aligning with the rest of the article, as pointed out by Mortos. That's the main goal of the rewrite.
