While the above feedback is probably trending too close to vehemence, I agree that the article would be improved by tightening up some of the longer running sentences and wordier descriptions. I personally value concise language over precise language though, so take that with a grain of salt.

On a different note, the pacing of this article felt off to me. The transcript seems like its strongest element, but after that concludes it goes back to describing comparatively unexciting anatomical features. Though I don't think it could end on the transcript, I do think the amount of text between the end of that section and the end of the article slows the pace down an unfortunate amount.

Junior Staff - Site Criticism

22 Sep 2019 16:20

This was really enjoyable.

+rambles

-OFF

Just a few things that jumped out at me:

SCP-4866 removes its sunglasses, revealing a second, smaller pair of sunglasses underneath the first.
附加记录：    

The surreal humour is great here, and it also works as an indicator of just how weird this anomaly is.

Hey there, pal. You look like the kind of guy that understands quality when he sees it. Am I right? You’ve got the ligaments of a discerning organism. A cool cat out and about looking for trouble on the down-low. That's fly. In fact, I've got something that'll impress the shit out of every one of your nociceptors so bad that they won't know what debrided them.
附加记录：    

Your dialogue is always fantastic, Cadaver, and this is no exception. The mixture of an inhuman perspective and the very human salesman attitude comes across really well here.

(SCP-4866 turns to look directly into the security camera’s lens.)
附加记录：    

Everything after this is just incredible. You’ve built up the tension with the intimidation from the anomaly throughout the log before this, and the shift here breaks that tension with some humour, but not in a way that undercuts the build-up before this.

I feel like I have to agree a little with what others have said about the description of the anomaly’s make-up after the transcription not feeling fully necessary. It’s not something that bothered me too much, but it does feel like the article ramps down in intensity after the high of the transcript.

Initial investigation has revealed that a corporation operating with the name “Optimark Industrial, Inc.” exists in several financial registries
附加记录：    

I heard about this Optimark thing that’s getting going, and I have to say that this is a great first impression for it. I’m definitely interested in seeing more of what they’re about!

+1
