I love the picture for this one, and the idea of an SCP monorail tunnel appeals to me a lot. That said, there are a few things I dislike here.

The first, and the least important, is that we already have a Site-XX utility tunnel SCP.

Second, some of the writing seems a tad heavy handed. Easily fixable.

The main problem I have here, though, is the SCP's effect. By which I mean its lack of an effect of its own. By borrowing random effects from other SCPs, this article simultaneously crosspollinates several articles and detracts from the creativity of the article itself. The end result is, to me, a sub-par article that feels forced, as though the author just wanted to write an SCP for the sake of writing one, with no particular inspiration.

The core noun is good (anomalous monorail tunnel + awesome picture), but the verb of the article (random effects from other SCPs) doesn't seem strong enough to stand on its own.

That last addendum, I think, is a step in the right direction. Perhaps if that aspect were developed further, this concept could grow into a solid SCP.

A monorail tunnel? We can be just like Black Mesa!

Anyways, what I think is that this article suffers from a certain lack of description, or even implied description. We are told that the monorail seems to have absorbed the effects (there's a problem here;how does the tunnel know what's anomalous? How do the effects get absorbed when they are so varied in nature? Never mind answering those, they're just nitpicks and are solved by simple suspension of disbelief) but we aren't really shown what happens, besides a few imploding personnel here and there. I'm not asking you to spoil the mystery by spelling everything out, but at least hint at some of the effects that have gone through and mixed with others. For this article, just showing us the output won't do, I think. (It's a fine line, though. Too much in the other direction and the artifact's mechanism is laid bare, exposing it as a mental construction instead of a subject of investigation. )

I seem to have rambled a bit. I think it's the result of typing slowly from a cell phone. Short version: show us some evidence.
