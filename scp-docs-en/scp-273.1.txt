*soapbox*

If you're thinking of a humanoid SCP as a character, I can safely say it's not going to go well, no matter what you do—because you're fundamentally thinking of it the wrong way. Evidence: Whiny telepath ("Saint"), is dying, but 231 (110-Montauk) isn't. Looking at my attempts: baleeted-795 and baleeted-770 were erased, because they were characters with SCP-like effects. However, the asian girl in 466 is just a host, and that article is at a comfortable +5. Some of the older character-SCPs are getting away with it, but that's because herp derp cant criticize teh classix lol. Yet, 132 isn't looking so good…

TL;DR: SCP characters get deleted.

22 Aug 2010 12:19

Very, very meh to me. It seems like a lot of focus is put on how she consumes things. When I think a human SCP with kind of freaky powers, I don't think of how they eat. I think of how it affects them. From the interview, it seems like she doesn't want to hurt people and she views herself as demonic, but the rest of the article is really about how she eats things. I don't want to think 'Wow, I want to be this person.' I want to think 'I never want to be this person, let alone be near them.'

I've downvoted the article for now, but I think that you MIGHT be able to save this with a good old fashioned rewrite. Human SCPs are hard to write, honestly, and if this is your first attempt I'd say try again but with something that isn't human. Take a waffleiron and make it weird or something.
