I thought about adding something on this point, but didn't want to make the SCP even longer. I'd like to keep the -1s human-sized, while the -2s can grow indefinitely large.

附加记录：    

First off, as oft-stated, there is no canon per se. That said, most articles/tales that address the Foundation's dealings with external forces and organizations have it acknowledged, however quietly, by a variety of world governments and militaries. The US, Soviet, Nazi, Chinese, British, Iranian, and Canadian governments come to mind off the top of my head. No formal and definitive origin story has ever been definitively established for the Foundation, though several possible and mutually contradictory stories do exist. In at least some of them, the Foundation was originally created as a partially military organization. Similarly, the GOC is often shown as being in league with governments. So, I think it probably safe to assume (at least major) governments are aware of the Foundation, though the general population most certainly would not be.

Second of all, it's been established that some parts of the Foundation itself are structured militarily. At least one General (Bowe) has been established as being part of the Foundation. I don't think this is what the author was meaning to imply here, but it's worth noting.

Here's how it goes in my version of the (non) canon:

The Foundation is not quite as secret as it would like to be. It's known at high levels of the military and some other government organizations.

It doesn't normally cooperate in this way with other organizations, but the O5's can pretty much do what they want, and are not always exempt from politics. The Foundation is keeping a population of -1's and -2's around for experimentsand they're pack rats in general. It's the same reason they keep all sorts of other dangerous living SCPs around. Most of them are former D-class, and as such are not involved in operations. The Foundation is very suspicious of the couple of non-D-class converted (a hint that there is something not quite right about this SCP's effects).

This is a good SCP. Some minor points, though, mostly to do with this paragraph:

It is designed to emit a mixture of ████████ and ██████ radiation and concentrate the rays on a human subject, previously injected with [DATA EXPUNGED], [DATA EXPUNGED], and SCP-███ blood. -  once.

- The idea of a purposefully-designed SCP sits badly with me, although I realise this opinion is not universal.

- Simply injecting the subject with the blood of another SCP seems overly simplistic. Unless SCP-███ is an existing SCP specifically characterised by having blood that can somehow transform its subject, or at the very least not cause some kind of horrible reaction (as happens when you give someone the wrong blood type, for example) I'd make it so that the blood needed to undergo some kind of processing first. Otherwise it just starts sounding like alchemy.
