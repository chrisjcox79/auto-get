TL;DR: The containment procedures are sloppy but the interview logs carry the article. +1

28 Jun 2014 16:56

ClickReview

ClickHide

Best practice in relation to SCP-2055 is currently limited to constant monitoring of key personnel and the suppression of public knowledge.
附加记录：    

Should be a 'the' in front of the sentence.

In the event of automated monitors being triggered the nearest available security officers should be alerted and authorised to carry out immediate physical intervention.
附加记录：    

Should be a comma between triggered and the. Authorised is spelled Authorized.

In cases where remote monitoring has allowed SCP-2055 to be interrupted, affected individuals have become highly agitated and resistant.
附加记录：    

This doesn't need to be in the containment procedures.

Once removed from the location of onset, abnormal behaviours cease, with the individual exhibiting no memory of preceding events.
附加记录：    

Ditto this.

Due to the difficulty in predicting the occurrence, prevalence or individual outcomes of SCP-2055, constant surveillance is currently the only certain counter-measure.
附加记录：    

This has already been stated as the first line in the containment procedures.

The logs should probably be put in collapsibles just for neatness.

Re: TL;DR: The containment procedures are sloppy but the interview logs carry the article. +1

01 Jul 2014 16:44

I'd like to lay in my opinions against Soulless' here and say that

a) 'Best practice' is definitely correct and 'the best practice' is incorrect in this instance - they have subtly different meanings and should be used in slightly different contexts

b) A comma is not necessary where you have indicated, and 'authorised' is a perfectly legitimate spelling of the word

c) Methods of dealing with affected individuals are very much part of containment when the SCP is a phenomenon which affects large numbers of people

d) Agree with you about the redundancy in your penultimate point

e) I don't really think collapsing the logs is necessary. They're such an integral part of the piece. I think collapsing them will (incorrectly) project that all-too-familiar twist incoming feeling.
