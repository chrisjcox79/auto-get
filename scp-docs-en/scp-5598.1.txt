Hi, this is a poem about not understanding, and as thus, I suspect the audience might not understand it. As much as it is an homage to the obtuseness of Mid-century American poets like Bernstein, Antin, Olson, and Ginsberg, it is still mostly meant to be understandable as a mainlist article.

This was also election week therapy.

TL;DR: It's a bog-standard Series I item (with a touch of Series III terminology) filtered through seventy years of American poetry.

05 Dec 2020 19:30

One of my mild frustrations is that a work like this, when received by an internet audience of primarily working young people mostly steeped in genre fiction, is seen as extremely avant-garde when in fact it's a pale imitation of a now elderly poetic form first invented in the early 80s. What we see as the avant-garde is now hopelessly out of date. Bernstein is 70. David Antin is dead. Kenneth Goldsmith's post-digital internet utopianism became obsolete in 2015 when he was cancelled for turning Michael Brown's autopsy report into a poem. The days of looking to Twitter for art are long rotted six years on since. And it's very hard to try and find new ways of looking at the world in a post-post-digital humanistic landscape whose intelligentsia are more likely to have Invader Zim as a reference point to glom onto than any sort of brand new literary advancements, mostly as a result of childhood nostalgia steeped in the trauma of living in this late-capitalist hellscape.

This is not a criticism strictly speaking of the wiki's userbase, who are intelligent writers and readers, it's more an indictment of myself. Regardless of this poem's actual artistic worth or value as an SCP object/story, it was very stupid of me to parade it out and expect a ticker tape parade for my genius, if only because it's not very good and no one, me included, would be anywhere near the intellectual circles required to point that out with any serious authority. I just have a chronic illness where combining the SCP format, normally the denizen of funnee peanut memes and clickbait Youtube videos for children when it isn't left-wing internet urban fantasy/horror stories, with avant garde writings is endlessly fascinating. The sooner I grow out of it the smarter I will be.
