Two objects falling through a near-vacuum from the same starting position towards the surface of a planet have a decent chance of eventually coming into contact with one another. Especially if one of those things is biological and would tend to, shall we say, flatten out a bit on impact.

You are correct that it is completely infeasible that fossil records would be able to definitely identify a human.
