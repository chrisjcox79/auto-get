Protip: Always google something you're unfamiliar or unsure about.

An Arming Sword is the technical name of what most people nowadays would call a longsword. One handed, simple crossguard, blade between 28-31 inches (70-80cm). They are also known as Knightly Swords.

Viking Age Sword or Carolingian Sword.

Nevermind. It's dated to a transitional period between Carolingian and Knightly Swords, where neither terms would be a perfect fit.

To be completely  pedantic, the sword shown isn't a Viking Era sword at all. The crossguard is too wide, and the brazil nut pomel wasn't in use during that period.

It's definitely a Carolingian or pre-knightly arming sword. I preserved it from the original article.

A  swords (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ulfberht_swords).

Basically no crossguard, the knurled  pomels, and double edged with no taper would typify a viking age sword.
