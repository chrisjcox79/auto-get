附加记录：    

Actually, this is more where different authors diverge. If it was easy to absolutely prevent the button from being pressed, I'd personally call it Safe. In practice, even the miniscule chance that the button could accidentally be pressed, for instance in a breach event where something fell on the locked box, might necessitate much stronger containment protocols, bumping it up to Euclid or probably Keter - because that would be difficult to contain to a satisfactory extent. A nuke would be Safe because you can pretty reliably guess that it's not going to go off on its own.

Other authors may or may not disagree. But either way, this one is Safe.

Also, Anax, the humanoid SCPs example doesn't actually contradict what Tabbyclaw said That's why most humanoid SCPs that would otherwise be Safe get bumped to Euclid.

Oh yeah, this is totally safe. No argument there.

The thing about a nuke, though, is that those are specifically designed to be safe and inert. A poorly made nuke, one that's pouring off radiation, would definitely not be Safe. It is indeed a matter of author preference, but I really feel people don't just sit down and evaluate the resources required to contain something. One hard and fast rule leads to some unpleasant corner cases, and I think only the more case by case approach that is the de facto victor without an official standard does a good job at avoiding problematic ratings, especially with the correction mechanism of these threads.
