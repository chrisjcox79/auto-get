Fold

20 Aug 2011 01:55

Hm.

I don't dislike this, but I don't like it either. I like the idea of it, though; I'd like to see a man-eating tree SCP.

You'd mention the hook was that this thing is too intelligent for a tree (animal-level intelligence at least), but on reflection that doesn't necessarily come off in the article. For all we know, the Mkodo were just idiots.

A way to get across that it's animal intelligent is to give it more animalistic traits. Like, it appears to carefully arrange bones, etc, or discard the bones and arrange the skulls, for some unknown purpose. That seems to be what you're hinting at in the article already, but it's not clear why the tree is doing it. It doesn't need a motivation or purpose for that, but why not give it one? Some kind of mating behavior? Is it learning to attack prey? Can it build traps? Does it lure animals in close? What animals does it prefer to prey on, typically? Medium-mass mammals? Perhaps not large prey animals that could drastically harm it (and avoiding them would be another sign of intelligence). How much does it need to eat? Do insects serve as snacks, while, say, an antelope or capybara would sit there digesting for a while? Does it strangle / constrict such victims first?

And how did it get the villagers to see it as requiring human sacrifice (intentionally or not)? That's intriguing. Expansion might help.

Oh, and if it's a tree that explicitly exhibits animal behavior without any biological explanation, it's definitely anomalous, so that should take care of some of these other comments.

Minor nitpick: if it lives primarily in high-precip areas, what's the one in the picture doing in the Sahara?
