We've got so many diseases and parasites that rely on creep-factor that I don't have any ideas on revitalizing this one. The unique thing it has going for it is that it resembles the modern cold at times, perhaps leading the reader to think "What if I've been infected?" It's an interesting idea but hard to carry out, and the execution here seems lacking. Any thoughts on how to expand on that?

I haven't had a whole lot of time to properly revise this one (or anything else, for that matter), but I don't want to see it deleted, so a partial revision will have to do for now. I agree with cadence that part of 129's appeal is it could be out there, right now, so I'm thinking that should be played up. Maybe give Stage Three less obvious symptoms so it wouldn't look totally out of place in "the real world". Also, 129 can infect more than just people, so maybe an incident involving infected dogs, or cats, or bears….

Also, this may just be me, but it seems like, as written, there really isn't anything substantively justifying the Keter classification. None of the effects listed or even obliquely hinted at seem to justify a classification higher than "Euclid". All we have justifying this thing's classification is the author's say-so and a throwaway reference to drastic measures needing to be taken in the case of a Stage Four or Stage Five outbreak. I may be in the wrong here, but it just strikes me as coming down on the wrong side of the line between "creepy and mysterious" and "obnoxiously obfuscated" as far as the justification for its class.
