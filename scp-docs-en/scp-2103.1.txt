Fold

18 Aug 2016 02:42

I like the idea, finding it intriguing and with a lot of possibility. But there are a number of problems with how it's written.

First, as has been pointed out, the whole  means without reaching that level of understanding in other areas?

This is clearly NOT a  object. It's able to be activated from a distance, accidentally. It can alter reality retroactively. And the limits of what can be altered by storing memories is unclear. One person accidentally shoving memories around containment procedures or even research on other SCPs could conceivably lead to a retroactive containment breach. Because of all this complexity and possibility, it feels like the write-up really undersells the power, potential, and danger of this object.

Also, the revised containment procedures don't feel like they really fit. I understand why they'd switch to a group being put in charge. But the whole  via the jar in a manner that has a big enough impact to affect the entire group of researchers, a documented dissenting opinion likely wouldn't offer much of a counterpoint.

I had originally intended to make one point about why I was doing a neutral vote, but kept finding more to point out and convinced myself to downvote. I'd love to see a rewrite that really explores the potential of the jar.
