19 Jul 2019 13:29

Hi, original translator here like three years later. A couple thoughts.

I chose to translate this article simply because it was the featured SCP back then (which people never had the time to update, so it was stuck there for quite a long time). I ran into a lot of difficulty deciding how to render the big gotcha moment where qiqiao is revealed to not be a classical chinese name into English. Ultimately I chose to substitute the pun by giving it the psuedo-chinese name Kyet Khaewt, giving a mysterious and exotic impression before the reader realizes near the end that it's just a garbling of Kit Kat. I chose not to annotate cultural allusions like the golden dragon and toad (common symbols in Daoist alchemical literature) to maintain that exoticism. The charm of the entire article rests upon this simple turn, so I wanted to be natural and intuitive to the English reader instead of being burgeoned by footnotes or overtly literal translations.

I also agree that the end table was rather too random, partially because it was written so early on. It does have a pattern to it as explained (although I think you missed the references to the MARS bar), but as the original was already written in a way where it was not quite obvious, I decided to leave it that way as well. Faith to the original and all, even the flaws.
