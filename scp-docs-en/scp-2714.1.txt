30 Apr 2016 15:39

here's what's going on.

Mr Escoffier was a Tintin fanatic who tried to summon lovecraftian monstrosities, but their minds accidentally got trapped within the Tintin book.

When you open the book to the page in question, the monstrosities' (Tintin-based) bodies instantiate, but remain mindless and inert, because their minds are trapped in the panels of the book. They keep screaming and ranting in Tintin-based vocabularies, and they can't even stop making Tintin allusions.

Their bodies are based on the four main characters of Tintin (three humans and a dog): Tintin (orange hair and light blue outfit), Captain Haddock (black hair and dark blue outfit), Professor Calculus (black hair and green outfit), and Snowy (white dog). The whiskey is because Captain Haddock was a heavy drinker, and they're giant barnacles because one of Captain Haddock's character traits was his ridiculously ornate swearing, including a phrase which was translated into English as .

If the monstrosity minds could control the bodies, they could wreak great terror on this pathetic world. But they can't. They can't control the bodies, they can't access them, they can't even make the bodies die or de-instantiate. All they can do is alter the text in the speech bubbles on two pages of one copy of one graphic novel.

And then you close the book, and the entities' minds are forced back into dormancy.

Actually, I've realized  that AWCY isn't even necessary here.

The barnacles are indicative of why. They allude to the phrase , which only appeared in the English translation. Escoffier was a francophone. Why would he have the English-language version instead of the French-language original?

He was a collector and a completionist. This is why he had the translated version  but also, it's why he used the translated version. He didn't want to use his prized original! It was a first printing! It was even autographed! If he sacrificed it in the ritual, it'd be ruined!

So instead of sacrificing to his gods something he truly valued and prized and cherished, he used something which he considered a barely-adequate substitute. Maybe he figured the gods wouldn't notice?

And now his gods are lobotomized blobs of flesh based on the English dialogue.

If you look at the original French, Capitaine Haddock's original swearing . That basically means he was swearing by gunports from which cannons were firing. Imagine what would have happened if that had formed the basis of the godbodies.

The godminds aren't trapped in the book forever, though. Just until the original summoning is complete. They just have to form , then they'll finally be free.

Literally, they have to form billions of barnacloid godbodies. A minimum of two billion. 2 * 109.

And they can only form three at a time. And each triad takes ten minutes to form. And they can only form when the book is open to the right page.

The book would have to be open to that page for over eleven thousand years.
