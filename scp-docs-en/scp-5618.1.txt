Fold

01 Dec 2020 15:57

This article isn’t technically bad, but it’s bland and that might be worse. SZ and AF are barely characters; their dialogue is spartan and only tells us that they’ve worked together before. Both the disaster and the buildup feel underwhelming because the CK class shift is never expounded upon and the containment procedures don’t do a good job of demonstrating the escalating scale of the threat. The ending is similarly predictable — the timeline was wiped out but I don’t feel any sense of loss because I barely knew it. There’s a lot of similarities here at the idea level to Lily’s Proposal, but the former actually escalates the threat and thus intrigue of the anomaly and the latter actively creates a melancholy tone that sells the poignancy of its core idea. 5618, in comparison, lays out a threat but fails to make the escalation feel meaningful or convey the poignancy of the end of the world.
