I like this. I really, really do. It's horror, but not the squicky kind. Losing your ability to do some that brings you joy, something you make a living with or something you're simply good at is a special kind of fear. What makes it even worse is this can easily happen via non-anomalous means. Brain injuries can happen to anyone. The symptoms from exposure appear to match.

Also, I enjoyed seeing a GOI (especially one like AWCY) feeling the logical consequences of their actions. If you're a dick, you're eventually going to piss someone off who can harm you in return. And they may just not care they'll get caught in crossfire.

This feels too close the what I dislike about AWCY? even if it's not the intended point. It seems overt anomalous art is always centered around  would be interesting aspects of art to explore through SCPs that aren't depressing or dangerous. Maybe it's me forgetting articles or not reading the right ones, but a lot of the stuff that seems to get close to exploring artists tends to be kinda depressing or generally negative. A more positive outlook of art would be welcome.

SCP-4390 is about a guy making a real life version of a board game because he likes that stuff, and there's no dead participants. I find that type exploration of artists and art much more interesting than grabbing the cliched elitists  and making them malicious.

Kalinin's Metacritique 2 does a way better job of explaining my point, and there's a quote that I really like.

+block

–block

Just imagine the things that artists could do if the strictures of what is possible are completely lifted. What if Michelangelo were not bound by the practical limitations of the dimensions and accessibility of the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel? What kind of journey could a Kiowa shaman provide if he had something more powerful than mere peyote to dispense? What wonders could the pharaohs have commanded if the laws of physics were optional?
