This skip is basically Mr. Hax in terms of its concept, which is that ultimately, the created hates its creator, and struggles to deal with that. Hax bogs itself down in its anomaly (more impressive than this one yet still not really playing into the concept or character much) and follow-through (Hax’s feelings and motivations felt muddied and confusing, but had actual emotional weight). I get not every anomaly is gonna like existing, but they have to be interesting.

And yes, this is compared to Hax because it follows almost the exact same beats and doesn’t improve on that article at all. Your follow-through ends with “mad about existing” (incidentially, my favorite Mister), and also this guy’s existance is a living Hell, so I guess I’m sad for him. This doesn’t have any character traits or personality pieces I remember at all after finishing, whereas I can at least remember with Hax that it felt super bitter but still sorrowful and wistful. This? He’s just mad because he’s angry.

So it's a good skip for people who don't like the group, but it's not actually a good skip.

Well, the Circus is in this one, so I'm pretty much contractually obligated to upvote. It's an obligation I'm happy to fulfill, though—the weird anatomical descriptions have some really nice imagery, and his creation backstory is refreshingly blasé. This is probably the worst  writers. Decibelles two years ago was. frankly, way too kind by comparing this to Mr. Hax. There was an actual reason behind the creation of Mr. Hax that felt somewhat plausible.

What's the joke here? Don't play too many video games? Wow wasn't the exact premise of another Mister Against Weed *cough* Ms. Mad About Video Games *cough* or anything.

Why?

Itsense.

Was it?  Then the kid makes it and just puts this actual person  in a closet to snort dominoes until he gets caught? Wtf? Why would literally anyone do that unless they were seriously sadistic?

Then you look at the portrait, and it's just like wow. The following Foundation-involved Misters Against Weed skips have portraits: Mr. Top Text/Mr. Bottom Text.

Let'sdifference

*Sighs*

It doesn't black out the eyes like every single other one in the entire series. Literally, the only ones that don't are: Mr. Get Anything For Free In Any Shop (who is currently wanted by the FBI, so they presumably don't want to mask his identity or anything).

I have yet even to mention the contrived reason for this thing to even exist. It obviously wasn't part of the original joke?? Like, it was a set list of Misters Against Weed. Obviously, that shouldn't stop someone from making a good story or going with a cool concept if they have one to tell, but you gotta actually address why it wasn't part of the original joke (a la Mr. High Quality).

Severeinconsistencies

Aforward?

Literally the only way, the only way this would be remotely plausible was if it was made by an outsider trying to get into the group (similar to SCP-3078). Oh, but hey it looks like wannabee GAW member makes a thing with a big 

Inconsistencies you say? What inconsistencies? Well, allow me to show you:

First the interview: [Blue highlights are mine]

Well, why not?? You're telling me that the guy who has nothing better to do besides being  is just straight up tired of the having the only conversation he's ever had that didn't involve snorting toy pieces or tricking people for their money?

The alternative to this exchange was one where the Connect Four game-set that is literally his heart resets which causes him  (ie, a heart attack). That is a plausible reason for me to believe he needs to stop answering questions, and it would demonstrate his actually terrible health.

The opening line is so unbelievably forced that I can't believe it was played straight.

Second, I'm sorry, what? The RA found out about him? Why was this mentioned no where in the report? This is a rather huge implication that an SCP was discovered at a college dorm, but there are no traces of this incident? Couldn't this be used to establish a timeline here?

What happened to the kid's  if you were immediately kicked out? Was the kid not punished for harboring an anomalous person in a college dorm this entire time?

Maybe this was all at Deer College, who freaking knows at this point.

Lastone

BigOOF

First reports of SCP-3245's existence had been documented in Atlanta, GA, when online records related to the Gamers Against Weed GoI were found during a standard web crawl operation. The activity of SCP-3245 during the six month gap between its creation and recovery by Foundation agents remains unknown.
附加记录：    

This has to be the most telling piece of this article. There's literally zero mention of who created the skip despite that being something *SCP-3245* would obviously know. He has all the incentive in the world to say, too, considering he hates GAW and all. In fact, you gotta wonder why he isn't begging to be allowed to help get the kid detained by the Foundation.

Still, you have to ask yourself how the Foundation doesn't know something as trivial as where Mr. Playing Games Is Bad For Your Health was for six months. They could just you know.. ask him.

I guess the Foundation also doesn't care that there is some college out there creating anomalies on the fly with apparently detrimental repercussions to their creations. They didn't bother even mentioning the kid's internet handle in the report, which is like a bare-minimum for this kind of stuff.

Oh, and let's not forget the missed opportunity here. The most critically acclaimed aspect of GAW? It's the thing that only had a passing mention here. It's honestly probably for the better that the Chatlogs don't exist here, though.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

I know Roget writes good stuff because I have read and loved it all. This, however, is a disappointment to that amazing track-record and should probably be removed from the mainsite. It just isn't good. -1
