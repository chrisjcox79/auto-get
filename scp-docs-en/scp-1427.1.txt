Fold

09 Jul 2012 23:24

If its effects are caused by an electromagnetic signal of some kind, it is possible to build a containment vessel which would block those. Considering the fact that the signal would necessarily need to be complex to have this sort of effect, the bit-rate would need to be high (if this thing really is pulsing every 7ns), which means that it would not be a ELF signal. And while VLF electromagnetic signals are hard to block, non-ELF signals can be. Build a thick box of appropriate metal (what metal is appropriate and the thickness varies depending on the frequency; lead will stop gamma rays, while something a sheet of aluminum foil will stop visible light), put the item in there, and then either sink it under a few hundred or more feet of water (which does not transmit non-ELF signals well) or stick it down at the bottom of a mile deep mineshaft. Problem solved.

While the Foundation might be willing to prop up a backwater dictatorship if there was a good reason, when cheaper, less disruptive, and more effective containment procedures are possible, then I don't see them doing that. I'd expect that sort of behavior out of the Chaos Insurgency perhaps, but the Foundation has a difficult enough time containing things without unnecessarily meddling in world politics.

Downvoted for unnecessary containment procedures and unneeded interference in world politics.

It's an easter-egg alluded to by the dissent at the bottom, but the Soviets tried to destroy it in 1957. They failed, and evacuated the area shortly thereafter instead. The Foundation doesn't think it can do better, and  it doesn't have a nuclear weapon on-hand to try.

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyshtym_disaster

And actually, the artifact doesn't 'want' much. It was originally a device designed to keep pesky Homo erectus from making trouble. It just happens to have been overengineered, and the descendants of Homo erectus which it was designed to effect are now worldwide. Totalitarian conditioning mitigates its effects not because of its design, but on the principle of inoculation: it's a less powerful version of what thing is attempting to do to all man-kind.
