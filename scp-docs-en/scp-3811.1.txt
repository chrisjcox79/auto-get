Fold

18 Sep 2017 04:43

There's a few things standing in the way of making this article really shine, chief among them the agonizingly long interlude of the blogger's story. It intrudes on the main narrative without bringing anything fresh to the table. It features a basic, flat character magick'd on a collision course with fate - and tells in a roundabout manner what is already known (or conveyed more efficiently by being alluded to via interview).

It might have served an illustrative purpose if the mechanics of the anomaly weren't already plainly laid out, or it might have offered a readers a character to connect to if they'd been developed to have their own voice instead of being an expository set-piece, but as it stands, it's distracting and a waste of space.

The second issue is how far suspension-of-disbelief must be stretched to accommodate the notion that they will actually allow personnel to expose themselves apparently for shits and giggles. It reads to me as lolfoundation trying to be played straight, except it's nonsensical to begin with. I just can't conceive of the rationality behind it when they have guinea-pigs at their disposal. It's unpredictable, known to have a potentially detrimental effect on the subject's psyche, and has little actual value compared to conventional treatments.

This reminds me of Rimple, but it's missing all the elements that makes it succeed. Where in 948, personnel exposure makes sense due to its predictability and beneficial nature - here it is haphazard, dangerous, and random. Where 948 hones in on its characters and delivers a concise story, here it reads like slapdash list of weird things, explanatory found-note interlude, and the beginning of a character study stapled to the end.

While the interviews provided some insight and raise important questions, it ends up being spoon-fed to the reader in an uninteresting fashion, by a poorly-established character with a set of contrived circumstances I simply cannot swallow. I would recommend strengthening the main character's personality, motives, and mind-set and try to have her develop over the course of the story - everything else is window dressing at the moment.

-1
