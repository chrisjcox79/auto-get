Here am I floating 'round my tin can

Far above the moon

Planet Earth is blue

And there's nothing I can do
附加记录：    

Recommended listening here, if you need more.

This is probably the best thing I've ever written. Although, I usually determine that based off of the simple formula C = Q, where C represents Confusing and Q represents Quality.

Massive, massive, massive thanks to anyone who managed to read that entire mindfuck while it was still a draft, including TrustyOlValet2 does not match any existing user name. I can't remember my critters, so please let me know if I missed you.

Additional thanks to all the people that submitted to my headcanon questionnaire. Totally unnecessary, but it just adds an extra layer of meta.

Finally, thanks to you for reading. And hopefully, for helping out Berkeley.

Wouldn't it suck to not exist?

kicked can down the road

09 Aug 2020 02:28

The statement Bob picked up a can did help clarify a few things for me about the unwritten reality that is left off the page. There are realities that the reader can infer until the author writes something that contradicts them. Bob must have an arm and a hand to grasp the tin can and the can must have been resting on a surface for him to pick it up. It's the reader's nature to fill in the gaps of information that the author creates. I might be completely wrong. In that next sentence the author could reveal that Bob is an octopus and it picked up the can with a tentacle

Swanns, Pickman, and voids

17 Jun 2020 12:34

I usually go this way as well, although I invite the alternate interpretation. I kinda see this article as the exact opposite interpretation of Swann's Proposal is 'just' the idea of authors governing a lower reality through their writings, then IHP proposal is the narrative itself as a force or being that remains active regardless of what or who is watching (or in other words, its the theory of narrative causality), and SCP-5309 is that it is ultimately the reader who indirectly controls everything that the author does not explicitly spell out. This reminds me a lot of the empty hand from DC comics

I had played with the concept of narrative voids in my head for a while, but its kinda cool someone else had similar ideas. Maybe I can do something with this concept in a future article
